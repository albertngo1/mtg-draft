## Summary

Ben and Ethan turn the analytical lens on themselves, walking through two of their own full 45-pick Secrets of Strixhaven draft logs rather than critiquing the pros. The framing question is the central tension of the format: greed and raw power versus mana base and responsibility. The first draft is a Soup/Converge build that begins with two Snarl Songs, and the recurring debate is whether Ben should have prioritized tapped dual lands and Environmental Scientist (fixing) over greedy double-off-color bombs like Ark of Hunger. Ethan's repeated thesis: "If my mana base were already better, I would want more fixing. With my mana base being bad, I want a double off-color card" — meaning fixing should be taken precisely when the mana is shaky. Ben went greed every pick, yet the deck still went 7-win and crushed because of how easy true five-color mana makes hitting Converge.

The second draft is Ethan's, opening with Prismari, the Inspiration (UR mythic) and then a contentious P1P2 where he takes Slumbering Trudge over Impractical Joke. Ben argues Trudge is a beatdown/mid-range card that doesn't fit either of Ethan's likely destinations (Prismari tempo or Soup), while Ethan defends it as simply the best card and better than his weak low-end blue. The deck resolves into a blue-red base splashing green for Sundering Archaic and Arcane Omens, leaning heavily into a late-game build maximized by Prismari and supported by tech like Return the Favor.

The broader meta-discussion: the Lords agree there are effectively six viable SOS archetypes (the five college color pairs plus Converge/Soup), that Converge is more an "ethos" than a fixed shell, and that the format suffers from "sameness" — cards have largely static value with few battlefield surprises. Their biggest takeaway is that drafts with early power are easy and full of interesting decisions, while powerless drafts are very hard to escape with more than a 3-3 or 4-3 deck. They tease a future episode on how to salvage the scrappiest, power-starved drafts.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Six real archetypes:** the five colleges plus Converge/Soup. The colleges are Silverquill (WB), Prismari (UR), Witherbloom (BG), Lorehold (RW), Quandrix (GU).
- **Converge is an ethos, not a fixed shell.** The goal is always the same: don't lose to aggro while going bigger than the other Converge decks. Components vary draft to draft, but a true five-color mana base actually makes hitting Converge counts (one of each color) surprisingly easy.
- **Snarl Song is a premier Converge/Soup payoff.** Two Snarl Songs by P1P4 effectively locks you into Converge — basically nothing should push you off (only multiple white rares like Practiced Scrollsmith, and even then you'd just play Lorehold-leaning Converge).
- **Take lands aggressively once you know your lane.** When committed to Converge, snap tapped duals and true five-color lands. The episode flags not taking Spectacle Summit and Titan's Grave (instead taking Wander Off / Feed the Swarm removal) as the key misplays — removal is easy to pick up later; fixing isn't.
- **Great Hall of the Biblioplex > Terramorphic Expanse for Converge.** Terramorphic counts as five sources mentally but yields only one color once cracked, taking the other basics off the table; the true five-color land keeps all colors live and can become a 2/4 body.
- **Fixing-when-poor heuristic:** If your mana is already good, take more power; if your mana is bad, that's exactly when an off-color fixer (Environmental Scientist, Potioner's Trove) pays off most. Environmental Scientist into Proctor's Gaze is "easy mode" for a Converge mana base.
- **Cost of Brilliance is excellent in Witherbloom/Silverquill mid-range** (you get cards, a counter, and Reparation/Reparte triggers) but bad in Soup/control — there are better ways to draw, and you want cards that disrupt the opponent's plan.
- **Quandrix tempo ("Quandrix small")** wins not via small bodies alone but by presenting a juicy threat (the increment-counter two-drops) the opponent wants to target, then blowing them out with Royal Treatment or a hexproof/+3 trick held up — the growing two-drops let you keep trick mana available every turn.
- **P1P2 is nearly weightless in SOS** — whatever you take has essentially zero impact on the rest of your draft. Prismari Charm, Visionaries Dance, Essence Scatter, Rubble Rouser are all fine; just take what you want. (Prismari Charm's "1 to each of two targets" is a clean answer to Essence-Knit Scholars.)
- **Visionaries Dance** is impressive in Snarl Song/Converge decks and a strong common, especially with rebuy effects and a late-game payoff to dig toward.
- **Zafri/Zaffai-style "free spell" payoffs** are mostly weak but spike with surveil lands (Wilderness Reclamation impression) and with Wisdom of the Ages — putting a 5/7 down plus free mana on later turns solves the "can I do enough each turn to not die" problem.
- **Tide-Hauler (Exhibition Tide-Hauler)** is one of the best Soup endgame cards (mill 3 on cast, mill 10 if 5+ MV).
- **Slumbering Trudge** is a beatdown/mid-range card (4-mana 6/6 with no ETB). Red interacts poorly with 6 toughness, but every other color answers it (blue bounce, white/black removal, deathtouch fights). It's awkward in Prismari and weak in Soup — don't splash a color for it there.
- **Artistic Process** is a strong, flexible UR-adjacent red card: primarily a "deal 6" (a red answer to Trudge), occasionally a sweeper (2 to each creature you don't control), rarely the 3/3 haste flyer. Modal order on the card reflects usage frequency.
- **Sundering Archaic** is the best card in its pack regardless of your path — a Converge exile threat worth splashing for.
- **Arcane Omens** has a high ceiling (Converge discard) and is great to speculate on late to keep the Converge dream alive.
- **Return the Favor (RTF)** is excellent tech in a UR Soup deck — copy/redirect removal for a blowout (e.g., turn their removal into a 3-for-1) and especially copy their Wisdom of the Ages in the mirror; easy to pitch in red when situational.
- **Grave Researcher** reads better than it plays in Soup — the best Witherbloom/Silverquill mid-range decks want creatures to stay on the battlefield (or trade), so surveiling creatures into the bin to enable its reanimate Reparation is awkward; it's often a glorified Centaur Courser.
- **Loot vs. true build-arounds:** Loot doesn't change the deck you draft, it just powers up a normal Prismari/Soup shell; the witch's cauldron-style build-arounds ask for more work and sometimes still miss the format's power bar.
- **Format reality:** drafts with early power are easy and rich in decisions; power-starved drafts are very hard — aim to escape those with a tight, proactive two-color deck that makes control/Soup decks' lives harder, and be happy with a 3-3 or 4-3.

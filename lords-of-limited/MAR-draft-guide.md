## TL;DR meta read

- **Format verdict (week 1):** A high-synergy, small-set format designed for 4-person pick-2 draft. Decks look "on rails" — the five allied color pairs are prescribed, the card pool is shallow, and the table dictates your colors more than your preferences do.
- **Best color pairs (week 1):** Blue-White (tempo/modified flyers) and Green-White (in-web / spiders) are the early frontrunners. Blue-Black is also excellent if open. Red is the problem child — card quality is low relative to everything else.
- **Do not force archetypes.** The single most important skill is reading signals quickly. You CANNOT soft-force in pick-2 draft. Take the two best cards pack 1 pair 1, then let the table tell you where to go.
- **Key mechanics:** In-web (websling; this set's ninjutsu-adjacent bounce-and-replay), Mayhem (madness variant — discard to cast from graveyard), Connive (loot + counter), Modified (creature has counter, aura, or equipment).
- **Removal is tiered aggressively.** The top removal spells (Mothwing Shroud, Spectacular Tactics, Damning Caress, Death Flame Burst) are high picks. Medium removal (Shock, Perilous Lunge, Scorpion Sting) you'll play but take threats over them.
- **Winning lines:** Bury the opponent in card advantage (many uncommons enable this), or go tempo — get ahead with early threats and protect them. Red-Black and Blue-White can come out fast and hard.
- **Enemy color pairs are real** (week 1 update): Black-Green, White-Black, and Blue-Red (essentially Jeskai splash) come up roughly 1 in 5 drafts. Not forced, but they happen when the table gives you those colors.

**Enemy color pairs (week 1 new information):** Black-Green (self-mill + creature density, splash red via Leeline Weaver), White-Black (strong rares pull you here), Blue-Red/Jeskai (drafting blue open + good red rares). These arise from signal-reading and open seats, not deliberate forcing.

## Format principles

**Draft navigation in pick-2 4-person format:**

- **Pack 1 pair 1:** Take the two best cards regardless of color. Even if they're in disparate guilds, don't take a power-level hit to match them up.
- **Pack 1 pair 2:** Still mostly take the two best cards. But actively notice what powerful cards are *missing* (signals about what your right neighbor took). Don't lock into a color pair yet — the cost of being wrong is huge.
- **Pack 1 pair 3:** This is the pivot pack. Signals are now clear. If you have to abandon ship, do it boldly and aggressively. You will not get another clean pivot opportunity.
- **After pair 3:** You're largely locked in. Draft your colors, leverage the wheel (gold cards in your pair almost always wheel if you're the only drafter in your seat).

**The cardinal rule: you cannot force.** The table decides. Two players at every table will be the sole drafter in one color — be one of them by reading signals, not by forcing.

**Signals in pick-2 are twice as strong and twice as fast.** You see the absence of good cards two at a time. Gold cards wheeling = definitive confirmation your pair is open.

**Sending signals matters.** You are incentivized to lock in early and send clear signals so your neighbor to the left knows not to enter your color pair.

**Don't over-index on synergy mechanics.** Decks end up less synergy-focused than the card pool implies because fewer packs are opened. You often won't hit the right ratio of payoffs and enablers. "Take good cards and make a deck full of good cards" — synergy will happen naturally when it happens.

**Curve and creatures:**
- Almost no playable 1-drops outside of white (City Pigeon). Starting your curve at 1 is a white-exclusive luxury.
- The format has very little junk/chaff at common — nearly every common is a card you're willing to play. This means the wheel has real cards in it.
- Deck-building is where the real decisions live (you often end up with 30+ playables when a color is open).

**Removal tiers:**
- **First picks:** Mothwing Shroud (O-Ring variant), Spectacular Tactics (destroy power-4-or-greater OR counter + hexproof), Damning Caress (destroy + 2 life for 3 mana with villain), Death Flame Burst (deal 4 for 3 mana)
- **Play but take threats over:** Scorpion Sting (-3/-3), Shock (not impressive — modified creatures outgrow it quickly), Perilous Lunge (sorcery speed fight, capped at one copy)

**Toughness thresholds:**
- 4 toughness dodges most common removal (Shock, Pumpkin Bombardment, Scorpion Sting)
- 5 toughness also dodges Death Flame Burst — Favored Fighter's 5-toughness body was noted as the week-1 magic number

**In-web (websling) notes:**
- Works best when you have cheap evasive threats (City Pigeon, Spider Girl) to get tapped and recur ETB creatures
- Out-of-combat tap enablers exist (vehicles; Flash Thompson; tapping your own creature to add mana with Supportive Parents) — not as dependent on attacks connecting as it first appeared
- Opponents will learn to block aggressively vs. green-white; Spectacular Tactics gives you the blowout to punish those blocks
- Don't over-engineer in-web — if your cards are good, the triggers will happen naturally

**Splashing:** Green enables the easiest splashes thanks to Leeline Weaver (mana dork) and basic-land tutors (Xan, Vine Weaver Chariot). Surveil lands (dual lands) are fine for splashing but rarely worth spending picks on unless your draft goes long.

**Legendary commons:** There are many. Be aware of legend-rule tension; second copies in hand are awkward but sometimes conniveable away or usable as alternate-mode activations.

## Card notes (community consensus)

### White

- **City Pigeon** (W — 1/1 flyer, leaves-battlefield makes food): Sleeper that evolved into a legitimate role-player. "City pigeon has been good, not great." Don't over-prioritize it chasing the nut curve; move it down in pick order. Week 1: still good, just not top-common.
- **Spectacular Tactics** (1W — destroy power-4+ OR counter + hexproof): Top-2 white common. Excellent against both the format's big threats and as a blowout in in-web combat scenarios. Ethan's pick for Fusion Gaming card of the week-1 episode.
- **Mothwing Shroud** (2W — O-Ring): The other top-2 white common. Ben slightly prefers it over Spectacular Tactics. Best removal available.
- **Web Up** (2W enchantment — O-Ring variant): Also excellent removal. "Banishing Light / Oblivion Ring of the format."
- **Wildpack Squad** (2W — 2/3 that gives first strike + vigilance at start of combat): Catches the eye for offense and defense impact. Probably not a top white common but worth noting.
- **Daily Bugle Reporters / Principled Referee** (3W — 2/3, distribute counters or graveyard recursion of 2-or-less): Both evaluate similarly. Strong ETB, relevant modes. Take over City Pigeon in most scenarios.
- **Spider UK** (3W — 3/4, draw a card + 2 life if 2+ creatures enter on your turn, in-web 2W): Top white uncommon. Consistently evaluated as #1 white uncommon by both hosts.
- **Flash Thompson / Basil Cabaretti** (1W — 2/2 flash, tap or untap a creature): "Sets up websling turns super well." Good in offense and defense. Ben's #2 white uncommon.
- **Sudden Strike** (1W — destroy attacking or blocking creature): Ben's #2; Ethan left it off (prefer proactive cards). Fine if you want more removal.
- **Selfless Police Captain** (1W — 1/1 that enters with a counter, dumps counter on leaves): Excellent with in-web; gets counter, gives it away on bounce. Sleeper.
- **Zora Spider Fancier** (W — 0/2 legendary, life on creature enter, counter on spider enter): "If you see Zora and can kill it, you need to kill it." Key build-around for the spiders deck. One of the few places where Shock looks good.
- **Gallant Citizen** (GW hybrid — 1/1, draw on enter): Top hybrid common, most important enabler in green-white. Always played in every GW deck.
- **Wonderweave Aerialist / Skyward Spider** (WU hybrid — 2/2 ward 2, flying while modified): Week 1 consensus as Blue-White's best common. Sam: "absolutely busted." Ethan: "a gigantic problem." Take over most cards in blue-white. Underrated by community; wheels later than it should.

### Blue

- **Snatchback / Whoosh** (1U instant — bounce with kicker draw): Ben's #1 blue common all the way through. Week-1 data shows it underperforming (possibly because people draw the second pip incorrectly), but both hosts still rate it first.
- **Fantasmal Vision / Mysterio's Fantasm** (1U — 1/3 flying vigilance villain, mills on attack): Data breakout of the week-1 episode — #1 blue common by GIH WR per 17Lands. "Wild to me" — the flying vigilance body is incredibly resilient to removal (counters make it a 2/4+ flying vigilance). Key villain type matters for blue-black. Both hosts: "poo pooed" initially, now fully on board.
- **Unstable Experiment** (1U instant — draw a card, then creature connives): Both hosts in agreement this is excellent. "Played out like a two mana combat tutorial — draw two, get a counter." High on both top-common lists.
- **Drix's Interception / Amazing Acrobatics** (UU instant — counter target spell, tap up to two creatures): Preview episode: initial garbage vote. Crash course: Ben came around fully. Week 1: "performing very very well." Counter + tempo swing is devastating. Don't neglect this.
- **Spiderbite Web Warden** (2U — 2/2, bounce up to one non-land permanent, may bounce your own): #1 blue uncommon on both hosts' lists. The optional bounce-your-own clause is relevant for in-web resets and protecting modified creatures.
- **School Days** (3UU instant — draw 3 OR counter + draw): Preview: Ethan garbage, Ben great. Crash course: Ben reversed to great, Ethan on board. Week 1: not discussed, implied good. A split-card counterspell plus draw-3 at 5 mana is powerful if you can hold it up.
- **Robotics Mastery** (4U flash aura — +2/+2 and make two 1/1 flying robots on enter): Both hosts agree: "nuts," "absurd every time." Tutor target for Serena Bargain Spinner. Worth splashing.
- **Ozor / Doc O Sinister Scientist** (4U — 4/5 common, becomes 8/8 with 8+ cards in graveyard, hexproof with another villain): "Great" on both hosts' lists. Easy to get 8 cards in graveyard in blue-black. Closing finisher.
- **Beetle Legacy Criminal** (3U — 3/3 flyer legendary villain, exile from graveyard to give flying + counter): Evaluated as close to good but knocked for being legendary. "Garbage" final verdict from both, though a first copy is close.
- **Dog Sinister Scientist / Ozor Chronicler of Collapse** (same card as Ozor above, confirmed).
- **Mob Lookout** (BU hybrid — 0/3, target creature connives on enter): Preview: Ethan liked it, Ben did not. Week 1: Ethan confirmed 3-copy blue-black deck, liked them for enabling Mayhem and conniving into win conditions. "Blue black got shafted with Mob Lookout" — still a debate.
- **Serena Bargain Spinner** (3 AZR hybrid — 3/2 flying on your turn, tutors aura or equipment): High pick in blue-white when Robotics Mastery or Stitcher Wings are in the deck. The curve of Serena into Robotics Mastery on 5 is "real good."

### Black

- **Damning Caress / Venom's Hunger** (4B sorcery — destroy target creature, gain 2 life; costs 2 less with villain): Week-1 consensus: "far and away the best black common." Always have a villain = 3-mana destroy + gain 2. Early preview underestimated this at 5 mana.
- **Scorpion Sting** (1B instant — -3/-3): Good but falls off hard vs. modified creatures or creatures with 4+ toughness. Ben: moved it down in pick order, doing better since. Week 1: C++ tier removal. Not in the same tier as Damning Caress.
- **Spots Portal** (2B instant — put a creature on bottom of library, lose 2 unless you control a villain): Top black uncommon on both hosts' lists. Conditional removal that's usually unconditional in black.
- **Nil / Tombstone Career Criminal** (2B — 2/2 villain uncommon, returns villain from graveyard, villains cost 1 less): Top-2 uncommon alongside Spots Portal. "Old Rutstein from OTJ" comparison. "Gravedigger that makes villains cheaper." Excellent in any black deck.
- **Swarm Being of Bees / Wetdu** (2B — 2/2 flash flying legendary, Mayhem 1B): Week-1 hosts' pick for black's #2 common (behind Damning Caress). Flash flying that cantrips itself into a 1-mana 2/2. Legendary kept it in check.
- **Merciless Enforcers** (B — 2/1 lifelink villain, activated ping ability): Week-1 surprise overperformer. "Constantly surprised me." Cheap villain with types plus useful late-game mana dump.
- **Risky Research / Eerie Gravestone**: Gravestone (2-mana artifact — cantrip + mill 4, put creature into hand for 1B) is good in black-green specifically; hits the creature density threshold. In blue-black decks, often can't reliably mill 4 and hit. Still a solid two-for-one card on its own.
- **Ethics Gift of Malice** (4B — 4/5 deathtoucher, exile from graveyard to give +X/+X and deathtouch): Week-1 overperformer. Impossible to double-block. Good in red-black too (pitch aggressively, cast from graveyard for +2/+2 buff).

### Red

- **Death Flame Burst** (2R sorcery — deal 4 damage, Mayhem 1R): #1 red common. "Insanely good." Preview: Ethan skeptical; crash course: came up. Week 1 settled on C++ tier, better than Shock.
- **Shock** (R — deal 2 damage): Week-1 verdict: "not good." "Embarrassing against Wonderweave Aerialist with ward 2." Play one copy maximum; serious diminishing returns. Kills Leeline Weaver specifically — "shock looks real good" near Zora.

- **Restless Razerkin / Superior Foes of Spider-Man** (2R — 3/3 trample, exiles top card on MV4, you keep access until it's replaced): #1 red uncommon consensus. "Really strong card." Single-pipped so it gets splashed away. Week 1: "red green had three of these, had to kill it on sight."
- **Sadistic String Puller / Spider Islanders** (3R — 4/3, Mayhem 1R): Ben: sleeper common, very good. Sam: similar thoughts. The common version of Lavaorn Goblins.
- **Masked Meow** (R — 1/1 haste, discard and sacrifice to draw): Preview: "garbage." Crash course: Ben reversed hard — "really good and really important for red's low-curve aggro plan." Sam also fully on board. Key enabler for the mayhem deck.

## Signals & seat reads

- **This format teaches signal-reading, not forcing.** You genuinely cannot force a color pair. The table will punish you by pack 1 pair 3 if you ignore the signals.
- **Gold cards wheeling = your pair is open.** If a Gallant Citizen, Spider Girl, or Scarlet Spider Cane wheels back to you, you are unambiguously the sole drafter in that pair. Keep going.
- **Absence of cards is a signal.** If pack 1 pair 2 has zero white cards, your right neighbor almost certainly took two white cards. Factor this into your pair-2 decision even before committing.
- **Red is the easiest color to find open.** Week 1 community consensus is that red is the weakest color. Pods with one or zero red drafters are common. If you end up base red, you can often sweep red cards off the wheel.
- **White is the best starting color** (Ben crash course): "I'm going into drafts hoping to start white and hoping to start blue. Those are the two deepest, most flexible colors." White pivots cleanly into either GW or UW. Blue pivots into UW or UB.
- **Sending signals to your left matters** (unique to pick-2): By locking in and passing nothing in your pair, you can effectively bully your left neighbor out of your color pair. "You're incentivized to send very clear signals" — Ben week 1. This is more important than in normal draft.
- **What wheels:** In a 4-person draft with only 5 archetypes, non-hybrid gold cards almost never wheel if two people are in adjacent archetypes. Hybrid commons (Gallant Citizen, Leeline Weaver) often wheel because they fit multiple decks but nobody prioritizes them.
- **Pivot advice:** If you haven't committed by pair 2-3, you have exactly one clean pivot opportunity. Take it aggressively. After pair 4, you're stuck wherever you are.

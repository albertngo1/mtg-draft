## Summary

This is the second draft of a Secrets of Strixhaven early access event (on a free Wizards of the Coast preview account). Coming off a Silverquill (WB) draft, the drafter deliberately tries to do something different and leans into Lorehold (RW) — specifically a tokens-go-wide build that also dabbles in the "cards leaving your graveyard" theme. Early picks include a token-making rare, Teacher's Pest, Hop to It, Kill Roll, Spirit Call Enthusiast (a tokens payoff), Rubble Rouser, Group Project (a rare team pump for a go-wide board), Molten Note (the scalable red/white removal/finisher), and multiple copies of Ancestral Anger (ended up with four). The deck shapes up as a lean, aggressive Lorehold token deck with light interaction and a thin spell count, leaning on payoffs like Spirit Call Enthusiast, Lorehold Charm, and the leave-the-graveyard cards (Charging Strife Knight, Hardened Academic, Living History, Owl Historian).

The drafter is happy with the curve being "lean and mean" but repeatedly flags two concerns that come back to haunt the deck: not enough payoffs (wishing one pick had been the five-mana enchantment / Practiced Scrollsmith) and a shortage of real removal/interaction. Picks like Wilt in the Heat and Stand Up for Yourself are taken specifically to shore up interaction. The deck ends up splashy-curious but ultimately stays RW.

In the games, the deck shows well and produces genuinely interactive, decision-filled matches, but loses 0-3. The first loss is to a Prismari (UR) opponent who casts an absurd number of board wipes / sweepers (described as "six sweepers," including a Brotherhood's End-style three-damage-to-each effect), simply out-attritioning the go-wide plan. Later games are lost to mana issues (stuck on double white, then single white), a life-gain Witherbloom (BG) deck the aggro plan can't race, and topdeck wars against decks with card advantage and lifegain. The drafter concludes the games were close and interactive but that Lorehold aggro is an awkward spot when sweepers and lifegain decks are floating around the format.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Lorehold (RW) supports a tokens-go-wide plan.** Token makers (the spirit-token rare, Hop to It, Group Project's bodies) plus payoffs like Spirit Call Enthusiast ("whenever one or more tokens you control enter...") and Lorehold Charm's team pump (+1/+1 and trample) form a coherent aggressive deck.
- **Spirit Call Enthusiast is a strong tokens payoff** and notably also triggers off treasure-token makers (e.g., the treasure-making goblin common, Glassright), not just creature tokens — worth keeping in mind when evaluating treasure cards.
- **Group Project is a rare mass pump** that the drafter prioritized over more interaction (Duty Beyond Death, Unsubtle Mockery, Expressive Fire Dancer) precisely because it scales with a wide board and has minor leave-the-graveyard synergy.
- **Molten Note is premium RW removal/reach** — for X (or set damage equal to mana spent), deals damage to a target creature and untaps your team, with an 8-mana flashback. High pick.
- **Don't over-commit to the "leave your graveyard" subtheme without enough enablers/payoffs.** The drafter repeatedly wished a key pick had been the five-mana enchantment (Practiced Scrollsmith / a Scrollsmith-style card) for more payoff density. Cards like Charging Strife Knight, Hardened Academic, Living History, Owl Historian, and Kyodai reward graveyard departures but need critical mass.
- **Interaction is scarce and must be actively drafted in Lorehold.** Wilt in the Heat (4-mana deal 5, costs 2 less if a card left your graveyard) and Stand Up for Yourself (destroy a creature with power 3+) were taken specifically to fix a thin removal suite.
- **Beware over-drafting redundant tricks** — ending up with four Ancestral Angers is a sign of a shallow late-pack pool, not a plan. Most went unplayed.
- **P1P1 / early signals:** LSV was passing into this seat. The drafter saw Witherbloom (BG) lifegain cards wheeling and flowing (Hypothesis-style growth payoff, mascots, Golgari lands) and considered Witherbloom was open, but stayed Lorehold — a reminder to read what's actually wheeling.
- **Format watch-outs for aggressive Lorehold:** UR/Prismari decks loaded with sweepers (Brotherhood's End-style 3-to-each, draw-then-wrath effects) punish go-wide token strategies hard, and BG/Witherbloom lifegain decks are very difficult to race. "It's a tough sell to play aggro when there's a lifegain deck out there."
- **Mana base matters:** the deck repeatedly got stuck on double-white then single-white draws, which crippled the aggressive plan. Prioritize fixing lands when the curve depends on a demanding color.
- **Other notable cards seen/evaluated:** Practiced Scrollsmith (rated above the five-mana enchantment), Rubble Rouser, Pigment-style "prepared" payoffs, Glassright (token-making artifact, good for Enthusiast), Antiquities on the Loose, Kill Roll (removal), Expressive Fire Dancer, Repel Calamity (underwhelmed), Render Speechless.

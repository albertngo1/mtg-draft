## Summary

This is the second draft of Day 2 of an Arena Open, following a 3-1 run in Draft 1 with a no-rare blue-white (Azorius) Tempo deck. The drafter opens Aelia and is immediately pulled in a direction that feels uncomfortable — a "this is going to be a draft" situation where white dries up fast and the deck drifts off its early Boros/Azorius leanings. After taking Aelia and a couple of green/white cards (Tunnel Tipster, Nervous Gardener), then picking up power and fixing like Pick the Players, Roots of Life, treacherous terrain, and Smuggler's Copter (Satchel), the draft keeps shifting. The Satchel/Copter pick is later judged the only clearly bad pick of the draft, since the deck moves away from the colors that supported it.

Drafting against strong opponents who do not pass good cards late, the deck ends up Golgari (BG) splashing red for finishers (World Soul's Rage and treacherous terrain), powered by a heavy graveyard/self-mill theme. Key engine pieces include triple District Onlooker, double Basilica Stalker, double Aftermath Maverick, Vitu-Ghazi Inspector, Slimefoot/Strider, and the big payoff Chalk Outline (a seven-pick that the drafter is thrilled to get). The deck has 14 creatures plus removal (double Murder, Slice, the X spells) and is built around "leave the graveyard" / collect-evidence-adjacent value, though the drafter notes much of his graveyard interaction is self-mill rather than collect evidence.

In the matches, the deck loses to a friend (Matt / stunlock) on Izzet (UR) splashing white, a deck full of fliers, counterspells (Reasonable Doubt), and burn (Shock, Felonious Rage, Out Cold). Across multiple tense games, the drafter is repeatedly ground out by the onslaught of evasive fliers and gets punished by tempo-negative blocking decisions — notably blocking with Basilica Stalker instead of Vitu-Ghazi Inspector, which he flags as a costly misplay. The video ends on a close loss, with the drafter happy with the deck's look but unable to deal with the flier-heavy opposing deck.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **P1P1 power matters in MKM:** Aelia is a clear, powerful first pick. In a refined metagame, raw power and bombs over the top matter — a no-rare Tempo deck can go 3-1 but you want real power when you can get it.
- **Read the table and stay open:** White drying up early signaled the seat wasn't open in that color. Be willing to pivot off your P1P1 color (here, off white/Boros toward Golgari) when signals push you.
- **Late signals are loud against good drafters:** Strong opponents don't pass premium cards late, so seeing a Roots/onlooker stream wheel (triple District Onlooker, double Basilica Stalker) is a strong sign that graveyard-value Golgari was open.
- **Smuggler's Copter (Satchel) is premium but color-commit aware:** The Copter pick was the drafter's one regret — it's extremely powerful and colorless, but he took it while still uncertain on colors and then drifted into a graveyard build that didn't capitalize on it. Take the powerful colorless card, but be honest about whether your final deck supports the body/crew.
- **Golgari (BG) graveyard value archetype:** Built around "leave the graveyard" payoffs — Chalk Outline (huge, snap it up around pick 7), Roots of Life — fed by self-mill creatures (District Onlooker, Aftermath Maverick, Strider) and Vitu-Ghazi Inspector / "it doesn't add up" effects.
- **Collect Evidence vs. self-mill distinction:** Don't assume high creature count is mandatory for graveyard payoffs. This deck's graveyard fuel was mostly self-mill from the creatures themselves, not collect evidence, so the usual "need lots of creatures to feed collect evidence" rule was relaxed.
- **Removal is real and abundant:** Murder is "a magic card" — straightforward premium removal. Black provided multiple removal spells (Murder x2, Slice). Don't overlook black's removal density when a color opens up.
- **Splash for finishers + fixing:** A light red splash was justified by World Soul's Rage and treacherous terrain as finishers, with terrain doubling as fixing (Basic Land cycling for two, also dumps eight cards/evidence into the yard). Fixing came from terrain, Strider, and Nervous Gardener.
- **Meddler vs. utility two-drops:** Meddler is solid (enables a Maverick to attack and start surveil/value), but cut when removal count and answers (Pick the Players, Slice, double Murder, X spells) are higher priority. Tunnel Tipster ramps but does little else — cuttable in a deck that doesn't need the ramp.
- **Pick the Players is maindeckable:** A board-friendly card that doubles as removal/answers; worth a main slot, and a fine sideboard-in against artifacts/enchantments.
- **Cards to skip:** treacherous terrain is on the restricted/banned-from-decks "list" online and rarely shows up in paper, but in this draft it's a fine pick as a finisher + fixing. Assemble the Players was rated a weak card. Felonious Rage / Sanguine Savior were passed early as too situational/hidden-power.
- **Gameplay lesson — blocking discipline vs. fliers:** Against an Izzet (UR) fliers + counters (Reasonable Doubt) + burn (Shock, Out Cold) deck, careless blocks cost games. Blocking with the bigger/more valuable Basilica Stalker instead of Vitu-Ghazi Inspector was a key misplay; preserve your better creatures and respect the opponent's likely burn when committing blocks.
- **Disguise/Cloak as curve filler:** Red disguise creatures (the 5/4, the 3/2 that makes a Thopter) are reasonable late filler; you can usually pick up cheap disguise bodies to fill a curve, so don't reach for them early.

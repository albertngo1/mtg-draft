# Prerelease Crash Course: Secrets of Strixhaven

- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sonKik39H9o
- **Channel:** Lords of Limited
- **Published:** 2026-04-13
- **Set:** Secrets of Strixhaven (Limited)

## Summary

Ben Waring and Ethan Saks deliver their pre-release crash course for Secrets of Strixhaven (SOS) Limited, recorded from Ben's bachelor party. After a quick refresher on the format's mechanics, each host brings five "format truths" before walking college-by-college through the colors, their top uncommons, notable commons, multicolor uncommons, and sleeper commons. Their headline read: gold cards are heavily pushed (10 per college — three commons and seven uncommons), so most decks will be roughly half gold cards, and reading signals to land in an open college will be richly rewarded.

The mechanics: prepared (an adventure-style mechanic where creatures carry an attached spell that lives in exile once the creature becomes prepared); repartée (Silverquill WB — triggers when you cast an instant/sorcery that targets a creature); increment (Quandrix GU — a +1/+1 counter when a cast spell's mana value exceeds the creature's power or toughness, an evolve variant); flashback (Lorehold RW); infusion (Witherbloom BG — bonuses if you gained life this turn); and opus (Prismari UR — instant/sorcery payoffs, with a bigger effect at mana value five or greater). Both hosts are very high on prepared, calling it clean, easy to understand, and full of built-in tension; they love flashback sitting at the intersection of opus (a cheap spell becomes a 5+ mana value spell on flashback) and Lorehold's leaves-the-graveyard payoffs.

The college power ranking (Ethan, "100% correct"): Witherbloom #1, Silverquill #2, Prismari #3, Lorehold #4, Quandrix #5. Black is called the best early color; Quandrix/green has weak commons but excellent uncommons, so it shines mainly when green is open. Removal that gets creatures off the battlefield (kill/exile, not bounce) is premium because prepared creatures re-accrue value, and Essence Scatter is hyped given high creature counts. Ethan also pushes the "secret sixth college" of Rakdos (Ratosse) — a base-black burn/control/value deck splashing the best white or red gold cards, leaning on Rubble Rouser, Leech Collector, gravediggers, and an iconic-spells-on-creatures engine.

They close on splashing (Ben pro, Ethan cautious — but both agree the best decks are streamlined two-color decks while you keep flexibility for contested seats), Converge payoffs like Snarl Song, and a final framing of the format as Jeskai-space versus Abzan-space with a lot of bleed and a "loose Jeskai guy" running around.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **College power ranking (Ethan's):** Witherbloom (BG) > Silverquill (WB) > Prismari (UR) > Lorehold (RW) > Quandrix (GU). Black is the best early color.
- **Gold cards are king.** 10 gold cards per college (3 commons, 7 uncommons). Decks will be roughly half gold cards; gold cards are pushed well ahead of most mono-color cards. Take powerful gold cards aggressively P1P1 and let a lane present itself.
- **Read signals and change lanes.** With 5 colleges and 8 seats, two players can have a college to themselves. Reward for staying flexible between two colleges early and feeling out what's open.
- **Best decks are streamlined two-color decks** — despite fixing and Converge, don't overextend into 3+ colors as plan A. Know how to maneuver if your seat is contested.
- **Prepared defines the format.** Adventure-style spells on creatures stock your deck and you rarely run out of gas; standalone one-mana combat tricks get worse as a result.
- **Removal that removes is premium.** Kill/exile spells beat bounce and enchantment removal because prepared creatures re-accrue value (e.g., cards that re-prepare like Abigail). Exile also matters because black and Lorehold have recursion.
- **Essence Scatter is excellent** — high creature counts (boosted by prepared) make it cleanly answer prepared creatures and ETBs before they land.
- **Witherbloom (BG):** primary black, secondary green. Life-gain/pest grind, strong two-for-ones, great late game. Slight worry it's an A+B synergy deck that can be picked apart by interaction. Key enablers: Bog Water Lumimerette (common engine), Teacher's Pest, Pest Mascot, Pest Brood Sloth. Strong black uncommons: End of the Hunt, Eternal Student, Foolish Fate, Poisoner's Apprentice. Notable black commons: Cheerful Osteomancer (common Gravedigger via prepared Raise Dead), Wander Off (exile removal), Last Gasp, Send in the Pest.
- **Silverquill (WB):** primary white, secondary black; the most aggressive deck. White is split between Silverquill and Lorehold with little overlap, so white cards usually slot into one or the other. Repartée wants instant/sorceries that target creatures. Top white uncommons: Quillblade Laureate, Spirit Call Enthusiast, Group Project, Summoned Dromedary, Primary Research (build-around). Best common candidate: Elite Interceptor (a near-Thraben Inspector that curves into itself); Eager Glyph Mage also elite. Ajani's Response is a strong cost-reduced removal spell.
- **Lorehold (RW):** primary red, secondary white. Flashback glues opus + leaves-the-graveyard payoffs and fixes original Strixhaven's clunkiness. Red has lots of synergy and cheap removal. Top red uncommons: Impractical Joke (cheap removal), Garrison Excavators, Charging Strife Knight, Pigment Wrangler. Notable red commons: Tome Blast (flashback burn — Ben loves, Ethan skeptical), Bombard-plus (Unsettle Mockery, deal 4 + surveil 1), Zealous Lorecaster (5-mana 4/4 that rebuys an instant/sorcery), Rubble Rouser, Goblin (Treasure-making prepared). Wilt in the Heat (cheap when a card left your graveyard) is a notable RW common.
- **Prismari (UR):** primary blue, secondary red. Slow to start, big payoffs once casting 5+ mana value spells. Blue has the strongest prepared creatures and a "holy quadrinity" of instants: a 2-mana counter, a 3-mana draw, a top/bottom spell, and a 5-mana make-a-3/3-surveil-2. Top blue uncommons: Matter-Bending Mage, Campus Composer, Orisa Tide Choreographer. Notable blue commons: Essence Scatter, Run Behind, Hydro Channeler (fixing for splashes), Spellbook Seeker. Visionary's Dance is a notable UR common Lorehold may also want.
- **Quandrix (GU):** primary green, secondary blue. Weakest college overall, but green's uncommons are excellent — great when green is open. Increment wants you to curve out and single-spell, which is disruptible by interaction or missed land drops. Top green uncommons: Environmental Scientist (premium fixing/curve), Thornfist Striker, Pest Brood Sloth, Snarl Song (Converge payoff — a real build-around). Notable green commons: Studious First-Year (prepared Rampant Growth), Follow the Lumins (wants the cards to go to graveyard but they don't). Cuboid Colony is a strong turn-2 play but a poor topdeck.
- **Multicolor uncommons to prioritize:** Abigail, Poet Laureate (WB, re-prepares); Stress and Vibrant Outburst (UR removal/two-for-ones, splash targets); Practiced Scroll-Smith (RW, friends with cheap red removal); Startled Relic Sloth (RW 4/4 trample lifelink that exiles from graveyards every turn); Paradox Surveyor (GU); Teacher's Pest and Luen Exchange Students (BG); Scolding Administrator (WB 2-drop). Honorable mention: Mind Roots (BG) — discard two, possibly put a land into play.
- **"Secret sixth college" — Rakdos (Ratosse):** base black burn/control/value splashing the best white or red gold cards. Enablers: Rubble Rouser, Leech Collector, the BG/black drain creatures, Send in the Pest (sorcery to trigger opus), Melancholic Poet, common gravediggers (black + red), Zealous Lorecaster loops.
- **Converge** is mostly bad on the punisher-style cards, but Snarl Song (GU uncommon) is a house and worth building toward; a base GU deck can splash for it with surveil lands.
- **Flying is good.** Inkling-token makers (Eager Glyph Mage, Eternal Student) and evasive threats are strong.
- **Format shape:** Jeskai-space vs. Abzan-space, with heavy bleed (especially in the Jeskai/UR-RW overlap). Expect to play two-color-plus-a-splash often.

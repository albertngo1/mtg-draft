## Summary

Ben Wernicke and Ethan walk through the two featured drafts from the Secrets of Strixhaven Pro Tour: Christopher Larson's day-one draft and Nathan Steuer's day-two (8-0) draft. Both featured drafters made the top eight, which the hosts note is rare for the day-one and day-two featured players to both achieve. Before the drafts, the pair talk format feel: they reaffirm their week-one read that the format splits into "late-game/soup decks vs. aggro decks," with best-of-three (and Witherbloom in particular) playing better than best-of-one. Ethan champions Cost of Brilliance as his card of the week for black-based aggro (especially Silverquill), comparing it to a common-rarity Killian's Confidence that keeps the gas flowing.

Christopher Larson opens Snarlsong P1P1 and commits to a base-Witherbloom converge/"soup" deck, prioritizing cheap removal that stays in color (Grapple with Death, Last Gasp) and fixing (Shared Roots, dual lands, Potioner's Trove). The hosts debate his picks, notably preferring Stargaze over Noxious Newt at P1P6 (card advantage and avoiding feeding control-mirror removal), and Mind Rot over Archaic's Agony in pack three. Christopher's resulting deck is an almost all-rare converge list that the hosts call a touch midrange and light on card advantage, but he 3-0s by facing the aggro decks (Lorehold twice, then Silverquill in the finals against streamer DeFour).

Nathan Steuer, sitting at 8-0, navigates a tricky base-Witherbloom draft. He takes Essence Net Scholar P1P1 (the hosts leaned Vibrant Outburst), then Ambitious Augmenter, and famously passes Force of Will and Wisdom of Ages for on-plan picks like Snooping Page. The pest-mascot count is a recurring stress signal: multiple Pest Mascots appear early and none wheel, confirming a second Witherbloom drafter at the table. Pack two rewards him with Black Loafing Pest and life-gain payoffs (Bogwater Luminary), but pack three is a dud. The hosts disagree with a few picks (Eternal Student over Shopkeeper's Bane, Noxious Newt over Forum Necroscribe), and flag a mysterious Decorum Dissertation in his final list. Nathan goes 2-1 on day two but still makes top eight as roughly the sixth seed.

The episode closes on format meta-game talk: the hosts don't expect big shifts in best-of-one because the pods are weak — you either get a very good aggro deck or a very good late-game deck. Ben reports finally building a functional base-Lorehold/Jeskai control deck splashing blue rares, and reiterates that single-pip Snarlsong is splashable in a way a hypothetical "4GG" version would not have been.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Format axis:** The format is essentially "late-game / soup (converge) decks vs. aggro decks." Best-of-three plays deeper and is friendlier to Witherbloom; best-of-one pods are weak, so you'll usually open into either a very good aggro deck or a very good late-game deck — read which one and commit.
- **Snarlsong is a premium uncommon and a P1P1-worthy build-around.** Because it's single-pipped green, you can splash it even in a base-Lorehold/Jeskai shell, not just heavy-green converge. Once you take it, lean into five-color converge and don't fear getting "cut."
- **Converge ("soup") deck checklist:** finishers, cheap early removal to survive, catch-all removal, and card draw. Prioritize cheap in-color removal (Grapple with Death, Last Gasp) and fixing (Shared Roots, dual lands, Potioner's Trove, Environmental Scientist, studious-first-year effects).
- **Potioner's Trove** is a key soup engine (3-mana any-color rock that also gains 2 life after you cast an instant/sorcery) and a high pickup. Watch whether it starts rising up pick orders as soup gets more contested.
- **Stargaze over a cheap value creature in converge:** card advantage is scarce in soup decks, and playing a Noxious Newt-style two-drop just feeds control-mirror removal. Caveat: Stargaze is swingy — great at high life, unplayable when low. Rapturous Moment is a weaker card-draw alternative the hosts aren't high on.
- **Don't play creatures into the control mirror just to die.** A turn-two Noxious Newt is exactly what the opposing control deck wants to kill; Lorehold opponents also run Tone Blast. Tone Blast is a near format-defining tempo card to play around.
- **Cost of Brilliance** is excellent in black-based aggro (especially Silverquill) — a common "Killian's Confidence at home" that both presses the board and keeps cards flowing so aggro doesn't run out of gas.
- **Pest Mascot count = a Witherbloom signal.** Seeing three-plus Pest Mascots early and none wheeling means another Witherbloom drafter is at the table; expect to fight over Bogwater Luminary and pest payoffs. Take Bogwater Luminary aggressively when you're committed to life-gain (e.g., after picking up Black Loafing Pest).
- **Life-gain payoffs matter in Witherbloom:** Black Loafing Pest, Bogwater Luminary, Leech Collector (bloodletting off pest tokens), Shopkeeper's Bane (a pest that gains life), Luminary's Favor (powerful trick, doubled if you gained life). You don't strictly need the A+B package, but payoffs reward building around it.
- **Eternal Student (Fallen from Grace) is a trap** — a "good" Magic card that just doesn't matter in this format. The hosts repeatedly take format-relevant cards over it (e.g., Shopkeeper's Bane).
- **Top-end removal over filler:** Forum Necroscribe is a strong Witherbloom top-end; the hosts would take it over Noxious Newt despite the Newt's combo with Knockout Maneuver and barrage synergies. Knockout Maneuver is a premium green removal/fight spell — take it over the harder-to-set-up Withering Curse, especially in open-decklist (Pro Tour) play.
- **Open decklists weaken setup/wrath cards** like Withering Curse and make drafts generally tougher.
- **The Paradigm spells (Decorum Dissertation etc.) are optional to cast every turn** — you do NOT have to keep casting them. The red paradigm is insanely good: get ahead, then stop casting once your library gets low so you don't deck yourself. The black one (Decorum Dissertation — draw 2 / lose 2 each turn) is weaker; against a life-gain opponent it can backfire.
- **Pro Tour pod context:** Pods are scrappier/weaker than ladder, which favors midrange converge decks (less likely to face a second dedicated soup deck) and makes zero-sum removal picks more valuable than on ladder.

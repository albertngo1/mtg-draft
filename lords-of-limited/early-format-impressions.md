# Secrets of Strixhaven: Early Format Impressions

- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7U6MhblA7LA
- **Channel:** Lords of Limited
- **Set:** Secrets of Strixhaven (Limited)

## Summary

Ben and Ethan debrief after early access / pre-release for Secrets of Strixhaven, an Innistrad-meets-Strixhaven set built around the five colleges (Silverquill WB, Prismari UR, Witherbloom BG, Lorehold RW, Quandrix GU) and mechanics like magecraft/Opus, increment, repartee, prepared, and learn/lesson. Their biggest big-picture takeaway is that there is a sizable gap between the roughly 10 commons that actually matter (almost all interaction) and everything else. With ten gold cards per college at non-rare, you are heavily incentivized to either find the open college and load up on redundant synergy pieces, or just take the most powerful cards and interaction and play a high-power two-color (or splashy multicolor) deck. There is no sixth "off-college" archetype worth chasing.

Both hosts caution against committing to a college for its common gold payoffs, because you can't tell which colleges have been drafted ahead of you. Chasing a redundant synergy plan that doesn't come together (e.g., Witherbloom life gain without enough enablers/payoffs like Bogwater Gloomrot/Bogwater Lurker) leaves your deck flat and often unsalvageable. They emphasize that the draft itself is unusually high-variance for deck quality: a bad draft frequently cannot be cobbled back into a functional deck. Late premium gold cards should be treated as signals to jump into an open college.

On gameplay, games are both snowbally (every college has a snowball mechanic) and grindy (incidental life gain forces grinds), which pushes value away from white aggro and toward colors that play a strong late game. Ethan soured on white after early access (best white cards all want to attack, and aggro struggled to close before Prismari's late game or Witherbloom's life gain took over), while Ben had more success with white Lorehold/Silverquill aggro. Their recurring mantra: prepared creatures (and synergy cards generally) must be good on their own up front — you want cards that "ask nothing of you and do a lot." Cards needing several things to line up (fractal payoffs, Lorehold leaves-the-graveyard tricks) generally don't clear the format's power bar.

Tentative college power rankings: Prismari 1, Witherbloom 2, Quandrix 3, Silverquill 4, Lorehold 5. Green is a big mover up (big bodies bully the board), and Quandrix ranks high on raw green/blue card density rather than its gold cards. Lorehold is weakest because you're largely supposed to ignore its graveyard theme and just curve out — but it lacks the raw closing power to do so.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Two viable lanes:** (1) Find the open college and load up on redundant synergy pieces, or (2) draft the most powerful cards + interaction in a high-power two-color (or splashy) shell. Avoid loose, non-redundant synergy decks — they fall flat.
- **~10 commons matter, almost all interaction; quality falls off a cliff after them.** Don't try to find your lane via commons.
- **Treat premium late gold cards as signals.** Picks 5-8 (or a high density of gold) can justify jumping into an open college, especially without a high-power start.
- **Don't pick a college for its common gold payoffs** (Bogwater Lurker, Send into the Past) — you can't see what's been drafted ahead of you. Send into the Past, for example, only goes into Witherbloom *because Witherbloom is open*; Silverquill has zero interest in it.
- **Prepared creatures must be good as a bare creature up front.** They reward being ahead/at parity and are awful when behind. Landscape Painter is the cautionary example (often just a 2/1 Goblin Piker).
- **Best common in format (per Ben): Tone Blast** (R sorcery, 2 dmg any target, flashback 4R). There's a line between creatures that die to it and those that don't. Unsubtle Mockery (2R instant, 4 dmg to creature + surveil 1) and Last Gasp (B instant, -3/-3) are also premium.
- **Commons that matter list (~9 of 10 are interaction):** Elite Interceptor (W 1/2 prepared, tap/untap + draw), Run Behind (U bounce), Last Gasp (B -3/-3), Wander off (3B exile creature), Tone Blast (R), Unsubtle Mockery (2R), Bramble Barrage (G fight/pump), Grave with Death (BG destroy artifact/creature + gain life), Wilt on the Heat (RW, deals 5, exiles — broken when cast for 2). White's contribution is creatures, not interaction.
- **White is creatures-focused and pointed at attacking.** Down on white after early access; aggro struggled to close before Prismari/Witherbloom take over. Ajani's Response moves down (filler interaction unless a 4c control deck emerges).
- **Lorehold: ignore the leaves-the-graveyard theme.** Play good flashback spells and just curve out and beat down; the engine pieces (Rubble Rouser in Lorehold, molten note flashback at 6RW) aren't worth it. Rubble Rouser is a Prismari card if anything.
- **Prismari (UR) is the #1 college.** Best lines play cheap interaction on turns 1-2, relevant 3-4 drops, backed by spells. Spectacular Skywhale (2UR 1/4 flyer, grows huge with Opus) must be killed on sight. Expressive Fire Dancer, Deluge Virtuoso, Tackle Artist, Pigment Wrangler reward spell density.
- **Witherbloom (BG) life gain is incidental, not A+B.** Your kill spells just "oops, gain a life," so it's lower-effort synergy than feared. Needs redundant enablers/payoffs (Bogwater Lurker, Bogwater Gloomrot, Tenured Concocter) to function.
- **Quandrix (GU) ranks high on green/blue card density, not its gold cards.** Increment "just happens" and can't fall flat. Avoid the fractal build-around payoffs (fractal tender 3/3 is atrocious). Cube Colony, Encouraging Aviator, Terrafractal are good.
- **Green is a big mover up.** Big bodies (Cactarantula-style 4/5 vigilance ward, Tenured Concocter 4/5) bully the board and grind well. The G 1/2 deathtouch mana dork is excellent; Studious First-Year, by contrast, is too weak.
- **Repartee is best when triggered by removal spells, not by targeting your own stuff.** Quillblade (W 1/1 double strike prepared, gives a creature +1/+0 + double strike) is a top-3 white uncommon; Inkling Mascot wheels give Silverquill its redundancy.
- **Mind Rot (1BG, discard 2 + ramp a land) starts at B-, a pull into Witherbloom.** A good two-drop into Mind Rot into chunky threats is hard for many decks to beat.
- **Eternal Student (3B 4/2, exile from yard for two 1/1 fliers at INSTANT speed) is broken** — note it's not tapped and not sorcery-speed restricted.
- **Cards that "ask nothing and do a lot" are the target:** Infirmary Healer (G 2/3 prepared Stream of Life), Encouraging Aviator (2U flyer, bankable jump = repeatable Opus trigger in Prismari), Dissection practice (B instant, drain + combat trick, C+), Killian's Confidence (Silverquill, recurs itself — sneaky on Arena). Enns Debate Moderator is the anti-example (too slow, too many hoops).
- **Watch for late-game grind enablers in a possible "oops all removal" control deck:** Diary of Dreams + Potioner's Trove (mana/life rock; combos with infusion via holding priority).
- **The draft is unusually high-variance for deck quality.** A misdrafted synergy deck often cannot be salvaged — you need all the tools in your tool belt; you can't easily force an open lane.

# Am I The Only BW Drafter at the Table??? | Secrets of Strixhaven | Magic: The Gathering

- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yN458RU9CGw
- **Channel:** Lords of Limited
- **Set:** Secrets of Strixhaven (Limited)

## Summary

This is an early-access first draft of Secrets of Strixhaven, played with a WotC preview account, so the host is openly unfamiliar with many of the rares and mechanics. He opens by waffling between a few P1P1 options (Niece of the Forum, Quillblade Laureate, and a Witherbloom-flavored exile-an-instant-or-sorcery rare) before settling on commons. From there the draft quickly funnels into Silverquill (WB, black/white), and the running joke of the episode is that essentially nobody else at the table is in white — he repeatedly notes he is "the only white drafter," which means high-quality white cards keep wheeling and flowing to him.

The deck comes together as a removal-heavy Silverquill build leaning on Repartee (the "whenever you target with an instant or sorcery" mechanic), Quillblade Laureate (a recurring all-star pickup he gets multiple copies of), Abigail, and a splashy planeswalker (Ral) that he picks up mid-draft and which ends up being the MVP. He stacks a deep removal suite — multiple Ajani's Response, Wander Off, Repel Calamity, Last Gasp, Feed the Swarm, Dissection Practice — to the point where his bigger deckbuilding worry is having enough Repartee triggers and a functional creature curve rather than enough interaction.

In the matches he plays three Silverquill mirrors and a Quandrix (GU) opponent, finishing 3-3. The wins are largely carried by Ral, the planeswalker (he explicitly notes every game he won had the planeswalker and the game he lost without it). Quillblade Laureate and Elite Interceptor over-perform, while Eager Glyph Mage and Repel Calamity underwhelm relative to expectations.

Overall a solid but unpolished first run: a clean read of an open color (white was wildly underdrafted at his table), a removal-dense Silverquill deck, and a planeswalker bomb doing a lot of the heavy lifting.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Read the open color hard.** White/Silverquill was almost completely unrepresented at his table — he saw white bombs and good commons wheeling all draft. When a color is this open, lean in and take the gifts; he was "the only BW drafter."
- **Silverquill (WB) wants Repartee enablers and payoffs together.** Repartee triggers on "whenever you target a creature/permanent with an instant or sorcery." You need both enough targeting spells AND enough Repartee creatures to make the synergy live — he had to actively count his triggers (~9) during deckbuilding.
- **Quillblade Laureate is a strong, repeatable pickup.** A 2-mana 1/1 double striker that comes down "prepared" to hand out +1/+1 and double strike. Excellent target for your own targeted spells and a great Repartee enabler. He happily took multiples; rated "excellent" in the recap.
- **Elite Interceptor over-performed** — rated "excellent" in the wrap-up. A cheap white creature worth prioritizing.
- **Eager Glyph Mage is closer to filler than its reputation suggests** — he was repeatedly offered it, passed, and concluded post-match it's overrated.
- **Repel Calamity (destroy a creature with power or toughness 4+) underwhelmed** — conditional removal that's fine but not a high pick.
- **Don't over-prioritize big top-end.** He noted Lorehold (RW) was stacked upstream and several six-drop "Colossus" cards were floating; he intentionally avoided clunky sixes to keep a functional curve.
- **A planeswalker (Ral) was the deck's MVP** — he won every game he drew it and lost the one he didn't. Prioritize planeswalker bombs highly even out of your colors if reasonable.
- **Watch your curve, not just card quality.** With tons of good four-drops flowing, he repeatedly took two-drops to avoid a top-heavy, non-functional curve.
- **Removal can become a surplus.** His interaction suite (multiple Ajani's Response, Wander Off, Last Gasp, Feed the Swarm, Repel Calamity, Dissection Practice) got so deep that creatures and Repartee enablers became the scarce resource. Dissection Practice is a cheap way to trigger Repartee.
- **Know what does/doesn't trigger Repartee.** "And the Hunt"-style removal that doesn't target is a knock against it in a Repartee deck. Spells that target (e.g., Render Speechless, Dissection Practice) play better.
- **The five Strixhaven colleges:** Silverquill (WB), Prismari (UR), Witherbloom (BG), Lorehold (RW), Quandrix (GU).

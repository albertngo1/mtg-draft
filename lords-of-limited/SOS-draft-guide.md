# Secrets of Strixhaven (SOS) — Draft Guide

> Agent-primary reference, synthesized from 10 Lords of Limited episodes (per-episode notes + transcripts in `sources/`). Treat as expert/theory opinion — weight it like a CGB grade; **17Lands GIH WR stays primary**. Synthesized from videos current as of 2026-06-06.
>
> IMPORTANT TIMELINE NOTE: prerelease/early-access episodes made predictions that the retrospective episodes **reversed**. Where they conflict, trust the *late/retrospective* read (50-takes wrap-up, mid-format episodes). Biggest reversal: Witherbloom was predicted #1 college, finished **dead last**.

## TL;DR meta read

- **The format is "soup vs. aggro."** Two macro-strategies dominate; midrange is mostly dead. The five college color-pair "themes" rarely define your deck — they just happen or don't. Don't draft toward a mechanic.
- **Soup (greedy multicolor, Converge-fueled, Potioner's-Trove-enabled value/control) is the single best thing to do.** The strongest control/ramp soup beats Lorehold/Silverquill aggro because cheap interaction crushes their early game. 17Lands favors **white** only because it can't represent multicolor decks well.
- **White and blue are the two best colors** (identical to original Strixhaven): white = best aggro base, blue = best late-game/control base.
- **Fastest path to wins:** stay open picks 1-8, take raw power + interaction (not commons), then either (a) commit to soup once you have ≥1 over-the-top payoff, or (b) if power-starved, build a focused, proactive 2-color board deck (Silverquill/Lorehold aggro, Quandrix tempo) to harass the soup decks and settle for 3-3/4-3.
- **The worst place to be is "bad soup"** — an unfocused control pile with finishers but no early interaction. It loses to everything. Never force soup without payoffs.
- Games go **long and grindy** (incidental life gain everywhere). Rares/mythics define games; you have time to find them. You can win by decking. Don't fall out of your role's tempo position — aggro behind = dead by turn 4-5.

## SOS format principles

- **Commons barely matter — don't pick them picks 1-8.** Huge cliff between rares/uncommons that matter and filler commons; more commons in your deck = more losses. (Ben softens this: take fitting hits and power, but NEVER sacrifice power level early.) The open lane usually reveals itself picks 6-8 — don't lock a color early.
- **Take the best card out of every pack, picks 1-8.** Don't take power-level hits to get "deep" in a color. You're almost never trying to get deep in one color.
- **Stay open until ~P1P10.** Worst-case draft = you can't jump ship and you already clearly know your deck at end of pack one.
- **Never force soup. "Soup needs time to simmer."** Only commit once you have ≥1 over-the-top payoff (e.g. P1P1 Mathematics, early Wisdom of Ages, two Snarl Songs by P1P4). Forcing soup with no payoffs = atrocious decks, fastest path to 0-3.
- **Removal is premium, efficient, plentiful — but matchup-dependent.** Pack removal, but not too much; it's dead vs. creatureless soup. Bridge this with card advantage + filtering (Pursue the Past discards dead removal AND feeds graveyard synergy).
- **Card draw/filtering > raw card advantage** because hitting land drops matters enormously and there's always somewhere to sink mana. Never stumble. **17-land format** (one host went 18 in a sketchy soup base, but 17 is standard).
- **Take your mana base highly once you know your lane.** College decks: snap your college duals (gold 2-drops wheel, so grab the land now). Soup: Terramorphic Expanse + Potioner's Trove are the omnipresent 5c fixing; duals matter more for 2-color colleges. 2-color wants ~2-3 on-color tapped duals, multicolor ~4-6.
- **Great Hall of the Biblioplex > Terramorphic Expanse for Converge** — Terramorphic counts as 5 sources mentally but yields one color and eats your basics; the true 5c land keeps all colors live (and can become a 2/4 body).
- **Fixing-when-poor heuristic:** if mana is already good, take more power; if mana is bad, that's exactly when an off-color fixer (Environmental Scientist, Potioner's Trove) pays off most.
- **Be ahead or at parity; punish being behind.** Aggro must be ahead; soup needs parity; prepared cards want anything but behind. Falling out of role loses fast.
- **Don't play around specific answers.** Too many possible instants per color (Essence Scatter, Disdainful Stroke, Spell Pierce; Bitter Triumph, Last Gasp). Make the straightforward play — but sequence around counter mana (play 2-drop + 3-drop so a counter only stops half your turn) and don't jam bombs into open blue when avoidable.
- **Sandbag prepared-spell finishers** (Quillblade Laureate, Kiroll) to punish big tap-out turns (Wisdom of Ages, Divergent Equation).
- **College mechanics mostly "just happen"** (Repartee/Rep RT, Magnum Opus, Infusion, Flashback, Increment) — they don't ask you to build differently, which is why the format feels samey. The one exception is "leaves the graveyard" — worth slightly over-drafting payoffs (Rubble Rouser, Ascendant Dust Speaker) if you have ~5 cards that care.
- **Avoid 2-toughness creatures that can't grow**, especially at 3+ mana — they fold to Tome Blast and let slow decks do their thing. Prefer 3-toughness or creatures that grow out of burn range.
- **Six real archetypes:** the 5 colleges + Converge/Soup. Converge is an *ethos*, not a fixed shell. A true 5c mana base makes hitting Converge counts (1 of each color) surprisingly easy.
- **Core principle (mid-format):** play to stop what the opponent is trying to do, not just to press your own plan.

### Mystical archive / bonus-sheet over-performers
**Hop to It, Brain Freeze, Disdainful Stroke, Spell Pierce, Stock Up, Abrade** (Stock Up = exactly as busted as expected). Cheap protection vs. soup's expensive removal: **Royal Treatment** (G), **Masterful Flourish**, **Chase Inspiration** (U) — niche, only good when you go counter-culture and play to the board. Note: hosts wish such cheap protection existed in *white* (God's Willing / Feat of Resistance would break the format).

### Prepared mechanic
Clean, well-designed two-for-one (Ancestral Recall / Stream of Life / Reanimate / Rampant Growth on cards) — but overshadowed by the four-for-ones and six-for-ones. Prepared creatures must be **good as a bare creature up front**; they reward being ahead, awful when behind. Infirmary Healer (G 2/3 prepared Stream of Life) and Studious First Year are good examples; Landscape Painter (often just a 2/1 Piker) is the cautionary one.

## Signals & seat reads

- **5 colleges, 8 seats → two players can own a college.** Reward for staying flexible between two colleges early and feeling out what's open.
- **Open lanes reveal themselves picks 6-8.** Don't lock a color early; a high density of one college's commons at pick 7 is strong info even if you don't take the card.
- **Pest Mascot count = Witherbloom signal.** Seeing 3+ Pest Mascots early and none wheeling = another Witherbloom drafter is at the table; expect to fight over Bogwater Luminary + pest payoffs.
- **Premium late gold cards = signals to jump into an open college** (picks 5-8), especially without a high-power start.
- **White was wildly under-drafted at some early tables** (the "only BW drafter" episode) — white bombs + commons wheeling all draft. When a color is this open, lean in.
- **Gold two-drops (Inkling Mascot etc.) wheel reliably** — take the college land first, grab the body pick 14.
- **Read signals by card *type*, not just color** — density of aggressive creatures vs. removal vs. a college's gold vs. late-game enablers.
- **Pro Tour / weak pods favor midrange converge** (less likely to face a second dedicated soup deck) and make zero-sum removal picks more valuable than on ladder. Bo3 plays deeper and is friendlier to Witherbloom than Bo1.
- **Late Wisdom of Ages / Snarl Song wheeling** = soup is open / under-contested; expect the meta to tighten as players stop letting payoffs wheel.

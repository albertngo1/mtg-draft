## Summary

This is the Lords of Limited "crash course" preview episode for Murders at Karlov Manor (MKM), recorded before the set's release and before the Pro Tour in Chicago. Hosts Ben and Ethan walk through the big-picture shape of the format first: how removal lines up against creature toughness, which mechanics are supported, sweepers, fixing, life gain, and mana sinks. They note this is the first set with the new "play booster" structure, which means more total uncommons than commons and no guaranteed common of every color in a pack — changes they expect to matter for draft over time but plan to largely draft into the format as usual for now.

On the stats: removal is efficient and low on the curve, but skews heavily to sorcery speed (13 of 22 removal spells are sorceries), which makes the instant-speed options (Galvanize, Lightning Helix, Long Goodbye) more premium. Shock is strong because toughness-2 creatures are common (46% of creatures). Notably there is no removal that specifically punishes one-toughness creatures, which raises the stock of X/1 threats like the Boros hybrid common Dog Walker. Ward 2 on Disguise creatures pushes removal toward cheaper, instant-speed answers and toward effects that get around ward (fights, bites, edicts).

Mechanic-by-mechanic, the hosts read Detectives (UW, very deep, "impossible to fail") and Izzet (UR) artifacts as the two strongest, most linear archetypes — both expected to be aggressive/tempo and oppressive when they curve out. Disguise is spread evenly across colors, with the gold/hybrid common Disguise creatures (flipped face up as gold cards) being the standout. Suspect (RB) and Collect Evidence (UG) are more contained; the hosts are cautiously higher on Collect Evidence after seeing the payoffs (Evidence Examiner, Chalk Outline, Insidious Roots), though enablers may be the bottleneck. Cases are all uncommon and mostly archetype-specific ("more meme than dream"), though Case of the Shattered Pack and Case File Auditor stand out. Investigate/Clues are everywhere, so decks rarely run out of gas — which may make pure control harder. Green does the fixing (no red/blue treasures).

The back half is the grading-difference segment and top commons/uncommons rankings. The running gag is Ethan competing in the Pro Tour, so he jokingly "lies" about the format, and Ben repeatedly accuses him of "cognitive dissonance" — most heatedly over Night Drinker Moroii (Ben B, Ethan D). Other big disagreements: Museum Night Watch, Glint Weaver, Detective's Satchel, and whether Murder is still good in 2024. Both land on similar top-3 lists for several colors, agree the hybrid gold commons (Dog Walker, Gadget Technician, Fairy Snoop, Sanguine Savior) are among the best cards in the set, and close by hyping the format and the play-booster rarity shift (deep uncommons could boost colors like black).

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Best archetypes (early read):** UW Detectives is the standout ("impossible for this deck to fail") — aggressive tempo, very deep at common. UR Izzet artifacts is the other top, linear deck (Case of the Filched Falcon into Gleaming Geardrake is an oppressive curve-out). Both are linear and punishing when they curve.
- **Removal is efficient but sorcery-heavy:** 13 of 22 removal spells are sorceries, so instant-speed removal (Galvanize, Lightning Helix, Long Goodbye) is at a premium. Galvanize (deal 3, or 5 if you've drawn 2+ cards) is a top-tier red common — crack a Clue first to deal 5.
- **Shock is strong:** ~46% of all creatures have toughness 2. No removal specifically punishes 1-toughness creatures, which raises X/1 threats like Dog Walker.
- **Ward 2 on Disguise creatures** pushes the format toward cheap, instant-speed removal and effects that bypass ward — fights/bites, edicts (Extract a Confession), and -X/-X (Slick Sequence / "slice from the shadows").
- **Disguise** is spread evenly across colors; RG, GW, WB are the "official" Disguise color pairs but don't get the best Disguise commons. The hybrid gold commons (which can be cast face up as gold cards) are the best Disguise cards — other decks benefit from them too. Disguising on turn 3 trades evenly on mana but isn't strongly punished; hosts are skeptical of leaning on Disguise as a primary plan against the linear UW/UR decks.
- **Disguise payoffs:** Selesnya Sentry (free +1/+1 counter on it and the flipped creature when any face-down permanent flips up — a team anthem) and Greenbelt Radical (4/4 that, when flipped, puts a +1/+1 counter on each of your creatures and gives trample).
- **Suspect (RB):** grants menace + can't block. Concentrated in RB so it won't flood the format. Rewards knowing who's the beatdown. Rune-Brand Juggler (2/2 menace, ETB suspect one of your creatures, then sac a suspected creature for -5/-5) is a strong, versatile 2-drop pull into RB. Toxin Analysis (deathtouch + lifelink + investigate for B) pairs well with suspected menace creatures that get double-blocked.
- **Collect Evidence (UG):** better than it first looks thanks to payoffs (Evidence Examiner, Surveillance Monitor, Chalk Outline, Insidious Roots), but enablers/getting creatures into the yard may be the bottleneck. Split cards juice Collect Evidence (cast the cheap half, the other half's high mana value counts as evidence). Bite Down on Crime (G fight with collect-evidence cost reduction) re-evaluated upward.
- **Cases** are all uncommon and mostly archetype-specific ("more meme than dream"). Exceptions: Case of the Shattered Pack (easy to solve thanks to hybrid commons; fixes and then grants flying/double strike/vigilance), Case File Auditor (digs for enchantments, fixes for Case spells), and Case of the Gateway Express (cheap white aggro payoff, good in most white decks).
- **Investigate / Clues** are everywhere, in every color — decks rarely run out of gas, which may make pure control harder since aggro also has card advantage. Clues are great mana sinks. Payoffs: Izzet Interrogator (poison finisher off sac'ing Clues), Case of the Filched Falcon → turn a Clue into a 4/4 flyer.
- **Detectives (34 cards, mostly UW, some BR/G):** plays like UW Knights from MOM. Private Eye and Crime Stopper Sprite are pushed; Thinking Cap is an annoying 1-mana equip on Detectives. Out Cold (tap two, stun) is a curve-topping nightmare for UW.
- **Premium colorless:** Smuggler's Copter-tier colorless picks aside, the set's best flexible commons are the hybrid gold commons — top tier: Dog Walker (RW), Gadget Technician (UR), then Fairy Snoop (UB), Sanguine Savior (WB), Orzhov.
- **Sweepers:** No Witnesses (WW wrath), Deadly Cover-Up (BB 5-mana wrath), Incinerator of the Guilty (mythic), Ill-Timed Explosion (UR rare). No cheap mass -X/-X effects at lower rarity.
- **Fixing:** Green does the heavy lifting (Nervous Gardener is a top green common; no red/blue treasures). Colorless options (Case of the Shattered Pack) plus lands (Escape Tunnel, Public Thoroughfare, Branch of Vitu-Ghazi). Format likely supports 18 lands given the abundance of mana sinks (Clues, flipping Disguise creatures).
- **Mana sinks are plentiful** (Clues, flipping Disguise creatures for ward-2 cost) — Ben argues it's an 18-land format; Ethan notes it depends on the deck.
- **P1P1 / pick priority:** prioritize instant-speed removal (Galvanize, Lightning Helix, Long Goodbye), the best hybrid gold commons, and the deep UW Detective commons (Novice Inspector, Cold Case Cracker, Out Cold). Black has banger-after-banger uncommons (Leering Onlooker, Fester-Leech, Undercity Eliminator), which may boost black despite weak black creature commons.
- **Play booster structure note:** more uncommons than commons now, and there's no longer a guaranteed common of every color in a pack (only 4 of 5 colors guaranteed across commons) — so color signals from commons read differently than in old draft boosters.
- **Hosts' debated picks:** Murder still graded highly by Ben (best in UB control) but Ethan only C+ ("Boomer showing"). Night Drinker Moroii — Ben B (curve Disguise on 3, flip on 4 for a 4/2 flyer + a 2-drop), Ethan D (dies to a sneeze). Glint Weaver, Detective's Satchel, and Museum Night Watch were other major gradation gaps.

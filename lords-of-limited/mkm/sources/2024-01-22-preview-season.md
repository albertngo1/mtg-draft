## Summary

This is the Lords of Limited preview/prerelease episode for Murders at Karlov Manor (MKM), Ravnica's "whodunnit" set. Because it is recorded during spoiler season before the full set, every card evaluation here is a *prediction* — the hosts (Ben Wary and Ethan) are reacting to the commons and uncommons revealed so far and guessing how they will play, not reporting tested results. They note that MKM has dumped an unusually large number of commons early, so this episode is heavy on commons rather than the usual uncommon-dominated preview.

The bulk of the episode walks through the new and returning mechanics: Disguise (morph with ward 2 — cast face down as a 3-mana 2/2, flip up later for its disguise cost), Cloak (manifest with ward 2), Suspect (gives a creature menace and stops it from blocking until it leaves or is un-suspected), Collect Evidence (exile cards from your graveyard with total mana value at least the listed number as a cost), Cases (enchantments with a top ability, a "solve" condition, and a payoff once solved), plus returning Investigate/Clues, Surveil, and split cards. The Detective creature type ties together a tribal blue-white archetype.

The hosts then go guild by guild through the ten two-color archetypes, calling out likely premium commons and weak cards, before a "Great or Garbage" lightning round of contested evaluations. Recurring themes: hybrid-mana disguise costs make several commons very flexible high picks (and may even enable near-mono-color decks), red is leaning hard into artifact/Clue sacrifice synergies, and Collect Evidence (the Simic GU mechanic) is viewed as the clunkiest, late-game-only mechanic.

These are first-impression predictions; the hosts repeatedly flag that real evaluations will come after they actually draft the format.

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Mechanics primer (predicted importance):**
  - **Disguise** = morph with ward 2. Cast face down for 3 generic as a 2/2, flip up anytime (not using the stack) for its disguise cost. Ward 2 means removal trades down on mana. Remember to reveal face-down cards at end of game in paper or risk a game loss.
  - **Cloak** = manifest with ward 2; can put non-creature cards face down too (flip a non-disguise card up by paying its mana cost).
  - **Suspect** = target creature gains menace and can't block until it leaves the battlefield or is un-suspected. Dual-purpose: push your own attackers OR shut off an opposing blocker.
  - **Collect Evidence N** = exile cards from your graveyard with total mana value at least N. Generally a late-game cost; needs graveyard fuel (self-mill, cycling, trading off disguise creatures).
  - **Cases** = enchantments with an always-on top ability, a middle "solve" condition checked at your end step, and a payoff that activates once solved.
- **Hybrid disguise costs are the format's flexibility engine.** Cards like Gadget Technician (UR), Dog Walker (RW), Fairy Snoop (UB), and Granite Witness (WU) can be taken by either color of their pair (or splashed), making them higher picks than their raw stats suggest. May push mana bases toward near-mono-color builds.
- **Archetypes (guild pairs):**
  - **Azorius (WU) Detectives:** fast midrange, detective tribal payoffs, sac Clues for late refuel. Private Eye (anthem + unblockable), No More Lies (a Mana Leak — better in Constructed but fine in Limited).
  - **Dimir (UB) Clues control:** stall, generate Clues, grind value. Fairy Snoop predicted a top common.
  - **Rakdos (BR) Suspect aggro:** suspected attackers can't be blocked back. Deadly Complication and Clandestine Meddler highlighted.
  - **Gruul (RG) big Disguise:** ramp into face-down board, flip up haymakers.
  - **Golgari (BG) graveyard / Collect Evidence value.**
  - **Boros (RW) Battalion:** reward attacking with 3+ creatures. Lightning Helix reprint, Meddling Youths, Dog Walker.
  - **Selesnya (GW) go-wide Disguise:** flood face-down creatures, payoffs on flip (Sworn Sentry).
  - **Orzhov (WB) pint-size Disguise:** reward power 2 or less.
  - **Izzet (UR) artifact sacrifice:** Clue/thopter sac engine, evasive thopters. Gleaming Geardrake, Gadget Technician strong.
  - **Simic (GU) Collect Evidence:** seen as the weakest pair / clunkiest mechanic.
- **Premium colorless pick:** Smuggler's Copter (the format's top-end colorless card).
- **Predicted strong commons:** Fairy Snoop (UB), Dog Walker (RW), Gadget Technician (UR), Novice Inspector (white, a Thraben Inspector reprint — never cut it), Deduce (think-twice variant), Out Cold (3-mana tap two + investigate, called "great"), Galvanize, Slime Against Humanity (build-around but you need ooze/named cards in exile to scale it — easy to misread), Scene of the Crime (artifact Clue land), Red Herring (2/2 haste Clue with good types).
- **Predicted weak / niche cards:** Curious Cadaver (4-mana 3/1 too slow), Riveteers/Raffish Scoundrel (clunky), Undercover Crocodile (expensive), Bite Down on Crime (4-mana sorcery fight, needs setup), Shady Informant, Goblin Maskmaker, Branch of Vitu-Ghazi (colorless land + disguise 3 not worth it).
- **Removal of note:** Long Goodbye (UB, kills MV3-or-less, great vs cheap disguise flips), Galvanize (UR, scales if you've drawn 2+), Shock reprint (cast for 1, can ambush face-down 2/2s), Extract a Confession (efficient edict), Not on My Watch (white exile attacker).
- **Mana fixing:** Public Thoroughfare and Scene of the Crime are real playables; Nervous Gardener (disguise, fetches basic) and Topiary Panther (cycles + loads evidence) praised as enablers for splashes and Collect Evidence.
- **P1P1 / pick-order advice:** prioritize flexible hybrid-disguise commons and clean Investigate cards (Novice Inspector); Collect Evidence cards (especially high numbers) suffer diminishing returns, so take only a couple unless you're built around it.
- All evaluations are predictions — the hosts note real tier lists come after the full set and actual drafts.

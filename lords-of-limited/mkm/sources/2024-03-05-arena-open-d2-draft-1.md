## Summary

This is a high-stakes Arena Open Day 2 draft in Murders at Karlov Manor. The format is four matches per draft: a 3-1 record or better advances to the second draft, and reaching the second draft at 4-0 gives you a loss to give plus a shot at gems and cash prizes. The drafter opens Perimeter Enforcer and builds toward a white-based aggressive Tempo deck, ultimately landing in a Detective-heavy Azorius (WU) "go wide" shell with a strong low curve and the Fuss // Bother go-wide payoff.

The draft is notable for a tight, disciplined approach: the pilot leans hard into a low curve full of Detective creatures (so cards like Thinking Cap, Cold Case Cracker, and the projector inspector / "investigate" engine all gain value), and treats Granite Witness, Exit Specialist, Seasoned Consultant, and Out Cold as the backbone. A late realization that the deck had "tons of creature augmentation and not a ton of interaction" prompts a key main-deck swap: cutting Thinking Cap for Make Your Move, which immediately pays off in the matches. The deck runs 17 lands on the strength of its low curve.

In the matches, the deck goes 3-1. Match one is a grindy, frustrating win against a green ramp/Tulir deck where the pilot repeatedly gets blown out by "No Witnesses" in scary spots but ekes out the win. He then beats an Izzet (UR) Tempo deck and a Dimir (UB) "mediums" deck to lock up second-draft qualification, before losing a very close match four to a Rakdos (BR) splash deck powered by double Detective's Satchel, Cranko, and repeated Thopter generation — a loss sealed by forgetting that the opponent's orangutan (Reckless... / Soul-Guide-style creature) had reach.

The overall takeaway is a clinic in refined, late-format MKM Azorius Detective Tempo: prioritize a low curve, lean on the Detective/investigate synergies, value cheap interaction highly, and respect the format's swingy combat tricks (especially No Witnesses).

## Key Draft Takeaways

- **Azorius (WU) Detective Tempo is a premier archetype**: a low curve of cheap Detectives plus flyers, backed by cheap interaction, can Tempo opponents out. Maximize the number of Detective creatures so investigate/Clue payoffs scale.
- **P1P1 / early picks**: Perimeter Enforcer is a strong white aggressive uncommon worth first-picking. Makeshift Binding and Torch the Witness are the premium common removal; Binding got bumped over Torch here because of an existing white card, and starting mono-white off Binding is a happy position.
- **Removal is the scarcest resource** — the pilot explicitly says going into pack 3 that removal is "really the thing I need." Cheap interaction (Out Cold, Makeshift Binding, Make Your Move) is at a premium.
- **Detective payoffs**: Projector Inspector (the "projector inspector" / investigate-on-attack Detective) is repeatedly called "wildly underrated" and is excellent with go-wide and with extra Detectives. Thinking Cap, Cold Case Cracker, and similar cards all reward a high Detective count.
- **Go-wide payoff**: Fuss // Bother is the go-wide payoff this Azorius deck wants — great alongside flyers (Neighborhood Guardian) and token-makers (Bubble Smuggler).
- **Good commons/uncommons in the WU shell**: Granite Witness, Exit Specialist, Seasoned Consultant, Out Cold, Novice Inspector, Analyst (great with double Projector Inspector), Crime Scene Investigator / Watchdog-type bodies, Private Eye (bounce + Tempo), Call a Surprise Witness (value recursion of cheap creatures).
- **Curve discipline enables 17 lands**: the deck ran 17 lands because the curve is low; the pilot contrasts this with running 18 at the Pro Tour for a WWUU six-drop (Ezerix-style bomb) which demanded more lands and color intensity.
- **Cut overcommitment to "augmentation"**: too many auras/equipment ("creature augmentation") with too little interaction is a trap. Trimming Thinking Cap for Make Your Move (cheap interaction) was the winning last-minute change.
- **Respect format tricks**: No Witnesses is a devastating combat blowout that repeatedly punished aggressive attacks; play around it in close spots. Reasonable Doubt (suspect/counter-style trick) is good Tempo and useful vs. card-advantage engines like Outrageous Robbery.
- **Cloak/Disguise/morph awareness**: face-down 2/2s create "pause" interactions — opponents bluff and you must respect flip-ups (ward 2 to flip). The pilot uses face-down bodies for flexible double-blocks and flip-up tappers.
- **Signal reading**: examiner being open early (Evidence Examiner) was read as "signal adjacent"; repeatedly seeing Granite Witness wheel confirmed the WU lane was open.
- **Watch out for color-intensive opponents**: a Dimir (UB) "mediums" deck and an Izzet (UR) Tempo deck are beatable; a Rakdos (BR) splash deck with double Detective's Satchel + Cranko + Thopter generation is a powerful, grindy threat that can out-value you.
- **Combat math discipline**: the costly match-4 loss came from forgetting an opposing creature (orangutan) had reach, allowing a flyer to be blocked — a reminder to re-check evasion/keywords before committing to lethal.

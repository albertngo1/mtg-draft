# ZNR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Zendikar Rising (ZNR) is a **party-and-kicker** format. Speed is **medium-to-fast** — Kenji noted it's "not that aggro" but red-white warriors with Maul of the Skyclavers can close games very fast, and aggro on the play often just wins via die roll.
- The dominant archetypes are **Blue-Black Rogues** (mill-synergy control), **Black-White Clerics** (lifegain + party), **Red-White Warriors** (aggro), and **Blue-Green Kicker** (tempo-value). Party (Cleric/Rogue/Warrior/Wizard) is the cross-archetype glue.
- DFCs (double-faced land-spells) are high picks — they are a "free roll" in your deck, replacing a land while providing an upside body or spell. Take them early in any deck.
- **Kicker** is a major mana sink in the late game; decks with 5-7 kicker cards can play them for full value. Roost of Drakes is the signature kicker payoff in blue.
- Bot draft note (VOD 1 and 2 are quick/bot draft): "I don't know how the signals are supposed to work out."
- Kenji: "I hated playing versus rogues. Mill is not that bad usually but then you combine that with the instant-speed factor — everything at instant speed — and it's just like okay come on."

## Archetypes

**Blue-Black Rogues (Mill-Synergy)**
- Gameplan: play Rogues at flash speed (especially Soaring Thought-Thief), mill the opponent with Shadow Stinger and other mill effects, then power up your Rogues once they have 8+ cards in their graveyard. Win via combat or milling out.
- Key cards: **Soaring Thought-Thief** (2/3 Flash that pumps ALL your Rogues when opponent has 8+ in graveyard — "super annoying to play against"), **Shadow Stinger** (mills 2 when it attacks), **Expedition Skulker** (solid 2-drop Rogue), **Cunning Geyser Mage** (3/2 wizard for value), **Anti-Cognition** (counter that becomes free once they have 8 milled).
- Loot Mage's Domination (?): "Blue blue blue X — gain control of target creature with mana value X, costs 3 less if you've milled them some." Massive swing card; stealing their best threat often wins the game.
- **Inscription of Insight**: for 4 mana: one good mode; for 8 mana (kicked): all modes including making a big creature. Strong flexible spell.
- **Inscription of Ruin** (black inscription): can kill a small creature, make the opponent discard two, OR return a small creature from your graveyard. Excellent kicked.
- **Mind Rot** effects (Mine Drain etc.): play these when the opponent is most mana-constrained or when you can hit multiple spells. "This is the best turn to mine drain since we're just planning on playing four drops." Also great at full grip to make them discard two.
- **Charix, the Raging Isle**: 4-mana 0/17, effective Ward 2, can pay 3 to pump itself +X/-X where X = number of Islands. "Really good crazy card." With enough islands becomes a one-shot killer: "Charix is actually lethal by itself — we can pump it plus 6 minus 6."
- **Roost of Drakes** (blue): every kicker spell you cast makes a 2/2 Drake. "Sleeper card — y'all sleeping on the Roost, it's good enough." Need at least 3-4 other kicker cards before it's worth playing.
- Don't overvalue Ruin Crab: "People overestimate the importance of the crab." Mill two per land drop is good but not the win condition — it's a bonus. The crab killed Kenji by milling him out in one game.
- Skyclave Plunder: 5-mana draw 3 (or more with party members). Excellent card draw; plan your party mix to maximize it.

**Black-White Clerics (Lifegain/Party)**
- Gameplan: curve out with Clerics, trigger party synergies, use Deadly Alliance (cost reduction based on party) and Core Celebrant (?) for value. Win via incremental advantage and card quality.
- Deadly Alliance: key removal spell that benefits from party count. Multiple copies are fine.
- Core Celebrant: "That was nuts" — per Kenji, strong uncommon for the cleric deck.
- Shepherd of Heroes: 5-mana 3/4 flyer that gains a lot of life. Good top-end Cleric.
- Marauding Blight-Priests: bad in decks NOT on lifegain plan; these go way down in value if you're not in white-black lifegain.
- Zof Consumption (DFC) (?): 4-mana Instant that drains 4 life — excellent in Clerics. Kenji debated firing it "real loose" and noted you should "be a little bit careful" about when to cast it.
- Moss Pit Skeleton: recursive Cleric; Kenji killed his own to trigger synergies: "I kill my own Moss Pit Skeleton, it puts an enchantment in their graveyard, pumps up my scavenger, rate my play." Can be a deliberate sac outlet.
- Full Party (Cleric/Rogue/Warrior/Wizard) unlocks Skyclave Plunder's full draw and reduces Seagate Colossus's cost dramatically.

**Red-White Warriors (Aggro)**
- Gameplan: curve out aggressively. Maul of the Skyclayes is an extremely powerful rare that can close games by itself. Kite Sail (?) is relevant in the warrior deck. Not detailed across these VODs since Kenji was on the other side.
- Maul of the Skyclayes: "Maul is so stupid good" — Kenji lost a game purely to it, called it a near-unwinnable threat.
- Snapping Sailback (?)/Risen Riptide: 0/5 that becomes a 5/5 if you kick a spell — good blocker and threat.

**Blue-Green Kicker (Tempo)**
- Vine Gekko: 2/2 for 2 that reduces kicker costs by 1 and grows with each kicked spell. Key two-drop for the archetype.
- Gnarly Colony / other green kicker creatures: curve fillers that enable the kicker payoffs.
- Roost of Drakes + Geyser Mage (kicked): "Roost into Geyser Mage is kind of disgusting."
- Spoils of Adventure: draw 3, gain 3 life — requires party count. "Spoils is basically Roost cause it's card draw." Triple Spoils in a deck is "kind of disgusting" card advantage.
- Into the Roil (Into the Royal in transcript): bounce + kicker draw. Keeps board interaction flexible.
- Chilling Trap: counter spell that gets cheaper with wizards. "I like Chilling Trap quite a bit" — worth a slot with 3+ wizards.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Anti-Cognition + Soaring Thought-Thief**: pass turn with Anti-Cognition open, flash in Thought-Thief if they don't play anything. If they do play something worth countering, counter it. "A great counter."
- **Shadow Stinger + Soaring Thought-Thief** (once 8+ milled): Stinger mills 2 on each attack; Thought-Thief pumps all Rogues. Together they create a fast clock that generates itself — mill triggers more milling, pumps your attackers.
- **Inscription of Insight (kicked) + board state**: returns two creatures to opponent's hand AND draws two AND makes a creature. Use when tapping out is safe. "Bounce both riptides, draw two cards, and make a big creature — this is probably gonna prompt a scoop."
- **Loot Mage's Domination + milled opponent**: costs 3 less if they have cards in graveyard. Steal their 4-drop for 1 mana. Brutal swing.
- **Charix + Islands**: the more islands you play, the more it can pump. With 6 islands, Charix can swing as a 7/11 trample effectively — opponent must leave a chump back.
- **Moss Pit Skeleton + Thwart the Grave** (?): opponent's combo was Moss Pit Skeleton + Murasa Sproutling recursion + Blood Beckoning loop. Be aware of multi-card graveyard recursion loops in black-green.
- **Royal Mage + Into the Roil** (Into the Royal): bounce-draw combo. "We have the royal mage with the into the royal combo that we can start going off on if we want." Each Into the Roil kicked with a Wizard out nets extra value.
- **Spoils of Adventure + high party count**: draw 3 + gain 3 for 5 mana. "Imagine not being able to cast the Spoils of Adventure." Prioritize hitting all four party types (Cleric/Rogue/Warrior/Wizard) in blue-white/blue-black builds.
- **Roost of Drakes + any kicker card**: each kicked spell = free 2/2 Drake. "Roost of Drakes — you know what it does, right? It wins you the game."

## Card tips

- **Maul of the Skyclayes**: highest-impact rare in the format; near-game-winning by itself. Snap-first-pick.
- **DFCs (double-faced land spells)**: always take them early regardless of archetype. They're a free roll — "kind of like a free roll in your deck."
- **Feed the Swarm**: black removal that can hit enchantments — premium. "That was a good removal spell so we'll just take that here."
- **Deadly Alliance**: cost-reducing removal; multiple copies are excellent. Going fourth pick when black is clearly open is a great sign.
- **Shadow Stinger**: late pick value card (9th pick here); mills 2 on attack. "This card is just stupid annoying."
- **Subtle Strike**: combat trick that gives a creature +1/+1 and death touch. Premium instant. Kenji wheeled multiple copies and took all of them.
- **Shepherd of Heroes**: 5-mana 3/4 flyer that gains significant life — Kenji called it "the best card in the pack" over other options.
- **Skyclave Plunder**: "Big card draw effect. Baseline 5-mana draw 3, and as long as you have some party member cards you can look at more and choose." Value scales hard with party count.
- **Pack Beast**: provides all four party types in one card. Great for enabling Spoils of Adventure and Skyclave Plunder.
- **Thwart the Grave**: graveyard hate/recursion; wheeled 9th pick in one draft — "insane" late pick.
- **Nectar Pot** (?): repeatedly mentioned as bad. "This is why you don't play nectar pots — not very good."
- **Shell Shield**: free-roll protection spell (pay 0 or 2 for hexproof). Fine as a late cut in decks that want to protect a key creature.
- **Ruin Crab**: "I am not big on Ruin Crab" — only draft it if you have a dedicated mill-win plan and other mill enablers; don't just drop it in a Rogues deck hoping it helps.
- 16 lands is fine in blue-black (Kenji played it); can go 15 or 16 in aggressive kicker decks with good DFC fixer coverage.
- In bot draft, kicker synergy cards (Vine Gekko, Roost of Drakes) go later than they would in human draft — pick them up.

## Pitfalls

- **Not playing a DFC as a land early**: Kenji: "Oh you know what I'm going to regret not playing out the mauling as a land — that was really stupid." If you need the land drop, play it face-down.
- **Firing removal too early on wrong targets**: "I just fired off my Feed the Swarm on a one-one" — cost him the game. Save premium removal for the threats that actually win the game.
- **Missing lethal**: in the p3PcqY15ve8 draft, Kenji missed lethal because he cast Spoils of Adventure instead of activating his win-con: "I just drew the card, didn't take two seconds to think, and fired it off. They were actually just dead on board." Always do the math before drawing cards.
- **Punting on counter timing**: in the Rogues mirror, Kenji didn't fire off a key steal/domination when the opponent tapped out, then lost because they had open mana later. "I could have gone for the dragon on the turn they had open mana."
- **Waiting too long to attack**: "I don't think I'm supposed to attack with my [5/4] — they can just take the hit from the 5/4 and kill me with one good top deck." When you have a large trample threat and opponent is low, push damage.
- **Overloading on the same type**: "Three Feed the Swarms is quite a bit — now I wish I had taken the Gloom Hunter." Balance creature count vs. spell count.
- **Not blocking with the correct creature**: Kenji noted blocking the wrong creature in a damage race can force bad trades. Identify which creature your opponent wants dead and don't give them the trade.
- **Being on the draw vs. aggro**: "I think sometimes this format just comes down to who won the die roll" vs. red-white warriors on a fast curve. Mulligan more aggressively when on the draw vs. known aggro.

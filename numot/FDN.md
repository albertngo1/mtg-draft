# FDN — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Core-set feel, lower power.** Kenji describes Foundations as "kind of like a core set" — games are more about curve and card quality than synergy explosions. Best-of-one plays at a moderate pace.
- **Black is likely the strongest color.** Kenji takes early black cards aggressively and identifies Bake into a Pie, Vampire Nighthawk, Hungry Ghoul, and Tragical Banshee (?) as premium pickups. "Blue black was really strong" — repeated across multiple VODs.
- **White-blue flyers is one of the best archetypes.** Kenji explicitly calls it out: "blue white flyers, that was the good archetype." Dazzling Angel (3-mana 2/3 flying, gain 1 life whenever another creature ETB) is repeatedly flagged as "one of the best Commons in the format."
- **Spells-matters (blue-red) was also real.** Kenji identifies a red-blue deck that pumps creatures when you cast instants/sorceries — he names the payoff as "Balthor" (3-mana creature, pump team on non-creature spells?). Regrets not forcing it.
- **Removal is scarce and premium.** No Stab, no Bake into a Pie, no Banishing Light in some drafts leads to weak decks. "No stabs kind of sucks, no banishing lights. The day of judgment pulled its weight." Missing targeted removal is the most common deckbuilding failure point.
- **Curve out still wins in Bo1.** Decks that curve out — even without deep synergy — beat better-constructed but slower decks. "If you can just curve out in best of one that often makes up for a lack of interaction or removal."
- **Format is shallow by design.** Kenji did ~96 Foundations drafts total (82 Premier + 14 Traditional). Late in format, identifies that decks win with curves, not combos.

## Archetypes

### Blue-White Flyers (Azorius)
- **Gameplan:** Curve 1-drop lifelinker (Healer's Hawk) into Youthful Valkyrie (2-mana 1/3 flying that grows when another flyer ETB) into Imperian Eagle (?) into Guardian of the Air (6-mana 1/1 lifelink that ETBs with two 3/3s). Win in the air.
- **Key cards:** Dazzling Angel ("very very good" — consistently flagged as best white common), Youthful Valkyrie (1/3 flyer that grows with each angel; strong two-drop), Imperian Eagle (top end flyer, Kenji first-picks three in one draft and calls it "Eagles looking op"), Healer's Hawk (1-drop flyer/lifelinker), Scatter (Essence Scatter — countermagic), Refute (2-mana counter that also draws/surveil), Helpful Hunter (best white common two-drop), Squad Rallier (tutors creatures CMC 3 or less — grabs Eagles, Hunters, and Valkyries).
- **Strength:** Consistent, hard to interact with. Double Eagle is a strong clock that demands immediate answers.
- **Weakness:** Flooding out is brutal — had several games with 9+ lands drawn. Light on hard removal; Essence Scatter can be dead against non-creature-heavy opponents.

### Blue-Black Control/Value (Dimir)
- **Gameplan:** Survive with blockers and removal (Bake into a Pie, Brineborn Cutthroat, Telerian Terror(?)) while generating card advantage through Tishana's Tidecaller(?) and Spectral Sailor. Win with large threats or card advantage.
- **Key cards:** Bake into a Pie (kill + food token — "can't go wrong"), Hungry Ghoul ("one of Black's better two-drops"), Vampire Nighthawk (3-mana flying deathtouch lifelink — "Yikes. That is a card"), Tishana's Tidecaller/Brineborn Cutthroat (flash/instants payoff; grows on spells cast in opponent's turn), Spectral Sailor (1-drop flash flyer with activated loot; "Spectral Sailor was great"), Micro-mancer (4-mana 3/3 that tutors 1-mana instant/sorcery), Refute, Curator of Destinies (6-mana 5/5 flying look-at-top-5).
- **Strength:** Card advantage grinds out opponents. Nighthawk demands immediate answer.
- **Weakness:** Needs instants/flash density for Brineborn. Micro-mancer is "insane" but needs 1-mana spells in deck to tutor.

### Black-White Lifegain/Value (Orzhov)
- **Gameplan:** Curve creatures, use Day of Judgment as reset, grind with value creatures (Guarded Air, Angel of Finality) and recurring threats (Revenge of the Rats).
- **Key cards:** Day of Judgment ("pulled its weight" multiple times — key reset), Youthful Valkyrie, Angel of Finality (exiles graveyards on ETB — strong in graveyard-matters metagames), Helpful Hunter, Hungry Ghoul, Gutless Plunder (sac outlet + loot), Revision of the Rats(?) / Revenge of the Rats (make X rat tokens, flashback — "has actually been really really good"), Banishing Light (exile enchantment), Campus Guide (ramp/fixer), Guardian of the Air / Guarded Air (6-mana 1/1 + two 3/3s — "gives us a lot of value off of one card").
- **Strength:** Day of Judgment into Revenge of the Rats is a real two-card reset+wide combo. Resilient to aggro if you can hit 4-5 mana.
- **Weakness:** Even split of two colors leads to awkward hands. Needs targeted single-target removal (Stab/Bake) that was often unavailable.

### Blue-Black Homunculus Combo (Dimir)
- **Gameplan:** Use Strix Lookout (2-mana creature, whenever you draw second card each turn make a copy of a creature) with Tatiova the Merrow Regent(?) (legendary merfolk that doubles copies of things you control) and Homunculus Horde (4-mana creature — when it ETB or attacks, double your creature count of its type).
- **Key cards:** Strix Lookout, Homunculus Horde ("whenever or at each player draws... make a 1/1 flyer"), Tatia/Tatiova(?) (legendary that doubles — "play land draw a card double my homunculus"), Zombify (reanimate — lets you loot away bombs and reanimate), Burglar Rat (discard effect), Vampire Nighthawk, Waltz (?) (bounce effect that also draws).
- **Strength:** When Horde + Tatiova are both out and drawing lands, becomes "too good, too good." One of Kenji's 7-win runs in this set.
- **Weakness:** Only one actual removal spell. Multiple removal-heavy opponents end this fast. "If we ever play against a deck with even a handful of removal spells we're going to have a bad time."

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Dazzling Angel + Youthful Valkyrie:** Angel triggers "gain 1 life when another creature ETBs" while Valkyrie grows when angels ETB. Playing Angel while Valkyrie is already in play gives Valkyrie +1/+1 AND gains you 1 life. Very synergistic in WU flyers.

2. **Micromancer + 1-mana instants/sorceries:** Micro-mancer tutors any 1-CMC instant or sorcery. In a flash deck with Fleeting Distraction (cycle-draw) and other 1-mana spells, it fetches your key interaction. "Distraction is actually another hit for our micromancer."

3. **Homunculus Horde + Strix Lookout:** Strix makes a 1/1 flying copy of a creature when you draw your second card each turn. If you draw and trigger Strix targeting Horde, you get a Horde copy. That copy ETBs and doubles the creature count again. Exponential until opponent has removal.

4. **Day of Judgment + Revenge of the Rats:** Day clears the board, then Rats makes X tokens where X is your creature count in graveyard. Post-wrath, if you had 3-4 creatures, you get 6-8 rat tokens with flashback for double. Kenji calls this "the old day of judgment into rats."

5. **Leyline Axe (?) + Telerian Terror(?) / large creature:** Leyline Axe begins in opening hand if you start with it, giving equipped creature +1/+1, double strike, trample. Equipping to the ward-2 Terror made a "6/6 double strike trampler with ward two" that demands multiple answers.

6. **Zombify + Tatia / Meteor Golem:** Loot away expensive bombs, reanimate via Zombify. "Reanimating Tatia is still pretty good." Zombify + Meteor Golem creates an efficient 7/5 removal-on-ETB line for 5 mana combined (3 Zombify + 2 for Golem already in yard).

## Card tips

- **Dazzling Angel** — Best white common by Kenji's evaluation. "Very very good. Not in my deck maybe but very very good." 3-mana 2/3 flyer that gains 1 whenever another creature ETBs. Pairs with any token production or creature-dense strategy.
- **Snare Spinner** — "1/4 reach deathtouch, put a +1/+1 counter on a creature you control [on ETB]. That card was so so good. That was like the best green common in the format." Extremely high value for its mana cost.
- **Youthful Valkyrie** — Strong two-drop flyer in any white deck that runs other angels. 1/3 baseline with growth potential. "Being a 1/3 flyer has been really good."
- **Helpful Hunter** — "One of the best white Commons." Standard pick early.
- **Hungry Ghoul** — "One of Black's better two-drops." 5-pick in pack one across multiple drafts — always taken when available.
- **Spectral Sailor** — 1-drop flash flyer with activated loot. "Spectral Sailor was great." Triggers Brineborn Cutthroat on opponent's turn.
- **Bake into a Pie** — Black removal + food. "Kill a creature, make a food. Can't go wrong with that." Consistently passed too late when in the wrong seat.
- **Vampire Nighthawk** — 3-mana flying deathtouch lifelink. "Yikes. That is a card." First-pick-worthy in any black deck.
- **Refute** — 2-mana counterspell with upside. "Refute was good." Multiple copies playable in flash-heavy Dimir builds.
- **Banishing Light / Luminous Rebuke** — White exile. "No banishing lights [and] no stabs, the deck was weak." Don't skip these when available.
- **Imperian Eagle** — Strong blue flyer (3-mana 2/2 flying, "other creatures with flying get +1/+1"?). First-picked multiple times; "Eagles looking op." Worth high picks in WU.
- **Guardian of the Air / Guarded Air** — 6-mana white card making two 3/3s with a 1/1 lifelinker. "Gives a lot of value off one card." Not splashy but reliably good.
- **Campus Guide** — 2-mana 2/1 that tutors a basic land. "Fine" two-drop that fixes mana in multicolor or splash builds.
- **Solemn Simulacrum** — 4-mana artifact that ramps and draws. "Kind of hard to pass up the value of Solemn since it just goes into every deck."
- **Brineborn Cutthroat** — 2-mana flash creature that grows when you cast spells on opponent's turn. Strong in flash/instant-heavy Dimir. "Zero instances currently... not looking great" — needs a full spell-based build.
- **Day of Judgment** — White wrath at 4 mana. "Pulled its weight" multiple times. "Devastating" life total swings when followed up with threats.

### Pitfalls

- **Drafting half a synergy deck.** In VODs 1 and 2, Kenji ends up halfway between blue-white flyers and blue-black instants because he didn't commit. "My deck doesn't really have as much cohesion." In FDN, committing to a clear archetype by pack two is important.
- **Missing targeted removal.** Decks with no Stab / Bake / Banishing Light struggle badly. "No stabs kind of sucks. No banishing lights... the day of judgment pulled its weight but." Always prioritize at least 2-3 pieces of targeted removal.
- **Overvaluing late-game bombs without support.** Curator of Destinies (6-mana 5/5 flyer that looks at top 5) was never cast in an entire run; Leyline Axe never started in opening hand. Expensive bombs without fixing or acceleration are dead weight.
- **Not casting Fleeting Distraction on your turn to hit land.** Multiple times Kenji notes he cycled Fleeting Distraction on his own turn when he should have saved it for end of opponent's turn to trigger Brineborn or Tishana's effects. "I should hold the lands at this point."
- **Running 3 Telerian Terrors(?) / too many conditional counterspells.** "I'm at 8 instants for my two Telerian Terrors. I don't think we'll be playing any of those." Counterspells that require heavy instant count are a trap if the rest of the deck is creatures.
- **Over-splashing without fixing.** Kenji took green bombs (Spinner of Souls, Bite Down) mid-draft, then cut them because he lacked green sources. "I think probably drafted this pretty poorly... didn't see a single stab, no bake into a pie." Know the fixing ceiling before committing to a third color.
- **Not sacrificing Hungry Ghoul at the right time.** In the mirror, Kenji's opponent failed to sac Hungry Ghoul before blockers to deny life gain. "They're supposed to sack and have a 3/3 hungry ghoul instead of a 2/2." Know your sacrifice triggers.

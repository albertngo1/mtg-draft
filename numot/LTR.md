# LTR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Grixis (black-red-blue) colors are the bread and butter. Kenji says "as long as you stay in the Grixis colors in this format you're doing fine."
- Tempt (the Ring mechanic — putting the Ring's temptation on a creature) is a key resource. Kenji tracks his tempt count constantly and plans his attacks/sacrifices around reaching specific tempt levels. Full tempt (4th temptation) gives the ring bearer +1/+1, menace, deathtouch, lifelink, and makes excess blockers unable to block — extremely hard to race.
- Black-red is the default aggressive/midrange pairing. Red-blue is viable too. Green is much weaker; Kenji rarely drafts it.
- The format is at end-of-life in these VODs and Kenji has drafted it ~100 times. Card evaluation is highly refined.
- Lorien Revealed (blue land cycler that draws 3 when hardcast) is explicitly the #1 most-drafted card in Kenji's data. Peller Gear (?), Survivor (Nazgul or another cheap early drop), and Coral's End follow.

## Archetypes
- **Black-Red Aggro/Midrange (primary):** The dominant strategy. Cheap creatures (Rohirrim Lancer, Survivor), sacrifice effects (Lash of the Balrog, Grima's Patrol (?)), and tempt payoffs. Flame Smith (?) acts as a universal damage source. Aomer (?) described as "as close to an uncommon bomb as you're getting" — generates a large creature immediately. Call of the Ring supported by a cheap ring bearer (Lancer on turn 1, Call on turn 2 is a near-concede for opponent).
- **Blue-Black Control/Mill:** Torment of Gollum (?) as the primary win condition alongside Lorien Revealed for card advantage and Survivors as early plays. Mill win condition is slow but viable — Kenji drafted it once with great success using Berserker (?) for the mill plan. Needs ~6 Mana to hardcast Lorien for the draw-3 effect. Stern Scolding (?) and other counters supplement removal.
- **Red-Blue Spell/Tempt (secondary):** Ranger of Eons (?) + fire effects, using Lorien Revealed as card draw. Less common but viable.
- **Tempt build (any color):** Rally the Hornburg (?) + Lorien Revealed combo gives an instant tempt trigger. Two Rallies + Elrond (?) = instant tempt on demand. Kenji notes it's important to have multiple ways to turn on tempt proactively.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Call of the Ring + Rohirrim Lancer (or any cheap ring bearer):** Turn-1 Lancer, turn-2 Call is described as "do you concede?" — the ring bearer grows continuously, gains menace, deathtouch, lifelink as tempt progresses.
- **Rally the Hornburg + Elrond (?):** Two Rallies with Elrond gives an instant tempt trigger without needing to attack. Kenji notes "two rallies with Elrond is really good, it gives you an instant trigger of the tempt."
- **Lash of the Balrog + any small creature:** Best with fodder from Club tokens (Prancing Pony (?)), muster tokens, or rescued creatures. Kenji prioritizes getting at least 1-2 sac effects in every black deck.
- **Desperate Rescue + ring bearer / Lancer:** Rescue lets you sacrifice the Lancer to Lash, then get it back; Kenji holds Rescue specifically to protect ring bearers or regain board presence.
- **Black Breath + Flame Smiths:** Black Breath clears all 1-toughness creatures. With triple Flame Smith pings, it becomes a selective wrath. Kenji says it's worth running with several tempt effects even though he doesn't usually main-deck it.
- **Torment of Gollum + opponents' forced discards:** Torment makes opponent discard down to 2, then they have to discard again on upkeep if they have more. "He who casts Torment first wins the game."
- **Press the Enemy + Lorien Revealed:** Pressing a 4-drop lets you cast Lorien Revealed for free. Kenji calls this "the best it's ever gonna be."
- **Grond (?) fling:** Kenji observed opponents throwing Grond at his creatures — a legitimate line in red with sacrifice outlets.

## Card tips
- **Lorien Revealed:** "Best card in the pack, not close" as a P1P1 if nothing is better. Never feel bad cycling it early for mana fixing; hardcasting for draw-3 at 6+ mana is a late-game luxury. The #1 most-drafted card in Kenji's format data.
- **Aomer (Eomer (?)):** "As close to an uncommon bomb as you're getting" — generates massive immediate board presence, repeatedly described as a game-changer when top-decked.
- **Nazgul (Survivor / "Gollum" (?)):** Premium 2-drop; Kenji calls it his preferred ring bearer for Call of the Ring and the first thing he builds around in blue-black.
- **Coral's End:** #1 most-drafted card per Kenji's stated data. Flexible instant-speed exile removal.
- **Lash of the Balrog:** Strong removal requiring sac; Kenji always wants 1-2 in his black decks. "Double Lash is fantastic."
- **Grima's Patrol (?):** Underrated sacrifice outlet that also provides a blocker; Kenji notes it as a clean way to fuel Lash and get tempt value.
- **Muster the Rohirrim (?):** Good token producer; works well with Club (?) for sacrifice fodder.
- **Bilbo (Bilbo Baggins):** Win-condition legendary with Mithril Coat — can become indestructible. There and Back Again combo: Bilbo makes a treasure that fuels replay. Kenji refers to it as "trilbo" dream (cast 3 times in a game).
- **There and Back Again:** Solid source of extra tempt triggers; not always great early on.
- **Gothmog:** Consistently noted as weakest of the 5-drops Kenji runs — "four mana pass" energy; he keeps it but would cut it first.
- **Mithril Coat:** Kenji flip-flops on this ("hate the coat") but includes it when he has multiple legendaries. Good with Bilbo; prevents claim/kill on key threats.
- **Birthday Escape (?):** Cheap spell with cycling; Kenji regrets not cycling it on turn 1 in some games to dig toward Survivor/torment.
- **Rohirrim Lancer:** Premium 1-drop — triggers tempt and attacks through. Kenji's default first ring bearer.
- **Fell Beast (?):** "Beast is lethal by itself" — strong flying threat in blue; Kenji references it as a top blue uncommon.
- **Radagast:** Opponent's Radagast is called out as "completely ruined my life" — it tutors creatures continuously and must be killed immediately.

## Pitfalls
- **Don't pass Lorien Revealed late:** It's so impactful that Kenji says he feels bad land-cycling it, but it's correct when you need mana. Never let it table — take it even 3rd or 4th pick.
- **Don't ignore Radagast (opponent's):** Kenji lost a game he should have won by not killing Radagast immediately; it generated too many creatures. "I should have done it a while ago."
- **Don't run a deck with too few tempt triggers:** You want at least 4-5 ways to trigger the Ring. Without consistent tempt, the late-game scaling breaks down.
- **Don't hard-cast Lorien Revealed too early:** At 6 mana it draws 3, but the format is fast — cycling it for a land when mana-stuck is usually correct, even painful.
- **Knife Wound is weak removal:** Kenji cuts it from his builds. "Not big fan of knife wound."
- **Great Hall of the Citadel is weak:** Kenji's 3rd most-drafted card (end-of-format greediness) but acknowledged as a bad signal that something went wrong.
- **Hate drafting is not worth it:** Kenji explicitly says "hate drafting is still not relevant or worth it" in this format.
- **Don't make ring bearer choices casually:** Incorrect ring bearer assignment (e.g., putting the ring on a haunt token instead of a Ranger when you have sacrifice effects in hand) can cost you triggers and game-states.
- **Watch for Club tokens / token flood on opponent's side:** Kenji notes that wide token boards (especially blue-black Shalob (?)) can overwhelm if you don't have a wrath or sweeper effect.

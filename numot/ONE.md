# ONE — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Phyrexia: All Will Be One (ONE) is a poison/oil-counter format. Kenji repeatedly called it "all will be one" (BO1) a punishing format and expressed relief when moving to BO3, which he described as "a lot better" for showcasing the deck's power.
- Being on the play matters a lot — Kenji noted several game results were directly influenced by play vs. draw, especially against aggressive strategies.
- The format has very swingy bombs (Atraxa, Nissa, Elesh Norn, Battle Chair (?), Tyranax Rex (?), Mondrak) that can close games quickly. Kenji acknowledged "every Limited format is going to have bombs" but called some ONE bombs "particularly egregious."
- Corrupted (10 poison threshold) is a real win condition — opponents actively try to race to it.
- Red-Green is widely considered the best or joint-best deck. Blue-Red is second-best due to proliferate density. Red-White equipment/aggressive is also strong.

## Archetypes
- **Red-Green (oil/counters)**: Best deck per Kenji. Built around creatures that enter with oil counters (Lattice Blade Mantis, Cephalopod Sentry (?), Churning Reservoir (?)) plus payoffs like Armored Scrap Gorger and All-Will-Be-One enchantment. Cultivator of Blades and similar ramp creatures are key; "don't underestimate Cultivators, they are really good." Trolls provide late-game grinding. Two Mantis (haste, oil) are high-priority targets.
- **Blue-Red (proliferate)**: Second-best. Relies on proliferating oil counters to grow threats and gain value from cards like Experimental Augury, Mesmerizing Dose, Volt Charge, Hex Gold Slash, and the Chimera. Skitter Strixhaven (?) and Synthesizer provide card advantage. "Blue-red is probably the second-best deck because you have a lot of proliferate."
- **Red-White (equipment/toxic)**: Solid aggro deck. Key pieces: Nahiri (planeswalker, underrated per Kenji — "it does a ton of work"), Eternal Wanderer, Rebel Salvo, Hex Gold Slash, Hover Wings, War Whip, Batter Fist, Swooping Lookout. Aims to suit up creatures and apply pressure. Gleeful Demolition provides artifact synergy. Furnace Strider is the main reason to run red splash.
- **Green-White (toxic/splash)**: Kenji drafted one green-heavy deck splashing white (Eternal Wanderer) and black (Glissa Sunslayer). Thirsting Roots and Scrap Gorger provide fixing for 3-color builds. "Staying super open" allows picking up powerful non-green rares late.
- **Blue-Black/Poison control**: Seen in opponents' decks — Cephalopod Sentry, Slaughter Singer, Plague Nurse, Retrained Observers. Kenji noted it is difficult to fight through the poison pressure.

## Card interactions & combos
- **All-Will-Be-One + any non-creature spell**: Each time you put a counter on a creature you control, All-Will-Be-One pings the opponent for one. With proliferate and oil cards, this can mean multiple triggers in one turn. Kenji called this "insane" and kept trying to assemble it.
- **Ovika Enigma Goliath + cheap proliferate spells** (Volt Charge, Experimental Augury): Each instant/sorcery makes hasty tokens; proliferate then stacks counters across them. Kenji took Ovika first pick and tried to build around it.
- **Armored Scrap Gorger + Glissa Sunslayer**: Scrap Gorger exiles a card from opponent's graveyard to gain a counter; Glissa removes counters from opponents' artifacts and permanents. Kenji noted Glissa on her own "just wins the game" if unanswered — first strike plus death touch plus counter removal is backbreaking.
- **Furnace Skull Bomb + Synthesizer**: Skull Bomb can target your own Synthesizer or Chimera to get the ETB trigger again. Kenji explicitly noted "I can put this on like the immobilizer, that's really good."
- **Skitter Strixhaven (?) + Voracious Varmint (?) / proliferate**: Having a creature that gains counters and proliferating every turn snowballs very fast. Kenji noted the Scrap Gorger "going off" with Churning Reservoir on turn one is game-ending.
- **Eternal Wanderer's -4 + multiple creatures**: Flickering your own creatures resets ETB value (Basilica Shepherd, Chimera Rabble). Kenji used this repeatedly to generate extra 2/2 tokens and reset the Chimera's oil.
- **Nahiri's +1 (force attack) + 3/3 blocker**: Forces opponent's 2/2 to attack into your 3/3, eating it. Kenji called this "a way to protect herself or even be used as a removal spell."

## Card tips
- **Rebel Salvo**: Costs 1 mana if a creature with a +1/+1 counter attacked this turn (Helm of the Host reduces equip cost). Key common in Red-White. Take it over Hex Gold Slash early ("obviously we're not passing Wanderer there" — but Salvo-over-Slash is an exception explained by deck context).
- **Experimental Augury**: Take as many as you can in Blue decks — it digs for bombs AND it proliferates. "I'd probably run as many auguries as we can pick up just to dig for our bombs, plus they proliferate."
- **Swooping Lookout**: "Not a card that looks like much but it actually is okay" — blocks all mites, has vigilance, can attack for 1 with Batter Fist.
- **Saw-Blade Scamp**: Not good in Red-White (requires artifacts). Lean & Lightbringer is better there even as a 3-drop.
- **Chimney Rabble**: Always good, especially late when you can trigger it multiple times. Kenji ran 2 in Blue-Red.
- **Prophetic Prism / Thirsting Roots**: Key fixing enablers for splash colors. Scrap Gorger also provides black fixing (exiles from graveyard, gains oil counter, searches up any land type).
- **Free From Flesh**: Good removal/counter removal, especially if you need to put counters on a creature for Scrap Gorger or proliferate synergy.
- **Cultivators of Blades (?)**: Underrated — they add oil counters to themselves and provide ramp. Core to Red-Green. "Don't underestimate the Cultivators."
- **Glistener Seer**: Fine early drop but "does nothing the turn you play it unless you have Cultivators generally." Don't prioritize past pick 5-6.
- **Battle Chair (Sylvac Battle Chair ?)**: "Generally going to be the biggest thing on the battlefield." Even just as a value piece, the moving equipment ability is relevant occasionally but not always.
- **Planar Disruption**: Top-tier white removal. Kenji passed Disruption to take Nahiri P1P1 but actively wanted it in pack 3.
- **Hex Gold Slash**: Best or near-best common removal in the format. Always take it over most commons.

## Pitfalls
- **Over-relying on BO1 keep standards**: Kenji kept several slow hands and got punished. In ONE specifically, curving out fast is critical — "you can't come back if you fall behind."
- **Not running enough cheap interaction**: "Our deck was pretty good, it needed some more cheap uh interaction." In ONE the removal spells (Slash, Disruption, Rebel Salvo) are so impactful that missing them hurts more than in average formats.
- **Running tap-lands in aggressive decks**: "I really can't be playing off of curve" — Hunter's Maze (?) and similar tap-lands were actively avoided. Only worth it in control-oriented green builds.
- **Fuseling in non-sacrifice builds**: Kenji explicitly said "I don't think we want Fuseling in this deck, I think we'd rather have Free From Flesh."
- **Cromulence (?) of Chorus**: "I don't really like Chorus outside of poison decks, it just doesn't have much synergy."
- **Curator in low-artifact decks**: "Curator has very few hits, just take the land." Don't take Curator unless you have 8+ artifacts.
- **Forgetting Rummage vs. Loot distinction**: Kenji explicitly noted "if that was loot I would have kept the land but since it's rummage no." Know your discard effects — rummage (discard first) is more constraining than loot (draw first).
- **Punting with Wanderer's -4**: In one game Kenji forgot opponents could sacrifice creatures to a Mondrak in response to the exile. "I was maybe supposed to uptick on their Mondrak." Always check sacrifice outlets before using Wanderer's bounce ability.

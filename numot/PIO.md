# PIO — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Pioneer Masters (PIO) is a reprint/nostalgia set built around the Pioneer legal card pool. The key mechanical themes are: **devotion** (colored pips in mana costs matter), **+1/+1 counters**, **Gates** (land-type synergy), and **red aggro** (heroic triggers, pump spells).
- Red aggro is described as one of the best or outright best strategies. Kenji stated this repeatedly: "Red is one of the best colors in this format especially the Uber aggro red decks" and "soft-forcing red if you see Rimrock Knights and Crusaders go mid-pack, slam dunk them."
- The Gates archetype (Guild Summits, Gate Colossus, Gates Ablaze, Gatebreaker Ram) is legitimate but has key weaknesses: fights for both lands and spells in draft, loses badly to red aggro (which has no good blocks against the curve), and is vulnerable to mana screw (Kenji drew 3-basic openers multiple times with 10 gates in deck).
- The format has been rotating new "devotion matters" cards in weekly, changing card evaluations. Kenji noted new includes each week during the late format.
- Being on the play is especially important in Pioneer Masters — heroic and pump-spell decks curve out so fast that drawing even one extra card matters.

## Archetypes
- **Mono-Red / Red Aggro (heroic)**: Top-tier. Built around Rimrock Knight (best red common — adventure pumps +3/+0 then the Knight bodies), Akroan Crusader (heroic: make 1/1 haste tokens), Labyrinth Champion (heroic: deal 1 damage to target creature), Phalanx Leader (?), Frostburn Weird, Scab-Clan Berserker (renowned), Boulder Salvo (2 mana deal 3 to creature or player, synergy with Rimrock adventure), Dragon Mantle (fire-breathing), Follow the Hammer (fight spell). Whip of Erebos is a "sleeper bomb" in black-heavy builds. Goblin Rabblemaster makes tokens, puts on huge pressure. Peros God of the Forge (?) closes games via ping damage. "Turn 1 Crusader, turn 2 double Rimrock Knight, attack for seven."
- **Red-White (red heroic + white support)**: Splashing white adds Stasis Snare, Compulsory Rest, triplicate-style token payoffs, and late flyers. Kenji considered this and said the deck is "slightly more powerful" than mono-red but less consistent.
- **Gates (Temur base, Green-Blue-Red)**: Centered on Gate Colossus (big colorless creature that returns from graveyard), Gatebreaker Ram (gets bigger for each gate), Gates Ablaze (sweeper that doesn't kill Ram), Guild Summit (draw a card per gate), District Guide (search any basic or Gate). Wants 10+ gates and ≥2 Gates Ablaze. "Temur at the base — green, blue, and red." Archway Angel gains life for each gate. Gate Colossus + Guild Summit = near-unbeatable grind if assembled.
- **Green-Black Graveyard/Counters**: Self-mill plan using Golgari-flavored cards — Commune with the Gods (?), Lotleth Troll (discard creatures to grow), Nemesis of Mortals (costs less per creature in graveyard), Gnaw to the Bone (life gain), Catacomb Sifter (scry on death). Avatar of the Resolute (new devotion include) is a 2-mana 3/2 reach trample with +1/+1 counter synergy. Seed Guardian, Savage Punch as removal.
- **Blue-Green-Black (Progenitor Mimic control / gates hybrid)**: Progenitor Mimic copies the best creature on the battlefield at start of your upkeep, making tokens. Good targets in PIO: Gate Colossus, Disciple of Phenix (?), Dinrova Horror, Catacomb Sifter. Kenji described this as "a ton of good hits." Dreadbore and Ob Nixilis's Cruelty as removal splashes.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Rimrock Knight (adventure) + Boulder Salvo**: Cast "Rimrock" as an adventure (pump +3/+0 to a creature), then the Knight comes back as a creature. Next turn, cast Boulder Salvo for only 1 mana (since you cast a sorcery spell this turn / adventure counts). Kenji highlighted: "Whether you are using the adventure mode and then casting the Salvo for one mana, or maybe you've already used the adventure mode and you're just casting the Knight and then Boulder Salvo for two — great value."
- **Akroan Crusader + Dragon Mantle (fire-breathing aura)**: Crusader gets heroic from Dragon Mantle ETB (makes a 1/1 haste token). Dragon Mantle then gives fire-breathing for repeated attacks. Creates insurmountable board pressure by turn 3-4.
- **Rimrock Knight (pump) + Fall of the Hammer**: Rimrock pumps a creature +3/+0, then Fall of the Hammer has that creature "fight" (deal damage equal to power) targeting an opponent's creature. With 4+ power creatures this trades up. Kenji explicitly noted "Follow/Fall of the Hammer with Triple Rimrock, pump up, pump up a creature with the Rimrock Knight and then shoot it with Fall of the Hammer."
- **Labyrinth Champion + any targeting spell**: Each time you target the Champion with an instant or sorcery, it deals 1 damage to target creature. This lets cheap spells (Dragon Mantle, Rimrock Knight, Witches' Mark) also kill small blockers or ping the opponent.
- **Peros God of the Forge (?) + cheap heroic creatures**: Peros is inactive until devotion to red is ≥5 (becomes a creature). While inactive, whenever another creature enters the battlefield, it deals 2 damage to each opponent. With a full board of 1-drops, each new creature ETB pings for 2. Kenji noted "this card is nuts" and regretted not playing land one turn earlier because it would have given him a third Peros activation for +3/+3 and trample.
- **Progenitor Mimic + Nylea's Disciple (?)**: Mimic copied the Disciple, making a token each upkeep that gains life. "Gain two, then four, then six — and that's just if the Disciples themselves live." Kenji won a game by stabilizing at 60+ life and then decking the opponent.
- **Gate Colossus + Guild Summit**: Summit draws a card every time a gate enters. Colossus recycles itself from the graveyard when a gate enters. Together, they draw cards and redeploy an 8/8 every turn you play a gate. "That was a draw-five right now. Insane."
- **Whip of Erebos**: "Don't sleep on Whip of Erebos even just the four mana giving all of your creatures lifelink — very strong." The recursion is bonus; the lifelink alone stabilizes races.

## Card tips
- **Rimrock Knight**: "One of if not the best red common in the format. Just does everything you want it to and more." Take it early, take multiples.
- **Witches' Mark (?)**: Listed as one of the best red commons alongside Rimrock Knight. Key card draw for mono-red. "I think Witch's Mark is one of the best cards in red at common."
- **Draconic Roar**: Better than Oath of Chandra in most builds because it's instant speed and doesn't require a Dragon in hand to be effective. "Draconic Roar is actually just better in this format unless you can plan on triggering the Oath of Chandra."
- **Goblin Rabblemaster**: "Great at pushing pressure and especially if you have any ways to back up the tokens." First or second pickable in red.
- **Akroan Crusader**: "First pick a 1-mana 1/1." In context — it's that good with heroic payoffs around it.
- **Gatebreaker Ram**: "By far the easy pick" as a 2/4 vigilance that gets +1/+1 for each gate you control, and has hexproof from targeting while you have 3+ gates. Core Gates card.
- **District Guide**: "2/3 for 2 that searches any Gate or basic" — key tutor for the Gates deck. Prioritize it. Kenji passed it once early and regretted it.
- **Gates Ablaze**: Sweeps all non-Gate creatures for X (equal to the number of Gates you control). Can take out entire boards while leaving Gate Colossus and Gatebreaker Ram alive. Take these as high priorities in the Gates deck — "double Gates Ablaze is nuts."
- **Savage Punch**: "Nice little removal spell, can actually push a ton of damage as well if you have ferocious online" (ferocious = controlling a creature with 4+ power). Cheap fight spell in green.
- **Gore-Clan Rampager**: "This card is just so good every time." A 4/4 for 4 with trample that can be cast for surprise in response to blocks. One of the best uncommons in red-green.
- **Catacomb Sifter**: Scries every time a creature you control dies, and makes a 1/1 Eldrazi Scion token. Excellent in any black deck and fantastic Progenitor Mimic target.
- **Avatar of the Resolute (new inclusion)**: Two mana 3/2 reach trample, enters with a +1/+1 counter for each other creature you control with a counter on it. Strong curve-filler in any green counter deck.
- **Ob Nixilis's Cruelty**: "One of the best removal spells available." -3/-3 plus the creature can't be regenerated or returned from the graveyard. Splashable for 1 black.

## Pitfalls
- **Not playing land when Peros is active**: Kenji lost a game because he held a land (saving it for card synergy) while Peros was on the battlefield. "I should have played land number eight after drawing it because I had Peros on the battlefield — if I drew land number nine I would be able to triple activate the ability giving my creatures +3/+3 and because I had a trampler it became very relevant." Always play your land when Peros is active unless there is a very clear reason not to.
- **Playing Sword/Ordeal target too early on draw**: Playing the Adaptive (?) on turn 1 while on the draw "was a mistake" — on the draw it dies for free. "I shouldn't have played out the Adaptive on turn one when I shouldn't have." Play 1-drops that can't survive a turn on the draw more cautiously.
- **Keeping gate-heavy hands with zero spells**: Multiple times Kenji lost game 2-3 of a match because he kept a hand with 3 basics and no gates in a deck running 10 gates. "The odds of seeing three basics in back-to-back hands with no gates is so crazy bad." Still — he acknowledged it's a variance issue, not always a keep error. Mulligan hands that don't have a gate in a Gates deck if they also have weak early plays.
- **Gates deck vs. red aggro**: "Red aggro decks beat up on the Gates deck really well. Often times." Without Gates Ablaze, the Gates deck has very poor early interaction and the red deck curves under it.
- **Progenitor Mimic targeting your opponent's creatures**: Viable but risky (they can just kill their own creature to stop the chain). Better to target your own high-value creatures (Sifter, Colossus, Disciple, Angel).
- **Over-committing resources when opponent has mana up**: In multiple games, attacking into open mana with multiple cards in opponent's hand led to getting blown out by fight spells or tricks. "They have five cards in their hand — that's a lot of interaction."
- **Not taking Guild Summit early enough**: Kenji passed Guild Summit twice "to see if it wheels" and regretted it. "I got punished severely" the first time. Guild Summit is the engine of the Gates deck and should be taken early.
- **Miscalculating Primal Might pump amount**: Kenji overpaid by 1 mana on a Primal Might, which let his opponent survive at 1 life. "All because I pumped the uh with the Stable Master, gave them an out." Count damage precisely when using X-spells in combat.

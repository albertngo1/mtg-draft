# DFT (Aetherdrift) — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Green is the best color in the format by a significant margin; Kenji acknowledges he kept trying to avoid it and getting punished. Green gives access to the best chunky creatures (Hazard of the Dunes, Ketron(?), Green Belt Guardian, Regal Imperiosa(?) — a 3-mana 5/4) and combat tricks.
- White is the weakest color. Kenji says red-white (Boros) is "so weird" and needs to draw well consistently. Every win with a Boros deck is "effectively two wins" because they are so difficult to pilot.
- Cycling/discard is a real archetype (Artillerists + cycling package). It can go off when it gets going, but gets rolled by big green creatures if it can't assemble the engine.
- Maximum speed / Start your engines is the core format mechanic. Many cards scale dramatically once maximum speed is achieved.
- 4/4 toughness is noted as "the magic number" for relevant creatures in the format.
- Boros decks need to curve out and get lucky; a missed saddle-up or any stumble on mounts/vehicles is often game-losing.
- Green-black is a very strong color pair; gives access to Brood Wagon (top uncommon), graveyard synergies, and good creatures at every point on the curve.

## Archetypes
- **Esper artifacts (WUB):** Repurposing Bay as the engine card — a "Birthing Pod for artifacts" at 3 mana. Draft a ladder of 3-, 4-, 5-, 6-mana artifacts; sacrifice up the chain. Kenji explicitly says "this is a good Esper artifact shell with Repurposing Bay in it." Requires good stabilization before the combo fires; Pack Doll Terror(?) is the payoff — drains opponent whenever you play an artifact. Won 7 games with this build.
- **Boros aggro (RW):** Low-curve mounts and vehicles. Aim for 9+ mounts and vehicles total for Push the Limit consideration. Cloudspire Captain (?) is the key uncommon — a 2-mana creature with a pump/anthem effect. Gliders, Gilded Goa(?), and Spike Spitter as the backbone. Three Cloudspire Captains is the dream. Push the Limit may not be worth running unless you naturally hit 9+ mounts/vehicles with good fixing.
- **Blue-green value / big stuff (UG):** Transit Mage (?) searches for 4/4+ toughness artifacts. Seeker Serpent(?) as top-end finisher (activate to make huge, unblockable(?), attack). Scurry Foot(?) generates tokens. Splash red or black for removal. Typical endgame: jam 5/4+ creatures, use bounce spells and removal to protect.
- **Red-blue cycling (UR):** Artillerist x4 as the core — whenever you cycle/discard, deal 1 damage. Moo(?)(vehicle that loots at max speed), Skyray(?) as flyers that generate speed. Wreck Remover (4-mana remove, also cycles), Ironclad (1-mana cycler). Transit Mage can grab Hold Drifter(?) and Wreck Remover as targets. Deck wins by pinging out opponents. Vulnerable to big green creatures.
- **Green-red big stuff (RG):** Regal Imperiosa(?) (3-mana 5/4) as the star. Molt Tender for ramp. Hazard of the Dunes, Ketron(?). Seat of Arma / Path Breaker Ibex(?) as overrun-style finishers. Bounce Off and Plow Through(?) as combat tricks. Kenji noted this is a powerful direction but lost to fast decks.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Repurposing Bay + Pack Doll Terror(?):** Sacrifice Pack Doll Terror to find the next artifact on the curve; each artifact cast drains opponent. Chain: Thopterist makes a Thopter → sack Thopter → grab 3-drop artifact → cast → drain → sack again → grab 4-drop. Bay wins games where you've stabilized, loses when behind.
- **Artillerist x4 + cycling package:** Quad Artillerist means each cycle or discard deals 4 damage total. Pair with Moo(?)(loot at max speed) to draw into more cyclers. Transit Mage grabs Hold Drifter + Wreck Remover as targets.
- **Seat of Arma + Path Breaker Ibex(?):** Both pump your board to match the largest power among creatures you control. Using Seat of Arma first then Ibex(?)'s trample trigger creates a massive trample attack.
- **Guidelight Matrix(?) + vehicle/artifact package:** Matrix can animate a vehicle (Path Cruiser(?) etc.) as a "creature" to block or crew, doubling utility.
- **Serpent Ace(?) + Crew / Max Speed:** 2-mana 3/3 that gets huge once activated, makes it very hard to lose once online.

## Card tips
- **Serpent Ace(?):** One of the best blue uncommons/commons. Activate early and it just takes over games.
- **Brood Wagon:** Probably the best uncommon in the format. Has it all — makes tokens, triggers on entry, strong stats. "Just as good as Ketron(?) if not better."
- **Hazard of the Dunes:** Better than first impression. Kenji says it rivals Regal Imperiosa(?) for best green card in packs.
- **Pack Doll Terror(?):** The repeatable drain effect with artifacts is the real engine. Getting two of these online is "drain 8 in my hand." These are the MVP of the Esper shell.
- **Bounce Off:** Always-playable blue interaction. Hits vehicles mid-crew attempt for a free "kill."
- **Spectacular Pile-Up(?):** White board wipe (AoE); people heavily avoid white, so you can often 5th pick a cycling wrath. Cycling makes it always playable.
- **Flood the Engine(?):** Blue instant with multiple modes (can tap a vehicle, good interaction). Consistently useful.
- **Push the Limit:** Needs 9+ total mounts and vehicles to be reliably good. Don't force it.
- **Katchon(?):** Green rare/mythic — Kenji says "if your opponent resolves Katchon versus you it's just like is that the end?" Context suggests this is a large creature with game-ending impact.
- **Gilded Goa(?):** Good red 2-drop that generates a Treasure, then becomes a 4/4 when saddled. Excellent Boros curve card.
- **Road Rage(?):** Strong red combat trick; scales with number of vehicles in deck.
- **Collision Course:** Blue instant, good interaction especially when opponent has artifacts.
- **Mult Tender(?):** Green 1-drop, best in green-black due to graveyard synergies.

## Pitfalls
- **Green avoidance:** Kenji explicitly said he kept trying to dodge green and got punished. Green is open and powerful; draft it when signals allow.
- **Repurposing Bay without a stabilization plan:** Bay requires you to be alive long enough to chain. Don't draft it without a plan for the first 4-6 turns.
- **Boros with no mount/vehicle for turn 2-3:** The deck collapses with no way to saddle up the early creatures. A hand with Ox (mount, needs saddle) and no other creatures is a mulligan.
- **Cycling deck vs. big green:** 4x Artillerist wins by pinging but gets rolled by 4/4+ tramples. Need some answer.
- **Running Push the Limit under 9 mounts+vehicles:** Kenji had 9 total in his Boros deck and still didn't run it. Don't include it speculatively.
- **Mole(?):** Kenji had terrible luck hitting land off of Mole(?) (milling lands, missing creatures). He said "I would never cut them because of something like that, but that is always extremely frustrating."
- **Over-tapping to activate abilities before attacks:** Kenji noted several "I did not mean to tap like that" moments with Bay and other activated abilities. Hold priority carefully.

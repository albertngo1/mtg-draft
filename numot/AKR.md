# AKR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?). Set is Amonkhet Remastered — a mashup of Amonkhet and Hour of Devastation cards.

## Format speed / meta read

- **Fast aggro format dominated by red-white.** Kenji returns repeatedly to the conclusion that RW "beats down" and you either curve out or you lose. Multiple opponents show up with nearly identical RW aggro lists.
- **AKR is significantly faster than standalone Amonkhet** because Hour of Devastation's removal and bombs are mixed in but the aggro cards from AKH are still abundant. More wraths exist (Sweltering Suns, Anger of the Gods, Hour of Revelation, Wrath of God, "there are like 10 different wrath effects, no joke — like four of them in red-white"), but wraths with cycling are fine in aggro (you cycle them when you don't need them; they're "goddamn insane" when you do).
- **Glorybringer is the defining rare.** Kenji keeps this in or near his first picks whenever possible. "I think I figured out the format: you just curve out into Glorybringer. Just tap out basically every turn until turn 5 where you play your ringer."
- **The Zombie archetype (white-black) is the strongest synergy deck.** The combination of Wayward Servant, Lord of the Accursed, Vizier of the Anointed, and Sunscorch Champion drains the opponent out while going wide. Described as "our deck is insane" and went 7-0.
- **Deserts matter more than they look.** Sand Strangler and Wall of Forgotten Pharaohs are unplayable without deserts; always pick up basic deserts to enable them. "There's a chance I take this desert and get one or two more." Somebody was taking deserts very high in one draft, leaving none.
- **Trials + Cartouches are the other synergy axis.** The Trial/Cartouche loop (bouncing the trial when a cartouche enters) is very powerful — opponents bounced a single blue trial 3 times to go infinite on value. Having even 2–3 cartouches makes trials considerably better.
- **Best-of-one punishes mana problems hard.** Several losses traced to flooding on plains with no mountains in hand (or the reverse). AKR's fixing is primarily Evolving Wilds and basic deserts; mismatched mana is a recurring issue.

## Archetypes

### Red-White — Glorybringer Curve-Out
- **Gameplan:** Play 1-2-3 and then Glorybringer on turn 5. Every card should have exert synergy, cycling, or both. The deck kills opponents who stumble and loses to decks that kill Glorybringer cheaply.
- **Key cards:** Glorybringer (the chase rare; first pick basically always), Gust Walker (2/2 flying first-strike when exerted; best common), Fan Bearer (2-drop that taps their blocker; "the card is good"), Honored Crop-Captain (good two-drop), Ahn-Crop Crasher (3/2 haste exert that makes a blocker unable to block), Cartouche of Zeal (cheap haste + toughness reduction), Combat tricks: Blur of Blades (instant-speed -2/-1 ping; good in a pinch), Brute Strength (+1/+1 trample), Impeccable Timing (removal), Magma Spray (exile, great vs recursive creatures).
- **Curve is the gameplan:** "I didn't play the beta — I just wanted to wait till the game fully releases." Same philosophy with picks: don't pick 5-drops early when you need 2-drops.
- **Cycling matters:** Sweltering Suns, Cast Out, and Forsake the Worldly are all fine in this deck because they cycle when dead. Sweltering Suns specifically is a 3-for-1 when opponent is the aggro deck.

### White-Black — Zombie Tribal
- **Gameplan:** Build a board of zombies, drain opponents out with Wayward Servant triggers and Lord of the Accursed menace buffs, and grind with Vizier of the Anointed for card advantage.
- **Key cards:** Wayward Servant (2-drop that drains 1 each time any zombie ETBs — stack multiples), Lord of the Accursed (gives all zombies menace, activated +1/+1 counter ability), Vizier of the Anointed (draws a zombie when another zombie ETBs; "both are really good" alongside Sunscorch Champion), Sunscorch Champion (lifelink counters deck; "does a lot of work"), Those Who Serve (2/4 for 3; excellent blocker with zombie synergy), Binding Mummy (2/2 zombie that taps a permanent when another zombie ETBs), Festering Mummy (1/1 that -1/-1s something on death — fills the zombie count).
- **Strength:** Extremely powerful when the synergies fire. "Our deck is insane" — built one with 2 Wayward Servants, Lord of the Accursed, and Vizier of the Anointed, won 7 games.
- **Tip:** Anointer Priest (non-zombie) doesn't fit well unless you have a dedicated embalm subtheme. Cut it for actual zombie bodies.

### Red-Green — Exert / Big Creatures
- One VOD showed a mono-red splash green build. Kenji rates green as "other way around — white is good, green is okay." Useful as a secondary: Nissa Planeswalker splash (?), Scaled Behemoth, other big threats. Not a first-choice color.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Trial of Zeal + any Cartouche:** Returning Trial of Zeal each time a cartouche enters lets you destroy a creature repeatedly. "Two trials of ambition and five cartouches? Yeah, that seems fun." Stack several cartouches and you get effectively unlimited removal triggers.
- **Glorybringer + Exert anything:** Glorybringer's exert ability deals 4 to a non-Dragon and untaps your other exerted creatures (it doesn't, actually — the untap is generic exert). The key is that tapping it and killing their blocker makes your other exerted creatures effectively free attackers next turn.
- **Wayward Servant + Binding Mummy (or any zombie ETB):** Each Wayward Servant drains 1 per zombie ETB. Two Servants = 2 life lost per zombie you play. Combine with Lord of the Accursed menace grants for a drain clock opponents can't block.
- **Vizier of the Anointed + Sunscorch Champion:** Champion's flicker-style ability (tap, gains life, puts 1/1 counter) can be chained with Vizier to refill your hand with zombie cards each trigger.
- **Cycling spells + Magma Spray/Blur of Blades:** Having removal that also cycles when you flood is the core AKR card advantage engine. Kenji identifies Cycling as specifically enabling "Sweltering Suns in aggro — if you don't need it, cycle it; when it's good, it's goddamn insane."
- **Fan Bearer + 3/3+ attacker:** Tapping their only good blocker end of their turn clears a lane. At 3+ mana, you can double-spell: play a creature then activate Fan Bearer, leaving one tap-target without an answer.

## Card tips

- **Glorybringer is the best card in the format** and should be forced into every RW or RX build that can support it. "You just curve out into Glorybringer."
- **Black Cartouche of Ambition** swings games. In an aggro format killing a creature and slapping lifelink onto your own attacker is frequently 6+ life swings per turn. "The black cartouche swings games in this format so hard."
- **Green cartouche vs blue cartouche:** Green (reach/+1/+1) is better as a splash if you have the trial; blue trial ("slow" and bouncing it is "too slow" in aggro) was rated the worst trial.
- **Struggle // Survive:** Instant-speed fight with aftermath +1/+1 counter cycle is the best green removal. "Good removal" — first pick in most RG/GW packs.
- **Sand Strangler** is only good with deserts; with 2+ deserts it's a 3/3 that kills a creature = premium; with 0 deserts it's unplayable. Pick up basic deserts proactively.
- **Magma Spray over Open Fire** when you have recursive creatures to fight (embalm, aftermath). Otherwise both are fine; Magma Spray always answered the Anointer Priest problems.
- **Pathmaker Initiate:** Niche but real — makes a creature you control unblockable for a turn. Used to sneak in Burning Fist Minotaur and Glorybringer for decisive damage.
- **Burning Fist Minotaur:** First-strike 2/1 that can grow; "going to solo them next turn" is a frequent comment. Good with Cartouche of Zeal giving haste.
- **Shefet Dunes / Sheltered Thicket / Irrigated Farmland:** The cycling dual-type lands are premium picks — they enable Struggle and count as deserts for Sand Strangler. "Get them when they were on sale" is the sentiment.
- **Monuments (red, black):** "The black and red monuments are unplayable." Red monument is ranked "second best" sarcastically by a viewer; Kenji confirms "sure, sure, we'll go with that." Only the white (Oketra's) and green (Rhonas's) monuments are worth running.
- **Initiates' Companion (green):** "All of the other colors have better two-drops — even green has better two-drops right? We have the Ronises already." Below-rate and cuttable.
- **Ruthless Sniper (blue-black):** "Very very fun" in a cycling/discard deck; noted as a consideration but not pursued in these drafts.

## Pitfalls

- **Curving into 5-drop with no red mana:** Multiple losses came from having Glorybringer stranded because of 4 Plains and 0 Mountains. Running at least 6 Mountains when RW is mandatory.
- **Trials without cartouches:** "The problem with the trials in best-of-one, especially this format — they're just too slow. If you have a lot of early removal, sure, but spending your turn playing a trial — often not gonna be good enough." Running trials without 3+ cartouches to rebuy them is a trap.
- **Fan Bearer on defense only:** Players forget Fan Bearer taps their own creature. Tapping your own large creature to activate is wasted tempo; always point it at their best creature, not yours.
- **Splashing green without deserts:** "I need to run like eight forests — I don't like it." Going green splash requires fixing that isn't always available.
- **Evolving Wilds in aggressive RW:** "Oh, the pain of a tap land in your red-white deck." Tap lands cost you a full turn of aggression. Only run them when the fixing is essential for your off-color bomb.
- **Keeping 1-land hands into Glorybringer:** "I risked it for the biscuit" — Kenji mulliganed into too many Plains-only hands and died before casting his Glorybringer. Keep hands with at least 2 lands and 1 action card.

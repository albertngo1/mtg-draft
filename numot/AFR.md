# AFR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 3 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set; one VOD had no captions). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Red-black is the default power lane.** All three available drafts end up in RB or very close to it; Kenji calls it "your classic red-black" and jokes he invented the archetype. The commons (Vampire Spawn, Dragon Fire, Grim Bounty, Swarming Goblins) are deep and consistent enough to 7-0 with.
- **Best-of-one rewards proactive curving.** Decks that curve 2→3→4 with relevant bodies win before the opponent stabilizes. Kenji went 7-0 in one session and 6-2 in another with this approach.
- **Format is medium speed** — games can be decided by turn 4–5 if one player floods or the other hits a large bomb (Earth Cult Elemental at 4 mana, a 6/6 black dragon, Mordenkainen). Not as fast as some; a little removal and a 2/3 body like Vampire Spawn gives enough time to stabilize.
- **Matchmaking bug note:** Kenji repeatedly queues into the same opponent back-to-back on Arena at the time; he recommends pausing before re-queuing after a match if you want to avoid this.

## Archetypes

### Red-Black — Sacrifice / Treasure Value
- **Gameplan:** Build a board of medium bodies, generate treasures via Deadly Dispute and sac outlets, clear the way with efficient removal, then convert small advantages into wins.
- **Key commons/uncommons:** Vampire Spawn (2/3, drains 2; "literally everything you could ever want" — take every copy), Swarming Goblins (token maker with tribal upside — "better than Horde Robber"), Dragon's Fire (instant, damage equal to a dragon you control; "normally I take Magic Missile over Dragon's Fire but if I already have one Magic Missile…"), Grim Bounty (sorcery removal; 2-for-1 with Treasure), Warlock Class (enchantment, over-performs "so much more than people expect"), Battle Cry Goblin (haste lord for go-wide moments), Treasure-producing 2-drops like Hobgoblin Bandit Lord (?).
- **Splash options:** Kenji splashed Mordenkainen (double-blue) in one draft using 2 copies of Grim Bounty plus a Temple of the Dragon Queen(?) for fixing. Concluded it's viable with enough dual lands/Treasures.
- **Sac synergy:** Deadly Dispute + Feign Death form the core loop. "With Deadly Dispute and [treasures] they both make treasures." Price of Loyalty was a one-of high-roll top-deck in two games.

### Blue-X (secondary — less defined from these VODs)
- Kenji was steered into blue twice when he opened Morton's Kind (Mordenkainen?) but only as a splash target. Blue as a main color was not observed in the available transcripts.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Mordenkainen + any dragon:** Mordenkainen's dog gets enormous as it copies the highest power on the board. Combined with a Black Dragon or Earth Cult Elemental, the dog reaches 12/12+ territory quickly. "Imagine a 12/12 dog and yet it just gets blocked by a little devil boy."
- **Vampire Spawn + equipment (Morning Star):** Trample on Vampire Spawn plus the drain-2 trigger creates large life-swing turns that close games. Equip before attacks when they can't trade profitably.
- **Swarming Goblins + Battle Cry Goblin:** Multiple hasty tokens trigger the battle cry lord on the attack. "Double battle cry goblin" is called out as a real game-ending sequence.
- **Ogre (Hoarding Ogre?) + Grim Bounty:** Ogre adds a sac outlet and Grim Bounty produces a Treasure — chaining them generates value while clearing threats.
- **Dragon's Fire + any Dragon in play:** Becomes unconditional removal for 1R. Having a Black Dragon in play while casting Dragon's Fire is "just four damage" (=the dragon's power) against anything. Prioritize if already running 2+ dragons.

## Card tips

- **Vampire Spawn is the best black common by a wide margin.** 2/3 drain-2 is undervalued by the pod; Kenji wheeled it at pick 8 in one draft. "Taking every Vampire Spawn we see because that card is great — a 2/3, it's a vampire, and it drains for two — literally everything you could ever want. And it's a common."
- **Warlock Class is premium removal.** It's categorically removal and a win condition in one package; Kenji rates it "third, maybe second behind Bounty" among black cards but plays it over many alternatives due to how much it over-performs.
- **Magic Missile vs Dragon's Fire:** Magic Missile (?) preferred slightly overall for flexibility; Dragon's Fire is better when you have 2+ dragons. Having the first Magic Missile in hand is the cut-off: "normally I take Magic Missile over Dragon's Fire, but if I already have one Magic Missile, I don't know — who knows."
- **Check for Traps (?):** A sideboard-able enchantment-hate card that Kenji maindecked. "I like this card, it's good." He waited multiple turns to get maximum value before casting it (hitting 3 enchantments in one game).
- **Feign Death:** The one-mana protection spell; he picks it up but not urgently. "I do want to pick up a Feign Death eventually."
- **Classes (most) are bad:** "Most of the classes are pretty bad" — only Warlock Class consistently appeared as a main-deck card across transcripts. Bard Class and Rogue Class were mentioned as unplayed or narrow.
- **Hobgoblin Bandit Lord (?):** Low-end two-drop for RB; not exciting but fills the curve.
- **Zalto / Hobgoblin Captain (?):** "Zalto sucks," "I don't really care for Hobgoblin Captain" — avoid as picks.
- **Equipment density matters:** Running 2+ Morning Stars enables Armory Veteran (?) as a legitimate 2-drop. Otherwise cut it.
- **16 lands is the right number** for RB with Treasures. Grim Bounty and Deadly Dispute generate enough mana that 17 is frequently one too many.
- **Long Rest** was called out once as "very strong" even though slow — relevant in control-ish builds.

## Pitfalls

- **Playing into Talisman of Hierarchy (?) too aggressively:** One loss traced back to attacking into their Talisman, which drained for 2 each activation while Kenji was low on blockers. "I was playing into it."
- **Not leaving blockers:** "I got super aggressive and greedy — I needed to leave back a creature." Keeping an attacker home when opponent has mana open for tricks/sac can flip close games.
- **Overloading on removal, under-loading on threats:** "I wouldn't call my deck a beatdown deck, especially if I'm not playing the Valor Singers." In one build Kenji cut all the Valor Singers and regretted not having enough pressure.
- **Dragon's Fire timing:** Firing it early on a non-dragon turn is a mistake; save it until a dragon is in play for full damage. "And it all came down to turn three when I didn't need to Dragon's Fire and I did — that was my mistake."
- **Mordenkainen as a greedy splash:** Powerful but double-blue is punishing. Only splice it in when you have the Temple + 2 Grim Bounties or equivalent fixing. Don't force it with fewer than 3 reliable blue sources.
- **Matchmaking instaque:** Waiting 30–60 seconds between games reduces the chance of queuing into the same deck you just lost to and letting them adjust.

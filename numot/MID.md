# MID — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- **Day/Night** flips matter for tempo; Kenji frequently plans around not triggering nightfall unnecessarily ("I'll let them flip it to nighttime and just pass").
- **Coven** is a real deckbuilding constraint in Green-White and Blue-White — you need creatures of three different powers; Kenji adjusts cuts to ensure coven is achievable by turn three.
- **Farmer (Sungold Farmhand)** is repeatedly identified as the best fixing card and one of the best cards in green-white splash decks — "if these Hoarders were Farmers I don't think I could lose." Absence of Farmers makes white splashes much worse.
- **Rise of the Ants** (?) is called out as "too good" and the title card of the green-black ants deck — Kenji actively builds around it.
- **Organ Hoarder** is "like a draw two" in the self-mill deck; but Farmer is better for curve and fixing.
- Bo3 drafts let Kenji sideboard more actively; blue-red spells is a real archetype you sideboard graveyard hate against (Graveyard Lantern).
- Four-power creatures dominate combat; the format is "so big at four fours."
- Interlopers (Spirit tokens from Delver-style cards?) are a real evasion threat in blue-red; Plummet is a viable sideboard card.

## Archetypes
- **Green-Black self-mill / ants ("Ants Too Good"):** Rise of the Ants as the dream card — produces two 1/1 tokens each turn and grows; Organ Hoarder (mill 4, pick up a card with the best type) as the card selection engine; Shadow Beast Sighting (?) (self-mill + flash creature) for interaction; Locked in the Cemetery (?) as cheap graveyard disruption / interaction; Celestus (?) ("the lard" — activated ability to flip day/night); Clear Shot for removal; Geist Wave (?) for bounce. Splashing the Graph Keeper (?), a blue rare that mills opponents, is possible with Organ Hoarders for fixing. Key insight: the deck wants Farmer for fixing more than a second Organ Hoarder.
- **Green-White coven / midrange ("Coven Mayhem"):** Cigarda, Fumblefoot (?) (legendary that lets you search your library for a creature card each time a creature dies — Kenji says "Sagarda" throughout) as the engine rare; Harvest Tide Sentinel (?) (repeating tap-down ability, untaps at coven) is taken "every single time"; Search Party Captain (2/2 for 2 that reduces spell costs when you have four or more creatures) as the early play; Ritual of Hope as a finisher; Homestead Courage / Defiant Mending (?) for cheap coven-enabling pump; Borrowed Time for enchantment removal; Clear Shot for fights; Dual Craft Trainer (?) for pump. Rule: "tap out turn two for sentry, turn three I can use the bolt." Kenji runs 18 lands when accumulating many four-drops.
- **Blue-White tempo / disturb ("Bo1 Makes Me Sad"):** Falcon Abomination as the anchor three-drop (3/3 flyer that taps something on ETB); Scab Wrangler (?) (2/2 that makes a 2/2 Zombie when you sacrifice a creature); Silversmith (?) as a two-drop; triple Flip the Switch (?) (instant-speed counter + card draw) as the control package; Sunset Revelry (?) for life + tokens; Farmhand for fixing; Search Party Captain for curve; Memory Deluge as the late-game draw. Kenji notes this build has zero disturb/flashback intentionally in BO1 — just solid threats plus counters.
- **Blue-Red spells:** Opposed archetype (Kenji plays against it frequently); runs Seize the Storm (storm spell), Alchemist / Festival Crasher / Infernal Grasp-style cards; sideboard Graveyard Lantern to exile flashback cards.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Cigarda + Dual Craft Trainer + Harvest Tide Sentry:** Search for a creature with Cigarda's trigger, play it, immediately get a Harvest Tide Sentry attacking — any creature dying (sac, trade, removal) can chain-dig your library.
- **Rise of the Ants + Celestus (the Lard):** The Ants trigger each turn gives tokens; Celestus forces day/night transitions; when you have both online plus a self-mill engine you generate tokens and life each turn ("ants ants ants ants").
- **Organ Hoarder in self-mill:** Mills four, then you pick the best card type — in a graveyard-matters deck this is "like a draw two" since milled creatures become reanimation targets.
- **Shadow Beast Sighting (?) + locked-in-the-cemetery (?) interaction:** The flashback/disturb cards are non-bos with Locked in the Cemetery if they share a card type; Kenji notes his lock-in effects get awkward once he has too many flashback cards since he can't lock in cards he wants back.
- **Graph Keeper splash:** Blue rare that mills opponents; with Evolving Wilds + Organ Hoarders for fixing, splash is viable in the green-black deck.
- **Ritual of Hope with coven:** Kenji uses it as a combat trick but also as a pseudo-anthem that wins races; the +1/+1 to all creatures when you have coven is enormous.
- **Flip the Switch + untapped creatures:** In the Blue-White deck, Flip the Switch (instant-speed tap-and-bounce or counter?) rewards passing with open mana; always hold it up rather than main-phasing it.
- **Borrowed Time + Cigarda:** Cigarda's trigger is "dies" so Borrowed Time being destroyed returns the exiled creature — only relevant in removal-heavy opponents but worth tracking.

## Card tips
- **Organ Hoarder:** Take it over most things early; mills four then lets you pick the best card type. Wants 18 lands in green-black because you need black for it.
- **Harvest Tide Sentry:** Kenji says "I take every single sentry I see" in Green-White; the repeating tap-down that turns on at coven is oppressive.
- **Clear Shot:** "Removal is not easy in green-white" — pick it high.
- **Search Party Captain:** Lowers spell costs but is weaker on the draw; consider cutting some for sideboard games where opponents have interactive cards.
- **Rise of the Ants:** Title-worthy card — the deck is literally named after it. Pick it early in green-black, build around it.
- **Dual Craft Trainer (?):** Pump + trigger engine; Kenji leads with it to get value before playing Cigarda.
- **Ritual of Hope:** Better than Bramble Armor as a finisher; the all-creatures pump wins combat completely. Pick it over less synergistic creatures in coven decks.
- **Falcon Abomination:** Taken as the "too good to pass" three-drop in Blue-White; tapping something and making a 1/1 Zombie is immediate value.
- **Borrowed Time:** Priority removal in Green-White that handles anything non-land; curves well under the four-drops.
- **Farmhand (Sungold Farmhand):** Fixing + recurring land searching; Kenji laments not finding Farmers in multiple drafts — they upgrade every green-white build significantly.
- **Plummet (sideboard):** Bring in one vs blue-red or any deck with Interlopers / large flyers; "I definitely should bring in one seeing at least two interlopers."
- **Evolving Wilds:** Take it for fixing if splashing; also enables Locked in the Cemetery in the self-mill deck (sacrifice it, trigger landfall-style effects).
- **Larger Zombie:** Underrated in the self-mill deck — comes back from the graveyard and provides a threat that survives most removal.

## Pitfalls
- **No Farmers in a green-white splash build:** "Just having one Farmer would be such a huge upgrade." Without Farmers, white splash cards get much weaker and the deck loses consistency.
- **Missing land drops in Bo1:** "The rules of this game: be on the play, curve out. We didn't do either of those." Kenji is explicit that missing land drops + not having the play = very bad.
- **Coven with all same-power creatures:** All creatures having the same power (e.g., multiple 3/3s) breaks coven. Kenji cuts cards specifically to maintain three different powers.
- **Roost (Gavony Dawnguard? / Moorland Rescuer?):** Kenji says he won't play the Roost unless he has enough flashback and disturb enablers — needs at least four or five cheap recurring cards, not four at seven or eight mana.
- **Commando (?):** Fine sideboard card in Green-White; Kenji cuts it from the main when he has enough sentries, then sideboards it in for creature-dense matchups.
- **Self-mill decking yourself:** The green-black deck can deck itself if games go long; Turn to Earth (?) and careful graveyard management are necessary. Running 41 cards in game three vs a mill opponent is specifically called out as correct.
- **Day/Night timing:** Triggering nightfall prematurely (by casting two spells on your turn) flips your own creatures to their weaker side. Kenji consciously skips second-main-phase spells to hold day.
- **Borrowed Time in a fight/duel-heavy meta:** Opponents running four Duel for Dominance can re-fight your Borrowed Time target; bounce effects (Geist Wave?) mitigate this by resetting their fight-spell targets.
- **Search Party Captain on the draw:** The mana discount matters less on the draw when you're already behind on board; Kenji sides some out vs fast opponents.

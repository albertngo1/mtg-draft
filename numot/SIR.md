# SIR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
Shadows over Innistrad Remastered is a midrange-to-synergy format with multiple distinct archetypes. Key mechanics: **madness** (discard outlets + madness cards), **werewolves/transform**, **delirium** (4+ card types in graveyard), **self-mill/blue-green graveyard**, **clues** (investigate), **flashback**, and **emerge**. Format speeds: Rakdos madness aggro is fast and punishing when it comes together; green-white humans/werewolves has great midrange curves; blue-green self-mill is a grind deck. Kenji notes Rakdos madness was his least favorite to play against because "it feels so weird/bad when your opponent goes two-drop madness enabler into the perfect madness curve out." 17Lands data cited in one VOD: Azorius ~57%, Dimir ~47.7% (lowest), Rakdos ~56%, Gruul ~55%, Selesnya ~57% (highest), Orzhov ~55%, Golgari ~53%, Simic ~50%. Dimir played only ~4,000 times vs. Rakdos ~17,000 — strong signal that blue-black is underpowered.

## Archetypes
- **Rakdos Madness (RB)**: Best when assembled with cheap discard outlets (Olivia's Dragoon, Ravenous Bloodseeker, Furyblade Vampire) plus madness payoffs (Alchemist's Greeting, Gisa's Bidding, Weirded Vampire). The key is getting BOTH halves — enablers alone or payoffs alone underperform. Kenji says "I had five discard effects but only two madness cards" as the failure mode. Blood Mist is "surprisingly playable" with all the combat tricks (Borrowed Hostility, Rush of Adrenaline, Uncaged Fury). Turn 2 discard-outlet into turn 3 madness creature into Blood Mist double-strike is the "feels so bad to play against" curve. 
- **Green-White Humans/Werewolves (GW)**: Deathcap Cultivator as ramp/fixing; Hamlet Captain for pump; Tireless Tracker for card advantage; Village Messenger as aggressive 1-drop that flips fast with Neglected Heirloom. Moonlight Hunt and Clear Shot as removal — "too good to not prioritize." Pack Guardian as flash threat/combat trick. Kenji ended up with 10 humans + 7 wolves/werewolves and called the deck "really solid." Neglected Heirloom on Village Messenger is a legitimate win condition: a 5/5 first-striking Menace creature by turn 4.
- **Blue-Green Self-Mill (UG)**: Manic Scribe as the premier win condition ("manic scribe is greatest wincon of all time") — mills 3 on ETB, then 3 more each upkeep once you have delirium. Forbidden Alchemy for self-mill and card selection. Grapple with the Past for graveyard recursion and digging. Epitaph Golem to prevent decking yourself. Nissa's Renewal / Nod of the Bone(?) for life gain to stay alive. Obsessive Skinner grows with delirium. Gnarlwood Dryad as cheap deathtouch delirium card. Fleeting Memories + clues = extra mill. Graph Mole(?) as clue-synergy threat. Groundskeeper for land recursion. Laboratory Brute for self-mill. Kenji says this deck "feels like playing a constructed deck in limited when you get Emrakul in this style" — Emrakul, the Promised End is the ultimate payoff if you can assemble it.
- **Blue-Red Spells**: Galvanic Bombardment as cheap removal; Alchemist's Greeting as madness outlet; Rise from the Tides as win condition (zombie army). Take Inventory for card selection. Kenji considered it but ended up in UG instead. Thing in the Ice as a potential reset button, but noted it's "kind of bad for me because [opponent's] Scab and Occultist are horrors."
- **Mono-Blue Mill**: Kenji ran this once — double Rise from the Tides, Manic Scribes, Spontaneous Mutations, Drag Under, Take Inventory, Pour Over the Pages. "16 land mono blue double rise from the tides." Results were mediocre (2-3) due to flood/screw variance and not having spontaneous mutations in time.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Manic Scribe + Delirium**: Once you have 4 card types in graveyard, Scribe mills 3 on each opponent's upkeep — stack two scribes for 6 per upkeep, easily milling a 40-card deck in 6-7 turns.
- **Forbidden Alchemy + Manic Scribe**: Alchemy mills 4 (3 to graveyard + choose one) and gets you closer to delirium; then replaying it with black flashback keeps the engine going.
- **Grapple with the Past + Epitaph Golem**: Grapple brings Epitaph Golem back from graveyard; Golem then shuffles your graveyard into your library preventing self-mill loss and recycling the engine.
- **Silent Departure on own Manic Scribe**: Bounce your own scribe, replay for another ETB mill trigger — "departure on my own scribe, replay it, mill them 3 and then 6 on upkeep from two scribes together."
- **Olivia's Dragoon + Blood Mist**: Discard a land to give Dragoon flying; Blood Mist then gives double strike — a 4/2 flying double-striking trampler "sheesh" for 2+1 mana at instant speed.
- **Uncaged Fury + Borrowed Hostility**: Stack tricks for surprise burst. Kenji was hit for lethal by exactly uncaged fury + bounce (Silent Departure) + a third spell — "they had the fury plus bounce and I got punished."
- **Deranged Welp (?) Menace + Blood Mist**: Two-power Menace + Blood Mist in combat is very hard to block profitably. The deck "had so many of the good enablers" and the tricks made it work.
- **Tireless Tracker + Game Trail / lands**: Each land play after tracker makes a clue; sac clues to draw. With deathcap cultivator ramp you can make 2-3 clues in a turn.
- **Moonlight Hunt + multiple wolves/werewolves**: Each wolf/werewolf you control adds +1/+1 to the fight target — with 5-6 wolves you fight for +5/+5, which is a 2-for-1 removal spell.

## Card tips
- **Olivia's Dragoon**: "One of the reasons why Olivia's dragon is so good is because it's two mana and a free discard outlet" — premium pick in any black deck, especially madness.
- **Manic Scribe**: Take it early. "Manic scribe is greatest wincon of all time." Even as a defensive body it's useful; once you have delirium it ends games fast.
- **Galvanic Bombardment**: "Blue red spells being the bees knees" — Bombardment gets better with each copy you play (scales to 1/2/3 damage). Take multiples.
- **Clear Shot**: Kenji calls it "great, often a two-for-one." One of the best non-rare removal spells in the format. Pick it highly.
- **Incendiary Flow**: 2-mana deal 3 exile if creature — "completely forgot about that one, that's a good pickup." Exile clause matters against recursion decks.
- **Tireless Tracker**: "One of the better green cards in the format." Synergizes with Moonlight Hunt, game trail, and basically everything in RG.
- **Epitaph Golem**: "First Epitaph Golem is important; having two is not even that bad." Two different card types (artifact + creature) helps delirium; second copy is a safety net. Common in remastered (was uncommon in original).
- **Neglected Heirloom**: Pairs perfectly with cheap flip creatures — equip for 1, transform, suddenly a first strike/double strike equipment. Take it when you're in RG werewolves.
- **Village Messenger**: "If the opponent doesn't have anything to do against it [on turn 1] you know... it flips into a 2/2 Menace which generally hits them for quite a bit of damage." Aggressive 1-drop, can snowball hard.
- **Hamlet Captain**: "10 humans" were in the deck — Captain pumps them all. Synergizes with white-green human count from wolves overlapping.
- **Spider Spawning** (opponent's): "Spider spawning deck was one of the best things you could be doing" — can run more than 40 cards if you have enough instants/sorceries. Watch for it as a late-pack signal.
- **Kindly Strangers**: "Really strong delirium card, pick 1 pack 1 quality for sure." Take it over generic cards when you're in delirium strategies.
- **Sever the Bloodline**: "This used to be a rare too by the way, now it's uncommon" — devastating exile-all spell, watch for it.

## Pitfalls
- **Forcing madness without the payoffs**: "I had five discard effects but only two madness cards." The enablers without payoffs is just a mediocre aggro deck. Must draft both sides. The missing card is usually Alchemist's Greeting.
- **Not discarding the mountain for the Blood Mist / Dragoon attack**: Kenji says "that might have been the mistake that game — not discarding the mountain" when he had two 2-drops to play and a Dragoon that could have become a 4/2 double-strike trampler.
- **Dimir (Blue-Black)**: Lowest win rate in the format (~47.7%) by a significant margin. "Blue black is just really not a well supported" pairing. Avoid unless forced.
- **Playing Manic Scribe when opponent has delirium**: "One of those scenarios where playing out a scribe might not actually be good because if we play out a scribe and they hit delirium then that gains Menace" — be careful in mirrors or when opponents are also filling graveyards.
- **Not having enough grapples in the mill deck**: "Really do needed that one more grapple with the past" — without recursion, the mill deck decks itself and loses. Two-three grapples is the target.
- **Undervaluing Epitaph Golem**: Easy to dismiss as slow but it's the insurance policy for the entire self-mill strategy. "Having two is not even bad as well" since the second one can recycle the first.
- **Playing into Spider Spawning**: In Rakdos vs. green decks with self-mill, watch for Spider Spawning. "Even if we draw a mountain I don't think we have a way to come back from this one" once they flood the board with spider tokens.

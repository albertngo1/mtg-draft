# SNC — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
Streets of New Capenna is a 3-color (shard/wedge) format dominated by connive and shield counters. The format rewards two-drops heavily — Kenji says "that my friends is the power of twos" after winning with an Azorius connive deck. Connive (draw, then discard) is the core mechanic enabling graveyard value, growing creatures, and synergies with cards that care about card types or creatures in graveyards. Blitz (cast for less, haste, sacrifice at end of turn, draw a card) is a key tempo/value tool. Most decks can be described as one of: connive-aggro (Obscura/Azorius), Riveteers blitz-aggro, Brokers midrange (GW/Bant), Maestros removal-control. Kenji favors staying in 2 colors with easy splashes over forcing 3 colors. Murder is notably weaker here than in most sets because "so many creatures in this format are just built-in two-for-ones" — Inspiring Overseer, Brazen Upstart, blitz creatures all replace themselves.

## Archetypes
- **Azorius/Obscura Connive (WU or WUB)**: Raffine's Informant (2/1 connive on attack) is the engine — "informant is maybe even better than Jewel Thief at this point; it can turn into a three-two itself, comes down a turn sooner, and it enables a lot of the counters-matters cards." Three Informants is a synergy-dense draft. Stack with: Expendable Lackey (1/1 that makes a fish when another fish is made — Fish tokens), Rigo (Brokers Ascendancy? — "a two-two flyer for two that draws a card when toulouse attacks"... actually Rigo(?)), Metropolis Angel, Toulouse (Toluz, Clever Conductor — connive card, exiles discarded cards), Celebrity Fencer (grows with counters), Security Bypass (makes creatures unblockable), Majestic Metamorphosis (6/6 angel for 2 as combat trick), Rooftop Nuisance (taps down opponents), Extraction Specialist (lifelink 2-drop that returns creatures with 2 or less power). Kenji won 7-2 with this deck going wide with fish tokens and connive value.
- **Brokers Bant Midrange (GWU)**: Cleanup Crew (2/2 that becomes a 6/6 and gains 4 life minimum, with better modes) is "pseudo-bomb, 6/6 is generally the biggest thing on the battlefield." Disciplined Duelist (double-strike with shield counter) is the A+ two-drop. Exotic Pets (grows with counters). Lagrella the Magpie (3-color legend, excellent ETB). Freelance Muscle (grows based on board). Giada, Font of Hope (2/2 vigilance flyer for 2 that buffs angels). Depopulate as board wipe. Brokers Charm (versatile instant). Little Chat (?)  — "card has just over performed every single time." Kenji ran 8/7/7 colored sources. Key note: "I did not have many Celestial Regulators... a 2/3 flyer for 3 that tempos down the opponent which just lets me keep the race pressure up and protects us from cards like the Duelist." The Regulator is underrated.
- **Maestros/Riveteers Midrange (BRG)**: Grizzly Sigil (enchantment — target creature gets +1/+1 counters and ward?), Professional Facebreaker (menace, makes treasures when creatures deal combat damage), Masked Bandits (5/5 menace for 3B blitz, leaves 2/2), Girder Goons (4/3 for 4 or 2 blitz, leaves 2/2 on death), Night Clubber (1-toughness killer), Corpse Explosion (X-cost wrath based on dead creatures), Dig Up the Body (graveyard recursion), Involuntary Employment (steal a creature). Kenji's Jund-style deck: "14 straight up black red cards." Key interaction: blitzing Girder Goons then using Corpse Explosion for a big board wipe. Fight Rigging (counters a creature up repeatedly) is a sleeper win condition.
- **Obscura/Dimir Control**: Murder, strangle, deal gone bad, hold for ransom. Kenji noted murder is "so much worse in this format, though." The format punishes pure removal decks because every threat generates value.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Raffine's Informant + Extraction Specialist**: Discard an Informant to connive, then Specialist returns it for +1/+1 counter and lifelink trigger — "get back Informant and then get a second trigger off of Toluz when the Specialist grabs back the Informant."
- **Toluz (Toulouse) + Rooftop Nuisance (?) on an opponent creature**: Sac the nuisance target to lose, draw a million cards — Kenji says "target Echo sack to Lose draw a million cards."
- **Expendable Lackey + Rigo (?)**: Lackey makes fish (1/1 tokens), Rigo draws a card when a fish deals combat damage — recurring card draw engine. "Turn two lackey into Rigo and just started drawing extra cards basically every turn, kind of nuts."
- **Celebrity Fencer + connive/counters**: Fencer gets a +1/+1 counter every time you connive — with three Informants attacking every turn it becomes massive quickly.
- **Majestic Metamorphosis on Shield Creature**: Kenji notes "I think everybody forgets that you can target artifacts with Majestic Metamorphosis" — turning an artifact into a 4/4 flying angel, relevant combat trick.
- **Metropolis Angel + any creature with a counter**: Angel draws a card when it attacks alongside a creature with a counter (from counters-matters synergy). "Kind of just like a backup Rigo that we can probably turn on more frequently."
- **Corpse Explosion + Blitz creatures in graveyard**: Blitz a Girder Goons (power 4), then corpse explosion for 4 damage to all creatures. Kenji uses this to wipe boards — "I'm guessing I can do it for three right now."
- **Grizzly Sigil + Involuntary Employment**: Sigil puts counters on stolen creatures, then employment steals them — "steel the titan turns the muscle into an 11/11."
- **Fake Your Own Death + Blitz**: Cast a blitz creature (it will sacrifice at end of turn anyway), fake its own death to return it — gaining extra ETB value and a treasure. "I could give myself a treasure token by faking its death."
- **Masked Bandits blitz + corpse explosion**: Blitz Bandits (they die, leave 2/2), use the corpse explosion immediately after for big damage based on Bandits' 5 power.

## Card tips
- **Raffine's Informant**: Top 2-drop in the format. Draft 3+ copies without guilt. "After starting with two informants I don't think you're supposed to pass the third." Pairs with: dagger, metamorphosis, bypass, guidance.
- **Disciplined Duelist**: "That duelist was just an A+ pickup" at pick 6. Underrated because of how hard it is to block — double-strike + shield counter means trading is nearly impossible without two cards.
- **Rooftop Nuisance**: "Nuisance has been a huge huge all-star in these type of decks." Taps down a creature temporarily — pseudo-removal and tempo piece. Take it over most filler at any pick.
- **Cleanup Crew**: "I'd call cleanup crew bomb-adjacent" — baseline gain 4 life plus modes that kill enchantments/artifacts or give trample. 6/6 body dominates most boards. Kenji first-picks it repeatedly.
- **Celestial Regulator**: "A two-three flyer for three that tempos down the opponent which just lets me keep the race pressure up and protects us from cards like [Disciplined Duelist]." Kenji says his first loss came from not having one. Highly underrated.
- **Murder**: Specifically undervalued in SNC — "murder is so much worse in this format" because opponents' creatures generate value on ETB. Spot removal is less efficient than tempo/pressure.
- **Dig Up the Body**: "I like having one dig up the body if I'm playing black." The recursion pays off in attrition games. Worth taking over lands in late picks when in black.
- **Girder Goons**: 4/3 that blitzes for 2 and leaves a 2/2 token — one of the best blitz creatures. Take 2 copies easily; they synergize with corpse explosion and involuntary employment.
- **Security Bypass**: "All the security bypass did was like a glorified looting mechanism" in one game — connive outlet, but also makes a creature unblockable. Enabler rather than standalone.
- **Hold for Ransom**: Solid aura removal that costs the opponent mana to pay off. Worth taking over filler; even wheeled in pack 3 in the tournament VOD.
- **Brokers Charm**: Versatile instant — removal, draw, or pump. "Take another charm even though the other one wheeled." Take multiple.
- **Eerie Sorin (?)** / **Shakedown Heavy (?)**: Heavy (3-power 1-drop menace?) "was drawing too many extra cards a turn now" for the opponent. One of the scariest aggressive cards.

## Pitfalls
- **Running too many lands without connive**: "Often times it's better to run fewer lands when you have a lot of connive." Connive effectively thins the deck — 16 lands is often correct with multiple Informants.
- **Toluz exiling discarded cards**: "Toluz, clever conductor exiles the card I connive so if I want to put something in the graveyard for [Extraction] Specialist it first gets eaten." The Toluz + Extraction Specialist synergy doesn't work.
- **Taking murder over tempo early**: Kenji regrets taking murder over Hold for Ransom in early picks because "I might be moving away from black." Pure removal is trap-ish — stay flexible.
- **Not holding Lagrella on the battlefield**: "Maybe I punted — Lagrella kill their flyer with Broker's Charm, then I can't really attack on the ground into Lagrella." Sacking Lagrella immediately rather than using her as a blocker was identified as a game-throwing play.
- **Security Bypass without evasion creatures**: "Bypass is a lot worse when you don't have the double-striker or something." Don't take it highly without the right enablers.
- **Overvaluing wiretapping**: "Wiretapping has gone so far down in my opinion, it's crazy." Card was considered early but passed repeatedly — too slow in the format.
- **Not using Extract the Truth correctly**: Kenji punted by using the "make opponent sacrifice a creature" mode when he could have taken the opponent's Sleep with the Fishes — "I 100% punted there, if I take the sleep with the fishes there I don't think I can lose."
- **Playing greedy attacks with Muscle without trample**: "Freelance Muscle doesn't trample" — blocked by 1/1 tokens constantly. Needs support spells or a clear path. Can't just goldfish into blockers.

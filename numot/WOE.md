# WOE — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Format is **medium speed** — aggro is viable (red-white beats, mono-red) but not oppressively fast; midrange green decks are the natural predator of aggro when they come together. Kenji rates the format **4/10** overall: functional but not a top-tier limited format.
- Red-white and mono-red are legitimate aggro strategies, but green-black food/value and five-color green are the power-ceiling decks. Kenji actively forced both extremes across these VODs.
- Rats don't block — build around that or play around it.
- **Celebration** (two or more non-land permanents enter your battlefield in a turn) is the key aggro enabler; curving out two-drops into two-drops turns it on naturally.
- **Bargain** (sacrifice an artifact, enchantment, or token as additional cost) is the format's value engine; tokens and Roles feed it efficiently.

## Archetypes

**Red-White Aggro (Celebration)**
- Gameplan: curve out 1-drops and 2-drops, trigger Celebration each turn, use roles/pump spells to grow threats and protect attackers. Bounce effects (e.g., Imodane's Recruiter [?] — "imidan recruiter" in transcript, 2/2 that gives all your creatures +1/+1 and haste when it ETBs) act as pseudo-removal via re-trigger loops.
- Key payoffs: Ash Party Crasher (2/2 haste that grows each turn if you've had two non-lands ETB), Belligerent of the Ball (Celebration grants +1/+1 and Menace at start of combat), Gallant Pie-Wielder (gains double strike when Celebration is online), Harried Spearguard (haste, recursive threat), Imodane's Recruiter (best uncommon in the format per Kenji — straightforward win condition).
- Princess Takes Flight (?): an enchantment/role that gives +2/+2 and flying; the loop with the two Celebrants (bounce it back, re-apply, bounce the threat) is "always an absurd play — always felt like you couldn't win when your opponent bounced their Princess Takes Flight back."
- Keep two ways to remove your own Princess Takes Flight (Torch the Tower, Archon's Glory, Celebrant bounce) so you aren't stuck paying its cost permanently.
- Spellbook Vendor: 2/2 that at the start of combat lets you pay 1 to make a Sorcerer Role for a creature — strong value engine in the deck.
- Witches' Mark: instant-speed role spell that can be bargained to kill enchanted creatures/tokens — key removal and bargain enabler.
- Monstrous Rage: highly rated pump spell, also played in Standard, very strong.
- Witchstalker Frenzy: Kenji called it a "nut" card; board-sweeper/pump effect that can swing games.

**Mono-Red (soft force)**
- Kenji drafted this successfully as a change-of-pace build. It relies on the same Celebration shell but goes harder on cheap threats and witches marks. Boundary Lands Ranger (?): "rummage" creature that discards lands; valuable for finding the Realm-Scorcher Hellkite (6-mana 4/6 flying haste that adds 4 mana if bargained) on time.
- Realm-Scorcher Hellkite: called a "crazy red flying bomb." The key late-game threat in mono-red.
- The critical mistake Kenji flagged: "I discarded the land to the second Witches' Mark when I drew the six-mana dragon off the first one" — don't throw away land when you need to hit six mana for your top-end bomb.
- Also flagged: don't play around counters you're not sure they have; going for the dragon when they have open mana is better than waiting and running out of time.

**Green-Black / Five-Color Green (Food/Value)**
- Gameplan: stabilize early with removal and cheap creatures, build a food/token engine, land one of several bombs. Candy Grapple is the lynchpin removal spell — Kenji took four copies in one draft: "fifth pick pack one made no sense, I don't know what's going on."
- Feed the Cauldron: solid early removal.
- Hatching Plans: 2-mana enchantment that draws 3 when sacrificed — pairs with any bargain effect. Kenji calls it "so nice, just casual draw three for two mana." First-pickable; a core card for the archetype.
- Hamlet Glutton: 6/6 trample that gains 3 (or 5 when bargained) for five mana. "Big bomb green creature, card was nuts."
- Regal Bunicorn (?): grows based on permanent count; can be a massive threat with enough board presence.
- Scream Puff: triggers food production when it hits the opponent; "fantastic with Parallel Lives" (bonus sheet).
- Sweet Tooth Witch: food-maker, solid 3-drop value.
- Hearth Elemental (?): red splash; "a lot of instances and sorceries in our deck already" makes it a worthwhile splash finisher.
- Edge of the Wilds (?): [possibly "Edgewall Innkeeper" variant — transcript unclear; "edge wall in" — creature that draws a card when an Adventure creature enters].
- Five-color fixing: Fawn (?), Prophetic Prism, Evolving Wilds, Brave the Wilds (?), Crystal Grotto (?), Return from the Wilds (?). "There's enough fixing in the format" to support five colors.
- Kenji deliberately forced Parallel Lives (bonus sheet enchantment that doubles tokens) in one draft as a fun build; it performed poorly: "ruins my parallel lives… four mana extra one token — this is why you don't play Parallel Lives." However he noted Imodane's Recruiter + Parallel Lives would make four 2/2s.

**Green-White / Five-Color Splash decks**
- Agatha's Champion (?): five-mana threat Kenji called "just too good" and "honestly a better pick up even though it doesn't do anything with parallel lives."
- Graceful Takedown: green removal. "Loses out on a graceful takedown here but grapple has more synergy with the hatching plans."

## Card interactions & combos

- **Hatching Plans + any bargain card** (Hamlet Glutton, Feed the Cauldron, Candy Grapple, Hopeless Nightmare, etc.): sacrifice Hatching Plans as you cast the spell for "draw three" essentially free. Six bargain enablers in one deck = "very very easy play."
- **Spellbook Vendor + Wicked Role**: the Vendor makes Roles; if you have the Wicked Role on a creature and want it off (to reset the bonus), Vendor can add a different Role which knocks the Wicked one off. "We can get rid of our own Wicked Role with the Spellbook Vendor next turn."
- **Ash Party Crasher + any bounce** (Celebrant ETB trigger, break the spell [?]): bounce Ash, replay for another haste attack that grows on entry. Kenji: "if I bounce the ash then they will get to trigger there" — use bounce to replay and grow Ash, not to dodge interaction.
- **Celebrant (the bounce-a-creature creature) + Princess Takes Flight**: bounce PTF back to hand then replay onto your attacker. "Always an absurd play." Two Celebrants in the deck means you can do this repeatedly.
- **Two-Headed Hunter + Monstrous Rage / double strike enabler**: Hunter is a 5/4 with Menace; combine with double strike for a one-shot kill threat.
- **Witches' Mark + bargain**: mark an opponent's creature, then sacrifice the role token as a bargain cost to "kill" the enchanted thing (if the enchantment is destroyed via the bargain the creature reverts). Kenji: "remember with Witches' Mark if I opt to Target something and they kill that thing in response — it's gone forever."
- **Curiosity + Catapult** (mentioned as a remembered interaction from the format): equip Curiosity to any pinging creature, ping opponent, draw a card; if you draw an instant/sorcery, keep going. Not drafted in these VODs but called out as a known engine.

## Card tips

- **Candy Grapple**: "There are not four better cards you could take in the opening pack over Candy Grapple [other than a rare/Mythic or Imodane's Recruiter]." Going fifth pick pack one is deeply wrong; always prioritize.
- **Imodane's Recruiter**: "Best uncommon in the format. Basically a one-card win con." 3-mana 2/2; Adventure makes two 2/2s with vigilance for 5. High first-pick priority.
- **Torch the Tower**: "Premium one-mana removal spell." First-pickable.
- **Gingerbread Hunter**: "Removal AND a 5/5 that makes a food card" — called "insane."
- **Gumdrop Poisoner** (?): "Really really good. Makes a food, is a 3/2 Lifelink that often kills a creature." High pick priority.
- **Welcome to Sweet Tooth**: "Another insane card — two-mana Saga that just does a bunch of good stuff."
- **Tough Cookie**: "Really good. Two-mana 2/2 that makes a food, can turn a non-creature artifact into a 4/4."
- **Hopeless Nightmare**: good early-game discard/bargain setup in grindy decks. Kenji sideboarded it to bargain away early for Scry 2.
- **Archon's Glory**: "Decent white trick." Also valuable as a bargain source to remove a Role from your own creature.
- **Titanic Growth** / pump spells in GW: opponent's deck had these; always play around them when blocking.
- **Rats don't block** in this format — account for this in combat math.
- **Young Hero Role**: attached to a creature with toughness 3 or less; it attacks, gets a +1/+1 counter. Useful to grow small creatures before they exceed the threshold. Put it on a 2/2 Celebrant that will attack as a 4/3 — still in range.
- **Royal Roll**: gives Ward 1 — opponent needs two things to enter to beat it. Useful on key threats like Spellbook Vendor.
- **Cooped Up**: passivism effect (can't attack or block). Solid removal.
- Cutting lands: "16 lands is totally fine" for aggressive red-white. Five-color green should go to 17 or add more fixing sources rather than land count.
- In aggro, "hold open tap-down > putting a counter on your creature" — don't waste tempo on role assignment when you can tap their blocker.

## Pitfalls

- **Parallel Lives**: do not draft around it. It was a "four-mana extra one token" when it wasn't immediately destroyed. "This is why you don't play Parallel Lives."
- **Witches' Mark targeting**: if you target a creature with Witches' Mark and they kill it in response, the role is gone forever. Confirm they can't respond before committing.
- **Not attacking with enough creatures**: Kenji flagged multiple spots where he attacked with fewer creatures than he should have: "If I attack with the spearguard as well, yeah maybe that was a punt on my part." Always count trample damage before passing.
- **Discarding lands when you need bombs**: "The big mistake was discarding the land to the second Witches' Mark when I drew the six-mana dragon off the first one." Once you hit your bomb in hand, stop pitching lands.
- **Playing around counters you don't know exist**: "I got too scared and I didn't go for the dragon on the turn they had open mana... they didn't have something to bargain away." If they can't actually counter profitably, go for the threat.
- **Feed the Cauldron timing**: Kenji noted firing it "real loose" cost him a game — save it for the right moment, not the first available target.
- **Not double-blocking when stabilized**: "If I double-block there I don't think I can lose" — costing himself a game by trying to play around Kellen's Light Blades (rare removal). Assess likelihood before playing around low-probability outs.
- **Playing Prophetic Prism and not bargaining it**: "I would have been able to still hold up my Royal Treatment — that actually could be a huge punt." Prism is a bargain source; don't forget to use it as one.
- **Green-black requires enough top-end**: "I haven't seen a single Hamlet Glutton yet in fact... we haven't really seen any big finishers." Don't enter the archetype without reliable bombs to close.

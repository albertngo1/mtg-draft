## Supersessions (grade/opinion flips between first-impressions and later reviews)

This channel's spread is narrow (the reviews came only ~3 days after first impressions), but the run does reveal several reversals — and the **6/19 early-access draft** then the **6/24 ranked draft** are the reality checks on the grades:

- **FORMAT SPEED: "racy/midrange-tempo" (6/19) → "SLOW" (6/24) — the headline reversal.** The 6/19 early-access read leaned racy/tempo. After actually drafting the launch format (6/24), Alex flatly reverses: "this set feels like *I got a two-for-one, holy crap*" rather than "everything draws a card, you never run out." He pins it as **old-school resource-management Limited** — explicitly "Dominaria United / original Dominaria," a "similar cadence to Final Fantasy, **maybe even slightly slower**." Games went long (cited deck counts: opponents at 12 cards while he sat at 16, lots of board stalls, "a lot of 4/4 flyers, board stalls, flying's good"). **Verdict: SLOW, grindy, removal-and-timing-driven. The newest read wins — this is the over-arching format correction.**
- **Removal: "weak" → "actually pretty good" (within 6/13), then "SCARCE in practice" (6/19), reinforced (6/24).** The 6/24 deck-review verdict is emphatic: "removal is at a premium in this set **more so than usual**." A slow format makes the thin removal hurt more, not less.
- **"Random four-mana 4/4s" / no-ETB midrange bodies: better than their grade in the slow format (6/24).** Because you want to **bait the opponent's removal onto your medium threats** and save yours for their good cards, vanilla-ish 4-drops gain value ("makes random 4-mana 4/4s better — if they have to use removal on that rather than my actual great cards, that's a win"). A partial walk-back of the "expensive vanilla = bottom-tier trap" rule *for the mid-curve*; the top no-ETB fatties still get raced.
- **Removal: "weak" → "actually pretty good" (within 6/13), then "SCARCE in practice" (6/19).** Alex's blind fear was that removal would be weak to prop up the expensive no-ETB legends; he reversed *within the first-impressions video* ("turns out the removal in this set is pretty good"). But after actually drafting (6/19) he flipped back partway: **removal felt scarce and was a recurring draft complaint** — prioritize it. **Verdict: removal is fine in quality (Lightning Strike, Dark Deed, Web Up, Cruel Alliance) but THIN in quantity.**
- **Plans: blanket optimism (6/13) → "good in sealed, jury's out for draft" (6/18) → solid value engines that rarely reach the final chapter (6/19).** Alex spent the most tier-list time on plan/saga cards and softened from "more good than not" to format-split. In the VOD he repeatedly cast sagas but "never got to the third chapter" — **late chapters are aspirational; value the early chapters.**
- **Atlantis Attacks: C+ (blue review) — the one copy is good, the second is a trap (6/19).** Played read validates the cautious C+: "the number you want is ONE… there were spots I wished it wasn't in my opener. It's still a seven-drop." Verdict: fine one-of, never two.
- **HULK SMASH!: C (red review) → "more of a constructed card than a limited card" (6/19).** The cheap-with-a-villain upside matters far less when you rarely have spare turn-one mana. Verdict: below its grade in practice.
- **Madame Hydra: D+ (multicolor review) → "okay, four mana is really small, not huge on it" (6/19).** Reliably triggers (everything's a villain) but underwhelmed even when working. Verdict: the D+ holds.
- **Artifacts (UR): "pretty well supported" (6/18) → "really just the one payoff, I cut the whole subtheme" (6/19).** The artifact deck dried up in his draft; he pivoted to connive/villains. Verdict: artifact-matters is the **shallowest** of the supported themes — don't force it.
- **Blue: "I think blue's the best color" (6/13 hedged prediction) → CONFIRMED & strengthened every step.** The blue review calls blue "the best color by a clear margin," 6/18 names it a sealed pillar, and the 6/19 VOD title/content is built around **blue being the standout color.** This is the single most-confirmed take of the run. (6/24 nuance: with a deck that splashed into white/black off green, Alex still rated the blue-flyer decks his opponents played as the scary ones, and reiterated "I like the blue cards" as his only firm color read.)
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Helicarrier: Alex C / Mark D+ (multicolor review) → "playable, fine, not awesome" — Alex defends it (6/24).** He ran it over community pushback ("what's gotten into you, Alex? a crew-6 that doesn't self-crew?") and it carried multiple games as a 4/5 flyer that brings two bodies. Verdict: a real playable in the slow format ("vehicles that come with value/tokens are always underrated"), roughly holding its C — *not* Mark's D+.
- **Madame Masque & Ms. Marvel, Kamala Khan: over-/around-grade in practice (6/24).** Madame Masque was again "a good one — one of the best uncommons in the set, if not the best" (echoes 6/19's "a little too good"). Ms. Marvel, Kamala Khan (the rare, C+ in review) "has been a little better than expected" as a 5-mana flash flying lifelinker with an indestructible power-up — a genuine bomb in the games shown.
- **Atlantis Attacks: re-affirmed "appropriately costed common seven-drop," not broken (6/24).** Pushed on chat calling it outrageous, Alex held the cautious line: "a pretty balanced card… a good card but appropriate for a common seven-drop." Consistent with the 6/19 "one good, second a trap" read.

## Format speed / meta read

*(Pre-data. No 17Lands. Games-played input is the 6/19 early-access draft and the 6/24 ranked draft — the latter wins on conflict.)*

- **Overall verdict (6/19): "a pretty good, above-average Limited set… you'll enjoy your first few drafts, but not an all-timer."** Both draft and gameplay rated "totally fine."
- **Speed: SLOW & GRINDY (6/24 — supersedes the 6/19 "racy" read).** The episode title flags it — "Marvel Drafts are Here! (and They're...Slow?)" — and the games confirm it. Alex's read: **old-school resource-management Limited**, "Dominaria United / original Dominaria" vibes, "a similar cadence to Final Fantasy, maybe even slightly slower." What makes it slow: most creatures are defensively-statted, "a lot of 4/4 flyers and board stalls," and **flying is the way through** stalled boards. Card advantage is *less* of a grind-engine than recent sets — "this set doesn't feel like you never run out of cards… it feels like *I got a two-for-one, holy crap*," so individual two-for-ones swing games. Practical consequence: **timing and sequencing matter a lot** ("the little dance between your good cards and removal" — bait the opponent into spending removal on your medium threats, hold yours for their bombs), and **doing nothing is sometimes correct** ("there are going to be turns where you do nothing instead, because it's the better long-term play").
- **Removal: good quality, scarce quantity — and at a premium "more so than usual" in the slow format (6/24).** Lightning Strike (R), Dark Deed (B), Web Up / Super Villain Lockup (W), Cruel Alliance (B), Punishing Punch (G), Frozen in Ice (U) are the benchmarks. Prioritize it hard — you will be short, and a slow format magnifies the gaps. Many legends are synergy linchpins (e.g. Iron Fist, Living Weapon — "snipe it turn three and a chunk of their deck stops working"), so spot removal earns extra value.
- **Mid-curve no-ETB bodies gain value in the slow format (6/24).** Random 4-mana 4/4s and similar "good cards, nebulous plan" midrange creatures play better than their grades because they soak removal you'd rather not eat on your bombs. (The *top* no-ETB fatties still get raced — the bottom bucket below holds.)
- **Board wipes: essentially NONE (6/19, reconfirmed 6/24).** "There aren't very many board wipes in this set." Only "deal 2–3 to everything" effects (Avengers Disassembled, "three mana deal three to everything is one of the few"). **Consequence: go-wide and sticky single threats are unusually safe; white bombs with no sweeper to answer them (Monica Rambeau, Captain America, Super-Soldier, Avengers Assemble!) are punishing. Board stalls form and flyers break them.**
- **"Big dummy" no-ETB fatties get punished.** Alex's explicit three-bucket rule for expensive creatures: (1) expensive **with an ETB/dies effect** = always playable; (2) **Kang-tier** (no ETB but strong if you untap with it) = pretty good; (3) **expensive vanilla-ish with no ETB** (the "plethora of 5-mana 4/4 flyers") = bottom-tier trap.
- **Goodstuff with synergy support, not a hard synergy format.** Themes "come together without much work" (6/13); in practice (6/19) Alex took the better card over the on-theme card repeatedly and let *what was open* (connive/villains) define his deck. The 6/24 ranked deck doubled down: a green base splashing white + black for the best cards (She-Hulk x-was-2, Killmonger, Dark Deed, Ms. Marvel) — "fairly lightly themed, a good mix of creatures and removal… the theme is good cards. It's a midrange deck." His verdict: **"I think this is something you're going to find pretty commonly in this set"** — lightly-themed goodstuff midrange is a default, and **no color pair looked un-viable** ("any color pair not looking viable? Not really — I'm not opposed to anything in particular").
- **Power & complexity live in the legends.** No legendary commons; the uncommon-and-up legends carry the format's depth and several "rival rares" (Justice, Vance Astrovik; Agent Maria Hill; Ka-Zar). The set is **rare-defined** more than uncommon-defined (6/17): your first-pick rare often sets your direction.
- **Mana/fixing: good.** Gain-life tapped dual lands in ~50% of packs; an untapped rare dual cycle ("very close to OG duals in Limited"); colorless utility lands (Surveillance Room, Baxter Building). Default two colors or two-plus-a-splash; **red is the best splash** (its payoffs cast fine a turn late). Lock splashes with the rare duals.
- **Five mechanics:**
  - **Connive** — Alex's favorite ("one of the best Limited mechanics they've ever printed"); smooths draws, central to UB. Caveat: most blue connive commons cost 4, so cheap enablers (Red Room Recruit) are prized.
  - **Teamwork** — pay an alternate cost by tapping creatures with total power ≥ N (crew-for-spells). Played smoothly in practice ("not finicky"); concentrated in WU; rewards a body-heavy build over a spell-heavy one.
  - **Power-up** — exhaust-style mana sink for +1/+1 counters; cost reduced by mana value if cast that turn. Plays well but rewards sequencing patience (don't auto-jam on curve). Self-counters feed the GU counters deck.
  - **Plan** — enchantments that accrue counters off a trigger, then sacrifice for a payoff. Hardest cards to evaluate; better in sealed than draft; value the early chapters.
  - **Transform / "secret identity" hero↔villain MDFCs** — flexibility of counting as both types matters more than the flip itself; type-matters payoffs are everywhere.
- **Color strength (Alex's read): Blue #1, White #2**, with black close behind for the villain shell. Red and green read as supporting/midrange. The sealed pillars (6/18) are **white-based heroes, black-based villains, or any blue deck.** On 6/24, asked for ranked color rankings, Alex stayed loose — "very roughly, I like the blue cards, I like the white cards, the rest I'm unsure of" — and re-stressed no pair looked dead. So **blue/white as the safe defaults holds, but the back half of the color list is genuinely open** this early.

## Archetypes (10 two-color pairs, ranked)

*(Ranked by Alex's early read + the 6/19 draft. No win-rate ordering is possible yet — re-rank when Arena data lands. Note: the **hybrid signpost** in each pair is generally at-or-above the gold signpost in power and goes in more decks, so it's the higher pick.)*

### 1. UB — Draw your second card / Connive (Alex's de-facto best in practice)
- **Plan:** The calculating mastermind — connive to smooth draws, trigger "second card each turn" payoffs, grind card advantage. This is what Alex's 6/19 deck became (blue base, R/B splash) and what felt strongest. Connive + villains were *open*.
- **Signposts:** **Kang, Temporal Tyrant** (gold; C/C- — 3/4 connives on attack + drains on your 2nd draw, much better on the play), **Ghost, Spectral Saboteur** (hybrid; D+/C- — 3-mana flash 2/2 unblockable, flexible body, secretly wants connive). Also **Scientist Supreme of A.I.M.** and **Taskmaster, Mercenary Mimic** are UB uncommons.
- **Read:** Connive is Alex's favorite mechanic; the payoffs (Madame Masque, Construct a Cosmic Cube, the token-makers) over-delivered. Watch that the cheap connive enabler (Red Room Recruit) is scarce.

### 2. GW — Heroes matter (strong, low-effort)
- **Plan:** Assemble heroes; payoffs turn on automatically because there are ~91 heroes in the set. One of the two best-supported tribal themes.
- **Signposts:** **Black Panther, Vanguard** (gold; C/C+ — 4/4 that goes wide / pumps when non-token heroes enter), **Spider-Man, To the Rescue** (hybrid; C+ — 3/2 flash/vigilance/reach that grants indestructible). Plus **Black Panther, Wakandan King** and **Storm, Windrider** as GW uncommons.
- **Read:** Rick Jones, Destined Sidekick wants ~10 heroes (easy outside black); Wakandan Royal Guard and The Thing reward going wide. White is the hub — the hero theme flexes into WU and RW too.

### 3. BR — Villains matter (strong, auto-on)
- **Plan:** Flood villain triggers and push damage to the face. "Almost all black creatures are villains," so payoffs are reliably online.
- **Signposts:** **Bullseye, Death Dealer** (hybrid; C — sac-artifact/discard to ping 2 repeatedly, kills the format's many 1–2 toughness creatures and goes face; rated above the gold), **Madame Hydra** (gold; D+ — 2/1 menace token per villain cast, feast-or-famine, dies to Lightning Strike). Plus **Ares, God of War** and **Avengers: Under Siege** (a standout BR saga).
- **Read:** Villain payoffs (Baron Strucker, Madame Masque, The Masters of Evil, Crossbones) auto-turn-on. Avengers: Under Siege overperformed in the 6/19 draft. Madame Hydra underwhelmed even when triggering.

### 4. WU — Teamwork (Alex's preseason "best archetype" pick)
- **Plan:** Tap creatures to upgrade instants/sorceries; untap effects make teamwork "free." Alex's 6/13 pick for best archetype, mostly *because blue and white are the two best colors*.
- **Signposts:** **Captain America, Living Legend** (gold; B- — untaps the first creature tapped each turn, pseudo-vigilance enabler), **Spider-Woman, Secret Agent** (hybrid; C+/B- — flash 1/4 that taps and locks down a creature, strong vs. the format's big bodies). Plus **King T'Challa** and **The Mighty Thor, Jane Foster** as WU uncommons.
- **Read:** Teamwork played smoothly. Wants a body-heavy build (you need creatures to tap). Agent Maria Hill is the one card that specifically rewards paying teamwork.

### 5. RW — Spells / Prowess / Tricks (most aggressive pair)
- **Plan:** Cheap threats + combat tricks + prowess; untap-your-creature and target-your-own-creature synergies recur.
- **Signposts:** **War Machine, Legacy of Iron** (hybrid; C+ — 1/3 flyer that pumps another creature by its power each combat), **Thor Odinson** (gold; C- — 5-mana 4/4 flying vigilance with double prowess, a vanilla-ish Serra Angel that gets punished on Arena). Plus **Daredevil, Man Without Fear** and **The Super Hero Civil War** (an A+ saga) as the premium RW rares.
- **Read:** Take Up the Shield (W) and Blazing Crescendo (R) are the premium tricks. Iron Fist + a white untap trick is a key combo. Favors tricks that keep creatures attacking turn after turn.

### 6. BG — Graveyard (low-commitment, but signpost-dependent)
- **Plan:** Reclaim fallen forces; the key threshold is just **2 creature cards in graveyard**, hit naturally by trading. Not a self-mill deck.
- **Signposts:** **Killmonger, Scourge of Wakanda** (gold; **A-/B+ — the standout of the entire gold cycle**; sac a creature → destroy any nonland permanent, becomes a 5/4 with 2 creatures in yard), **Titania, Rugged Rumbler** (hybrid; D+/C- — a 5/5 with ward you usually cast turn 3 by discarding, but you're unhappy paying full price). Plus **The Serpent Society**.
- **Read:** "If your signpost gets poached, the deck falls apart" — Killmonger is the linchpin. Hellcat is great sac fodder for it. Single-pip green fatties (Savage Land Dinosaur) favor the BG mana base.

### 7. RG — Power-up / Ramp (fine midrange)
- **Plan:** Big bodies + mana sinks; grow creatures via power-up. Alex is mildly skeptical ("growing creatures doesn't deal with a deck drawing cards… but maybe that's enough").
- **Signposts:** **Hulk, Gamma Goliath** (gold; C — 6/5 reach trample that cheapens other power-ups), **Abomination, Terrifying Titan** (hybrid; C — 4/4 trample that power-ups into a 7-mana fight). Plus the **Wolverine** pair, **Alien Invasion**, **Escape to the Wilds**, **Ravenous Tyrannosaurus**.
- **Read:** Power-up is "instant-speed and tried-and-true," one of the better mechanics. Several artifact/villain payoffs (HYDRA Assault Robot, Crimson Operative) are notably weak *in GR specifically*.

### 8. WB — Attack alone (good payoffs, historically suspect theme)
- **Plan:** Send one operative on a solo attack, pile bonuses on it; a small equipment sub-theme rides along (equipment is best when one creature attacks).
- **Signposts:** **U.S.Agent, John Walker** (hybrid; B — 3/2 that enters making and equipping a Sturdy Shield +1/+2, becoming a 4/4; a standalone-good card playable in any W or B deck — rated well above the gold), **Black Widow, Double Agent** (gold; C — 3/2 deathtouch giving the lone attacker first strike + menace). Plus **Winter Soldier, Icy Assassin**, **Cloak and Dagger, Entwined**, **The Kingpin of Crime**.
- **Read:** Alex's most historically-doubted theme ("why attack with one thing?"), but he thinks the payoffs (Agent 13, Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., Luke Cage, HYDRA Infiltration) are good. Not a sealed pillar. Balance creature count so you always have an attacker.

### 9. UR — Artifacts (shallowest supported theme)
- **Plan:** Artifacts matter — but payoffs are thin. Alex started here in the 6/19 draft and *cut the whole subtheme* when it dried up.
- **Signposts:** **Iron Man, Master of Machines** (gold; B- — flying/vigilance that scales per artifact + draws; clear best signpost), **Speedball, New Warrior** (hybrid; C — effectively spell-hexproof but a weird low-confidence build-around). Plus **Iron Man, Titan of Innovation**, **Vision Quest** (an A-/B tutor), **Armor Wars**.
- **Read:** "You won't get a true all-artifact deck — evaluate each artifact on its own merits." Iron Lad wants ~8+ artifacts. No treasure/clue/food token theme to feed it. Don't force it.

### 10. GU — +1/+1 Counters (weakest pair)
- **Plan:** Pile counters via power-up creatures (which self-counter) more than spells; cash them into payoffs.
- **Signposts:** **Ant-Man, Colony Commander** (gold; D+/C- — pay 1 on attack to counter a creature + spit out insects, wants to be on the play), **Beast, Erudite Aerialist** (hybrid; D — 3/3 that flies + draws on combat damage *only if* you counter it that turn, too few enablers). Plus **Moon Girl and Devil Dinosaur**, **The Astonishing Ant-Man**, **Absorbing Man**.
- **Read:** Alex's most-skeptical archetype both preseason and after review — "the payoff may not keep up with a card-drawing deck." Enablers are abundant, payoffs uninspiring. "A deck you draft once in a blue moon."

## Card tips

Grades below are Alex's **per-color set-review predictions** (recorded 2026-06-16/17, before meaningful games) on an A+→F scale, plus the channel's qualifiers: **build-around** (low floor, high ceiling in the right shell), **sideboard**, and **bomb**. Where Alex and co-host Mark split, shown as `Alex X / Mark Y`. **No 17Lands data exists yet** — these are hypotheses. Where the 6/19 draft moved a card, the played read is flagged inline.

### White (W)
- **Agent Maria Hill** — B (white review). 1W 2/1 hero; +1/+1 counter and draw a card whenever tapped to pay a teamwork cost. The one card that specifically rewards teamwork; a lot of value for one mana, playable in most white decks. "One of the best uncommons" (6/18).
- **Crowd of True Believers** — Alex C / Mark D+ (white review). 1W 1/2; pumps + gains 1 life on the lone attacker. Cheap WB attack-alone support; awkward when you can't attack.
- **Helicarrier Strike** — C- (white review). 1W instant, teamwork 2; deal 2 (4 with teamwork) to an attacking/blocking creature. Efficient defensive removal; exactly what the WB/WU racer wants, weak in WR/WG.
- **Panther Pounce** — C- (white review). 1W instant; investigate + give a creature +1/+0, flying, untap. Divisive value/trick for WR; no toughness so not a true two-for-one, but a copy or two is fine.
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Spy Kit** — C- (white review). 1W equipment, equip 1; +1/+1 + untap & scry 1 on attacking alone. The best hope for equipment synergy at common.
- **Brave Brawler** — Alex C- / Mark C (white review). 1W 2/1 lifelink with a power-up. Filler two-drop; playable because good twos are scarce.
- **Raft Security Officer** — D+ (white review). 1W 1/3; 2-mana tapper (cheaper vs. power ≤3). Pricey filler tapper; better vs. equipment decks.
- **Take Up the Shield** — Alex B- / Mark C+ (white review). 1W instant; +1/+1 counter + lifelink + indestructible. Top-tier combat trick that leaves a permanent counter; "easy to underrate, play the 1st and 2nd copy" (6/18).
- **Agent of Atlas** — C (white review). 1W 2/2 prowess. Bread-and-butter body for the WR tricks deck.
- **Murdock's Crusade** — Alex C- / Mark C (white review). 1W sorcery, teamwork 4; exile a toughness-4+ creature (and/or a 4+-MV enchantment with teamwork). Scarce answer to big midrange threats; the enchantment mode rarely matters, don't want many.
- **Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.** — C- (white review). 2W 2/4; +1/+1 to the lone attacker (exalted-style). Better than it looks; keeps cheap creatures relevant in WB.
- **Hero in Training** — Alex C+ / Mark B- (white review). 2W 2/2; ETB draw a card, +2 life with another hero. Solid above-rate value common.
- **Kree Commandos** — D+ (white review). 2W 2/1 flying vigilance prowess. A lot of stats but 1 toughness blocks/survives poorly; for all-in WR.
- **Web Up** — B- (white review). 2W enchantment; O-Ring any nonland permanent (Banishing Light). Reliably good removal, rarely bad.
- **Wakandan Drone Flock** — Alex C- / Mark D+ (white review). 3W 3/3 artifact flyer; ETB scry 2. Fine but unexciting; artifact type barely matters in white.
- **Borough Backup** — Alex C / Mark C- (white review). 4W sorcery; two 2/2 vigilance hero tokens + basic landcycling 2. No real drawback; basic landcycling fixes mana and feeds BG. Mark lower on the cyclers.
- **Political Triumph** — Alex C+ / Mark C- (white review). 1W plan; scry 1 + plan counter per creature ETB → at 4, draw + team-wide +1/+1. Great cast turn one, weak top-deck. Low confidence (a plan).

## How to draft MSH (Limited Level-Ups read)

A synthesis of Alex's review takes + the 6/19 early-access draft. **All pre-/early-format — re-weight once 17Lands lands ~2026-06-26.**

- **Pick priorities, roughly:** (1) bombs/removal that go in any deck (the splashable gold rares — Daredevil, Wolverine, Avengers: Under Siege, Cloak and Dagger, Vision Quest — plus Lightning Strike, Dark Deed, Web Up, Punishing Punch, Cruel Alliance, Frozen in Ice); (2) the standout uncommons that "rival rares" (Justice, Vance Astrovik; Agent Maria Hill; Ka-Zar; Killmonger); (3) efficient evasive bodies and good 2-drops (the set is short on good twos — Alex consciously valued them); (4) synergy payoffs *only once you know your lane is open.*
- **Start open, go deep in one color, follow what's open.** This is a "10-archetype, any-pairing" set, not a commit-early-via-gold set. Alex's heuristic: get deep into one color (for him, blue) so you can take whichever second color is most open. He read the connive/villain lane as open because those cards kept flowing while artifacts dried up.
- **Be in blue if you can.** Blue is the most-confirmed take of the run — best commons (Justice, S.H.I.E.L.D. Deployment Drone, Falcon), best card advantage, the connive engine, and Alex's standout pick from preview through actual play. Expect the Arena population to underrate it early.
- **Prioritize removal — you will be short.** Quality is fine; quantity is thin. Don't pass cheap interaction, and don't over-rely on combat tricks as pseudo-removal.
- **Prioritize the rare dual lands and Surveillance Room to enable splashes; splash red.** Fixing is good, and red's payoffs (Lightning Strike, Kang) cast fine a turn or two late.
- **Overrated / be careful:** the "plethora of 5-mana 4/4 flyers" and other no-ETB fatties (bottom bucket — they get raced/chump-attacked); Madame Hydra; HULK SMASH! (constructed card); a *second* Atlantis Attacks; the GU counters archetype (weakest pair); forcing UR artifacts (shallowest theme).
- **Underrated / sleepers:** A.I.M. Synthoids ("don't sideboard it" — villain + artifact + surveil, fills the 2-drop gap); H.E.R.B.I.E. Scout Unit; Surveillance Room (play it even without a splash); Trickster's Stratagem and We Say Thee Nay! (the population will mis-pick these); the basic-landcyclers (real fixing + BG graveyard fuel); Take Up the Shield ("easy to underrate, play 1st and 2nd copy").
- **Signposts to watch while drafting:** flowing connive/draw-2 cards (UB open) → lean into Madame Masque / Kang / Construct a Cosmic Cube; late villain payoffs → BR; an early Killmonger → BG (and don't let yours get poached); white bombs (Monica Rambeau, the Captains) are unanswerable in a sweeperless format, so a hero/white deck is a strong default.
- **Sealed (6/18):** lead with your best cards, then aim for one of three buckets — a **white-based hero deck, a black-based villain deck, or any blue deck.** Splash freely with the good fixing. Plans are better in sealed than draft.
- **Format expectation:** above-average, **slow/grindy midrange** (6/24 supersedes the earlier "racy" read), no board wipes, sticky threats, removal-light, lots of board stalls broken by flyers. Old-school resource-management Limited ("Dominaria United / Final Fantasy-or-slower"); individual two-for-ones swing games; timing and removal-baiting matter. Lightly-themed goodstuff midrange is a fine default, and no color pair looks dead. "You'll enjoy your first few drafts" — not an all-timer.

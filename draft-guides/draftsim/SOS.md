## Format in one line

**White aggro vs. value piles.** The format splits into two camps:

- **Fast attackers** — Silverquill (WB) and Lorehold (RW) deploy cheap creatures and removal and try to end the game before the soups stabilize.
- **Slow value / soups** — Prismari (UR) and Quandrix (GU) control and 5-color Converge piles built on removal, card draw, and late finishers.

**Witherbloom (BG)** sits awkwardly in the middle on lifegain synergies that mostly don't pay off — the reviewer's least-favorite archetype.

Format principles the guide leans on:
- **Removal is very good** — premium commons/uncommons across colors enable the control plans.
- **Creature density is low** — decks tend to run light on bodies; don't over-cut creatures.
- **Green is the weakest color** unless you're specifically in Quandrix/Converge ramp.
- **Splashing** is realistic mostly in white decks, and only for exceptional cards.
- **Converge is exceptionally strong** — multicolor piles are the reviewer's favorite thing to do; the payoffs "feel like cheating."

## Mechanics

- **Prepare** — mixed. Prepared creatures range from staples (Elite Interceptor, Studious First-Year) to filler; don't auto-play every one.
- **Repartee (Silverquill)** — "worked as advertised." Rewards removal spells and repeatable buffs; core to WB.
- **Opus (Prismari)** — variable; some decks use opus as prowess, others as a creature payoff.
- **Infusion (Witherbloom)** — "kind of a bummer." Common payoffs underwhelm; set lacks strong lifegain payoffs.
- **Flashback (Lorehold)** — excellent. Pursue the Past, Tome Blast are efficient and high-value.
- **Increment (Quandrix)** — useful but rarity-dependent; good uncommon/rare payoffs, mediocre commons.
- **Paradigm** — "range from great to should-be-banned." Every member except **Decorum Dissertation** is P1P1-worthy.
- **Converge** — exceptionally strong; the backbone of the soup decks.

## Archetypes

### Lorehold Midrange (RW)
- **Plan:** efficient creatures + removal, leveraging flashback to grind value over time.
- **Key cards:** *Commons* Elite Interceptor, Ajani's Response, Stone Docent · *Uncommons* Pursue the Past, Tome Blast, Wilt in the Heat · *Rares* Improvisation Capstone (Bomb), Practiced Offense (Bomb).
- **Take:** solid; leans on strong uncommons for early board presence, flashback payoffs (Pursue the Past, Tome Blast) provide exceptional value.

### Silverquill Aggro (WB)
- **Plan:** cheap threats, trigger repartee with removal + repeatable buffs to grow creatures.
- **Key cards:** *Commons* Elite Interceptor, Cost of Brilliance, the removal suite · *Uncommons* Strixhaven Skycoach, Molten Note · *Rares* Practiced Offense (Bomb).
- **Take:** prioritizes speed and early board dominance; repartee rewards pairing removal with creatures.

### Prismari Control (UR)
- **Plan:** control with removal + card draw, opus as secondary synergy, build to late finishers.
- **Key cards:** *Commons* Essence Scatter, Run Behind, Quick Study, Hydro-Channeler · *Uncommons* Divergent Equation, Stress Dream, Unsubtle Mockery · *Rares* Exhibition Tidecaller (Great), Wisdom of Ages (Great).
- **Take:** blue-based soups excel at removal density; Exhibition Tidecaller milling ~1/3 of the deck makes it a removal priority.

### Quandrix Ramp (GU)
- **Plan:** ramp into big threats + late payoffs; increment creatures and converge cards for value.
- **Key cards:** *Commons* Studious First-Year, Hydro-Channeler · *Uncommons* Pensive Professor, Ambitious Augmenter · *Rares* Mathemagics (Bomb), Germination Practicum (Great).
- **Take:** converge cards elevate the deck significantly; Mathemagics gives unmatched inevitability with ramp.

### Witherbloom Midrange (BG)
- **Plan:** creature-heavy midrange on infusion + lifegain; struggles to find consistent synergy support.
- **Key cards:** *Commons* Studious First-Year, Noxious Newt, Burrog Barrage · *Uncommons* Old-Growth Educator, Colossus of the Blood Age · *Rares* Postmortem Professor (Great).
- **Take:** infusion payoffs underwhelm; the reviewer's least-favorite archetype, narrow support.

### Converge Soups (5-color)
- **Plan:** draft the best removal, card draw, and finishers regardless of color; converge payoffs do the heavy lifting.
- **Key cards:** *Uncommons* Arcane Omens (Great), Snarl Song (Great), Together as One (Great) · *Rares* all converge cards, Wisdom of Ages, Exhibition Tidecaller.
- **Take:** the reviewer's favorite thing to do in the format; converge cards "feel like cheating" on power level.

## Best removal (ranked)

1. **Ajani's Response** — efficient white removal; the only splashable white common.
2. **Last Gasp** — black's primary common removal.
3. **Essence Scatter** — blue's key creature interaction.
4. **Unsubtle Mockery** — red's premier removal spell.
5. **End of the Hunt** — excellent uncommon green removal.
6. **Wilt in the Heat** — multicolor uncommon, exile effect.
7. **Molten Note** — multicolor uncommon, reusable flashback mode.

## Top commons (revised rankings)

- **White:** Elite Interceptor → Ajani's Response → Stone Docent → Interjection
- **Blue:** Essence Scatter → Run Behind → Quick Study → Hydro-Channeler
- **Black:** Last Gasp → Cost of Brilliance → Wander Off → Melancholic Poet
- **Red:** Unsubtle Mockery → Rubble Rouser → Tome Blast → Heated Argument
- **Green:** Studious First-Year → Burrog Barrage → Noxious Newt → Mindful Biomancer
- **Multicolor:** Pursue the Past → Render Speechless → Wilt in the Heat → Grapple with Death → Visionary's Dance

## Key uncommons

- **Underrated / missed gems:** Arcane Omens, Sundering Archaic, Snarl Song (converge); Divergent Equation (control finisher); Strixhaven Skycoach (flexibility); Molten Note; Colossus of the Blood Age.
- **Overrated:** Quill-Blade Laureate, Muse Seeker (dies easily), Spectacular Skywhale (too fragile), Old-Growth Educator (narrow).

## Rare re-reviews (notable grade changes vs. the reviewer's set review)

- **Improvisation Capstone:** Good → **Bomb** (effect is uncapped; initially misread).
- **Practiced Offense:** Great → **Bomb** (broken with flashback).
- **Exhibition Tidecaller:** Usually Not Good → **Great** (a "10" on opus, mills ~1/3 of the deck).
- **Wisdom of Ages:** Usually Not Good → **Great** (perfect soup finisher).
- **Vastlands Scavenger:** Bomb → **Great** (underwhelming hits).
- **Wildgrowth Archaic:** Bomb → **Great** (too fragile).
- **Colorstorm Stallion:** Great → **Bomb**.
- **Witherbloom, the Balancer:** Great → **Usually Not Good** (awkward to cast, poor payoff).
- **Maelstrom Artisan:** Great → **Usually Not Good** (clunky, vulnerable).

## Supersessions (newer take wins)

Genuine opinion reversals across the run. Format: **Topic** — early take → late take. Verdict.

- **Azorius / Izzet as "the two best decks"** — #737 (early) hyped UW Detectives as the best deck and UR Artifacts/Tempo as a clear #2 → by mid-format 17Lands (#738) and the Sunset Show (#743) the settled read is **Boros #1 and "all four white color pairs" as the top tier**; UR/UW are fine but not the format-definers. **Verdict: the early "UW/UR are the two best" line is SUPERSEDED — the format is a Boros/white-pairs format.**
- **Galvanize** — #735/#738 (early) treated it as the premier red trick and threat benchmark → end-of-format it's a fine 2-mana removal trick but not the best red common (Person of Interest / Shock outpace it on 17Lands). **Verdict: solid, not top — and Shock is often better (kills disguise creatures for 1 less, instant). DOWN slightly.**
- **Red Herring** — #735: "Okay I guess," underpowered Valley Dasher comparison → #743: **significantly overperformed** — haste + sac-as-blocker in the 2nd main + always replaces itself made it a real aggro/sac player. **Verdict: UP in dedicated aggro/artifact-sac shells.**
- **Goblin Maskmaker** — #735: lukewarm, the one-drop they liked least → #743: **actually good** — ramps disguise creatures out a turn early; with 7-8 disguise creatures, 2 copies was a build-around. **Verdict: UP in disguise-dense decks.**
- **Crimestopper Sprite** — #735: looked solid (C/C+) → #743: **underperformed** — stats line up poorly vs. disguise 2/2s, Collect Evidence 6 is too high, and it stays a 2/2 late. **Verdict: DOWN.**
- **Unscrupulous Agent** — #735: liked (Ravenous Rats pedigree) → #743: **underperformed** — not a bad card, dragged down by black being the worst color. **Verdict: DOWN (color-tax, not card-quality).**
- **Green overall** — early: looked mediocre → #743: **made a comeback** — its cards work *together* even if none is individually great (the opposite of black). **Verdict: UP — green is a real color.**
- **Black overall** — early: had cards that looked fine (Murder, Long Goodbye, Extract a Confession all graded C+/B-) → #743: **the worst color "by so much"** — penalized every pair it was in; even its good cards were suppressed. **Verdict: DOWN, hard. Avoid black unless you open a bomb rare.**

## Format speed / meta read

- **MKM is the fastest format LR had ever seen on 17Lands** (#737: fewest average turns ever recorded; highest on-the-play win% advantage ever, ~53.5%+). Marshall's settled grade is **B− with a 7/10 speed soapbox** — playable, balanced enough, but build-arounds got run over and the die-roll matters too much. "Draft white, draft a low curve — that is basically where this set is at" (LSV, #743).
- **Be on the play, have a turn-2 play, curve out.** Not having a board impact on 2 of the first 3 turns loses to *most* decks, not just aggro. On the draw with no two-drop into the opponent's turn-2 AND turn-3 play ≈ 20% to win (#743). Win the die roll.
- **White is the best color, not close** (#737, #738, #743 — consistent the whole run). All four white pairs are the top tier. White commons are the best commons in the set; white has the best removal.
- **Black is the worst color "by so much"** (#743) — it drags down every pair it touches (Rakdos, Dimir, Golgari all bottomed out). **Avoid black unless you open a bomb rare** (#739: "this is why you draft black — not for the common depth, but for the bomb rares"). Good players avoid black too, so black bombs flow late — when you ARE there, you can get paid off.
- **Green made a comeback; red is very good; blue is good.** Settled color order ≈ **White > Red > Blue ≈ Green >> Black** (#743).
- **Good two-drops are scarce and sacred; four-drops are everywhere.** The format is *defined* by having good 2-drops (Novice Inspector is effectively the #1 common in the set). Even mediocre 2-drops (Seasoned Consultant, Repeat Offender) make the deck because you must affect the board early. Don't overload on 4-drops.
- **Disguise / ward-2 is present but NOT format-defining** (#738) — MKM won't be remembered as a "morph set." Most disguise commons are mediocre: a face-down 3-mana 2/2 ward-2 played as your *first* play already feels behind. **Disguise is curve-filler, not a plan.** 3-toughness on expensive flip creatures is a specific liability (dies to Galvanize).
- **Ward-2 warps removal evaluation; it felt heavy-handed.** Many removal spells literally print "can't be countered" (Slice from the Shadows, Long Goodbye, Out Cold, Suspicious Detonation) to patch around ward. Cheap instant removal (Shock, Galvanize, Makeshift Binding) is king; 3+ mana removal needs the "uncounterable" clause or extra value to compensate.
- **Combat tricks are better than usual** because disguise/face-down plays make the defender fear them — which (counterintuitively) *speeds* the format. Unauthorized Exit / bounce is premium partly because it blows tricks out.
- **Clues / investigate are ubiquitous and a core mechanic to exploit.** Good decks (UW, UR, even Boros off its tricks) generate Clues incidentally and never run out of gas — which also makes pure control harder (everyone has card advantage). LR's best mechanic grade went to **Collect Evidence** (#743): well-designed, easy to enable, cards are priced to cast without it.
- **Cases are fine (synergy rewards, not busted); Suspect is LR's least-liked mechanic** (#743) — Menace + can't-block makes blocking less relevant in an already-fast format.
- **Extraordinarily bomb-heavy rare pool** (#736) — "rares that pretty much just take over or end games." But the Sunset "Pauper or Prince" verdict is **Pauper** (#743): disguise blunts bombs enough that an all-common low-curve Boros deck feels fine against a good rare. Bombs matter, but you don't *need* them outside black.
- **Best-of-3 plays slower / decks are a little worse** (#739) — don't panic at a marginally worse BoX deck; sideboard tricks (Make Your Move main 1-2, Pick Your Poison) matter more.

## Archetypes

Ranked by the settled Sunset Show (#743) read, cross-checked against the mid-format 17Lands win rates LR cited in #738 (shown where given). Release-week archetype hype (UW/UR #1-2) is superseded.

### Boros (RW) — Aggro / Go-wide — STRONGEST / #1
- **Plan:** Curve out with cheap creatures, close with combat tricks or an **On the Job** go-wide explosion. Extremely redundant — even a suboptimal build wins because the pieces are interchangeable. **60.2% win rate at ~60,000 games mid-format (#738) — best deck by a wide margin in BOTH win rate and volume, and it isn't self-correcting because it's that deep.**
- **Curve:** Novice Inspector / Frantic Scapegoat (1) → Reckless Detective / Marketwatch Phantom / any functional 2-drop (2) → Inside Source / Dog Walker / On the Job (3) → Person of Interest (4, don't stack these).
- **Key cards:** Novice Inspector (best common in the deck), Dog Walker (multiple tokens), Inside Source (token-maker, contested with WU), On the Job (the breakout — pseudo-overrun that also makes a Clue; "would have been good without the Clue"; 2 copies is a build-around), Reckless Detective (the "nut curve" partner to Novice Inspector), Shock / Galvanize / Makeshift Binding (removal), Auspicious Arrival (pump + Clue), Haazda Vigilante, Case of the Gateway Express, Lightning Helix.
- **Strength:** Redundant; needs almost no rares; generates Clues off its own tricks/creatures so never runs out of gas. **Be predisposed to white** — look for reasons to STAY white, not exit.
- **Weakness:** Most-contested deck at the table; don't be the 3rd white drafter in the pod. Person of Interest is a trap to over-prioritize (4-drop clog). Aim for **0 Suspicious Detonation** (the pay-5 mode is terrible).

### Azorius (WU) — Detectives / Tempo — STRONG / top white tier
- **Plan:** Low-curve detective tempo. Curve out with detectives, use Clue card-draw to play both early and late. "On rails" — easy to build, naturally cohesive. (#737 called it the best deck — that early hype is **superseded** by Boros, but WU stays in the top white tier.)
- **Key cards:** Novice Inspector, Makeshift Binding, Inside Source, On the Job (better here in some builds), Private Eye (detectives get +1/+1 and become unblockable when you crack a Clue — game-winning payoff), Perimeter Enforcer (1/1 flying lifelink that grows with detectives — hard to cut), Marketwatch Phantom, Seasoned Consultant, Granite Witness, Out Cold, Cold Case Cracker (3/3 flyer for 4 that makes a Clue on death — perfect statline), Thinking Cap (equip-1 on a detective; only good here, where you make extra creatures).
- **Strength:** Premium white rares (Aurelia, Ezrim) push it over the top but aren't required.
- **Weakness:** Often short on hard interaction — don't be shy about bounce/tricks.

### Selesnya (GW) — Go-wide / Curve — STRONG (top white tier)
- **Plan:** White-core curve-out with green acceleration (Tunnel Tipster) and a few flip/counter payoffs. 58.0% win rate at ~15k games mid-format (#738, #2 by data).
- **Key cards:** Novice Inspector, Dog Walker, On the Job, Tunnel Tipster (ramps + grows off face-down ETBs), Nervous Gardener (fixing + body), Sumala Sentry / Crowd Control Warden (disguise-counter payoffs), Buried in the Garden (A-: O-Ring + ramp aura, a real reason to be GW), Trostani / Tolsimir as bomb top-ends.
- **Note:** It's "a white deck at its core" — you compromise the green plan more than the white plan.

### Orzhov (WB) — Curve / Value — STRONG but black-taxed (weakest white pair)
- **Plan:** Aggressive curve that can play slightly longer than the other white pairs; vampires/disguise drain, Teysa value engine. 56.8% at ~17k games mid-format (#738) — but the Sunset read tempers it because **black is bad**, so this is the weakest of the four white pairs.
- **Key cards:** Teysa, Opulent Oligarch (A; first-pickable — attack → investigate EOT → crack Clues → 1/1 flyers → snowball; "absurd once it gets going"), Wisp-Drinker Vampire (B+; 2/4 flyer, drains when a power-2-or-less creature enters, grants deathtouch+lifelink), Sanguine Savior, Dog Walker, Inside Source, Call a Surprise Witness (recur a creature with a flying counter — great Teysa backup), Long Goodbye, Extract a Confession (black's best common), Kaya, Spirits' Justice (splashable PW bomb).
- **Note:** A two-Teysa + Call a Surprise Witness build keeps a Teysa in play almost always. Soul Search can exile their removal before you commit Teysa.

### Simic (GU) — Defensive-speed Midrange — GOOD (best non-white; the format's saving grace)
- **Plan:** Survive the early game with cheap blockers, counters, and bounce; transition to card advantage via Clue-cracking and flipping large disguise creatures that dwarf aggro's board. 56.7% at only ~9.4k games (#738) — above average but heavily UNDER-drafted, so it needs to be open. LSV (#743): without Simic being good the format "would have fallen apart into a very one-note thing."
- **Marshall's core trifecta:** **Reasonable Doubt** (2-mana counter — counter the thing, rarely wait), **Unauthorized Exit** (2-mana bounce — blows out tricks, bounces a disguise creature at 4), **Deduce** (cycling cantrip + Clue — if they give you nothing to counter, fire it and keep the loop).
- **Key defensive creatures:** Jaded Analyst (2-mana 3/2 defender — LSV's favorite; run it out and block everything), Cold Case Cracker (4-mana 3/3 flyer + Clue on death), Vitu-Ghazi Inspector (1/3 reach), Evidence Examiner (2/2 + Clue).
- **Finishers:** Surveillance Monitor (3/3 that makes a Thopter each Collect Evidence — engine with Evidence Examiner), big disguise creatures (Undercover Crocodelf(?), Vengeful Creeper, Topiary Panther) that flip into 5/5+ bodies, Out Cold, Doppelgang (fine at 5 mana, game-over at 8 — Simic can actually ramp there; LSV's favorite card).
- **Weakness:** Must be open — don't force it. You'll face Boros ~6-7x more than the mirror, so build for it. Lower your bar: cheap 2-for-1s are good enough, you don't need perfect sequences. Don't over-value pump tricks here. Stay close to pure GU (Nervous Gardener enables a light splash at most).

### Izzet (UR) — Artifacts / Clues / Thopters / Removal-Tempo — FINE
- **Plan (two flavors):** (a) **Artifact tempo** — Gleaming Geardrake ("the king" uncommon that opens the lane), Gadget Technician, Detective's Satchel, thopters; or (b) **removal/draw control** — LSV's #739 walkthrough deck: triple Galvanize + double Torch the Witness + Shock + double Deduce, kill everything and draw cards, curve topping at 4 ("you don't need to go above 4"), **16 lands** with that many cheap spells + surveil duals.
- **Key cards:** Gleaming Geardrake, Gadget Technician (3/2 for 4 face-up + a thopter; flexes 3-drop or 4-drop), Harried Dronesmith (makes a haste thopter every combat), Detective's Satchel, Maverick Thopterist ("almost a bomb"), Shock, Galvanize, Torch the Witness (LR's "mythic uncommon of the format" — 1 color = max flexibility, red goes with everything but black), Out Cold, Unauthorized Exit, Reckless Detective, Deduce, Lamplight Phoenix (don't cast until 4 evidence in the yard or it never comes back), Fugitive Codebreaker, Red Herring (triggers Forensic Gadgeteer + sac → Galvanize for 5).
- **Strength:** LSV "liked drafting it"; the Dronesmith + Satchel thopter factory is real.
- **Weakness:** Don't take 3/2 mid-bodies (Innocent Bystander) or Jaded Analyst in the removal-tempo build — that's for decks short on early plays. Krenko's Buzzcrusher being an *artifact* is a real downside in this set.

### Gruul (RG) — Disguise Aggro / Tricks — FINE
- **Plan:** Curve out with disguise bodies + combat tricks, top with flip-up haymakers. Yarus (A-: gives creatures haste, draws off face-down combat damage, recurs face-down creatures) and Anzrag are the centerpieces; explosive but less synergistic than UR.
- **Key cards:** Tin Street Gossip (4/4 vig + R/G for flip costs), Bolrac-Clan Basher (double strike trample), Riftburst Hellion (6/7 reach top-end), Goblin Maskmaker (**UP end-of-format** — ramps disguise creatures; 2 copies is a build-around), Fanatical Strength / The Chase Is On, Topiary Panther, Nervous Gardener.

### Rakdos (BR) — Suspect / Spells Midrange — WEAK (black-taxed)
- **Plan:** Suspect aggro + sacrifice + Detective's Satchel value. Looked fine on paper; the spells-matter package and black both underperformed — Judith, Carnage Connoisseur posted a **50.1% GIH WR** (vs. ~54-55% average), the data-backed proof the pair was non-functional (#743).
- **Key cards:** Rune-Brand Juggler, Clandestine Meddler (Menace + surveil — combos with Toxin Analysis fights), Deadly Complication, Blood Spatter Analysis, Detective's Satchel.

### Dimir (UB) / Golgari (BG) — Control / Graveyard — WEAK (black drags them down)
- **Dimir:** Pure control is hard because everyone has Clue card-advantage; playable only with bombs. Faerie Snoop is a fine flyer but unexciting.
- **Golgari:** The Chalk Outline / Insidious Roots / Aftermath Analyst graveyard build-arounds were **the format's biggest casualty** — too slow for a 7/10-speed format. Chalk Outline (B/A in a built deck) and Insidious Roots reward "creatures leaving the graveyard," but they brick against the curve. Not where you want to be unless the lane is wide open and you have multiple payoffs.

## Card interactions & combos

1. **On the Job + go-wide board** — flip/cast it on the opponent's turn and attack with everything; pseudo-overrun + a Clue. "Pre-groan test." Even as a +3/+3 trample on one creature + a Clue it's fine value. 2 copies is a build-around.
2. **Harried Dronesmith + Detective's Satchel** — Dronesmith makes a haste thopter each combat with an end-step sac trigger; sac it to the Satchel for a thopter token + Clue. Generates a flyer every turn — "becomes a factory." (Hold priority so Arena doesn't auto-sac the value away.)
3. **Crime Novelist + Harried Dronesmith** — every thopter being made/sacrificed triggers Crime Novelist for mana + a counter.
4. **Surveillance Monitor + Evidence Examiner** — Examiner makes a Clue; Monitor makes a Thopter each Collect Evidence; together you flood the board while drawing.
5. **Cryptic Coat loop** — pay 1U to bounce the Coat, 2U to recast; each cast cloaks another top-of-library card into a ward-2 unblockable and attaches. With UU up each turn you build a board of unblockables. (LSV: one of the best Limited cards he's seen.)
6. **Anzrag, the Quake-Mole + combat tricks** — when Anzrag is blocked, untap your team and get an extra combat; stack a +X/+X trample trick for 20+ damage.
7. **Shock + Galvanize** — ping in combat, then Galvanize the same or another target for the burst; classic 2-card removal/reach.
8. **Red Herring → sacrifice → Galvanize** — sac the Herring (clue-fish) to draw, then Galvanize for 5; also feeds Forensic Gadgeteer / Cornered Crook sac costs.
9. **Teysa, Opulent Oligarch + aggressive attacks** — only needs "opponent lost life this turn" to investigate EOT; crack the Clues for 1/1 flyers; the loop compounds fast.
10. **Teysa + Call a Surprise Witness** — attack with Teysa, trade it, recur it (now with a flying counter); the engine keeps resetting. Double Teysa + Call ≈ a Teysa in play almost always.
11. **Clandestine Meddler / Shady Informant + Toxin Analysis** — connect with a Menace creature, then use Toxin Analysis (deathtouch + lifelink + investigate) as a fight trigger off the combat damage to snipe a blocker.
12. **Goblin Maskmaker + disguise creatures** — sac the 1/1 to ramp a disguise creature into play a turn early; with 7-8 disguise creatures + 2 Maskmakers it's a build-around (**UP end-of-format**).
13. **Pyrotechnic Performer + any flip** — every time a creature you control turns face up, it deals damage = its power to each opponent. Sit on it and let every disguise flip chip away. ("Best red card in the set by win rate" per LSV's PT observation.)
14. **Presumed Dead on a disguise creature** — it dies, returns face-up as its full-power card (the line that lifts Presumed Dead from a D to playable).
15. **Tunnel Tipster + any disguise creature** — free +1/+1 counter whenever you play a face-down creature; runs away with the game on turn 2.
16. **Snarling Gorehound / Wisp-Drinker Vampire + power-2-or-less ETBs** — both reward small creatures entering; disguise creatures (face-down power 2) trigger them naturally.
17. **Case of the Crimson Pulse + hellbent** — once your hand is empty, draw 2 per upkeep (+1 on your draw step); a fun "don't deck yourself" sub-game in low-curve aggro.
18. **Doppelgang at 5 vs. 8 mana** — a clone at X=1 (5 mana, the safety valve), game-over at X=2 (8 mana). Simic can realistically ramp there.
19. **Coveted Falcon + Makeshift Binding (or any enchantment)** — give the opponent your enchanted permanent, then steal it back on attack; they keep the (now useless) enchantment. Rewards a white enchantment sub-theme.

## Card tips


Grades below are LR's **release-week set-review predictions** (#735 commons/uncommons, #736 rares/mythics) on roughly an A+→F scale (split grades shown as Marshall/LSV). The Sunset Show (#743) late verdict wins on conflict and is flagged.

### White (W)
- **Novice Inspector** — B (review) but the **#1 common in the format end-of-format**. 1-mana detective that makes a Clue; auto-pick, splashable, never cut. "A messed up card."
- **Aurelia's Vindicator** — A+. "Almost certainly the best card in the set." 4/2 flying lifelink ward 2; disguise hides the cost, flips to exile multiple creatures (they return to YOUR hand if it leaves). LR's best bomb.
- **Wojek Investigator** — A. 2/4 flying vigilance angel that blocks everything and draws continuously.
- **Makeshift Binding** — B. 2W O-Ring + gain 2 life; the extra value beats ward-2 evaluation; first-pick removal.
- **On the Job** — D+ (#735, wildly low) → **breakout uncommon, top tier** (#738/#743). The early grade is **superseded** — pseudo-Overrun that makes a Clue; "put 17 creatures in your deck and it works."
- **Perimeter Enforcer** — B. 1/1 flying lifelink that grows with detectives; hard to cut in WU/Boros.
- **Inside Source** — C+ (review) but a top-pick token-maker in white aggro (1/1 + a 2/2 token + a detective pump for 3 mana).
- **Make Your Move** — main-deck 1+ (2 in BoX); destroys artifact/enchantment/power-4+ creature — hits a huge share of MKM's rares and big disguise flips.
- **Defenestrated Phantom** — C (review) → "one of the worst disguise creatures" (#743). 5 mana to flip into a 3-toughness 4/3 flyer isn't enough. Avoid.
- **No Witnesses** — C. White 5-mana wrath, but the opponent (most creatures) usually gets a Clue back; weak wrath.
- **Doorkeeper Thrull** — D/F. Doesn't even stop disguise reveals; wrong set for the effect.

### Blue (U)
- **Cryptic Coat** — A+. Near-infinite ward-2 unblockable threats for 3 mana; one of the best Limited cards LSV has seen.
- **Cold Case Cracker** — B-. 3/3 flyer for 4 + Clue on death; perfect statline for WU/GU defense; happy to trade into anything.
- **Out Cold** — C. Uncounterable tap-two + investigate; want multiples in blue tempo. "Finish off the game."
- **Deduce** — C- (review) but a "really pushed" cantrip in practice (#737): 2-mana draw + Clue; double Deduce = pass with 2 up and don't care which you draw.
- **Reasonable Doubt** — C+/C. 2-mana counter (pay 2 + suspect); "good in every format"; core Simic piece; combos with holding Clue mana. Cut extras in removal-flush decks.
- **Unauthorized Exit** — bounce + surveil 1; premium Simic tempo, blows out tricks, bounces a disguise creature at 4.
- **Jaded Analyst** — D (review) → underplayed-but-good 2/3 defender in Simic (LSV's favorite blocker). Skip it in aggressive/removal-flush decks.
- **Hard Evidence** — "best blue common" (a reprint, #739); cheap card-flow + a Clue.
- **Crimestopper Sprite** — C/C+ (review) → **DOWN** (#743): poor stats vs. disguise, CE 6 too high, stays a 2/2. (See Supersessions.)
- **Benthic Criminologists** — D. 4UU 4/5 that sacs artifacts to draw — redundant with Clues already drawing; "just bad" (but a "pleasant surprise" as a clue-deck 5-drop late, #743).
- **Living Conundrum** — Build-around B (review) → "after extensive testing, not really a thing" (#739/#743); LSV loved it conceptually, it never worked.
- **Behind the Mask** — D. Two bad modes stapled together.

### Black (B)
- **Vein Ripper** — A+. Triple-black 6/5 flyer; ward = they sac a creature, drains 2 on every creature death. Absolute bomb (triple-black is the only caveat).
- **Massacre Girl, Known Killer** — A. 2BB 4/4 menace, your creatures have wither, draw when their creatures die small. Punishes combat AND removal.
- **Homicide Investigator** — A-. 1B 2/2; investigate whenever a non-token creature you control dies (once/turn). Trivial to trigger, repeated card advantage.
- **Extract a Confession** — C+ (review) → **black's best common** (over a point higher than Murder on 17Lands, #743). Edict bypasses ward 2 and fuels Collect Evidence.
- **Long Goodbye** — C+. 1B uncounterable removal (MV≤3); great vs. cheap disguise flips, awkward vs. a flipped MV-4+ creature. Black's best removal common but don't take black early for it.
- **Deadly Cover-Up** — B. 5-mana BB wrath; reliable, the bonus hand/library exile rarely matters. Control shells only.
- **Unscrupulous Agent** — C+/B- (review) → **DOWN** (#743): good pedigree, but black being bad suppressed it. (See Supersessions.)
- **Murder** — C+/B-. Serviceable but passive; "you never feel ahead." Below Extract a Confession; black is bad anyway.
- **Polygraph Orb** — F. 5-mana Read the Bones with a weak ability; bad even in black.

### Red (R)
- **Incinerator of the Guilty** — A/A+. 6/6 flying trample; on combat damage, Collect Evidence X to deal X to all their creatures/planeswalkers. Trample = it almost always connects = it ends games.
- **Torch the Witness** — B (review) → **LR's "mythic uncommon of the format"** (#743). XR scalable removal + investigate; 1 color = goes in every red deck. Premium.
- **Shock** — B-/C+. Best red common conversation; kills disguise 2/2s for 1 mana, instant. LSV ran 3 at the PT and "was closer to wanting a fourth than cutting one." Never side it out.
- **Person of Interest** — C+ (review) → **most powerful red common by 17Lands** (#743). 4 mana for a 2/2 + a 2/2 detective token.
- **Lamplight Phoenix** — A-. Recurring 3/3 flyer; great stats + Collect-Evidence recursion (don't cast under 4 evidence).
- **Pyrotechnic Performer** — A-. See combos; LSV's "best red card by win rate" from the PT.
- **Reckless Detective** — B/B-. 2-mana 0/3 that filters cards and taxes trades; the "nut curve" partner to Novice Inspector; "cracked." Don't run it in decks already flush with early plays.
- **Galvanize** — B (review). 2-mana deal 3 (5 if you drew 2+). Fine removal trick; **Shock is often better** (1 less vs. disguise, instant).
- **Krenko, Baron of Tin Street** — B+. Snowballs goblins off artifacts/Clues.
- **Felonious Rage** — **UP end-of-format** (#743) — "looks kind of bad" so it flowed; LSV played multiples at the PT. Underrated trick that makes a detective token if the creature dies.
- **Red Herring** — C (review) → **UP** (#743): real in aggro/sac shells (haste, sac-as-blocker, replaces itself).

## Pitfalls

- **Be predisposed to white; have a turn-2 play; curve out.** Take the good two-drop over the expensive removal when the curve needs it. Not having a board impact early loses to most decks. Win the die roll.
- **Avoid black unless you open a bomb rare.** Black is the worst color "by so much" and taxes any pair. The upside: black bombs (Vein Ripper, Teysa, Massacre Girl) flow late because good drafters avoid black too — when you commit to black for a bomb, you can get paid off, but don't go there speculatively.
- **Don't lean on disguise as your curve.** A turn-3 face-down 2/2 as your *first* play is already behind. Disguise is supplement/filler, not a plan. Watch the 3-toughness liability vs. Galvanize on flip creatures.
- **Don't jam expensive/passive removal.** Murder fills you up without putting you ahead; over-loading on it loses badly. Cheap instant interaction (Shock, Makeshift Binding) and defensive 2-drops instead.
- **Tap-lands cost games.** Public Thoroughfare is near-unplayable; cap tap-lands in aggressive decks; the format punishes a tapped turn-2.
- **Collect Evidence is free; clues are incidental — don't warp picks for them.** The graveyard fills naturally from trading; good decks (UR, UW, Boros) make Clues without forcing the archetype. Don't "Turbo Flotsam // Jetsam" — opponents top out at 3-4 mana and you mill back commons.
- **Aim for 0 Suspicious Detonation; don't over-prioritize 4-drops** (Person of Interest clogs the curve in aggro). Don't take 3/2 mid-bodies or Jaded Analyst in removal-tempo UR.
- **Land count:** **17 default**; **16 in low-curve removal-tempo UR** (LSV's #739 deck, with surveil duals + many cheap spells: "keep Eliminate the Impossible, cut an Out Cold, go 16"). Build-arounds got left behind, so don't draft 18-land control without real payoffs.
- **Best-of-3:** decks are a little worse and the format a little slower — don't panic. Main-deck Make Your Move (1-2), pack Pick Your Poison, and don't side out a trick the opponent has already seen — swap for a *different* unseen trick; the unknown is worth more in game 3.
- **Build-arounds are a trap in a 7/10-speed format.** Chalk Outline / Insidious Roots / Aftermath Analyst graveyard piles and collect-evidence engines were the format's biggest casualties — only commit to a build-around with multiple payoffs and an open lane.

## Supersessions (newer take wins)

- **Prismari aggro / "the blue-red beatdown deck"** — set review and #851 (early) imagined a real blue-red prowess/opus aggro plan (Elemental Mascot, Spectacular Skywhale, Expressive Firedancer, Deluge Virtuoso, Muse Seeker) → by mid-format and #857 the verdict is **"the deck never materialized"** (LSV: "I don't think I ever had a blue red aggro deck that I was like this is great"). The blue-red SPELLS deck (Prismari Converge / All-Spells) is the real deck; the aggressive prowess cards are scrap. **Verdict: shift the whole prowess/opus-aggro creature suite DOWN at least a full grade; do not draft a UR creature-aggro plan.**
- **Quandrix (GU) as its own archetype** — set review and #851 sketched a real GU tempo/increment deck → #857: **"Quandrix basically didn't exist. It was just part of the fivecolor deck."** A standalone GU tempo deck is a chase-the-dragon experience; almost every Quandrix draft folds into Prismari-Converge / 5c soup. **Verdict: don't force GU as a 2-color deck — base it blue-red and splash green for fixing/ramp.**

"It really has ruined us for every mana rock for a long time." Mid-format LR was sorting commons by Trove count *first* when opening sealed pools. **Verdict: HUGE UP. ## Format speed / meta read

- **SOS is a polarized two-pole format.** LSV's one-sentence summary: **"5c soup vs. White-X aggro."** Almost every match involves one of those two players. Most other "decks" (Quandrix, UR aggro, Witherbloom mid-range) collapse into one of the two poles or don't get drafted at all.
- **White is the strongest color, not close.** Silverquill (WB) and Lorehold (RW) are the **two best-performing archetypes mid-format** — week-one data: RW 58.1% / WB 57.4% / UR ≈ UG ≈ 55–56% / BG 54.3% (#851). Settled #857 read: **"if you're only thinking about winning and the table is relatively open, you should be in white-black or white-red."**
- **Be on the play.** LSV (#852): **"I just don't think you're supposed to choose to go second in this day and age."** Every deck has card-advantage mechanisms; spending your mana every turn matters more than the extra card. Marshall agreed: "this is one of the slowest sealed formats we've had and I wanted to be on the play every single time." Arcane Omens existing alone is a reason to be on the play.
- **The set is bomb-heavy at rare, but the Pauper-or-Prince verdict is Pauper-ish.** #857: **"you could construct a great deck without any rares"** — the aggro decks need rares less than the soup decks. Rares matter, but the tipping point is that **most rares can be killed with common removal** and counter spells are actually good.
- **Removal is good across every color and every rarity.** Cheap red removal at 2/3/5 mana for 2/4/6 damage (Tome Blast, Tomb Blast(?), Heated Argument(?), Unsubtle Mockery, Rubble Rouser); Last Gasp + Wander Off in black; Erode + Johnny's Response(?) + Wilt in the Heat in white; Banishing Betrayal + Essence Scatter + Brush Off in blue; Grapple with Death in green-base. Don't play random fragile creatures in spell decks — they die for free.
- **Format has a "5+1" archetype structure.** Five school pairs (BW/UR/BG/RW/UG) plus the unwritten 6th archetype, **Converge / 5c Soup**, which is wide open and the most fun to draft (LSV's #857: "ice cream for dinner — it can be a lot, but it's a really fun experience"). Wither bloom is a noticeable step behind; Quandrix doesn't really exist as a 2-color deck.
- **Speed-by-archetype:** Silverquill is the **fastest deck** in the format, but **"still a little shy of pure aggression"** — it has Killian's Confidence + Pull from the Grave + Pursue the Past, so it can play long. Lorehold is **closer to mid-range than aggro**, with assertive pressure from Elite Interceptor + Pursue the Past + flashback. **You're not playing a 1-2-3 aggro deck in either** — both are assertive but have grindy elements.
- **Don't pre-commit to a school early; commit to white.** Open a strong white card (rare, Elite Interceptor, Scolding Administrator, Stirring Honormancer) and let pack 1 tell you whether black or red is open. The win-rate gap between WB and RW is small enough that you should just play the open lane.
- **In the 5c soup deck, your removal slate doubles as your draw slate.** Stress Dream (5-mana deal-5 + slight-of-hand effect), Traumatic Critique, Together as One, Stock Up — these "spells" all do two things at once. That's how the deck out-cards Witherbloom.
- **Mechanics played out:** Prepare ≈ fine (most prepared creatures are pseudo-ETBs — "can you kill it in one turn? No, I'm casting the spell"). Repartee ≈ disappointing ("not very loud — in three years you'll be like 'what was that one?'"). Opus ≈ would have been great if UR aggro existed, suffered because it didn't. Infusion ≈ basic, fine. Lorehold's flashback/leave-graveyard ≈ won the design — actually works this time. Increment ≈ good mechanic, suffered because Quandrix wasn't its own deck. Converge + Paradigm + Book(?) ≈ great. **The format wins were the bonus sheet (mystical-archive-style, one in every pack, including premium reprints like Brainstorm / Ancestral Recall on the Emeritus cycle, Last Gasp, Burst Lightning, Stock Up, Library of Alexandria, Bitter Triumph, Disdainful Stroke) and the converge / 5c soup deck.** Marshall's final grade: **A−**.

## Archetypes

Ranked by the settled Sunset Show (#857) read, cross-checked against week-one 17Lands win rates from #851 (shown where given). The five "schools" plus the unwritten 5c-soup deck.

### Lorehold (RW) — Aggro / Mid-Range / Flashback — STRONGEST / #1
- **Plan:** Curve out with Elite Interceptor + cheap creatures, generate value through Pursue the Past + flashback + leave-graveyard payoffs. **Not pure aggro** — it has enough grindy elements (Pursue the Past, Pull from the Grave from a black splash, Arc of Hunger) to play a long game and still win. **58.1% week-one GIH WR (#851) — best deck by win rate.**
- **Curve:** Elite Interceptor (1) → Spirit Mascot / Practiced Scrollsmith / Inkling Mascot(?) (2) → Carrel, History Buff / Pursue the Past / Charging Strife Knight (3) → Garrison Excavator / Startled Relic Sloth / Hardened Academic (4) → Colossus of the Blood Age (5).
- **Key cards:** Pursue the Past (#1 common in the format by win rate — "the engine"), Elite Interceptor (the Thraben Inspector of the format — "if you never pass Elite Interceptor you're well on your way to drafting this right"), Tome Blast (premium burn), Unsubtle Mockery (often the most-picked red common), Rubble Rouser (best card if your deck can use it), Practiced Scrollsmith (B+ — "if this isn't good, the deck doesn't work"), Startled Relic Sloth (4-mana 4/4 trample lifelink — pushed), Hardened Academic (RW 2/1 flying haste, enabler + payoff), Wilt in the Heat (4-mana exile-instant, sometimes 2-mana), Spirit Mascot (delivered better than the C- review).
- **Premium uncommons:** Carrel, History Buff (auto-include), Garrison Excavator (B+ in Lorehold, shrug elsewhere), Stirring Honormancer (5-mana can't-trip threat).
- **Key rares/mythics:** Lorehold the Historian (the dragon), Practiced Offense (A — "complete game-ending card for 5 mana, even off one creature"), Stirring Hopesinger ("absolutely absurd"), Aziza, Mage Tower Captain (B+ — copy your own removal), Suspend Aggression, Ark of Hunger ("never beaten it — unbeatable artifact"), Resonating Lute (build-around all-spells finisher, but UR more than RW).
- **Strength:** Redundant; one of the few decks that beats both 5c soup (under it) AND the aggro decks (close races + Pursue the Past).
- **Weakness:** Vulnerable to a stumbled curve since 17Lands is showing all-aggro-curve as the highest win rate. Don't be the 3rd white drafter at the table.

### Silverquill (WB) — Reparte / Curve / Drain — STRONG / #2
- **Plan:** Curve out with reparte creatures; every time you target a creature (yours or theirs) something pops. **Fastest deck in the format**, but still tempered with Killian's Confidence + Pull from the Grave value. **57.4% week-one GIH WR (#851).**
- **Curve:** Scolding Administrator (2) → Inkling Mascot / Honorbound Page (2-3) → Imperious Ink Mage / Abigail, Poet Laureate / Stone Docent (3) → Eager Glyph Mage / Stirring Honormancer (4-5).
- **Key cards:** Scolding Administrator (B+ — "two mana 2/2 menace that grows and gives away counters; if you don't kill it right away it stacks two counters then dies for value"), Inkling Mascot (solid 2/2 flier-on-demand), Killian's Confidence ("the card I lose the most to in Silverquill — way for them to trade turn 3 mana for card-plus-trigger-plus-counter every turn — that card is good at making sure you don't run out of gas"), Silverquill Charm (the best charm — pump / exile power-2 / 3-life drain at instant), Render Speechless (UP late — "I splash it every once in a while; it's so good in the suit mirror"), Imperious Ink Mage, Abigail Poet Laureate (B+ — repeatable prepare), Pull from the Grave ("really back-breaking — get your two best creatures back"), Cost of Brilliance, Eager Glyph Mage (the filler that's always great — 3W 3/3 that makes a 1/1 inkling token on ETB), Locust Spray, Send in the Pest, Honorbound Page (3W 3/3 first strike prepared), Stone Docent (1W 3/1 — "the cards that just ask nothing of you").
- **Premium uncommons/rares:** Stirring Honormancer (5-mana can't-trip rhino bard), Snooping Page (B — 3-mana 2/3 can't-be-blocked + draw + lose 1), Lecturing Scornmage, Inkshape Demonstrator (3W 3/4 ward 2 reparte lifelink — "even better in Silverquill"), Abigail, Poet Laureate.
- **Key rares/mythics:** Emeritus of Woe (3B mythic — 5/4 with Demonic Tutor attached as the reparte spell; the demonic tutor is just upside, all extra), Conciliator's Duelist (WWBB 4/3 + draw + blink), Moment of Reckoning (3WWBB — "destroy/return up to four"; turns any board into a win), Antiquities on the Loose (A- — flashback army-in-a-can), Stirring Hopesinger (3-mana 1/3 flier life-link that pumps your whole board on every reparte), Informed Ink-Treader (1W 2/2 vigilance — makes 1/1 fliers on every reparte), Nita Forum Conciliator.
- **Strength:** The fastest deck; the only deck that consistently punishes 5c soup's tap-out turns.
- **Weakness:** WWBB on Conciliator's Duelist / Moment of Reckoning is real — don't over-splash. Tragedy Feaster is a trap in Silverquill (you don't gain life consistently enough).

### Prismari (UR) — Spells / Converge / Removal — GOOD / #3 (the soup deck)
- **Plan:** Three flavors, but **only one of them actually wins**:
  - **(a) Straight UR all-spells:** ~zero creatures, tons of removal + counters + card draw + 1-2 finishers (Brain Freeze, Exhibition Tidecaller, Math &amp; Magic's(?), Traumatic Critique). LSV's preferred build.
  - **(b) Prismari Converge:** UR base + Potioner's Trove + 1-2 off-color duals + 2-4 converge cards (Together as One, Snarl Song, Arcane Omens, Sundering Archaic). "The deck I've done better with — still count on Stock Up and Quick Study and Vibrant Outburst from the UR base, but get to cast the converge bombs too." Difference from straight UR is "literally two Troves and two dual lands and a Terramorphic Expanse plus two converge cards."
  - **(c) Prismari Aggro (Opus prowess):** **dead on arrival**. Don't draft this.
- **Win conditions (most important — these decks need a closer):**
  - **Exhibition Tidecaller** (1U 2/1; mill 10 on any spell once you're at 6+ mana) — **61.4% GIH WR**. Don't run it out early; wait until you have protection and you can finish next turn.
  - **Math &amp; Magic's(?)** (Mathemagics — XXUU draw 2X) — 10 mana makes them draw 16 (often lethal); 12 mana makes them draw 32 (always lethal). "Looks like you won the game of solitaire — boom and their head explodes."
  - **Brain Freeze** (1U mill X+X cards per spell cast this turn) — the SECRET best card in the spell mirror; dead vs aggro. LSV: "live in fear of it."
  - **Wisdom of the Ages** (4UUU — return all instants/sorceries from yard, no max hand size). LSV: "I cast it, return 11 cards and my opponent's like 'I don't want to play this game anymore.'"
  - **Divergent Equation** (XXU — variable X-spell + recursion).
  - **Traumatic Critique** (XUR — kill creature + draw 2/discard 1; finisher when X scales).
  - **Together as One** (6-mana converge: draw X + deal X + gain X — the best card in the set).
  - **Resonating Lute (Loot?)** (2UR — lands tap for 2 of any color + draw card if 7+ cards in hand) — "Mana Flare plus Library of Alexandria for you. Every game I played it I won in a landslide."
- **Removal slate:** 10-12 removal cards. Stock Up (premium), Tome Blast (Firebolt-ish), Unsubtle Mockery, Cost of Brilliance, Last Gasp, Vibrant Outburst (deal 3 + tap one — can hit face!), Burst Lightning(?), Banishing Betrayal (bounce + surveil — buys time), Essence Scatter, Brush Off (the 2-mana counter when countering instants/sorceries — gold in the mirror).
- **Card draw:** Stock Up (best), Flow State (never cast without 2 cards already in graveyard), Quick Study, Deduce, Seize the Spoils, Visionary's Dance, Stress Dream (5-mana deal 5 + slight of hand — both removal AND card draw, very strong).
- **Counters:** want 1-3. Disdainful Stroke (fine; can miss late), Brush Off (gold in the mirror — "want one in all my decks, would play two"), Essence Scatter (want 2; gold for stopping a Tidecaller), Daze (great when on the play in heavy-island decks), Spell Pierce (LSV main-decks).
- **Build skeleton:** 10-12 removal / 5-7 card draw / 1-3 counters / 1-2 pure finishers / 0-2 mana rocks / no random creatures.
- **Key creature rule:** **"Really try not to put random creatures in your deck."** No Hydro Channelers, no Elemental Mascots, no Expressive Firedancers, no Muse Seekers. ETB creatures (the Lorehold 4/4 expel, the 3-mana 2/2 man-o'-war bounce) and dragons are the exceptions.
- **Strength:** Open at most tables; the converge angle gives you angles soup can't match.
- **Weakness:** Polarized matchups (counters dead vs aggro, cheap removal dead in the mirror). LSV: "lean more to beat the aggro decks; against control you can sometimes draw out of a bad start, against aggro you just die."

### Witherbloom (BG) — Mid-Range — WEAKEST OF THE FIVE SCHOOLS
- **Plan:** Gain life, infusion payoffs, pest tokens, drain attrition. The "least interesting" archetype (#857). **54.3% week-one GIH WR (#851) — bottom by half a point.**
- **Why it's bad:** **"Mid-range stuck in the middle"** — too slow to close vs Silverquill/Lorehold, too small to out-grind UR spells / 5c soup. Doesn't choose a lane in a polarized format.
- **When to draft it:** the lane is wide open (the cards literally nobody else wants — Mindful Biomancer, Noxious Newt, Pest Mascot — are in the bottom of every pack) AND you have a strong rare (Professor Dellian Fel, Vicious Rivalry, Cauldron of Essence, Tragedy Feaster).
- **Key cards:** Teachers Pest (B− — "the card that makes Witherbloom pop"), Grapple with Death (B+ — kills creature OR artifact + gains life — flexible removal), Bogwater Lumaret(?), Witherbloom Charm (mid charm), Pest Mascot (C+), Pull from the Grave (when splashing into Silverquill).
- **Key rares/mythics:** Witherbloom the Balancer (the dragon), Vicious Rivalry (A — one of the only sweepers in the format and life-gain triggered), Cauldron of Essence (B — sac engine; needs creatures to feed it), Professor Dellian Fel (mythic PW that takes over empty boards), Arnyn Deathbloom Botanist (disappointing — pests aren't dense enough), Tragedy Feaster (rare — works in dedicated Witherbloom because you can sac pests/lands, but loses a lot of luster if you can't gain life), Blech, Loafing Pest, Slumbering Trudge (B), Vastlands Scavenger (rare with deathtouch + bind-to-life).
- **Subtype:** A more aggressive "Wither Bloom Beatdown" exists that runs Biomancer + Noxious Newt + cheap curves; some PT players liked it. Marshall and LSV are skeptical but acknowledge the lane being open is the saving grace.

### Quandrix (UG) — Increment / Ramp — DOESN'T EXIST
- **Reality:** #857: **"Quandrix basically didn't exist. It was just part of the fivecolor deck."** LSV had one straight UG Tempo deck "chasing that dragon" all format and never got it again.
- **Plan in theory:** Ramp + Increment payoffs + X-spell finishers. The components are there, but the removal slate isn't.
- **Why it folds into Prismari Converge:** Trove already gives you all the fixing you need without committing to green, and Potioner's Trove takes the slot a Studious First-Year would have taken. Marshall's observation: **"good colorless fixing eats green's lunch."**
- **Key cards if you do try:** Studious First-Year (B− — best green common; 1-mana 1/1 that prepares Rampant Growth), Environmental Scientist (1G 2/2 that searches up a basic — Civic Wayfinder minus 1 mana, "somehow free"), Shared Roots (the bonus-sheet Rampant Growth), Quandrix Charm (the best charm by power level — pump, scry/draw, or destroy enchantment), Tester of the Tangential, Pterafractyl ("Terafractyl" — the great card name; XGU 1/0 flier that enters with X+1 counters; explosive at high X), Mind into Matter (XGU — draw X + put permanent into play tapped), Berta Wise Extrapolator, Skycoach Conductor (B+ rare — 2U 2/3 flier that blinks an ETB creature each combat; very strong in a tempo build), Matterbending Mage (LSVs's "right up my alley" — 2U 2/3 mana-wurm bounces a creature on increment trigger).
- **When it's actually open:** ~rarely. If you do open a great UG card (Mind into Matter, Skycoach Conductor) just call it Prismari Converge with a heavy green base and splash red.

### "5c Soup" / Prismari Converge — UNWRITTEN 6TH ARCHETYPE — STRONG (the most fun and the most common 3+ color deck)
- **Plan:** UR base + Potioner's Trove + 1-3 off-color duals + Together as One / Arcane Omens / Snarl Song / Sundering Archaic. Same removal slate and card-draw skeleton as Prismari, but you splash for the converge bombs.
- **The "soup" decks are NOT one deck** — there are multiple flavors:
  - **No-win-condition soup** (LSV's favorite) — "really has to cobble it together," cards like Cody (mana rock) + recursion + procrastinate.
  - **Splash white for removal** — Defiant Salvo(?) (the 1W "destroy creature with power 4+" off the bonus sheet) + Stand Up for Yourself.
  - **5c with multiple Troves** — 2 Troves "is the green light to just go nuts."
- **Key non-creature anchors:** Potioner's Trove (the key — see Supersessions), Together as One, Arcane Omens, Snarl Song, Sundering Archaic ("really good because it messes with the other graveyard — chews up Wisdom of the Ages targets while also stopping you from decking"), Stock Up, Resonating Lute, Vicious Rivalry, Moment of Reckoning, Choreographed Sparks (... F per LSV; ignore), Splatter Technique, Together as One, Mathemagics, Mind into Matter, Wisdom of Ages, Divergent Equation, Procrastinate (#857 from C- to fringe playable — "in the late game one of your best removal spells; lock a creature up for 6 cat turns").
- **Strength:** Plays all your good rares regardless of color. Best matchup vs Witherbloom. Engine cards (Wisdom of Ages → Snarl Song → Arcane Omens loop) just win games solitaire-style.
- **Weakness:** Mana is bumpy. Loses to fast white aggro draws. Aggro decks have removal that just rots in their hand vs your no-creature deck. **Lower-bar games:** "good draft = +1 wins; bad mana = -2."

### "Best of the rest" Mardu / Esper / random splashes
- LSV did "one Mardu deck that was pretty good" in sealed. Three-color white-base decks happen when you have multiple Lorehold + Silverquill payoffs, but a true 3+ color non-converge deck is rare.

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Wisdom of the Ages → recur all your removal/draw → win on inevitability.** "Cast it, return 11 cards, opponent concedes. They walk away thinking 'damn, almost got him' — no, you didn't."
2. **Divergent Equation → return Snarl Song + 2 spells → cast Snarl Song again → out of reach.** The 5c soup engine.
3. **Trove + Arcane Omens on turn 4 for X=3** — wipes their hand on a turn they normally tap out. "I lose to that card in my Prismari deck."
4. **Exhibition Tidecaller (6+ mana) → mill 10 → win OR force a kill spell.** Wait until late. Even if they kill it, you milled 10.
5. **Tablet of Discovery (turn 4 with island in hand) → Quick Study off the top → cast it via Tablet → free draw.** Mana-positive on a card-neutral card. (Tablet of Discovery: 2R 2/0(?), tap for 2 of any color, spend only on instants/sorceries.)
6. **Sky Coach Conductor + Matterbending Mage** — Conductor blinks Matterbending Mage for repeated ETB-bounce; Mage bounces a creature each time. Sick UG tempo combo.
7. **Resonating Lute + Wisdom of the Ages → 4UUU with a Lute in play casts Wisdom for 6 mana; draw 11, then untap and cast 22 mana of spells.** "Looked completely ridiculous, like I was playing a cube deck."
8. **Studious First-Year (1G prepare Rampant Growth on turn 2) → free converge enabler.** Best 1-drop in the format for any green-based shell.
9. **Scolding Administrator turn 2 → menace beats face → if killed, dumps 2 counters on your Inkling Mascot.** "Just hits with menace 3 times before they can even do anything."
10. **Killian's Confidence loop** — 2 mana: pump + draw card + later pay B/W to return Confidence from graveyard. Repeat once a turn for unlimited gas in Silverquill.
11. **Pursue the Past → discard land → draw 2 + gain 2 life + put card in yard for flashback** then **Pursue the Past flashback for 1RW → draw 2 again.** Engine + life + yard-fill.
12. **Emeritus of Woe (3B 5/4) → Demonic Tutor on ETB.** The Emeritus cycle is the bonus sheet's headline: literal Ancestral Recall (UEmeritus of Ideation), Demonic Tutor (Woe), Lightning Bolt (Conflict), Regrowth (Abundance), Swords to Plowshares (Truce). All A or A+ in their decks. Ideation is the most broken in the all-spells deck.

## General format tips

- **Open a strong white card → stay in white.** Both top decks are white-based. Let pack 1 tell you if black or red is the splash.
- **First-pick Potioner's Trove over almost everything else if you're not in white.** Or even if you are. Multiple Troves is the green light for 5c soup.
- **Build for the mirror you'll see most.** Half your matches will be against 5c soup. Counters (Brush Off, Disdainful Stroke, Essence Scatter) are gold in the mirror but blanks against aggro. Lean toward beating aggro because aggro draws don't let you draw out; control draws sometimes do.
- **Don't play random creatures in spell decks.** Just don't.
- **Don't try to draft UR aggro.** It doesn't exist. The cards that look like UR aggro (Elemental Mascot, Skywhale, Firedancer, Muse Seeker) are scrap.
- **Don't try to draft pure Quandrix.** It folds into Prismari Converge or 5c soup. Take green for fixing/ramp, not for a UG deck.
- **Don't draft Witherbloom unless the lane is screaming open** (Tragedy Feaster, Professor Dellian Fel, Vicious Rivalry, 2+ Teachers Pest in the bottom of packs).
- **Sealed: scan rares first, then COUNT YOUR TROVES (right-arrow → see colorless). Two Troves = go 5c.** With zero Troves, narrow to a 2-color shell or commit hard to green for fixing.
- **In sealed, default to going BIG / 5C SOUP.** The mana fixing is good enough that you can usually do it. The exception: if you have a really strong Silverquill or Lorehold core (Practiced Offense, Stirring Hopesinger, Informed Ink-Treader, Antiquities on the Loose, Killian's Confidence + creature curve), commit and prey on the slow soup mirrors.
- **In sealed, mill cards (Brain Freeze, Exhibition Tidecaller) are mirror-breakers.** ~60-65% of your matches will be the slow mirror.
- **Mind the X-spell wraths.** Wisdom of the Ages, Divergent Equation, Mathemagics, Together as One, Moment of Reckoning, Splatter Technique — these all just end games when fired on parity. Hold counters for them in the mirror.
- **Don't choose to draw.** Even in a slow format. Card-advantage mechanisms are everywhere; spending mana every turn matters more than the extra card. LSV: "I just don't think you're supposed to choose to go second in this day and age."
- **Land count:** **17 default.** Marshall's #855 walkthrough: "we have tons of stuff to do with our mana, I wouldn't go 16 here." Even the all-spells UR deck plays 17.
- **Best-of-3 is more interesting than usual** because of the polarized matchups. Sideboard counters in vs the mirror, board cheap removal in vs aggro.
- **Be a little extra paranoid about Arcane Omens.** "I have a category at the end here that are cards I lose to with my Prismari deck — Arcane Omens is on that list." Witherbloom should heavily prioritize it; everyone should respect it.

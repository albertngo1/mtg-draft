## ⚠ Recency rule (read first)

- **The newest episode wins on any conflict.** A later read supersedes an earlier one.

## TL;DR meta read

- **LR's settled color order: Green ≈ Black > White >> Red ≈ Blue** (#767), with the huge caveat that **the format self-corrected** and by the end all ten pairs were draftable *if you were the only one in them*.
- **Best two colors to START in: Green and Black** — "they branch out into the most good color pairs" (Marshall, #767). White is fine but branches less.
- **Format grade: solid B** (both hosts, #767). "Nice job, thumbs up." Praised for being self-correcting and accessible; dinged for an **on-rails draft** (once you're in a tribe you stop caring about non-tribe cards) and a fast, comeback-poor game pattern.
- **Speed: FAST — ~2nd-fastest Arena format ever** at the time (~8.6–8.7 avg turns), with a **~53.3% on-the-play win rate** (one of the swingiest die-rolls on record, #764). Not having a turn-2 play loses to most decks. Be on the play; build a low curve.
- **Pauper-or-Prince: PAUPER.** "You could have a deck with no rares and be fine" (Marshall, #767). Rares were good but very few were unbeatable (Season of Loss the exception). Commons carry the format.
- **Top commons (settled):** **Carrot Cake** (W — "we knew it'd be solid, ended up *great*, we got that one wrong"), **Cache Grab** (G — best green/Forage common), **Savor** + **Nocturnal Hunger** (B — best common removal), **Pond Prophet** (UG — the card that makes Frogs work, LSV's favorite), **Sunshower Druid** (G — "did good work on its own"), **Scales of Shale** (B trick — "more competitive with Savor than you'd think").
- **Best mechanic: Talents** (both hosts, #767) — "spawned entire decks by themselves… a card type I'd like to see more of." **Worst mechanic: Expend** ("played out worse than I thought — draw a land and your cards just sit there").

## Archetype / color-pair tiers

Ranked by the settled Sunset Show (#767) read, cross-checked against Sierkovitz's mid-format 17Lands data (#764). NOTE: #764 uses Ravnica guild shorthand for the pairs — translated here. The headline is **self-correction**: mid-format the spread top-to-bottom was only ~3% GIH WR, and by the end every pair could win a draft if it was open.

### Squirrels (BG) — Forage / Graveyard value — STRONGEST / #1
- **Plan:** Forage + Food + graveyard recursion grind. The deepest, most synergistic pair — "black-green most synergistic overall; can win with minimal *good* cards if the colors synergize" (Paul Cheon, #764). **Tied for the most-drafted AND highest-win-rate pair mid-format (~57%).**
- **Key cards:** Cache Grab (the breakout common — 2-mana Forage that returns a permanent + a Food in Squirrels), Bakersbane Duo (Food + body), Vinereap Mentor (3/2 Squirrel making Food on ETB/death), Corpseberry Cultivator (stacks with other Forage), Heaped Harvest (Food + ramp + re-Forage), Curious Forager, Scavenger's Talent (best-in-shell — Food on death → mill on sac → reanimate), Wick the Whorled Mind / Wick's Patrol (build-around: "cast it once you're good, twice you win"), Hazel's Nocturne, Camellia the Seedmiser, Ygra Eater of All (bomb).
- **Strength:** Highest floor in the set — the cards make each other better and you don't need rares.

### Frogs (GU) — Blink / Bounce / Spells — STRONGEST / #1 (LSV's favorite)
- **Plan:** Cheap value creatures + self-bounce/blink to re-trigger ETBs, grind out card advantage. **#1 archetype for top players by the late format** (#767), and the breakthrough that kept blue alive in a fast era. LSV: "my favorite Limited deck in a long time — I draft frogs more than I should."
- **Key cards:** **Pond Prophet** (the linchpin — UG 1/1 ETB draw; "overpaying for stats and drawing a card, just leeching, that's my jam"), Sunshower Druid (0/2 Forage value — "did great work on its own out of Pond Prophet's shadow"), Dour Port-Mage (draw when your creatures leave + a bounce engine), Splash Portal (blink), Kitnap (bounce your own Sunshower Druids for value), Long River Lurker, Three Tree Scribe (Frog build-around — "can go pretty nuts"), Dreamdew Entrancer(?), Helga Skittish Seer(?), Eddymurk Crab ("I've lost to that thing to break a race"), Eluge the Shoreless Sea (triple-U bomb).
- **Note:** Needs density and is technically demanding (Cirao ran a U-shaped 11–12 cheap drops + 5–6-drops curve and played 40-minute control games with it). Must be open — but when it is, it's elite.

### Rabbits (GW) — Go-wide / Offspring / Food — STRONG / top tier
- **Plan:** Curve out with Offspring tokens + go-wide payoffs; close fast. **Selesnya (GW) was tied for most-drafted and top-tier win rate (~57%, #764).** One of the "safe strong decks" early.
- **Key cards:** **Carrot Cake** (the all-star — "did more of what the Rabbit deck needed"; eats Food AND makes go-wide value), **Intrepid Rabbit** (3/2 Offspring 1 + team pump — phenomenal), Harvestrite Host, Treeguard Duo (grows + vigilance off Talents — "keeps impressing"), Brave-Kin Duo, Hop to It, Builder's Talent / Innkeeper's Talent (both elite, see Talents), Warren Warleader (bomb), Beza the Bounding Spring (bomb). LSV's build-around heuristic: "two Carrot Cakes + two things that eat Food + three ways to make Food justifies the whole package."
- **Note:** Wants on-curve creatures + go-wide payoffs; don't overload removal.

### Bats (BW) — Lifegain / Drain — STRONG (white-black, branches well)
- **Plan:** Lifegain triggers + drain attrition; one of the pairs G/B/W "branch into" cleanly. Solid mid-format, top-half of the pack.
- **Key cards:** Starscape Cleric (Bat-theme drain payoff), Moonrise Cleric (2/3 lifegain Bat), Lunar Convocation (LSV: "I love it, I probably overvalue it"), Zoraline Cosmos Caller (bomb), Starseer Mentor, Valley Rotcaller (menace + drain for your small creatures), Savor / Nocturnal Hunger (removal), Maha Its Feathers Night (bomb).

### Lizards (RB) — Aggro / Damage — GOOD
- **Plan:** Cheap aggressive bodies + burn/damage payoffs; "red-black kind of a head with blue and the all-blue pairs" mid-format (#767). The best red-based pair.
- **Key cards:** Scales of Shale (the format-defining trick — instant +X + gain 5 life; "more competitive with Savor than you think in a good RB Lizards deck — I'd take the Scales"), Cindering Cutthroat, Fireglass Mentor (card-advantage engine if you ping them), Hired Claw (A−, premium 1-drop), Flamecache Gecko, Gev Scaled Scorch (bomb), The Infamous Cruelclaw.

### Mice (RW) — Aggro / Valiant / Go-wide — GOOD but contested
- **Plan:** Low-curve aggro with Valiant (target your own creature) triggers. Assertive but eclipsed by Rabbits as the premier go-wide deck.
- **Key cards:** Heartfire Hero (1-drop Valiant all-star), Emberheart Challenger (2/2 haste prowess, Valiant), Manifold Mouse (A — Offspring 2 + double strike/trample grant), Might of the Meek, Brave-Kin Duo, Brightblade Stoat. **Pitfall (#764):** Carrot Cake is *worse* in RW than people think (no Food engine) — a "false friend" that's worse than Seedpod Squire here.

### Rats (UB) — Go-wide rats / Discard / Threshold — MIDDLING (better than it looked)
- **Plan:** Go-wide Rats + discard + Threshold payoffs. General perception was "bad," but **Dimir hit ~60% for top players** late (#764) — competitive in skilled hands, just under-drafted.
- **Key cards:** Tidecaller Mentor (3/3 menace + Threshold bounce), Mind Drill Assailant (fills yard for Threshold), Mindwhisker (rare payoff — "you destroy people with Mindwhiskers, really good in multiples"), Thought Shucker, Persistent Marshstalker, Vren the Relentless (bomb), Ruthless Negotiation / Feed the Cycle (cheap black value).

### Raccoons (RG) — Expend / Forage / Food — MIDDLING ("just okay," the last pair)
- **Plan:** Forage/Food goodstuff with Expend payoffs. The weakest pair most of the format (#767: "red-green the last pair, just okay"). Lighter on synergy than Squirrels — feels generic.
- **Key cards:** Wandertale Mentor (2/2 mana dork that grows off Expend — "my opponents always have it on turn 2, I draw it turn 7"), Bark-Knuckle Boxer, Junkblade Bruiser (Expend payoff, but five-drops are crowded), Muerra Trash Tactician, Hugs Grisly Guardian, Bakersbane Duo. **Expend is the worst mechanic** — whiffs on a land draw.

### Birds (WU) — Flyers / +1+1 counters — WEAK on average, ELITE when open (#2 ceiling)
- **Plan:** Evasive flyers + counter synergies. Mid-format this was a bottom pair and the mass read was "blue-white sucks, don't draft it." BUT for skilled, selective drafters late, **Azorius posted ~60% — the #2 archetype ceiling** despite being drafted least (#767). The signature "stick it out and you got rewarded" lane.
- **Key cards:** Plumecreed Mentor (boosts ground creatures — but "I've really not seen that card be good," #767 UNDER), Plumecreed Escort, Seedpod Squire, Kastral the Windcrested (bomb), Shrike Force, Jackdaw Savior (recurs flyers — "good in every white deck"). **Discipline required:** "you really have to know when it's time to go for Azorius."

### Otters (UR) — Spells / Prowess — WEAK (best in expert hands only)
- **Plan:** Spells-matter prowess/tempo. Badly positioned in a creature-combat format; the lowest-floor pair (Izzet bottom mid-format). LSV pulled ~70% on Arena with it, but "for the wide player pool, it's bad."
- **Key cards:** Stormcatch Mentor, Tempest Angler, Alania's Pathmaker (best Otter common, 4/2 impulse-draw), Coruscation Mage, Stormsplitter (build-around), Otterball Antics, Ral Crackling Wit / Alania Divergent Storm (bombs), Stormchaser's Talent (good card, bad deck). **Trap:** Harnesser of Storms (rare) — "missed opportunity, you have to play the card *this* turn and it doesn't line up."

## Format principles

- **Be on the play; have a turn-2 play; curve out.** ~53.3% on-the-play (#764). Not having a board impact early loses to most decks, not just aggro. Better players build a **U-shaped curve** (11–12 one/two-drops, a gap, then 5–6-drops) — that's the real edge separating skill cohorts (#764).
- **Start Green or Black; let pack 1 tell you the pair.** They branch into the most good pairs (BG, GW, BW, RB). Don't pre-commit to a pair pick 1 — but once you're ~6 picks deep into a tribe, the draft goes on rails (you stop caring about non-tribe cards), so commit and tighten synergy.
- **This is a typal/synergy format — cards have NARROW homes.** Don't think "green card," think "BG Food/Forage card." Carrot Cake is great in GW Rabbits and bad in RW Mice; Moonrise Cleric is bad outside BW Bats (#764). Mis-homing a "false friend" tribal card is the #1 build mistake.
- **Food + lifegain are disproportionately strong** in a fast, comeback-poor format — they buy the turns that let your synergy come online. Five of the six best commons make Food or gain life (Savor, Carrot Cake, Cache Grab, Scales of Shale, Bakersbane Duo, Intrepid Rabbit, #764).
- **Pauper-or-Prince = Pauper.** Draft a clean curve; you don't need bombs. Season of Loss is the one rare you genuinely "can't beat no matter what" (#767).
- **Removal is a touch weaker than usual** because creatures do the work — but cheap instant removal/tricks (Savor, Nocturnal Hunger, Sonar Strike, Scales of Shale) are still premium. Don't over-draft sorcery-speed removal (Banishing Light) early; you get run over before it matters.
- **Talents are the format's best build-arounds.** If you open Builder's, Innkeeper's, Hunter's, or Scavenger's, jam it and bend the deck toward it. Deciding *when* to level them is what makes them play more interestingly than Sagas (#763/#767).
- **The format is self-correcting — trust the table.** "Blue is bad / it's fast / it's on rails" were all true *early* and all partially wrong by the end. If a normally-bad lane (Azorius, Dimir, Otters) is wide open and you have payoffs, it can win the draft.

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Pond Prophet + Splash Portal / Kitnap / Dour Port-Mage** — bounce/blink your own Pond Prophet (and Sunshower Druids) to re-draw repeatedly; the Frogs value engine.
2. **Cache Grab in Squirrels** — Forage returns your best permanent *and* makes a Food (Squirrel bonus); the breakout green common.
3. **Heaped Harvest sequence** — play turn 3, sac for ramp/Food into a Forage trigger, net a card; great Squirrels glue.
4. **Carrot Cake + Food-eaters (GW)** — make Food, convert it to go-wide value; "two Cakes + two payoffs + three Food sources justifies the package" (LSV).
5. **Scavenger's Talent (Squirrels)** — Food on every creature death → level to mill on sac → level to reanimate; first mode alone is good enough, the full ladder is "disgusting."
6. **Builder's Talent + Bumbleflower's Sharepot** — Sharepot's two-permanent ETB triggers Builder's twice; with Offspring/permanent ETBs the +1/+1 and token engine snowballs.
7. **Innkeeper's Talent** — pile counters every turn → level 2 gives counter-permanents Ward 1 (un-removable) → level 3 doubles counters. "Wins games if left alone."
8. **Wick the Whorled Mind / Wick's Patrol loop** — recur in a BG ramp/graveyard shell with Hazel's Nocturne; "cast it once you're ahead, twice you win."
9. **Manifold Mouse (Offspring 2) + double-strike/trample grant** — broken in combat math; pumps a whole Mice/Valiant board.
10. **Downwind Ambusher (flash 4/2)** — flash in to blow out combat math every time it comes down.
11. **Hunter's Talent** — "the biggest groan test — slots into any deck that can cast it, asks nothing, and they got one of the best cards while you got a regular creature."

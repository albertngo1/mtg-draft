## TL;DR meta read

- **Multicolor / good-stuff format.** Gates provide fixing across 10 color pairs and reward building a multi-color value pile. The default end-state for many drafts is 3–4 color good stuff enabled by Gates and Courier's Briefcase, not a tight two-color synergy deck. Gates payoffs (Gate Colossus, Gates Ablaze, Archway Angel, Ubar Gatekeepers) are real but require deliberate drafting.
- **Format speed: midrange to somewhat aggressive.** Heroic/bestow creates go-tall aggro (Boros is the marquee aggro deck), but chunky 4/4s and 5/5s dominate. Ethan's initial fear of a hyper-aggro format did not fully materialize — green big-bodies and sacrifice/graveyard grinds were very competitive.
- **Best strategies (latest read):** Blue-Black exploit/delve (Dinrova Horror, Undead Butler, Heartpiercer Manticore), Black-Green value/grind (Possessed Scab loops, Catacomb Sifter, District Guide), and Blue-Green big creatures (Sky Rider Elf, Narback Rhino, Progenitor Mimic). Boros heroic is the aggro deck.
- **Key mechanics:** Heroic (target your creatures for value), Bestow (versatile auras-or-creatures), Monstrosity (upgrade once), Delve (fuel graveyard to cast large threats cheaply), Evolve (grow with better creatures), Gates-matters, Exploit (sacrifice for value), Devotion (bonus sheet only in Week 3).
- **Removal notes:** Dreadbore and Ultimate Price are the premium removal spells. Wild Slash was initially predicted to be top-tier common removal but underperforms — combat tricks and creature growth push things out of 2-damage range easily. Edict effects (Devour Flesh, Pker Predation (?)) are especially valuable because they bypass hexproof (important given Glade Cover Scout and Alpha Authority).
- **Curve / big-threats:** Having access to 4/4s and 5/5s is paramount. Opponents' large ground creatures (Narback Rhino, Gmag Angler (?), Woodland Wanderer) were repeatedly highlighted as the hardest thing to beat. Dreadbore was flagged as a premium specifically because it kills big creatures and Planeswalkers.
- **Bonus sheets change the format each week:** Planeswalkers week (Oaths, flip walkers), Spells week (fuse split cards, Bloodfire Enforcers (?)), Devotion week (Disciple of Phenax (?), frostburn weird, Karanos (?)). Devotion week makes monocolor more viable; Gates week pulls in the opposite direction.

## Format principles

1. **Gates are the primary fixing — prioritize them early.** 10 dual Gates plus Evolving Wilds and Courier's Briefcase allow aggressive multi-color splashing. Green Gates (Gruul, Golgari, Simic, Selesnya) are most contested because green is strong and multi-color green is the common landing spot.

2. **Courier's Briefcase is a sleeper engine card.** In a multi-color good-stuff deck, Briefcase provides ramp, fixing, and late-game card advantage. Combined with District Guide finding Gates and Possessed Scab recursion, it creates durable value chains. Ethan ran triple Briefcase and called the deck "insane."

3. **Possessed Scab (?) is one of the best commons/uncommons.** Recur any instant, sorcery, or creature every turn — this closes out games. Never cut the first copy, run as many as you can see; Ethan ran five and described cutting one as "Criminal."

4. **Undead Butler is a keystone common for black.** Mills 3 (fuels delve), provides exploit fodder, and on death recurs a creature. Powers up Treasure Cruise, Gmag Angler, and exploit synergies simultaneously. Pick it highly in any black deck.

5. **Removal that handles 4/4s and 5/5s is premium.** Ethan's biggest takeaway from early access: "Having four-fours and five-fives is so important in this format… that's why Dreadbore is going to be so premium." Wild Slash, while predicted as a top common, underperforms because creatures get pumped out of its range easily.

6. **Edict effects punch above their weight.** Devour Flesh and Pker Predation (?) are generally weak but here they handle hexproof creatures (Glade Cover Scout, Alpha Authority targets) — the primary way to interact with them without combat tricks.

7. **Treasure Cruise at common is the format's top blue payoff.** Enable it with Undead Butler mills, Fagi Archaeologist (?), consider (surveil helps), T-Crop Skirmisher (?). "There's always time for ancestral recall."

8. **Bonus sheet awareness is mandatory for signals.** Each week rotates different power cards. Gates/devotion week determines whether to lean mono-color or multi-color. Devotion week: watch for Frostburn Weird (double-pipped 2-drop, excellent), Karanos (the God), devotion payoffs. Devotion week makes green monocolor more viable and devalues Gates slightly.

9. **Bestow curve: evaluate on both modes.** Key bestow creatures are those with low creature cost (2 mana) AND low bestow cost (4–5 mana). Leaf Crown Dryad (2/2 reach for 2, bestow +2/+2 reach for 4) and Hopeful Idolon (1/1 lifelink for 1, bestow +1/+1 lifelink for 4) are the exemplars. Expensive bestow (6–7 mana) is rarely the primary plan.

10. **Heroic enablers: look for multi-function spells.** Coordinated Assault triggers heroic on two creatures AND gives first strike. Ardenvale Tactician's adventure taps two creatures while triggering heroic. Fall of the Hammer and Savage Punch are fight spells that also heroic-trigger. Value each heroic enabler on how many extra functions it serves.

11. **17-land base is standard; Pilgrim's Eye is an honorary land.** With multi-color splashes, run 17 lands + MDFCs counting as lands. Pilgrim's Eye is a land-finder that helps hit multiple colored sources; treat it as a land when counting.

12. **T-Crop Skirmisher (?) is quietly excellent.** 2/1 for 2 with embalm, works with self-mill and sacrifice subthemes, returns as a 2/1 in late game. Plays well in blue-black and grindy midrange shells.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Green being wide open is the primary signal to read.** Green is the hub of PIOM — it enables fixing (Gates, District Guide, Briefcase), has top commons (Narback Rhino, Leaf Crown Dryad), and goes into every good archetype. If green cards are wheeling, you are probably not fighting for it.
- **Gates going late = Gates deck is open.** In the Dec-11 draft, multiple possessed scabs went very late. Gates decks require green Gates most (Gruul/Simic/Golgari guildgates get snapped up early by anyone going green). If you see green Gates in picks 5–7, Gates is open.
- **Gates missing early = don't force Gates.** Ben observed: "gates pack is missing again so Gates clearly not open." A pack with no green Gate in picks 1–4 means someone is in that lane.
- **Dimir exploit signals:** If Undead Butler, Heartpiercer Manticore, and T-Crop Skirmisher are all passing to you in pack 1, Dimir exploit is available. The deck needs 2-drops badly (Butler, Skirmisher, Blood Tithe Harvester) — if they're wheeling, jump in.
- **Bonus sheet awareness changes signals per week.** During devotion week, mono-color cards (Frostburn Weird, Disciple of Phenax) going late = devotion decks not being drafted. During Planeswalker week, Oath cards and flip walkers passing = Planeswalker synergy seat is open.
- **Pivot advice:** If you start black and see no exploit pieces, pivot toward black-green grind with Possessed Scab as the backbone — the cards overlap significantly.

## TL;DR meta read

The single sampled episode is a gameplay match, not a format analysis. What follows is inferred from the drafter's pick decisions and in-game commentary only:

- **Green-blue (Simic) appears to be a real and functional archetype.** The drafter (Ben or Ethan — likely Ethan based on context) builds a green-blue value deck with multiple sagas, defenders/archers, bounce, and blossom prancer as a top-end finisher.
- **17Lands data was actively consulted during the draft** — the drafter checked GIH WR on picks and deferred to them: Behold the Unspeakable over Spring-Leaf Avenger (63.1% vs 60.8%), Blossom Prancer highlighted as 65.2% — "these win rates are wild."
- **Reckoner's Raid** came up multiple times as a highly contested black rare (hated multiple times, passed around). It signals black is a competitive color, and Reckoner's Raid is a premium card in that color.
- **Tempo and loops matter.** The green-blue deck leveraged Sky Turtle (bounce loops) with Careful Cultivation and Season of Renewal recursion to generate repeated value. The drafter explicitly wanted this as the deck's "over the top" plan.
- **Sagas are premium.** Multiple sagas appeared across packs and were taken/hated highly. Fable of the Mirror-Breaker (?) appeared as LSV's card, and the drafter expressed relief at not passing it.
- **White-black (Tameshi? (?)) and black-red were both in the pod,** suggesting multiple viable black decks. The drafter avoided black despite seeing good black cards, citing correct color positioning.
- **Farewell is a bomb that warps deckbuilding** — the drafter made multiple decisions around playing around Farewell (an LSV card), cutting enchantments and artifacts from the sideboard and sequencing plays differently.

## Format principles

The following are inferred from draft and gameplay decisions in the single video. No explicit "format principles" segment exists.

- **17Lands GIH WR is actively used by the hosts during the draft itself** — they openly check win rates and trust them over gut feeling. "Behold vs. Spring-Leaf: 63.1 vs. 60.8, not even close." This reinforces the project-wide rule: data beats theory.
- **Blossom Prancer is top-tier** (65.2% GIH WR cited at time of recording). A pick-2 quality card, possibly the best green card in the set at that point in the format's data.
- **Spring-Leaf Avenger** is also excellent (60.8% GIH WR cited), just clearly below Prancer when both are in the same pack.
- **Sky Turtle enables recursive loops** with bounce effects and sagas (Season of Renewal, Careful Cultivation). The drafter built toward this explicitly, prioritizing Season of Renewal as a high-want card to enable turtle loops.
- **Sagas are premium across the board.** Multiple sagas appeared and were fought over. Green saga (Careful Cultivation), various color sagas all treated as high picks. A rare saga (Fable of the Mirror-Breaker (?)) was flagged as too dangerous to pass.
- **Behold the Unspeakable** is a strong card (63.1% GIH WR cited); worth first-picking.
- **Reckoner's Raid** is a premium black card — the drafter hate-drafted it twice across packs rather than letting it reach opponents. Treat as near-bomb level.
- **Fade from History** (bounce/removal enchantment) is premium removal. The drafter was "thrilled to see it pick 8 pack 1" and called that "getting an A playable late — really good." It performed well in games.
- **Moon-Snare Specialist** is a strong blue card — taken over fixing (Uncharted Haven) when other blue cards were already in the pool.
- **Suit Up** is "actively good," taken twice. The drafter noted it may be slightly weaker in certain matchups but still worth playing.
- **Grove Archer** ("consistently impressed by") — a two-drop that came up multiple times. Drafter took it over Futurist Technician, noting it also hates the card away from a teammate. Three were drafted, though only two were planned for the main.
- **Bearer of Memory** — fine three-drop; "happily" taken. Two were drafted but drafter was unsure about playing both.
- **Careful Cultivation** — actively good in the GU loops deck; provides mana and enables enchantment/saga synergies.
- **Hovering Bike (?)** — drafter noted "I probably like hoverbike more than I should" but kept cutting it in sideboard decisions for matchup-specific cards. May be overrated by feel but still played.
- **Futurist Sentinel** — multiple copies were drafted; drafter ended up with four and planned to play all of them in the main. Valued as resilient grindy threats.
- **Jukai Preserver** — the drafter was "underwhelmed" by Jukai Preserver in this deck specifically. Preferred Tails of Valakut (?) over it given the defensive/recursive gameplan.
- **Koi Fish / Prosperous Thief (?)** — taken late as a small role-player; drafter acknowledged "koi is not very good" in certain matchups.
- **Technician** (Futurist Technician) — taken over Grove Archer in one spot, described as "fine."
- **Farewell** is a bomb enchantment sweeper (exile mode). LSV had one; the drafter made extensive sideboard and in-game decisions to play around it. If your opponent has it, avoid overloading the board with enchantments/artifacts.
- **Reckoner's Raid** (hate-drafted twice) and **Fable of the Mirror-Breaker (?)** (flagged as "too good to pass") are bomb-level rare sagas in red/black.
- **Team draft note:** "Power level should be a little lower" in team draft pods — the drafter acknowledged taking some cards they "like a little less" than normal because team draft pods are smaller (6 players, 3 packs each direction means more overlap in colors).

## Card notes (community consensus)

Only cards mentioned with an explicit evaluation are listed. This is a gameplay episode — most evaluations are pick-order implied, not stated outright.

### Blue
- **Behold the Unspeakable** — 63.1% GIH WR (data cited in video); P1P1 quality. The drafter took it first over Spring-Leaf Avenger.
- **Sky Turtle** — taken P1P3; enables bounce/loop synergies with Season of Renewal. High value in recursive decks.
- **Moon-Snare Specialist** — taken over Uncharted Haven (fixing) when already blue; good blue card, not the fix.
- **Suit Up** — "actively good," taken twice. May underperform in some matchups but a main-deck card.
- **Futurist Sentinel** — multiple copies valued; resilient threat in the GU grindy deck. Up to 4 played.
- **Futurist Technician** — "fine," taken over Grove Archer in one spot.

### Green
- **Blossom Prancer** — 65.2% GIH WR (data cited); drafter called it "insane." Best green card in this deck and possibly format.
- **Spring-Leaf Avenger** — 60.8% GIH WR; excellent, just below Prancer. Would have been P1P1 in most other packs.
- **Grove Archer** — "consistently impressed by archer." Two-drop that blocks, taken multiple times. Drafter took three but likely playing two main.
- **Bearer of Memory** — "happily" taken; fine three-drop. Two drafted.
- **Careful Cultivation** — good in the GU value/loops archetype; mana acceleration + enchantment synergy.
- **Season of Renewal** — highly desired ("we really want to get a Season of Renewal for our turtle loops"); enables infinite-value recursion with Sky Turtle. Never saw one in the draft.
- **Jukai Preserver** — drafter "underwhelmed" by it specifically in this deck. Not a snap include.
- **Tails of Valakut (?)** — preferred over Jukai Preserver; "pushes us over the top a little bit." Good in this deck.

### Black
- **Reckoner's Raid** — hate-drafted twice. Flagged as too powerful to pass. Premium black card, near-bomb.
- **Dockside Chef (?)** — mentioned briefly; not in the drafter's deck but appeared in opponent's.

### Red
- **Fable of the Mirror-Breaker (?)** — flagged as "too good, this card is too good" when considering whether to hate-draft vs. take another card. Bomb-level rare saga.

### Colorless / Artifacts
- **Automaton** — "not surprising to see automaton going late." Signals the card is not premium; low pick order.

### Rares & bombs
- **Blossom Prancer** — 65.2% GIH WR; top rare/uncommon in the format at time of recording.
- **Farewell** — bomb sweeper (exile enchantments, artifacts, graveyards — opponent had one); warps deckbuilding around it.
- **Fable of the Mirror-Breaker (?)** — rare saga; bomb-level, drafter considered taking just to deny opponent.
- **Reckoner's Raid** — bomb-level black rare; hate-drafted twice.

## Signals & seat reads

Coverage is too thin for confident seat-read advice. From the draft observed:

- **Blue signals:** Blue felt "very cut" in packs 2 and 3 ("we really didn't see blue in pack 1 right"). The drafter noted this explicitly. Being in blue in a six-person team draft meant being squeezed by LSV (LR) who was also green-blue.
- **Green signals:** Green appeared open early in this draft. Multiple late archers and bearers. The drafter got three Grove Archers and multiple bearers, suggesting green was relatively open.
- **Black** was contested — multiple players on black variants (WB, BR). If black is crowded, premium cards like Reckoner's Raid and removal get cut up fast.
- **White-aggro** appeared to be open on one side based on blade blessers and selfless samurai floating late without being hated.
- **Companion (Spirited Companion?)** — drafter noted "there's no way companion wheels" and was correct — it did not wheel. Treat this as a signal it's a high-pick common that dries up fast.

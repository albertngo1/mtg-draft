## TL;DR meta read

- **Aggressive format.** Both Ben and Ethan emphasize this repeatedly and settle on it firmly by mid-to-late episodes. Plan A is to be the beatdown. The best commons and uncommons reward you for attacking early and often.
- **Red is the best color, white is close behind.** Red has the best removal (Scorching Dragonfire, Soul Seer, Shock) and aggressive payoffs. White has the best creature suite for aggro (Seasoned Hallowblade(?), Basri's Acolyte(?), Anointed Chorister). Green is a solid second color in aggressive shells.
- **Best archetypes (settled read): Red-White aggro, Green-White aggro/counters, Red-Blue spells.** These are the Tier-1 strategies. Blue-Black reanimator is real but Tier 2. Green-Black midrange is a fallback.
- **Black is the worst color.** Its commons are weak, it can't aggro effectively, and even its best uncommons (Devotee, Eliminate, Gourmand) don't compensate for weak two-drops and a poor curve.
- **Mechanics that matter: +1/+1 counters (green-white), sacrifice outlets (black-red), reanimation (blue-black).** Shrines are a late-game niche strategy, viable but clearly sub-optimal compared to the aggressive game plans.
- **Removal hierarchy: Scorching Dragonfire > Soul Seer > Shock > Eliminate > Finishing Blow.** Dragonfire exiling relevant; five-mana removal (Finishing Blow, Hunter's Edge) is often too slow for what this format demands.
- **Curve matters more than synergy.** Decks that curve out with 2-drops into 3-drops into 4-drops consistently beat decks trying to assemble multi-card reanimation or sacrifice loops. Don't over-synergize.

## Format principles

**Speed and curve:**
- This format is "quite aggressive" (confirmed by both Ben and Ethan by mid-format). You want to be the aggressor. Green-black and other midrange piles get beaten by consistent low-curve decks.
- For drops: be very picky about four-drops. The only four-drops worth taking in aggressive decks are Basri's Acolyte(?), Gale Swooper(?), and Hunter's Edge. Cards like Dilophosaurus(?) and Sabertooth Mauler(?) do NOT belong in aggro decks.
- Aim to have two-drops, three-drops, and quality fours. Five-drops should be zero or near-zero in your aggressive deck.
- In Best-of-One specifically: hand smoothing means mana-light hands are less punishing, but the format is so fast that stumbling on mana is still brutal. Keep one-land hands only if you have low-CMC curve.

**Two-drops are critical:**
- Black is weakest here — its two-drops (Feted Imp(?), Masked Blackguard(?)) are all mediocre. This is a major reason black is the worst color.
- Best two-drops overall: Anointed Chorister (white), Anointed Chorister in counter decks scales out of control, Mag Mutt(?) (red/white hybrid), Death-Bloom Thallid(?), Drowsing Trianon(?), Walking Corpse is fine filler.
- Short-sword is an important equipment — makes any two-drop into a real threat.

**Removal:**
- Scorching Dragonfire is the best common removal spell in the format. It kills ~75% of commons/uncommons and exiles, which matters against reanimation.
- Shock is the second-best removal common — kills ~50% of creatures but is instant-speed and efficient.
- Grasp of Darkness kills more things than Dragonfire (9 more creatures on average) but costs double black and puts you in the weakest color — not worth the color commitment.
- Eliminate is the best black removal uncommon; it scales unusually well because it kills creatures that have gotten +1/+1 counters (which other removal like Dragonfire can't hit after they've grown).
- Five-mana removal (Finishing Blow, Hunter's Edge as instant) is often too slow. Two max in a deck; prefer not to have to lean on them.

**Splashing:**
- Not a "splash-friendly" format in Best-of-One. Ben explicitly says he prefers not to splash in BO1. Double-color costs make splash mana awkward.
- If you do splash, dual-lands (Blood Fell Caves, Rugged Highlands, Temple of Malady, etc.) and Meteorite are your tools.

**Signals and commitments:**
- Red cards going late is a strong signal to move in. Goblin Wizardry going 7th-8th pick means someone is wide open in red-blue or red-green.
- Blue-black gold uncommon (Obsessive Stitcher adjacent cards) going early means blue-black reanimator is contested.
- Black going late is common — it IS the worst color — but don't assume it means anything about other colors.
- Commit by pack 2. Tri-color decks are punished by the aggressive format.

**Demonic Embrace(?):**
- This aura is deceptively powerful: +3/+1 flying, recurrable from graveyard by paying 3 life + discarding a card. Suits up any big creature into a game-winning threat. Works in almost any black deck. Take it highly.

**Alchemist's Gift(?):**
- One of the most flexible commons: gives deathtouch OR lifelink for one mana at instant speed. Can enable "death touch ping" with Skeleton Archer(?) to kill any creature. Nearly as good as Finishing Blow in some builds, triggers life-gain payoffs, and stalls aggro with lifelink.

**Chrome Replicator:**
- Payoff for playing multiple copies of a creature. If you have 3+ copies of a creature (triple Pteranodon, double Acolyte, etc.) this becomes a must-answer threat. Take it if your draft is shaping up with natural multiples.

## Card notes (community consensus)

### White
- **Seasoned Hallowblade(?)** — Arguably the best white uncommon/rare in the format. Two-mana 2/2 with an "indestructible until end of turn" discard activation makes it nearly impossible to trade with profitably. Ben: "one of the best aggressive cards in the format." Ethan: "I think in best-of-one this card just does it all."
- **Basri's Acolyte(?)** — Tier-1 uncommon. Distributes three +1/+1 counters across your team when it enters. Back-breaking in mirrors; makes your other creatures grow out of removal range. One of the best four-drops in any white aggressive deck.
- **Anointed Chorister(?)** — Best white one-drop, maybe best one-drop period. Scales to 4-power lifelink with a simple activation. Does serious work in both offensive and defensive roles. Take it highly.
- **Concordia Pegasus(?)** — Solid aggressive two-drop flier. Not exciting but does the job in aggro.
- **Feat of Resistance(?)** — Premium combat trick / protection spell. Gives +1/+1 and protection from chosen color until end of turn. Ethan: "I've right-click conceded Feat of Resistance more than anything else." Protects key creatures from removal AND blows out combat. Take it early. Ben treats it as near-removal quality.
- **Gale Swooper(?)** — Good four-drop for aggressive decks; one of the few four-drops worth running. 3/2 flying.
- **Swift Response(?)** — Decent white instant removal but situational (requires attacking or blocking creature). Fine removal spell, not premium.
- **Celestial Enforcer(?)** — Six-mana 3/4 with tap-down ability. Too expensive for most aggro decks; mentioned as a potential main-deck card in best-of-one but mostly sideboard.
- **Selfless Savior(?)** — One-drop that can sacrifice to give indestructible to a creature. Mentioned as scary early threat in white aggro.
- **Makeshift Battalion(?)** — Aggressive uncommon; mentioned but not heavily featured.
- **Daybreak Charger(?)** — Acceptable curve-filler in white aggro.
- **Basri's Solidarity(?)** — Uncommon instant that puts +1/+1 counters on all your creatures. "Back-breaking sometimes" per Ethan; best in go-wide counter decks (green-white). Can be a game-winning top-deck.

### Blue
- **Rousing Read(?)** — Good aggressive card in red-blue spells; gives +1/+1 and flying plus card draw when target creature deals damage. Enables the blue-red spells game plan.
- **Rain of Revelation(?)** — Draw 3 discard 1 for four mana. Good card-draw spell but "not what you want to be doing in most decks" per Ben; competes for the four-drop slot that can be better used for attackers.
- **Experimental Overload(?)** — "Busted" in blue-red spells deck. Makes an X/X token equal to instants/sorceries in graveyard and lets you rebuy them. Not for creature-based decks.
- **Roaming Ghostlight(?)** — Blue common flier with bounce ability. Appeared frequently as an opponent's card. Noted as annoying for Seasoned Hallowblade decks.
- **Opt(?)** — Cantrip, noted as a blue common but not highlighted highly for most decks.
- **Obsessive Stitcher(?)** — "Probably the best card" in its pack for blue-black reanimator. Discard outlet + draw engine. Key piece of the UB game plan.
- **Wall of Runes(?)** — 0/4 defender that draws a card when it enters. Good in blue-black reanimator as a cheap blocker plus card selection.
- **Mistral Singer(?)** — Flying creature mentioned as good in blue-based aggressive decks. Goes with red-white flying strategy.
- **Frost Breath(?)** — Tap-down spell; noted as "scary" in blue tempo/aggro.

### Black
- **Liliana's Devotee(?)** — Best black uncommon per theory meeting; zombie tribal payoff. Makes a zombie whenever a nontoken creature dies on your end step (if you pay 1B). Generates ongoing value. Pairs well with sacrifice effects and removal.
- **Eliminate(?)** — Best black removal uncommon overall. Kills creatures with CMC 3 or less that have had things happen to them (counters, etc.). Scales better than expected because it still kills "activated" creatures that grew out of Dragonfire range. Theory meeting: "I would take Eliminate over Gourmand, I would take Eliminate over Malefic Scythe."
- **Liliana's Devotee... [Gourmand?]** — Vraska, Swarm's Eminence(?) or "Gourmand" is a strong black uncommon zombie payoff. Listed alongside Devotee as top black uncommons. Theory meeting puts Devotee/Eliminate/Gourmand as the top three black uncommons in roughly that order.
- **Malefic Scythe(?)** — Equipment uncommon that grows when creatures die. "Harder to get counters than initially thought" because black decks don't attack aggressively enough to force blocks. Good but not tier-1 black uncommon.
- **Death-Bloom Thallid(?)** — Ben's second-best black common; "2B creature that dies and creates a 1/1 sapling token." Works well with sacrifice synergies. Body plus token means two synergy triggers.
- **Grasp of Darkness(?)** — Best removal in black but costs double-black. "Best card that suffers most from being black." Kills 9 more creatures than Dragonfire but costs you being in the weakest color with a double-black pip. Still playable when you're deep in black.
- **Skeleton Archer(?)** — Good black common; pings a creature or player for 1 when it enters. Theory meeting: "Skeleton Archer is back-breaking when it hits — great against white decks with one-toughness creatures." Combo with Alchemist's Gift for deathtouch ping to kill any creature.
- **Finishing Blow(?)** — Five-mana destroy target creature sorcery. Black's unconditional removal at common; one or two max in the deck. Theory meeting: "You really only want one maximum two copies." Too slow to be reliable in an aggressive format.
- **Crypt Lurker(?)** — Four-mana 3/4 (guaranteed). Discard a creature or sacrifice a creature to draw a card. Good in reanimator as both a sacrifice outlet and a body.
- **Rise Again(?)** — The reanimation spell at three mana. Theory meeting: "People are taking this too highly right now — you need three pieces: discard outlet, big monster, and the spell itself." Real power but requires setup. Blue-black reanimator is the best home. Early take was overhyped; settled read is "fine but don't go after it aggressively."
- **Demonic Embrace(?)** — Recurring aura: +3/+1 flying. Can be recast from graveyard by paying 3 life and discarding a card. Ben: "This card is very strong." Puts any creature into "must answer" territory and generates massive damage swings. High-pick uncommon/rare.
- **Alchemist's Gift(?)** — One-mana instant giving deathtouch or lifelink. Ben (theory meeting): "I would prefer to end up in a black deck with three Alchemist's Gifts and one Finishing Blow than the other way around." Extremely versatile; almost comparable to Finishing Blow in many situations.
- **Carrion Grub(?)** — Four-mana creature whose power equals the number of creatures in your graveyard. "3/5 on average in a normal game" per Ben; excellent blocker with upside. Good in blue-black reanimator and green-black where creatures die naturally.
- **Liliana's Steward(?)** — One-mana 1/1 that lets you discard a creature to look at target opponent's hand and exile a non-land, non-creature card. Body too irrelevant; theory meeting: "irrelevant body and irrelevant effect" most of the time. Filler.
- **Sanguine Indulgence(?)** / **Vito, Thorn of the Dusk Rose(?)** — Vito is a 3/1 for three with a lifedrain trigger. "Three-mana 1/3 pretty unimpressive and the clause is too all-in" (day-0 take). Not a first pick.
- **Village Rites(?)** — Instant sacrifice outlet: sacrifice a creature, draw two cards. One max per deck; theory meeting: "Not what I want in a non-dedicated sacrifice deck." Requires disposable bodies.
- **Feted Imp(?)** — Flying 1/1 for two with draw-a-card on death. Theory meeting: consensus is "I don't like any of these" (alongside other weak black two-drops). Black's two-drops are universally mediocre.
- **Bad Deal(?)** — Each player discards two cards. Sideboard quality; in a control mirror potentially useful but rarely main-deckable. "Best in pure control which doesn't really exist in black."

### Red
- **Scorching Dragonfire(?)** — Best common removal in the format. Instant, deals 3 to creature or planeswalker; if lethal, exiles instead. Ben: "kills three-quarters of commons and uncommons." P1P1 quality.
- **Shock(?)** — Second-best common removal. Instant, deals 2 to any target. "Kills about half the commons and uncommons." Efficient and splashable. Take it early.
- **Soul Seer(?)** — "A great way to start a draft" per Ben; described as "far and away" a top pick. Appears to be a removal spell dealing 5 damage that also removes indestructible temporarily. (Caption garbled; "deals 5 to a creature, loses indestructible until EOT.")
- **Hobble Fiend(?)** — Red creature with trample; good in red-green where combat tricks become dome reach. Ben: "I didn't know Hobble Fiend had trample — that's great." Better than it looks.
- **Burn Bright(?)** — Combat trick / token payoff; "looking awfully good" for go-wide red decks. Works well with Goblin Wizardry tokens.
- **Crash Through(?)** — Cantrip that gives trample; "great in blue-red spells as a cantrip" per Ethan; less critical in creature decks.
- **Pitch-Burn Devils(?)** — Good blocker plus burn; "does what Pitch-burn Devils does which is hold off an entire board."
- **Magma(?)** / **Magma Opus(?)** / red two-drop — Frequently referenced as a great two-drop in red; "primo two-drop pickup."
- **Goblin Wizardry(?)** — Instant speed two 1/1 goblin tokens with haste. Good in red-blue spells and red-white go-wide. Goes late enough to pick up; "if Goblin Wizardry is going 7th that means red-blue is wide open."
- **Battle Rattle Shaman(?)** — Four-drop that grants haste/+2/+0 to a creature on upkeep. Considered for red-white but "clunky"; one of the few four-drops that makes the cut.
- **Kinetic Augur(?)** — Four-drop that digs through your deck on ETB; good in red-blue spells. "Seems like a very strong card."
- **Igneous Cur(?)** / other red creatures — Referenced as good but not heavily evaluated.

### Green
- **Drowsing Trianon(?)** — "One of the best drops at common" per Ben. 1G for a 3/3 defender that can attack as a 3/3 if you control a creature with power 4 or greater. Ben: "really high on this card right now — so good." Getting multiples makes a "pretty sweet deck." Third pick late = signal green is open.
- **Llanowar Visionary(?)** — "Best card in the pack" multiple times. Ramp creature that draws a card (3G for 2/2 that taps for G and draws). Bridges your curve in green-heavy decks.
- **Pride Malkin(?)** — Green-black common creature; "really good aggressive start" with Pteranodon. Distributes +1/+1 counters and has trample/reach(?) synergy.
- **Pteranodon (Drowsing Pteranodon)** — "Cream of the crop" per Ethan. Common flying creature with a powerful enters-the-battlefield and synergy with augment effects. Triple copies enables Chrome Replicator. Core green-white aggressive finisher.
- **Thrashing Brontodon(?)** — "Ties the room together" per Ben; good stats plus can destroy an artifact or enchantment when it enters. Main-deckable in best-of-one since people run Dubs and auras.
- **Garruk's Harbinger(?)** — "Hexproof from black" means it dodges all of black's removal; when it deals damage look at top X cards and take a creature or Garruk planeswalker. Great in green-white and green-black.
- **Colossal Dreadmaw(?)** — Big vanilla 6/6 trample for 6. Fine reanimation target; can close out games when Demonic Embrace'd. Not exciting but does the job.
- **Warden of the Woods(?)** — 7-mana 6/5 hexproof. Good but expensive; "much better than Dreadmaw as a common but not sure how much better." Big target for reanimation or ramping green decks.
- **Hunter's Edge(?)** — Green fight spell (common); one of green's best removal options. Good in green-white and green-black where your creatures are large enough to win fights.
- **Snare Spinner(?)** — Green flash creature with reach; Ethan: "gives us a profitable way to interact with flying things — one of the weaknesses of green-black." Great blocker and answers flying threats.
- **Carrion Grub... (see Black)** — Also a green-black creature worth noting in that archetype.
- **Invigorating Surge(?)** — Green instant giving a creature +1/+1 counter and doubling its counters. "Broken" in green-white counter decks. With Conclave Mentor(?) + Drowsing Trianon(?), curves into massive combat-ending turns.
- **Wildwood Scourge(?)** — Green creature that grows whenever a +1/+1 counter is placed on any creature you control. With multiple counter-givers (Acolyte, Surge, Malkin) becomes enormous quickly. Ethan: "This + Basri's Solidarity — holy cow."
- **Conclave Mentor(?)** — Green-white gold uncommon that doubles +1/+1 counters; "the card I was talking about — very strong in green-white aggro." Key build-around for the counters deck.
- **Fungal Rebirth(?)** — Returns a permanent from graveyard; mentioned as a reanimation-adjacent effect but "more of a green-black card."

### Multicolor
- **Conclave Mentor(?)** — GW uncommon. Doubles +1/+1 counters placed on your creatures. Core piece of green-white counter aggro. "Very strong." Pick 1 quality if you're in the lane.
- **Liliana's Devotee...** (see Black for details on the full zombie suite)

### Colorless
- **Short-Sword** — Common equipment; equip for 1, +1/+1. "Short-sword has just gotta be fine" per Ethan; makes cheap creatures relevant and is best-in-slot in aggressive decks with Seasoned Hallowblade and Pteranodon. Ethan finds it late pick 3 which suggests people undervalue it.
- **Palladium Myr(?)** — Two-mana artifact creature (2/2?) that taps for colorless mana. Ben: "Palladium mirror has been fantastic" in his black-red reanimation deck — ramps into turn-4 or turn-5 big plays. Niche but real.
- **Chrome Replicator(?)** — Four-mana artifact that creates a 2/2 construct token and then creates another token each time you cast a creature you have 2+ copies of. "Not bad" with triple Pteranodon, double Acolyte, etc. Enables "bonus Pteranodon" play patterns. Take when your curve has natural duplicates.
- **Meteorite(?)** — Five-mana artifact that fixes mana (adds any color) and deals 2 damage when it enters. Useful for three-color strategies and as ramp in control decks.
- **Skyscanner(?)** — Three-mana 1/2 flier that draws a card when it enters. Ben: "I just don't think it's good enough power and toughness for three mana in a format that feels this beatdown." Usually a cut. Can be fine with Short-Sword equips and Battle Rattle Shaman but generally not a priority.
- **Sanctums (Shrine cycle)** — Enchantments that drain life based on how many Shrines you control. Best used in multiples (at least 2-3). Red and blue shrines are the most powerful. Viable as a late-game plan in a defensive mardu/multi-color shell but explicitly a "fun/niche" strategy, not optimal. "Two one feels like trophying sometimes when you draft these weird decks."

### Lands
- **Blood Fell Caves / Temple of Malady / Rugged Highlands / Scoured Barrens** — Dual lands with enter-tapped drawback. Useful for two-color decks; Ben frequently picks them up to enable two-color play. Important in a format where color commitment is real.
- **Radiant Fountain(?)** — Colorless gain-2-life land. Appeared in gameplay; minor life gain can be relevant against aggro.

### Rares & bombs
- **Chandra (planeswalker)** — "Seems pretty busted" in early gameplay, even after a missplay. Strong mid-game threat in red-based decks.
- **Mugen / Ugin, the Spirit Dragon(?)** — "Opie" per Ben; a format-warping bomb. Opponents built entire strategies around cheating it out or reanimating it.
- **Sublime Epiphany(?)** — Six-mana blue instant that bounces, counters, and copies. "That card's pretty nuts" per Ben; "it will not be the first or last game lost to Sublime Epiphany."
- **Nine Lives(?)** — White enchantment that prevents damage; mentioned as "sweet in a control deck" but not a build-around.
- **Demonic Embrace(?)** — Rare aura in black; see Black section. High pick rare.
- **Seasoned Hallowblade(?)** — White rare(?) creature; Ethan calls it arguably better than Bane-Slayer Angel "in best-of-one." Outstanding two-drop.
- **Basri's Solidarity(?)** — White instant uncommon putting +1/+1 on all creatures; see White section.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Red going late** = red is wide open; move in immediately. Shock, Dragonfire, and Soul Seer should not be passing P1 picks 4+.
- **Goblin Wizardry going late (7th-8th pick)** = explicit confirmation red-blue spells is open in your seat. Snap move in.
- **Multiple Rise Again in late picks** = either reanimator is contested (someone is taking the payoffs) or (more likely early-format) people are over-drafting it. Don't assume it means blue-black is open.
- **Green-white gold uncommon (Conclave Mentor?) going early** = green-white counters is contested. Pivot if you see it go pick 1-2.
- **Black cards going very late** = normal (black is the worst color), not necessarily a signal that black is open. If grasp of darkness is going pick 6+, the table is soft on black but that doesn't make black better.
- **Drowsing Trianon late (pick 6+)** = green is open or at least being undervalued. Good time to move in.
- **Pivot advice:** In an aggressive format, don't be afraid to abandon your first pick if picks 2-4 are screaming a different color is open. Ethan: "It's possible I just made a mistake by passing the blue-black gold uncommon." Commitment matters — tri-color is punished.

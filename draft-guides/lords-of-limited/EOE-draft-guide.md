## TL;DR meta read

- **"Play to the board" is the format's cardinal rule.** Every archetype, including control and ramp, must maintain board presence. A clear board while your opponent has creatures is the fastest path to losing.
- **Feast-or-famine dynamic with spacecraft.** Whoever resolves spacecraft while ahead snowballs; spacecraft is weak when behind. Don't commit a spacecraft when you're already on the back foot.
- **Green is the glue.** Green is the only color with real fixing; non-green decks generally cannot splash. Green soup (UG 4-5 color) is the format's most powerful thing to do.
- **The format's central tension is big-game vs. small-game.** Big green ramp soup vs. black sacrifice/aggro; know which plan you're on and how to execute it.
- **UBG ("Salty") is the format's identity.** Both hosts rank UBG as the top-three colors; WR are clearly worse. Don't force WR unless clearly open.
- **Sequencing turns 2-5 is crucial.** Lander tokens, warp, spacecraft station activations, and double-spell triggers all depend on correct play order. Missequencing costs games.

## EOE format principles

- **Every deck plays to the board.** Even control decks must maintain board presence. A fully-clear board on your side is losing, not "setting up."
- **Take the best card out of each pack picks 1-8.** Power gaps are clearly defined in this format; going down a tier to stay in colors is a large mistake. (Both hosts aligned on this in mid3 and retro.)
- **Stay open longer than feels comfortable.** The best decks reveal themselves via what's flowing, not what you commit to early. A P1P1 green card doesn't obligate you to green soup — it obligates you to take the best card P1P2.
- **Spacecraft is context-dependent.** You want at least one spacecraft in every deck. But spacecraft is not a standalone bomb — it's strong when ahead, weak when behind. Don't assume it's always a slam.
- **Lander tokens are good but not unconditional.** Early-access episode flagged landers as "super hot"; mid-format corrected: landers need synergy (landfall creatures, Gene Pollinator, fixing for soup) to be worth prioritizing. Standalone lander generation is fine but not premium.
- **Green is the only fixing color.** Non-green decks do not splash. Command Bridge (the common fixing land) helps but isn't enough to enable multicolor non-green.
- **Double-spell triggers require sequencing.** Cards that care about casting two spells (Station Monitor, Sunstar Lightsmith, Ilvoy Infiltrator, Gigastorm Titan) need you to have two castable spells available on that turn. Build your curve and hand with this in mind.
- **Warp without payoffs is mediocre.** Warp felt exciting in preview but mid-format clarified: warp is good in UW double-spell (enables second spell triggers) or when it puts something into exile for void payoffs. Standalone warp creatures are not a priority to collect.
- **Void payoffs are conditional but swingy.** Tragic Trajectory (−10/−10 with void) and similar cards reward building a deck that consistently voids. Don't run void payoffs if your deck doesn't generate void triggers reliably.
- **Command Bridge is a signal.** When Command Bridge is flowing late, green-based multicolor is being drafted by your pod-mates.
- **Red has a lot of cards and not many good ones.** A recurring observation in every episode: red common depth is low. Don't be pushed into red by quantity alone.
- **13 cards per pack; bonus sheet lands appear ~1 in 8 packs.** Budget accordingly — don't assume you'll see many station lands.

## Signals and seat reads

- **Green flowing late = green soup is under-drafted; jump in.** Galactic Wayfarer and Gene Pollinator are the clearest signals — if they're passing you late in pack one, someone isn't in green.
- **Cavern Harrier late = red is overdrafted, not open.** Ben explicitly called this out in mid3. Red appears plentiful (many cards per pack) but the good ones dry up fast.
- **Command Bridge late = multicolor is being picked** at the table; your fixing may tighten.
- **Spacecraft concentration in one pair = avoid that pair.** Station abilities require multiple spacecraft to matter; if you're fighting over the same few spacecraft with a pod-mate, one of you loses.
- **Nanoform Sentinel late = blue is being overlooked for blue black.** The card was lapping the table in the Pro Tour draft at picks 4 and 8. If you have Tractor Beam, pick Nanoform Sentinel aggressively.
- **Two or more Code Cracker Hound early = white-blue double-spell is wide open.** UW double-spell is tight; seeing its signpost cards late tells you the lane is yours.
- **Pack 3 dynamics vary.** The color you sent signals about in pack 1 comes back to you; the color you were receiving signals about dries up. Shuhei went 1-2 at the PT partly because pack 3 had no green despite green flowing in pack 1 — his neighbor had picked up that same vein.

## Supersessions (early-was-wrong, now corrected — NOT live disagreements)

These are settled by recency. The early take is dead; do not treat it as a competing opinion.

- **Spacecraft evaluation reversed.** Preview (07-14): skepticism about spacecraft, treated as niche. Early-access (07-28): "spacecraft are out of this world!!" — core format mechanic. Retro (10-06): spacecraft confirmed central but clarified as context-dependent (strong when ahead, weak when behind; every deck wants one but it's not unconditional). Trust the retro nuance.
- **Lander tokens over-hyped early.** Early-access called landers "super hot" in almost all contexts. Mid-format corrected: landers are good specifically when you have payoffs (Gene Pollinator, landfall creatures, soup fixing). Standalone lander generation is fine but not worth prioritizing heavily. Retro confirmed this.
- **Blue-white double-spell archetype.** Preview and early episodes were uncertain about the archetype's ceiling. By mid3 and retro, it was confirmed as one of the two best archetypes (alongside UG soup), specifically with three Code Cracker Hounds making a "very tight" deck per the Pro Tour friend Jaz. Trust the later read.
- **Parig Becker under-valued then corrected.** Preview and early episodes barely mentioned this card. Mid1 and mid3 elevated it to "second-best black common." Superseded — treat as a known high pick in black.
- **Warp standalone value oversold.** Preview framed warp as independently powerful due to reusability and cost reduction. Mid-format nuanced: warp is excellent in UW (enables double-spell triggers cheaply) and for void generation, but collecting warp creatures outside of those contexts doesn't make a deck. Trust mid/retro read.

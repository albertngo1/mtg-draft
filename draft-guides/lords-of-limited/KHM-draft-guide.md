## TL;DR meta read

- **KHM is called a "GOAT-ed" format** by the host after multiple flashback drafts. Descriptors: fun, diverse, real synergies matter.
- **Snow is a core mechanic and resource.** Snow lands and snow permanents unlock key cards (Icebind Pillar, Avalanche Caller, Blizzard Brawl). Specifically Temur (UR/ug) and Sultai (BUG) are flagged as the primary snow homes; "the snow land stuff is really in Sultai."
- **Blue-Red Giants is a real, powerful archetype.** The host drafts it deliberately, and it involves multiple synergy pieces: Mistwalker (1/4 flyer that counts as a giant for Glimpse), Glimpse the Cosmos (scry/draw with giant synergy), Calamity Bearer (doubles giant damage — enormous with Mistwalkers), Basalt Ravager (damage equal to number of types), and Berg Strider.
- **Removal is scarce and valued.** Demonbolt and Squash are the primary cheap red removal commons. The host takes both over other options repeatedly and notes the deck is "light on removal" with only Tundra Fumarole, Basalt Ravager, and Squash.
- **Mistwalker is the best blue common in the Giants shell.** The host ends up with four copies and states "play set of that card, it's really really good." It is a 1/4 flyer (giant for Glimpse synergy), efficient, and resilient on defense.
- **Changelings enable giant synergy.** Bloodline Pretender (a shapeshifter) counts as a giant for all payoffs and can be named giant; similar for other changelings.
- **Dwarven Hammer is the best card in some packs** but was passed for synergy — the host names it "best card here" and then takes Glimpse the Cosmos instead to stay in the giants plan.

## Format principles

- **Snow lands are a scarce, must-draft resource.** The host takes Snow Mountain and Snow Island over nearly all non-land cards when available ("we will be taking snow mountains and islands over basically everything"). Running out of snow permanents is a real failure mode — Icebind Pillar and Bergstrider require snow mana, and Avalanche Caller (mentioned as desirable but cut for lack of snow) needs snow to activate.
- **Glimpse the Cosmos is closer to two mana "look at three, pick one" than its mana cost suggests.** The host calls it "Dig Through Time in a deck with changelings or giants" — you get to recast it from the yard for a single blue if you have a giant in play. In a giants deck it is a premium card-selection engine, not a marginal cantrip.
- **Calamity Bearer is the bomb payoff for Giants.** "Plays extremely well with the Mistwalkers" — a Mistwalker that attacks into a pump becomes a 4-power threat doubling its damage. The host specifically drafts around assembling Bearer + Mistwalkers.
- **Shapeshifters / Changelings count as giants** and trigger all giant synergies. Bloodline Pretender was taken specifically because "shapeshifter seems good — we are getting there on types." Name it giant when it enters and it slots into the Basalt Ravager / Glimpse / Calamity Bearer engine.
- **Demonbolt is the premier red removal spell;** always taken over Bound in Gold and Elven Bow equivalents. "You're definitely taking Demonbolt over Bound in Gold, that's not particularly close."
- **Squash is conditional but worth including.** The host takes it over Giant's Amulet because "removal spell, potentially cheap removal spell" in a deck with three giants and three shapeshifters. Cheap conditional removal is still worth a slot.
- **Battle of Frost and Fire is a trap card.** The host has opened it pack-one-pick-one three times across multiple flashback drafts and has never taken it. "Just not good enough. The green creatures are bigger. Changelings exist." Despite looking powerful, it underperforms. Avoid.
- **Run Ashore (bounce + topdeck) is functional utility** in the giants deck — can reset a Pretender mid-combat or bounce a problematic Bound in Gold.
- **Icebind Pillar is underrated:** "Tap target artifact or creature for just a snow mana. It's a really nice follow-up." Only requires one snow mana; in a snow deck this is a repeatable tap-down that locks key threats.
- **Replicating Ring provides snow permanents** for Icebind Pillar and Bergstrider activations. Synergy pick in a snow-light build.
- **Land count: 17 is fine** with Raiders (two-drops that smooth draws) and Glimpse/Strategic Planning for card flow. The host settles on 17 + Replicating Ring + strategic planning in hand. "18 with the two Raiders" is also viable for color stability.
- **Foretell cards play well with Raiders** (pitch foretell cards to Raider for cycling). The host explicitly mentions pitching Glimpse the Cosmos to Raider to find land, calling it a clean line.
- **Curve matters:** the host regrets not leaving back Mistwalkers to block when racing, noting "the theme of this run is Mistwalker on blocks." Don't over-commit attacking with your best defenders when they double as relevant threats.

## Card notes (community consensus)

> Single source. Later/retro overrides do not apply — all takes are from the same video. Caption garble is significant; see the caveats section.

### White
- **Bound in Gold** — Taken late/passed early; host explicitly says Demonbolt is "not particularly close" over Bound in Gold. Filler removal.
- **Clarion Spirit** — Called "one of the better uncommons in the set." Wheeled late pack one, which the host flagged as surprising/late signal.

### Blue
- **Mistwalker** — Host's highest-praise common. "Play set of that card, it's really really good." 1/4 flyer, counts as a giant for Glimpse synergy, resilient on defense, triggers Calamity Bearer damage-doubling. Took four copies deliberately.
- **Glimpse the Cosmos** — Premium card selection in giants decks. "Dig Through Time in a deck with changelings or giants" — recurs from graveyard for one blue with a giant in play. P1P1-quality take in blue-giants.
- **Augury Raven** — "Meh." Below curve filler.
- **Disdainful Stroke** — Taken late as a freeroll; the host doesn't expect to play it but notes "you certainly can."
- **Strategic Planning** — Useful card flow, plays well with Port of Karfell (return a creature from yard). Sideboard/flex slot.
- **Run Ashore** — "Bounce and top." Utility; resets Bloodline Pretender or answers Bound in Gold. Functional but clunky.
- **Brinebarrow Intruder** — Mentioned as a possible pick (?) over Strategic Planning; the host takes Planning. Playable but lower priority.

### Black
- **Port of Karfell** — "Pretty strong land. Mill four, then return a creature from your graveyard to the battlefield tapped." Blue-black land; strong in grindy decks. Host notes "blue-black is a less desirable color pair" but the land's power pulled toward it briefly.
- **Priest of the Haunted Edge** — Mentioned as a snow payoff in Sultai; not taken here (wrong colors).
- **Draugr Thought Thief** — "Three mana 3/2 surveil one. It's playable — in that it has power and toughness." Barely-playable filler.
- **Skull Cairn** — Listed but not discussed. Filler.
- **Death Knell Berserker** — Listed but passed. Filler in non-black decks.

### Red
- **Demonbolt** — Premium removal; "definitely taking Demonbolt over Bound in Gold, not particularly close." Best red common for removal.
- **Squash** — Conditional removal worth including with enough giants/shapeshifters. "I think we have to take Squash over the Amulet" — removal > artifact in this deck.
- **Tundra Fumarole** — "Three mana deal four, add colorless for each snow spent." Called "totally fine" as difficult-to-cast but functional burn. Snow-mana upside rarely relevant; treat as 3-mana four damage.
- **Berg Strider** — Giant-type creature; synergizes with Glimpse. Host picks it in pack one for the giants plan. Also has a snow-mana activated ability to tap something.
- **Calamity Bearer** — "Four mana 3/4 — if a giant source you control deals damage, it deals double instead." Called a bomb payoff. "Plays extremely well with Mistwalkers." The premier uncommon/rare in the giants archetype.
- **Basalt Ravager** — "Deals X damage equal to the greatest number of types you have." With three giants + three shapeshifters it can hit for 6-12. "Ravager's going to be swole, swoletastic." The damage-dealing giant that combos with Calamity Bearer for 12 damage in one shot.
- **Cinderheart Giant** — Taken late as a large threat; gets it done in games.
- **Battle of Frost and Fire** — **Do not take.** "Just not good enough. Green creatures are bigger. Changelings exist." Opened P1P1 three times across flashback drafts, never taken.
- **Cavalry Firewalker** — Mentioned as a passable two-drop in RU; not taken here.
- **Seize the Spoils** — "Don't think I'll play." Filler in non-sacrifice decks.
- **Frostfire Arcanist** — "Need doubles of multiple instants/sorceries for this to really be good." Narrow; not taken.
- **Dwarven Hammer** — Named "best card in the pack" in one pack, then passed for Glimpse (snow synergy preference). "Dwarven Hammer is the best card here" — very high pick in Boros equipment builds, just not taken due to deck direction.
- **Dwarven Reinforcements** — Possible late pick; wheeled in the video.
- **Run Amok** — "I don't think we're aggressive enough for Run Amok." Needs an aggressive shell.

### Green
- **Glittering Frost** — "I don't think there's any sequence where we end up Temur, but if there is, it's going to be with a card like that." Snow-fixing payoff for Temur builds.
- **Sarulf's Packmate** — Mentioned as a gift being passed to neighbors. Presumably a solid body with upside.
- **Elven Bow** — Mentioned alongside Dwarven Hammer as a pick consideration; Hammer rated higher.

### Multicolor
- **Alrund, God of the Cosmos / Hakka, Whispering Raven** — "Alrund is excellent." The flip card: Hakka is a 2/3 that on combat damage returns to hand and scries two; Alrund is a 1/1 that grows with cards in hand and foretold cards, then draws by naming a card type. Host calls Alrund "excellent" and "Alrund's a babe, for sure" after seeing it win a game. A premium bomb.

### Colorless / Artifacts
- **Replicating Ring** — Taken over Helm (?) for snow-permanent count for Icebind Pillar activations. Also eventual 8-land payoff. "Another snow-producing permanent is nice to see."
- **Giant's Amulet** — "Five mana 4/5 hexproof — holds the ground real well" but removal taken over it (Squash). Defensive tool; likely better in slower builds.
- **Longboat (?) / Kol (?)** — Host says "love me a Kol" and takes it; unclear if this is a card name or caption garble for a two-drop.

### Lands
- **Snow Mountain / Snow Island / Snow Plains** — Top priorities once in a snow deck. "We will be taking snow mountains and islands over basically everything." Snow Plains called "one of the weaker snow lands cuz the snow land stuff is really in Sultai."
- **Sulfurous Mire** — Snow-producing dual (black-red); taken for Icebind Pillar snow-mana requirements.
- **Port of Karfell** — "Pretty strong land." Blue-black; mill four + reanimate a creature. See Black section.

### Rares & Bombs
- **Alrund, God of the Cosmos** — Premium bomb (see Multicolor above). Explicit "excellent" rating.
- **Calamity Bearer** — Bomb-level uncommon in giants decks (see Red above).
- **Bloodline Pretender** — Strong shapeshifter; "name giant" for all giant synergies. Three-mana pick in the giants shell. Takes all creature types in play, so improves with diverse creature types.
- **Egon, God of Death** — "This card is not good." Hard pass.
- **Ascent of the Worthy** — "Not a good card. Better than it reads, but still pretty bad." Avoid.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Bound in Gold wheeling late** is notable — the host says "kind of surprised to see that" and takes it as a signal the white section of the pack was opened by an aggressive drafter who doesn't want removal enchantments.
- **Snow lands not appearing in packs** is a real constraint — the host comments repeatedly on being "light on snow" and having to take snow lands over playables multiple times. If snow lands dry up early, the Icebind Pillar / Avalanche Caller plan falls apart. Monitor snow land availability and adjust depth of snow-synergy cards accordingly.
- **Giants / Mistwalkers going late** (pack 2 produced many) signals that the neighbors are not in the giants archetype. The host had a difficult pack 2 ("this is going to pull me deeper into red, blue-red specifically") where good non-giant cards appeared but the lane stayed open.
- **Boros signals:** Dwarven Hammer, Cavalry Firewalker, and Run Amok being available suggest Boros is open in the pod. The host passed Hammer deliberately; its late availability would signal Boros territory is clear.
- **White-heavy packs (pack 2):** "This pack is a miss. Lots of green and white." When your target colors are dry, take fixing or the best off-color card rather than reaching for marginal on-color filler.

## TL;DR meta read

- **The format is soup vs. aggro, full stop.** Despite five "college" color pairs and officially 15 archetypes, in practice there are two macro-strategies: greedy multicolor control/value ("soup") and proactive 2-color aggro. Midrange is almost totally dead. The college mechanics happen naturally — you do not need to "build toward" them.
- **Soup (greedy multicolor, Converge-fueled, Potioner's-Trove-enabled late-game control) is the best thing to do.** The strongest soup decks crush Lorehold/Silverquill aggro because cheap interaction dismantles their curve. 17Lands shows white/Boros on top only because it cannot represent 4-5 color piles.
- **White and blue are the two best colors** — identical to original Strixhaven. White is the best aggro base; blue is the best late-game/control base. This also means the best aggro (Silverquill, Lorehold) and the best late-game (Prismari soup, Quandrix soup) are both accessible from those two colors.
- **Do not take commons in picks 1-8.** The gap between good uncommons/rares and the commons that actually matter is massive. Exception: the commons-that-matter list below.
- **Draft matters enormously in this format.** If your draft goes off the rails, your deck is often unrecoverable. Being the sole drafter of an open college is the highest-EV seat. Redundancy is mandatory — if you're Witherbloom, you need several life-gain enablers, not a pocket of two.
- **Games are slow and go long.** Incidental life gain (Infusion, pests, Potioner's Trove) means aggro must have a way to keep gas flowing (Cost of Brilliance). Rares and mythics define games because you always have time to find them. A bad soup deck is the worst place to be — it loses to everything.
- **Removal is premium but matchup-dependent.** Pack interaction, but use looting/rummage effects (Pursue the Past, Rubble Rouser) to ditch dead cards in the wrong matchup. Tone Blast and Last Gasp are the format's best commons.

## Format principles

**Draft strategy:**
- Identify the open college early and commit hard. Being the lone drafter in a college means you wheel your key two-drop commons and build the necessary redundancy.
- If the open college isn't clear by pick 4-5, default to "most powerful cards, open for soup." The worst outcome is mid-commitment — a synergy deck without enough redundant pieces.
- **Don't pick commons pack 1, picks 1-8** — this is Ben's explicit soapbox. Take hits in power level; the uncommons and rares are far ahead of most commons. Exception: removal commons you actively want.
- Once you have a soup payoff (Snarl Song, Wisdom of Ages, Together as One), taking fixing over raw power is correct. With two Snarl Songs, nothing should push you off Converge.
- **Dual lands are undervalued**, especially once you've committed to Converge. The blue-red, blue-green, white-black, red-white tapped duals are all priority picks once your lane is clear.
- Take fixing over the second copy of a mediocre card. The manabase is what lets soup do its thing.
- In pod drafts, Converge fixing is even more important — the other drafter competing with you will have far less access.

**Deckbuilding:**
- **17-land format.** You rarely run out of things to spend mana on. Only soup decks with sketchy manabases ever play 18.
- Creature counts are extremely low in the best late-game decks. 3 creatures is common; 0 creatures is not unheard of in Prismari control.
- Pack both removal and card advantage. Removal alone does nothing when the soup mirrors go long.
- Matchup-dependent removal (bounce, discard) is less reliable. Include looting/filtering to hedge.
- The aggro decks (Lorehold, Silverquill) need **Cost of Brilliance** or similar to keep gas flowing in the mid-game. Without it they run dry.
- **Infusion triggers can be stacked** via Potioner's Trove (cast spell → hold priority → tap Trove → life gained before spell resolves). The Discord surfaced this rule interaction mid-format.

**Key lines:**
- **Soup vs. aggro math:** If aggro falls behind on board by turn 5, the game is over. If soup stumbles on a land drop or misses their key removal spell, they also lose. Whoever falls behind their plan first loses — very little comeback mechanic.
- Aggro decks should ask constantly: will this creature die to Tone Blast (two toughness or less)? If yes, it's a liability.
- Prepared creatures: take the creature trade if you need to. Don't protect the creature just to extract the prepared spell — if you're behind, the floor matters more than the ceiling.

## Card notes (community consensus)

### White

- [**Elite Interceptor**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Elite%20Interceptor) (1W, 1/2, prepared → tap/untap draw card): One of the top two commons in the format. Excellent in Silverquill and as an early Repartee enabler. At sorcery speed the prepare trigger is weaker than a clue token, but Repartee upside compensates.
- [**Quillblade Laureate**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Quillblade%20Laureate) (1W, 1/1 double strike, prepared → gives double strike to a target): Top-3 white uncommon. Comes with its own targeting trigger for Repartee and snowballs quickly. Far better than Enns Debate Moderator.
- [**Eager Glyph Mage**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Eager%20Glyph%20Mage) (3W, 3/3, makes 1/1 flyer on ETB): Ben loved it; Ethan concluded it belongs in Lorehold (go-wide payoffs) not Silverquill. **Overperformed less than expected — not a commons-that-matter card.** More of a Lorehold support card.
- [**Rehearsed Debater**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rehearsed%20Debater) (2W, 3/3 vigilance, Repartee +1/+1): Above-rate three-drop. Attacks as a 4/4 vigilance in practice because of the implied trick. Highly recommended.
- [**Soaring Stone Glider**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Soaring%20Stone%20Glider) (2W, 4/3 flyer, exile 2 from yard or pay 1W as additional cost): Good 5-drop equivalent. Pairs with Primary Research for value engine; fine on its own as a 5-mana 4/3 flyer.
- [**Daydream**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Daydream) (W, exile/return own creature, +1/+1, flashback 2W): Both hosts said **garbage** — sorcery speed is too slow, flashback cost is uncastable in practice.
- [**Graduation Day**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Graduation%20Day) (W enchantment, Repartee → +1/+1 counter on a creature): Both hosts called **great** after deliberation — makes combat miserable for the opponent and turns every removal spell into a buff.
- [**Primary Research**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Primary%20Research) (4W enchantment, ETB return ≤3 MV card from yard; draw card each end step if something left yard): Called "great" at preview. After playing, both hosts cooled on it — slow and clunky in the white aggro context. Fun for Lorehold grinds but not a pull into the color.
- [**Enns Debate Moderator**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Enns%20Debate%20Moderator) (1W uncommon, blink a creature, +1/+1 per exile each end step): **Mover down.** Asks for multiple pieces to line up; never comes down on turn 2; below the format's power level bar.
- [**Ajani's Response**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Ajani%27s%20Response) (4W, destroy creature, costs 3 less vs. tapped): Early-impression enthusiasm quickly reversed — filler interaction in the early weeks. Only relevant in four-color white control (rarely achieved).
- [**Summoned Dromedary**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Summoned%20Dromedary) (3W, 4/3 vigilance, 1W to return from yard): Surprisingly functional as a Jeskai late-game looping engine. Not a pull, but worth including in deep-control lists.

### Blue

- [**Run Behind**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Run%20Behind) (U, instant, bounce + surveil 1 (?)):  A commons-that-matter card. Especially crucial in Prismari where red lacks reliable answers to large threats.
- [**Essence Scatter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Essence%20Scatter) (1U, counter creature): Great. When you hit it at the right moment — countering a 4- or 5-drop — it can end the game. Not flashy but frequently format-defining.
- [**Landscape Painter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Landscape%20Painter) (1U, 2/1, prepared → Vibrant Idea draw 2): **Garbage** (both hosts, early and mid). Worst-case is a 2-mana 2/1 with a draw-2 spell that rarely fires. Literal poster child of the "creature needs to be good up front" lesson.
- [**Encouraging Aviator**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Encouraging%20Aviator) (2U, 2/3 flyer, attack → prepared Jump instant): **Underrated.** Lets you bank the jump and use it a turn later, enabling three simultaneous attackers. 2 power on a flyer is a step up from typical 1-power variants. Good in Prismari (cheap Opus trigger) and Quandrix.
- [**Fractal Anomaly**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fractal%20Anomaly) (2U instant, make 0/0 fractal with +1/+1 per card drawn this turn): Initially misread as "spells cast." Hosts re-evaluated as **garbage** once they saw "cards drawn." Tough condition to reliably exploit.
- [**Deluge Virtuoso**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Deluge%20Virtuoso) (2U, 2/2, ETB tap and stun, Opus +1/+1 / 5-mana +2/+2): Preview garbage call reversed — both hosts **very impressed** mid-format. Basically prowess with a built-in stun. Hard to block in the early turns.
- [**Procrastinate**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Procrastinate) (XU, tap creature, put 2X stun counters): Discussed extensively — still **garbage** per the hosts. Leaves the creature on board; prepared spells still get cast off it; too many drawbacks vs. enchantment-style removal.
- [**Muse's Encouragement**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Muse%27s%20Encouragement) (4U instant, create 3/3 flyer + surveil 2): **Garbage** (preview). Fine in Prismari, but you can't jam multiples and the body loses a point of toughness vs. paying 5 for a 3/4 flyer elsewhere.
- [**Orisha Tide Choreographer**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Orisha%20Tide%20Choreographer) (4U, 2/2 ward, costs 3 less if total toughness ≥10, ETB draw 2): Looked like Mulldrifter. **Mover down** in the early impressions episode — rarely played for 2 mana, the body is irrelevant once you've drawn 2, and it's weirdly similar in floor to Landscape Painter.
- [**Hydro Channeler**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Hydro%20Channeler) (1U, 1/3, taps for blue or filters to any color for instant/sorcery): **Mover down.** Not a consistent ramp engine; gets ignored in combat; below the format's power level bar.

### Black

- [**Last Gasp**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Last%20Gasp) (1B, instant, -3/-3 until end of turn): One of the very best commons in the format. Trading up on mana with a two- or three-drop is consistently strong.
- [**Wander Off**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Wander%20Off) (3B, instant, exile creature): Excellent premium removal. Ethan loved it.
- [**Cost of Brilliance**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Cost%20of%20Brilliance) (2B, instant, draw 2, lose 2, +1/+1 counter on a creature): Ben's card of the format. **Most important card for aggressive black decks.** Better than Killian's Confidence in Silverquill. Keeps gas flowing in the mid-game so aggro decks don't run dry. Not good in soup.
- [**Mind Rot variant**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Mind%20Rot%20variant) / **Arcane Omens** (4B converge, opponent discards X): Powerful discard payoff for soup. Ben opened with "Mind Twist is still good in 2026." When you converge for 4-5, it's format-warping; snap-concede territory.
- [**Dissection Practice**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Dissection%20Practice) (B instant, lose 1 gain 1, target creature +1/+1, another -1/-1): One mana that triggers Repartee and Infusion simultaneously, plus a potential combat blowout. Both hosts were impressed. **C+ / filler-plus.**
- [**Melancholic Poet**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Melancholic%20Poet) (1B, 2/2, Repartee → opponent loses 1 life / you gain 1): Mid-format mover up. Works in Silverquill as a drain engine and incidentally in Witherbloom as a "makes any removal spell gain-a-life."
- [**Eternal Student**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Eternal%20Student) (3B, 4/2, 1B exile from yard → two 1/1 flyers at instant speed): Instantly spotted as powerful — the instant speed on the token generation is the broken part. BUT the hosts' retrospective verdict: **bad in this format** because neither black college (Witherbloom's life-gain plan, Silverquill's aggressive plan) wanted a grindy midrange card, and the best decks had almost no creatures anyway.
- [**Send in the Pest**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Send%20in%20the%20Pest) (1B, opponent discards, make 1/1 pest): **Virus Beetle but worse.** Flashback makes discarding cards from your hand painful. The format goes too big for a "discard 1" to matter. Worst of the format's Virus Beetle variants.
- [**Grave Researcher**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Grave%20Researcher) (2B, 3/3, Repartee surveil 1, if ≥3 creatures in yard becomes prepared → Reanimate): Ben liked it; Ethan called it "centaur courser" in practice — idea is more exciting than the implementation. Hard to turn on in mid-range Witherbloom/Silverquill where you want creatures on board. Some late-game blowout potential vs. opponents who play Reanimate from it.
- [**Stargaze**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Stargaze) (XX BB, look at 2X cards, put half in hand, lose X life): Very swingy — broken when you're at 15+ and stable; unplayable when you're at 9. Good in soup as a burst card-draw payoff when ahead.

### Red

- [**Tone Blast**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Tone%20Blast) (1R, sorcery, deal 2 to any target, flashback 4R): **Best common in the format** per Ethan, strongly agreed by both. The "litmus test" for creature sizing — if your creature dies to this, it's a liability. Flashback makes it a two-for-one. Naturally leaves the graveyard for Lorehold.
- [**Unsubtle Mockery**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Unsubtle%20Mockery) (2R, instant, deal 4 to creature + surveil 1): Finally "they made Bombard good." The surveil is excellent for Lorehold graveyard management and for hand-smoothing in soup. Both hosts are big fans.
- [**Expressive Fire Dancer**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Expressive%20Fire%20Dancer) (1R, 2/2, Opus → +1/+1 until EOT, 5-mana Opus → double strike until EOT): **Commons-that-matter.** Hard to block in early turns; combined with double strike for Opus, it snowballs quickly. Better than Deluge Virtuoso at low power level.
- [**Artistic Process**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Artistic%20Process) (3RR, modal sorcery, deal 6 / deal 2 to all opponents' creatures / make 3/3 flying haste): All three modes are impactful if slightly overcosted. Good in Prismari (triggers Opus) and as a board wipe against small creatures. Ben's pick at preview was revised upward.
- [**Dual Tactics**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Dual%20Tactics) (R, sorcery, deal 1 to creature + can't block, flashback 1R): Both hosts called **great**. Sneaky-good because it does two things at once (kill X/1 + free attacks), triggers Repartee for your own stuff, and flashback gives a second copy.
- [**Molten Note**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Molten%20Note) (X RW, deal damage to creature = mana spent, untap all your creatures, flashback 6RW): Reasonable as a one-shot removal spell. Flashback cost is effectively never cast. **Mover down** from preview hype — fine common, not a format-defining role.
- [**Wilt in the Heat**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Wilt%20in%20the%20Heat) (RW, instant, costs 2 less if ≥1 card left your yard this turn, deal 5 to creature, exile if it dies): One of the best commons. Can randomly be cast for RW (two mana) when Lorehold does its thing; always at least a solid 4-mana deal-5.
- [**Rubble Rouser**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rubble%20Rouser) (2R, 1/4, discard/draw looting + tap exile from yard for R + 1 damage per activation): Good in Prismari; **not good in Lorehold** per Ethan (Lorehold needs to get the game over, not grind). Provides card filtering to discard dead matchup-dependent removal.
- [**Dual Tactics**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Dual%20Tactics) (R, flashback 1R): see above — great.

### Green

- [**Noxious Nute / Noxious Newt**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Noxious%20Nute%20/%20Noxious%20Newt) (1G, 1/2 deathtouch, taps for G as mana dork): Useful ramp for Witherbloom and Quandrix. Vulnerable to Tone Blast. Ethan argues not worth prioritizing early in converge lists — the deathtouch/ramp creature is correct, but the format punishes playing small bodies.
- [**Studious First-Year**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Studious%20First-Year) (G, 1/1, prepared → Rampant Growth): **Worse than previewed.** Confirmed under-performer early format. Only truly wants to be in converge decks (to fetch a basic for each color). Even there, the one-mana 1/1 body is a liability. Never play in a two-color deck.
- [**Environmental Scientist**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Environmental%20Scientist) (1G, 2/2, ETB search basic → hand): Excellent ramp. Windmill-slam in any converge or Quandrix list. Safer and more consistent than Studious First-Year. Ethan named this the pick Ben should have made over Arc of Hunger in the Pop Quiz episode.
- [**Infirmary Healer**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Infirmary%20Healer) (1G, 2/3, prepared → Stream of Life): **Good.** The 2/3 body is fine on two, and Stream of Life later fuels Infusion / Witherbloom triggers / Opus. Asks nothing of you; has incidental upside in multiple archetypes.
- [**Tenured Concocter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Tenured%20Concocter) (4G, 4/5 common vigilance, Infusion +2/+0, draws a card if targeted by opponent): **Breakout over-performer.** Both hosts were wrong about this card at preview. Annoying to deal with; Ethan put it on the commons-that-matter list mid-format. Durable body that generates value when removed.
- [**Shopkeeper's Bane**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Shopkeeper%27s%20Bane) (2G, 4/2 trample, attack → gain 2 life): Better in Witherbloom than the hosts initially thought. Nathan's pick over Eternal Student at the PT was validated (Bane wheeled back to him; Eternal Student never appeared in his deck).
- [**Bramble Barrage**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bramble%20Barrage) (1G, instant, Gruul fight variant — target creature gets +1/+1 if you've cast another instant/sorcery, then fights): **Mover up** — pairs extremely well with the deathtouch 1/2 mana dork. Provides combat-trick style removal in green.
- [**Snarlsong**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Snarlsong) (5G, sorcery, converge — make two fractals with power/toughness = converge, gain X life): The converge deck's premier finisher. **Should be pack-1 pick-1 quality.** Single green pip means it's easy to splash from nearly any base. Ben and Ethan both agree — nothing pushes you off Snarlsong once you have two of them.

### Multicolor

- [**Bogwater Luminary**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bogwater%20Luminary) (BG, 2/2, whenever any creature enters you gain 1 life): Core Witherbloom engine. Not a pull-into-the-color card; only prioritize it once you know you're Witherbloom or a life-gain payoff deck.
- [**Pest Mascot**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Pest%20Mascot) (1BG, 2/3 trample, whenever you gain life +1/+1 counter): Strong Witherbloom payoff. Curves into the two-drop enablers beautifully. Very good.
- [**Teacher's Pest**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Teacher%27s%20Pest) (BG, 1/1 menace uncommon skeleton, attack → gain 1 life, BG → return from graveyard tapped): Aptly named annoyance. Reassembling Skeleton plus life gain and menace. Repeatable leave-graveyard trigger for Lorehold hybrid lists.
- [**Luin Exchange Student**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Luin%20Exchange%20Student) (2BG, 3/4 uncommon, enters prepared → Pest Friend BG make 1/1 pest; exile a creature from your graveyard to prepare again at sorcery speed): Busted when it goes unanswered — can produce three pest tokens from one activation cycle. Top Witherbloom uncommon. Choke point: getting creatures in the yard is hard.
- [**Witherbloom Charm**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Witherbloom%20Charm) (BG, instant, choose one — sac permanent draw 2; gain 5 life; destroy ≤2 MV nonland): **Best charm** per Ethan. Two-mana destroy-on-curve is excellent; gain 5 is a race-swinger; sac a pest to draw 2 is gas in the mid-game.
- [**Grapple with Death**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Grapple%20with%20Death) (1BG, sorcery, destroy artifact or creature, gain 1 life): Premium Witherbloom kill spell. Removing anything plus incidental life-gain triggers for a clean 3 mana. Arc hit on the Herbalism mana-value flexibility (destroys artifacts, too).
- [**Inkling Mascot**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Inkling%20Mascot) (WB, 2/2, Repartee → gains flying + surveil 1): Excellent common two-drop for Silverquill. Exactly what the deck wants — flies over, generates graveyard knowledge.
- [**Silverquill Charm**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Silverquill%20Charm) (WB, instant, choose one — +2/+2 on a creature; exile ≤2 power creature; opponent loses 3 / you gain 3): **Great charm.** The exile mode catches Prismari's whale and other low-power threat-creatures off-guard. Three-life drain is a race-ender.
- [**Elemental Mascot**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Elemental%20Mascot) (1UR, 1/4 flying vigilance, Opus → +1/+0 until EOT; 5-mana Opus → exile top card + play until next turn): **Great** — Ben and Ethan both called it excellent. 1/4 flying vigilance is a good defensive body that holds the fort and generates card advantage late. Top Prismari common.
- [**Prismari Charm**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Prismari%20Charm) (UR, instant, choose one — surveil 2 draw; deal 1 to each of up to two targets; bounce nonland permanent): Both hosts called it **the best charm** — all three modes are independently worth two mana.
- [**Rapturous Moment**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rapturous%20Moment) (4UR, sorcery, draw 3 discard 2 add UURR): Juicy. Provides massive card selection and floats mana for follow-up spells. Ethan was high on this; Ben cooler — confirmed as merely decent, not a top finisher.
- [**Quandrix Charm**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Quandrix%20Charm) (GU, instant, counter spell unless pay 2; destroy enchantment; creature becomes 5/5 until EOT): Worth including in every Quandrix deck; the counter mode alone justifies the slot.
- [**Cuboid Colony**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Cuboid%20Colony) (GU, 1/1 insect flash flying trample, increment): Strong two-drop for Quandrix. Flash lets you play it at end of opponent's turn into an immediately 2/2 flying trample next turn with any spell cast. Good even as a late-game top-deck thanks to prepared spells boosting it.
- [**Lorehold Charm**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Lorehold%20Charm) (RW, instant, choose one — sacrifice artifact; return ≤2 MV artifact/creature from graveyard; team +1/+1 trample): Divisible by the hosts — Ethan called it stinky; Ben argued the reanimate mode at instant speed triggers all your leave-graveyard stuff. **At minimum situational; not the clean pull that Witherbloom Charm is.**

### Colorless / Artifacts

- [**Potioner's Trove**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Potioner%27s%20Trove) (3, artifact, taps for mana of any color; if you've cast an instant/sorcery this turn, tap to gain 2 life): **The Golden Egg Award** — glues every soup deck together, provides relevant lifegain on any turn you cast a spell, and doubles as fixing. The best non-rare common in the format.
- [**Diary of Dreams**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Diary%20of%20Dreams) (2, artifact book, instant/sorcery → page counter, 5 tap draw a card — costs 1 less per page counter): Potential build-around in a spell-heavy control list. Both hosts were higher on it mid-format than initially expected, but it never reached common-knowledge tier.
- [**Biblioplex Tome Keeper**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Biblioplex%20Tome%20Keeper) (4, artifact creature 3/4, ETB — prepare a creature or unprepare a creature): Ben called **great**; Ethan **garbage**. The unprepare mode on an opponent's bomb is real upside, but the disagreement persists — thin evidence.
- [**Skycoach Waypoint**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Skycoach%20Waypoint) (colorless land, taps for colorless, 3 + tap → prepare a creature): Both hosts said **garbage** — too mana-intensive, pays for things you could do elsewhere; utility land baseline hasn't been good in years.
- [**Great Hall of the Biblioplex**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Great%20Hall%20of%20the%20Biblioplex) (land, taps colorless, also taps for any color to pay for instants/sorceries only, becomes 2/4 wizard if you cast instant/sorcery): Ben loved it; both hosts concluded it is **strictly better than Terramorphic Expanse in Converge** because it provides true five-color access (not just one basic) and can become a threat late.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Reading the open college is the most important skill in this format.** With 10 multicolor gold cards per college at non-rare, you will feel the density of a given college dropping quickly if someone is cutting you.
- **Watch for density over premium.** You don't need to see two Silverquill rares to know it's open — if Inkling Mascots and Scolding Administrators are wheeling, that is the stronger signal.
- **Gold cards late in pack 1 (picks 5-8) are pivot invitations.** A pick-6 Quandrix dual land or a pick-7 Grapple with Death means that college may be wide open to your right.
- **Converge/soup is harder to read open.** Since the payoffs (Snarlsong single G pip, Arcane Omens) are easy to splash, two or three drafters can both attempt it. Potioner's Trove wheeling late (pick 9+) is a strong signal that late-game soup is wide open.
- **Inkling Mascots and Bogwater Luminaries are the "canary in the coal mine" for Silverquill and Witherbloom respectively.** If they never wheel, you are contested.
- **What typically wheels in SOS:** mediocre gold commons (Wild Hypothesis), non-blue/non-white fixing, non-Snarlsong Converge payoffs (Archaic's Agony), and situational sorceries (Daydream).
- **Pivoting advice:** The gold card density makes pivots costly in packs 2-3. If you have only 3-4 gold cards from one college in pack 1 and they are underwhelming, you are likely better off staying flexible toward Prismari soup than forcing Lorehold if pack 2 opens something better. Never pivot late into Witherbloom unless you have both the life-gain enablers AND payoffs — the redundancy requirement is brutal.

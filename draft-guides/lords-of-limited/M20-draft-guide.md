## TL;DR meta read

- **Red is considered one of the best colors** — Alex Quartermaine and the hosts note red is deep and its cards work very well together in an aggressive game plan. Confirmed across both episodes by multiple red drafters.
- **The best single red archetype is mono-red or near-mono-red aggro**, built around Pack Mastiff, Goblin Smuggler, Lava Coil Brawler (Lavakin Brawler?), and the 3–2 Chandras cards. Red goes very wide and very fast.
- **White is a strong second color** — Raise the Alarm is called the best white common and best common overall by Ethan. Blue-white (skies/fliers) and red-white aggro both function. White gets cut fast; pick white signals early.
- **Blue-black is a real late-game archetype** — grindy control/value with Gravedigger, Blood Burglar, Audacious Thief, Scholar of the Ages recursion loops, and top-end fliers. Wins by stabilizing and grinding.
- **Elementals matter** — Lavakin Brawler synergizes with Ember Cat and Scampering Scorcher; Cavalier of Flame is a bomb rare that rewards going deep on red elementals.
- **Removal is scarce at common** — Shock, Chandra's Outrage, and Rabid Bite are highly prized. Stone-zero removal is a real draft outcome; prioritize removal above most other picks once you are established in a color.
- **Format speed is medium-fast.** Aggressive decks (red, red-white) are common and capable. Blue-black control needs strong early plays to survive; having ≥2 one-drops or two-drops matters enormously.

## Format principles

- **Red goes wide and deep — draft it as mono-color if you can.** The more mountains you have the better Chandra's Regulator gets. Ember Cat plus Lavakin Brawler plus Scampering Scorcher create real elemental synergy; don't dilute unnecessarily.
- **Pack Mastiff is excellent in multiples.** Three to five copies are realistic — each one buffs all the others and they go wide for Goblin Smuggler to make unblockable. Don't undervalue this synergy; the board can snowball fast.
- **Goblin Smuggler is a high pick** (hosts note the LoL Discord pushed this card's reputation up). Making Pack Mastiffs unblockable or combo-ing with Audacious Thief / Fencing Ace is a real game-ending engine.
- **Raise the Alarm is the best white common** (Ethan, GP Vegas episode). Take it over most alternatives; the two tokens support Griffin Protector, inspired-charge finishers, and equipment synergies.
- **Removal is scarce — prioritize it.** Disfigure, Murder, Shock, Chandra's Outrage, Rabid Bite, Agonizing Siphon, Bone Splinters, and Pacifism are the maindeck-quality removal; don't let them go. Having "stone zero removal" is a real and painful outcome.
- **Curve is critical in this format.** The GP Vegas blue-black deck post-mortem: having only one 1-drop and two 2-drops (with Anticipate half-counting) made the deck fragile against aggressive decks. Prioritize 2-drops once your color is locked.
- **Run 18 lands in high-curve grindy decks.** Blue-black control with Scholar of the Ages, Gravedigger, and top-end fliers warrants 18. Red aggro runs 16–17.
- **Season of Growth is a real card** — works extremely well with Rabid Bite and Infuriate (draw on each targeting). If you are red-green, take Season of Growth and plan around it.
- **Flame Sweep is a sideboard card in aggressive decks.** Ben debates playing Flame Sweep and ultimately puts it on the sideboard. Kills too many of your own creatures in a Pack Mastiff deck. Bring it in against go-wide opponents.
- **Anticipate / Tome Bound Lynch (?)** are spells that enable Scholar of the Ages to loop removal. Don't undervalue these in a blue-black shell with Scholar.
- **Mana base ratios matter.** For a green splash off a red base: 5 green sources (Evolving Wilds + 4 Forests) is enough for 3 green cards. Chandra's Regulator requires a heavy mountain count — going splash-green weakens it.
- **Blue-white in GP Vegas wheeled both Inspired Charges** — if multiple copies of a go-wide payoff wheel, you are almost certainly in the correct, underdrafted lane.

## Card notes (community consensus)

Episode shorthand: **show1** = M20 Showdown Video 1 (2019-08-11); **gp** = GP Vegas M20 Live Showdown (2019-09-08).

### White

- **Raise the Alarm** — "best white common," "best common" overall in Ethan's opinion (gp). High pick. Creates two bodies that enable Griffin Protector and Inspired Charge finishers. Wheels when white is open.
- **Griffin Protector** — solid white common, rewards going wide (gp, show1).
- **Daybreak Chaplain** — playable one-drop but low ceiling; value depends on life-link role (gp).
- **Griffin Sentinel** — three Griffins all made Ethan's GP deck; strong with Empyrean Eagle (gp).
- **Inspired Charge** — powerful finisher for go-wide decks; two copies wheeled in GP Vegas, confirming open lane (gp).
- **Inspired Captain** — fine go-wide payoff, playable but not exciting (show1).
- **Ancestral Blade** — "very very very good," called possibly the best white uncommon by Ben (gp). Instant +1/+1 and equipment on ETB is efficient.
- **Pacifism** — premium removal; Ben corrects himself for taking Master Splicer over Pacifism and calls it a miss pick (gp).
- **Eternal Isolation** — sideboard-quality removal; brought in vs. Attempts (?) (gp).
- **Devout Decree** — powerful sideboard card, not a maindeck card (show1, gp).
- **Warden of Evos Isle** — both hosts express little enthusiasm for this card (show1).
- **Daunting Angel** — decent pick up; goes in a white fliers deck but lower priority than the above (show1).

### Blue

- **Cloud-Kin Seer (?)** — Ben's P1P1 in GP Vegas. Strong uncommon/rare for blue-white or blue-based tempo; considered better than a color split early.
- **Sleep Paralysis** — playable removal-ish; Ethan takes Pattern Matcher over it P1P2 to stay mono-blue, suggesting Sleep Paralysis is only a medium pick (gp).
- **Empyrean Eagle** — windmill-slam in blue-white fliers; very late pick in pack 2 that locks in the blue-white strategy (gp).
- **Pattern Matcher** — Ethan's P1P2; good when you have duplicates. The deck building payoff requires multiples of a creature (gp). Works with Griffin Sentinels.
- **Winged Words** — card draw that goes well in a fliers deck; "much-needed card advantage" (gp).
- **Yaro's Wave Crasher (?)** / Yargle's Wave Crasher (?) — 4/4 for 4 blue; "more powerful for 4 mana" in blue-black, but Ben calls the pack "stinky" (gp).
- **Sage's Row Denizen** — Ethan flirts with the mill plan but repeatedly acknowledges it rarely comes together (gp). Mostly a filler 2/1.
- **Anticipate** — meaningful enabler in Scholar of the Ages loops (gp).
- **Frost Lynx** — Ethan comments he doesn't like it very much, then concedes it's a decent pickup (gp, show1).
- **Boreal Elemental** — blue elemental, hard to cast; Air Elemental considered better (show1).
- **Air Elemental** — 4/4 flier, strong standalone but color-intensive (show1).
- **Master Splicer** — Ben took it over Pacifism and called that a mistake (gp).

### Black

- **Murder** — "fantastic follow-up," best black common (gp). Snap P1P2 after Disfigure.
- **Disfigure** — Ben's P1P1 at GP Vegas over Looming Shaman. "Responsible pick" even if the shaman is tempting (gp).
- **Agonizing Siphon** — premium common removal / burn. Ben and Ethan both note passing these feels bad (gp, show1).
- **Gravedigger** — "awesome" two-for-one in blue-black; good with Wave Crasher recursion, Bone Clad Necromancer (gp).
- **Blood Burglar** — Ethan specifically praises it in red-black/black aggro: "blood burglar into goblin smuggler is a nice little 2→3 hit" gaining lifelink every turn, forcing opponents to remove Smuggler first (gp).
- **Audacious Thief** — Ethan's bread-and-butter card in black; combos with Goblin Smuggler for unblockable card draw (gp, show1).
- **Rotting Regisaur** — Ethan goes back and forth on this card (show1). "Went up, went down, now back to B." Good in aggro (black-red/black-white where you empty your hand), bad in grindy black-green where you want cards in hand.
- **Bone Splinters** — best black common for entering black early; don't want multiples unless you have sacrifice fodder (Sanitarium Skeleton) (show1).
- **Blood for Bones** — nice 2-for-1, enables discard synergies with Kelton Raider (?)/Chandra's Regulator; locks you into black (show1 Ethan).
- **Gorging Vulture** — playable in blue-black; can trade down and gains life; feeds graveyard (gp).
- **Blood-Soaked Altar** — both hosts mention this as a significant card-disadvantage cost; only playable with Sanitarium Skeleton (gp, show1).
- **Sanitarium Skeleton** — important combo piece with Blood-Soaked Altar and Rotting Regisaur; Ethan actively looks for them when he has discard outlets (gp).
- **Bone Clad Necromancer** — "much better" than Gruesome Scourger; can make multiple bodies, recurs with Gravedigger and Soul Salvage (gp).
- **Scholar of the Ages** — engine card for blue-black; loops Disfigure + Murder with Anticipate. Ben notes he took it knowing he'd "like to play" it even if blue doesn't materialize (gp). Warrants 18 lands.
- **Fathom Fleet Cutthroat** — functional; "like a Sage's Row Denizen" but different trigger (gp).
- **Villainous Wealth (?)** / Villus, Broker of Blood (?) — Ethan sees it, considers taking it, passes, immediately regrets it (gp).

### Red

- **Cavalier of Flame** — clear P1P1 bomb; 6/5 haste, ETB discards and draws, dies to deal X damage equal to lands (show1). Wants you to be heavy red.
- **Chandra's Regulator** — strong uncommon in a heavy red deck; Ben goes all-in when he opens it (show1). Weak if you're spread across colors.
- **Goblin Smuggler** — very high pick; makes a creature unblockable until end of turn. "I blame the discord for letting everyone know how good goblin smuggler is" (show1 Ben). Combos with Pack Mastiff, Audacious Thief, Fencing Ace.
- **Pack Mastiff** — Ben takes 5 copies across show1 draft; each one buffs all others, go-wide snowball with Goblin Smuggler. Synergy is "so good" (show1).
- **Lavakin Brawler** — Ben takes 3; synergizes with other elementals. "3/4 for 3 red" and gets a bonus power/toughness for each elemental you control (show1).
- **Ember Cat** — good 2-drop in red; helps cast Cavalier of Flame; synergizes as an elemental. Ben regrets not having more of these vs. Pack Mastiffs (show1).
- **Scampering Scorcher** — excellent with triple Lavakin Brawler; haste, makes hasty tokens, all elementals (show1). Ben takes it and is happy.
- **Infuriate** — "very good 1-mana combat trick, will almost always make the cut in my red decks" (show1 Ben). Multiple copies desirable, especially with Season of Growth.
- **Shock** — premium common; seen very late in show1, suggesting red was contested. First-pick quality if the red seat is open.
- **Chandra's Outrage** — one of the top 3 red commons alongside Shock and Lavakin Brawler; Ben notes he never got one despite being deep in red (show1).
- **Flame Sweep** — sideboard card in aggro decks; Ben trusts a tweet from Venelius (?) saying always run it, then concedes it kills his own two-drops and cuts it to the side (show1). Bring in vs. go-wide opponents.
- **Tectonic Rift** — situational closer; playable in desperate situations (show1 Ben takes it late).
- **Rip-Scale Predator (?)** — good common finisher in mono-red; "one of the better common finishers" (show1 Ben). Takes three copies.
- **Fencing Ace** — "awesome with smuggler" — double strike on an unblockable creature is a game-ender (show1 Ethan).
- **Fire Elemental** — "not good but not awful"; synergizes with Lavakin Brawler but expensive (show1).
- **Goblin Bird-Grabber** — filler flier; both hosts are reluctant to play it (show1).

### Green

- **Rabid Bite** — Ben calls this his number-one common in the format at one point, above Leaf Kin Druid (show1). Works excellently with Season of Growth. Efficient removal that scales up with big creatures.
- **Leaf Kin Druid (?)** / Leafkin Druid — high pick in green; Ben says Rabid Bite is better than it because Cavalier of Flame wants heavy red while Leafkin wants heavy green, creating tension (show1).
- **Season of Growth** — strong synergy engine in red-green; combos with Rabid Bite and Infuriate to draw cards. Ben notes it's better in his deck than Ember Cat would have been (show1).
- **Woodland Champion** — fine to medium; both hosts mention it but don't take it early (show1, gp).
- **Brightwood Tracker** — mana-sink that provides card advantage; Ben cuts it to run more mountains for Chandra's Regulator (show1).
- **Net-Caster Spider** — mentioned multiple times as a reasonable consideration but rarely taken (show1).
- **Thrashing Brontodon** — taken late as a hate/value card; strong artifact/enchantment answer (show1 Ethan).
- **Silverback Shaman** — Ben P1P1s a draft at GP Vegas with it from the blue perspective; solid rare/uncommon (gp).
- **Overgrown Elemental** — Ethan passes it at GP Vegas in favor of Shared Summons; considered a second-tier pick (gp).
- **Mammoth Spider** — playable; both hosts mention it as a consideration but filler (gp).

### Multicolor / Gold

- **Empyrean Eagle (Blue-White)** — "windmill slam" uncommon; pushes you hard into blue-white and enables multiple Griffin Sentinels (gp). Very high pick.

- **Manifold Key** — "does a reasonable Goblin Smuggler impression if you don't have one" (show1 Ben); Ben cuts it after deciding Goblin Smuggler's evasion is redundant. Playable as unblockable enabler.
- **Ancestral Blade** — see White above.

### Lands

- **Evolving Wilds** — very high priority for two-color decks; fixing for splashes. Ben notes five green sources (one Evolving Wilds + four Forests) is enough for three green cards (show1).
- **Swiftwater Cliffs / Dismal Backwater** — functional tapped duals; take them when committed to the two-color pair (gp).

### Rares & Bombs

- **Cavalier of Flame** — clear P1P1; rewards heavy red; discussed extensively (show1).
- **Chandra's Regulator** — "quite good" if you're deep red; trash if you're splashing (show1).
- **Empyrean Eagle** — see Multicolor. Forces you into blue-white (gp).
- **Scholar of the Ages** — engine rare for blue-black grindy decks; Ethan builds around him (gp). With Disfigure + Murder + Soul Salvage = near-infinite value.
- **Field of the Dead** — Ethan considers and immediately discards it as "not about that life" even at P1P6 (gp).

- **Rotting Regisaur** — rated B uncommon; Ethan oscillates on it (show1).

## Signals & seat reads

- **If red removal (Shock, Chandra's Outrage) flows late**, red is open — commit hard. If you see none, be cautious: you may be fighting another red drafter without knowing it (show1 Ben's exact situation).
- **Raise the Alarm flowing late is a signal white is open.** Ethan deliberately passes it to cut Ben while taking it himself to bait Ben into green; if two come around to you early, white may be underdrafted (show1 Ethan).
- **Inspired Charge wheeling = your lane is clear.** Both copies wheeled in the GP Vegas draft, confirming Ben was the only go-wide white drafter (gp).
- **Frost Lynx flowing** might indicate blue is underpopulated; late Frost Lynx confused Ben about Ethan's color during the show1 draft (show1).
- **If you see late Goblin Smuggler / Pack Mastiff**, red-aggro is open. These are high picks that shouldn't wheel.
- **Pack 2 pivot:** the hosts both demonstrate late pack 2 pivots (Ethan into white, show1). If you see late powerful cards in an adjacent color in pack 2, pivoting is viable if your two-drop count is adequate. Don't over-commit before pack 2 signals confirm your lane.
- **Muyoyan Lang (?) / Moat Piranhas (?)** — Ethan mentions this as a card he wants and never sees. If the blue mill/control cards are flowing, blue may be open for that archetype.

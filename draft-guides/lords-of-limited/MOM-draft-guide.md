## TL;DR meta read

- **Best color pair is blue-black.** Ethan explicitly states it is "the best color pair in the set" in the Day 2 retrospective and builds there multiple times when the seat is open.
- **Red-black sacrifice is a second-tier powerhouse.** The early drafts showcase it heavily: cheap sacrifice outlets (Jury, Pyretic Prankster, Furnace Gremlin), payoffs (Stormclaw Rager, Marauding Dreadship), and removal (Final Flourish, Vanquish the Weak). The deck is lean, mean, and curves out well.
- **Green-blue incubate is the third big archetype.** Glistening Dawn, Tangled Skyline, Storm the Seed Core, and incubate enchantments go wide with big tokens.
- **Battles are central to the format, not optional.** Every deck should want some. The front side is the value you draft for; the flip side is upside. Pressure your opponent's battles aggressively — creatures that can attack and block (dual-threat bodies) are significantly better.
- **Incubate tokens are everywhere.** Flip costs are cheap if you have fixing; incubate-two or -three tokens become 2/2s or 3/3s that power convoke payoffs and sacrifice fodder.
- **Companions are highly underrated.** Ethan is an advocate: post-nerf companions are like "starting with a divination for a specific card." Gigantha, Lutri, and Yorian are the top picks. Do not skip on lands when companioning (18 is the floor; 19 for Karumonë/Yorian).
- **Curve low; prioritize good two-drops.** Both early and late episodes emphasize cheap bodies, especially ones that can later become sacrifice fodder or convoke material. Cards like Ichor Drinker (one-mana lifelink → incubate 2) and Nazumi Informant are repeatedly snapped up early.
- **Removal is always premium.** Deadly Derision, Final Flourish, Vanquish the Weak, and Cosmic Hunger are prioritized high. Conditional removal (Assimilate Essence) is generally too slow.

## Format principles

- **Draft a good two-drop almost every time you can.** Cheap bodies are fuel for convoke (Thunderhead Squadron, Meeting of Minds, Zephyr Singer), sacrifice (Jury, Stormclaw Rager), and backup counters. Ichor Drinker (one black, 1/1 lifelink → exile for incubate 2) is elite. Nazumi Informant (two-mana ravenous-rats effect) is a build-around for Broodlord decks.
- **Prioritize creatures that can apply pressure AND block.** The format is two-directional. Ethan flags explicitly that "creatures that can play offense and defense are going to be quite good" — highlighted by Onakke Javelineer (?) (5/4 reach, deals damage to battles). Pure attackers that can't block are punished.
- **Battles are core cards, not bonus cards.** Draft them with the understanding that you need to pressure them to get value. Some battles double as cheap interactive spells (Invasion of Mercadia / Invasion of Asgold). The back sides range from good to busted.
- **Unsealed Necropolis is a card every black deck wants one of.** Two-mana instant that returns up to two creatures from graveyard to hand is "big game at instant speed." Take it comfortably over filler in any black deck.
- **Overgrown Pest is one of the best commons.** Three-mana 2/2 that on ETB looks at the top five cards and can put a land or double-faced card into your hand — that is a land-fetch AND a DFC-fetch, giving you incubate token flips and battle flips. "Probably the best green common and one of the best commons in the set."
- **Marauding Dreadship is a sleeper.** Three-mana 4/1 haste vehicle (crew 2) that incubates 2 on ETB. You get a 4/1 that attacks immediately AND a 2/2 token, for three mana. "I think dreadship is low-key insane."
- **Convoke is a real archetype incentive, not just flavor.** Meeting of Minds (4-mana draw 2 with convoke) is excellent in creature-heavy decks. Zephyr Singer (4-mana 3/4 flying vigilance convoke, gives fliers to things that convoke it) is a bomb. Even medium convoke spells enable explosive turns.
- **Companion mechanics — always evaluate the paw print.** Post-nerf companions cost 3 mana to put into hand (a three-mana divination for a specific card). This is underrated — Ethan is emphatic. Key tips:
  - **Never skip on lands when companioning.** 18 minimum; 19+ for Karumonë or Yorian.
  - **Gigantha** (no card has more than one of same mana symbol) is the easiest to companion in this set because most commons and battles are single-pipped. Best reason to NOT companion is multiple double-pipped bombs.
  - **Lutri** (each non-land card has a different name) fits any blue or red deck. Take it early and adjust pick order to avoid duplicates.
  - **Yorian** (60-card deck): best when found early. Need roughly 11 playables per pack. Green base for fixing. 27–28 lands. Blink ETB creatures (battles, Overgrown Pest, incubate enchantments).
  - **Lurrus** (all permanents CMC ≤ 2): best in red-black sacrifice. Never skimp on lands. Misses out on most battles.
  - **Keruga** (all cards CMC ≥ 3): "Secret two-drops" are land cyclers. Base blue for Afaras Dispersal (?). Don't play card-draw spells alongside it.
  - **Umori** and **Zirda** are near-unplayable — battles are permanents without activated abilities (Zirda) or aren't creatures (Umori), so you lose too much of the format's power.
- **Removal priority:** Deadly Derision (two-mana instant destroy, makes treasure) > Final Flourish (two-mana -2/-2, kickable to -6/-6 with sac) > Vanquish the Weak (exile CMC 3 or less) > Cosmic Hunger (green bite) > Volcanic Spite (one-mana 3 damage to creature/battle + loot). Conditional counterspells like Assimilate Essence are too risky — usually too slow unless on the draw against aggro.
- **Fixing / splashing:** Single-pip land-cyclers (the red and white ones are best; double-pip blue/black/green are weaker) are reasonable to splash yourself. The rare-slot Invasion of Alara is a real splash target if you have Gigantha as companion and incidental fixing. Jungle Hollow / Thornwood Falls / Tranquil Cove are key dual-lands for three-color splashes.
- **Mana count:** 17 lands for aggressive sacrifice decks with mountain cyclers as the 17th. 18 for midrange. Companion decks 18–19.
- **Phyrexian double-faced creatures** (one-pip cost, flip into something when certain conditions are met) are excellent — they're DFCs that Overgrown Pest can fetch, they trigger incubate-enchantment draw, and they're cheap enough for Lurrus.
- **Don't overrate Stasis Field** ("base power and toughness 0/2, defender, loses all abilities — especially in a world of backup, that's so bad").
- **Seal effects and "etched host doombringer" style drains** are consistently underrated. The "gain 2, drain 2" on a black five-drop (Etched Host Doombringer (?), 3/5 that drains and manipulates battles) is expected to over-perform.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Blue-black is the best pair; expect competition.** If you're passed an Ichor Drinker, Nazumi Informant, or Skyclave Aerialist, that's a signal that blue-black may be open. If these dry up fast, pivot.
- **Red-black is the second-best seat and likely to be undervalued** by players unfamiliar with the sacrifice synergies. If you see late Pyretic Pranksters, Stormclaw Ragers, or Marauding Dreadships, you're probably in an open lane.
- **Green-blue tends to dry up.** Glistening Dawn and Tangled Skyline are the two key uncommons — if you miss both, the archetype loses a lot. Overgrown Pest will signal green openness if it is still on the table late.
- **What wheels:** the early draft notes show Pyretic Prankster wheeling multiple times (people undervalue it), Marauding Dreadship going late (sleeper), and land cyclers going very late. Convoke uncommons like Branch Burner and Artistic Refusal (?)(the "over-costed" ones) tend to wheel.
- **Pivot advice:** If you start in blue-black and it closes off, the most natural pivot is green-black (overlapping black core). Red-blue (convoke flyers + burn) is a secondary pivot if red is open. Don't force the incubate payoffs if you didn't get the enchantments — they're build-arounds that need critical mass.
- **Companions:** Grab the paw-print early if you see it, then adjust pick order. Gigantha is the easiest to companion (single-pip format). Lutri is free value if you're only seeing one-ofs anyway. Yorian requires a hard commitment in pack 1.

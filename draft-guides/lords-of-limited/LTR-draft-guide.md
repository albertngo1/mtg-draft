## TL;DR meta read

- **Slower, grindier format than early impressions suggested.** Control and card-advantage-dense decks overperformed aggro. The correct framing is "avoiding the aggro trap" — extending the game is rewarded. You can genuinely run out of gas if you do not build carefully.
- **Grixis (blue/black/red) is the best color tranche.** The data initially pointed toward Mardu being best; the retrospective confirmed Grixis was correct all along, especially among top drafters. Blue is underappreciated and deep; black is the best single color by a large margin.
- **Color power ranking (retro, decisive): Black > Blue > Red > White > Green.** Green is fully playable under the right conditions (multi-color Legends deck) but is the worst standalone color.
- **Ring tempting is the central mechanic.** Getting to level two (the loot step) is the most important threshold. You need 6–9 ring-tempting cards in your deck for it to matter consistently. Decks that ring-tempt outperform decks that don't in late-game parity situations. This mechanic benefits both aggro (attackers get skulk/unblockable) and control (built-in finisher and hand-selection).
- **Best archetypes (retro): Blue-Red #1, Red-Black #2 (when uncontested), Blue-Black #3, Blue-White #4.** Red-White aggro is viable but risky and gets worse in best-of-three.
- **Card advantage is scarce.** Unlike recent sets, you can run out of cards if you do not include ring-tempting and two-for-one effects. Lorien Revealed, Arwen's Gift, and The Bath Song are the premium card-draw cards; Nasty End (?) is critical for non-blue-black decks.
- **Removal is solid.** Smite the Deathless (red, kill anything), Fear Fire Foes / Eomer (red uncommon two-for-one), Claim the Precious (black, efficient), Bitter Downfall (black instant) are the premier answers. Aura-based removal is unplayable.
- **Land count: 17 for most decks, 15–16 acceptable in green decks with Many Partings + land cyclers.** Two land cyclers ≈ one land for deck-building purposes.

## Format principles

**Ring-tempting:**
- Get 6–9 ring-tempting cards in your deck. This is not optional for competitive records.
- Level two (loot every combat) is the primary threshold to reach; level four (ring-bearer deals 4 to face each hit) is the finisher.
- Multi-tempting cards like Bilbo Retired Burglar (tempts twice) and Frodo Baggins (tempts on each legend entering) are disproportionately powerful.
- You can turbo ring-tempt with two copies of Samwise the Stouthearted — the legend rule lets you recur the same Samwise repeatedly, jumping multiple levels in one turn.
- Gollum Patient Plotter + Haunt of the Dead Marshes creates a recur engine that also ring-tempts repeatedly.
- Cards that say "the ring tempts you" bury that text in sagas and chapters — don't miss it.

**Card advantage:**
- This format has very few two-for-ones at common/uncommon. Do not assume you will not run out of cards; build proactively for it.
- Blue's draw spells (Lorien Revealed, Arwen's Gift, Bath Song) are the premium sources. Non-blue-black decks need Nasty End (?) (the black sac-a-creature/draw-2-or-3 spell) to not run out of gas.
- Quarrel's End (?) (red discard-a-card, draw-2, make a 1/1) is critical for red decks without blue access.
- Ring-tempt level two gives effective card selection (looting) but not card advantage. You still need real card draw to stay ahead on resources.

**Removal:**
- Aura-based removal is **unplayable** main deck (Bewitching Leechcraft, Fog at the Barrow-Downs, the white aura). Do not play these; they get blown out easily. Sideboard only in best-of-three.
- Smite the Deathless is the best red common, dealing six with ring-tempt.
- Fear Fire Foes is Plague Wind; best uncommon removal.
- Black Breath (the minus-1/-1 to all opponent creatures sorcery) is a silver-bullet anti-wide card; run one copy in all black decks. The ring-tempt tacked on makes it maindeckable even when not maximally relevant.
- Claim the Precious is excellent black removal; however it is contested and you should be prepared to not get it.
- Do NOT play Mordor Trebuchet. It is unplayable. Do not build around it.

**Mana base and land cyclers:**
- Standard land count is 17. Green decks can get to 14–15 with Many Partings and two or more land cyclers.
- Two land cyclers roughly equal one land. Three-four cyclers in green decks enable land counts of 12–13.
- Land cycler power ranking (retro): Lorien Revealed #1, Generous Ent #2, Troll of Khazad-Dum #3, Eagles of the North #4, Oliphaunt #5.
- Lorien Revealed is almost always a spell you want to cast (draw three) not just cycle — budget mana to cast it when possible.
- Oliphaunt is usually just a tapped mountain. Do not seriously expect to cast it.
- 17 lands + land cyclers is the correct default for control blue decks that need every land drop.

**Curve and body sizing:**
- 1/3s have never been better; 2/1s have never been worse. The format rewards defensive bodies that also attack well later when ring-tempted.
- Pelargir Survivor (one-mana blue 1/3, taps for colored mana for spells, mills opponent for six mana) is the Golden Egg Award winner — most underrated common in the set.
- The four-drop slot is extremely congested. Do not thoughtlessly draft every good four-drop; prioritize curve diversity.
- Relentless Rohirrim (4/3 that tempts on ETB) was wildly underrated going into the format; it is a real card worth multiple copies.
- X/1 creatures are consistently bad because the format is full of Smite the Deathless and other ping effects. Avoid outside the dedicated red-white aggro deck.

**Signals and priorities:**
- Black is deeply contested; do not get attached to it unless you are getting clear signals of openness OR you open a high-tier rare (Orcish Bowmasters, Witch-King of Angmar).
- Blue is undervalued by most of the table; it goes deeper than black and you can wheel key blue pieces more reliably.
- Green signals: the on-ramp for green is Many Partings / Wose's Pathfinder in picks 4–8. If those do not wheel or show up in your packs, do not force green.
- Starting a draft with black and then pivoting into Blue-Red is completely valid when black is cut. Blue-Red benefits from early red cards that keep the pivot seamless.

**Sideboarding (best-of-three):**
- Black Breath in the sideboard is critical against wide strategies.
- Cast Into the Fire (deal 1 to each of up to two creatures, or exile a target artifact) handles artifacts and one-toughness creatures; run in sideboard.
- Westfold Rider (one mana, destroys an artifact or enchantment) is a key sideboard piece for artifact/enchantment interaction.
- Fog at the Barrow-Downs and Bewitching Leechcraft are situationally useful against specific threats (Bilbo, aura decks) in games two and three.
- Red-White aggro gets significantly worse in best-of-three as opponents bring in food and life gain.

## Card notes (community consensus)

Dedupe across episodes; retro takes win where conflicts exist. (?) marks names uncertain due to caption garble.

### White
- [**Errand-Rider of Gondor (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Errand-Rider%20of%20Gondor%20%28%3F%29) — Best white common. Three-mana 3/2 that lets you loot (discard/draw) and draws a second card if you control a legendary. Essential to white decks of all types: aggro, control, midrange. If this card is not good for you, you are building wrong.
- [**Protector of Gondor (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Protector%20of%20Gondor%20%28%3F%29) — Solid four-drop 3/3 that makes a 1/1 token. Good filler; fills the curve.
- [**Banish from Ederas (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Banish%20from%20Ederas%20%28%3F%29) — Overperformer award. A five-mana sorcery exile spell that costs two less if the target is tapped. Went from "never play this" to "happy to run two copies."
- [**Reprieve**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Reprieve) — One-mana instant that bounces a spell and draws a card. Nathan Sawyer ran three copies at the PT; Ben/Ethan prefer Relentless Rohirrim over it for ring-tempting, but it has real applications particularly in white-heavy controlling builds.
- [**Faramir Field Commander (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Faramir%20Field%20Commander%20%28%3F%29) — White uncommon. Three-mana 3/3 legend; when the ring tempts you (and you name another creature the bearer), you make a 1/1 human; at your end step if a creature died you draw a card. Extraordinary in black-white.
- [**Stalwart of Osgiliath (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Stalwart%20of%20Osgiliath%20%28%3F%29) — Five-mana 4/3 that ring-tempts on ETB, then grows with a +1/+1 counter whenever you draw your second card each turn. Was thought unplayable; turns out it's a 5/4 the turn you cast it if you have any ring-temping already. Run happily in decks with 8-9 ring-tempt sources.
- [**Bill the Pony (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bill%20the%20Pony%20%28%3F%29) — Three-mana legendary horse with interesting toughness-deals-damage ability. Good in legends decks; makes food tokens.
- [**Second Breakfast (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Second%20Breakfast%20%28%3F%29) — White trick that gets better in best-of-three pod play; opponents are not expecting it on Arena.

### Blue
- [**Birthday Escape**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Birthday%20Escape) — Best blue common. One-mana blue sorcery: draw a card, the ring tempts you. Efficient ring-tempting plus cantrip. Ben was early on this card and got vindicated by the retro.
- [**Lorien Revealed**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Lorien%20Revealed) — One-mana Island cycling, or five mana draw three. One of the best spells in the set. Always counts as a spell for graveyard-counting (Gandalf's Sanction). Cycle it early, cast it when you need cards.
- [**Pelargir Survivor (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Pelargir%20Survivor%20%28%3F%29) — Golden Egg Award winner. One-blue, 1/3, taps for colored mana when you cast spells, and at six mana you can tap to mill the opponent for three. Incredible ring bearer, great defensive body, win condition in long games, and helps splash off-color removal. Most underrated common.
- [**Saruman's Trickery**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Saruman%27s%20Trickery) — One-blue-blue instant: counter target spell, amass orcs one (make a 1/1 that combines into an army token). Mystic Snake analogue. #1 uncommon by GIH win rate among top users (retro confirms). Gets board presence while countering; important control piece.
- [**Arwen's Gift (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Arwen%27s%20Gift%20%28%3F%29) — Three-mana scry-2 draw-2 (costs one less with two legends). Excellent in all blue decks.
- [**Bath Song**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bath%20Song) — Ben's pet card; two-mana enchantment saga. Chapter one: draw-2-discard-1 on ETB. Chapter two: same. Chapter three: draw card for each creature your opponent controls (blue blue activation). Play it as early as safely possible. Doubles up with Samwise and Revive the Shire for a mill-opponent loop. Retro verdict: one of the best uncommons in the set.
- [**Bilbo Retired Burglar (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bilbo%20Retired%20Burglar%20%28%3F%29) — One-blue-red uncommon. Ring-tempts twice (on ETB and leaves play). Creates a Treasure when it dies if it's your ring bearer. Must-kill threat that punishes the kill with ring advancement. Ethan's #3 uncommon (behind Eomer and Fear Fire Foes).
- [**Gandalf's Sanction (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gandalf%27s%20Sanction%20%28%3F%29) — One-blue-red sorcery. Deals damage to a creature equal to the number of instants and sorceries in your graveyard; excess damage goes to the opponent. Zenith Flare variant; the finisher for blue-red spells decks. Building around it raises the value of all instant/sorcery spells including Lorien Revealed cycles.
- [**Fiery Inscription**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fiery%20Inscription) — Two-red enchantment. When it enters, ring tempts you. Whenever you cast an instant or sorcery, deal two damage to the opponent. Inscripton + any spell = two free damage every turn. Strong build-around; Ben ranks it ahead of Gandalf's Sanction for the PT.
- [**Meneldor Swift Savior (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Meneldor%20Swift%20Savior%20%28%3F%29) — Three-mana 3/3 flying legendary. When it deals combat damage to an opponent you can blink a creature you control. Generates repeated value; carried some games that had no business being won.
- [**Stern Scolding**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Stern%20Scolding) — One-mana blue counter targeting power-1-or-less creatures. Narrow but powerful in the right build.
- [**Council's Deliberation (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Council%27s%20Deliberation%20%28%3F%29) — One-blue draw a card; when it's in your graveyard and you scry you can cast it for free. Good value in scry-heavy builds.
- [**Gray Havens Navigator (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gray%20Havens%20Navigator%20%28%3F%29) — Two-mana 3/2 with flash, scry 1 on ETB. Ben is higher on this than Ethan.

### Black
- [**Dunland Crebain (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Dunland%20Crebain%20%28%3F%29) — Best black common. Two-mana 1/1 flyer; ETB amass orcs one (combine the 1/1 into an army token, making a 2/2). A two-for-one that pressures and flies. Ben's #1 common overall; Ethan's top-three. Safe pick that keeps you flexible.
- [**Claim the Precious**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Claim%20the%20Precious) — Efficient black removal that destroys a creature. Two-black instant (check exact details). Contested; does not always come to you.
- [**Bitter Downfall**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bitter%20Downfall) — Black instant removal. Premium; always take it when it arrives.
- [**Lash of the Balrog**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Lash%20of%20the%20Balrog) — One-black instant: sac a creature or pay four to destroy target creature. Efficient flexible removal.
- [**Nasty End (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Nasty%20End%20%28%3F%29) — One-black instant: sac a creature as additional cost; draw two cards (draw three if the creature was legendary). Critical in any black deck NOT paired with blue. Black's way to stay in the game on cards. One or two copies is ideal; run it.
- [**Torment of Gollum**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Torment%20of%20Gollum) — Three-mana black sorcery: look at opponent's hand, they discard a card, amass two. Good four-drop that clogs a very competitive slot. Excellent but there are so many competing four-drops in black.
- [**Black Breath**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Black%20Breath) — Two-black sorcery: creatures your opponent controls get -1/-1 until end of turn, ring tempts you. Silver-bullet vs wide strategies. Run one copy in all black decks; the ring-tempt on a situational spell makes it maindeckable.
- [**Gollum's Bite**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gollum%27s%20Bite) — Small black removal spell at uncommon. Top-five black uncommon.
- [**Haunt of the Dead Marshes (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Haunt%20of%20the%20Dead%20Marshes%20%28%3F%29) — One-black 1/1: ETB scry one, and you can pay two-black to return it from the graveyard tapped (requires controlling a legend). Excellent with Gollum Patient Plotter. Appears in all black decks.
- [**Nazgul**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Nazgul) — Two-black uncommon 1/2 with deathtouch; the ring tempts you on ETB and it gets +1/+1 counters whenever the ring tempts you. #1 black uncommon (retro). Multiple copies can be present in a deck.
- [**Gollum Patient Plotter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gollum%20Patient%20Plotter) — Black uncommon. ETB creates value, has a sacrifice engine with ring-tempt. #2 black uncommon. Engines extremely well with Haunt of the Dead Marshes.
- [**Voracious Fellbeast (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Voracious%20Fellbeast%20%28%3F%29) — Uncommon six-mana flyer; opponent sacrifices something on ETB, you get a food token if they did. #3 black uncommon.
- [**Gothmog Morgul Lieutenant (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gothmog%20Morgul%20Lieutenant%20%28%3F%29) — Three-black 3/3 legendary: ETB amass one, and all creature tokens you control have deathtouch. #5 black uncommon (vs Ben who preferred it; Ethan slightly prefers Bitter Downfall). IMPORTANT: this gives ALL tokens deathtouch — your rally 1/1 humans, your 1/1 spirits from Oath of the Grey Host, etc. Kill on sight.
- [**Easterling Vanguard (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Easterling%20Vanguard%20%28%3F%29) — One-black 2/1 that amasses orcs when it dies. Good two-drop for black aggressive or midrange builds.
- [**Sam's Desperate Rescue**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Sam%27s%20Desperate%20Rescue) — One-black sorcery: return target creature from graveyard to hand, ring tempts you. Always want one copy; two copies good in decks with land-cycler creatures to enable early ring-tempting even without a creature on board.

### Red
- [**Rally at the Hornburg**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rally%20at%20the%20Hornburg) — Best red common. Two-mana instant: make two 1/1 white human soldier tokens with haste. Goes in every red deck; powers up Book of Mazarbul and Erkenbrand. Engine card for red-white.
- [**Smite the Deathless**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Smite%20the%20Deathless) — Best red removal common. Deals damage to any target (six with ring-tempt). First-pickable in isolation. Flexible enough to play in any red deck regardless of style.
- [**Rangers Firebrand (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rangers%20Firebrand%20%28%3F%29) — One-red sorcery: deal two to any target, ring tempts you. Premium one-mana removal + ring-tempting. Outstanding in multiples.
- [**Goblin Fire Leaper (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Goblin%20Fire%20Leaper%20%28%3F%29) — One-red 1/1: one-and-red gives it +1/+7 until end of turn, and when it dies it deals damage equal to its power to a creature. Good aggressive one-drop.
- [**Rohirrim Lancer (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rohirrim%20Lancer%20%28%3F%29) — One-mana 1/1 with menace; when it dies the ring tempts you. Underrated aggressive one-drop that also enables ring-tempt insurance. Excellent in red-white.
- [**Relentless Rohirrim (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Relentless%20Rohirrim%20%28%3F%29) — Three-mana 4/3 that ring-tempts on ETB. Was expected to be filler; turned out to be a real card worth running multiple copies of. Gets your ring level up immediately.
- [**Quarrel's End (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Quarrel%27s%20End%20%28%3F%29) — Two-red sorcery: discard a card, draw two, make a 1/1 human. Critical anti-flood card for red decks without blue. Run it.
- [**Foray of Orcs (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Foray%20of%20Orcs%20%28%3F%29) — Three-red: amass orcs two, then deal damage equal to the amass army's power to a creature. Flame Tongue Kavu variant. Excellent in red-black or any controlling red deck.
- [**Improvised Club (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Improvised%20Club%20%28%3F%29) — One-red instant: sac an artifact or creature as additional cost; deal four to any target. Excellent in decks with sacrifice fodder (tokens from rally, rohirrim lancers, etc.).
- [**Erkenbrand Lord of Westfold (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Erkenbrand%20Lord%20of%20Westfold%20%28%3F%29) — Three-red uncommon 3/3 legend: whenever a human enters the battlefield under your control, your team gets +1/+0 until end of turn. Combos explosively with rally at the hornburg, creates huge swings. Best in red-white.
- [**Eomer of the Riddermark**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Eomer%20of%20the%20Riddermark) — Top uncommon in the format (retro consensus). Five-mana 5/4 haste that makes a 1/1 if it is the greatest power creature. Wins games on its own. Ben and Ethan both agree this is the best uncommon in the set, narrowly over Fear Fire Foes.
- [**Fear Fire Foes**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fear%20Fire%20Foes) — Red uncommon. Your creatures fight all of your opponent's creatures simultaneously; each opponent's creature must block if able. Plague Wind variant. Second best uncommon; best in large boards against wide opponents.
- [**Book of Mazarbul**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Book%20of%20Mazarbul) — Three-red uncommon enchantment saga. Amasses one, then two, then gives your team +1/+0 and menace. Go-wide payoff; central to red-white aggro. Ethan thinks it's broken early on.

- [**Gandalf's Sanction (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Gandalf%27s%20Sanction%20%28%3F%29) — See Blue section (blue-red signpost).

### Green
- [**Many Partings**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Many%20Partings) — Best green fixing common. One-green sorcery: search for a basic land, put it into your hand, make a food token. The backbone of multi-color green fixing. Cannot be interacted with (unlike Wose's Pathfinder). Ben's preferred green card.

## Signals and seat reads

- **Black being open:** If black commons (Dunland Crebain, Claim the Precious, Lash of the Balrog) are wheeling past pick five, black is open. This does not happen often at competitive tables; do not count on it. More commonly, one or two good black cards come and then it dries up by pick three or four.
- **Blue being open:** Blue goes deeper than black — Pelargir Survivor, Stern Scolding, Birthday Escape, even Lorien Revealed can wheel more often than expected, especially at Arena tables. Blue is the most consistently available color throughout a draft.
- **Red-White signals:** Rohirrim Lancer and Rally at the Hornburg wheeling (pick eight or later) are strong signals that red-white is open and you are essentially alone in the lane.
- **Green signals:** Many Partings and Wose's Pathfinder in picks four through eight are the on-ramp cards. If you see them, green may be open. If they are gone or don't show up, green is likely contested or simply not flowing to your seat.
- **When black is cut:** Do not anchor to black once it dries up. The correct pivot is usually into Blue-Red (best deck), using any early red cards as your bridge. If you started black and blue-red is also not flowing, find the most open single color and pair it with your black foundation.
- **Legend-deck signals:** Great Hall of the Citadel is the glue. If you see multiple Great Halls, the table is likely not fighting over the legends plan. Frodo Baggins appearing mid-pack (pick five or later) confirms green-white legends is wide open.
- **What wheels in this format:** Late-pack ring-tempting filler (Stalwart of Osgiliath, Rohirrim Lancer, even Easterling Vanguard) goes very late and can fill your deck without sacrificing early picks for them. Do not overdraft early picks for cards you can consistently pick up in picks 10–14.

## Supersessions (early-was-wrong, now corrected)

1. **Green is unplayable (Early Access, 2023-06-17) → Green is viable as a multi-color legends base (Mid-format, 2023-07-03 and retro, 2023-08-08).** Early Access: Ben was loudly dismissive of green ("green is terrible, I'm done playing green"). By the Extended Edition episode, Ethan developed the five-color Legends / Great Hall archetype around green-white, and Ben came around. Retro verdict: green should have at least one drafter per pod; the "stay away from green" advice evolved into "stay away from mushroom watchdog green" rather than green wholesale.

2. **Mardu is the best color tranche (early data / Early Access) → Grixis is the best color tranche (retro, 2023-08-08).** The 17Lands data early in the format pointed to Mardu (red-white-black) as the best performing colors. Ben was early in pushing back on this. The retro episode confirmed: "This is a Grixis format." The data among top drafters confirmed Grixis colors outperform; Mardu colors had a lower skill floor so they overperformed in aggregate.

3. **Palantir of Orthanc: Ethan initially skeptical → accepts it is strong (but disagrees with Ben on top tier).** Never became a full reversal; Ethan remained slightly lower on Palantir than Ben throughout the format's life.

4. **Blue-Red considered risky early → confirmed as the clear best archetype (retro).** Early episodes had blue-red as one of several viable top archetypes. By the PT prep episode (episode 332) and retro, it was unambiguously the #1 deck. Quote from the retro: "Blue-Red #1. Got to be number one."

## Live disagreements (genuine, unresolved)

- **Dunland Crebain vs Smite the Deathless as the #1 common overall:** Ben had Crebain at #1 (safe, flexible, always good); Ethan had Smite #1 (best fits the blue-red deck he wants to draft, works in every red deck). Never fully resolved; both are legitimate takes depending on preferred approach.
- **Saruman's Trickery vs other early picks:** Ben thought Saruman's Trickery was better as a first pick than Dunland Crebain in a vacuum (higher ceiling, blue); Ethan slightly preferred the stability of Crebain. Trickery turned out to be the #1 uncommon by GIH WR (retro), which vindicates Ethan's revised position, though neither strongly committed.
- **Bath Song ranking:** Ben considers it the best uncommon in the set and his favorite card in the format; the hosts never reached full agreement on exactly where it slots relative to Eomer and Fear Fire Foes. Ethan acknowledges it is excellent but does not have the same emotional attachment.
- **Horn of Gondor:** Ben takes it very highly (P1P1 over many black uncommons, in his top-two rares). Ethan thinks it is overrated and gets awkward in games where you can't activate it. Ben's enthusiasm for it was never fully endorsed by Ethan.

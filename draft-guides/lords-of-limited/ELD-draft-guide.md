## TL;DR meta read

- **Mono-color rewards are real and powerful.** ELD heavily incentivizes mono or near-mono builds via adamant (payoffs when you spend 3+ of one color), hybrid uncommons, and color-specific utility lands (Castle Embereth, Dwarven Mine, Witch's Cottage, etc.). Draft accordingly — slant hard to one color and treat the second color as a splash when possible.
- **Aggro/beatdown is the default axis.** Red-based aggro (especially mono red with Seven Dwarves, Embercleave, ferocity of the wilds, Redcap/Rimrock Night package) is the premier deck and came through most clearly in the flashback episode. White-red Knights is the closest second.
- **Best archetypes (flashback verdict):** Mono Red (Seven Dwarves shell) ≥ Red/Green Non-Humans (Grumgully) > Blue-based value (Emry, Midnight Clock, Dance of the Manse) > Red/White Knights > Green-based midrange (Trail of Crumbs, Keeper of Fables, Witch Stalker). All lanes are draftable; format is fairly balanced at common.
- **Adventures are the glue of the format.** Most two-for-ones come from adventure cards; they are the primary source of card advantage, activate Golden Egg / Curious Pair synergies, and interact with Innkeeper of the Vale, Edgewall Innkeeper (?), and Mysterious Pathlighter. Prioritize them freely in any archetype.
- **Key mechanics:** Adventures (universal value), Adamant (color rewards), Food (life gain / Trail / Wicked Wolf synergy), Escape (late-game recursion, conditional), Knights tribal (Equipment, Inspired Veteran, Rimrock Night, Burning-Yard Trainer), Seven Dwarves (linear synergy), Fires of Invention (build-around bomb).
- **Removal is premium and contested.** The top removal commons (Searing Barrage, Scorching Dragonfire, Bake into a Pie, Turn into a Pumpkin, Reave Soul, Outmuscle) pick early and wheel rarely. Giant Killer is the best white removal common. Have at least 2–3 pieces; black-red and pure red have the most removal density.
- **Curve matters a lot.** Two-drops are critical; the format can punish slow starts. Rimrock Knight, Curious Pair, Weaselback Redcap, Faerie Guidemother, and Gingerbread Cabin-style cheap plays set up turn-3 and turn-4 power.

## Format principles

**Commit to a color early, stay deep.**
ELD rewards players who lean mono-color or near-mono. Adamant, hybrid uncommons, and utility lands (Dwarven Mine, Castle Embereth, Witch's Cottage) all get materially worse in two-color decks. The hosts repeatedly noted mana bases of 11–7 or 12–5 as correct; "balanced" mana splits were suboptimal.

**Adventures first.**
Adventures are the primary card-advantage engine in the format. Most of the best two-for-ones come from adventure creatures (Rimrock Knight, Giant Killer, Order of Midnight, Beanstalk Giant, Curious Pair, Lovestruck Beast). Innkeeper of the Vale (?) triggers off adventures for more card draw. Pick adventures over vanilla creatures of equivalent power.

**Golden Egg is better than it looks, worse when you know what you're doing.**
Golden Egg was called out as overrated once players got more reps. In early drafts it was a top common; with experience it's a good fixer/cantrip in specific shells (Emry, Trail of Crumbs, adamant enabler, blue splashes) but a mediocre two-drop in aggro. Calibrate value to deck needs.

**Fires of Invention is a build-around, not a bomb.**
Fires rewards a specific construction: low-to-the-ground threats, plenty of card selection (Merchant of the Vale is the best partner), and a plan for the two-spell-per-turn cap. In a generic red deck it is an on-bow that hampers the aggro plan. Ben took it first pick in Showdown 1 and again in the flashback episode — both times the build-around failed to come together. It can be powerful (Showdown 3 Ethan built the blue shell around it with Midnight Clock and Dance of the Manse), but requires full commitment.

**Removal evaluation.**
- Top-tier (take early): Scorching Dragonfire, Searing Barrage, Bake into a Pie, Murderous Rider, Giant Killer, Turn into a Pumpkin, Reave Soul.
- Good (take freely): Glass Casket, Trapped in the Tower, Scalding Cauldron, Outmuscle, Doused in Doom (?), Epic Downfall.
- Situational: Joust (great with large creatures, mediocre in aggro), Fling (only in dedicated packages), Blow Your House Down (sideboard primarily).
- Slaying Fire was consistently rated above Scorching Dragonfire by Ethan: the adamant mode going face is relevant; the tiebreaker matters late.

**Trail of Crumbs requires a food density threshold.**
Trail of Crumbs is an engine card (revealed as top-tier mid-format), but it needs ~5+ food producers to function. Best food sources: Golden Egg, Witches' Oven + Gingerbread Cabin, Curious Pair, Tempting Witch, Bartered Cow. With two copies of Trail the deck generates real sustained advantage; with one Trail and few food sources it is a mediocre enchantment.

**18 lands is sometimes correct.**
Decks with Midnight Clock, Trail of Crumbs, Sorcerer's Broom, Witching Well, or other mana sinks often want 18 lands. Decks with aggressive curves (mono red, RW Knights) are fine at 17.

**Seven Dwarves — commit or skip.**
Three+ copies of Seven Dwarves is the threshold where the card becomes genuinely powerful (3/3 for 2, then 4/4 etc.). Below that it is a below-rate two-drop. In the flashback draft Ethan noted wheeling two copies as a signal that nobody else was drafting the package and expressed excitement about the deck's ceiling.

**Signpost scarecrows and the signpost cycle.**
The signpost uncommons (e.g., Henge Walker for mono-red-ish artifacts) are fine filler; the signpost scarecrows (the 2/4 vigilance colorless) are actually reasonable mana fixers for multicolor splashes and worth one copy in value decks.

## Card notes (community consensus)

Later/flashback takes override earlier ones. (?) = name reconstructed from garbled auto-captions; use with caution.

### White
- [**Giant Killer**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Giant%20Killer) — Best white common. One white for 1/2 with tap-a-big-creature adventure; the adventure and body are both good, crew gets rid of 4+ power threats permanently. P1-pickable. (multiple episodes)
- [**Inspiring Veteran**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Inspiring%20Veteran) — Best RW Knights uncommon; anthem for Knights is real. Slightly awkward to cast early (needs 2-color mana), but fine at 11/6+ mana splits. (Showdown 1, Showdown 3)
- [**Faerie Guidemother**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Faerie%20Guidemother) — Solid aggressive one-drop. Gives flying/+1/+0 as the adventure. Good in aggro, especially mono-red or RW. Goes late enough to pick up later than it deserves. (multiple)
- [**Mysterious Pathlighter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Mysterious%20Pathlighter) — 3/2 flyer for 2W that buffs adventuring creatures. "Below replacement" outside dedicated adventure decks per Ethan (Showdown 2). Floor is decent as a flyer. Requires 4+ adventures to justify taking early.
- [**Glass Casket**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Glass%20Casket) — Exile a creature CMC 3 or less. Clean removal; pairs with Emry. Premium in the artifact shell. (Showdown 3, flashback)
- [**Ardenvale Tactician**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Ardenvale%20Tactician) — Best white common by some measures. 2W for 2/3 vigilance (adventure: tap a creature). "Really really good" per Ben. Top common, confirmed multiple episodes. (Showdown 3)
- [**Trapped in the Tower**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Trapped%20in%20the%20Tower) — 1W enchantment that stops a creature from attacking/blocking. Excellent tempo/removal in aggro; better than it looks. Take early. (multiple episodes)
- [**Silver Flame Squire**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Silver%20Flame%20Squire) — White one-drop with adventure. Good with Youthful Knight for value; mostly filler but supports the curve. Good in multiples with Youthful Night.

### Blue
- [**Emry, Lurker of the Loch**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Emry%2C%20Lurker%20of%20the%20Loch) — Build-around bomb uncommon. Casts artifacts from the graveyard; curves perfectly with Golden Egg and Witching Well. Ben passed her to Ethan in Showdown 3 and Ethan crushed with the resulting deck. Worth taking around mid-pack and committing. (Showdown 3, flashback)
- [**Midnight Clock**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Midnight%20Clock) — ⚠ Both hosts "missed on it initially" — now rated very highly. Ramps mana, acts as a mana sink, and eventually refills hand or resets game. Called "ramps, it's a mana sink, it's a payoff all wrapped in one neat little package." (Showdown 3)
- [**Dance of the Manse**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Dance%20of%20the%20Manse) — Bomb payoff for artifact decks. Ethan built Showdown 3's deck around it. Cast for 5 or 6 it retrieves multiple artifacts with power/toughness equal to X. Combined with Emry and Midnight Clock, the deck was dominant. (Showdown 3)
- [**Tome Raider**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Tome%20Raider) — 1/1 for 1U adventure (pay 2U for Foreboding Fruit: draw a card, scry 2). Good with Fairy Vandal; provides card selection. "Vandal into Tome Raider is nice." (flashback)
- [**Fairy Vandal**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fairy%20Vandal) — Gets +1/+1 counter whenever you draw your second card in a turn. In a draw-heavy deck can quickly become a 4/4 or 5/5 wearing Embercleave. Very good in UR draw-two. (flashback)
- [**Witching Well**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Witching%20Well) — 1U artifact: pay 3U to draw 2. Good with Emry; extra copies help fire off cheaply once you have the mana. Fine in blue decks, specifically powers Fires of Invention. (flashback)
- [**Improbable Alliance**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Improbable%20Alliance) — Build-around that makes a 1/1 flyer whenever you draw your second card each turn. Late in Showdown 1 (Ethan splashed it); the UR deck with this and Pyromancer was noted as wide-open at the table. (Showdown 1)
- [**Merfolk Secret Keeper**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Merfolk%20Secret%20Keeper) — Mill-focused; generally filler outside mono-blue mill. Goes late. (multiple)
- [**Run Away Together**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Run%20Away%20Together) — Two-mana bounce two creatures. Useful tempo spell; specifically good with Runaway Steam-Kin (?) and Emry (bounces Emry back to recast). See also Showdown 3: Ben sideboarded it against aggro. (Showdown 3, flashback)
- [**Unexplained Visions / Into the Story**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Unexplained%20Visions%20/%20Into%20the%20Story) — Ben consistently rated Into the Story low ("markedly worse than" Drawn from Dreams equivalent). Unexplained Visions rated as "like urban evolution... draw four in most scenarios" by Ethan when it resolved. Value depends on opponent's graveyard count. (Showdown 2)

### Black
- [**Murderous Rider**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Murderous%20Rider) — Black premium removal spell with a body. 2BB instant destroy target creature or planeswalker (you lose 2 life); comes back as a 2/3 lifelink. Ethan's P1P1 in the flashback. (flashback)
- [**Order of Midnight**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Order%20of%20Midnight) — 2B 2/2 flyer with adventure (Alter Fate: return target creature from your graveyard). "Consistent bird" that "will be very strong in any deck." Ethan rates it highly. Flashback: P1P1 over Feasting Troll King. (Showdown 2, flashback)
- [**Reave Soul**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Reave%20Soul) — 1B sorcery destroy a creature with power 3 or less. Efficient conditional removal; bread-and-butter in black. (multiple)
- [**Reaper of Night**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Reaper%20of%20Night) — 3B 3/4; adventure (Harvest Fear: target opponent discards two cards). Good curve occupier, the discard adventure does real work. Ethan noted wanting exactly one copy, not multiples. (Showdown 2)
- [**Lost Legion**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Lost%20Legion) — 2B 2/3; look at top 3 cards of a library. Noted as a color-committing but good three-drop. Good with Wicked Guardian (3+ toughness creatures synergy). (Showdown 2)
- [**Wicked Guardian**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Wicked%20Guardian) — 3B draw-a-card ETB if you control another creature with 4+ toughness. Good with Lost Legion and Reaper of Night which are 3+ toughness. Noted in multiple episodes. (Showdown 2)
- [**Deathless Knight**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Deathless%20Knight) — 4BB 6/4 haste (returns from graveyard whenever you gain life). Great if you have consistent life gain (Resolute Rider, food, etc.); "deathless knight is best" in the dedicated black shell. (Showdown 2, flashback)
- [**Bake into a Pie**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bake%20into%20a%20Pie) — 3B instant: destroy target creature, create a food. Strong removal with food bonus for Wicked Wolf. Premium common. (multiple)
- [**Bitterblossomed/Barrow Witches (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bitterblossomed/Barrow%20Witches%20%28%3F%29) — 3B (Barrow Witches) return a Knight from your graveyard. Noted as combo with Order of Midnight; creates a loop. Worth one copy in any Knight-heavy black deck. (Showdown 2)
- [**Witches' Oven**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Witches%27%20Oven) — 1B artifact: sacrifice a creature, create a food. Good in Trail of Crumbs decks and with Cauldron Familiar for infinite loop (if both present). Rated mediocre without the combo. (multiple)
- [**Cauldron Familiar**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Cauldron%20Familiar) — 1B 1/1: on ETB drain 1 and you gain 1. When a food is sacrificed, you may return it from graveyard. Mentioned as a good common but not game-warping outside the Oven combo. (Showdown 2)
- [**Epic Downfall**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Epic%20Downfall) — 2B sorcery exile a creature CMC 3+. Great at hitting big threats that other removal misses. Splashable off eggs. (flashback)
- [**Savvy Hunter**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Savvy%20Hunter) — 3B 3/3 that makes a food on attacks and when it deals combat damage. Called "best card in the pack" by Ben when seen in pack 2. (Showdown 1)

### Red
- [**Embercleave**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Embercleave) — Legendary Equipment bomb rare. Flash, attaches for free to attacking creature, gives double strike + trample. "One of the biggest baddest bombs in the set." (flashback) Wears best on Fairy Vandal, large Witch Stalker, Wildwood Tracker, non-humans with counters.
- [**Bone Crusher Giant**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Bone%20Crusher%20Giant) — 3R 4/3 with adventure (Stomp: 2 damage that can't be prevented, target can't gain life this turn). Excellent rate; adventure is relevant. P1P1 quality. (flashback)
- [**Opportunistic Dragon**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Opportunistic%20Dragon) — 4R 4/3 flying that steals a non-land permanent when it enters (if opponent controls one). "Pretty clear first pick." (Showdown 3)
- [**Searing Barrage**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Searing%20Barrage) — 3R instant deal 5 damage to a creature. Premium common removal; frequently the best card available. (multiple)
- [**Scorching Dragonfire**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Scorching%20Dragonfire) — 2R instant deal 3 damage to target creature or planeswalker, exile it if it dies. Good common removal. Noted as slightly below Slaying Fire but still top-tier. (multiple)
- [**Slaying Fire**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Slaying%20Fire) — R instant deal 3 damage (or 6 with adamant); can go to face. Ethan rates above Scorching Dragonfire: "adamant mode going face" is real value late. (multiple)
- [**Rimrock Knight**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Rimrock%20Knight) — 1R 3/1 with adventure (Boulder Rush: +2/+0 to target). Both halves are good; the adventure enables Innkeeper synergies. "Card I have been impressed with quite a bit." Nonhuman for Grumgully. (multiple)
- [**Redcap Raiders**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Redcap%20Raiders) — 2R 3/2: when attacks, can tap another nonhuman creature you control to give Redcap Raiders +1/+1 and trample. Excellent in non-human aggro; note it requires a creature to tap. (multiple)
- [**Weaselback Redcap**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Weaselback%20Redcap) — R 1/1 haste; when another creature is sacrificed you may pay R to put it on top of library. "Very good in these red aggressive style decks... should not be going this late." (flashback)
- [**Raging Redcap**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Raging%20Redcap) — 1R 2/1 double strike. Pure aggro beater; fragile but punishing. Good with Grumgully (enters as 3/2 double strike). (multiple)
- [**Heraldic Banner**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Heraldic%20Banner) — 3 artifact; tap for colored mana; creatures of chosen color get +1/+0. Called "absolutely fantastic in mono-color decks, mono red specifically." (Showdown 1)
- [**Torbran, Thane of Red Fell**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Torbran%2C%20Thane%20of%20Red%20Fell) — 2RR 2/4; all red sources deal +2 damage. "Absurd in mono red." (flashback) Very late-game threat; synergizes with Brimstone Trebuchet (?).
- [**Ember Eth Paladin (Embereth Paladin?) (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Ember%20Eth%20Paladin%20%28Embereth%20Paladin%3F%29%20%28%3F%29) — 3R 4/1 haste, ETB with +1/+1 counter if adamanted. Both Ben and Ethan noted this card is rarely adamanted in non-mono builds; "four mana four one haste is not exciting" without adamant. Filler even in mono red unless you're 12+ mountains. (multiple)
- [**Ferocity of the Wilds**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Ferocity%20of%20the%20Wilds) — 3R enchantment; non-human creatures get +1/+0 and trample. "Very good in mono red, very good in red-green non-humans." Noted as a mid-format discovery; should be picked higher than it goes. (Showdown 3, flashback)
- [**Sundering Stroke**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Sundering%20Stroke) — 6R sorcery; deal 7 damage divided among 1–3 targets (deals 7 to each with adamant). "This card is powerful... I wonder how many people know how good this is — this was sort of a late discovery in the format." Worth one copy in mono red as a finisher. (flashback)
- [**Fires of Invention**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fires%20of%20Invention) — 2RR enchantment; can only play spells during your turn; may cast 2 spells per turn without paying mana equal to lands you control. Build-around; both hosts failed to assemble the deck multiple times. (multiple) Requires Merchant of the Vale as card selection to find it and fill dead turns. Synergizes with Witching Well / Golden Egg; does NOT play well with aggro creatures.

### Green
- [**Grumgully the Generous**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Grumgully%20the%20Generous) — 2RG 3/3 (gold uncommon); non-human creatures enter with an additional +1/+1 counter. The defining card of the RG archetype; changes the whole deck's calculus. Two copies is the ideal. (Showdown 2, flashback)
- [**Fierce Witch Stalker (?)**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Fierce%20Witch%20Stalker%20%28%3F%29) — GG 5/4 trample (?) (Fierce Witchstalker proper). "Powerful common... feared switch stalker." Very good green common, noted as near top-tier. Aggressive rate and trample punish opponents. (multiple)
- [**Wildwood Tracker**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Wildwood%20Tracker) — G 1/1; when another non-human creature ETBs, tap two target creatures. "Multiple Wildwood Trackers is a very big game for this deck [RG]." Good with Grumgully. (Showdown 2)
- [**Outmuscle**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Outmuscle) — 2G sorcery; put a +1/+1 counter on a creature, then fight another creature. "Best common" in green multiple times. Flexible removal + growth. (multiple)
- [**Keeper of Fables**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Keeper%20of%20Fables) — 3G 4/4; draw a card whenever a non-human you control deals combat damage to a player. Card advantage engine in non-human decks. "Very good." (multiple)
- [**Escape to the Wilds**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Escape%20to%20the%20Wilds) — 3RG sorcery; exile top 5, play until end of turn, plus one land. "Draw four in most scenarios" per Ben (comparing to Urban Evolution). Red-green bomb; confirmed as a real draw-4 effect in the format. (Showdown 2, flashback)
- [**Beanstalk Giant**](https://scryfall.com/search?q=Beanstalk%20Giant) — 5GG 5/5 creature; adventure Fertile Footsteps costs 3G and puts a basic land onto the battlefield. Both halves excellent; the adventure is played on 4 for ramp, the body comes down late. Big payoff for green decks. (Showdown 2)

## Signals & seat reads

- **Seven Dwarves going late** is the clearest signal you're the only Dwarves drafter at the table. In the flashback Ethan wheeled two and said: "must be we're the only seven dwarves drafter — that is good news." If you pick up a Dwarven Mine late, lean in aggressively.
- **Ferocity of the Wilds going late** signals no one is in red-green non-humans. It should not be going late but does when players misvalue it. "Mid-format discovery" per Ethan; pick it up as a signal.
- **Improbable Alliance + Pyromancer / UR draw-two going very late** signals no one is in the draw-two shell. In Showdown 1, Ethan saw these going late and noted only one blue-red drafter at the table; the deck appeared wide open.
- **All ember-paladin variants not wheeling** is a bad sign for mono red — it means red is contested upstream. Ben experienced this in Showdown 3 and noted it too late to pivot cleanly.
- **Green cards (Witch Stalker, Outmuscle) not present in early packs** signals either green is being picked clean upstream or packs are simply weak. Don't read too much from a single weak pack.
- **Pivoting off Fires of Invention:** If Merchant of the Vale isn't flowing and card selection is sparse, cut Fires and play your red cards. Ben was stuck with it multiple times when the support didn't come.
- **White aggro signals:** Ardenvale Tactician, Flutter Fox, and Inspiring Veteran going early means white aggro is contested. Path-lighter and Silver Flame Squire going late means white adventures is open but weaker.

## Supersessions (early-was-wrong, now corrected — NOT live disagreements)

- **Midnight Clock: undervalued → premium.** Early episodes: neither host flagged it as a build-around. By Showdown 3, both stated "we both missed on it... very very powerful, great in blue decks just like it ramps it's a mana sink it's a payoff." Reversal confirmed.
- **Ferocity of the Wilds: ignored → high pick.** Early episodes: neither Ben nor Ethan was prioritizing the enchantment. Flashback episode: Ben noted it should be picked "highly" in red-green decks and mono red, calling it a mid-format discovery. Both hosts took it highly in the flashback draft. Reversal confirmed.
- **Golden Egg: overrated → situational.** Ben stated directly in Showdown 1 that Golden Egg is "one of my best commons in the set." By Showdown 2 he clarified: "goes down in value once you know what you're doing a lot more." Calibrated downward.
- **Bog Naughty (?): looks busted → underperformer.** In the flashback episode Ethan explicitly stated this card "looks busted but did not play out as good as it looks most of the time." No explicit early episode over-rating captured, but the flashback correction is noted.
- **Tournament Grounds: sleeper → do not prioritize.** Ben took it in Showdown 1 thinking it would enable a Knight splash. In the flashback he explicitly said "tournament grounds is not a good card you should not be putting this in your deck." Reversal confirmed.

## Live disagreements (genuine, unresolved)

- **Into the Story vs. Unexplained Visions:** Ben rated Into the Story very low throughout ("markedly worse than" premium draw spells); Ethan in the flashback episode called a resolved Unexplained Visions "basically draw four" and was more positive about it. Both agree they're not auto-includes, but Ben is a harder no than Ethan.
- **Trail of Crumbs vs. Fierce Witch Stalker (P1P1 level decision):** In Showdown 2, when both were available around pick 7-ish, Ben chose Fierce Witch Stalker over Trail of Crumbs, saying "red green's plan definitely wants fierce witch stalker more." Ethan (also in Showdown 2 for his draft) took Trail higher. Both agree Trail is better with more reps and food support, but pick priority in the early format wasn't settled between them.
- **Curious Pair as adventure count payoff:** Ethan viewed it as good with Trail and Innkeeper synergies; Ben took it more as a filler adventure. Not a heated disagreement but different weighting in early format.

## Format speed / meta read

- **Slow format. ("The sword.")** All three episodes converge here, confirmed in the final ep. Games go long, board stalls are common, missing land drop 3 is "an auto-lose" (mulligan accordingly). Flooding hurts less than screwing.
- **Power level is "scrappy," not low.** Power comes from **synergy, not raw card quality**. "It's not that two-for-ones are back, it's that five-for-ones are gone." Commons make up the bulk of good decks; rares are mostly modest two-for-ones. Compared repeatedly to the Lords of Limited cube and Kaldheim/Kaladesh-Remastered-style "feel-it-out synergy."
- **The central tension:** you must be *doing something synergistic*, but you must NOT jam bad cards to do it. "Synergy doesn't mean making bad cards good — it means making good cards better, and choosing the right last 7–8 cards." The best synergy cards (Mr. Fantastic, Echo, H.E.R.B.I.E.) require *no work* — they're good cards that happen to combo.
- **Curves are atypical.** It is trivially easy to fill 3- and 4-drop slots (the 4-drop slot gets badly clogged with good cards) and **hard to fill the 2-drop slot**. Do NOT play bad filler two-drops — you "0-for-1 yourself." Land-cyclers count as your two-drop (cycle on 2, play a 3-drop on 3). Typical builds: 3–5 real two-drops + 2–3 land-cyclers; 16–17 lands; creature counts run high (17–18 common) because so many engines are creatures.
- **4 toughness / 4 mana value is THE magic number.** You need to attack through 4-toughness bodies and to kill them. This is why **Lightning Strike (deal 3) is underwhelming** — it misses the cards that matter.
- **Removal is scarce — prioritize it and hold it.** Spend high picks on interaction; the "catchall" removal (exile / destroy outright) is what matters. Patience with removal (waiting for the creature that matters) is a key play pattern. The black instants and white exile spells are the premium removal.
- **Heroes (white-leaning) vs. Villains (black-leaning).** White = heroes-matter, black = villains-matter, the other three colors mix. Doing the *hero* thing is easier and better-supported than the *villain* thing.
- **Draft navigation is back.** 10 archetypes, real signal-reading, you can get deep into a color and pick your second color late. This is the single most praised feature. Unlike recent guild sets, you are NOT on rails. **Draft with preferences again** — if you find an underrated package, you can re-draft it repeatedly.
- **Breaking board stalls:** flying and trample close games; resource-advantage engines (the uncommon lands, card-draw engines) win the long grind. Both paths are real.

## Card notes

Later episodes supersede earlier. **All takes are pre-data predictions.** `(?)` marks low-confidence readings. Every card name below matches the printed/Arena name in `cards_MSH.ndjson`.

### White

- **Agent 13, Sharon Carter** — Both hosts' #1 white uncommon. 2W 3/2; attack alone → investigate. Two-drop into Agent 13, attack, make a clue, opponent is far behind. Value engine, not purely aggressive.
- **Okoye, Dora Milaje Leader** — Army-in-a-can; 3W 3/2 that makes two 1/1 soldiers, attacking tokens get first strike. Audibled UP to a top-2 white uncommon in both the crash course and the final ep. "Point to you on this one" — wasn't on Ben's radar without Ethan.
- **White Widow, Free Agent** — 3W 2/3, choose: counters on up to two creatures, or return an artifact/enchantment from graveyard. Flexible two-for-one; Ben's #3 white uncommon.
- **Agent Maria Hill** — W 2/1; tapped to pay a teamwork cost → +1/+1 and draw. Looked broken in preview as a secret blue-white gold card; **cooled in the crash course** because teamwork spells are thin. Still good in a real teamwork deck.
- **Patriot, Shield Wielder** — 1W 2/2; 2,tap give another creature +2/+0 and hexproof. Flag-bearer style protection; "great" if you can reliably hold it up in a tricky blue-white shell.
- **Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.** — 2W 2/4; attack-alone creatures get +1/+1. Ethan's favorite sleeper — "basically a 2/4 exalted," good in multiples. The format wants you attacking with large single threats.
- **Hero in Training** — 2W 2/2; ETB draw a card, +2 life if you control another hero. "Absurd" common — a cantrip body that's a great counter-target.
- **Murdock's Crusade** — 1W sorcery, teamwork 4; exile a creature toughness 4+ (and/or exile an enchantment MV 4+). **Moved UP in the final ep** — unlike Lightning Strike it actually kills the enginey 4/5-drops that matter. Real removal.
- **Web Up** — 2W enchantment; O-Ring (exile a non-land permanent). The white catch-all removal; Ben specifically praised including it. Note: Guerrilla Gorilla can blow it up.
- **Take Up the Shield** — 1W instant; +1/+1 counter, lifelink + indestructible EOT. Ethan: "Biosynthetic Burst 2.0," likes it; Ben left it off his list. Quality hinges inversely on how good your hard removal is.
- **Super Villain Lockup** — 1W enchantment, flash; exile a *tapped* opponent's creature. Good (removal is good), but can never hit something that never attacks (e.g. Mr. Fantastic).
- **Helicarrier Strike** — W instant, teamwork 2; deal 2 (or 4 with teamwork) to an attacker/blocker. Ethan graded it ~0 (bad if white is purely aggressive); came around once mana-efficiency + "4 is the magic number" mattered. Improves white's curve.
- **Panther Pounce** — W instant; target player investigates, target creature +2/+0 (?) gains flying, untap it. "A lot for one mana" — can't get blown out (you keep the clue), and triggers token/artifact-ETB synergies. Sleeper.
- **Borough Backup** — 4W sorcery; make two 3/2 vigilance hero tokens, basic land cycling. Imperial-Oath-flavored; great with token payoffs and as a flood insurance / splash enabler.
- **Brave Brawler** — W 2/1 lifelink, power up 4W (+2 counters). Watch-list two-drop (Ari Lax / Mark Anderson liked it). Plays as a turn-2 lifelinker that later becomes a 4/3 problem.
- **Political Triumph** — W plan; creature ETB → scry 1 + plan counter; at 4 counters draw + put a +1/+1 on each creature. The least-bad plan; "nutter butters" on turn 1, terrible topdeck. Hosts remain wary of plans overall.
- **Captain Marvel, Earth's Protector** — Greater-or-garbage: **garbage** (preview). A 4W 4/4 flying vigilance Serra Angel with conditional flash — too many vanilla 4/5-drops with no ETB. Possible sleeper if it survives.
- **Crowd of True Believers** — W 1/2; tap an attack-alone creature for +1/+0 and 1 life. **Literal F — do not play it.** Named explicitly as unplayable.

### Blue

- **Justice, Vance Astrovik** — Both hosts' #1 blue uncommon. 1U 2/2 flyer; ETB bounce a non-land permanent, grows when your non-lands get bounced. "Incredibly pushed" — value mode (3/3 flyer) or tempo mode, plus +1/+1 synergy. The "year of mana wars continues."
- **Mister Fantastic, Reed Richards** — 2U 2/4 reach; whenever one or more tokens you control enter, draw. Final ep: "phenomenal," worth pivoting for, and crucially requires *no work* — it's just good when you draft good token-makers. Member of the Fantastic Four infinite combo (see below).
- **Falcon, Winged Wonder** — 3U 3/4 flyer; makes Redwing (1/1 flyer that surveils on attack). Pure good-stats two-for-one; "single-handedly won a game surveilling through a board stall." A top-3 blue uncommon for both by the final ep.
- **Echo, Perceptive Prodigy** — 2U 1/4 vigilance; 1,tap copy an activated/triggered ability from a creature you control. "You do NO work to make this good" — the ideal MSH card. Doubles Killmonger ETBs, etc. Build-around-adjacent but free.
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Deployment Drone** — 2U 2/2 flyer that brings a 1/1. "Bread and butter, just great" — a real two-for-one that's also an artifact and a token-maker. Every blue drafter takes it.
- **We Say Thee Nay!** — 1U instant; Quench (counter unless pay 2), teamwork 2 → pay 4 instead. A scalable counter that's a premium card; great with the clogged-4-drop turn (opponents tap out for their many good fours). Holds up alongside a land-cycler — "chef's kiss."
- **Trickster's Stratagem** — 3U sorcery; put an opponent's creature 2nd-from-top or on bottom, then your creature connives. Synergistic removal — triggers draw-two and counter payoffs. Sorcery speed is a downside but the card's strong enough.
- **Iron Lad, Diverging Destiny** — 2U 2/2 flying vigilance artifact; tap to draw if top card is an artifact. **Cooled in the crash course** as the hosts soured on artifacts; only good with ~15+ artifacts.
- **Atlantis Attacks** — 5UU sorcery, teamwork 4; make a 6/5 hexproof Leviathan and/or bounce 1–2 non-lands. "Sleeper great" — game-ending if cast; pitch it to connive when clunky. Sweet spot is one copy; a blue-green ramp target.
- **Frozen in Ice** — 2U aura; tap + lock + strip abilities from a creature. Sleeper removal — blue needs to get things dead, and *not* sending them to the graveyard is a bonus vs. graveyard decks. (Caveat: Web Up / exile is even better.)
- **Futurist Forge** — 1U artifact; ETB draw, then 3U sac → draw two. Card-advantage in installments; deep-blue common.
- **Wiccan, Rising Magician** — 4U 4/4 flyer; cast a non-creature spell → flicker another non-land non-token permanent. Genuine disagreement: Ethan high (flickers your busted ETBs, foils plans), Ben "garbage" (mostly a vanilla 5-mana 4/4). Unresolved.
- **Bold Biochemist** — 1U 1/3, power up 6 (+1 counter, draw two). Ethan: a fine relevant-late two-drop ("Mulldrifter/Landscape Painter back"). Ben: "stinks." Live disagreement; lean Ben given the slow, high-toughness format.
- **Aerial Doombot** — U 1/1 flyer, power up 5U (+3 counters). Scrappy hyper-aggro-in-the-air filler. "Questions about it," but one of the cards you scrap out with if forced low.
- **Super Intelligence** — U aura; enchanted creature's controller draws each upkeep. Greater-or-garbage: **garbage**, but Ethan will "absolutely" try a meme deck. Cute with Speedball (hexproof-plus) or Patriot protection.

### Black

- **Dark Deed** — Both hosts' #1 black uncommon and possibly the **best removal in the format, full stop**. 1B instant, −4/−4. Efficient, kills the 4-toughness bodies that define the format.
- **Klaw, Sonic Subjugator** — 1B 2/2; ETB reveal (1 + creatures in your yard) cards, you pick one to discard. "Insane" — Thought-Stalker-Warlock vibes; at worst a 2/2 that strips a card, at best Thoughtseizes their hand. Can't hit on curve.
- **Madame Masque** — 4B 3/2; ETB connives (brings a 2/1 menace token via its own draw-two), then each draw-your-second-card makes another 2/1. "Broken," significantly better than Moonstone. **Ben omitted it in the crash course by misreading it** — corrected to a top-2 black uncommon.
- **Cruel Alliance** — 2B sorcery, teamwork 2; exile a creature MV 3 or less (with teamwork: exile anything + gain 3 life). Ethan's pick for **best removal in the format** — flexible, exiles, life buffer. Sorcery speed barely matters given the play patterns.
- **Hour of Defeat** — 3B instant; destroy a creature, surveil 1. Solid catch-all; both rate Cruel Alliance slightly higher.
- **Arnim Zola, Bio-Fanatic** — 2B 2/3; 3,tap make a 2/1 menace villain token if 2+ creatures in your yard. "Good" repeatable token engine in graveyard-black.
- **Yellowjacket, Heartless Marauder** — B 1/2 flyer; when another villain enters, +2/+1 (?) and lifelink EOT. A premium-ish two-drop in a format starved for them; a 2/2 flying lifelink in the right deck. Caveat: don't force villains to enable it.
- **Baron Strucker, HYDRA Overlord** — 2B 2/2; villains cost 1 less, your villains connive (once/turn). **Moved DOWN** — Ben defended it as a manalith-plus-connive in the crash course; final ep "not that good, not even a reason to draft villains." A 3-mana 2/2 that dodges combat is fragile.
- **Moonstone, Harsh Mistress** — 3B 2/4 flyer; discard a card → exile-and-play it through your next turn. Connive payoff; "premium if you're conniving, but you don't *need* it if you're already popping off." Worse than Reed Richards in the 4-mana-2/4-that-draws slot.
- **HYDRA Troopers** — 2B 3/2; ETB make a tapped 2/1 menace if 2+ creatures in yard, else mill 2. Hyped as "best creature in the format if enabled," then **walked back**: bad on turn 3 unenabled, irrelevant late, can't enable itself, NOT a reason to be in black-green. Play it in any black deck, but it's "as good as your deck is."
- **Robot Domination** — 3B plan; cards to your yard from anywhere → draw, lose life, plan counter; at 3 counters make three 2/2 robot villain tokens. The best plan with white's — but you want your *own* sac outlets (Kingpin's Enforcers, Project Deathlok Soldier, Bullseye) to trigger it at will, not rely on combat.
- **The Masters of Evil** — 5B 5/6; other villains +2/+1, can discard it to tutor a plan. "Great, no notes" — anthems a board and is castable if you have one.
- **Red Room Recruit** — 1B 1/2; ETB connive. Ethan "great" sleeper (free yard fuel for black); Ben "too small-ball." Live disagreement.
- **HYDRA Infiltration** — 3B enchantment; ETB opponent discards two; attack-alone → drain 1. "Definition of a sleeper but I feel like I'm in a coma reading it" — Ethan likes the white-black grind plan; Ben skeptical.
- **Ninja of the Hand** — 2B 2/2 deathtouch, power up 4B (each opponent discards, +1 counter). Ethan still "buying stock"; Ben "too clunky, telegraphed." Lean Ben.
- **Decoy Ploy** — 1B (?) instant; return a villain and/or hero from your graveyard. Recursion glue for hero/villain decks.

### Red

> Red is the consensus **worst color** — its cards "fall off a cliff after the top two," it gets pulled in four disparate directions without good cards for any of them, and it's the only color with no army-in-a-can uncommon. The bright spot is the self-targeting / non-combat-damage package, but it's fragile.

- **Iron Fist, Living Weapon** — 2R 2/3; cast a spell targeting your creature → Iron Fist gains "tap: deal damage equal to power to any target." "Rare power level at uncommon," the best red card. The payoff that makes self-targeting work. Must be answered the turn it lands.
- **Truck Toss** — 2RR instant (2 less with a vehicle); deal 4 to any target. Ben's #1 red uncommon — the biggest burn chunk, and "4 is the magic number." (May get edged by the red creatures on a holistic re-rank.)
- **Hawkeye, Young Avenger** — 3R 2/4 reach; your non-combat damage to opponents/their permanents gets +Hawkeye's power. Ethan's favorite red card — a real build-around (Lightning Strike, Truck Toss, Hawkeye's Bow all go face). Risk: legendary, dies to removal and you're done. With Hawkeye out, Repulsor Blast's teamwork mode domes for 4.
- **Loki Laufeyson** — 1R 2/1; 1,tap copy your next instant/sorcery MV ≤ Loki's power; power up 4R (+2 counters). Pop-off potential — doubles Lightning Strike the turn you untap.
- **Photon Blast Barrage** — XR sorcery; copy X times, each deals 1 to a creature (split as you like). Ben reframed Ethan's evaluation: it's better than "3-for-1 / 4-for-2" math suggests. Bad vs. high toughness; great at mopping up tokens. Combos with **Red Hulk** (enrage) for ~15 damage off an X=4 cast.
- **Crimson Operative** — 3R 3/2 artifact prowess; ETB impulse-draw (play top card through next turn). "A two-for-one in a desolate landscape of one-for-ones." Notable red common; Ethan prefers the 3/2 printing.
- **Lightning Strike** — 2R instant, deal 3 any target. **Consensus hot take: not that good** (~C+). Misses the 4-toughness cards that matter. Would be better if red were better. Still your stock burn.
- **Repulsor Blast** — 3R sorcery, teamwork 2; deal 5 to a creature (+2 to its controller with teamwork). "Closer to 4-mana-kill-anything than not"; red needs to kill bigger things.
- **Blazing Crescendo** — 1R; target creature +3/+1 EOT, impulse-draw. Sleeper — cantripping pump that targets your own creature (turns on Iron Fist / Colleen Wing), and it replaces itself.
- **Jessica Jones, Private Eye** — 2R 2/3; tap + stun her, impulse X = her power. "A good card that might be homeless" (crash course). Final-ep softening: maybe red-white wants it (pump her power → exile 4–5 cards), draws two-ish every other turn on her own.
- **Misty Knight, Hero for Hire** — 1R 3/1; 2,tap discard a card, draw for each card discarded this turn. Cool with connive/land-cyclers. "If it weren't red, absolutely" play it — held back by being red.
- **Machinesmith Automaton** — 2R 2/2 artifact trample; grows when another artifact enters. Comes down turn 3, gets big in a long game. Not a reason to draft blue-red; one of the less-embarrassing artifacts.
- **Hire a Crew** — 2R instant; make a 2/1 menace villain, your creatures +1/+0 EOT. Ethan pitched it (token + buff + villain); Ben "every box it checks is too small." Lean Ben.
- **Kree Commandos** — 2W (white, listed here for the aggro-air discussion) 2/1 vigilance flying prowess. Ethan's late add for low-power blue-white prowess-tempo. (Note: this is a *white* card.)

### Green

> Green's top uncommons are bangers (Ka-Zar especially) then "fall off a cliff." Both of green's removal spells can cost a single mana — a huge plus. Hard to get *into* green unless you see a top uncommon early.

- **Ka-Zar of the Savage Land** — 4G 3/2; play lands from top of library + makes Zabu (legendary 2/2 cat that grows on landfall). Both hosts' #1 green uncommon, "bonkers/ridiculous" — Oracle of Mul Daya stapled to a body and a token.
- **White Tiger, Ava Ayala** — G 2/2, power up 5G (+1 counter, make the Tiger God, a legendary 4/4 that can't be blocked by more than one). Excellent two-drop that stays relevant — cast on 6 as a 3/3 + 4/4. A top-2 green uncommon.
- **Punishing Punch** — 2G instant (2 less if 2+ creatures in yard); your creature deals 2×power to a creature. Single-green kill-almost-anything at instant speed in green-black. Premium, very efficient.
- **Wakandan Royal Guard** — 4G 4/4 vigilance; ETB +1 counter (or +2 if the target is another hero). Ethan's bump-up; turns the blue mana-war (Justice) into a 4/4 flyer, or makes a 6-power haste-y board. Ben thinks it's out of place vs. the other notable commons.
- **Go Nuts!** — G sorcery, teamwork 3; +1/+1 counter and/or your creature fights theirs. "Has been nuts" — one-mana interaction, hugely tempo-positive when you double-spell. **Caveat the hosts learned the hard way:** you must already have 3 power on board to pay teamwork (you can't count the buffed creature). Green's removal being one-mana is "big game."
- **Pet Avengers** — 3G 4/4 reach, power up 6G (+1 counter, make a 3/2 vigilance hero). Genuinely good stats, but **both flagged it as a possible non-mover**: stuck in the clogged 4-drop slot, low ceiling, "makes the board stall rather than break it." Best in a green deck that *wants* to stall. Not a reason to go green.
- **Undercover Skrull** — 1G 1/1; +2/+2 and all creature types if 2+ creatures in yard, taps for any color. "Really good" — a two-drop that ramps 2→4 and becomes a 3/3 late. Rare praise for a green manalith.
- **Ant-Man's Army** — 2G 3/2; ETB make a food or treasure. "Really good" — low ceiling, very high floor; checks artifacts/ramp/fixing/life-gain/tokens boxes. Better than Pet Avengers per the final ep (3 mana, not 4).
- **Rapid Rescue** — G instant; mill 2, put a permanent from them into hand, gain 2 life. (Seed of Hope reprint.) Ethan "very good, very hard to miss"; **Ben whiffed on it twice on stream and swore off it** — genuine live disagreement on the permanent-count tension. Smooths green-black into a turn-3 HYDRA Troopers.
- **Hulkling, Burgeoning Bruiser** — 2G 2/3 vigilance hero; grows when a bigger-statted creature enters. A "counter factory" engine — pairs with Mister Hyde, Monster Within to build a draw loop (add counter / pull counter to draw). Not great alone; great in a +1/+1 home.
- **Reptil, Dinomorpher** — G 1/2; pay to become a 3/5 (3 mana) or 6/6 trample (6 mana) until EOT. Greater-or-garbage: **garbage.** Unique but a lot of mana for a one-turn effect.
- **Hellcat, Undying Vigilante** — GG 2/2 haste; dies → returns with a +1/+1, loses abilities, gains haste. Greater-or-garbage: **garbage** as a pick — needs ~11+ Forests; only a reward in a heavy-mono-green draft, never a card you warp toward.
- **Tigra, Feline Fury** — 1G 2/1 (?) flash trample; +1/+1 whenever you gain life. Needs ~2 life-gain sources and a 3rd toughness before it's exciting; off-theme. "Please do this thing" printed on it but the support isn't there.

### Multicolor (gold / hybrid)

- **Killmonger, Scourge of Wakanda** — BG 3/3; ETB optionally sac a creature → destroy a non-land permanent; +2/+1 if 2+ creatures in yard. **The best gold uncommon by far — "two gradations ahead of the pack."** Predicted top of the 17Lands power rankings. Removal + body + yard-enabler; worth pivoting for.
- **U.S.Agent, John Walker** — Orzhov hybrid 3/2; ETB make & auto-equip "Sturdy Shield" (+1/+2, equip 2). "Great" — flexible mana value, token + power-buff to punch through, auto-equips so no tempo loss.
- **Captain America, Living Legend** — WU 3/4 vigilance; first time each of your creatures taps on your turn, untap it. "A great monster" — well-statted, enables teamwork-then-attack and **crews S.H.I.E.L.D. Helicarrier then untaps your crew**.
- **Black Widow, Double Agent** — WB 3/2 deathtouch; attack-alone → first strike + menace. "Extremely oppressive from the white-black deck" — effectively unblockable when attacking alone.
- **Iron Man, Master of Machines** — UR 1/4 flying vigilance artifact; +1/+0 per other artifact, draws on attack if an artifact entered. The blue-red signpost. Good "if there have to be artifact decks," but only draft it when nobody else is fighting for it ("blue-red otter in Bloomburrow"). Kill it and the deck does nothing.
- **War Machine, Legacy of Iron** — Boros hybrid 1/3 flyer artifact hero; at combat, another creature gets +War Machine's power. Good in an aggressive Boros build — buff it once and it's a 2/3 flyer handing out +2/+0 each turn.
- **Abomination, Terrifying Titan** — Gruul hybrid 4/4 trample, power up 5 (+1 counter, fight a creature). Big, gets bigger, potential two-for-one. "Love this card."
- **Hulk, Gamma Goliath** — RG 6/5 reach trample (5 mana), other power-ups cost 3 less, power up 6RG (+5 counters). Huge; punishes you for not killing it (a 9/8 trampler the turn after). Gruul's headliner beater.
- **Kang, Temporal Tyrant** — UB 3/4; connives on attack, drain 1 on your second-card-drawn. The blue-black signpost; accrues value but is "just a 4-mana 3/4," emblematic of the set's understated signposts.
- **Madame Hydra** — BR 2/3; cast a villain → make a 2/1 menace. **"Terrible."** Doesn't meet a 2026 power threshold (4-mana 2/3 do-nothing). The reason red-black is weak.
- **Ant-Man, Colony Commander** — GU 2/2; attack + pay 1 → +1/+1 on a creature; putting a counter on a creature makes a 1/1 insect (once/turn). Understated 3-mana 2/2 that asks for a lot of mana and work for a minor payoff. Both lukewarm.
- **Beast, Erudite Aerialist** — GU (Simic hybrid) 3/3; has flying if you put a +1/+1 on it this turn, draws on combat damage. 4-mana 3/3 that does a lot of work for a small payoff. Both lukewarm.
- **The Serpent Society** — BG rare 3/4 deathtouch ward, 5 poison counters; when another deathtouch creature you control dies, each opponent sacs a non-token creature. Saw it "absolutely savage" people via instant-speed deathtouch (Widow's Bite). Strong build-around.

### Colorless / Artifacts / Equipment

> General read: the **equipment theme is dead** ("so much garbage equipment," three black uncommons wasted on it are "F-tier zeros"). The good artifacts are just good cards on their own.

- **Captain America's Shield** — 2-mana rare equipment, indestructible; +8/+0, vigilance, taps a blocker on attack, equip 2. **"Absurd," moved way up** — on a flyer it's an oppressive recurring threat. Only beaten by exiling the carrier.
- **H.E.R.B.I.E. Scout Unit** — 2-mana artifact 2/1 flyer that cantrips (per the namesake discussion). High-floor two-for-one flyer that also ramps; C++ but doesn't always fit the clogged 4-drop slot. Best in lower-ceiling / aggressive decks (the higher your deck's ceiling, the less you want it).
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Helicarrier** — 4-mana 4/5 flying vehicle; ETB make two (?) 1/1 soldiers, crew 6. Ben thought "terrible," **revised to "pretty good"** — broken with Captain America (crew, then untap your crew). Almost always a blue-white card.
- **Falcon's Wing Harness** — 1U equipment; ETB attach, equipped creature +1/+1, flying, ward 1, equip 2U. "A good card" — auto-equips, grants evasion. "Would have lost to this if I didn't have Web Up." Maybe best in blue-green (make big chunkers fly). One-of, not an equipment theme.
- **Avengers Tower** — uncommon land; taps for hero-only any-color, 4,tap dig 3 for a hero. Greater-or-garbage: **great.** "Everything is a hero or a villain." Impressive at grinding out board stalls.
- **Baxter Building** — uncommon land; 4,tap draw a card if you control a 4-toughness creature (trivial in this format). **The most impressive stall-breaker** in early access — effectively "4, tap, draw a card." (Also can filter mana toward bombs like Kang the Conqueror's take-an-extra-turn.) Preview greater-or-garbage was "garbage"; **revised up** in early access.
- **Villainous Hideout** — uncommon land; 3,tap a villain you control connives. The least-good of the three uncommon lands, but still a real card-selection/stall engine.
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Spy Kit** — W equipment; +1/+0, equip 1, attack-alone → untap + scry. "Questions about it," but a fine way to scrap out tempo in an aggressive white deck. Not a high pick.
- **A.I.M. Synthoids** — 2-mana 1/3 robot villain artifact; ETB surveil 2. Looks like it does everything (artifact + villain + yard-filler) but **"too low impact" — not the golden egg.** Predicted to rise in Pro-Tour pod play where good cards are scarcer.

### Rares & bombs

**The Fantastic Four infinite combo:** Human Torch, Johnny Storm + The Thing, Ben Grimm + Invisible Woman, Sue Storm + Mister Fantastic, Reed Richards in play together loop on your draw step and win on the spot. A real (if rare) win condition; the hosts flagged it as a "first on the LoL achievement list." Don't draft *for* it, but be aware of it as opponent and as upside.
- **Kang the Conqueror** — 2UU mythic 4/5 flyer villain; power up 5UUU (+1 counter, take an extra turn, power-ups locked during it). Mostly a great 4-mana 4/5 flyer that threatens to win if it reaches 8 mana; an opponent "savaged" Ben by filtering triple-blue with Baxter Building into the extra turn.
- **Red Hulk** — 4RR 6/7 reach trample, enrage (dealt damage → +1 counter, then deals that many to any target). The Photon Blast Barrage combo piece (~15 damage). Big standalone threat.
- **Mjölnir, Hammer of Thor** — 3R mythic legendary equipment; ETB deal 4 to a creature, double equipped creature's damage, equip "worthy" (legendary non-villain red/white), plus 2R discard → deal 2 to each creature. Ben's initial "shoehorned" reaction reversed to "this is good" — flavorful and powerful.
- **Thor Odinson** — 3RW 4/4 flying vigilance prowess legendary. "A juiced Serra Angel" — the biggest baddest thing if it dodges black removal / blue bounce.
- **Doom Reigns Supreme** — 1B rare plan; villain ETB → drain 1, plan counter; at 5 counters exile opponent's top 5 and cast up to two free. Preview "B"-ish; needs an all-villain deck and 5 triggers — the jury (and the format) soured on plans generally.
- **Titania, Rugged Rumbler** — 2BG 5/5 villain; additional cost discard-or-pay-2, ward discard-or-pay-2. A 3-mana 5/5 ahead of schedule, but "are you ever going to want to discard?" and ward 2 is the only protection. Removal-dependent evaluation; both lukewarm.

## TL;DR meta read

- **Slower than advertised.** Despite being the vehicle set, DFT drove well under the speed limit — this is one of the slower formats in recent memory. Two-drop 2/2s are poorly positioned; plan for longer games.
- **Green was the best color** by a wide margin at most points in the format. Hazard of the Dunes (5/4 reach exhaust) and Migrating Keron (6/6 trample reach) defined the rules of engagement. **Every deck needed a plan to beat those two cards.**
- **Black was not far behind green** — possibly ahead depending on who you ask. Wreckage Wicker Folk, Spin Out, Pack Doll Terror are deep, synergistic commons that pulled black into every major archetype.
- **Color power order (retro):** Green ≥ Black > Red = White > Blue (at common). Blue's commons are weak but its *uncommons* are exceptional — Spike Shell Harrier is the likely best uncommon in the set.
- **Archetype power order (retro, decisive):** 1) Green-Black (graveyard/exhaust) 2) Blue-Black (artifacts) 3) Red-Black (Start Your Engines) 4) Blue-Red (discard/cycling) 5) Big White 6) Four-color no-green 7) Green late-game soup 8) Green-Red beats.
- **White was disrespected early but viable** — specifically as a *big* white deck (Broadcast Rambler, Rides End, Marshal's Path Cruiser), not as aggro. Aggressive white builds with Nesting Bot were a trap.
- **Push the Limit** (the Zenith Flare of this format) is the most exciting build-around: cycle/discard cycling vehicles, then vomit them onto the battlefield at sorcery speed. The best versions of this deck are among the best decks you can draft.
- **Fixing:** Night Market (common cycling land) is a premium fixer. Play one colorless Start Your Engines land per deck if your mana base can support it. Splashing is possible but requires intentional fixing picks.

## Format principles

**Speed and curve**
- Format is significantly slower than advertised. Plan for the long game. Two-mana 2/2s are "poorly positioned" — they get bricked by 1/3s, die to Grim Bobble, and don't advance board states.
- Two-drops with three power OR three toughness are the sweet spot (Scotty's rule, as cited by Ben/Ethan). Gilded Goa and Layon Surveyor felt disappointing as 2/2s.
- Board stalls happen but primarily in green-on-green matchups. Non-green synergy decks either pop off or get stomped — it is not a universal board-stall format.

**Green is the Boogeyman — build around it**
- Hazard of the Dunes and Migrating Keron(?) are the lynchpin commons to beat. Every non-green deck needs a plan: six-power blocker (Marshal's Path Cruiser, Apocalypse Runner), exile removal (Rides End), or a synergy package that goes over the top (Push the Limit, Haunt the Network).
- Rides End (exile a tapped permanent, 3 less for tapped) is the best white common and answers both of these.

**Removal evaluation**
- Clunky removal is the best it has ever been: Crash and Burn, Siphon Fuel, Scrap Compactor all warranted maindeck slots because the format demanded answers to big threats.
- Grim Bobble and Lightning Strike — early front-runners for best commons — fell short. They're good in non-green matchups but often near-blank against green's large creatures.
- Broken Wings (destroy artifact/enchantment) was maindeckable, possibly in multiples. Rivert Monument and Ether Siphon were real mill win conditions; Perilous Snare (??) was also a threat worth hedging against.
- Rides End is the premier common removal spell. Spin Out (Murder variant) was a premium rare as a common.

**Mechanics — Start Your Engines / Max Speed**
- Start Your Engines is a late-game mechanic in practice — you need 3–4 turns minimum to reach Max Speed. It rewards the one-two punch: play a threat, then a removal spell to clear a blocker and deal damage, ratcheting speed to 2.
- Momentum Breaker is the linchpin of the Red-Black Start Your Engines archetype. It functions as a sorcery-speed edict ONLY if you built your deck wrong (many pilot tokens around). In the correct red-black deck, which is trading creatures off aggressively, it reliably hits a meaningful creature and swings the race.
- Hour of Victory (3-mana 2/2 + two tokens, max-speed demonic tutor) and End Rider Spike Spitter (4/3 reach, max-speed howling mine) are the premier payoffs.
- Start Your Engines was "savagely underrated for most of the format," per the retro. Surveyors turning into real two-for-ones, End Rider Catalyzer tapping for double mana, and oil gorger gaining lifelink were all overlooked.

**Mechanics — Exhaust**
- Exhaust is a great late-game mana sink — "everything is kicker." In a slow format it shines because you regularly reach the activation threshold.
- Scurry Foot (1/1, exhaust to +1/+1 counter + 3/3 elephant) was explosively synergistic once the exhaust payoffs were assembled.
- Elvish Refueler (exhaust lord — activate exhaust abilities twice per turn) enabled ridiculous turns in the teamer exhaust decks.
- Boom Scholar (reduce exhaust costs by 2) was the red-green exhaust engine.

**Mechanics — Vehicles**
- Vehicles were largely playable but not busted. WOTC threaded the needle. The key insight: **crew cost matters enormously.** Crew 1 is miles better than Crew 3.
- Pilot tokens (1/1 that crew/saddle as if power 2) were the lubricant that made high-crew-cost vehicles functional. Guidelight Matrix gave crew 2 for free.
- Push the Limit is the vehicle-set build-around. It animates all vehicles in your graveyard and gives them haste. With cycling vehicles (Clamorous Ironclad cycling 1, Skybox Fairy cycling 2) as the discard/cycle engine, you dig toward Push the Limit and then end the game. 15 lands is the right count with 3–4 cyclers; 14 was achievable with 10+ cyclers.

**Mechanics — Cycling**
- Cycling supported both the Blue-Red discard deck and the Push the Limit shell.
- Marauding Mako (1/1, cycling 2, put counters when you discard) and Scaming Skyray (?) were serviceable cycling payoffs. Monument to Endurance was the best cycling payoff. Captain Howler was also excellent.
- Cycling vehicles (Clamorous Ironclad, Skybox Fairy, Detention Chariot cycling 1W, Thundering Brood Wagon cycling 2) are typically late picks that make the Push the Limit deck tick.

**Fixing and land counts**
- Night Market (common: ETB tapped, tap for any chosen color, cycling 3) is a top-10 common and enables splashes. Play as many as you can.
- Starting Column (3-mana Manalith, Start Your Engines, Max Speed: tap + sac to draw 2 / discard 1) is the "Golden Egg" of the format — glues every archetype, enables splashes, and delays color commitment. Named best common to tie archetypes together (retro).
- Colorless lands that start your engines (Amat Raceway, Avisher Raceway) are playable — run 1 max per deck, supports 8/8/1 mana distribution. More in mono-color builds.
- Fixing felt tight. If you pick up a splash target in pack 1, prioritize fixing from there. Marshal's Path Cruiser ramp-sacks a basic land on ETB — a hidden way to fix a splash.
- Standard 17 lands. Cut to 15–16 with cycling-heavy Push the Limit builds.

**Signals and open lanes**
- Three meta phases occurred: (1) early format — force green / white is terrible; (2) mid-format — green contested, grixis/Start Your Engines wide open; (3) late — Push the Limit discovered, white rehabilitated.
- Avoid committing to gold cards early unless it is specifically Gastl Thrill Seeker (black/red) and you are naturally steering that way already.
- Green being overdrafted means white is often underdrafted — Rides End and Broadcast Rambler available 5th-8th pick is equity waiting to be claimed.
- Uncommons drive play-booster era drafts. Blue's commons are bad but its uncommons are incredible — if blue uncommons are flowing, that is a real signal.

## Card notes (community consensus)

Later (retro) takes override earlier ones. Garbled names marked (?).

### White

- **Rides End** — Best white common. Exile a tapped permanent (3 less if tapped) at instant speed. Answers Migrating Keron, splashable. The premier common removal spell. Retro: "easy, all hail Rides End." (mid, late, retro)
- **Broadcast Rambler** — 5/4 vehicle, ETB creates 1/1 thopter, crew 1. Gives white a way to tussle with green's 6/6s (six total power across two bodies). Foundation of "Big White" decks. Was wildly underrated early. (late, retro)
- **Air Response Unit** — 3/3 flyer vigilance, crew 1, uncommon. Solid finisher and helps race toward Max Speed with evasion + vigilance. (preview)
- **Spot Cycle Scouter** — 3/2 vehicle, ETB scry 2, crew 1, uncommon. Excellent curve play; Ben compared favorably to Air Response Unit early on, slight back-off later. (preview)
- **Cloudspire Coordinator (?)** — 2/3 uncommon, mounts/vehicles you control get +1/+1, saddles/crews as if power 2 greater. Oppressive in red-white and green-white. (preview)
- **Canyon Vaulter (?)** — 1W uncommon 3/1, whenever it saddles/crews a mount or vehicle in your main phase that permanent gains flying. Evasion enabler for Max Speed. (preview, early)
- **Nesting Bot** — 1W common 1/1, Start Your Engines, dies → 1/1 servo token, Max Speed: +1/+1. Strong pull into white in preview season. Retro: "nesting bot is basically a blank piece of cardboard in this format" — white was not the aggro color. White-aggro builds were a trap; big white was correct. (preview "B-" → retro "avoid in aggro")
- **Tune Up** — 3W sorcery, return artifact from graveyard; if it's a vehicle it becomes a creature. Niche but combos with Push the Limit: cycle a bomb vehicle early, reanimate on turn 4. (early)
- **Explosive Getaway** — 3RW rare uncommon (?), exile up to one target artifact creature (returns at next end step), deal 4 to each other creature. Sweeper + blink. Massively underrated; got late in draft because white was disrespected. Home in big white and Push the Limit. (mid, retro)
- **Detention Chariot** — 4WW 6/6 vehicle, cycling single white, Exile a nonland permanent on ETB. Used in Push the Limit as a cycling O-Ring. (late, retro)
- **Veteran Beast Rider** — 1GW uncommon 3/4, untap your creatures at end of step, pay 4GW for +1/+1 team pump. Impressive in sealed and big white decks; splashable. "Three-four for three mana that threatens four-five." (late, retro)
- **Layon Surveyor (?)** — 1W 2-drop Start Your Engines, first strike on your turn, max speed: draw a card. Better as a blocker than an attacker per late-format analysis. Two-mana 2/2s poorly positioned. (retro)
- **Unswerving Sloth** — 3WW 5/5 mount, saddle 4, indestructible + untaps all while attacking. Initially excited about it; retro tone-down: five-mana 5/5 with no ETB is a knock. (preview)

### Blue

- **Bounce Off** — 1U common instant, bounce a creature or vehicle. Topped charts on 17Lands early. Key insight (late ep): it is about **patience**, not tempo — in a slow format, it saves your bomb on a key turn and makes opponents fear it in best-of-3. "Finally get this card." (late, retro)
- **Stock Up** — 2U common sorcery, look at top 5, put 2 in hand. Retro: best blue common. "Just better Dig Through Time — dig through time at home." Early episode called it garbage in a fast format; retro reversal: format is slow enough this was excellent. (preview "garbage" → retro "best blue common")
- **Roadside Blowout** — 2U uncommon sorcery, bounce creature/vehicle + draw card (2 less if target MV 1). Premium blue interaction; "bread and butter for every blue deck." (preview, mid)
- **Keen Buccaneer** — Common blue, exhaust loot ability; good in any blue deck as mana sink. "Just a good card in general." (late, retro)
- **Rangers Refueler** — 1U uncommon 3/3 vehicle, crew 2; whenever you activate an exhaust ability draw a card. Exhaust payoff in blue; Ben nicknamed it "Surf's Pack" (?). (preview, retro)
- **Thopter Fabricator** — 2U uncommon 4/4 flying vehicle; when you draw your second card each turn, make a 1/1 thopter. Bonkers in Push the Limit and cycling decks; "everything you already want to do." (late, retro)
- **Spike Shell Harrier** — 4U uncommon 4/4, bounce a thing, reduce opponent's speed. Ben's vote for best uncommon in the set. "My obsession." (late, retro)
- **Transit Mage** — Uncommon, tutor up an artifact. Tutors Boom Mobile, Skybox Fairy, Clamorous Ironclad. Key piece of Push the Limit. (mid, late)
- **Spectral Interference (?)** — 1U uncommon, counter creature/vehicle unless they pay 4. Underplayed but excellent vs. green decks (counters Hazard, Keron). Good sideboard/include in best-of-3. (late)
- **Midnight Mangler** — 1U 3/3 vehicle, crew 2; it is an artifact creature on opponent's turn already. The iconic 2-mana 3/3 in a new form. (preview)
- **Captain Howler (?)** — Blue uncommon, cycling payoff. "Nuts" in blue-red cycling. (mid, retro)
- **Ether Siphon** — 1UU uncommon artifact, Start Your Engines, Max Speed: whenever you draw a card each opponent mills 2. Ben/Ethan called it "garbage" and "what if Sphinx's Tutelage didn't work." However, Rivert Monument (?) and Ether Siphon together made mill a real win condition. (preview "garbage", retro "real win condition in the right deck")

### Black

- **Wreckage Wicker Folk** — 2B common 3/1 artifact, flyer, surveil 2 on ETB. Retro: "Everything Everywhere All at Once — did so much for every archetype." Best black common per both hosts. Artifact for affinity, surveil for black-green, flyer for Start Your Engines evasion, the list goes on. (all episodes)
- **Spin Out** — BB instant, destroy creature/vehicle. Murder variant. "When was the last time a murder variant was like 'wow this is premium at common?'" Premium removal, especially vs. green. (retro)
- **Pack Doll Terror (?)** — Common black artifact creature (?), deals damage/drain based on artifacts you control. "Insane in multiples." Triggered on itself. Key card for all black non-green archetypes: blue-black, red-black, white-black. (mid, late, retro)
- **Grim Bobble** — 1B common, -2/-2 + tap sac to surveil 2. Started as a top-5 common contender; fell sharply. Still first copy makes your deck; terrible vs. green. "Has the highest range of any card in the format." In pack doll terror decks, gets better; with trade the helm gets better. (all episodes, retro)
- **Momentum Breaker** — 1B uncommon enchantment, Start Your Engines, ETB edict (sac creature/vehicle or discard), sac it to gain life = your speed. The lynchpin of Red-Black SYE. Not a generic edict — requires building around it (aggressive creature trading) to avoid hitting tokens. "Premium for red-black, still play it in most black decks." (late, retro)
- **Mutant Surveyor (?)** — 2B 2/3, 2 to pump it and Max Speed effect. Black surveyor is ranked #1 of all five by a large margin — attacks as a 4/5 on turn 4 if you hit land drops, and "unblockable late." (late, retro)
- **End Rider Spike Spitter (?)** — 3R 3/4 reach, Start Your Engines, Max Speed: beginning of upkeep exile top card, may play it. "Howling mine" for your deck. Premium payoff for SYE decks. (mid, late)
- **Hour of Victory** — 1B uncommon enchantment, Start Your Engines, ETB creates 2/2 black zombie token; Max Speed: sac + search library for any card (demonic tutor). "3-mana 2/2 that can demonic tutor — that is a good card." Initially hard to evaluate; settled as a premier SYE payoff. (preview, mid, retro)
- **Hellish Sideswipe (?)** — B instant/sorcery, sac a creature/artifact to destroy a creature/vehicle. Bone Splinters variant; good artifact sac outlet. (mid, late)
- **Deathless Pilot** — Common 1/1, surveil 1 on ETB (?). Good in decks that want surveil or need artifacts. (mid)
- **Wicker Folk Indomitable (?)** — 3B uncommon 4/3 artifact creature, may cast from graveyard by paying 2 life + sac artifact/creature. Graveyard recursion. (preview)
- **Back on Track** — 4B sorcery, reanimate creature/vehicle + make 1/1 pilot token. Better than Tune Up per Ben: more flexible, creates a body. (preview)
- **Crypt Collar Chariot (?)** — 3B 5/5 Menace vehicle, whenever you discard cards make that many tapped 2/2 zombie tokens. "Rise from the Tides vibes." Fun with Push the Limit and discard effects. (late)
- **Engine Rat (?)** — Common black 1/1, tap ability to drain each opponent. Retro apology take: "We're sorry — you're a very good card." Underrated initially; came around quickly. (late, retro)
- **Maximum Overdrive** — 1B instant, +1/+1 counter + indestructible + deathtouch until end of turn. Outstanding combat trick; great for attacking into large green creatures. "Standing combat trick both offensively and defensively." (retro)

## Signals & seat reads

- **Green being contested** is the dominant signal of a well-formed pod. If green appears to be flowing (late Hazard of the Dunes, late Migrating Keron), someone has prioritized it incorrectly or the color is simply open. Take green if it is open and accept that you will be the best green deck at the table.
- **If green is cut or contested by multiple drafters**, the grixis wedge (blue-black-red) archetype opens up. This is where Start Your Engines and artifact synergies live and the format rewards you for finding it.
- **White flowing late is equity to scoop up.** Rides End, Broadcast Rambler, and explosive Getaway going 5th-8th pick = white is underdrafted. Jump in; don't draft white-aggro, draft big-white.
- **Push the Limit (red sorcery, rare-looking)** is a real wheel. The build-around pieces (Clamorous Ironclad, Skybox Fairy) are also late picks that blue-red cycling can also use; if you're seeing them freely in packs 2/3, someone may already be in Push the Limit or cycling is just open.
- **Transit Mage (blue uncommon, tutors up any artifact)** wheeling is a green flag for blue-red Push the Limit or artifact decks.
- **Starting Column going late** signals that Start Your Engines and gold-card decks are not being prioritized. Scoop up multiples.
- **Avoiding gold cards early** is strongly advocated. The format rewards reading your lane. Ben explicitly says: "I hate taking gold cards early in this format — I want to feel it out."
- **Pivot advice:** If black is cut, look for blue-red cycling or big white. If red is cut, look for blue-black or green-black. Never commit to a gold card in the first few picks without a strong color read.

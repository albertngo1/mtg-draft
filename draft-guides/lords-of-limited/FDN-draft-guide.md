## TL;DR meta read

- **White is the format's best color at common.** Healer's Hawk on turn 1 is a "scourge." White's commons are unusually deep, and white pairs — especially red-white and blue-white — are the consensus aggressive plans.
- **Black sacrifice is the clearest synergy archetype.** Black is dense with sacrifice synergies and fodder. Hungry Ghoul and Vampire Gourmand are the key sacrifice outlets; Vengeful Blood Witch is the payoff engine.
- **Blue-red spells is the clearest linear archetype.** Balmare Battle-mage Captain (signpost) and Tolarian Terror are the anchors. Blue is the secondary support color for spells; think twice and instants package.
- **Format speed: midrange aggro.** White aggro is the top threat; black sacrifice and blue control are the two main answers. Burst Lightning and Stab are the key removal commons — both answer 2-toughness creatures, making three toughness the "magic number."
- **Green is weak, especially at common.** Hosts repeatedly call it shallow and "woefully out of sorts." Best green decks lean on Garrick's Uprising + 4-power creatures. Overrun can cheese wins in go-wide green decks but green is the clear fifth color.
- **Commons are back.** The rare slot is relatively underpowered in this set. The most impactful cards are frequently at common or uncommon, not rare — prioritize deep commons over speculative rares.
- **Tribal themes (elves, goblins, cats) are real but minor.** Don't draft around them unless you stumble into multiples naturally.

## Format principles

**Removal and the magic number:**
- Burst Lightning (1R, deal 2, kicker 4 for deal 4) and Stab (1B, -2/-2 to a creature) are the key common removal spells. Both answer two-toughness creatures cleanly. Three toughness is the "magic number" — bodies that survive these are significantly better.
- Eaten Alive (B sorcery, sac a creature or pay 3B, exile a creature/planeswalker) is black's premium removal; sacrificing is often free in black decks.
- Bake Into a Pie (2BB instant, destroy + food) is expensive but flexible. Double-bake draws in the late game are common.
- Abraid (1R, deal 3 or destroy an artifact) and Fiery Annihilation (2R, deal 5 + exile equipment) are the strong red uncommon removal spells.
- Witness Protection (1U aura, shrink to 1/1 Citizen, loses abilities) is a zero-grade in crash course and generally not recommended — the 1-power body allows double blocks, and sacrifice outlets make it worse.

**Curve and mana:**
- White aggro forces opponents to think from turn one. Healer's Hawk (1W, 1/1 flying lifelink) on turn one is a serious game-piece.
- This is a two-color format. Heavy lean into one color is expected — "9/8 or 10/7 splits common, not 50/50." Mana fixing is limited.
- Fixing available: Evolving Wilds, Campus Guide (2-mana 2/1 that top-decks a basic), Burnished Hart (uncommon, 2/2 for 3 that sacrifices to search two basics), Grow From the Ashes (2G sorcery, search a basic; kicker adds another, both enter untapped — very good), Bushwack (split: fight spell or search a basic).
- Gold Vein Pick (2-mana equipment, +1/+1, makes a Treasure on connect, equip 1) provides incidental fixing and is a great tool for Healer's Hawk or aggressive decks.
- In green decks specifically, count Bushwack as a pseudo-land — you can run 16 lands in a green-based deck with multiple copies.

**Signals and color depth:**
- White, blue, and black are the "juiced" colors at common. Red and green are shallower.
- Red is a support color more than a base color — it adds removal (Burst Lightning, Abraid) and haste threats to whatever it pairs with (white aggro, blue spells, black sacrifice).
- Green's best commons are 4-power creatures and Garrick's Uprising. Neither is the spine of a deck — you need the Uprising density to make green work.
- Tribal themes (cats in white, elves in green, goblins in red) are present but not the primary draft signal. Ignore them unless rares are supporting them.

**Mechanics to know:**
- **Raid** (triggered if you attacked this turn): appears on medium-to-large creatures and enchantments. Harder to enable than it looks because early turns are often spent developing mana. Hosts are skeptical of morbid on five-drops for similar reasons.
- **Morbid** (triggered if a creature died this turn): similarly conditional; requires your opponent to trade or you to sacrifice — both happen less than you'd think. The ceiling on morbid five-drops is B+, but floor is zero.
- **Threshold** (seven+ cards in graveyard): powers up blue-black. Surveil, looting, and self-mill help enable it. Dreadwing Scavenger rewards you for hitting threshold with deathtouch and +1/+1.
- **Garrick's Uprising**: one of green's best non-rare cards. Each creature entering triggers a +1/+1 counter on an existing creature. Loops with Elvish Regrow (2GG, return a permanent from your graveyard to hand) for repeatable value.

**Land count:**
- 17 lands is baseline. Aggressive decks with Bushwack and low curves can manage 16.
- Blue-black and blue-control decks sometimes run 18 due to mana sinks (Arena-style loot effects, multi-spell turns).
- Rogue's Passage is a colorless land that lets a creature be unblockable for 4 mana. Worth including as an 18th land in heavy-blue or midrange decks — it closes games against stabilized boards.

## Card notes (community consensus)

### White
- **Healer's Hawk** (W, 1/1 flying lifelink) — Consensus top white common. A "scourge" that forces opponents to answer it immediately or fall far behind on life total and race. First pick quality in white aggro.
- **Helpful Hunter** (1W, 1/1, ETB draw a card) — Excellent common; a two-mana 1/1 that cantrips. Generates incremental value; synergizes with sacrifice (sac it with Gourmand) and counters (put +1/+1 on it). Ben rates it highly.
- **Healer Savior / Fadar Savior (?)** (3W, 2/3 lifelink, ETB put +1/+1 on up to two other creatures) — Banger common; gives plus-plus counters to two other creatures and has lifelink itself. High power.
- **Banishing Light** (2W enchantment, exile anything until it leaves) — Premium white removal at common. A first-pick-level card.
- **Dauntless Veteran** (1WW, 2/2, whenever it attacks creatures you control get +1/+1 until end of turn) — Ben is very high on this; Ethan is lower (zero in crash course). Ben upgraded to a one/four after debate. White decks that attack will play it; it's a potential 3/3 attacker that pumps the team. Dies to Stab/Burst Lightning (2 toughness is the problem).
- **Dazzling Angel** (2W, 2/3 flyer, ETB and each time another creature enters you gain 1 life) — Crash course: Ben grades four, Ethan grades one. Agreed on two after debate. Best in black-white lifegain decks; still fine in blue-white flyers.
- **Sun-Blessed Healer (?)** (1W, 3/1 lifelink, kicker 1W returns a non-land permanent with mana value 2 or less from your graveyard to play) — Both hosts' #1 white uncommon. Incredible. The kicked version is a three-mana 3/1 lifelink that gets back a two-drop. Very strong.
- **Cat Collector** (2W, 3/2, ETB make a food; whenever you gain life for the first time each turn create a 1/1 white cat token) — Ben's #1 white uncommon. Combines life-gain trigger with the food from its own ETB; creates a persistent token engine. Pairs very well with Healer's Hawk and life-gain density.
- **Guarded Air (?)** (5W, 1/1 lifelink, ETB make two 3/3 white Knight tokens) — Imperial Oath reprint. Ben's #2 white uncommon. Called "eminently splashable" and excellent as a late splash in blue-green or other decks with fixing.
- **Inspiring Paladin (?)** (2W, 3/3, first strike on your turn + during your turn creatures you control with +1/+1 counters have first strike) — Crash course sleeper pick for green-white +1/+1 counters.
- **Fleeting Flight** (W instant, +1/+1 counter, gains flying until end of turn, prevents all combat damage to it) — Sleeper common combat trick. Both a pump and an evasion grant; leaves a counter behind. Very strong in aggressive white decks.

### Blue
- **Tolarian Terror** (6U, 5/5 ward 2, cost reduced by 1 for each instant/sorcery in your graveyard) — Top blue common. The lynchpin of blue decks; can come down cheaply when you have spell density. Still needs the support of instants/sorceries to be reliably castable.
- **Mischievous Mystic** (1U, 2/1 flyer, whenever you draw your second card each turn make a 1/1 blue Fairy with flying) — Ben grades A-minus; both hosts agree this is likely the best blue uncommon. Snowballs immediately — opponents must answer it or face a token flood. Very good in any blue deck with cantrips or loot effects.
- **Fa Bloom Trick (?)** (2U instant, create two 1/1 blue Fairy tokens with flying, tap a creature an opponent controls) — Strong tempo and board-development in one card. Ben's #2 blue uncommon.
- **Micromancer** (3U, 3/3, ETB search library for an instant or sorcery with mana value 1, reveal it, put it in hand) — #3 blue uncommon for both hosts. Tutors for Think Twice, Fleeting Distraction, combat tricks, etc. "Will somehow still be busted even without Repossession."
- **Uncharted Voyage** (3U instant, owner puts target creature on top or bottom of library, surveil 1) — Discussed at length. Ben gives four; Ethan gives two. Agreed this is excellent in blue-red and blue-black (the "instant-speed package" decks). Ethan notes it's worse in blue-white against aggressive decks because spending four mana on removal is slow.
- **Think Twice** (1U instant, draw a card, flashback 2U) — Core cantrip that enables Tolarian Terror and Mischievous Mystic. Never cut in blue-red or blue-black.
- **Refute** (1U, counter a spell, draw a card, discard a card) — Sleeper uncommon; loot-stapled-onto-Cancel. Strong in blue-red and blue-black spell packages.
- **Etherize** (3U instant, return all attacking creatures to their owner's hand) — Ben loves it; Ethan grades zero. Consensus: bad. "People just don't attack with a lot of creatures often" and four mana to bounce some creatures is not good.
- **Abyssal Harvester (?)** (appears in gameplay — 4-5 mana black-blue creature, exiles a creature card from graveyard that was put there this turn, creates a copy except it's a nightmare, then exiles other nightmares you control) — Showcased in the blue-black draft video. Ethan rates it highly and takes it over Bake Into a Pie at one point. Very powerful in a surveil/loot deck.

### Black
- **Stab** (B, creature gets -2/-2 until end of turn) — One of the two key common removal spells alongside Burst Lightning. First-pick-level in black. "Two toughness creatures die to Stab" is the format's #1 removal note.
- **Eaten Alive** (B sorcery, as additional cost sacrifice a creature OR pay 3B; exile target creature or planeswalker) — Premium removal; the sacrifice-or-pay-3 modes both work well. In sacrifice decks, the creature is usually free. A+ in black sacrifice builds.
- **Bake Into a Pie (?)** (2BB instant, destroy target creature, you get a food) — Expensive but unconditional. Played in multiples in blue-black control decks throughout the videos.
- **Vampire Gourmand** (1B, 2/2; whenever it attacks you may sacrifice another creature — if you do, draw a card and it can't be blocked this turn) — Crash course: Ben grades four, Ethan grades one; both end up on four. "Free sacrifice outlet" + card draw + unblockable. Very powerful in any black sacrifice build.
- **Hungry Ghoul** (1B, 2/2; pay 1, sacrifice another creature, put +1/+1 counter on this) — Crash course "sleeper" highlighted specifically: "vital for black decks to function." Repeatable sacrifice outlet that grows. Take it early in black.
- **Vengeful Blood Witch** (1B, 1/1; whenever this or another creature you control dies, target opponent loses 1 life and you gain 1 life) — Ben's #1 black uncommon; Ethan has it #1 as well. "Black is so about sacrifice that I can't imagine taking Nighthawk over blood witch pack one pick one." Relentless life-drain engine.
- **Vampire Nighthawk** (1BB, 2/3 flying deathtouch lifelink) — Ben's #1 black uncommon; both hosts agree it's excellent. The stat line does all the work. Also played with Lay Line Axe (?) for deathtouch+double strike+trample.
- **Infestation Sage** (B, 1/1; when it dies create a 1/1 black-green Insect with flying) — "Doomed Traveler color-shifted." Key fodder for black sacrifice decks; provides an evasive body after death. Highlighted in crash course as a notable black common.
- **Arbiter of Woe** (4BB, 5/4 flying demon; as additional cost to cast, sacrifice a creature; ETB each opponent discards a card and loses 2 life, you draw a card and gain 2 life) — Crash course: Ben grades B; Ethan grades one; settled as a high pick. "Powerful effect in a format where raw power is scarce." The sacrifice cost is free in black builds. Strong curve-topper.
- **Infernal Vessel** (2B, 2/1 human cleric; when it dies if it wasn't a demon, return it with two counters as a 4/3 demon) — Crash course: Ben grades B-minus, Ethan grades one; both update to four. "Annoying to face" — opponents can't trade their two-drops favorably with it since it comes back bigger. Good sacrifice fodder that doubles as a threat.
- **Soul Shackled Zombie (?)** (3B, 4/2; ETB exile up to two target cards from a single graveyard, if at least one creature was exiled each opponent loses 2 life and you gain 2 life) — Both hosts call this out as a sleeper and put it on their notable commons lists. "Gain two drain two creatures in Black are always over-performers."
- **Burglar Rat** (1B, 1/1; ETB target opponent discards a card) — Crash course sleeper pick. Good hand disruption + sacrifice fodder. Ben highlights that resource wars happen in this format (not many two-for-ones), so making the opponent discard matters.
- **Revenge of the Rats** (2BB sorcery, create a tapped 1/1 black rat token for each creature card in your graveyard; flashback 2BB) — Crash course: Ben grades B, Ethan grades two. Settled lower. "Rats come in tapped" and the set-up required is high. Possible in blue-black or black-green with graveyard density, but don't draft around it.
- **Midnight Snack** (2B enchantment, raid ETB: create a food; pay 2B sacrifice: target opponent loses X life where X = life gained this turn) — Crash course "fun card" but both hosts skeptical. Ben grades two; Ethan grades zero. "When do you play it" — not on turn three, awkward timing. Possible in black-white lifegain as a finisher but generally low-pick.

### Red
- **Burst Lightning** (R instant, deal 2 to any target; kicker 4 for deal 4 instead) — One of the two key common removal spells. "Best Red common" per both hosts; on every notable commons list. The kicker mode is real value in the late game.
- **Abraid / A'braid (?)** (1R instant, deal 3 to a creature or destroy an artifact) — Top red uncommon. "Nothing better than deal three or blow up an artifact."
- **Heartfire Emulator** (1R, 2/2 uncommon prowess; pay R, sacrifice it: deal damage equal to its power to a creature or planeswalker) — Strong uncommon; Ben's red #2. Prowess + built-in removal. Very good in blue-red spells.
- **Fiery Annihilation (?)** (2R instant, deal 5 damage to target creature, exile up to one equipment attached to it; if it would die this turn exile it instead) — Ben's #3 red uncommon. Handles the biggest creatures and exiles Gold Vein Pick (bypasses artifact).
- **Dragon Trainer** (3RR, 1/1 that creates a 4/4 flying Dragon token on ETB) — Five mana for 1+4=5 power of which 4 flies. Hosts debate this — "what is clunky about five power for five mana?" It's fine; Ben's red #2 in crash course.
- **Goblin Borders (?)** (2R, 3/2 with raid: put a +1/+1 counter on it) — Crash course: Ben grades three, Ethan grades zero. Ethan calls it "atrocious." Red-green decks specifically might want it for 4-power payoffs, but generally dismissed. "This is the definition of a card you don't miss at end of draft."
- **Strongbox Raider (?)** (2RR, 5/2 with raid on ETB: exile top two cards, choose one to play until end of next turn) — Crash course: Ben grades four, Ethan grades two. "Atrocious when you can't trigger raid." Probably only in red-white (top of curve) or occasionally red-green.

### Green
- **Garrick's Uprising (?)** (2GG sorcery, +1/+1 counter on each of your creatures for each creature you control with power 4 or greater) — One of if not the most important non-rare card to green decks. Both Ethan and other players take multiples and it enables explosive turns. Works especially well with 4-power creatures and looping Elvish Regrow.
- **Elvish Regrow (?)** (2GG, 4/3 uncommon; ETB return target permanent card from your graveyard to your hand) — Ben's green #1 uncommon. "Pushed but in a sea of very mid cards." Can loop with Garrick's Uprising for repeatable value.
- **Felling Blow** (1G sorcery, put +1/+1 counter on target creature you control, then it fights a creature an opponent controls) — Ben's green #2 uncommon. Premium green removal (fight + counter left behind).
- **Nessian Hornbeetle (?)** (1G, 2/2 uncommon; at the beginning of your combat, if you control another creature with power 4 or greater, put +1/+1 counter on this) — Crash course debate: Ben grades B-minus, Ethan grades one. Both settle on four. "This just gets out of hand" — plays as a 2/2 that quickly grows in red-green or green decks with 4-power creatures.
- **Good Fortune Unicorn** (1GW, 2/2 signpost; whenever another creature you control enters put a +1/+1 counter on it) — Ethan rates it higher than Ben. "Unchecked wins games." Resolute Reinforcements (flash tokens) + this is a powerful engine. Ben concedes the card is powerful even if the archetype is weak.
- **Snakeskin Veil** (G instant, +1/+1 counter on target creature, gains hexproof until end of turn) — Ethan's green #3 uncommon. "Excellent in any format." Leaves a counter; protects key creatures from Stab/Burst Lightning.
- **L'War Elves (?)** (G, 1/1 Elf; tap to add one mana of any color your lands could produce) — Crash course: Ethan initially grades zero (bold stand), immediately walks it back to two. Useful in blue-green and black-green for ramping into five-plus drops. Not good in green-white. "Getting ahead on mana or generating value to mitigate being a land — two things that need to happen."
- **Overrun** (2GGG sorcery, +3/+3 and trample to all your creatures until end of turn) — "Will be good in bad decks." Ethan: "I just don't like cards that are only good in one specific board state." Ben repeatedly advocates for it; Ethan is skeptical. Works best in red-green and green-white. Ethan did trophy with a green deck that used Overrun to cheese games.
- **Treetop Snare-Spinner (?)** (3G, 1/4 spider, reach, deathtouch; activated ability 2G: put +1/+1 counter on a creature you control, sorcery speed) — Crash course: Ben grades four; Ethan grades two ("only good in green-blue and green-black"). Both agree it holds the ground and air. Very annoying to face.
- **Apothecary Stomper (?)** (4GG, 4/4 vigilance; ETB choose between putting two counters on a creature or gaining four life) — Ben almost puts it on his notable commons list but doesn't. Likely among green's best commons but didn't crack top 10.
- **Dwin's Elite (?)** (appears in gameplay repeatedly as a 3/4 reach creature in green) — A solid defensive body; used effectively in green-black decks. Not a first-pick-level card but fills an important role.

### Multicolor
- **Balmare Battle-mage Captain (?)** (1UR, 1/3 Bird Wizard flying; whenever you cast an instant or sorcery, creatures you control get +1/+0 and trample until end of turn) — Blue-red signpost. Both hosts very excited. "Balmore is going to be good and this archetype is going to be good — feel pretty confident." Best signpost uncommon in the set per crash course.
- **Dreadwing Scavenger** (1UB, 2/2 flyer; ETB loot; when it enters or attacks draw then discard a card; at threshold it's +1/+1 and deathtouch) — Blue-black signpost. Ben: "Not Smuggler's Copter, but it's not not Smuggler's Copter." ETB loot is the push that makes it good. Ethan initially more skeptical; gameplay shows it's very useful.
- **Emperion Eagle** (1WU, 2/3 flyer signpost; other flying creatures you control get +1/+1) — Blue-white signpost. Synergy with Healer's Hawk and any other flyers is obvious. "Healer's Hawk into two-drop flyer into this is going to be exceptionally annoying."
- **Fish Panda (?)** (2WB, 3/2 signpost; whenever you gain life put +1/+1 counter on it; when it dies return another non-bear creature with mana value equal to or less than its power from your graveyard to battlefield) — Black-white signpost. Four-mana 3/2 is a tough sell; Ethan is cautious. Both hosts worry about the 2-toughness (dies to Stab). But the recursion when it grows and dies is real.
- **Heroic Reinforcements** (2RW sorcery, create two 1/1 white Soldier tokens; until end of turn creatures you control get +1/+1 and gain haste) — Red-white signpost. "A banger returning here." Consensus excellent; one of the better signpost uncommons.
- **Perforating Artist** (1BR, 3/2 deathtouch; raid: at beginning of your end step if you attacked this turn, each opponent loses 3 life unless they sacrifice or discard) — Red-black signpost. Hosts agree black-red is really about sacrifice, not raid, so the signpost is somewhat misleading. The card itself is useful but not the core of the archetype.
- **Ruby Daring Tracker (?)** (RG, 1/2 haste; when it attacks while you control a creature with power 4 or greater gets +2/+2; tap: add R or G) — Red-green signpost. Ben thinks it's fine as a two-mana hasty ramper; Ethan more skeptical of red-green generally. The mana acceleration to four-drops matters most.
- **Wardens of the Cycle** (1BGG, 4/3 signpost; morbid: beginning of end step if a creature died this turn, choose one — gain 2 life or draw a card and lose a life) — Black-green signpost. Both hosts agree double-green-pip was added to prevent every black deck from splashing it. Very good in dedicated black-green decks.
- **Tayo'va Benthic Druid (?)** (3GU, 3/3 legendary; landfall: whenever a land you control enters, you gain a life and draw a card) — Blue-green signpost. "Really this is a six-drop" — you want to play it into a land drop. Unchecked it wins the game; likely never gets to be unchecked in a fast format.
- **Cranko (?)** (appears in red-black gameplay; rare that gives bonuses for goblins and other creature types, synergizes with sacrifice) — Taken early in the red-black draft. Hosts are very high on it in a red-black build.

### Colorless/Lands
- **Gold Vein Pick** (2-mana artifact equipment; equipped creature gets +1/+1; when it deals combat damage to a player, create a Treasure; equip 1) — On both hosts' notable commons lists. Excellent early equipment, especially on Healer's Hawk (flying lifelink + treasure = incredible). "Five-color Gold Vein Pick aggro" is a historical reference but the card is just generically strong.

## Signals and seat reads

- **White is overdrafted** — it's the consensus best color. If white is flowing late, be suspicious that others have already loaded up and you're getting the dregs.
- **Black is also popular.** Stab wheeling late (after pick 5-6) is a signal that black is open; conversely, stab disappearing early suggests heavy black competition.
- **Blue-red signals:** If Tolarian Terror or Balmare are coming around late, the lane is open. This is a high-value archetype that not many drafters will be fighting for at a table that respects white.
- **Green signals:** Garrick's Uprising late or multiple Elf-Sworn Giants/Gorehorn Raiders signals green is abandoned. Green is the easiest color to find open because players are skeptical of it.
- **Pivot advice:** The format rewards heavy commitment to one color. If you're deep in white and see Stab or Eaten Alive late in pack one, a black pivot might be correct — but don't split 50/50. Pick your dominant color and lean hard.
- **Red-black specifically:** The threaten effect (Involuntary Employment / "act of treason" effect) at common is a notable mechanic for red-black sacrifice. "Stealing and saccing back on the menu." If you see these cards going late, red-black sacrifice is open.
- **Gold Vein Pick** late signals equipment synergies are underdrafted — white-blue and white-red aggro players should prioritize it even mid-pack.

## TL;DR meta read

- **Format speed: expected faster-than-average.** Both hosts predicted the format would be proactive and snowball-y — "not M21 speed but faster than average." Aggressive curve-outs rewarded; few catch-up mechanics. Ethan noted everything incentivizes proactive play, "almost like a Ikoria-ish" feel.
- **Three macro archetypes dominate the draft landscape:** (1) **Party/tribal decks** (clerics, rogues, warriors, wizards), (2) **Kicker decks** (especially blue-green), and (3) **Landfall decks** (white-green, red-green). These three lanes have little overlap — like Ikoria, you commit to a lane early. Blue-white sits in the middle as a pivot/hub color.
- **Best colors (prediction): Blue #1, Black #2, Green #3, White #4, Red #5.** Blue's commons are all excellent tempo tools; black's removal suite is absurdly powerful; green enables multi-color fixing.
- **Best archetypes (prediction):** Black-white Clerics, Blue-red Wizards, Blue-black Rogues, Blue-green Kicker. Warriors rated a distant fourth among tribal decks.
- **Modal double-faced cards (MDFCs) are the defining innovation.** Every spell that's also a land dramatically improves consistency. "Think of them as lands that have spells on them, not spells with lands on the back." The expensive MDFCs are the best ones — a great late-game spell is also always a land in your opener. Rate the expensive b-minus/b ones very highly.
- **Removal quality is exceptional in black**, with Deadly Allure (?) ("deadly lines" — cheap scalable murder), Blood Chief's Thirst (unconditional at 4 mana or kills 2-drops for 1), Vanquish the Weak (B-minus, kills most things), and Hagra Mauling (destroy target creature MDFC). Red has Thundering Rebuke (deal 4, uncommon) and Roil Eruption (2 mana deal 3 common, very good). **Don't let the format's speed catch you with weak removal.**
- **Party mechanic: assume party of two** as your baseline when evaluating party cards; full party (all four types) is very hard to assemble outside of dedicated multi-color green builds. Blue-white is the easiest hub because it gets the widest spread of creature types (clerics + wizards + rogues/warriors across both colors).

## Format principles

- **Modal double-faced cards (MDFCs) are the core innovation — draft them highly.** Think of them primarily as lands that sometimes have effects, not spells. The expensive ones (b-minus or better) are the best because: (a) when in your opener they are lands, (b) when top-decked they are powerful spells. "The worst of these are still great." Take the expensive MDFCs (Hagra Mauling, Balagan Recovery, Skyclave Cleric (?), Ondu Inversion) over many good spells.
- **Party baseline = 2.** Evaluate party cards as if you have 2 creatures in your party most of the time. Getting to 3 is common; full party (4) is rare outside dedicated multi-color green builds. Do NOT assume full party when evaluating cards.
- **Blue is the best color.** Its commons are almost all excellent tempo tools — every top blue common involves kicker (Into the Royal, Bubble Snare, Cunning Geyser Mage (?), Risen Riptide). Bias toward blue early, especially blue-black or blue-green.
- **Black's removal is top-tier.** Deadly Allure (?) (scaled murder, b-plus), Blood Chief's Thirst (b-plus), Vanquish the Weak (b-minus), Hagra Mauling (a-minus MDFC) — black has more high-end removal than any other color. This is a major reason to be in black.
- **Red's commons are underwhelming but uncommons are strong.** Roil Eruption (common) and Thundering Rebuke (uncommon, 2 mana deal 4) are the red removal highlights; the rest of red's commons were rated mediocre. Red uncommons (Rockslide Sorcerer, Magmatic Channeler, Shatterskull Charger) are excellent.
- **Creature types matter for party but don't force them.** Blue-white naturally assembles the most types. Don't put bad cards in your deck just to "complete the party" — 2 members is enough for most payoffs.
- **Equipment matters more than it looks.** The auto-equip cycle (Maul of the Skyclaves, Ravager's Mace, Relic Axe, Scavenger's Blade) are auto-equip on ETB; equip costs are steep but the "play creature, get immediate boost" pattern is very strong. Maul of the Skyclaves rated a-minus / b-plus — potentially the best equipment in the set.
- **Be cautious with x/1 creatures.** Multiple effects punish 1-toughness: Subtle Strike (minus-1/-1 on one + counter on yours), the red pinging wizard uncommon (Rockslide Sorcerer), and a mini-sweeper uncommon (Cinder Clasm (?)) can all punish x/1s. Hosts explicitly noted wariness of x/1s in this format.
- **Kicker is a sub-engine, not just upside.** Kicker cards reduce spell variance because they're acceptable both early (unkicked) and late (kicked). Vine Gecko, Risen Riptide, Coral Helm Chronicler, and the b-minus/b land MDFCs all reward kicker-density. In blue-green, actively count your kicker spells (8+ is ideal for Vine Gecko / Roost of Drakes payoffs).
- **Landfall needs density.** You want ~9+ landfall creatures for the synergies to run deep. Scale the Heights (?), Harrow, and the returning-land creatures (Kazandu Stomper, Pyroclastic Hellion (?), Tazeem Raptor) create "extra" landfall triggers — but the deck requires commitment. Don't just splash landfall cards; either build around it or don't.
- **Fixing is available in green.** Reclaim the Wastes (lay the land + kicker), Harrow (sacrifice one land for two basics), Tangled Florohedron (mana dork MDFC), and Lithoform Blight (?) (enchant land) let green-based multi-color decks function. The Pathway dual lands (enemy and allied cycles) are c-plus/high picks when in your colors.
- **Curve advice:** One-drops matter! White one-drops (warrior + cleric) enable the auto-equip pattern. Luminarch Aspirant (put counter on a creature each combat step) is a b-rated two-drop. You want 1-2-3 curves with equipment or removal backup — don't ignore cheap drops in favor of powerful top-end.

## Card notes (community consensus)

### White

- **Binding of Life's Grace (?)** ("hierarch's binding" — pacifism variant) — B-minus. Strong for the format; few enchantment punishers. Double-white is a restriction; play only if base-white.
- **Luminarch Aspirant** — B. Puts a +1/+1 counter on any creature at start of combat each turn. "Very powerful" — strong two-drop, near must-kill.
- **Fearless Fledgling** — B. Two-drop 1/1 flyer with landfall +1/+1 counter and gains flying. "Premium two-drop"; b-rated, one of the better early landfall payoffs.
- **Canyon Jerboa** — B-minus. 2-mana 1/2 mouse (!) with landfall pump all your creatures. Fragile but "really impressive" — engine for go-wide landfall.
- **Shepherd of Heroes** — C-plus. Five-mana 3/4 flying cleric; gain 2 life per party member on ETB. "Probably second-best white common" — racing tool with a 3/4 flying body.
- **Maul of the Skyclaves** — A-minus / B-plus. Auto-equips, gives +2/+2, flying, first strike. "Best equipment in the set." Must-answer.
- **Journey to Oblivion** — B. O-Ring effect; costs one less per party member. "Four-mana O-Ring is good." Solid removal.
- **Squad Commander** — B-plus. Four-mana 3/3 warrior; make 1/1 tokens for each party member; team gets indestructible if full party. "Forgiving — even if it just makes two 1/1s it's very good."
- **Sky Clave Apparition** — B / B-minus. Exile non-land non-token CMC 4-or-less ETB; opponent gets x/x illusion token when it leaves. Slightly worse than Journey to Oblivion per Ethan (illusion token risks), but still strong.
- **Kor Blademaster** (?) ("core blade master") — C-plus. 1-mana 1/1 warrior double strike; equipped warriors get double strike. Strong in equipment/warrior builds.
- **Allied Assault** — C-plus. Two-mana instant pump 2 creatures (scales with party). "Plus two plus two to two creatures is quite good."
- **Attended Healer** — B. Four-mana 2/3 cleric; gain life each turn creates cat token; can give another cleric lifelink. "Engine in itself."
- **Kabira Takedown** (MDFC) — B. White instant deals damage equal to creatures you control; flip is tapped white land. "Quite good."
- **McKindy Stampede (?)** ("m'kindy stampede") — B-minus. Three white-white sorcery gives +2/+2 to team; flip is tapped white land. Situational finisher you never have to cast.
- **Akoum Outrider** (?) ("kabir outrider") — C. Three-mana 3/3 warrior; ETB puts +1/+1 on a creature per party member. Good in aggro; usually a 4/4 pump.
- **Practiced Tactics** — D-plus. One-white instant; deals damage = twice party count to attacker/blocker. Too unreliable; "considerably worse than Outflank."
- **Resolute Strike** — C. One-white +2/+2 combat trick; warrior may attach an equipment. Solid one-mana trick.
- **Expedition Healer** — C-plus. Two-mana 2/2 cleric; lifelink and vigilance as long as you control another cleric. "Good two-drop in cleric deck."
- **Tazeem Raptor** — C-plus. Three-mana 2/2 flyer; ETB may return a land to hand (landfall trigger). Winddrake with upside.
- **Farsight Adept** (?) ("far aside to death") — C / C-plus. Three-mana 3/3 wizard; ETB you and opponent each draw. Good in aggro (opponent gets extra card but you curve fast).
- **Prowling Felidar** — C. Four-mana 2/3 vigilance cat; landfall gets +1/+1 counter. Grows but slow to impact the board.
- **Arch-Priest of Iona** (?) ("archpriest of iona") — C-plus. One-mana cleric; power = party count; full party gives another creature +1/+0 and flying. Good one-drop in party-centric white.
- **Drana's Silencer** — C (a mythic MDFC; return any number of creatures with different CMCs from graveyard); rated A-minus as the best MDFC bomb.
- **Emeria's Call** — A. Mythic MDFC; three white-white makes two 4/4 flying angels + non-angel team gets indestructible. "Very good — you'll win a high percentage of games you cast this."
- **Tazri, Beacon of Unity** — B-plus. Five-mana (costs less per party member) 4/6 legendary; activated ability searches top 6 for party members. Rarely costs five mana.

## Signals & seat reads

- **Blue is undersold heading into week one** — hosts expect people to underrate blue's tempo commons (they're not flashy). Bias toward blue in early packs; cards like Into the Royal, Bubble Snare, and Sea-Floor Stalker may wheel more than they should initially.
- **The kicker lane (blue-green) is likely underdrafted early.** Vine Gecko, Coral Helm Chronicler, and the kicker payoffs require reading the table; if you see Vine Gecko go late, blue-green kicker is open.
- **Cleric payoffs are densely supported at common/uncommon in black-white.** If you're seeing Attended Healer, Marauding Blight-Priest, and Scion of the Swarm going late, the cleric lane is open.
- **Warriors are the most underdrafted tribe** — not because they're bad, but because Nahiri (the main payoff) is rare and the common warriors are unremarkable. Being the only warrior drafter gets you cheap warrior cards late.
- **Full party is hard — don't commit early to party-synergy-only picks.** Stonework Packbeast as a "flexible" party-enabler was predicted to be overdrafted by people trying to force party. Prioritize good cards that happen to have relevant creature types over bad cards that fill your party.
- **Pivoting:** If you start WU, you have the most pivot flexibility — can shift into rogues (black), wizards (red), or stay tempo. If you start Sultai (BUG), you can shift into kicker or rogues. Red-black is the hardest to pivot out of.
- **What wheels:** Landfall bulk (Spitfire Lagac (?), Canopy Baloth (?) etc.), the three-mana 3/3 "brute" green common, utility one-drops without types, and less-focused party cards. The combat tricks (Resolute Strike, Might of Murasa (?)) may go late — pick them up rounds 2-3 once your gameplan is set.

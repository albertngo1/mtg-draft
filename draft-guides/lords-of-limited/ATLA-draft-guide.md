## TL;DR meta read

- **Synergy + tempo format with a strong uncommon step-up.** Commons drop off a cliff relative to uncommons. An uncommon misdraft can spike your deck; skimping on uncommons relative to rares is the most common mistake.
- **Speed: midrange tempo.** Not hyper-aggro. The four-drop slot gets clogged fast — be intentional about your curve.
- **"Decks that have Islands and decks that lose to Islands."** Blue is the premier color. If you can be in blue, be in blue.
- **Bending power (retro, decisive): Airbending > Waterbending > Earthbending > Firebending.** Airbending is the best tempo tool (functions like Repulse attached to a creature). Waterbending enables the best control/grind decks. Earthbending wants to go tall first (to 4/4+) before going wide. Firebending is tricky to use well (best on combat instants/tricks, not just pumping your attacker).
- **Color power (retro, decisive): Blue > White > Red > Green > Black.**
- **Archetype power (retro, decisive): UW #1, then UB/UR/RW, then GW/WB/UG, then RG/RB, with GB at the bottom.**
- **Signals are clear — read them and pivot in Pack 1 if needed.** Don't over-commit to a two-color archetype before Pack 2.
- **Never force Shrines.** The hosts lost thousands of gems week 1 trying to build around them.

## Mechanics primer

### Bending (Firebending, Waterbending, Earthbending, Airbending)
Each bending mechanic is a colored mana ability on creatures and spells. They require specific mana types (represented as colored mana symbols) and trigger effects tied to that element.

- **Airbending** — The best bending mechanic in the format. Functions primarily as an offensive tempo tool rather than the defensive tool Wizards previewed it as. The analogy is Repulse: bouncing an opposing blocker and replacing itself. Most UW decks get significant mileage out of airbending effects. Note: enchantment-based removal (auras) gets blown out when the creature gets airbended — factor this into how much aura removal you run.
- **Waterbending** — Second-best. Fuels the grindier UB and UG decks. Enables value chains (mill/exhaust synergies, the Hama the Bloodbender build). Was predicted to be #1 bending pre-release, settled at #2.
- **Earthbending** — Adds +1/+1 counters to permanents. Prioritize going tall (getting a creature or land to 4+ power) before going wide. Key targets: Rumble Arena (a land that gets counters and has vigilance) and Secret Tunnel (rare land that makes a creature unblockable). Toph and Boulder payoffs require the tall setup to function well.
- **Firebending** — Most misunderstood mechanic. Don't just use firebending mana to pump your attacker. The correct usage is fueling combat instants and tricks during the attack step to get blow-outs. Ranked last in bending power.

### Exhaust
Tap this creature and it cannot untap next untap step (costs an extra {T} or permanent effect). Exhaust abilities are powerful but have a real cost. Evaluate whether the payoff justifies the tempo loss of not attacking/blocking next turn.

### Lessons
Lesson is a card subtype. Several spells "learn" — search your sideboard or discard a card and draw. Lesson spells synergize with UR builds especially. Firebending Lesson (1R instant, deal 2 or kicked 5) is the best red common partially because it's a lesson card feeding learn synergies.

### Shrines
Legendary enchantments that care about other Shrines. **Do not build around Shrines.** The payoffs are too fragile and require assembling multiple specific permanents. Ben and Ethan collectively "lost thousands of gems" week 1 trying. If you happen to have 2+ Shrines, you can pivot into them, but never draft Shrines as a plan.

### Clue tokens
Several cards make Clue tokens (2: sacrifice, draw a card). Blue has good clue-making at common/uncommon. Forecasting Fortune Teller (the best blue common) makes Clues.

## ATLA format principles

- **Blue is the best color, by a wide margin.** The sixth and seventh best blue commons were deeper than other colors' best commons. If you're cut from blue, you need a compelling reason (multiple copies of premium red/white uncommons). Default assumption: be in blue.
- **"Decks that have Islands and decks that lose to Islands."** This is the defining format read. Airbending and waterbending are both primarily blue-fueled mechanics.
- **Signals are clear; read them and pivot.** Unlike slow-signal formats, ATLA communicates which lanes are open fairly quickly. If premium blue cards table in Pack 1, you may be cut — check your signals. If premium cards stay in your lane, commit.
- **Never commit to two colors before Pack 2.** Get deep in one color first in Pack 1; add the second color after you've established a clear signal. Forcing Jeskai (blue-white-red) is a reasonable default when you have no direction — it gives the most pivot points.
- **Commons drop off a cliff; uncommons dominate.** The gap between the best uncommon and the fourth-best common in a color is enormous in ATLA. Always take the premium uncommon over the fourth-best common at pick 3–6 of Pack 1.
- **The four-drop slot clogs fast.** Be intentional about curve construction. Three or more four-drops in a row in your curve is a liability.
- **Aura-based removal is risky.** With airbending being the best mechanic, the opponent can bounce the enchanted creature and 2-for-1 you. Watery Grasp is the exception (it's good). Generic aura removal should be viewed skeptically. Path to Redemption is excellent precisely because it's not the "enchant a creature" type of removal (it's more like a pacifism that gains flying) — see Supersessions.
- **Never force Shrines.** Don't be seduced by the "five Shrines, maximum synergy" dream. The deck is unplayable in practice when it stumbles.
- **Earthbending: go tall first.** The mechanic rewards getting a creature (or the Rumble Arena land) to 4+ power before it rewards going wide. Draft a few good earthbend targets, not a hundred small +1/+1 counter enablers. Secret Tunnel (rare, makes a creature unblockable) and Rumble Arena (makes a vigilant land) are the premier earthbend targets.
- **Firebending usage tip.** Firebending mana generated by your creatures doesn't have to go to your attacker. Use it on combat instants and tricks during the attack step for blow-outs. Don't waste firebending by just adding +1/+0 to your attacker when the opponent knows it's coming.
- **Don't miss land drops — more punishing than average.** The format's power level at the top end requires you to hit your land drops reliably. Run 17 lands unless your curve is unusually low. 18 is correct with mana sinks or a high-curve deck.
- **Waterbending control decks want a critical mass.** Don't splash waterbending payoffs — you need enough creatures with the mechanic for it to form a real game plan. UB is the best home for the waterbendering control build.
- **Lessons and Learn synergies are real at density.** The UR Lessons deck requires a specific number of lesson cards in the maindeck/sideboard and learn triggers to function. Don't draft 1 lesson and 1 learn trigger; commit fully or don't. The payoff when assembled is very good.

## Top uncommons

### White
- **Ang the Last Airbender** (3W, 3/2 flyer, airbend one nonland permanent on ETB) — Strong ETB value, triggers airbend synergies, flies.
- **Invasion Reinforcements** (1W, flash, makes a 1/1 ally token) — Flash ambush with a free creature. Better than it looks; the token matters for go-wide payoffs.
- **Earth Kingdom Jailer** (2W, 3/3, exile artifact/creature/enchantment with MV 3+) — Efficient body with clean exile removal attached. 3/3 for 3 is a real stat line.

### Blue
- **Invasion Submersible** (2U, vehicle, ETB bounce a creature, exhaust ability that gives +3/+3 and becomes a 0/0 with +3/+3 counters when W+B tapped) — Flexible vehicle that provides immediate ETB tempo. Exhaust mode is a finisher.
- **Qatara(?) Bending Prodigy** (2U, 2/3, exhaust ability: pay W+B 6 → draw a card) — Solid body with card advantage engine attached. 2/3 is a good toughness for blocking.
- **Benevolent River Spirit** (UU, 4/5 flying ward 2, additional cost W+B 5) — ⚠ See Supersessions. Was initially hyped as a bomb; airbending makes it near-unplayable in practice.

### Black
- **Heartless Act** — Premium hard removal. If this is in ATLA, take it early.
- **Epic Downfall** (1B, sorcery, exile creature with MV 3+) — Clean exile at sorcery. The MV 3+ restriction rarely matters because the threats you need to answer are 3+.

### Red
- **Iro's Demonstration (?)** (1R, sorcery, Lesson — deal 1 to each creature and player, or deal 4 to one target) — Best red uncommon. Modal removal/sweeper as a Lesson enables UR synergies.
- **May Jaded Edge (?)** (1R, 1/3 prowess, exhaust ability — put a double strike counter) — 1/3 for 1R blocks well, prowess triggers off instants/sorceries, double strike counter is a finishing threat.
- **Fire Nation Attacks** (4R, instant, flashback — make two 2/2 red soldiers with Firebending 1) — Provides reach and goes wide. Flashback is real value.

### Green
- **Toph the Blind Bandit** (2G, power = number of +1/+1 counters on your lands, ETB earthbend 2 to lands) — The earthbend payoff. Sets up Rumble Arena and Secret Tunnel for massive threats. Power can reach 4–8+ in the right deck.
- **The Boulder Ready to Rumble** (3G, 4/4, attacks trigger: earthbend X where X = number of creatures you control with power 4+) — Payoff for going tall. Attack with your 4/4 Boulder plus a Rumble Arena counter-land; earthbend an additional creature or land.
- **Earth Kingdom General** (3G, 2/2, ETB earthbend 2, gain life when +1/+1 counters enter any creature you control) — Grindy value engine for the earthbend deck.

### Multi-color (top uncommons)
- **Suki Kyoshi Warrior** (GW, power = number of creatures you control, attacks → makes a 1/1 ally tapped and attacking) — The Selesnya all-in payoff. A 5/5+ that makes a free attacker every combat is a game-ending threat.
- **Jet Freedom Fighter** (RRRWW(?) 3/1, ETB deal damage equal to number of creatures you control to target, dies → distribute +1/+1 counters to surviving creatures) — RW's best gold payoff. ETB kills one threat or chips multiple; death trigger sets up your survivors.
- **Hama the Bloodbender** (UB, 3/3, mill 3 exile a non-creature nonland card, cast it with waterbend X) — The dream UB payoff. Mills, exiles, then lets you cast spells from exile using waterbend mana. Needs density of waterbend creatures.
- **Dragonfly Swarm** (UR, power = ?, flying, ward 1, dies → if Lesson in graveyard draw a card) — UR synergy piece. Flying evasion plus card advantage on death when Lessons are in the graveyard.
- **DY Agents(?)** (BG, 3/4, ETB earthbend 1 to two different permanents, attacks → drain for each creature you control with +1/+1 counters) — BG synergy card. Makes 2 earthbend triggers on ETB, provides life drain as an alternate win condition.

## Golden Egg

**Barrels of Blasting Jelly** (1, artifact, tap for colored mana / sacrifice for 5 damage to target) — The hosts specifically called this out as the format's golden egg: the card that looks innocuous but massively overperforms. It provides mana fixing AND a repeatable damage threat from sacrifice. Every deck wants this.

## Notable rares / bombs

*These rares were called out specifically; not a complete rare tier list.*

- **Rumble Arena** — Legendary land that can receive earthbend counters and has vigilance. A land that attacks/blocks as a vigilant creature with power equal to its counters. Premier earthbend target; take it highly.
- **Secret Tunnel** — Rare land. Makes a creature with +1/+1 counters on it unblockable. The other premier earthbend target alongside Rumble Arena.
- **Kyoshi Battle Fan** — ⚠ See Supersessions. Predicted as a golden-egg sleeper (crash course); evaluated as "basically not a Magic card" by early access.

## Card notes

Deduped across episodes; retro takes win. ⚠ = prediction-vs-reality reversal or notable over/underrated flag.

### White
- **Path to Redemption** — ⚠ Flagged as potentially unplayable in early access (airbending blows it out). Retro verdict: best white common, "Pacifism in 2025." The key insight: it's restrictive but cheap enchantment removal that stops attacking AND blocks — opponents can't just airbend a creature out of it profitably. Take it P1P1-viable.
- **Ang the Last Airbender** — Strong 3W 3/2 flyer. The ETB airbend effect bounces a nonland permanent (your own or opponent's). Excellent in UW tempo.
- **Invasion Reinforcements** — Flash + makes a 1/1 ally token at 1W. Underrated because it creates a creature for go-wide payoffs in Boros/Selesnya.
- **Earth Kingdom Jailer** — Clean 2W 3/3 that exiles MV 3+ permanents. One of white's best uncommons for answering the format's bombs.

### Blue
- **Forecasting Fortune Teller** — Best common in the format. ~1UU 1/3 that makes a Clue on ETB. Enables both the control grind plan and the tempo attack plan. Never wrong to take highly.
- **Invasion Submersible** — Vehicle that ETB bounces a creature (instant tempo). The exhaust mode is a kill condition. Best in UW.
- **Benevolent River Spirit** — ⚠ Initially talked about as a bomb (4/5 flyer ward 2 for UU). Reality: the additional cost condition and the airbending vulnerability make it near-unplayable in most games. Downgraded significantly by retro.
- **Qatara(?) Bending Prodigy** — Solid 2U 2/3 with an exhaust draw-a-card ability. Grindy card advantage in the blue tempo/control shell.

### Black
- **Deadly Precision** — Best black common. 1B sorcery: target creature's controller sacrifices it or pays 4. At common, this is exceptional. Take it early.
- **Epic Downfall** — 1B exile for MV 3+ creatures. Clean and cheap. One of black's best uncommons.
- **Heartless Act** — Premium removal if present. Take early.

### Red
- **Firebending Lesson** — R instant Lesson: deal 2 to any target, or kicked deal 5. The lesson subtype is the bonus — feeds UR learn synergies. Best red common.
- **Iro's Demonstration(?)** — 1R Lesson sorcery: deal 1 to each creature and player (board wipe mode), or deal 4 to one target. Amazing versatility. The best red uncommon.
- **May Jaded Edge(?)** — 1R 1/3 prowess. Exhaust: put a double strike counter on it. Blocks everything toughness-2 or less for free, threatens 6 damage when the double strike counter lands.
- **Fire Nation Attacks** — 4R instant with flashback: make two 2/2 red soldiers with Firebending 1. Provides wide attackers at instant speed plus a late-game threat via flashback.

### Green
- **Raka's Audience(?)** — 1G 2/1 that ramps on ETB. Two-drop mana creatures are always in demand. One of green's best commons.
- **Ostrich Horse** — 2G 3/1: ETB mill 3, then take a basic land card from your graveyard OR put a +1/+1 counter on it. The 3/1 body is relevant for aggressive boards; the ETB triggers self-mill and fixing. Feeds waterbend/earthbend.
- **Toph the Blind Bandit** — The earthbend payoff creature. Power equals the number of +1/+1 counters on your lands. Starts as a 0/3 and grows to 4/3, 6/3, etc. as Rumble Arena and other lands get counters. ETB earthbend 2 to your lands to start the engine.
- **The Boulder Ready to Rumble** — 4/4 for 3G that earthbends X (where X = creatures you control with power 4+) when it attacks. In the right earthbend deck this is a Turn 4 bomb.
- **Earth Kingdom General** — 2/2 for 3G: ETB earthbend 2, then life gain whenever +1/+1 counters are put on creatures you control. The earthbend deck's value engine.
- **Suki Kyoshi Warrior** — GW, power = creatures you control. Attacks trigger: make a 1/1 ally tapped and attacking. In a wide board this is a 6/6 or 8/8 that makes a free attacker every combat. The Selesnya finisher.

### Multi-color
- **Hama the Bloodbender** — UB 3/3. ETB: mill 3, exile a non-creature nonland. You may cast that card using waterbend mana. The UB grind finisher. Needs waterbend density.
- **Dragonfly Swarm** — UR flying ward 1 creature that draws a card when it dies if a Lesson is in your graveyard. The UR build-around piece.
- **Jet Freedom Fighter** — RW 3/1. ETB: deal damage equal to your creature count to target creature or player. Dies: distribute +1/+1 counters equal to its power among remaining creatures. RW bomb.
- **DY Agents(?)** — BG 3/4. ETB earthbend 1 to two different permanents (two separate triggers). Attacks: drain 1 life for each creature you control that has a +1/+1 counter. The BG bridge card.

## Supersessions (early-to-late opinion reversals)

These are cases where the earlier take was explicitly contradicted by later evidence. Listed by category.

1. **Firebending power ranking.** Preview: "Firebending will be ranked last of the four bendings." Early access: "Actually Firebending is hot right now, #2 or even #1." Retro: Back to last — "Firebending is the hardest to use and is the worst bending mechanic." The early access hype was a recency spike from a few explosive games; the retro reversed it. **Settled: Firebending = last.**

2. **Waterbending as #1 bending.** Ben (preview) predicted Waterbending as the clear best bending mechanic. Retro: Waterbending is #2, behind Airbending. **Settled: Waterbending = #2.**

3. **Airbending's role.** Preview: framed as a defensive tempo tool (protect your creatures). Retro: Airbending functions as an aggressive tempo tool, primarily on offense — analogous to Repulse (bounce their blocker, move forward). **Settled: Airbending = offensive, #1 mechanic.**

4. **Blue-White identity.** Wizards' stated identity: "UW Flyers Aggro." Early access / retro: UW is actually best as a board-flood + waterbending/airbending control or evasive tempo deck, not a pure flyers-aggro strategy. The surface archetype framing misled early drafters. **Settled: UW = tempo-control, not aggro.**

5. **Kyoshi Battle Fan as golden egg.** Crash course predicted it as a sleeper breakout card. Early access: "basically not a Magic card." Retro confirmed — it was a miss. **Settled: Kyoshi Battle Fan is not the golden egg; Barrels of Blasting Jelly is.**

6. **Path to Redemption.** Early access: possibly unplayable because airbending bounces the enchanted creature and 2-for-1s you. Retro: best white common, "Pacifism in 2025." The reasoning flipped because the actual gameplay showed opponents can't easily airbend out of it without losing tempo themselves. **Settled: Path to Redemption is premium.**

7. **Blue-Red Lessons deck viability.** Crash course: skeptical (worried about density requirements, lesson count). Early access + retro: the UR Lessons deck is confirmed real and one of the format's best decks when assembled. Ben's personal favorite archetype. **Settled: UR Lessons = A-tier.**

8. **Benevolent River Spirit.** Initially high priority (4/5 flyer ward 2 for UU sounds insane). Reality: the additional mana cost restriction and airbending vulnerability make it near-unplayable in practice. Multiple experienced players abandoned it after first-week testing. **Settled: Benevolent River Spirit = significantly below early hype.**

## Draft strategy notes

### Pack 1, picks 1–4
- Take premium blue cards highly. Forecasting Fortune Teller is a P1P1 candidate. Any blue uncommon from the tier list above warrants an early pick.
- Barrels of Blasting Jelly is a slam P1P1 if you see it — fits any deck, provides fixing and late damage.
- Avoid committing to two colors before pick 5–6 of Pack 1. Take the best card, note the color, read the signals around you.
- Default fallback when directionless: drift toward Jeskai (blue-white-red) for the most pivot points.

### Signals and pivoting
- If premium blue cards table or late-pack blue dries up fast, you are being cut. Pivot to the deepest lane that's open.
- White, red, and green are all viable second colors, but they have different mechanical identities (white = allies/airbend, red = lessons/prowess, green = earthbend/ramp). Know which mechanical package you're trying to support.
- Black is a real color but harder to assemble. It contributes best as a second color to blue (UB control) rather than as a primary color.

### Curve and archetype construction
- UW tempo: curve out with 2–3-drops with airbend/flying, use Path to Redemption + Earth Kingdom Jailer to handle threats, close with Ang and Invasion Submersible.
- UB control: prioritize waterbend creatures and reach density. Hama the Bloodbender is the dream top end. Use Deadly Precision and Epic Downfall to clear the path.
- UR Lessons: you need at minimum 6–8 lesson spells (maindeck + sideboard) and 4+ learn triggers. Iro's Demonstration and Firebending Lesson are the core. Dragonfly Swarm is the payoff creature.
- RW aggro: low curve, fill with Invasion Reinforcements, Path to Redemption, red tricks. Close with Jet Freedom Fighter.
- GW wide: draft for creature count. Suki needs 5+ creatures to be threatening. Back her up with Earth Kingdom Jailer and Toph for the midgame.

### Fixing and mana
- Three-color decks are viable but require explicit fixing. Barrels of Blasting Jelly is the primary fixer at common.
- Fixing is not abundant — be conservative with three-color ambitions unless you have Barrels early.
- Splash one color, not two, unless your fixing is exceptional.
- Run 17 lands; run 18 with high curve or mana sinks.

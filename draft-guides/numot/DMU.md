# DMU (Dominaria United) — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Two dominant archetypes at end-of-format: **Domain** (big creatures that scale with basic land types) and **White-black defenders/Wingmantle Chaplain** (the "stain on the format").
- Domain can be drafted as many color combinations: green-black, green-black-splash-X, or full 5-color. The key is getting enough dual lands (the "duels" — the 10 gain lands) and Sojourners.
- Red-white aggro is a legitimate archetype built around Enlister effects (Herbalist, enlisting creatures that get +1/+1 when you attack with another creature). Destroy Evil is called out as one of the best cards you can have in the RW deck; Kenji lamented not seeing a single one in one draft.
- Format is deep into its run at time of recording (156+ drafts); Kenji was playing for Mythic rank throughout.
- Two-mana 5/5s ("Root Walla" — two green mana, gets +2/+2 for each land type you have, effectively 5/5 with 3 land types) were MVPs in Domain lists.
- Wingmantle Chaplain (black-white) is noted as one of the most broken cards in the format — Kenji says "this is the one stain on the format."

## Archetypes
- **Green-black Domain:** Core pieces are Sojourner (3-mana 1/1 that gives 2 life when a land enters and has Domain pumping), Root Walla (2-mana scales with domain), Brawlers(?)/Nashoba Brawler(?) (2-mana 2/2 or bigger with reach/trample depending on domain), Shadow Prophecy (draw 2 scry 2, domain), ERG(?) (creature that gets bigger with domain), and Urban Repossession / Herb Org Repossession (recursive reanimation). Support with Bite Down (fight spell), Tail Swipe, Tribute to Urza(?) / Extinguish the Light (removal).
  - The dream curve: tapland T1, tapland T2, untapped land + Sojourner T3.
  - Build 8 green / 8 black sources; Sacred Peaks (colorless domain land) fills the off-color slot and is worth running even in 2-color decks for domain value.
- **Red-white aggro:** 5-6 two-drops (Herbalist, Cavalier, Sky Rider(?), etc.) + combat tricks. Key cards: Heroic Charge (pump all attackers), Resolute Reinforcements (makes 2 tokens), Sky Rider Patrol(?), Strikeforce Team(?) (makes tokens with enlist). Enlist enablers — Herbalist is called out as "insane in this deck" with many enlisters. Destroy Evil is the essential removal spell; lacking it is a big problem.
- **Blue-black control/Domain:** Can run Sojourner + domain package, splash blue for Essence Scatter, Thallarian Terror(?), Temporal Firestorm(?). War Leech (4-mana 7/4(?) or scales to 8/8 with 3+ Sojourners) is a payoff finisher.
- **Namata(?) / Primeval Warden(?) deck:** Namata is a key rare — whenever a non-token creature an opponent controls dies, put a 1/1 token on it. Pairs with Bone Splinters and soul Tender for recursion value. Kenji drafted this multiple times noting "I've had double Namata in at least three drafts."

## Card interactions & combos
- **Namata + Bone Splinters:** Sacrifice your creature to destroy theirs. Namata makes a 1/1 saproling when theirs dies. Then if you have Soul Tender you can eventually return the Namata.
- **Root Walla + Repossession (Herb Org):** Repossession returns two permanents. With a Root Walla in the graveyard you can return it plus a land for full domain, making Root Walla a 5/5 immediately. "Repossession is already two Root Wallas you know."
- **Sojourner x3 + War Leech:** Three Sojourners make War Leech a 4-mana 8/8 on the cheap. Kenji took War Leech specifically for this synergy.
- **Brawler(?) + Bite Down / Tail Swipe:** 2-mana Nashoba Brawler has reach + trample at high domain, giving you a fight creature that's hard to trade with.
- **Wingmantle Chaplain + Barricade + Conscripts:** Chaplain makes birds each time another defender enters, then Conscripts(?) sac for value and return. "This is the one stain on the format."

## Card tips
- **Root Walla:** Take as 2-drop of choice in domain builds. Even without full domain, pumping it to 4/4 is a great rate. MVPs of Kenji's domain drafts.
- **Sojourner:** Take "every single dual we see." Three Sojourners is the target. Each one gives domain, life gain, and makes War Leech bigger.
- **Dual lands / gain lands:** Draft every single one you see. Missing lands late means your domain never fires. Kenji regretted not taking duals when he could wheel them.
- **Sacred Peaks:** Worth running in 2-color domain decks as a weird "colorless" source that still adds domain count. "I don't think I cut Sacred Peaks as weird as it looks in this deck."
- **Nashoba Brawler(?):** Very strong 2-drop. Kenji takes it highly, notes three Brawlers in a pool is "pretty freaking sick."
- **Shadow Prophecy:** Good draw spell in domain. Also gives delirium points.
- **Herbalist (red-white):** "Really really good in red-white when you have the enlisters." With 6-8 enlisters, Herbalist's team pump is substantial.
- **Destroy Evil:** Called out as "one of the best cards you can have in these decks" for RW. Missing it is a major hole.
- **Extinguish the Light / Tribute to Urburzur(?):** Black removal; essential for domain decks. Kenji noted not seeing any extinguishes or tributes in one draft as "really bad signs."
- **Urban Repossession / Herb Org Repossession:** Recursive tool. Use modes: return creature + land (getting back a domain piece is huge). Also can kick for more value.
- **Lagomos(?) (RW rare):** "Actively bad for them" when he had Namata — made tokens that died to Namata repeatedly. Interesting combo.

## Pitfalls
- **Drafting domain without enough lands:** If you can't wheel dual lands, the 3-Sojourner dream collapses. Prioritize lands heavily in pack 2-3.
- **Cutting removal to run more creatures:** Domain decks can be tempted to cut removal for more fatties. Kenji noted "it's almost better to have just value cards instead of removal anyways" but immediately ran into opponents with bombs.
- **Failing to hit domain:** With Sacred Peaks and off-color lands, you can often play "pseudo colorless" spells while still counting toward domain. Don't skip these lands.
- **White-black Defenders / Wingmantle Chaplain:** Have some answer (Namata, big ground blockers). If they get 2 chaplains, you often lose.
- **Bone Splinters without a Soul Tender:** Splinters requires a creature sacrifice; without Soul Tender or Repossession recursion, you're losing bodies freely.
- **Keeping hands without forests in green-heavy decks:** Kenji mulliganed several hands with 8 forests in the deck that had no green sources. Said "I think better of it."
- **Playing Namata without protection:** If opponent kills Namata immediately, the deck stalls. Lead with the two-drops first.

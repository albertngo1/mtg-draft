# OTJ — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Outlaws of Thunder Junction (OTJ) is a crime-matters format with a strong multicolor/5-color archetype enabled by Desert fixing (Oasis Gardener, Bandit's Hall, Mirage Mesa, Lush Oasis, Treasure Dredger). Kenji repeatedly drafted 4-5 color "good stuff" and called it his preferred approach: "we're doing five color good stuff and that is just the way it's going to be."
- Railway Brawler is the most impactful Mythic in the format by far — Kenji opened 14-15 of them across his drafts and called it his "#1 opened and played Mythic" by roughly 2:1 over any other. Dance of the Tumbleweeds + Railway Brawler is the format's signature combo.
- The format has enormous bomb rares (Bristly Bill, Tumblewag, Gitrog, Bonnie Paul, Marchesa). Early green bombs can spiral out of control — specifically Tumblewag into Bristly Bill was described as "some of the most absurd early game bombs in the format."
- Red-White Boros aggro is a real and consistent archetype but can be mirror-matched easily. "Draft what's open versus draft what's fun."
- Blue-Black crime is a strong synergy deck (Marchesa, Blood Hustler, Jace, Villainous Wealth, Lazav, Intimidation Campaign, Terminal Agony).

## Archetypes
- **5-Color Good Stuff / Crime (base Blue-Black splashing)**: Kenji's preferred archetype. Uses Oasis Gardeners, Bandit's Halls, and deserts to fix for 4-5 colors. Key payoffs: Marchesa (value machine off crimes), Villainous Wealth, Bonnie Paul, Geralf (?), Blood Hustler (repeatable crime pinger), Intimidation Campaign. "Just need to find a good white card worthy of splashing." Aims to accumulate 8-10 crime triggers in the deck.
- **Red-Green Midrange/Stompy**: Second most-drafted by Kenji. Railway Brawler is the centerpiece — it makes all non-token creatures you control enormous. Dance of the Tumbleweeds ramps to it. Surewild Shot (?) gives the whole team trample once a large creature ETBs. Spikewoods Paladin, Dance of the Tumbleweeds, Clear Shot (?) removal, Savage Smash. "Red-green just good beats."
- **Boros (Red-White) Aggro**: Consistent BO3 option. Roving Rancher Homestead, Peddler, Vigilante, Kyé Keeper (?), Sharpshooter, Holy Cow (creature pump), Lasso of the Longarm (?), Eartha-Jo (?) for token doubling. Came together late in a draft ("we just salvaged huge we have four two-drops now"). Knife-Point and Deadlock Duelist (?) subarchetype exists but needs specific pieces.
- **Blue-Black Crime**: Marchesa as repeatable card draw engine off crimes. Blood Hustler, Jace (budget and regular), Terminal Agony (madness outlet with Jace), Consuming Ashes, Lazav (shapeshifter), Jagged Barons (?), Rope Master (?), Red Rock Sentinel (card draw/filtering). Guild Summit for fixing (?).

## Card interactions & combos
- **Railway Brawler + Dance of the Tumbleweeds**: Dance makes a token, then Railway Brawler makes it huge (Dance enters as a 4/4, Brawler makes it a 10/10 or larger with trample/haste). Kenji said "Dance of the tumbleweeds into Railway Brawler" is "kind of incredible." Explicit sequence: play Dance for a land, token enters; if Brawler is already out, the token gets +6/+6 or more and has trample/haste — attacks immediately for massive damage.
- **Brawler + Surewild Shot (?) + any large creature**: All creatures get trample once a large-enough one enters. Kenji made a 30/30 with trample/haste via this combo ("30/30 with trample/haste on like turn six").
- **Marchesa + Oasis Gardener / Bandit's Hall**: Every crime triggers Marchesa's "draw a card, lose a life." Multiple Gardeners and Bandit's Halls let you commit crimes cheaply (sacrifice land/pay life). "Marchesa just chains off so hard."
- **Blood Hustler + repeated crime triggers**: Blood Hustler pings (?) and grows; with 8-10 crime cards in the deck it fires off constantly. In one game: "she's at six and the Hustler's activated again on their turn — GG."
- **Jace + Terminal Agony (madness)**: Jace loots (draw/discard), letting you cast Terminal Agony at madness cost immediately. "We have the Jace with the Terminal Agony so we can even madness this out."
- **Brawler + Spinewoods Paladin / double Paladin (legendary legend trigger Paladin ?)**: Kenji mentioned plotting a Paladin then on the following turn playing Brawler into Paladin to make a "10/10 trampler." The legendary-trigger Paladin copies ETB triggers of legendary creatures.
- **Villainous Wealth + graveyard full of spells**: Hitting 7-9 Mana with a stocked opponent graveyard leads to "we hit a bunch of land damn it" but also "holy crap we hit hard." Best in the Blue-Black crime deck where you can stall long enough.
- **Geralf + Blue-Black spell chain**: Geralf (makes zombies whenever you cast instants/sorceries?) led to one game where Kenji made "four or five 4/4 zombies off the Geralf chain." Described as "insane crime deck" draw.

## Card tips
- **Spirewoods Armadillo (Spikewoods Armadillo ?)**: "Best card by far. Very, very good green card. Very hard to pass up." First-pickable. Also cycles, which gives it upside in multicolor decks.
- **Marchesa**: Initially Kenji was "bored of the green-white mount deck" and took Marchesa as a fun pick. At end of format treated it as a premium rare — "Marchesa is the fun pick-up." Requires crimes to chain but pays off immediately.
- **Mirage Mesa**: Better than Carnation Vista because Mesa is a desert for Desert's Due synergy. "Mirage Mesa is a desert for synergies."
- **Oasis Gardener**: A 3/3 for 3 that also fetches a land of any type when it ETBs — core fixer for 5-color. Kenji took multiple copies and called them foundational.
- **Bandit's Hall**: Sacrifice a land, commit a crime and draw a card (?). Low opportunity cost in multicolor decks with excess lands.
- **Desert's Due**: Strong removal that also commits a crime. Requires a desert but Mirage Mesa / Lush Oasis / Bandit's Hall all count. "Definitely strong enough removal."
- **Homestead Roving Rancher (?)**: "When you high roll with the Homestead it feels like you can't lose — just a 3/3 for two that draws extra cards." High priority in Boros.
- **Lasso of the Longarm (?)**: Gives a creature double strike while attacking. Makes a 1/1 Vigilante into a 3/3 double strike every turn starting on turn 3. "Vigilant with the lasso is a 3/3 double strike every turn starting on turn three — kind of great."
- **Great Train Heist**: Untaps all your creatures and gives First Strike. In the late game with 5+ power creatures, this is near-unstoppable. "If you have any reasonable board state all right nice round-one victory."
- **Cunning Coyote (?)**: Dodge (flash in) to ambush or protect a creature. Kenji considered but ultimately passed it; noted it "wrecks combat" with multiple creatures.
- **Return the Favor**: Strong sideboard card in BO3 — "Return the Favor as a sideboard card was really good for us in the last Arena Open."
- **Contagion Engine**: "Too slow in this format, it's like a fine sideboard card if your opponent's playing Outlaws because they will often have a bunch of small creatures."

## Pitfalls
- **Holding creatures too long waiting for a crime**: Kenji punished himself by holding Jagged Barons for a crime trigger while Tumblewag got extra counters. "I held the Jagged Barons for a crime and now we're letting the Tumblewag get an extra counter." If you can't commit a crime in 1-2 turns, just play the creature.
- **Not prioritizing deserts highly enough**: The first 5-color deck had too few deserts and couldn't reliably trigger Desert's Due or Omenpath Journey. "If this had like two more deserts I think it would be insane."
- **Drafting triple Knife-Point without Deadlock Duelists**: Kenji took 3 Knife-Points but "I don't think we saw a single Duelist" — the synergy never materialized. Don't over-commit to a sub-theme without the key pieces.
- **Not running enough 2-drops in aggro**: The Boros deck initially had zero 2-drops and was described as unplayable until pack 3 salvation. "We have no creature plays before turn three — oh man." In OTJ aggro, 2-drops are essential for not dying to faster decks.
- **Overpaying on Primal Might / combat tricks**: In the Blue-Green deck, Kenji pumped his creature one extra with Primal Might, which let the opponent survive at 1 life. "All because I pumped up one extra on the Primal Might." Precision math matters in combat-trick-heavy formats.
- **Playing Stable Master on turn 2 instead of Mana Dork**: "Of course you play the mana dork on turn two not stupid Sentry. Do they have Arcmage's Charm in their hand?" Prioritize ramping and fixing over defensive 2-drops.
- **Attacking with everything when opponent has untapped mana and cards in hand**: Multiple games went wrong when Kenji or opponents committed to aggressive attacks without accounting for tricks. "They have five cards in their hand so that's a lot of interaction."

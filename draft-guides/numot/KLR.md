# KLR — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
Kaladesh Remastered (KLR) is a fast-to-medium format built around energy and artifacts/vehicles. Energy is a big macro-mechanic that rewards having a high density of energy producers. Vehicles are central — creatures that crew vehicles get free value since you don't expose the vehicle to removal when not crewing. Red-white aggro (vehicles + tap synergies) is a top strategy. Blue-white fliers with cheap vehicles is strong. Black-white token go-wide is reliable. Green-based energy midrange/ramp has the highest ceiling but requires specific payoffs. Kenji notes "red green is the way to go for mass energy production" and "the teamer (?) colors are the way to go." Black-red aggro is explicitly called out as a strong aggro deck. Flash-back context: when the format relaunches, opponents forget the format so "you can get some really busted decks."

## Archetypes
- **Black-White Tokens (shown extensively)**: Hidden Stockpile + Underhanded Designs is the core engine. Hidden Stockpile: at end of step if a permanent left the battlefield, make a servo; sac a creature to scry 1. Underhanded Designs: pay 1 when an artifact enters to drain opponent for 1; sac for 2 to kill a creature. Multiple servo-producers (Servo Exhibition, Poisoner, Puzzle Knot) make these engines fire every turn. Dawn Feather Eagle is the top-end flier. Fairgrounds Warden is the tempo play. Die Young kills creatures by paying energy.
- **Red-White Vehicles / Night Market Lookout (featured extensively)**: Night Market Lookout (1/1: when it becomes tapped, drain opponent for 1) + cheap vehicles. Sky Skiff (2-mana 2/3 flier, crew 1) is the #1 vehicle. Mobile Garrison (3-mana crew 2: untaps a creature when it attacks) is the #2 — specifically combos with Lookout by untapping it after crewing so you can crew again. Spire Infiltrator (?) (when it becomes tapped, deal 1 damage) is a copy-of-Lookout effect. Pia Nalaar provides artifact tokens and a pump ability. Pyro Helix pings small creatures. Kenji says "these are the most important" pointing at Sky Skiffs; aim for 4 vehicles and 3–4 tap-drain creatures.
- **Blue-White Fliers**: Swooper (either Swooper — 3-mana 2/2 flier that makes a 1/1 energy token on attack) is "one of Blue's best commons." Cloud Blazer (?) (5-mana 2/2 flier: ETB gain 2 life and draw 2 cards) is the bomb. Spire Patrol (3-mana 2/3 flier: ETB tap a creature, it doesn't untap next turn) is strong tempo.
- **Green-Blue Energy**: Aether Hub / Servant of the Conduit for fixing. Rogue Refiner (3-mana 2/3 draw a card get 2 energy) is "pretty good." Long-Tusk Cub (2-mana 2/2: pay 2 energy put a +1/+1 counter on it) is "very good — what a freaking two-drop." Aetherworks Marvel (?) is rare payoff.
- **Electrostatic Pummeler Meme (attempted)**: Pummeler (3-mana 1/1 artifact: tap pay 3 energy, double power until end of turn; enter with energy = number of artifacts) + Built to Smash (attacking creature +3/+3, if artifact gains trample) = potential one-shot. Trophy Mage tutors it and the Dynavolt Tower (3-mana artifact: cast instant/sorcery to get 2 energy; pay 5 to deal 3 damage). Era of Innovation (enchantment: whenever you cast or get a token that's artifact or artificer draw a card pay 1 energy, then sack for 3 cards — ?). "Meme deck, we drafted for fun" — Kenji got one winning game out of three with the pummeler combo.
- **Green-Black / Multi-color Midrange with Herald of Anguish**: Herald of Anguish (7-mana improvise 5/5 flier: end step opponent discards, sac artifact to give creature -2/-2). Daring Demolition kills vehicles and creatures. Blossoming Defense protects key threats. Needs Puzzle Knots for improvise synergy. "I like how we opened both free-jam Regent (?) and Herald of Anguish."

## Card interactions & combos
- **Night Market Lookout + Mobile Garrison**: "A little bit of a combo right — the Mobile Garrison untaps the Night Market Lookout after it's crewed and then you can crew again." Result: crew Garrison, it attacks, untaps Lookout, crew again, two drain triggers per turn.
- **Hidden Stockpile + Underhanded Designs**: Each servo Stockpile makes triggers Designs (if you pay 1) for a drain. Sacking the servo (to scry with Stockpile) counts as a permanent leaving — so Stockpile self-triggers to make a replacement servo. Engine is nearly self-sustaining and drains for 1 each cycle. "Stockpile and Designs kind of goes hard — they're pretty close to rare quality."
- **Inventor's Goggles + Artificer creature**: Goggles auto-equips to artificers as they enter. "Turn one goggle, turn two poisoner — immediately self-equips. Turns into a 2/3 death-toucher. That's great." The artificer list includes Poisoners, Artisans, Swooper.
- **Poisoner (Aether Poisoner — 2/1 deathtouch, ETB 2 energy; when attacks pay 2 energy make 1/1 servo) + energy sources**: Each servo makes attacks threatening; with Goggles on it, it becomes a 3/2 death-toucher that makes tokens.
- **Electrostatic Pummeler + Built to Smash**: "Cast on your pummeler, it gets trample and turns into a 4/4 base, then if you can pump it just once or twice — 8/8 or 16/16 trampler." Requires pump to be available before attack.
- **Trophy Mage + Pummeler/Dynavolt Tower**: Trophy Mage tutors exactly three-mana artifacts; grabs either the Pummeler or the Tower. "That is so sick for our deck."
- **Dynavolt Tower + instants/sorceries**: Each spell gives 2 energy; 5 energy = 3 damage to any target. With unbridled growths and high-spire infusions firing cheaply, the Tower can deal 3 damage every 2–3 turns.
- **Fenhaller (?) (large improvise creature: can't be blocked by artifact creatures) + vehicle-heavy meta**: Works as a finisher in artifact-dense environments.
- **Sky Skiff + Night Market Lookout**: 2-mana 2/3 flier crew 1 — crew with Lookout, drain for 1, attack for 2/3 flying. The ideal opening two turns of the Night Market deck.

## Card tips
- **Either Swooper (Aether Swooper — 2/1 flier makes 1/1 servo on attack for 2 energy)**: "One of Blue's best commons... I say it every single time I see it." Take high.
- **Voltaic Brawler (2-mana 3/2 trample haste, exile energy on attacks)**: "Turn two Voltaic Brawler is real good" — one of the format's best two-drops. Red-green's aggro cornerstone.
- **Revoke Privileges (2-mana enchantment: pacifism that also stops crewing vehicles)**: "This one stops the creature you're enchanting from crewing vehicles. Often times pacifism effects are awkward in a vehicle format — but this one stops it." Premium white removal.
- **Daring Demolition (4-mana: destroy artifact or creature)**: "The bread-and-butter black removal spell in the format. Kills vehicles, kills creatures, even if the vehicle isn't crewed." Always maindeck.
- **Die Young (2-mana: target creature gets -X/-X where X = energy you paid, you get 1 energy)**: Strong with energy producers; note it is a sorcery — sequencing matters.
- **Harness Lightning (2-mana instant: deal X damage to target creature where X is energy paid)**: Good removal; can hit big creatures with enough energy banked.
- **Servant of the Conduit (2-mana 2/2: ETB 2 energy; tap pay 1 energy for any color mana)**: Fixing plus pressure; "fantastic."
- **Long-Tusk Cub (2-mana 2/2: pay 2 energy put +1/+1 counter on it)**: "What a freaking two-drop" — grows quickly with energy; forces trade or races past opponent.
- **Puzzle Knot (2-mana artifact: ETB get 2 energy, sac pay 1 get 2 more energy)**: Six energy for two mana; core improvise enabler and energy filler. "Puzzle knots are six energy by themselves."
- **Night Market Lookout (1/1: when becomes tapped, opponent loses 1 life)**: Entire archetype built around it. Worth picking up multiples; needs cheap vehicles (especially Sky Skiff) to trigger without attacking.
- **Sky Skiff (2-mana artifact, crew 1: 2/3 flier)**: "Most important vehicle for the Night Market deck." Also independently strong in any deck.
- **Renegade Map (1-mana artifact: sac to search for basic land)**: Shaves a land, enables revolt, fixes mana, counts for improvise. Run in any deck that wants to splash.
- **Unbridled Growth (1-mana enchant land: tap for any color + mana + card-draw when sacked)**: "Very fun" — enables revolt and fixing simultaneously; run in any green deck that splashes.
- **Fabrication Module (3-mana artifact: whenever you get 1+ energy, put a +1/+1 counter on a creature)**: "So nice" — every energy source is also a counter; exponentially grows a threat over time.
- **Fretwork Colony**: "I would not recommend playing that card." Only good turn 2 and can never block — a liability otherwise.

## Pitfalls
- **Not revoking / arresting the key flier early**: Kenji lost a game "why did I not revoke privilege the Windkin Rider — that is a question I will be asking myself when I lose to it." Identify the must-answer threat and answer it before it gets out of hand.
- **Using Underhanded Designs aggressively and then losing the drain engine**: In one game, using the Designs sac ability too early removed the drain trigger for all future artifacts. "That use of the designs might have cost us."
- **All-in Pummeler with one interaction piece available**: "All in pummeler and one piece of interaction devastated us." The meme deck folds instantly to Select for Inspection (?) (1-mana bounce). Don't go full-combo without a protection piece.
- **Running 16 lands in a Pummeler deck**: "I said I was going to go down to 15 lands and I didn't — we saw 15 of 16 lands." With triple Unbridled Growth and Renegade Maps, 15 lands is correct in light-fixing decks.
- **Passing Voltaic Brawler when in red-green**: Kenji started out of the red-green lane and regretted missing multiple Brawlers and Puma Outriders (?) that went past. "I should have been taking these red cards."
- **Not having enough one-and-two drop early game for Night Market deck**: "I think we had a little bit too much top end — we needed a couple more early game plays especially the Skiffs." Sky Skiff is the critical enabler; without it the Lookouts just sit as 1/1s.
- **Attacking with Night Market Lookout when you don't need to**: Should have crewed the Garrison with Lookout and attacked with Garrison only — untaps Lookout for another crew. "I should have not attacked and that would have gained one back as well."
- **Being too greedy with vehicle sequencing**: "I missed lethal playing too quickly here — I could have played the Welding Fest Engineer, crewed The Garrison with the engineer, attacked with all." Slow down and count damage before passing.

# ECL — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Lorwyn Eclipsed is a midrange-to-grindy format. Tribal synergies (Elves, Goblins, Kithkin, Merfolk/Faeries) dominate, with a heavy-green Vivid/multicolor splash strategy also viable.
- Red is consistently open — Kenji notes "red is really open" across multiple drafts and often ends in base-red splashing 2–3 colors.
- Goblins should be played as a **grindy** deck, not an aggro deck; players who try to rush with Goblins misuse the archetype.
- Kithkin goes wide with pump effects; the scary uncommons are Eclipsed Kithkin variants and the 0/5 (?) that can give trample.
- Four wins is the draft-sustainability threshold for Premier Draft gem payback.

## Archetypes
- **Red-Black Goblins** (primary): Grindy midrange built around Curse Crafters, Gristle Glutton (rummage / card selection), Champion of the Whatever (?), Soul Immolation (sorcery: deal X to each opponent and each creature they control, X ≤ greatest toughness among creatures), Eclipse Bogart. Wants card advantage engines (Glutton, Burning Curiosity) to avoid running out of gas. Spry and Mighty (?) as a high-variance top-end pump spell. Blight Rot for minus-counter shenanigans.
- **Green-Black-Blue Elves** (with Marilyn / "Merilyn" = Deranged Hermit-style fairy lord?): Self-mill via Midnight Tilling, recursion package (Unbury, Grave Shifter changelings, Eulogist, Pennant). Dundulan Weaver as a consistent 2-drop. Marilyn tutors/triggers off elves entering from graveyard. Changelings fill elf/shapeshifter slots for synergy counting.
- **Blue-Red Elementals** (Vivid/flash): Built around Ashling's Command (instant — kill creature or artifact/enchantment, draw 2/make 2 Treasures depending on mode); wants Flamekin Zealots, Colrath Zealots, Blossom Bind, Kindle the Inner Flame, guild weavers. Gloom Ripper changeling = elemental for Behold triggers. Copy-effect creatures synergize with Ashling's Command lines.
- **Green-White Kithkin** (appears open in some pods): Creature flood + pump; Brigid (3/2 with transformability) is near-bomb rare.
- **Blue-Green Vivid / 5-color**: Possible but signals rarely align; Wicker Maw and guild weavers as fixing anchors.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Gristle Glutton + Blight Rot**: Glutton rummages; Blight Rot puts minus-one/minus-one counters. Glutton with a Blight Rot counter on it becomes a better pump target for Spry and Mighty because the differential is larger (negative power counts in the calculation).
- **Spry and Mighty + creatures with blight counters**: Spry and Mighty gives all your creatures +X/+X where X = power of your highest-power creature minus power of each other creature. Creatures with minus counters (power 0 or negative) create a massive differential, turning Spry and Mighty into a devastating pump.
- **Noggle Robber + Emptiness / Evoke creatures**: Noggle Robber (?) in play means evoked creatures return to hand instead of being sacrificed, enabling repeated evoke value.
- **Soul Immolation + Champion of the [?]**: Stack counters on the Champion via tribal activations before firing Immolation; the Champion's exit briefly lowers the X threshold, so time the Immolation before the Champion leaves (or after setting up a high counter count elsewhere).
- **Marilyn / Merilyn + Dundulan Weaver + Grave Shifter**: Dundulan mills on ETB; Grave Shifter gets back two creatures; Marilyn triggers off elves entering from graveyard for card selection. Loop generates significant value each turn cycle.
- **Ashling's Command + Gloom Ripper changeling**: Ripper is a changeling so it counts as an elemental for Behold (?) triggers on Ashling's Command targets; copy effects on the Ripper create three Rippers off one trigger ("you make one, I make three").
- **Pennant + Elf/Goblin/Changeling graveyard**: Pennant can name the relevant tribe and buy back key pieces (Grave Shifter, Grub); in Vivid/elemental builds naming Elementals retrieves Ashling's Command.

## Card tips
- **Goblin Glutton** (Gristle Glutton?): One of the best Goblin cards; the rummage effect is crucial for filtering draws. Kenji considers running up to three copies. Without card draw/filter, Goblin decks "run out of action."
- **Morcant's Eyes** (More Cat's Eyes? — tribal scry/draw): Described as an insane uncommon for Elves, sets up draws and acts as a late-game win condition. First-pickable in a weak pack.
- **Unbury**: Best card in its pack by a wide margin; especially powerful in Elves for graveyard recursion but good in any tribe-heavy deck. "Not close."
- **Cinder Strike**: Best or near-best red common; instant-speed kills most relevant creatures.
- **Boulder Dash**: Premium red removal; can be splash-worthy. Three copies is too many — prioritize the first two.
- **Assert Perfection** (?): Near-bomb rarity; take over most other cards.
- **Goblin Sharpshooter**: Old-card reprint; pings for one whenever a creature dies and untaps with each death. Devastating with deathtouch (Scarblades' Malice?). Kenji had never seen it in play before finally facing it — respect it as a near-game-winner.
- **Blossom Bind**: Cheap removal/tap enchantment; Kenji runs it happily in Vivid builds.
- **Midnight Tilling** (self-mill instant/sorcery): Enables Unbury and graveyard payoffs; run 1–2 in Elf builds, which allows cutting to 16 lands.
- **Burning Curiosity**: Draw engine in Goblins; not listed as essential but fills the card-advantage gap Goblins struggles with.
- **Eclipse Bogart**: Late-pick eclipsed creature that signals Goblins is open; getting one 8th pick is a strong signal.
- **Guttersnipe Gang** (?): Good Goblin 4-drop when you have synergies for the 1/1 counter package; weaker without counters to exploit.
- **Iron Shield Elf**: Fine two-drop filler, cuts when better options arrive.
- **Oversized Goblin Prankster** (?): Weak late-game draw, cut easily.

## Pitfalls
- **Going aggro with Goblins**: The tribe lacks the raw aggro tools of historical Goblin decks; playing it as a pure rush deck runs out of gas. Build grindier with rummage, recursion, and removal.
- **Lacking card advantage in Goblins**: Deck frequently loses without Gristle Gluttons or Burning Curiosity. Plan for it in the draft.
- **Splashing Spry and Mighty without fixing**: Kenji repeatedly notes poor fixing for a white splash; Noggle Robber alone is insufficient. Need 2–3 sources minimum.
- **Passing Nameless Inversion for tribal creatures early**: Kenji regrets passing Nameless Inversion for Grub in the Elves draft — instant-speed versatile removal (changeling) outvalues synergy creatures in most cases.
- **Over-raring the removal suite**: Three Boulder Dashes is too many; creature count suffers. Prioritize creatures once removal hits ~4–5 pieces.
- **Not reading mana issues in keeps**: Several games lost to drawing too many of one color with a two-color deck. Mulliganing risky three-landers is correct even though "mulligans are so bad in limited."
- **Embracing expensive creatures with no board presence**: Playing a six-drop into no board is a losing line; if behind, get on board even with sub-optimal two-drops.
- **Goblin Sharpshooter with deathtouch**: If the opponent has deathtouch (Scarblades' Malice), they can one-for-one your entire board. Kill it ASAP; no answer usually means GG.

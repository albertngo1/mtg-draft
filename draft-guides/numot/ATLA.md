# ATLA — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?). Set is Avatar: The Last Airbender (Quick Draft format, bot-draft environment).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Quick Draft (bot-draft) environment for all 4 VODs.** This is critical context: bots do not value shrines highly and will pass them very late even if they're opened. The format experience described is almost entirely bot-draft, which differs from human premier draft.
- **Shrines are the busted build-around.** Kenji explicitly says every time: "I say it every time I do these quick drafts — the bots don't take the shrines. So as long as they are opened, you're going to get them." The caveat is that shrines often simply are not opened in a given draft. This explains about half the VODs going shrineless.
- **If shrines ARE opened you commit hard to them.** "I always say it: if you are going to go shrines, you have to commit. And you can't be scared of not getting any." A full shrine deck with Earthbender Ascension + 3+ shrines is the most powerful possible configuration.
- **5-color good stuff is the natural fallback** when shrines don't appear. AKR has abundant fixing: Ang's Journey, Shared Roots, Manador (?), Divide Guide, Cycle of Renewal, water skins. Kenji ran 5-color in all 4 drafts including one with double white and double black splashes.
- **Lessons mechanic is the secondary engine.** Nearly every green/blue deck leans on Accumulate Wisdom (draw 3 if you have 3+ lesson cards in graveyard) + Iro (Iro, Grand Lotus — 4-mana 4/4 that flashes back lessons) + True Ancestry as the core value engine.
- **Boros aggro is the enemy.** The format has a functional Boros deck that beats the slow shrine/lesson decks when draws are bad. "Nothing doing versus the Boros decks." Kenji lost to a Hakakota (?) pump spell + Ascension trigger combo in one game.
- **Speed:** Slower than AFR/AKR overall. Games go long when both players are on multicolor lesson/shrine plans. The player with the better fixing wins.

## Archetypes

### Green-Blue-X — Shrines
- **Gameplan:** Ramp to 4 mana by turn 3 using Manador, Audience of the Restless (turn-2 ramp spell?), Shared Roots, and Divide Guide. Play a Crescent Island Temple or Kyoshi Island Plaza (shrines — legendary lands that produce tokens/triggers). Stack shrine effects and kill with tokens + trample from Earthbender Ascension.
- **Key cards:** Iro, Grand Lotus (first pick; "a little bit too good" even in shrine decks — flashes back lessons), Earthbender Ascension (enchantment; gives all your creatures trample and growth — "this is disgusting," "all my creatures have trample"), Divide Guide (2-mana 2/3 that ramps AND fixes every turn it's in play; "this card is just incredible every time — 2 mana, 2/3 that fixes, ramps, and makes everything else fix and ramp"), Crescent Island Temple (blue shrine — generates tokens repeatedly; main shrine to have), Kyoshi Island Plaza (green shrine — pulls lands from deck, pumps creatures; "two Kyoshi Islands pulling three lands out of the deck makes another monk"), Planetarium (3-mana legendary that scries/generates value; "Planetarium is quite the treat to get passed"), Audience of the Restless Ones (?) (2-mana cantrip accelerant; "turn 2 audience, turn 3 shrine, you all know how it is"), Manador (1-mana 0/2 dork that taps for green; "turn-two manador into shrine is pretty nice"), Boommy Bash (?) (4-mana lesson that creates a large creature; "Boommy" makes a 4/4 for 4 or 7/7 for 4 with 2 lessons in graveyard — "a turn four seven seven versus red green does not have many ways to deal with it"), Cabbage Merchant (?) (2-mana creature that produces food, shuffles itself back — prevents decking, creates infinite shuffle engine with True Ancestry).
- **Strength:** When Earthbender Ascension and 2+ shrines line up, the deck is nearly unbeatable. Death touch trample tokens are "disgusting."
- **Weakness:** Dependent on shrines being opened. With 0–1 shrines the deck reverts to a weaker 5-color midrange plan.

### Green-Blue-X — Lessons Deck (shrine fallback)
- **Gameplan:** Accumulate lessons in graveyard through Ang's Journey, Shared Roots, cycling, and play. Cast Iro on turn 4 to flash back the best lesson. Activate Accumulate Wisdom (draw 3) as the main card advantage spell once 3 lessons are in the graveyard.
- **Key cards:** Iro, Grand Lotus (core of the whole plan), Accumulate Wisdom (draw 3 for 3 if 3+ lessons in graveyard; "now I'm only one lesson away from drawing three"), True Ancestry (3-mana "buy back" — gets a creature from graveyard; enables Professor + Ancestry loops), Sparring Dummy (milling enables their own library for koi effects; "sparring dummy goes from good to great with two ancestries"), Geyser Leaper (?) (4-mana creature with water-tap ability to mill; good with mill payoffs), Divide Guide, Shared Roots, multiple Ang's Journeys (ramp + fixing; "getting into 5-color is natural with this much fixing"), Fortune Teller (?) (scry + replace early; "sixth pick fortune teller is perfect").
- **15–16 lands is the right count** with the heavy ramp package. "Two shared roots, three Ang's journeys — I mean, I could almost get away with 15 lands."
- **The deck still wins** even without shrines when opponents don't have bombs. "This deck can do stuff — nothing fancy, flashy, or whatever, but good enough for now."

### 5-Color Good Stuff
- **Gameplan:** Assemble 5 colors via fixing and play the best cards you opened. United Front (?), Day of Black Sun (5-mana wrath that deals damage equal to mana spent), and Tooff (?) are the payoff cards.
- **Day of Black Sun:** Black wrath dealing X damage; against boards of small creatures it's a one-sided board wipe. Kenji consistently splashed 2 Swamps for it. "If we wrath for four, everything dies except my badger mole — I'd say that's pretty good, game-winning."
- **United Front (?):** Expensive token maker; double mythic pack card. "Both of these are incredible — let's go five color funsies."

## Card interactions & combos

- **Iro + Accumulate Wisdom:** Iro enters, flashes back a lesson (e.g., Ang's Journey for a land). If 3+ lessons are now in graveyard, immediately cast Accumulate Wisdom for 3 cards. This is the combo. The lesson doesn't need to be targets; just being in the graveyard counts.
- **True Ancestry + Kyoshi Island Plaza:** Ancestry bounces Kyoshi back to hand; replay it to pull another land out of your deck and make another monk token. Kenji does this deliberately: "I'm supposed to ancestry that back and recast it because that unlocks six mana on next turn for planetarium."
- **Cabbage Merchant + food sac + shuffle:** Sac the food produced by Merchant to gain life and trigger effects, then use True Ancestry or Spirit Oasis(?) to shuffle the Merchant back into the deck and redraw it. "Didn't actually expect that to work but here we are."
- **Earthbender Ascension + death-touch creatures:** Giving death-touch trampling creatures their ascension makes every combat trade free for you. "Death-touch trample is kind of disgusting."
- **Sparring Dummy + True Ancestry + Iro:** Dummy mills the top of library each activation. Ancestry gets back Iro or other good creatures milled. Together they create a loop where every activation that hits a lesson adds to Accumulate Wisdom's count.
- **Boommy Bash + 2 lessons in graveyard:** Boommy is "a 4-mana 4/4 with [draw a card?]" at baseline but scales up. "A turn four seven-seven versus red-green does not have many ways to deal with it."
- **Submersible (Invasion Submersible?) + turn it into a creature:** Blue land-creature that can be animated at instant speed; used to dodge sorcery-speed removal and block an attacker opponents didn't account for. "Utilizing the untapped to activate submersible is worth it here."

## Card tips

### Blue (U)
- **Divide Guide is the best common/uncommon in the format.** "This card is just incredible every time — two mana, a 2/3 that fixes ramps and makes everything else fix and ramp." Never pass it. It does triple duty: ramps, fixes your 5-color mana base, and bodies up well.
- **Watery Grasp / Quench:** Blue interaction spells. Quench is good in this format because many opponents are ramping up and leaving mana open; a 2-mana counter that says "pay 2 or counter" hits at the right moment. Kenji identifies opponents sitting on quench multiple times: "I'm very much under the assumption they have quench."

### Black (B)
- **Raven Eagle (?):** Powerful card worth a splash; "definitely worth a splash. However, Shared Roots is premium basically" — the ramp/fixing beats the power card here.

### Red (R)
- **Combustion Technique:** Blue/white(?) exile removal that exiles — crucial for token/recursion opponents. "Combustion technique for exile" is the high-value application.

### Green (G)
- **Audience of the Restless Ones (?) is how you curve into shrines.** Turn-2 acceleration into a turn-3 shrine is the ideal line. Kenji takes it over almost everything in the first few picks if going shrines.
- **Wall-Top Sentries (?): "Blocks everything early, trades with most things, gains you some life — perfect for a shrine deck."** Premium for slowing down aggressive opponents while shrines accumulate. Take them late.
- **Rocky Rebuke (?):** Removal that hits attacking/blocking creatures; also can target lands Kenji calls it out for hitting an earthbent land: "go for the rocky rebuke on their land." Niche but real.

### Colorless / artifacts
- **Barrels of Blasting Jelly (?):** 2-mana instant "barrel" effect (exile target creature/land?); Kenji's go-to early removal. "More barreling in, cool." Fires off lands too in emergency.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Ang's Journey:** Ramp and fixing rolled into one lesson spell. Take all you can see; the third copy is where you consider stopping. "Three Ang's Journeys" is the standard count in Kenji's lesson builds.
- **Grand (?):** Blue card-draw creature; good when you want more card selection. "The grand-grand would have put us in such good shape" — losing it on an error was a key blunder. With lessons, Grand reduces costs.
- **Opa (?):** Bounce/return spell; flashy in the shrine deck to recycle shrines. "Opa's a good one."
- **Mechanist vs Submersible:** Kenji takes Submersible over Mechanist in the shrine deck: "I think this is better, or at least more synergistic, in a shrine deck." Mechanist is "a very good card but not a Katara or Iro or something."
- **Tooff (?):** Black removal creature; recurs itself. Kenji mentions it multiple times as a key draw. Splash target.

## Pitfalls

- **Committing to shrines when they won't be available:** "If you are going to go shrines, you have to commit and you can't be scared of not getting any." The corollary is that you need to be willing to pivot hard to a lessons/5-color plan when shrines don't appear. Do not draft too narrow (e.g., 3 Kyoshi Islands) if you only have one shrine payoff.
- **Drawing 3 of the same shrine:** "Drew our third Kyoshi Island — that was brutal." In the shrine deck you need diversity. Running 3 of the same shrine causes hands where you have shrine #2 or #3 but can't activate them because the legendary rule discards the extra.
- **Planetarium at sorcery speed:** "Oof, good thing I didn't planetarium — they would have had the origin for it." Tapping your legendaries when opponent has open mana is dangerous; Kenji learned to hold Planetarium until he could also pay for a counterspell.
- **Attacking with fixing creatures needlessly:** "I did not mean to attack with the Divide Guide because now I can't cast [spell]." Divide Guide and Manador should almost never attack; their value is in tapping for mana.
- **Leaving lessons in graveyard without triggering Accumulate Wisdom:** "I'm going to be able to play the Iro and accumulate draw three since I'll still have three other lessons in the graveyard" — you must count lesson density before firing Accumulate Wisdom. Spending Iro on a lesson that brings you to 2 lessons in graveyard (not 3) means no draw-3.
- **Trying to draft shrines in Premier Draft (human pods):** All observations about bots passing shrines late apply ONLY to Quick Draft. In human pods, high-power shrine rares will be taken; the strategy requires adjustment.
- **Day of Black Sun timing:** Cast it for the right X value to kill everything relevant. "If I wrath for three, what happens?" — miscounting will leave threats alive. Play around the opponent being able to hold up interaction by waiting for enough mana to pay for quench.
- **Under-curving in shrine decks:** Kenji observed that when he had no audience/manador on turn 2 but had a shrine on turn 3, he fell too far behind. "Cabbage merchant here needed to be an audience so that we could ramp on turn two into plaza on turn three."

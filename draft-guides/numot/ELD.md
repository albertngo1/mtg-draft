# ELD — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?). Set: Throne of Eldraine flashback drafts.

## Format speed / meta read
- Eldraine is a format Kenji explicitly dislikes and considers largely solved by end-of-format. He says the mono-color decks are better than the two-color decks as a general rule.
- **Oko, Thief of Crowns** is the consensus best rare/planeswalker; first-pickable and game-warping. He refers to it as "stupid sexy Oko." When you have Oko, your deck's primary win condition becomes "cast Oko and make foods/3/3s."
- **Midnight Clock** is Kenji's favorite card in the format; he will almost always take it and prioritize casting it safely underneath countermagic.
- **Folio of Fancies** (Mill enchantment that draws if opponent has more cards) is a format-defining combo piece in the Blue Mill deck alongside Secret Keepers. Kenji says he doesn't like drafting it but acknowledges it wins a large percentage of its games when drawn.
- Format has very little fixing; splashing beyond two colors requires green (Rose Thorn Acolyte / Gilded Goose / Curious Pair / Golden Egg for fixing) or being prepared for awkward mana.
- Mono-color decks (Mono Green, Mono Red, Mono Blue) are legitimate and often better than two-color piles.
- "I keep drafting mono-colored decks in this format, and it's not because there are only mono-colored decks — it's that I think the mono-color decks are just better than the two-color ones."

## Archetypes
- **Mono Green Big Stompy**: Yorvo, Lord of Garenbrig (giant grows with each green creature played); Witchstalker (4/4 trample for four, makes food); Giant Opportunity (instant: sacrifice food → make 7/7); Curious Pair / Merrel Leaf Rider (2/2 adventure) as curve fillers. Garenbrig Carver (?) as surprise "removal" that pumps a creature. Rose Thorn Acolyte (ramp + adventure). Beanstalk Giant (ramp + big body). Once and Future (double regrowth). Innkeeper (triggers off adventure creatures ETB). Deathless Knight (4-mana recursion 4/2 with lifelink). Witch Stalker at multiples very good. Run 17 lands with the heavy top-end.
- **Blue Mill / Tempo**: Secret Keeper (1-mana Mill-2 on attack), Midnight Clock (clock to 12 = draw new hand), Folio of Fancies (Mill X + draw if opponent has more), Didn't Say Please (counterspell that also mills), Drown in the Loch (counter or kill based on graveyard count). Wants cheap spells to enable Folio refuels. Strategy is "mulligan or look for Folio or Oko every game."
- **Mono Red Aggro**: Rimrock Knight / Barge In (instant: target creature gets +2/+0 until end of turn, red creatures get trample); Gingerbrute (1/1 haste that can become unblockable); Seven Dwarves (1/1 for 1, gets bigger with more copies); Red Cap Raiders / Red Cap Melee (pump); Bonecrusher Giant (adventure stomp + 4/3); Hinge Keeper (?); Searing Barrage (5-damage sorcery); Slaying Fire (3 damage instant, becomes 6 with Adamant); Torbran, Thane of Red Fell (bomb that adds 2 to all red damage). Jousting Dummy (artifact creature) + Crystal Slippers for equipment synergy. Robber of the Rich is a 2/2 reach haste but lacks backup removal; take it but know its limitations.
- **Blue-Green Tempo/Adventures (Oko splash)**: Innkeeper + many Adventure creatures (needs 7–9 to be reliable) for repeated card-drawing triggers. Oko makes Foods and 3/3s off creatures or artifacts; excellent with food production from Goose and Curious Pair. Thunderous Snapper (4/4 draw when you cast 5+ CMC). Sorcerer's Broom (sacrifice permanent → copy Broom) + food sacks = token generation. Return to Nature main-deckable vs. Revenge of Ravens and other enchantments.
- **Black-Green Food**: Savvy Hunter (2/2 that makes food when it attacks), Bake into a Pie (murder that makes food), Once Upon a Time (?), Boggart Forager (?), Witch's Cottage for recursion.
- **Knights (Mardu/BW/BR)**: Deathless Knight, Fervent Champion, Worthy Knight, Syr Conrad the Grim (4/4 that drains whenever creatures die or leave graveyards — "Syr Conrad is fantastic / nuts" as a 5/4); Belle of the Brawl; Mirror Leaf Rider; Acclaimed Contender. Paragon is a flash 2/1 that gives all knights flash.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Gilded Goose + Oko**: Goose produces food on ETB and taps for food; Oko's +1 makes food; both fuel Oko's -1 (turn food into 3/3). Together described as "constructed-level" in Limited.
- **Midnight Clock + Blue bounce spells**: Use Clock safely by playing it when opponent is tapped out; it draws you 7 once it hits 12 (beginning of upkeep). Bounce effects buy time. Float a mana with Clock before it pops to not lose a mana.
- **Folio of Fancies + Secret Keeper + Drown in the Loch**: Classic Mill combo. Drown counters anything when graveyard ≥ CMC, or kills at same condition. Secret Keeper mills 2 on each attack. Folio mills X (opponent's hand size) and draws you that many if they have more cards. Innkeeper + adventure creatures also helps draw into Folio/Oko.
- **Innkeeper + many Adventure creatures**: Each adventure creature that ETB draws you a card off Innkeeper. Run 7–9 adventure creatures for the engine to be reliable.
- **Once and Future (double regrowth) + combat tricks**: Buy back two key pieces (pump + creature, removal + creature) to reset tempo swings.
- **Broom + food sacrifice**: Sorcerer's Broom copies itself whenever you sacrifice another permanent. With multiple food sources (Goose, Curious Pair, cabins) you can generate a large Broom army. Not amazing but powerful if opponent ignores it.
- **Syr Conrad the Grim + any discard / mill**: Each time any creature goes to a graveyard from anywhere, Conrad drains 1. In a board-stall it drains rapidly; in a mill matchup it chains. "Conrad's pretty nuts."
- **Giant Opportunity + food token**: Sacrifice any food for a 7/7. With Goose/Curious Pair/Savvy Hunter/Witchstalker already making food, this can come down turn 3–4 as a 7/7 for 4 effectively.

## Card tips
- **Gilded Goose**: First-pickable; fixing, food production, blocker vs. 1/1 fliers, fuels Oko. "The goose does it all."
- **Oko, Thief of Crowns**: Strongest rare in format; +2 makes food, +1 makes a creature a 3/3 (removes abilities), -1 trades creature/artifact for an Elk 3/3. Generates enormous value even without the combo.
- **Midnight Clock**: Kenji says "every time I have access to a clock I want to play it." Always play it; try to resolve under opponent's countermagic by waiting for them to be tapped out.
- **Folio of Fancies**: Wins games in the Mill deck but is slow and loses to pressure. Not a card Kenji drafts by choice but respects its win rate. Works best when opponent stumbles.
- **Drown in the Loch**: The only thing that can counter the Clock is "the two-mana card that puts creatures back on top of library" (i.e. the blue instant bounce); most other counterspells are irrelevant vs. Clock.
- **Witchstalker** (Fierce Witchstalker: 4/4 trample for 4 that makes food): Best or near-best green four-drop. Especially good with Deathless Knight because the food trigger enables recursion.
- **Garenbrig Carver** (Ardenvale Tactician? / "Carver": 4-mana 4/4 trample?): Described as a "surprise all-star in green" and "basically a removal spell and a creature."
- **Beanstalk Giant**: Adventure for ramp (basic land to hand) + 6/6 for 7. Excellent fixing for splashes; the Giant body closes games.
- **Rose Thorn Acolyte**: "Really need to find these" — it's fixing + ramp + an adventure creature for Innkeeper. First priority in any green deck.
- **Return to Nature**: Main-deckable in this format; hits Revenge of Ravens, Lucky Clover, foods, and other key enchantments/artifacts.
- **Revenge of Ravens**: One of the most hated cards to face — lifelink/drain enchantment that punishes attacking. Kenji says "revenge of Ravens was one of the most annoying cards in this format." Plan to have Return to Nature or just race through it.
- **Deathless Knight**: Recursion with lifelink; keep food around to replay it from graveyard.

## Pitfalls
- **Forcing mill**: Kenji explicitly says "I don't want to do the mill deck" at the start of multiple drafts. It's polarizing — needs Secret Keepers and Folio/Drown to function and loses easily to aggressive pressure.
- **Over-splashing with bad fixing**: There is very little fixing; if you're not green, most splash attempts fail. Respect the mana requirements.
- **Staying on blue when no key cards**: Without Folio or Oko, the blue shells are mediocre. Know when to abandon blue mid-draft.
- **Hybrid/two-archetype piles**: Multiple times Kenji acknowledges "this deck is a hybrid of two archetypes which means it's bad." Commit to a strategy.
- **Playing Yorvo without trample note**: "Yorvo never gets trample with enough counters" — Kenji mistakenly thought it did. Yorvo is a huge body but needs pump/trample effects (Insatiable Appetite?) or evasion to close.
- **Playing Clock turn 2 into countermagic**: Wait until opponent is tapped to resolve Clock; casting it into open mana gives opponent free countermagic.
- **Keeping hands without action until turn 4**: In an aggressive format, slow hands die. Kenji says several losses came from "doing nothing until turn four."

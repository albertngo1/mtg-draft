# LCI — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Format is faster than it looks. Cheap evasive creatures — especially one-drop flyers — are highly valued. Kenji explicitly says in the Arena Open prep context that Staunch Crewmate is correct over a 6/6 rare because "it's cheaper in this more aggressive format."
- Captain Storm (2-mana UR artifact creature) is called out repeatedly as "one of the most egregious cards in the format" and an instant-win threat that demands removal on sight. Turn-two Captain Storm without an answer described as "a very quick loss."
- Unstable Glyph Bridge (?) appears frequently as an opponent bomb; having graveyard recursion or artifact-based tricks to navigate around it is valuable.
- Caves / hidden-land dual lands enable multi-color strategies; blue-white flyers was described as wanting at least 8 plains and going 10-7 or 9-7 on colors.
- Descend (permanents in graveyard) is a central mechanic — both for the payoffs and for tracking during games.

## Archetypes
- **Green-Black Self-Mill / Descend (turbo):** Kenji's favorite and most detailed VOD. The gameplan is to flood the graveyard quickly using In the Presence of Ages (?), frogs (Poison Dart Frog / land cyclers), and Marionette (?), then cast Bringer of the Last Gift (6-mana living death) or Mico Tyrant (?) for massive board presence. Bringer is noted as green-black's P1P1 bomb. Another Chance (?) is essential recursion. The deck wants 3-4 In the Presence of Ages, 2-3 frogs, and supplemental self-mill like Mineshaft Spider (3/4 reach, mills 2 on ETB). Can run 16-17 lands with the frog mana acceleration.
- **Blue-White Flyers / Artifacts:** Classic tempo/aggro plan. One-drop flyers (Miner's Guide Wisp (?), Ruin Lurker Bat, Spy Glass Siren) backed by pump (Pirate Hat / equipment), Cogwork Wrestler (?) for bounce, and Unstable Glyph Bridge as a defensive reset. Kenji runs 16 lands, values Dismantler as a ground blocker that also hates artifacts. Spring-Loaded Sawblades (?) praised as strong removal; Loadstone Needle (?) acts as a soft removal piece that also gives explore value.
- **Blue-Red Pirates / Artifacts:** Captain Storm + A Call to Call (?) + Appraiser + equipment. Artifact-heavy beat-down with card advantage engines. Pirate Hat (equipment) and Pickaxe (equipment) do heavy lifting. Wants 9+ artifacts and ways to make more (Plundering Pirate, Ancestor's Aid).
- **Blue-Black Descend / Control:** Confounding Riddle, Echo of Dusk (good 2-drop that becomes a 3/3), Inverted Iceberg (?), Waterlogged Hulk (?) for self-mill. Kenji found this archetype underdeveloped in his runs — "above average" deck but not explosive. Scampering Surveyor (?) bridges mana for splashes.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Bringer of the Last Gift + Another Chance:** Bringer reanimates everything; Another Chance recycles Bringer from graveyard back to hand for a second cast. Multiple Another Chances make the loop very resilient against wraths.
- **Bringer of the Last Gift + Hoverstone Pilgrim (?):** Hoverstone can put opponent's creatures from graveyard on the bottom of their library before Bringer resolves, so opponent gets nothing back when you cast it.
- **Mico Tyrant (?) + self-mill:** At beginning of end step, makes X 1/1 fungi tokens where X = times descended this turn. Generates massive boards when combined with Marionette, In the Presence of Ages, and other permanents entering the graveyard.
- **Captain Storm + A Call to Call (?):** Both trigger on artifacts entering — A Call triggers on your turn and opponent's turn (same as Poetic Ingenuity), providing continuous card advantage alongside Storm's draw.
- **Spring-Loaded Sawblades (?) + Pirate (tap-down) + any 1-toughness creature:** Tap the creature with a pirate, then Sawblades deals 5 damage to it. Kenji calls this a "combo" explicitly.
- **Loadstone Needle (?) + Spring-Loaded Sawblades:** Needle taps a creature, Sawblades kills it at instant speed.
- **Pirate Hat (equipment) + Waylaying Pirates (?):** Pirate Hat turns creatures into rummage engines; Waylaying Pirates can tap things; both enable continuous card filtering and tempo plays.
- **In the Presence of Ages + land cyclers:** Presence digs toward Bringer and can grab a land in addition to finding cards, unlike Another Chance which is pure tutor. Kenji notes running 3-4 Presences is correct in the turbo-mill build.

## Card tips
- **Spy Glass Siren:** One of the best uncommons in the format — 1-mana 1/1 flyer that makes a map artifact. Kenji takes it over Petrify P1P1.
- **Deep Cavern Bat:** Called "stupid good" uncommon; even without turn-two deployment it provides value later.
- **Bitter Triumph:** Highly splashable black removal. Take early and often.
- **Dead Weight:** Cheap permanent-based removal that also fuels descend. Kenji regrets not prioritizing it.
- **Loadstone Needle (uncommon):** "Format is so fast it effectively acts as a removal spell on a creature" with the tap, then you get explore value paying 1 repeatedly. Has synergy with Sawblades.
- **Pirate Hat (equipment):** Overperforms in aggressive decks with multiple small creatures — gives rummage, enabling card quality filtering throughout the game.
- **Appraiser (Geological Appraiser):** Notable that it's banned in other formats; in this limited context it's a solid card advantage piece.
- **Defossilize (?):** Best when you have land cyclers or a self-mill deck; awkward in pure aggro.
- **Compass Gnome:** Useful fixing for splashes and puts an artifact in the graveyard.
- **Panicked Altisaur (?):** 7-mana reach dinosaur — late-game finisher, fine to include if curve is low elsewhere.
- **In the Presence of Ages:** Versatile dig spell — grabs permanents AND can find a land, superior to Another Chance for the turbo-bringer deck as a result (Another Chance only finds non-land cards).
- **Treasure Map:** Kenji's stated favorite card printed in years; keeps you open, generates treasure for fixing, and flips into a draw engine.

## Pitfalls
- **Don't take O'Hare Tusk / Deepest Foundation (6/6 vigilance rare) over Staunch Crewmate or Spy Glass Siren:** Kenji made this choice and repeatedly noted he was punished for it — "it's cheaper in this more aggressive format; basically a 2-mana 2/1 draw card."
- **Don't over-commit to self-mill count and neglect interaction:** In the blue-black descend deck, Kenji ended 0-3 in part because he had insufficient answers to Captain Storm and relied too heavily on graveyard synergies without enough early interaction.
- **Don't run land cyclers in the blue-red pirate/artifact aggro deck:** "I'm not huge on these in the pirate artifact deck" — they dilute your artifact count and don't synergize with the beat-down plan.
- **Don't double-block/chump inefficiently in the lategame:** Kenji notes a specific mistake over-chumping with two creatures when a single block or redirected attack would have preserved lethal the following turn.
- **Captain Storm demands immediate removal:** If you can't kill it the turn it comes down, the game spirals quickly. Prioritize instant-speed interaction in decks likely to face it.
- **Don't give opponents free treasure by using removal on their triggered abilities before they've made the artifact:** Kenji gave an opponent the white source they needed to flip their Unstable Glyph Bridge by not timing the piranhas correctly before the treasure was created.

## Format speed / meta read

- **It's a "soup vs. aggro" format, and over the run it collapses into "just a five-color format."** Kenji's arc: early on he calls SOS a "two or three deck format" (converge + Lorehold + the two-color colleges); by mid-run he flatly says "this is now just a five-color format" and "everybody's playing the converge." He "came full circle" — loved it, then hated it once everyone converged (the ~05-13 detour), then **embraced soup once he started trophying with it (confirmed end-of-format 05-29: soup = best deck if open)**.
- **Games are super grindy and very long.** SOS produced some of the longest VODs Kenji can remember (multiple pushing 90 minutes). Card-advantage engines (Arc of Hunger, Potioner's Trove, Diary of Dreams, Divergent Equation, Wisdom of Ages) decide games more than aggression. **Decking the opponent is a real, frequently-used win condition** — Mathematics, Exhibition Tide-Hauler mills, and even Together as One can target the opponent to mill them out. (Earliest deck-out format he's seen — confirmed early 04-26.)
- **Life gain is everywhere and slows the format down.** Potioner's Trove, infirmary/healer effects, prepared-spell lifegain, Snarl Song. This makes it very hard for aggro to close — once soup stabilizes behind lifegain + big bodies, aggro loses.
- **Rock-paper-scissors:** soup beats two-color aggro *if it has its over-the-top payoffs and doesn't stumble on mana*; two-color aggro beats soup if it gets under it before soup stabilizes. **"Bad soup" — a converge pile with payoffs but no early interaction (or with fixing but no payoffs) — loses to everything.** Never force converge without payoffs.
- **Soup mirrors are decided by who draws/sticks the biggest payoff first — headline late MVP is Arcane Omens, "the mirror breaker" (05-29).** Other mirror-deciding bombs: Wisdom of Ages, Math Magics/Mathematics, Snarl Song, Divergent Equation. Counter redundancy beats a single haymaker. **Render Speechless is Silverquill's key anti-soup tool — the card that "hoses" soup (05-29); he plays around it constantly.**
- **Format feels stale/samey by mid-run** — Kenji compares it to Tarkir: Dragonstorm: a couple aggressive decks plus easy-to-assemble converge piles you face over and over. He concedes clearly-lost games to soup early ("saving myself 10 minutes"). By end-of-format this becomes mild fatigue ("about ready for this set to be done with," 05-31) — not the early "Magic's great" enthusiasm. (⚠ early enthusiasm superseded; see Supersessions.)
- **Two-color decks feel more mana-screwed than five-color.** A recurring (half-acknowledged-as-psychological) vent: 3-of-one-color hands plague his 2c decks while soup "draws its colors so easily." Practical upshot: soup with Terramorphic + Potioner's Trove fixes itself; tight 2c basic ratios brick more.
- **Speed of games, not power, is the reason to consider 2-color:** "If I was playing the spell/five-color decks I might have finished one or two matches; we're 3-2 in a fraction of the time." Two-color aggro resolves fast; soup grinds.
- **Tracked records:** Boros/Lorehold ~67-68% over his largest sample (e.g. 38-19 early; later trophied 7 of 13 drafts ~68% WR, ~3.5 of them RW). **9 trophies in 21 Contender drafts (~43%)** during the converge-heavy stretch. Lorehold is his best *personal* archetype; **soup is the strongest *deck* (confirmed end-of-format 05-29).**
- **Land counts:** 17 is standard. Run 18 in shaky soup bases or when you have rummage/pitch outlets (3x Rubble Rouser). 16 only for tight low-curve aggro. Splash benchmark: **~7-8 sources per main color** to cast reliably (late refinement: e.g. 7 blue / 6 green / 5 red, shave a red for a basic when converge rewards a source); a lone Plains splash is justified by ~5 converge cards. The looser Jeskai-soup hybrid bricked at 17 → wanted 18 + more top end (05-31).

## Archetypes

Ranked by Kenji's evidence (end-of-format verdict): Soup (S) > Prismari (A) > Lorehold ≈ Silverquill (B) > Quandrix ≈ Witherbloom (C), with **Witherbloom dead last** (05-30).

### Soup / 5-color Converge — S-tier (best deck in the format, confirmed end-of-format 05-29)
- **Gameplan:** cheap interaction early + ramp/fixing (Studious First-Year, Environmental Scientist, Shared Roots, Potioner's Trove, Proctor's Gaze, Terramorphic) → over-the-top converge payoffs. Win by going over the top or by milling.
- **Two flavors:** (A) **control/spell-heavy, Prismari-based** (Potioner's Trove + big X spells, Mathematics, Divergent Equation, Snarl Song, Wisdom of Ages); (B) **ramp/board, Quandrix/Temur-based** (Hydro Channeler, Fractal Tender/Mascot, Mind into Matter, Shared Roots, Bertha, Splatter Technique). Kenji's preferred foundation is a **blue-green base** (green fixing, blue draw/counters) splashing red first (burn + Snarl Song), then white/black for Arcane Omens / Render Speechless / removal. (Late: "super happy that I moved into green very late," 05-29 — green = the key splash enabler.)
- **Key payoffs:** Together as One (#1), Snarl Song, **Arcane Omens (the late MVP / mirror breaker)**, Divergent Equation, Mathematics/Math Magics, Wisdom of Ages, Sundering Archaic, Mind into Matter, Splatter Technique, Skywhaler, Fractal Mascot.
- **Soft-force it, never hard-force.** "If you're playing converge you can just play any of the colors, so you're never really missing out on picks." Frequently the only converge drafter at the table → late payoffs wheel.
- **Warnings:** "bad soup" (payoffs but no early interaction, OR good fixing but no payoff) loses to everything. **You need an actual win condition** (Mathematics / Wisdom / Snarl Song / Mascots / Skywhalers), not just value — "interaction alone doesn't win games." Loses to *better* soup (more counters / Wisdom of Ages / Mathematics — confirmed 06-01: his late losses were to opponents holding these) and to its own mana stumbles. As the field adapts, easy soup drafts close: "a lot harder to pull that off." Late meta shifted toward creature-based 2-color decks (fewer soup than mid-format), but **soup mirrors still common and decided by best top-end converge breakers (Arcane Omens) — and by whether the opponent has Render Speechless (05-29).**

### Prismari / Blue-Red (spell-density control) — A-tier (best two-color shell, natural soup base)
- **TWO DISTINCT BUILDS (late clarification 05-30):** (1) **aggressive pump/tempo** (tackle artists / fire dancers / pump spells) — Kenji's FAVORITE *fun* deck, "forces the fun," trophied with it 05-30 ("the old pump pump get you tempo"); and (2) **big blue-red control / spell-heavy** — more powerful, the natural soup base, but NOT his preferred build; both his 05-30 losses were to it. These are different decks — don't conflate.
- **Gameplan:** cheap interaction + growing spell-payoff creatures, win on card advantage and burn, or with a top-end bomb.
- **Two-drop pillars:** Expressive Fire Dancer and Tackle Artist — "get every Fire Dancer and every Tackle Artist I see"; both grow on instants/sorceries. Sanar/Cenar (?) (0/4 treasure-maker + tutors an instant/sorcery after you cast one — "the Wild Idea prepared spell is absurd"). Pensive Professor (0/2 Increment — draws on essentially every spell; "mini blue bomb," kill-on-sight). Magma-Blood / Sundering Archaic (?) (pumps team per color spent).
- **Top end / finishers:** Prismari, the Inspiration (M — 7/7 flying ward-5, all spells get storm), Spectacular Sky Whale (?) (+3/+3 permanently per instant/sorcery targeting a creature → out of burn range fast), Artistic Process (modal 6-to-one / 2-to-each / 3-3 haste), Skywhaler, Divergent Equation, Wisdom of Ages.
- **Aggro Prismari ("Bulk Up" / pump-spells variant)** is build (1) above (Fire Dancer + Deluge Virtuoso + Tide-Hauler, protect with Chase Inspiration, kill with Bulk Up + Monstrous Rage). Wants MORE two-drops (5–6 minimum for a reliable turn-2 creature), fewer 4+ drops; ~10-12 creatures is light. Kenji went 5-3 with it mid-run and at the time concluded the big-spell/converge Prismari is *stronger* — but **late he reaffirms the pump build is his favorite to PILOT (05-30), distinct from "strongest."**
- **Counters are premium here:** Disdainful Stroke, Brush Off, Essence Scatter, Spell Pierce, Mana Sculpt (3-mana Mana Drain variant if you control a wizard — but few wizards exist).

### Lorehold / Red-White (graveyard value) — B-tier (Kenji's best personal record; consistent early→late)
- **NOT pure aggro — a value/synergy deck with "leaves the graveyard" mechanics.** "This is not just play a creature, attack." Snowballs explosively when engines come online; stumbles hard if they're answered. (Best personal school early at 67%/38-19, and a reliable soft-force lane late — "last seven of nine drafts… Lorehold or Silverquill," 05-29.)
- **Core engine:** **Rubble Rouser** (rummage on ETB; tap + exile a graveyard card → ping 1, gain 1, make a 2/2) → **Garrison Excavator** on turns 3/4 (exiles graveyard cards / tutors land) — Kenji: "one of the nastiest things you can do in this format." **Arc of Hunger** closes long games (its early "soup MVP" billing has shifted — late it's a Lorehold card, with Arcane Omens the soup MVP; see Supersessions). Terramorphic Expanse is valued as "an extra graveyard card for free."
- **Key cards:** Rubble Rouser (take 3+ copies), Garrison Excavator, Arc of Hunger, Elite Interceptor, Spirit Mascot, Practiced Scrollsmith, Living History, Stone Docent, Pursue the Past, Tome Blast, Molten Note, Aziza Mage Tower Captain (?), Kiro (?) (4/4 trample lifelink, self-feeds graveyard), Sloth (4/4 lifelink trample, exiles).
- **Open very often** — Rubble Rouser wheeling to 8th–11th, "10 Spirit Mascots available this draft" = the seat is wide open. Can pivot to soup better than Silverquill.
- **Weakness:** desperately needs cheap removal/burn — "if this deck had cheap burn it would be amazing; as is it's probably just average." Folds to sweepers and to faster curves if behind; protect Arc of Hunger from artifact removal.

### Silverquill / Black-White (Repartee aggro/tempo) — B-tier (top 2-color answer to aggro; feast-or-famine)
- **Gameplan:** curve out 2-drops → target your OWN creatures with cheap instants/sorceries to trigger Repartee/grow the board → close before opponents set up. "Two-drop, Repartee creature into another, some targeted effects — you might just get run over." Late framing: **the main answer to aggressive creature decks, "a lot of tools" (05-29)**; his personal results lag the card quality (variance).
- **Key cards:** Stirring Hopesinger ("busted" — see combos), Scolding Administrator (premium 2-drop), **Render Speechless (the #1 Silverquill card AND the best non-rare answer to soup — counter + hand peek + discard + team pump; "always get at least one")**, Cost of Brilliance, Snooping Page (unblockable connector that draws), Abigail (?) (re-prepares creatures — but late DOWNGRADE: "not huge on Abigail," 05-29; lukewarm), Quillblade Laureate, Dig Site Inventory, Killian's Confidence, Ajani's Response, Forum Necroscribe (6-mana 5/4 ward, recurs creatures — "mythic uncommon"), Scheming Silverquill (2/3 fly lifelink → Sign in Blood), Mozio/Moseo (?), Decorum Dissertation, Inkling Mascot, Elite Interceptor. (Diary of Dreams is a frequent CUT here late — not enough permanents, 05-29.)
- **Glass cannon: "really relies on your creatures not being interacted with."** Against removal-heavy opponents it falls apart; historically goes ~3/3 for Kenji even with strong builds (1-3 with an "insane deck" to bad draws). **Cannot be a soup base** → ranked just behind Lorehold. Resigned that "I can never win against the three-plus-color decks."
- **Discipline:** needs **6+ two-drop creatures / 12+ creatures total** so spells have targets. Needs its own card-advantage plan (Cost of Brilliance, Snooping Page) since it lacks Prismari's raw draw.

### Quandrix / Blue-Green (ramp/fractal tempo) — C-tier (interaction-starved; great as a soup base)
- "From at least early access, Quandrix was the weakest of the schools" (⚠ early flat read — refined late: it's not raw-power weak, it's **interaction-starved**). The pure GU tempo deck does nothing about Arc of Hunger / soup payoffs and **has essentially no removal beyond counters** — "having no removal versus Silverquill is not where you need to be," "I just really had no removal" (06-02). Went 3-3 / 3-2 with a "medium" build, "medium results"; explicitly wouldn't draft it for an LCQ. **Late refined verdict: strong top-end (Quandrix Apex / "the Proof"; Echo on Fractal Mascot is "nuts"), but mediocre WITHOUT a removal splash.**
- **Better as the ramp-soup base.** Key cards: Quandrix, the Proof (M — 6/6 fly trample cascade), Bertha (4-mana fractal engine, "kill on sight"), Fractal Mascot/Tender (Mascot strong esp. as an Echo/copy target), Paradox Surveyor, Terrafractal, Hydro Channeler, Mana Worm (?) (2/5 trample grows on instants/sorceries), Tester of the Tangential, Splatter Technique. Needs protection (Royal Treatment, Chase Inspiration) to keep big threats alive. Note: Kenji did go straight blue-green no-splash to 5 wins once ("wasn't terrible").

### Witherbloom / Black-Green (lifegain / pest grind or overrun) — C-tier / DEAD LAST ("pretty garbage," 05-30)
- Kenji **actively discourages it** — early "from my experience, I don't like Witherbloom" (04-27) → **late HARDENED to "Wither Bloom is pretty garbage," dead last, passes the open seat (05-30)**; "really needs to high roll to win its games." It CAN produce results (7-1 in one featured run, only loss the mirror; 6-3 and 2-3 in others) but is the worst archetype. **"Always the most open archetype"** — consistent late signals, for good reason.
- **Two builds:** (1) **lifegain/infusion** — Bogwater Luminary (the critical 2-drop he "always fails to pick up"), Teach in the Pest / pest mascots (attack → gain 1 → turn on infusion), Poisoner's Apprentice (cast on T3 with infusion active = kill a creature), Thornfist Striker (3/3 ward-1, team trample while gaining life — "real good"), Old-Growth Educator, Arnyn/Arni (?) deathtouch drainers, Blech the Loafing Pest (pump all pests on lifegain). (2) **overrun** — **Root Manipulation (3BG, +2/+2 and menace)** = "best overrun in a while"; **menace beats trample here because creature counts are so low** — opponents can't double-block 3+ attackers. "They all got menace. GG's."
- **Weaknesses:** loses badly to converge (concede early); fragile to spot removal on key threats; **mana-stumble prone** — needs both B and G early ("just need a second black source") — most losses are one-color starts. Exile-heavy opponents (Wander Off, Silverquill Charm) shut down Cauldron of Essence / graveyard recursion. Plays better in Bo3.

### Mardu / off-college control — experimental
- Kenji once won a Contender title 6-2 with base Silverquill/Lorehold creatures + Arcane Omens + double Potioner's Trove as a converge/control package ("unlike anything I've ever drafted in this format"). Base two-color creatures with enough fixing to hit Arcane Omens for 3-4. Off-college wedges otherwise are not a thing — too many gold cards.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Stirring Hopesinger + any creature-targeting spell** (Render Speechless, Cost of Brilliance, Dig Site Inventory, Killian's Confidence, Send in the Pest): each targeting spell puts a +1/+1 counter on ALL your creatures. Snowballs rapidly; "busted." Works even outside Silverquill.
- **Diary of Dreams + a steady stream of spells/creatures:** accrues a counter per cast; Kenji shows it at 15 counters = 15 mana of activations. "This diary has been insane." Eventually a tap-to-draw engine. Get it down early; don't waste surveil activations when you can draw instead. (Caveat late: a frequent CUT in Silverquill if you lack permanents to fuel it, 05-29.)
- **Rubble Rouser + Garrison Excavator:** Rouser rummages cards into the graveyard, Excavator exiles them to ping + make 2/2s (and tutors land). The Lorehold backbone; "one of the nastiest things in the format." Flickering/bouncing Rouser re-triggers its leaves-graveyard payoff.
- **Arc of Hunger + Rubble Rouser:** activate Rouser to exile a graveyard card → Arc drains each opponent + you gain life + mill → Arc plays the milled land from graveyard → that leaving triggers Arc again → near-infinite chain. "Incredibly dumb."
- **Arc of Hunger + Potioner's Trove:** Arc drains opponents while Trove gains 2 per instant/sorcery — Kenji nets 12+ life and a fistful of cards while stabilizing. Arc triggers off ANY card leaving your graveyard, not just creatures.
- **Together as One at 5 colors (with Cody (?)):** Cody adds WUBRG; cast Together as One for "deal 5 / draw 5 / gain 5" — "a Cruel Ultimatum." Together as One can also **target the opponent to mill them** as a deck-out win.
- **Cody (?) + Together as One caveat:** Cody can't cast permanents — Kenji notes "I played Cody the second time and it could lose me the game." Only run in a near-all-spells build.
- **Snarl Song + Potioner's Trove:** paying Snarl Song's multi-color cost triggers Trove for life → "snarl for five, gain five, then Trove gain another two" ≈ 7+ life swing on top of the two 5/5s (or two 3/3s + tokens, caption-dependent). **Snarl Song + Lorecaster:** Lorecaster (?) rebuys Snarl Song from the graveyard for repeated token waves. (Late: Snarl Song reaffirmed as a premier soup finisher, 05-29.)
- **Mathematics + Wisdom of Ages:** Mathematics mills the opponent for X (X≈5 mills ~32; kill window ~10 mana when they're low on library); Wisdom of Ages returns all your instants/sorceries (no hand limit) to keep the mill engine fueled. Time Mathematics for when the opponent is tapped out — count their mana first. (Both are late mirror-deciding bombs, 06-01.)
- **Divergent Equation (UUX) rebuying removal/payoffs:** return up to X instants/sorceries (Banishing Betrayal, Heated Argument, Arcane Omens, the Equation itself) — "you can't beat the Divergent." A win-from-1-life stabilizer. Chain: "omens → divergent back the omens → omens again next turn."
- **Arcane Omens + Mind into Matter:** Omens discards their hand for ~4, then Mind into Matter draws you 6+. Out-values mirror opponents who try the same. (Arcane Omens is THE late soup MVP / "mirror breaker," 05-26/05-29.)
- **Sanar/Cenar (?) + Mika Wheel Captain (?):** cast Sanar's Wild Idea (draw 3 / discard 2 / add 5), with its ability on the stack re-activate Sanar; Mika triggers on sacrificing the treasure → copy Wild Idea = draw 6 / discard 4 + extra mana. (Note: Mika's ward is "ward 3 life," not mana — cheap to pay.)
- **Spectacular Sky Whale (?) + Sanar/Cenar (?):** casting Wild Idea pumps the Whale +3/+3 permanently → a 7/10 flyer out of burn range in a turn.
- **Pensive Professor + any cheap spell:** it's 0/2 with Increment, so essentially every spell after it draws a card.
- **Aziza Mage Tower Captain (?) + Tome Blast:** Aziza taps 3 creatures to copy your instant/sorcery — copy Tome Blast to kill two creatures, or 4-to-face + kill one.
- **Applied Geometry + Zealous Lorecaster — slow "infinite":** Geometry on Lorecaster → Lorecaster copies Geometry → rebuy Geometry → repeat, spitting out a 6/6 flyer copy each cycle (5+ mana per loop). Skycoach Conductor (?) (flash flyer that flickers a creature) can reset/flicker the Scrollsmith/Lorecaster to re-trigger.
- **Environmental Scientist + Applied Geometry:** Geometry the Scientist → copy a land it already found → two basics out of one creature.
- **Magma-Blood / Sundering Archaic (?) + Heated Argument:** Archaic adds power per mana spent; Heated Argument adds mana per the creature's power → a mana-power loop. "That's a huge combo."
- **Expressive Fire Dancer + Deluge Virtuoso:** both pump on each instant/sorcery; a stack of spells balloons both simultaneously. Also great with Practiced Offense.
- **Bulk Up + Monstrous Rage (same creature):** the one-shot kill of aggro Prismari (e.g. +3 then +4 + trample = 7). Caveat: both are on the Mystic Archive sheet, so you rarely see both in one draft.
- **Tackle Artist + Chase Inspiration:** deploy Tackle Artist holding up Chase Inspiration (hexproof) — protect the finisher and win the game.
- **Exhibition Tide-Hauler + a 5+ MV spell:** Tide-Hauler mills 3 on cast but **10 if you cast an MV-5+ spell the same turn** — a legitimate mill kill. "The name of the game is protect the Tide-Hauler"; re-bounce (Banishing Betrayal) to reuse. ("Insane, a one-mana win con" — confirmed even in the early window, 04-26.)
- **Potioner's Trove + Withering Curse (?):** Trove gains 2 as the wrath goes on the stack → effectively a 3-mana sweeper. Triple Trove + double Withering Curse lets any deck sweep cheaply.
- **Return the Favor + opponent's big spell / removal:** copy their Wisdom of Ages in the mirror, or redirect removal for a blowout. Caveats: doesn't trigger storm, doesn't work on non-targeted effects.
- **Effloresence / Bind to Life (?) + a double-block:** gives trample + indestructible while you've gained life — blow out a double-block on your attacker.
- **Postmortem Professor + Cauldron of Essence:** recur the Professor repeatedly to keep reanimating (e.g. a troll) — "nasty."
- **Necroscribe + a cheap targeting spell (Killian's Confidence):** don't run Necroscribe out naked — only deploy when you can immediately target it to trigger Repartee/recursion.
- **Slumbering Trudge + Owlin Historian:** Historian surveils/mills creatures into the yard; once 3+ creatures are there, Trudge's prepared reanimate comes online (a 6/6 for 4 if cast early).

## Pitfalls

- **Don't force converge without payoffs, and don't force a college if you're already 2-color when the converge seat was open.** Kenji's recurring self-criticism: "I think all I did was the Silverquill seat… maybe I was supposed to have been in converge" — he saw the Arcane Omens and the Snarls and didn't take them. Track converge payoffs even when 2-color. (End-of-format: soup is the best deck if open — bias toward the converge seat.)
- **Don't run synergy creatures out naked.** "I don't want to play out my Necroscribe until I can target it." Deploy 2-drop + 3-drop so a counter only stops half your turn; don't jam bombs into open blue ("not running out Magma-Blood there — too likely they have Essence Scatter").
- **Mana-base discipline > raw power early.** "Missing land drops is not how you win." Take Terramorphic / tapped duals / Trove over situational spells when mana is shaky (but a *land over a premium uncommon* is wrong — take the late Tome Blast). 17 lands standard, 18 with rummage/pitch outlets; ~7-8 sources per main color for a splash.
- **Creature/two-drop counts for synergy decks.** Silverquill/Witherbloom need 6+ two-drops / 12+ creatures or the targeting spells and lords have nothing to do. "My creature count is 10, which makes Grave Researcher much worse." (Pump Prismari likewise wants 5-6 two-drops min; ~10-12 creatures is light — 05-30.)
- **Play around Render Speechless / exile, not in response.** "Never kill your creature in response to the targeting effect — people surveil first thinking it'll live. Let the spell resolve, then decide." (Render Speechless is the single most-played-around card in the late soup mirror — 05-29.)
- **Don't over-respect rares/specific answers.** "I don't play around a rare." Too many possible instants per color — make the straightforward play, just sequence around generic counter mana.
- **Time your X-spells / mill spells around the opponent tapping out.** "Go ahead, tap out a little more," then fire Mathematics. Counting the opponent's mana before any X spell is the single biggest late-game skill.
- **Sequencing/main-phasing punts.** "I punted by main-phasing it / missed lethal." Cast cantrips (Pensive Professor) before attacks; don't main-phase instant-speed effects when holding them up gives information.
- **Interact with the engine, not the bodies.** Kill Potioner's Trove, Arc of Hunger, Pensive Professor, Bertha, Aziza — not random creatures. "Killing their Trove is just better — it's going to double-pump." Exile > -3/-3/bounce in a recursion-heavy format ("splash the ones that for sure get rid of something").
- **Aggro must be ahead; prepared spells are awful when behind.** "Be ahead or at parity; punish being behind." Don't cast prepared/lifegain reactively from a losing spot — they don't catch you up.
- **Don't burn flashback / win-cons early in a long game**, and don't deck yourself by cycling win-cons in mirrors. "Be more conservative playing out Bertha — wait until I have counter magic." Soup wants multiple win lines (mill + damage); single-dimension closing gets countered.
- **Concede clearly-lost games vs. soup** to save time/mental energy; not premature given how grindy they are.
- **Mulligan rules:** soup/ramp hands without 3 lands OR a ramp card; soup hands that can't cast anything relevant by T3-4 (missing a key color); two-color hands lacking a smoothing card. A keep with a filtering card (Flow State, a cycler) can justify a greedy 2-lander.

## Format speed / meta read

*All from 2 release-window drafts — provisional.*

- **Removal is premium and scarce.** Kenji's single most-repeated read: "removal is premium, there doesn't seem to be a lot of it / good removal." He P1P2'd **Punishing Punch** over a signpost gold card on this basis, and both of his losing drafts he blamed largely on being **removal-light** ("zero removal deck," "I really had no removal," "if I had drawn just one or two more things / a removal spell"). Prioritize interaction highly.
- **Curving out + playing first is very strong.** Recurring lament against opponents: "playing first and curving out is OP," "one drop, two drop, three drop on the play is nuts." Several of his Arena losses were to opponents who simply curved a clean 2→3→4 of villains and ran him over. He notes the format has a fast aggressive vector even though the bomb-rare games can grind.
- **Two-drops are scarce and worth prioritizing.** His decks repeatedly bottlenecked on the 2-slot — "I need some lower-curve stuff," "I just need a better two-drop," "the two drops are just going to be better" (cutting a cute rare for them). A **turn-2 Undercover Skrull / land-cycler "scroll"** is the dream curve-enabler — "any time you have a turn-two scroll you're probably pretty happy."
- **Bomb rares decide a lot of games.** Both VODs are full of "their deck is real good / they're just on a bunch of rares" — he lost a game to back-to-back **Crimson Operative** + **Iron Man** value, and won games purely off above-rate fatties (**Dr. Doom**, **Hulk**, **Fin Fang Foom**). "Sometimes you just need to play some above-rate chonkers."
- **The format supports 10 archetypes and rewards navigation/pivoting.** "Remember, there are 10 supported archetypes — a lot of times you'll see cards good in multiple strategies and you'll want to pivot." He soft-forces early ("I don't mind forcing some strategy at the start of a format when people don't know what they're doing, me included") but stays flexible.
- **Soup / splash-heavy decks are very buildable thanks to abundant fixing.** **Ant-Man's Army** (food/treasure), the **"scrolls"** (Undercover Skrull-type land-tap fixers), **basic land-cyclers**, and colorless/any-color lands (**Baxter Building**, etc.) let you splash freely. He literally built a 4–5 color "soup" deck in the release-day VOD and predicts: "later in the format I'm going to draft nothing but Ant-Man's Army five-color decks — say screw your synergies." (⚠ This is a fun-prediction, not a tested verdict — his soup decks were "cool, not good.")
- **Land counts:** he ran **17 land / 41-or-so-card** ratios ("17-41, the perfect ratio") in the greedy multicolor decks, leaning on cyclers/treasures/scrolls to smooth. Nothing settled — just his default-feel here.
- **Villain curve-out is a real, oppressive aggressive plan.** "This card is so sick for villain decks — multiple opponents just curved out two-drop villain, three-drop villain into [a payoff] on turn four and it's basically unwinnable." He repeatedly flags getting run over by black-red / red-black villain curves.

## Archetypes

*Only lightly sampled — Kenji drafted a Simic-ish +1/+1 splash pile and a 4–5c villain/ramp soup pile across the two VODs, and faced a handful of opponents. Ranks below are impressions, NOT a hierarchy.*

### Blue-Green (Simic / +1/+1 counters) — what he drafted in VOD 2
- **Plan:** the "+1/+1 counter synergy deck" (his words). Counter-makers + counter payoffs; he leaned on **Ant-Man, Colony Commander**, **Mister Hyde, Monster Within**, **Hulkling, Burgeoning Bruiser**, **We Say Thee Nay!** (connive = counter), **Trickster's Stratagem** (connive). Splashed black removal.
- **His verdict:** disappointed — went 4 wins and felt he underperformed ("with four Ant-Man's Armies I feel like we should have done more... needed more top end"). He noticed mid-draft the deck was pulled between **counters** and **artifacts** (Moon Girl and Devil Dinosaur, Iron Lad want artifacts, not counters) and never fully committed. Lesson he drew: **don't straddle the counters lane and the artifacts lane** — they pull apart.
- **Note:** **Blue-Red is the artifact archetype** (he confirmed, repeatedly passing/avoiding Iron Man into it). Blue-Green is the counters deck; don't expect blue's artifact payoffs to slot in.

### 4–5 color "soup" / villain ramp — what he drafted in VOD 1
- **Plan:** abundant fixing (Ant-Man's Army treasures, scrolls, land-cyclers, any-color lands) into the best bombs regardless of color. He splashed **Dr. Doom** (UB-ish), **Thanos, the Mad Titan** (for the "funsies"), **Ka-Zar of the Savage Land**, **Fin Fang Foom**, **The Super Hero Civil War**, plus green/red removal.
- **His verdict:** "cool, not good" — went **4 wins**; "a million powerful cards" but "removal is a bit lacking and the mana base could be sketchy." Won games when the bombs aligned (the famous **Thanos snap** game), lost when he stumbled on mana or drew no early plays. **Realistic takeaway:** soup is *very* buildable because fixing is everywhere, but a release-week soup pile that's bomb-heavy and interaction-light still loses to clean aggressive curve-outs.

### Villain aggro (black-red / red-black) — what beat him repeatedly
- He didn't draft it but **kept losing to it**: "another red-black villain deck, huh?" Two-drop villain → three-drop villain → a turn-4 villain payoff that "is basically unwinnable" when you're on the back foot. The villain-matters connive/menace package curves out hard. Worth respecting as an aggressive opponent even though LoL rates Rakdos low (see vs LoL).

### Other observed pairs (one-line impressions)
- **Green-White heroes:** the deck that "does heroes with good cards"; an opponent's go-wide hero-flyer board (Black Panther, Vanguard pumping a team) closed out a game on him.
- **Blue-Red artifacts:** confirmed as the artifact lane; an opponent's Iron Lad + Crimson Operative + Iron Man value pile buried him on card advantage. Real, but he avoided it.
- **Blue-Black:** he flagged the rare UB dual ("as long as they have a basic they're drawing cards") and called a UB opener "a good draw" but didn't build it. Archetype identity not yet sampled.

## Card interactions & combos

*Few and shallow — only what actually came up in two drafts.*

1. **Absorbing Man + the opponent's permanents** — copies "up to one target artifact, non-enchantment, or land" — and crucially **can copy the OPPONENT's stuff**, not just yours. Kenji copied an opponent's flyer to find lethal, and copied an opponent's **Death to Our Enemies** (a non-creature it can legally become since it's not an enchantment). Caveat he hit: the copy lasts "until your next turn," so a permanent-removal effect can catch the copied body in an awkward window. Also a soft ramp/blink-value piece (it's a 3-mana 4/4 vigilance baseline).
2. **Thanos, the Mad Titan — the "snap"** — 3-mana 4/4 deathtouch lifelink; power up **WUBRG + colorless** puts two counters on him and **destroys each other creature with mana value of the chosen parity (odd/even)**. A one-sided wrath if you build your board around the off-parity. The enabling problem: the power-up needs **colorless mana**, and "I don't know how many ways there are to add colorless." He paid it via **treasures / any-color sources** (and noted **Baxter Building**'s any-color mode covers the colorless pip). When it fires it's backbreaking ("Thanos got to freaking snap").
3. **Fin Fang Foom + a spell targeting an artifact/land** — 4-mana 3/5 flyer; whenever you cast an instant/sorcery that **targets an artifact or land, copy it** (new targets) and grow Fin Fang Foom. He set this up by playing **Ant-Man's Army** (which leaves a **treasure** artifact), then casting **Go Nuts!** targeting an *artifact creature* / using treasures as legal copy targets — "if this works out it looks absurd." Wants artifact creatures (or your own treasures) on board to chain.
4. **World War Hulk / Incredible Hulk (?) self-ping** — the big reach-trample Hulk gets a +1/+1 counter and untaps when dealt damage while attacking; **ping your own Hulk in combat to keep untapping/attacking.** (Caption-garbled between "Incredible Hulk" and World War Hulk; the printed bomb here is **World War Hulk** / **Red Hulk** enrage — treat the line as "enrage Hulks reward being damaged.")
5. **Photon Blast Barrage X-count** — "remember it's the number of times it's copied" — paying X=3 deals **4** instances of damage (the original split + copies), not 3. Easy to misread. Also pairs with **Loki Laufeyson** (copy your next cheap instant/sorcery) for extra reach — but only if you actually have instants/sorceries (see Pitfall on Loki).
6. **Doctor Doom indestructibility** — 6-mana, makes two extra 3/3 artifact-creature tokens and is **indestructible as long as you control an artifact creature or a plan**, plus draws a card / loses 1 each end step. "Better Precursor Golem"; the token-army keeps him indestructible. "This card annihilated me during early access" — one of the best rares.
7. **Madame Masque / connive payoffs + "draw your second card"** — several black cards (Madame Masque, Kang-type effects) trigger on **drawing your second card each turn**; treasures/cantrips that draw extra turn it on. (Lightly referenced; not deeply explored.)

## Card tips

*Pulled from actual picks/games in 2 release-window VODs. `(?)` = uncertain caption mapping. Sparse by design.*

### White (W)
- **Web Up** — Good O-Ring removal; he repeatedly flagged it as "good removal spell" and used it to answer a key threat. One of white's premium answers in a removal-scarce format.
- **Black Panther, Vanguard** — Opponent's hero-go-wide engine pumped a team and closed a game on him; respected as a real GW payoff.

### Blue (U)
- **We Say Thee Nay!** — Took the connive/counter mode happily ("let's take the counter there"); a flexible counter that also feeds +1/+1 / connive synergies.
- **Trickster's Stratagem** — "I've been a fan of this" — synergistic (connive = counter trigger). A repeat pick in his counters deck.
- **Wiccan, Rising Magician** — "Flicker guy," good with ETB-value cards; he picked it to re-buy his enters-the-battlefield value. Caveat he hit: the cast-trigger is a **forced** flicker on a non-creature spell, which can be awkward when you have nothing good to blink (he blinked a creature he didn't want to). Note Wiccan **can't flicker itself.**
- **Echo, Perceptive Prodigy** — "Really fun"; copies an activated/triggered ability — strong free-value engine (his words mirror the LoL "you do no work" read).
- **Iron Lad, Diverging Destiny** — Good in the artifact (UR) deck; an opponent's was "really good." Wants artifact density.
- **Moon Girl and Devil Dinosaur** — Pulled him toward artifacts mid-draft; he ultimately judged **Echo is just better** in his shell, and that Moon Girl needs both artifact density AND ways to draw your second card, which his deck lacked. "Sad to say."
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Deployment Drone** — "Great"; he was delighted to see it **wheel** ("wheeling is so sick to me") — a real two-for-one flyer-plus-token every blue drafter wants. Multiples late = blue/artifacts open.
- **Atlantis Attacks** — "Really devastating to curve into," and "only a common." Teamwork it to bounce two things + a 6/5 hexproof Leviathan. A game-ender; "the Kraken/Leviathan wins again."

### Black (B)
- **Cruel Alliance** — Repeatedly his removal-of-choice when desperate ("I'm probably just going to take this Cruel Alliance"); exile a small creature, or exile anything + gain 3 with teamwork. Premium flexible removal in the removal desert.
- **Hour of Defeat** — "Some more removal" / "fantastic"; he used it as a catch-all destroy. Solid, takeable.
- **Dr. Doom (Doctor Doom)** — Top-tier rare; "annihilated me in early access," "one of the better rares, not close." Indestructible token-army threat (see combos). Worth splashing/building toward.
- **Thanos, the Mad Titan** — Splashable 4/4 deathtouch lifelink with a board-wipe power-up; **the highlight bomb of the run** ("World First Thanos Snap"). High ceiling, but the colorless power-up cost is a real build constraint (see combos).
- **The Super Hero Civil War** — "Absurd / disgusting... one of the best rares in the set, not close." Powerful but "so awkward" to cast/use. He took it on raw power despite the awkwardness.
- **Kingpin's Enforcers** — Played as a sac-outlet / board piece; a fine repeatable token/villain engine in black graveyard shells.
- **Death to Our Enemies** — A black plan he ran in the soup deck; "if that's your first play of the game that's where you want it" (good on curve, bad as a topdeck — the plan-counter ramp wants to start early). At 4 counters deals 7 split among two targets + makes a treasure.

### Red (R)
- **Crimson Operative** — "Nice red common," and it **wheeled hard** (he saw 3–4 copies one draft). A 3/2 prowess artifact that impulse-draws on ETB — a real two-for-one. Note: he warned it's "not that good *without removal*" — it's a value body, not a finisher.
- **Punishing Punch** — *(green, listed under green below — flagged here because he took it over a red/gold card P1P2 for removal.)*
- **Photon Blast Barrage** / **Repulsor Blast** — Red's removal he scooped up in the soup deck; Photon Blast is better than its "X-for-Y" math looks (see combos) but bad into high toughness.
- **Lightning Strike** — Seen but de-prioritized; passed it for bigger payoffs. Stock burn, nothing special (consistent with the consensus "deal 3 misses the 4-toughness cards").
- **Loki Laufeyson** / **Kid Loki** — "So solid / so sick," and great removal-bait — but **dead without instants/sorceries to copy.** Kenji's hard-learned caveat: "we ended up with four sorceries, no instants, so these Lokis are actually kind of bad — still going to play it, but..." Only good with a real spell density.

### Green (G)
- **Punishing Punch** — **His marquee early take:** P1P2'd it over a signpost gold card because "removal is premium." Single-green, instant-speed, deals 2×power — kills almost anything in green-black. Premium.
- **Go Nuts!** — "One-mana removal" (fight) and a counter-maker; a repeat high pick. Note the teamwork requirement (need 3 power already on board to pay it). Green's cheap interaction is a big selling point.
- **Ka-Zar of the Savage Land** — "Amazing," and he saw **multiples** ("another Ka-Zar, yikes"). 5-mana play-lands-from-top engine + a growing Zabu cat token. A green bomb-uncommon; takes over long games.
- **Ant-Man's Army** — "Very good common," and the **fixing/soup glue** he prized most — "every Ant-Man's Army I see." 3-mana 3/2 that makes a food or treasure: ramp, fixing, splash-enabler, artifact-count, all in one. He ran **four** in one deck.
- **Undercover Skrull** ("the scroll"(?)) — The **premium two-drop fixer** he kept wanting ("turn-two scroll, you're probably pretty happy"). Ramps 2→4 and becomes a body late. Take it for the curve + fixing.
- **Restorative Technique** — "Kind of nice" — 3 mana, gain 2, fetch a basic to the battlefield, put a +1/+1 counter. Fixing + lifegain + a counter; nearly made his counters deck.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Killmonger, Scourge of Wakanda** — "A very decent splash card"; removal (sac → destroy a non-land permanent) + a body that becomes a 5/4 with 2 creatures in yard. He splashed it. Strong (LoL rates it the best gold uncommon by far).
- **Iron Man, Master of Machines** — The **blue-red artifact signpost**; he repeatedly considered it then passed, concluding he wasn't in the artifact lane. An opponent's was a real card-advantage engine. Good only when you're committed to artifacts and nobody's fighting you for it.
- **Hulk, Gamma Goliath** / **World War Hulk** / **Red Hulk** — Big reach-trample beaters; "Hulk is great." Punish you for not killing them (counters/enrage). He won games just jamming them as above-rate chonkers. Note the modal cast (e.g. pay RRGG+2 to play the big side directly rather than as a small creature you transform).
- **Fin Fang Foom** — 4-mana 3/5 flyer with a spell-copy engine (see combos); a strong gold/villain top-end he built around in VOD 1.
- **Ant-Man, Colony Commander** — The GU counters signpost; he ran it but found it "asks for a lot of mana and work for a minor payoff" (echoes LoL's lukewarm read). Fine, not exciting.
- **Hercules, Prince of Power** — "Solid"; 3-mana 3/3 that levels to a 4/4 with vigilance/indestructible/haste on the level-up turn. A passed-up role-player he liked.

### Colorless / artifacts / lands
- **Baxter Building** — Took it as fixing (any-color) AND a card-draw engine ("4, tap, draw a card if you control a 4-toughness creature" — trivial here). He **kept forgetting the draw mode** mid-game ("how long have I been forgetting about the card draw?") — a strong stall-breaker, don't sleep on it. Also pays Thanos's colorless pip.
- **Dependable Quinjet** — A fixing vehicle he took for mana smoothing in the soup deck; cuttable once you have enough fixing.
- **Captain America's Shield** — Black/format-wide indestructible equipment; he ran into it from the opponent side and **misplayed around it** (got blown out — see Pitfalls). Respect it.

## Pitfalls

*Mostly Kenji's own logged misplays across the two VODs — concrete and worth internalizing.*

- **Main-phase-kill a creature before it can get an indestructible/protection equipment.** His single most costly punt: he held removal on a **Titania** intending to use Hour of Defeat at instant speed, but "black has that equipment that gives indestructible" (**Captain America's Shield** / similar) — the opponent equipped it and the kill spell bricked. "I should have just main-phased killed the Titania. That's going to cost me." **In a format with indestructible-granting equipment, don't slow-roll removal on a threat that can be protected.**
- **Don't over-respect, but DO play around the few format-warping commons.** He got blown out by an **attack-alone** first-strike/menace creature he forgot the text on, and by indestructible equipment. Read the small synergy text on villains/heroes before combat.
- **Don't straddle two synergy lanes.** His VOD-2 deck drifted between **+1/+1 counters** and **artifacts** (Moon Girl/Iron Lad want artifacts; the rest want counters) and committed to neither — "maybe I don't need this counter... I have four creatures that make artifacts but not enough ways to draw extra cards." Pick a lane and feed it.
- **Loki / spell-copy cards need real spell density.** "We ended up with four sorceries, no instants — these Lokis are actually kind of bad." Don't draft spell-copy payoffs (Loki Laufeyson, Photon Blast chains, Fin Fang Foom) without enough instants/sorceries to fuel them.
- **Count copies correctly on X-spells.** **Photon Blast Barrage** at X=3 makes **4** damage instances (original + 3 copies), not 3. Misreading the copy count loses damage/tempo.
- **Mind Absorbing Man's copy duration.** The copy lasts only "until your next turn," and copying the opponent's permanent can leave a removable body in a bad window — sequence it when you actually need the effect, not preemptively.
- **Don't over-value cute rares over curve.** Twice he sided out / cut a "cool but not good" rare (**Secret Invasion** / Alien Invasion-type enchantment) for a plain two-drop, concluding "the two drops are just going to be better — this is not the right deck for it." Curve > novelty, especially when you're already removal-light.
- **Don't misclassify hero vs villain in combat math.** He "threw away an extra creature for no reason — I thought the warrior was a villain." Several cards care about hero/villain typing; check before you sac/block/trigger.
- **Don't trust early-access results as your Arena baseline.** His own warning: he crushed paper early access then went 0-3 / 4-win on release-day Arena. The field plays differently and tighter — recalibrate.
- **Splash discipline:** the format's deep fixing (Ant-Man's Army, scrolls, cyclers, any-color lands) makes splashing easy, but his **removal-light, bomb-heavy soup decks still lost to curve-outs.** Splash for *removal and a couple bombs*, not for a pile of fatties with no early game.

# FIN — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Blue-black is the premier color combination.** Kenji repeatedly says he "loves blue" in this format and that the blue-black archetype has strong card selection (Salvager, Syncopate, Stuck(?), Retrieve the Esper) that other color pairs lack. "Red-white often times doesn't have the good card selection that blue and black have access to."
- **Towns matter — Travel the Overworld is better than it looks.** Kenji references the "towns deck" mechanic (Town Greeter(?) triggers when you hit a town name?) and mentions under-valuing Travel the Overworld then regretting it. 17Lands data showed Travel lower than expected (56.4%) but Kenji suspects people don't build the deck correctly. "Could be lower as a result of people not drafting towns as well as they should be."
- **Moderate speed — not hyper-aggro.** Kenji describes the format as "not particularly fast" but also not slow. Going up to 7-8 mana spells is feasible. Combat is interactive, not race-oriented.
- **Removal quality varies by color.** Black has premium removal (Vein's Treachery = exile, Final Days = wrath, Evil Reawakened = reanimate/kill, Deadly Dispute = sac + draw). Red has burn (Blazing Bomb, Thunder Magic). Blue has counters (Stuck(?), Syncopate, Swallowed by Leviathan). White has limited options.
- **Side Quests are premium anchors.** Side Quest "Hunt the Mark" is called "probably the best individual card" in one pack. Side Quests generate treasures and flip into large threats. "Side quest and lance probably have similar win rates" — side quest first pick worthy.
- **Atraxa(?) is the mythic bomb.** Kenji says he's gone 7-0 twice with Atraxa in the format. "7-7 flying vigilance deathtouch lifelink usually is going to draw you at least three cards." Passed multiple other bombs to take it. Won with it third time too. Strong enough to justify multi-color builds.
- **Emperor of Palamecia(?) is the key blue 2-drop.** "Is this what it's like to draw your Emperor on turn two? The nuts." Triggers on every spell cast while it's in play. Missing it early leads to slow starts.
- **Sahagin(?) is a top aggressive threat.** Multiple decks feature triple Sahagin from opponents. Kenji loses to it when he can't counter it. "Triple Sahag again, huh? I don't know if we can beat this realistically."
- **Format rewards 170+ drafts to master.** "We're pushing a 170 or so drafts of the format." Deep card knowledge from extended grinding.

## Archetypes

### Green-Black Value/Wide (Golgari)
- **Gameplan:** Ramp with Gladiololis(?) (green 2-drop that makes lands/Chocobos?), generate value with Town Greeter (mills 3, gains 2 life for hitting a town), and grind with Final Days (wrath flashback), Evil Reawakened (reanimate + kill), Fight On (flashback reanimate two creatures from yard).
- **Key cards:** Gladiololis (strong green 2-drop, "pretty great"), Town Greeter (mills 3, can hit removal or value cards; "I milled my evil reawakened and my fight on"), Final Days ("our best card versus just mono removal"), Evil Reawakened ("nice" — kill or reanimate), Deadly Dispute (sac + draw two + treasure), Malboro/Malbero (?) (4-drop that mills opponents or self? — "getting back Malbero and Rexar kind of disgusting"), Rexar/Rexxar (?) (strong midrange threat), Circle of Power (enchantment — gives lifelink to wizards?), Black Mage's Rod (1-drop equipment ping?).
- **Strength:** Wide board develops fast, Final Days resets against single-threat decks. Fight On + Evil Reawakened gives recursive value.
- **Weakness:** Town Greeter can mill important pieces (Kenji milled both reanimation spells). Vulnerable to exile effects.

### Blue-Black Control (Dimir)
- **Gameplan:** Counter and bounce threats, generate card advantage with Emperor of Palamecia(?) and Retrieve the Esper (reanimation/draw?), win with Atraxa or large creatures. Use Stuck(?) (enchantment-based counter) and Syncopate(?) to deny threats.
- **Key cards:** Side Quest (treasure generation + 7/7 threat?), Emperor of Palamecia(?) ("Is this what it's like to draw your emperor on turn two — the nuts"), Swallowed by Leviathan (large blue counter/bounce?), Stuck(?) (versatile interaction — "stuck the lance, pass"), Syncopate (counter for 1 unless pay extra), Retrieve the Esper (reanimate?), Scorpion Sentinel (flash blocker?), Sahogen/Sahagan (?) (common blue threat), Combat Tutorial (2/2 flash creature?), Dragoon's Lance (equipment), Brinhilder(?) (strong uncommon white/blue?), Ring of the Lucii(?) ("Ring").
- **Strength:** Deep interaction. Multiple ways to find answers. Syncopate is the best one-mana counter. Atraxa as bomb kills games quickly.
- **Weakness:** Needs 2-drop on curve or falls behind. "Play versus draw difference" is enormous with Emperor.

### Red-White Aggro / Winota (Boros)
- **Gameplan:** Curve aggressively with non-human creatures + humans to maximize Winota/Bartz Klauser (?) triggers. Use burn (Thunder Magic = Lightning Bolt, Chocobo Comet = 3-damage + flashback?), equipment (Buster Sword, Coral Sword, Paladin's Arms), and Moogle/Delivery Moogle (?) (tutors Phoenix Down or equipment).
- **Key cards:** Winota / Bartz Klauser (4-mana — whenever non-human attacks, put human from top-6 onto battlefield attacking with indestructible; "Winota is Winota"), Chocobo Comet (3-damage spell with flashback — "one of the cards that can win you the game when you're flooding out"), Coral Sword (equipment/combat trick — "think of it more as a combat trick"), Minwu(?) (human cleric that pumps clerics?; "great with white mage's staff"), White Mage's Staff (triggers life gain + makes a cleric token on attack), Gladiololis (green human — "Gladiololis is a human, I'm absolutely playing it"), Stiltskin(?) (2-drop life gain?), Gala Cat (strong artifact payoff — "surprised at how good the Gala Cat has been"), Phoenix Down (reanimate — "it generally tends to wheel"), Moogle/Delivery Moogle (tutors Phoenix Down).
- **Strength:** Winota hits are explosive. Minwu + White Mage's Staff + Cleric token = +2/+2 pump + life gain on attack. "I hate Boros in this format but if you are going to play Boros, this is a good reason to do so."
- **Weakness:** Flood vulnerability without card selection. Color screw kills you — "white is my main color, I have drawn every green source." Humans are scarce; Winota without humans is a 4/4 for 4 — "not very good."
- **Humans in red-white:** Minwu, Gladiololis, Zack (?), Weapons Vendor(?), Item Shopkeep, and job-select cards (clerics etc.). Non-humans trigger Winota; humans are the payoff. Need 4-6 humans minimum to make Winota live.

### Blue Tempo (Mono-blue or UR/UW)
- **Gameplan:** Curve small threats, bounce key permanents (Stuck, Chocoo Kick?), draw with Lab Maniac(?) or Emperor, win with evasion + counter backup.
- **Key cards:** Emperor of Palamecia (key 2-drop — do not miss), Sahogen/Sahagin (top common), Syncopate (best cheap interaction), Swallowed by Leviathan (hard removal), Retrieve the Esper.
- **Note:** Even a poorly drafted blue deck can 7-win on good draws: "I have a very good creature curve and with three stucks maybe this isn't that bad."

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Winota + Gladiololis:** Gladiololis is a human and a non-human trigger-capable creature (if you have other non-humans attacking). Winota hits Gladiololis off the top and puts it into play attacking with indestructible. Gladiololis also gives other creatures trample but does NOT give itself trample. "Gladiolus gives other creatures trample though. Play Winota, attack, hit Gladiolus — that's hot."

2. **White Mage's Staff + Minwu + Cleric token:** Staff makes a cleric token on attack. Minwu pumps all clerics on attack and gains life equal to clercs' power. With Staff + Minwu attacking: the token triggers Minwu's pump, Minwu gains first strike and lifelink via Staff trigger, so first strike damage from Minwu is calculated before normal combat at higher power. Kenji: "Staff triggers on attack pumps both my creatures and then the first strike with Minwu's first strike life link which then will pump the coral before it would normally deal damage." They took exactly 11 — lethal.

3. **Moogle + Phoenix Down:** Moogle tutors Phoenix Down (or White Mage's Staff). Phoenix Down reanimates a creature then exiles it from your graveyard. When Phoenix Down itself goes to graveyard, Moogle can grab it again or grab the creature it got back. "Phoenix Down getting the Moogle... the Moogle can grab the Phoenix Down and then the Down can get back the Moogle once it goes away."

4. **Side Quest (Hunt the Mark?) + treasure production:** Side Quest flips when you have 3+ treasures. Treasures come from multiple sources (Cane of Byr(?) (?), Deadly Dispute sac, blitz ball sac, Oz(?)/Öza(?) artifact). Once flipped, side quest becomes a large threat. Always preserve at least 3 treasures before flip; don't spend them frivolously.

5. **Atraxa + treasure/colored source:** Atraxa needs W/U/B/G in play. Treasures provide any color — with 2 Treasures you only need two natural sources. Kenji used Kane's attack trigger (attack + get treasures) to generate off-color mana. "I already have all four of the color requirements met for Attra." Kane + side quest makes Atraxa very castable.

6. **Deadly Dispute + Evil Reawakened timing:** Deadly Dispute (sac artifact + draw 2 + treasure) puts creatures from hand/board into graveyard. If you sac a creature (not just artifact), you can Reawaken it next turn. Kenji noted "I could have zombified the plunder" — tracking what hits the yard from Dispute is important.

7. **Malboro/Malbero(?) + opponent's empty hand:** Malboro has some hand-attack effect ("now if they rip a good card off the top..."). When opponent is hellbent, Malboro as a blocker or threat forces them to play off the top. Reanimating Malboro via Fight On while opponent is out of resources was "disgusting."

8. **Zenos(?) — ETB NOT death trigger:** In VOD 3, Kenji loses a won game by bouncing his own Oz(?)/Öza because he forgot it flips when it leaves the battlefield (any zone change), not only death. Bouncing it triggered its flip/activated win condition for opponent. "Obviously I just died. I die exactly. Bouncing the Zenos didn't make sense." Never bounce permanents that have "leaves the battlefield" triggers unless you WANT them to trigger.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Dragoon's Lance** — 61.4% win rate per 17Lands (highest Kenji checked). Excellent equipment for blue-using decks.

### Blue (U)
- **Syncopate** — 1-mana counter (unless they pay extra). Best cheap interaction in blue. Always run; never leave pack.
- **Swallowed by Leviathan** — Large blue removal/counter. Kenji ran multiple copies. Counters unless pay 3+.
- **Combat Tutorial** — Kenji runs it as a cheap flash creature; "first play of the game" option.
- **Retrieve the Esper(?)** — Blue sorcery that ETBs two 3/3s (and maybe draws/reanimates?). "Retrieve crew. Tap. No." — used as a finisher. Good in late-game blue control.
- **Travel the Overworld / World Map(?)** — Under-valued. "I was mentioning people not valuing the lands high enough and then here I am not taking the lands presumably high enough." Take them earlier when running a towns-focused build.

### Black (B)
- **Side Quest (Hunt the Mark)** — "Probably the best individual card" in one pack. Treasure generation + large threat. First-pick worthy.
- **Emperor of Palamecia(?)** — Blue 2-drop, most important card in Dimir. "Is this what it's like to draw your emperor on turn two? The nuts." Miss this and your deck underperforms.

### Red (R)
- **Coral Sword** — Red-white equipment with combat trick function. "Think of it more of a combat trick." Cheap and relevant.

### Green (G)
- **Chocobo Comet** — Damage spell that also has flashback. "One of the cards that can win you the game when you're flooding out in these type of decks." High pick in red decks.
- **Gala Cat** — Artifact payoff — "surprised at how good the Gala Cat has been." Run 1-2 in artifact-dense builds (6+ artifacts).

### Multicolor / gold
- **Cloud of Darkness** — 60% win rate per 17Lands. Blue/black? top-end. Kenji noted it was the highest raw WR in the pack he checked.
- **Atraxa** — Auto first-pick. Kenji went 7-0 three times with it. Demands multicolor mana fixing but treasures solve it.

### Colorless / artifacts
- **Buster Sword** — Powerful equipment (2 to equip — "pretty good"). Makes your sword carrier a significant threat. "If you are going to play Boros, this is a good reason to do so."

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Gladiololis** — Strong green 2-drop ("Gladiololis is great"). Also a human (relevant for Winota). Also not just in green decks — splashable.
- **White Arasite(?)** — 59.2% win rate per 17Lands. Strong white common.
- **Queen Bronhy(?)** — Low win rate (52.2% per 17Lands). Kenji: "Terrible stats. Yeah I am going way far down for Queen." Avoid. "Whenever my opponent has this card, I can't kill it. Whenever I play it, it's just absolute dead."
- **Primal Garuda** — Kenji: "I am not a fan of Primal Garuda. Primal Garuda is probably real bad."
- **Aith Gainsboro(?)** — "It's an okay card. I don't think it's actually as good as say Travel the Overworld, Cloud of Darkness." Do not over-value — seems stronger than it is.

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting Boros without enough humans for Winota.** Winota is a 4/4 for 4 without humans. Kenji had only 3-4 humans after the draft: "Oh man, this might be the worst Winota I've ever seen." Actively draft Item Shopkeep, Weapons Vendor, Gladiololis, Minwu, Zack early when Winota is your P1P1.
- **Splashing without fixing.** Kenji regrets not taking Travel the Overworld / World Map(?) as fixing in a 4-color-splash deck. "I was mentioning people not valuing the lands high enough and then here I am." Fixing lands are premium in this multicolor format.
- **Bouncing your own "leaves the battlefield" permanents.** Zenos, Oz(?), and similar cards trigger on leaves-battlefield, not just death. Bouncing to "save" them triggers their flip/end condition. Fatal error — Kenji lost a won game this way.
- **No removal = Shantado/Cerberus(?) wins.** Kenji's high-bomb blue-black deck had "just no removal" and lost repeatedly to opponents resolving a single relevant threat. "As you can see, we just don't have much to do with them or we don't have the ways to survive till them." Run at least 3-4 pieces of hard removal.
- **Running Queen Bronhy/Aith despite bad stats.** 52.2% and 55% WR cards drag down overall deck quality. Don't take them over proven pieces.
- **Not playing Town Greeter optimally.** Kenji milled both Evil Reawakened and Fight On with Town Greeter, stranding him without reanimation. "I milled my evil reawakened and my fight on. Well, shoot. I milled both of my ways to get back my creatures." When you know your deck has key recursive pieces, be aware of what Greeter mills.
- **Holding too many lands with Gladiololis.** Multiple times Kenji says "I should hold my lands" but then mistakenly plays them. Gladiololis(?) wants lands in hand to pump itself or make Chocobos. "Oh I should be holding my lands. Whoopsies." Always check which cards want lands held vs. played.

# KHM — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
Kaldheim (KHM) is a midrange-to-slow format with a strong snow-matters theme. Snow synergies are the most rewarding and consistent plan. Red-white aggro (Giants / Warriors) is a real and fast counter-strategy. Kenji notes his sealed win rate was 80% but draft win rate was just over 50% — implying sealed rewards raw card quality while draft requires reading the table carefully. Giants is "one of the best strategies in the format, it's not even like we're forcing." The format supports multiple winning axes: snow control/midrange, Giants aggro, Elves (green-black), Warriors (green-white), and Runes/Equipment builds.

## Archetypes
- **Blue-Red Snow (primary)**: Mist Walker (3-mana 2/2 flier, likely hex-proof while untapped) is a cornerstone. Bears of Lujara (?) (3-mana enchantment: make a 2/2 Changeling, pump all shapeshifters/changelings to 4/4, or kill a creature) is the centerpiece bomb. Basalt Ravager pings for X where X is snow permanents — extremely good with multiple snow lands. Demon Bolt and Squash are the premium removal. Pillar of the Forsaken (?) taps creatures. Changeling creatures like Pilfering Hawk and Bloodline Pretender count for all tribes simultaneously.
- **Green-Blue Snow (trophy-winning build)**: Coma (Cosmos Charger / Koma, Cosmos Serpent — 7-mana 6/6 that makes 3/3 tokens and stops being targeted) is the top-end finisher. Use (Glittering Frost / double Frostpire?) to generate snow mana; double Glittering Frost makes mana-fixing trivial and lets you splash anything. Blizzard Brawl is key cheap removal. Sculptor of Winter is a snow-generating ramp piece — always take.
- **White Aggro (Warriors/Giants)**: Shepherd of the Cosmos (3-mana 3/3 flier that gets back a 2-drop) is a strong threat. Reinforcements (token makers), Packmate, Champion (?) synergize. Fast curve is essential.
- **Green-White Warriors**: Go-wide strategy; Rally the Ranks with 8+ Warriors can close games. Maya (5-mana landfall card that pumps and generates tokens) is the key finisher.
- **Red-White Runes/Equipment**: Runed Crown (?) lets you grab Runes for free. Elven Bow (enchant creature: +2/+2, flying, reach, haste — 3 mana) is "legitimately cracked." Pick is a recurring artifact. Runed Crown eighth-pick is possible.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Mist Walker + Bears of Lujara(?)**: "If you can curve Changeling into Bears of Lujara then you can never lose." Mist Walker is a changeling, so Bears pumps it to 4/4 immediately; the permanent pump effect applies to all shapeshifters including prior changelings.
- **Basalt Ravager + snow lands**: At 2–3 snow permanents it's a 4-damage ping; at 5+ it becomes a kill-anything effect. "Pilfering Hawk into Mist Walker into Basalt Ravager — kill your re-tough creature, shake my hand."
- **Frostpire + multiple Demon Bolts**: Frostpire (artifact that copies instant/sorceries when you cast them?) makes three Bolts effectively into six removal spells. "Three demon bolts with Frostpire is also kind of nuts."
- **Sculptor of Winter + Glittering Frost**: Sculptor taps snow lands for additional mana; Glittering Frost (enchant land: tap for any color + makes it a snow land) provides blue/red mana from forests. Two copies of each makes any 3–4 color deck trivially consistent.
- **Coma (Koma?) + Use (Inga and Esika? / Binding of the Old Gods?)**: Coma is hard to kill through normal creature removal since it's only hit by certain spells; using The Binding (?) to get a creature back or lock something down protects it. "Feed the Serpent still kills Coma" — a notable out opponents have.
- **Runed Crown + Runes**: Crown searches for a Rune and puts it into hand. Rune of Might (trample), Rune of Speed (haste), Rune of Flight (flying + reach) are all premium. Crown found eighth-pick shows how underdrafted this package is.
- **Elven Bow + Hoggy Mob (?)**: Giving haste to a big late-game creature for 3 mana creates a surprise threat; Kenji notes "Hasty hoggy mob — big old hasty hoggy mob" as a game-winner.
- **Seize the Spoils + Frostpire**: Seize (draw a card, discard a card) triggers Frostpire copy; gives card selection plus extra removal.

## Card tips

### Blue (U)
- **Mist Walker**: "One of the best three-drops in the format. What does Mist Walker not good with? Nothing." Take every copy. Hex-proof while untapped means it only dies at instant speed after you tap it.
- **Pilfering Hawk**: One-mana looter; "pilpering Hawk into Mist Walker into Basalt Ravager" is the optimal curve.
- **Giants Amulet**: Gives hexproof ONLY while the creature is untapped. "Do not forget about that" — critical for sequencing attacks vs. holding up combat tricks.

### Red (R)
- **Demon Bolt**: Premium removal; "second Bolt over Avalanche Caller" is correct — prioritize safe, flexible cards over synergies.
- **Crush the Weak**: Situationally good (kills aggressive tokens and 1-toughness creatures), bad against snow mirrors — run 0–1; can swap in/out based on matchup.

### Green (G)
- **Sculptor of Winter**: "I have to take Sculptor, I think taking this is too important." A snow-generating two-drop that becomes a blocker doubles as mana fixing.
- **Glittering Frost**: Not early priority but with double copies provides enough fixing to splash any color freely.
- **Blizzard Brawl**: "Brawl's really good" — fight spell that requires a snow permanent; early pick in snow decks.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Bears of Lujara (?)**: First-pick slam even on a splash; still good late-game, not just a turn-3 play. The third chapter (kill a creature) provides removal value even without the creature pump.
- **Lindworm (Ravenous Lindworm)**: Seventh-eighth pick value; large body that draws a card when it dies, strong in green snow midrange.
- **Sees the Spoils**: "Just ask and you get what you need" — reliable looting that digs for missing colors or key spells.
- **Snow lands**: "Taking snow lands is good, why would that be your bad" — always draft them when available; all snow synergies require snow permanents.

## Pitfalls
- **Not foretelling Struggle (?) when you should**: Kenji identified a game loss where casting Struggle (?) the turn before instead of foretelling would have left up interaction for the opponent's threat. "If I had foretold the Struggle we would have been okay."
- **Attacking with valuable creatures unnecessarily**: Multiple instances of attacking with creatures Kenji later regretted ("I did mess up — I should have looted before attacking"); each attack represents risk against unknown combat tricks.
- **Double-blocking into trample with pump in hand**: Opponent had a pump spell and Kenji's double-block resulted in losing two creatures. Check for pump before committing to blocks.
- **Moving equipment to the wrong creature**: "I messed up — I should have moved the emulate over to the ravager" for the Savior trigger; equipment sequencing matters for triggers.
- **Path of the World Tree**: Kenji faced an opponent with four copies and found it very hard to beat once stabilized; recognize it as a build-around combo worth respecting.
- **Under-running snow lands**: Multiple games where hitting a snow land would have unlocked Demon Bolt or Priest of the Haunted Edge. "They have zero snow lands right now — this is a very sad Priest."
- **Crushed by Linda (Lindworm) going late when behind**: Large ground threats that draw cards when they die create insurmountable card advantage if unanswered. Take removal over raw card advantage early in draft.

# MH3 — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- **Writhing Chrysalis** is called "an abysmal common" in the best sense — it is the single best common in the format and a key signal; seeing it late means Eldrazi is contested.
- Double-colorless mana costs cannot be paid with basics; you need the DFC landscape lands or Eldrazi spawn tokens as actual colorless sources. This is a critical constraint.
- The format has at least three distinct viable macro-strategies: **Eldrazi ramp** (Temur/Sultai), **Energy aggro/midrange** (Boros or Jeskai), and **Blue-black draw-two/tempo**. Each has poor overlap with the others — pivoting mid-draft is genuinely hard.
- Eldrazi is heavily contested; Jeskai energy was identified as the "correct seat" in one draft where Kenji ended up in Blue-Black.
- Flashback MH3 games often come down to who gets Echoes of Eternity or Crystalis Bloodbane (?) online; six-win bracket opponents consistently have multiple bomb rares.
- Format is fast enough that you need early interaction or a coherent aggressive plan — "you really need to be aggressive or have reach; the Eldrazi can go very, very big over the top of you."

## Archetypes
- **Jeskai Energy:** Gargoyle (the 2/2 flying persist artifact) as the bread-and-butter two-drop; Amp Raptor (5-mana 6/6 flyer) as the top end; Scurry of Gremlins as the go-wide finisher; Ghostfire Slice and Aether Revolt (?) (Revolt enchantment that deals damage = energy gained) as the payoffs; Royal Cartographer (2-mana 1/3 landfall → draw three) is the engine. Essence Reliquary (bounce permanents for value) as a combo piece with Scurry. Wrath of the Skies (pay X energy, destroy everything with mana value ≤ X) as the reset. Best when you get one each of revolt, Reliquary, and Scurry.
- **Blue-Black draw-two:** Sneaky Snacker (2/1 flyer that returns from graveyard when you draw your third card), Kami of Jealous Thirst (3-mana 1/3 deathtouch, free drain when you've drawn 3+ cards), Horde's Shadow Spinner (free draw trigger for the deck), Storm Drake (2-mana 3/2 hexproof — swap with opponent's creature, they pay energy to keep it). Psychic Frog (rare, but most-drafted rare Kenji opened) as the premier threat. Defiler (?) and deep analysis effects round it out. Wants to be strictly two-color because the mana demands are intense.
- **Blue-Green Eldrazi ramp:** Nightshade Dryad (2-mana 1/3 deathtouch that taps for any color or colorless) as the best two-drop; Malevolent Rumble as the second-best turn-two play; Petrifying Meddler (4/5 reach for five, stuns a creature on ETB) as a premier brick wall; Breaker of Creation (8-mana, hexproof from each color, annihilator 2, gains life equal to colorless permanents) as the finisher; Nyxborn Hydra (bestow trample) to give Eldrazi trample since hexproof prevents targeting by opponents. Horrific Assault (1-mana fight spell that gains 3 life if you control an Eldrazi) is called one of the best removal spells in the archetype. Gift of the Viper (1-mana instant: +1/+1 counter, reach, deathtouch, untap) is "about as good as a one-mana trick as you'll get."
- **White-Black modified/counters:** Obstinate Gargoyle (2/2 persist flyer that needs plus counters) + Area Auxiliary (?) (4-mana, pumps other modified creatures) + Marionette Apprentice (?). Lethal Throwdown as removal (×2 is enough; don't run three). Wrath of the Skies is awkward here since it blows up your own stuff, but the effect is too strong to omit. Less favored by Kenji but functional.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Essence Reliquary + Scurry of Gremlins:** Bouncing Scurry back to hand every turn generates a wave of Gremlin tokens with each re-cast; "bouncing Scurry every turn, pretty freaking hot."
- **Aether Revolt + any permanent leaving battlefield:** Revolt triggers the enchantment. Sacrificing a landscape for fixing, sacrificing a token, or having a creature die all turn on double damage mode — plan your sequencing accordingly.
- **Nightshade Dryad + Nyxborn Hydra:** Death-touch trample: "13 death-touch trample damage." The dryad taps for green to pay bestow costs and untaps via Gift of the Viper for double use.
- **Royal Cartographer + landscapes:** Each landscape (DFC land spell) produces two landfall triggers on resolution — two energy from Cartographer for one spell. Extremely efficient at filling the energy pool.
- **Storm Drake + opponent's expensive creature:** If you can't kill a fatty otherwise (e.g., colossus-type), swap with Storm Drake; the opponent pays energy equal to the card's mana value or you just force them to take back the Drake while you keep whatever they controlled.
- **Wrath of the Skies sequencing:** Get as many permanents into play as possible first, then generate a large energy count (via Scurry of Gremlins, Charge Bug, landscapes) so you can destroy everything up to a high mana value in one activation.
- **Consign to Memory vs Eldrazi cast triggers:** This counter denies the cast trigger (tokens, card draw) in addition to countering the spell — much better than a vanilla counterspell in a colorless-heavy meta; main-deckable.
- **Brain Surge (?) / contaminated landscape sequencing:** Kenji lost a game because he brain-surged and discarded the wrong card — should keep the good cards and shuffle away bad ones, not the reverse.

## Card tips
- **Writhing Chrysalis:** First-pickable common. If you see it late, Eldrazi is contested; if you see it early and consistently, commit.
- **Nightshade Dryad:** Best two-drop in the Eldrazi deck. Pick it like it's a rare.
- **Malevolent Rumble:** Best green common overall (alongside Dryad); ideal turn-two play in any green deck.
- **Petrifying Meddler:** "Fantastic brick wall" for Eldrazi; the stun counter on entry buys a turn even against large threats.
- **Consign to Memory:** Always main-deckable in this format — denies three cards, denies tokens, and can counter triggered abilities.
- **Amp Raptor:** "Modern-playable, very good" — reliable 6/6 flyer in energy decks.
- **Gargoyle (Riddle Gate Gargoyle? / Obstinate Gargoyle):** Core two-drop in Jeskai and White-Black; the persist means it comes back as a modified creature for pump synergies.
- **Wrath of the Skies:** Keep for the effect even if you'll blow up your own stuff; the energy payoff (getting energy for each thing destroyed) can refill the tank.
- **Autotapper warning:** Arena prioritizes leaving colorless lands untapped for their sac ability even when it is not correct. Manually tap during your declare-attackers step when using colorless sources.
- **Royal Cartographer:** Only good with dedicated energy production beyond just landfall; don't run it unless you have six or more other energy sources.
- **Ral (planeswalker):** Explicitly bad in Limited — Kenji passes it multiple times.
- **DFC land spells:** Even if you only play one of the two colors, the DFC is free value — play them over basics to thin the deck; can replace up to five lands in the count.

## Pitfalls
- **Failing to pivot early enough:** "One of the issues with this format is that it was really hard to pivot sometimes — a lot of the cards don't have great overlap in other archetypes." Commit to a lane by pack two.
- **Blue-Black with colorless sources:** Running landscapes in the draw-two deck dilutes the tight UB mana base; the deck wants strictly two colors because of double-blue and double-black costs. Only take contaminated landscapes (all three colors) as the exception.
- **Trying to play two archetypes at once:** The "disjointed" Blue-Black + Eldrazi top end deck Kenji built in the Brainsurge VOD finished 1-3; mixing draw-two synergies with Eldrazi payoffs means neither plan fully executes.
- **Casting the Cartographer without energy support:** If you're banking on landfall alone for energy, don't play Cartographer — the six-energy threshold to draw three is too slow without supplemental sources.
- **Playing Blue-White Wrath of Skies into your own board:** Think about whether you have enough energy sources to pay a high X before committing; running it with only four energy available is often a loss condition.
- **Echoes of Eternity:** If the opponent resolves it, concede to your attack — it doubles all colorless triggers and copying. "We are beyond belief dead."
- **Keeping bad hands in flashback formats:** Opponents may not know all interactions (e.g., forgetting you can't adapt a creature that already has counters); but you still have to keep functional hands.

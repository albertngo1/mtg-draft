# OM1 — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Pick-Two draft format (Spider-Man: Through the Omenpaths).** Each pick is two cards simultaneously. This creates wider, sometimes weird decks with higher card volume but less cohesion. The strategy changes accordingly: focus on two-card synergies and accept some filler.
- **Format was not designed with Limited in mind.** Kenji describes it as "not intentional" and says he was "trying to have fun" rather than optimize. "As much as this format is very shallow, no depth to it, you can still do some fun stuff — you just got to make yourself have fun."
- **Two-wins = paid entry back** in the pick-two format. Kenji treats 2 wins as the breakeven and plays accordingly. "Remember, in these pick-two drafts, as long as you get to two wins, you're basically getting your money back."
- **Green-white is the default power lane.** Kenji repeatedly passes green-white openings to try black or blue-based decks, acknowledging every time that GW is "just better" and the open lane. "I could have had a very, very good straightup green-white deck."
- **Through the Omenpaths** (a rare that convokes a big creature or provides a powerful effect) is called out as "a very difficult card to beat." Treat it as a true bomb.
- **Watcher on the Road** (blue rare; turns into two 3/3s on ETB; "even if you have no other villains and you just cast this on turn four — two three-threes on turn four") is described as the best blue card in the format. "Double Watcher on the Road is just nuts."
- **Remarkable Findings** (a land enchantment that pumps a creature every turn?) is called out as another hard-to-beat rare. "Remarkable Findings — that is going to be a very difficult card."
- **Villainous Wrath** is the only real wrath effect in the format. "The one true wrath in the format — a pretty good one." Take it early if in black.

## Archetypes

### Green-White — In-Web Tempo / Counters
- **Gameplan:** Curve out with 2-drops, use In-Web (?) to bounce creatures with counters back to hand and replay them for free, build a wide board, close with big in-web threats or Scion of the Earth Spider. "Green white is heavy creature-based deck with access to plus-one-plus-one counter mechanics."
- **Key cards:** Allesos and Prass (?) (Allesos and Prass — "one of the best green-white creatures"); Principled Referee (2-drop that generates tokens on ETB/attack — "curving two-drop, three-drop into referee is backbreaking often"); Spectacular Tactics (pump + kill a flyer — "just the best white common"; "it not only acts as a protection effect for your relevant creatures, but it also kills a big creature from your opponent in a pinch"); MACD / MacD (?) (3-mana creature — "MACD is very good"); Janai and Hoppy (?) (2-drop with flying on your turn — "turn two, Janai and Hoppy. Turn three, attack."); Chisac (4-mana 4/4 spider with abilities — "Chisac's a spider"); Basil (4-mana flyer?); Scion of the Earth Spider (5-mana 5/5 flying first strike trample lifelink haste; "other spiders you control also gain all those keywords").
- **In-Web mechanic:** Creatures with In-Web can be bounced back to hand to replay for ETB triggers and counter reapplication. "The only way to stop all of their In-Web would be to leave back both creatures, which is just not right."
- **Support of Parents / Celelesnia Archavist:** Two fixing pieces. Archavist also destroys enchantments. "Eccentric Arachnologist — very good with all the spiders that we have. And of course, fixing for the Scion."

### Blue-Black — Villain / Control
- **Gameplan:** Use cheap villain creatures (connive for card selection), scorpion stings and damning caresses as cheap removal, Watcher on the Road as the finisher, Nil (?) to recur key pieces, and outsmart-the-amateur (?)-type counterspells to protect the plan. Heavy removal package. "Our removal quality was through the roof."
- **Key cards:** Watcher on the Road (two 3/3s; "double Watcher on the Road is just nuts"); Scorpion Sting (1-mana instant removal — "early interaction is very important for blue-black"); Damning Caress (removal spell that also connives — "connive for a removal spell on [the threat] is going to be key"); Nil (reanimation-ish — used to recur Carlo or Kazuo?); Watu / Wetu / Wetudo (?) (bounce creature with flash — "wetdu, and obviously snatch-back was a good draw"); Azor (8/8 flying finisher?); Carlo (connive when it attacks?); Prison Break (?; brings back a discarded card or creature from exile); Outsmart the Amateur (?) (counter — "an counter to counter something and draw a card").
- **Villain synergy:** Connive triggers activate when villains deal combat damage. Creatures that are villains get extra effects from Vexed Bots (?) and Nil. "All my creatures thus far are villains — it's pretty nice."
- **Quint's Insight (2-mana blue sorcery):** With mayhem cards, becomes a filtering machine. "Beginning of your first main phase — if you have something in hand, you have to discard. But draw a card." The forced discard is manageable with enough mayhem enablers. "First pick. Quint is doing a lot of work here."
- **Note on Watcher on the Road deckout risk:** Carlo + Watcher triggers draw many cards. "I have to be careful about decking myself here. This will make a big Carlo no matter what." Manage the combo carefully in long games.

### Mardu (Red-Black-White splash) — Spider Mayhem
- **Gameplan:** Build a spider-heavy deck (Scions of the Earth Spider, Kraza — the "infinite 3/3s" spider?), support it with mayhem card triggers via Quint's Insight, and close with burn or Villainous Wrath finisher. "We had nine total spiders for Kraza — that's actually kind of nuts."
- **Key cards:** Kraza (red spider that grows/makes spiders — "You can't beat infinite 3/3s!"); Infernace (0/0 that fetches a mountain and grows; "gets plus one plus one for each mountain you control"); Knife Trick (?) (instant combat trick); Sadistic String Puller (mayhem trigger card); Fearsome Ridgeline (fixing land?).
- **Mayhem:** When you have a mayhem card in hand and discard to the Quint trigger, you get a bonus effect. "The mayhem deck has been okay." Works well with Knife Trick (discard to recast?) and Kney (?).
- **Red is not a strong main color.** "Generally I don't think of red as a good main color in this format." Stick to RB splash W or just BW unless a specific spider bomb forces it.

### Blue-White — Flying / Counters
- **Gameplan:** Play efficient 2-drops, suit them up with Mothwing Shroud (equipment/aura that grants flying + ward?) or in-web triggers, and clock with Margo (?) and Gobin (?). "Looking for the aerialists and stuff — let's try to go blue white."
- **Key cards:** Mothwing Aerialist (2-mana hybrid, ward 2, gains flying when +1/+1 counter put on it — "the most important common for the blue-white deck"); Principled Referee; Margo (?) - **Critical missing piece:** Every UW draft Kenji ran lacked the Mothwing Aerialist. "I'm missing the most important common — zero aerialists. Very unlikely to get one at this point."

## Card interactions & combos

- **Scion of the Earth Spider + any other spider:** Once Scion is in play, every other spider gains flying, first strike, trample, lifelink, and haste. A board of Chisac + Scion is almost unblockable and closes in two attacks. "Other spiders you control also gain all those keywords — very cool first pick."
- **Watcher on the Road + Carlo (villain connive creature):** Carlo connives when a villain deals damage; Watcher makes two 3/3 villains. Carlo can potentially draw 6+ cards in a single activation chain. "This will make a big Carlo no matter what — drew three cards. That's so big."
- **Nil + Kazuo (villain with enters trigger):** Nil returns a discarded creature; Kazuo has a powerful ETB that also hits noncreatures. "I can actually go Nil into the Casuo" after setting up the discard, generating immediate value.
- **Quint's Insight + mayhem cards:** The forced "discard if you have cards" clause of Quint triggers mayhem effects for free. "I had five mayhem cards — we've done a very good job of drawing them when we have the quint."
- **Infernace + mountains:** Infernace is a 0/0 that grows for each mountain; ramping into mountains makes it massive quickly. Used in Mardu spider decks to bridge turns and generate a body.
- **Prison Break + Nil:** Prison Break exiles a creature; Nil recurs a discarded card or just returns a lost creature. Chaining them extends value significantly. "Prison Break the Nil. Nil gets back Carlo."
- **Eerie Gravestone (?) + any card-draw engine:** Gravestone provides a way to filter draws; Kenji treats it as "extra card draw" in blue-black. Stacks with Watcher triggers to empty your deck quickly — be careful of decking yourself.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Spectacular Tactics is the best white common by a wide margin.** "I'm pretty sure this is just the best white common because the two supported archetypes for white are blue-white and green-white, and both of those are just heavy creature-based decks." Take every copy.

### Black (B)
- **Villainous Wrath is the only wrath.** Take it early as a one-of; it's critical against wide boards. "I'm going to go for it" — he almost always ends up casting it as a 3-for-1 or better.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Principled Referee is a premium 3-drop.** "Curving two-drop, three-drop into Referee is backbreaking often times." Take it over most other 3-drops.
- **Mothwing Aerialist is the most important common for blue-white.** The two-mana hybrid creature with ward 2 that gains flying is irreplaceable. Kenji went 0-for-4 drafts without finding one; adjust drafting to grab it in pack 1.
- **Watcher on the Road is the best blue rare.** "Even if you have no other villains, cast it on turn four — two three-threes on turn four." First pick.
- **Scorpion Sting is the best black common for blue-black.** "Blue-black really wants its early interaction." A second copy is a great pickup.
- **Rouse the Swarm (?) / Scout the City (?) are excellent fixing/token generators.** "Scout the City can help dig for a non-green-white land as well." Run at least 2 in green decks.
- **Xan (mana dork that ramps into 5-drops?)** is a critical support piece. Enables through-the-omen-paths and other 5-drops on turn 4. "They had Xan for the ramp."

## Pitfalls

- **Forcing black/blue when green-white is clearly open.** Every draft Kenji tried to play non-GW, he acknowledged GW was the better seat. "I could have had a very, very good straightup green-white deck." If green and white cards are flowing late, pivot.
- **Committing to Scion of the Earth Spider without enough fixing.** Scion needs WUBRG (?) — the five-color lands are essential. Without Support of Parents or Xan, it sits in hand. "I'd rather get the dual lands and stuff otherwise." Don't add more than 2 off-color basics without a fixing plan.
- **Attacking with your Chisac when holding back wins the game.** "Maybe my mistake was on turn three when I attacked with my Crime Scene Instructor. Maybe I'm supposed to just have left it back." Conserve power for the right swing.
- **Casting Wrath when opponent has an indestructible effect.** The one rare that grants indestructible eats Villainous Wrath. "The only card I was playing around — whatever that one rare is called." Know your opponent's colors before firing the wrath.
- **Letting the In-Web deck bounce freely.** Allowing creatures with In-Web triggers to swing, get bounced, and replay burns through your life total. "The only way to stop all of their In-Web would be to leave back both creatures." Assign blockers on every attack.
- **Quint's Insight decking yourself.** At low library counts, Watcher + Carlo + Quint can draw you out of cards. "I have to be careful about decking myself here." Count remaining library cards before activating.
- **Not having enough creatures in blue-black.** "The creature count's a little bit light, but you don't need that many creatures to win — especially when you have two Watchers on the Road, backed up with this much removal." Aim for 12–13 creatures minimum; lean on Watcher to supplement.
- **Missing lethal because of wrong attack sequences.** Kenji cost himself wins multiple times by attacking in the wrong order: "I meant to play island there — shakeing it off." Pre-plan the full attack sequence before committing.

# IKO — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
Ikoria (IKO) is a medium-speed format dominated by the cycling deck when assembled correctly. The dedicated cycling build (white-red base, Zenith Flare finisher) is the most consistent strategy; Kenji calls it "boring but good." Mutate strategies exist as an alternative but are more fragile — opponents can hold non-human creatures to deny good mutate targets. The format has relevant combat tricks (Snare Tactician tap-down, combat pumps) so over-committing into attacks is punished.

## Archetypes
- **White-Red Cycling (primary)**: Collect 9+ one-mana cyclers plus multiple Zenith Flares. Win with Flare; Snare Tactician taps down blockers as a secondary clock. Kenji says 17 cyclers with 3 Flares is an ideal build. Run 13–14 lands. Divine Arrow and Fire Prophecy are the removal spells of choice; Coordinated Charge can wheel and functions as a win condition.
- **Blue-Red / Izzet Cycling (variant)**: Similar plan but slightly weaker — fewer one-mana cyclers available in those colors. Cathartic Reunion-style looting effects help dig for Flares.
- **Black-Green Reanimator / Midrange**: Draft big creatures (Sand Worm, Honey Mammoth(?)), cyclers that can be reanimated via Back for More / Corpse Churn / Mole(?). Needs double recursion effects to be consistent. Grim Dancer (?) with lifelink is a good top-end. Cheville (?) + Ram Through is a noted combo — death-touch + fight kills anything.
- **Green-Black/Blue Mutate Goodstuff**: Build around Pouncing Shore Shark, Glimmer Bell(?), Reconnaissance Mission(?). Jodah's Giant Antha (Jiganth? — 5-mana companion?) was attempted but proved inconsistent; Garuda is the key blue bomb. Ruinous Ultimatum is the big payoff for five-color attempts.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Zenith Flare + Cycling count**: Flare scales with cycling cards in graveyard. Kenji notes: "when I have three Flares I don't need to be super aggro — I can just wait until one Flare is good enough." Never discard Flare with Fire Prophecy unless truly necessary; Flare in graveyard doesn't count for itself.
- **Fire Prophecy + own cycler**: Deliberately pitching a cycler you don't need lets you keep the cycling card count high in graveyard; Kenji notes you can Fire Prophecy your own Valiant Rescuer (?) to recycle it.
- **Snare Tactician + multiple cyclers**: Tapping down 2–3 attackers/blockers in a single turn is a frequent blowout. "When you have two Flares, one Mana cycler over Snare Tactician is still correct; when you have Flares you never pass a one-mana cycler."
- **Valiant Rescuer + cycling**: Makes tokens that add to Flare count and chump/attack; one of the top non-cycling creatures for the archetype.
- **Cheville (?) + Ram Through**: Gives Cheville death-touch, then Ram Through fights a large creature — guaranteed kill on anything. Kenji specifically calls this out as a build-around combo.
- **Back for More + Grim Dancer (?)**: Recurring a life-link creature repeatedly stabilizes life totals; pairing with Corpse Churn / Mole (?) fills graveyard for reanimation.
- **Giganta as companion (Jiganth? — large green companion)**: Adds three colors for Ruinous Ultimatum; Kenji tried it in a non-cycling deck to enable the Ultimatum splash.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Snare Tactician**: Strong in cycling decks as both enabler and threat; less good if you only have a few cyclers.
- **Coordinated Charge**: Wheels frequently; hold it to pair with a wide board and end the game.
- **Divine Arrow**: Reliable white removal; worth taking over most two-mana cyclers once you have enough cyclers.

### Blue (U)
- **Reconnaissance Mission**: Strong card-advantage enchantment; note it requires creatures to deal combat damage, so the cycling deck prefers attacking before cycling to trigger it.

### Red (R)
- **Fire Prophecy**: Preferred removal for cycling — cycles unwanted cards while killing; don't Fire Prophecy your own crystal (?) because losing a cycling card in graveyard matters.
- **Shredded Sails**: "Should cycle it more often rather than keeping in hand as a dead card."
- **Forbidden Friendship**: Generally not good enough; Kenji passed most copies.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Zenith Flare**: "The best rare in the format" for cycling. Multiple Flares = redundancy; three is the target.

### Colorless / artifacts
- **Crystalline Giant**: Artifact threat that opponents should prioritize removing with Appetite for the Unnatural.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **One-mana cyclers**: Never correctly passed once you are in the cycling deck. "It might not even be correct to take every single cycling card, but I'm not sure — take them anyway." Only consider passing if you already have 17+ cyclers.
- **Ambush**: Good early creature for a non-cycling midrange build.
- **Land count for cycling**: 13–14 is correct; going to 4 lands in actual play is common and intended.

## Pitfalls
- **Playing Zenith Flare main-phase when opponent has untapped mana**: Major punt pattern; Kenji lost one game by going wide main-phase, letting opponent respond to a Flare, then dying to their Flare. "Hold Flare until you have enough damage or until they tap out."
- **Over-greeding on five-color splash**: Attempting Ruinous Ultimatum without enough fixing caused inconsistent mana; the payoff requires Giganta as companion which forces companion restrictions.
- **Not cycling Shredded Sails early**: Keeping situational artifact removal in hand against non-artifact decks wasted cycling potential.
- **Playing into Snare Tactician taps**: Attacking with all creatures and giving them a free Snare trigger; especially dangerous with cycling decks that have multiple tappable threats.
- **Running too many two-mana cyclers**: They are filler; prioritize one-mana cyclers hard. "F your three-mana cycling" — Kenji specifically disdains the three-mana cycling creatures.
- **Not prioritizing green removal (Cheville/Ram Through) earlier**: Missed opportunities in the BG reanimator builds to stop fast mutate threats.
- **Forgetting Garuda is a sorcery-speed trigger check** (?): In the mutate/blue builds, sequencing Garuda incorrectly let opponents play around it.

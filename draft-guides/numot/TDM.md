# TDM — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- TDM (Tarkir: Dragonstorm) late-format meta is heavily polarized: **Mardu (Red-White-X aggro)** vs **Five-Color Dragons** / Sultai control. Kenji says he plays against "Mardu or red-white X aggro 50 plus percent of the time" at the end of format.
- "The first couple weeks of this format were great until, at least on Arena, everything turned into 'Look at me, I'm Mardu' and uh Fivecolor Dragons." — quality of games degraded as format became solved.
- Kenji repeatedly says "Look at me, I'm Mardu" as a sarcastic tag whenever he faces or accidentally builds Boros/Mardu — it's the default lazy aggro strategy everyone ends up in.
- **Five-color dragon** builds are powerful but require specific support: fixing lands, globes (mana rocks), and enough actual dragons to pay off the storm effects.
- **Dragon count matters** — both Roiling Dragonstorm and Teaming Dragonstorm (?) need 3–4+ dragons in your deck to be good. Two dragon storms with only 3 dragons is "the saddest saddest dragon storms I've ever seen."
- **Sultai** (Blue-Black-Green) is the third main archetype — graveyard value, card draw, surveil, big threats. Consistently described as a well-rounded alternative to the dragon builds.
- The format is **mid-range to slow** but Mardu can punish stumbles. Having early interaction for 1–2 key Mardu threats (Dalavan Pack Beast [?], Shock Brigade [?]) is essential in the non-Mardu decks.

## Archetypes

### Mardu / Red-White-X aggro — PREVALENT, POWERFUL
- Gameplan: curve out with cheap creatures, use pump and combat tricks (Duty Beyond Death [?], Rebellious Strike [?], Rebellious Charge [?], Frontline Rush [?]), overwhelm blockers with haste and mobilize tokens.
- Key cards: Shock Brigade (?) (haste creature, pumps on cast?), Clarion Conqueror (?) (rare: "activated abilities of artifacts, creatures, and planeswalkers can't be activated"), Rally the Monastery (?) ("one of the best uncommons in the set"), Frontline Rush (?), Static Snare (?), Bearer of Glory (?) (activated: gives death touch to tokens?).
- Kenji calls it the "bad guys" deck but respects its power, especially late format.
- Weakness: any deck with cheap early blockers and removal can stabilize. "If we can kill the Dalavan Pack Beast, we should be okay."

### Five-Color Dragons
- Gameplan: fix mana with tap lands + Dragon's Brood Relic (?) / Globes (mana rocks), cast dragon storms to generate tokens + bounce value, win with Hoof (?) (Craterhoof [?] effect: "+X/+X trample to all" where X = creatures) or Sonic Shrier (?) (5-mana 4/4 flying, ETB 2 damage + 2 life + discard).
- Key cards: Dragonstorm Globe (?), Dragon's Brood Relic (?) (tap to activate for value, lets you bounce dragon storms), Sagu Wildling (?) (large body with good stats), Roiling Dragonstorm (?), Teaming Dragonstorm (?), Surak (?) (3-mana 4/3 trample can't be countered; when controlled creature targeted by opponent: draw a card), Hoof (?), Sonic Shrier (?).
- Winning line: "Dragon storm into wildling. Make a 4/4. Make two 2/2s. Gain three life. Bounce back the dragon storm." — rinse and repeat for overwhelming value.
- Requires at least 3–4 actual dragons; without them the dragon storm cards are filler.
- Mana is the challenge: "seven tap lands — slightly sketchy but runnable." Kenji runs 16 lands when heavily fixing-based.

### Sultai (Blue-Black-Green) value
- Gameplan: surveil, card draw, graveyard recursion, big top-end threats. Awaken the Honored Dead (?) (sorcery: return creature + land from graveyard), Sagu Pummeler (?) (4/4 reach), Botist (?) (when tapped, look at top card: if land put in hand, else put in graveyard — pairs with Dragon's Brood Relic).
- Key cards: Botist (?), Surak (?), Avenger of the Fallen (?) (3-mana 2/4 deathtouch + mobilize X where X is graveyard creature count), Fang Keeper Familiar (?), Winter Night Stories (?) (draw 3, discard 2), Focus the Mind (?).
- Note: Botist + Dragon's Brood Relic = free tap triggers even when Botist can't profitably attack. A powerful engine.
- Sultai was "probably the right place to be" in many drafts where Kenji ended up in five-color instead.

### Mill (Glacierwood Siege build) — NOVELTY / VIABLE
- Kenji explicitly runs a Glacierwood Siege (?) build — "whenever you cast instant/sorcery, target player mills four cards." With harmonize cards (spells that trigger twice) and card draw, he consistently mills opponents out, going 0-2 → 6-2 in one session.
- "The siege being a alternate win con, but not the main plan" is the correct way to build it — "a good deck where Glacierwood Siege is still viable" rather than a dedicated mill deck.
- Mill payoff: opponents in Sultai often self-mill, making your Siege stronger. "Since they're also on Sultai like some of our other opponents, they're going to dig through their deck pretty quickly."
- Key support: Focus the Mind (card draw that harmonizes = double draw + double mill trigger), Unending Whisper (?) (harmonize), Teamer Battlecry (?) (makes spells cheaper), Auroral Procession (?) (returns permanents).
- Mill in limited otherwise: Kenji says "I always tell people not to mill your opponents" — but with dedicated support it is viable.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Dragon's Brood Relic + Botist**: Tap Botist to get Relic trigger without attacking. "You could tap your Botist even if it didn't have a good attack" — continuous land finding + surveil, fueling your hand.
- **Hoof with Fang Keeper**: Kenji calculates lethal multiple times — "If I flash in the Fang Keeper, I'm going to have four creatures with a hoof. That would be 4 8 12 16 24." Need to count creatures carefully. Hoof is a hard-cast instant/flash effect (?) that pumps all your creatures by +X/+X trample where X = creature count; with 5+ creatures the math gets out of hand quickly.
- **Roiling Dragonstorm + Teaming Dragonstorm (chaining)**: "Dragon storm into vanguard... the following turn we can go like monument into Vanguard." Using one dragon storm bounce effect to rebuy the other is the core loop.
- **Dragon's Brood Relic activation + dragon in hand**: "I can counter that. Can't counter that." — some dragon storms have specific counter abilities contingent on having dragons in hand. Know which is which.
- **Glacierwood Siege + harmonize spells**: Each harmonize card triggers Siege twice = 8 cards milled per harmonize spell cast. Stack multiple Focus the Mind and Unending Whisper for reliable mill-outs.
- **Rally the Monastery (?)**: "One of the best uncommons in the set." Creates multiple tokens when cast, giving wide board presence instantly — pairs with Frontline Rush pump and menace enablers.
- **Surak redirect trigger**: When any creature or creature spell you control is targeted by an opponent's spell/ability, draw a card. This activates even on targeted removal — forces opponent into a damned-if-you-do situation. "They're just giving me more cards."

## Card tips

- **Sarken's Resolve (?) / Sarin's Resolve (?)**: "If you're still not main decking this card, I think you're doing it wrong. Basically, every deck is going to have at least one flyer of some variety. And oftentimes they have multiple and it's still a combat trick in the scenarios where they don't currently have a flyer." — must-play in almost every deck.
- **Roiling Dragonstorm**: Playable even with few/no dragons. "Even if you don't have dragons, it's still good enough. And then if you do have dragons, it just becomes a lot better."
- **Botist** (?): "This card is just really good." Consistently undervalued late in the format. Tap to look at top card, put land in hand or put it in graveyard. Synergizes with everything in Sultai.
- **Fang Keeper Familiar** (?): Removal spell that costs 2 more when targeting a dragon — awkward in dragon mirrors. "Three mana to kill any non-dragon creature, but then five mana to kill a dragon. This card could often times be awkward."
- **Teaming Dragonstorm** (Jeskai version): Fixes mana and can enable multi-color splashes. The devotee version fixes for all three Jeskai colors.
- **Evolving Wilds / Thornwood Falls / tap lands**: Prioritize early for mana stability. "Let's just be smart and take lands for a bit." In five-color, taking a land over a playable is often correct.
- **Cotus (?)**: A 5/5 indestructible creature that mills cards and can cast what it mills — "five power Cotus" is a bomb rare that can run away with games.
- **Piercing Exhale (?)**: Kenji says "I just don't like piercing exhale" — conditional removal that doesn't hit enough. Avoid or use as filler only.
- **Twin Bolt**: Was in the format (multicolor set overlap?). Kenji initially discards it and immediately regrets it: "Uber punished for discarding that twin bolt." Cheap two-target removal is extremely valuable vs. Mardu's early curve.
- **Runnable land count**: With heavy fixing (7 tap lands), 16 lands is fine. With normal fixing, 17 is standard. Kenji considered dropping to 16 to fit Sarken's Resolve when flooding.

## Pitfalls

- **Not hitting mana with multi-color**: Multiple game losses stem from keeping two-landers, not finding the third color, and flooding. "I kept a hand that didn't have my one of my main colors... we just got unlucky to not hit it." — he admits it every time but keeps the hand anyway.
- **Overloading on dragon storms with few dragons**: Three dragon storms with only 2–3 actual dragons is "this deck is bad." The storms need critical mass — draft the dragon bodies alongside the storm enablers.
- **Discarding your best interaction**: In game 1 vs Mardu with Winter Night Stories, Kenji discards Twin Bolt. "Lesson learned. Don't discard your Twin Bolt." Value your small removal spells; don't discard them to card draw effects carelessly.
- **Clarion Conqueror shuts off Globe**: "Activated abilities of artifacts, creatures, and planeswalkers can't be activated. Well, that includes my Dragon Storm Globe." Rare hoser but be aware.
- **Assuming opponent has a trick pre-combat and over-holding**: Multiple instances of Kenji playing around phantom tricks and giving opponent free attacks. "I shouldn't have played around that."
- **Blocking order in Mardu mirrors**: When they have Duty Beyond Death (?), blocking the wrong creature means you get punished by them returning it. Kill the creatures without recursion first.
- **Playing too many tap lands**: Seven tap lands leads to "uphill battle" starts. Prioritize untapped sources for turn-2 and turn-3 plays.

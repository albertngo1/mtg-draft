# BLB — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Bloomburrow is a synergy-heavy format where staying in a tribe matters more than raw card power. Kenji rates the format 4/10 overall — playable but not particularly enjoyable, leaning on clean linear decks.
- Format rewards smooth curves and early development. Cash Grab on turn 2 "instead of development" on the draw is "basically conceding."
- Mana-screw on the draw in this fast format is often game-ending; keeping hands without turn-2 plays on the draw is punishing.
- Poly Wallop (4-mana removal) is awkward in the hand — frogs archetype makes it conditionally better; without frogs it is always a 4-mana spell.
- Green and black are consistently the most open/deep colors; red-blue (otters) is underrated but often underdrafted. Birds were the weakest archetype by late format.

## Archetypes
- **Green-Black Squirrels** — build around multiple Baker's Bane Duo, Dagger-Fang Duo, Dre Leader (?), Cash Grab (scry/draw engine), Cultivator (gets big off foraging), Dark Star Augur (card advantage with Offspring). Gameplan: develop a wide board with squirrel synergies that all produce food, forage to pump Cultivators, close with a big ground army. Kenji calls this "like a budget Constructed deck" when it comes together.
- **White-Black Life Gain (Bats)** — anchor on Carrot Cake (food + life-gain synergy), Builder's Talent (levels up to an engine), Life-Creed Duo (flying + life link), Glide-Dive Duo, Wax Wayne Witness, Harbinger of the Tides (?), Nocturnal Hunger, Fell, Savor (?). Tangle Tumbler provides a resilient threat that can swing AND block with vigilance. Kenji uses Heirloom Epic as a card-advantage engine that turns floods into action; key in the late game vs. Mana-screw.
- **Red-Blue Otters** — Wave Rider, Pearl of Wisdom, Otter Ball Antics (win condition via non-creature spell pings), Storm-Catch Mentor (?). "People still under-value otters; you can get an insane red-blue deck just because people are trying to avoid it." Kenji notes Antics becomes "effectively free Lava Axe" if opponent casts non-creatures each turn — forces racing.
- **Green + any color "good stuff"** — Blacksmith's Talent, Tender Wild Guide (ramp into 4-drop bombs), Scavenger's Talent (mill win con with double copy), Short Bow (pump), Rampager (big body), Moose (?), Bone Bind (recursion). Without Cash Grabs and squirrel payoffs, the deck becomes "good cards, no synergy" which is playable but under-powered.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Baker's Bane Duo + Dre Leader + Cultivator**: Bread-and-butter squirrel curve. Cultivator grows each time you forage; with Baker's Bane making food, Dre Leader refilling board, Cultivators snowball quickly to 4/4 or 5/5 without additional cards.
- **Cash Grab + Cultivator**: Cash Grab digs 4 cards for your squirrel payoffs AND creates a food, which directly powers the Cultivator pump trigger.
- **Builder's Talent + Carrot Cake / Bell (?)**: Talent levels off foods and 1/1 tokens; Carrot Cake makes food on upkeep; Bell (?) draws cards. "For Builder's Talent we want Carrot Cake and the Saver which makes a food."
- **Tangle Tumbler + food tokens**: Sack two foods to animate Tumbler as a creature; with vigilance it attacks and blocks same turn. With life-gain triggers from Carrot Cake / Cleric, you can keep re-activating.
- **Dark Star Augur + Offspring**: Running it out without Offspring "is one of those rare scenarios" — normally wait for the Offspring + kicker to maximize hits. Card advantage is strong but can backfire: Augur milled removal spells in one game, swinging the outcome.
- **Heirloom Epic (black) in white-black**: Functions as a card-advantage machine — draw a card each upkeep by paying life. "Can you imagine not having this heirloom epic we would have just been Mana-screwed out of the game."
- **Savor + attack**: Savor as an instant-speed removal trick mid-combat, not just pre-combat — good for forcing opponents to make bad trades.
- **Scavenger's Talent + Thorn Vault Forager (?)**: Mill win-con combo; with double Talent you mill opponent multiple times per turn cycle and win without attacking.
- **Bladebrand (?) + Purifying Dragon (?)[BLB context unclear — may be Blade Brand]**: Death-touch + ping ability for pseudo-removal.

## Card tips
- **Cash Grab**: Premium pickup at any point in the draft; Kenji hates passing it and says "every time I play this card I am very pleased." Especially crucial for squirrels to dig to payoffs. With 3 copies "should be fine with 16 lands."
- **Baker's Bane Duo / Dagger-Fang Duo**: Best 2-drop Commons in the format, essentially tied for top common slot. Take extremely early.
- **Savor (?)**: "One of my favorite common removal spells." Functions as instant-speed exile + protection; very flexible. Mana-efficient early answer to key threats.
- **Dark Star Augur**: Fantastic, but do not run it without Offspring if possible. "You don't normally want to play these type of cards without being able to Offspring them."
- **Nocturnal Hunger**: Best common black removal; always want at least one, ideally more.
- **Poly Wallop**: Acceptable if you have frogs (becomes conditional 2-mana removal), but "if I don't have any frogs, it's always going to be a four-mana removal spell." Often the worst card in a non-frog hand.
- **Overprotect**: Strong combat trick but often cut for efficiency; Kenji repeatedly wheels it late, signaling it's lower priority than it looks.
- **Short Bow**: "Been a really big fan of Short Bow" — underrated equipment, especially in squirrel decks wanting to pump.
- **Dagger-Fang Duo vs. Cash Grab at P1P8**: "I think the creature is generally going to be a little bit better but maybe Cash Grab is the better card" — situation-dependent.
- **Carrot Cake**: Core enabler for white-black; Kenji ended a draft with five copies ("penta-cake"). Worth taking over competing on-color cards.
- **Treetop Sentry (?) / Sentries**: Always want at least one in squirrel decks; provides a meaningful body.
- **Heaped Harvest**: Artifact that fixes mana AND counts as a food for foraging payoffs; solid utility piece.
- **Haven (?)**: Free off-color fixing; take it when you want to splash.
- **Keen-Eyed Curator**: Good 2-mana 7/7 trample eventually; "easy to turn it into a 2-mana win-con" — but Kenji ranked it behind Wix Patrol (?) at P1P3.
- **Bodyguard ("bushy bodyguard")**: Token-generating creature for food synergies; combine with Cultivator for board presence.
- **Warren Elder / War Leader**: Key late-game payoffs for go-wide white token strategies; hard to beat when left unanswered.

## Pitfalls
- **Over-relying on double Poly Wallop**: Kenji explicitly blames "the double poly wallup was the biggest issue" in his last Bloomburrow loss — too many expensive reactive spells strands you with a clunky hand.
- **Keeping hands without turn-2 plays on the draw**: "Cash grab is fine but we need development, we need board presence." Mulligan aggressively for curve.
- **Not playing the Augur when missing a land drop**: "I think maybe the messed up...where I massed up...was not playing the augur when I did miss that fourth land because then it just further and further exacerbated the issue of missing land drops."
- **Milling your own removal spells with Dark Star Augur**: In a late game where removal is scarce, augur can mill key cards like Nocturnal Hunger and Share Pod. "We needed to have not milled one of them."
- **Pivoting away from the open lane too late**: Kenji pivoted out of blue mid-P1 after noticing green was flowing — "despite not having any blue or black after those first picks I have no allegiance to it and we can pitch it real easy."
- **Glarb (?) splash without fixing**: Taking a splash card that requires an Island without actually having the fixing already in hand leads to awkward mana. "I'm regretting the glarb."
- **Orphaned synergy pieces**: Building squirrels without Cash Grabs leaves the engine without fuel. "This is where I'm regretting not having more cash grabs."
- **Attacking into a potential combat trick with a key creature**: Kenji notes missing damage by not pumping the Cultivator pre-attack and getting run away-togethered (?). Think through combat math carefully when behind.
- **Too much top end, not enough 2-drops**: "One glaring problem with this deck and I think it's just not having too much top end" vs. "my creature count might be a little bit low overall." Both are recurring issues; aim for 12+ creatures minimum.

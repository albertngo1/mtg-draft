# NEO — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **One of Kenji's all-time favorite draft formats.** He describes it as "better than any of the silly alchemy variants" and says he "put over 1,500 games into this format." Deep card pool across multiple archetypes.
- **Enchantment-matters and ninja are the two primary axes.** Green-white enchantments (sagas, shrines, auras) and blue-black ninjas are both well-supported. Red-white samurai is the third viable aggro lane.
- **Format has a strong aggressive red-black anvil deck** as Kenji's favorite non-Kami-War archetype. "Probably red-black anvil" when asked for best non-combo deck.
- **Five-color Kami of Cosmic Fury (Kamigawa: Kami War? — the WUBRG enchantment saga)** is a powerful late-game engine that Kenji force-drafted twice. Very fun but requires heavy fixing; results were mixed (3-3 and 6-2).
- **Ninja deck rewards patience and signals.** When pack 1 is all blue-black-open, pack 2 floods with ninjas and enablers. "We passed zero blue in pack one — so if I just get like another disruptor and a random cheap flyer, we can just stick with this." The payoff for committing early is a fully stocked ninja deck.
- **Two-mana 3/3 defenders (Bamboo Grove Archer) are format-defining defensive cards.** They shut down ninja decks cold and stabilize against red aggro. "A turn-two Bamboo Grove Archer can just stop them in their tracks." Always pick them early if not in aggro.
- **Turn-1 Okiba Reckoner Raid is one of the most annoying things in the format.** Red-black running it curves into Oni-Cult Anvil threats very quickly.

## Archetypes

### Green-X Enchantments — "Kami War" Five-Color Value
- **Gameplan:** Assemble a base of green enchantments (sagas, auras, mana ramp), splash all five colors via Tanuki (Prosperous Thief?), Grafted Growth, and dual lands. Win with Kami of Cosmic Fury (5-color saga that exiles a permanent, bounces something, makes the opponent discard, then transforms into a 6/6 flying legend — "Okagachi"), or assemble a chain of Brilliant Restoration + Imperial Oath + Fable of the Mirror-Breaker.
- **Key cards:** Kami of Cosmic Fury / Kami War (WUBRG saga; "can't pass a Kami War — that card is just a little bit too spicy for me"); Bamboo Grove Archer (2-mana 3/3 reach defender; "fantastic early game blocker — just a huge blocker for the early game"); Imperial Oath (6-mana saga, makes three 2/2 samurai; "if you have never cast Imperial Oath into Imperial Oath, you're missing out"); Fable of the Mirror-Breaker; Brilliant Restoration (7-mana, quad-white, returns all artifacts/enchantments from graveyard — extreme late-game bomb); Life of Toshiro Umezawa (one of the best uncommons; on 2 it pumps or drains -1/-1, flips into a 2/3 that lets you cast instants during your upkeep); Portrait of Michiko / Michiko's Reign of Truth (saga that puts counters on a creature, scaling it up); Geothermal Kami (bounces an enchantment you control to draw / gain life — enables rebuying sagas); Tales of Master Sashiro (saga that flips into a 5/5 vigilance).
- **Fixing needed:** Grafted Growth (enchantment that fetches a basic; "put Grafted Growth on your most abundant basic type"), Tanuki (dork that cycles for a basic), Terrarium (artifact that taps for mana), all the dual tap-lands. Running one of each basic is standard.
- **Kami War specific note:** Cast it at least once per draft session or it's all for nothing. "We drafted Kami War pick one pack one. We warped our mana base to play it. Haven't drawn it once — let's get it."

### Blue-Black — Ninjas
- **Gameplan:** Play cheap evasive creatures (1-drops with flying) to enable ninjutsu — bounce them after they attack, swap in a ninja, draw cards and apply pressure. Win through card advantage and air damage. Dakuchi (Thousand-Faced Shadow?) and Silver-Fur Master were key uncommons.
- **Key cards:** Dokuchi (Thousand-Faced Shadow — 5/5 with ninjutsu and "when it connects, search library for blue or black creature"?); Mukotai Ambusher (3/2 lifelink ninjutsu; "three two lifelink to bolster back our life total but rebuy the Modern Age for looting purposes"); Prosperous Thief (ninja that makes Treasure); Moon-Circuit Hacker (1UB?); Nezumi Prowler; Behold the Unspeakable (double-blue saga that kills creatures then draws cards and becomes a fat flyer); Gloom Shrieker (menace, gets a card from graveyard; "very good with ninjas and ways to bounce it").
- **Enablers:** Turn-1 cheap creatures (1/1 flyers) are critical. Okiba Reckoner Raid as a 1-drop. "Having a cheap creature turn one is critical — I always wish we had that on turn one which we can never do."
- **Hoverbike as a repeated enabler:** Crewing and having ninjutsu creatures bounce is a strong loop. "Having the hoverbike with a lot of ninjutsu — so that's a start."
- **Ninjutsu timing:** Ninjutsu triggers happen when the creature attacks and goes unblocked. Kenji often attacks first, then holds up ninjutsu mana. "Maybe just go for it to bait out the safekeeping — then attack with the vector glider, ninja in."
- **Behold the Unspeakable is premium.** "Splashing double blue for Behold the Unspeakable" is worth warping your mana for.

### Green-White — Saga Value / Wide
- **Gameplan:** Play enchantment creatures and sagas, curve into Imperial Oath for multiple bodies, close with Kami War or a pumped creature package. Very consistent, Kenji called it "my preferred baseline" when not forcing Kami War.
- **Golden-Tail Disciple (lifelink enchantment creature, pumped by Michiko's Reign):** "I would take one of these as an enchantment creature that can just buy us some time with the lifelink." Critical defensive piece that scales with saga triggers.
- **Season of Renewal (enchantment; shuffles cards from graveyard back into library, gains life?):** Critical anti-mill answer and recursive engine. "Really needed Season of Renewal in this versus this matchup." Kenji lost a game he was winning on mill because the opponent found Season of Renewal.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Kami of Cosmic Fury (Kami War) + Geothermal Kami:** The Geothermal bounces the Kami War saga back to hand, which resets its chapter counter. "Bouncing the Kami War — recast it. Draw extra cards. Monster Kami, make him discard a card." Each rebuy gets you another chapter-1 ETB (exile + bounce + discard). "I could bounce the Kami War next turn if I really want."
- **Fable of the Mirror-Breaker + Brilliant Restoration:** Fable's chapter 2 lets you discard and keep discarding high-value enchantments (Michiko's, Bamboo Grove Archer) to set up a massive Brilliant Restoration. "Fable flips, copies Kami, Kami bounces Kami War, then we just start eating every single permanent." The mirror token can also copy the Tales of Master Sashiro for double 5/5 attacks.
- **Imperial Oath + Life of Toshiro Umezawa:** Life of Toshiro's chapter 3 produces a 2/3 that lets you cast instants from the graveyard during your upkeep; casting an Imperial Oath here makes 3 samurai, then Michiko's Reign pumps them. Kenji calls back-to-back Oaths "feels like you can't lose the game."
- **Kappa Tech-Wrecker + ninjutsu:** After Kappa hits, you ninjutsu it out and back in repeatedly, reBuying its death-touch / ETB artifact destruction. "After you've gotten in with Kappa Tech-Wrecker the first time, you get to potentially ninjutsu it back and rebuy the value."
- **Colossal Sky-Turtle + Season of Renewal:** "You pair this with Season of Renewal and you can just go infinite" (mill + shuffle loop). Kenji called it out as a degenerate combo to enable from the control position.
- **Modern Age (2-mana saga that loots twice then becomes a 2/3 flyer) + Mukotai Ambusher:** Rebuying Modern Age through ninjutsu is "pretty juicy for looting." Ambusher ninjutsus in, attacks, then bounces on your next turn to ninjutsu Modern Age back out.
- **Containment Construct + Modern Age / Akki Ronin:** "Containment Construct — surprisingly good, especially with cards like Modern Age." Whenever you discard a card (via Akki Ronin's attack trigger or Modern Age's loot), the construct lets you cast that discarded card this turn.
- **Spinning Wheel Kick (green instant; GGX, deal X to each of X creatures?):** Called out repeatedly as "the card I'm scared of" when attacking into green decks. "The one card in the format that scares me." Avoid overextending into it.

## Card tips

- **Kappa Tech-Wrecker is one of the top uncommons in the format** regardless of archetype. "This is just one of the top uncommons in the format — it doesn't really put you into a straightforward archetype. Doesn't matter whether you're in ninjas or Kami War." Take it over most non-bombs.
- **Bamboo Grove Archer (2G, 3/3 defender with reach) is one of the best two-drops.** Pick it early as a defensive anchor in any green deck. "Turn-two Bamboo Grove Archer can just stop them in their tracks."
- **Life of Toshiro Umezawa is one of the best uncommons.** "So so brutal — it's not even necessary on turn two, although if you have it on turn two, it's the nuts." Gain life baseline, pump or drain depending on board state.
- **Imperial Oath (6G, saga — 3×scry + make 2/2 samurai) is massively overperforming** as a "win con" and "removal vacuum." "Sweeping up value while they're flooding." Take it; it wheels less than you'd expect.
- **Cheap sagas play better than they look.** Kenji consistently notes this across multiple picks. "Azusa's Many Journeys doesn't look all that powerful but it plays out better than it looks — true again of the majority of cheaper sagas."
- **Season of Renewal must be respected.** It recovers from mill, recycles key enchantments, and invalidates a mill win. Draft it highly if building a long-game plan.
- **Colossal Sky-Turtle** is one of the best cards in the format for value decks ("one of the most insane value cards in the format"). A huge flyer that cycles from graveyard.
- **Specialist (?) and Prowler (Nezumi Prowler)** are the best ninja commons. Pick 2 Specialists minimum.
- **17 lands is correct for ninja decks**, not 16. "I think people usually run too few in their ninja decks — we still have a lot of stuff to do with our mana." Use the extra land to enable your ninjutsu activations on curve.
- **Grafted Growth goes on your most-represented basic type.** "You always want to put a Grafted Growth on your most abundant type." Don't put it on the splash color's basic.
- **Terrarium (2-mana artifact that taps for a color you're missing):** A must-run in 3+ color decks. Also an artifact for Roadside Reliquary activation.
- **Roadside Reliquary (land that draws if you have artifact + enchantment):** "Roadside Reliquary — kind of a free-roll card-draw land." Run one in any enchantment deck.
- **The Ratosai Sentinel (2-mana artifact that mills 3 on tap):** Good for setting up Brilliant Restoration targets. "Sometimes you can end up decking yourself in this format, so having access to sentinel is awkwardly pretty good — it does have synergy: target player mills three cards."

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting a Kami War deck without enough fixing.** Kenji went 3-3 in one run after failing to pick up dual lands. "Fixing we got was not good. Sadly we just didn't see much fixing. If I had moved into ninjas, I could see a world where playing the blue was just better."
- **Undervaluing blue when it's open.** In multiple drafts Kenji passed multiple strong blue cards while committing early to other colors. "Blue was super open here. Blue was flowing hardcore. That's too bad. Oh well. We've made our bed."
- **Playing the Kami War as a dead card.** Several games went long because the Kami War required five colors of mana. Don't lean on it as your only win con; the deck should function without it. "Games where I cast the Kami War are games we win" — but you have to set up the mana.
- **Not mulliganing hands that lack a two-drop in ninja decks.** "Not having a two drop in this deck is really bad — I generally mulligan this type of hand." The ninja deck lives and dies by turn-2 plays.
- **Bouncing the wrong thing with Kami War's second mode.** The second chapter bounces any non-land permanent to your opponent's hand — this can be your own card for reuse or their threat. Think before selecting the target.
- **Over-relying on mill when opponent has Season of Renewal or Colossal Sky-Turtle.** "It's always risky to mill green when Season of Renewal and the Turtle exist in the format." Check the opponent's colors before committing to a mill plan.
- **Wasting ninjutsu by attacking into unfavorable blocks.** When they can double-block your ninja, the value evaporates. Kenji lost games by attacking when he should have held back. "Maybe I should have held the familiar so they can't kill it — right now the familiar is the only thing that's going to allow us to attack into larger critters."

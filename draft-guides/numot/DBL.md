# DBL — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- DBL is the Innistrad Double Feature: a combined draft of Midnight Hunt (MID) and Vow of Midnight (VOW). Two full sets in the pack, which means higher variance and more bomb-density than a single-set format.
- Day/Night tracking is fundamental to every game — cast two spells on your turn to flip to day; don't cast spells to let night flip. Every creature with a flip trigger cares. "Day/night: cast two spells on your turn to flip to day." Missing day/night timing is a game-losing error.
- Format is medium speed — not the fastest (aggro is viable) and not the slowest. Curves from turn 2 onward still matter. Kenji was eager to leave the format for Kamigawa by end-of-run, suggesting moderate overall quality.
- Bombs are plentiful because two sets merge. First-pickable rares exist in every pack. Splash potential is high — Moonveil Regent, Light Up the Night, and Halana and Alena (?) are all splash-worthy.
- Kenji went 0-3 once in the format ("first 0-3 in the format"), noting the RW aggro build's 0-3 was driven by opponent-side bombs (double Ill-Tempered Loner), not deck weakness.

## Archetypes
- **Green-Black Werewolves / Good Cards** — Abrupt Caretaker ("one of best cards you can open — 4/4 hexproof, basically never dies"), Hound Tamer (flip = value engine that finds creatures), Eccentric Farmer (GB graveyard tool; good with reanimation), Hookhand Mariner (?), Halana and Alena (bomb rare splash), Ritual Slaughter (?). Goal: curve out with efficient hexproof threats, leverage Day/Night flipping for werewolf upsides, stabilize with lifegain from Blood Fountain.
- **Red-White Aggro** — Moonveil Regent (P1P1 splashable bomb), Brutal Cathar / Ardent Elementalist (?), Ill-Tempered Loner (bomb adjacent), Angel Fire Ignition (double-use via flashback), Brimstone Vandal (pingers), Alluring Suitor (aggressive rare). Goal: curve out aggressively, use Day/Night to flip werewolves, close with pump tricks (Angel Fire Ignition) and combat damage.
- **Red-Black Vampires** — Voldaren Bloodcaster (flips fast with graveyard enablers), Light Up the Night (P1P1 "kills everything"), Dreadhound (wheeled win-con, fantastic), Neonate's Rush (?), Eaten Alive (removal combo enabler), Undying Malice (recursion combo enabler), Bladebrand + deathtouch synergies, Purifying Dragon (?). Goal: aggressive vampire synergies, trade efficiently, recur threats with Undying Malice.
- **Blue-Red Spells** — Suspicious Stowaway × 2 ("way too good, wins games by itself"), Festival Crasher (gets +2/+0 per instant/sorcery cast), Voltaic Visionary (good in spells), Mysterious Tome (higher pick than most players take it), Cathartic Pyre (solid removal), Flip the Switch (key interaction), Dissipate (better with Day/Night cards). Goal: cast multiple spells per turn for Festival Crasher power boosts, grind card advantage with Stowaway.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Festival Crasher + double spell turn**: Festival Crasher gains +2/+0 for each instant/sorcery cast. On a turn where you cast two spells, Festival Crasher becomes an 5/2 or 6/2 that can attack for massive damage. Combine with Moonveil Regent for ping damage on top.
- **Eaten Alive + Undying Malice**: "I sacrifice a creature to Eaten Alive, and then that creature gets to come back with Undying Malice for free" — sacrifice your own creature that has Undying Malice on it as part of Eaten Alive's cost, then the Undying Malice triggers bring it back. The opponent's creature is exiled for effectively 0 net resource cost. "That's how we're going to exile things in this format."
- **Bladebrand + Purifying Dragon (?)**: Give a creature deathtouch with Bladebrand; if that creature has a ping ability (like Purifying Dragon's damage-redirect), the deathtouch + ping kills any creature at instant speed. "If we get Bladebrand and the dragon together, we can kill things with the deathtouch and the ping." Kenji calls the deathtouch ping "excellent."
- **Angel Fire Ignition (flashback)**: "Gives it first strike, trample, and like counters and stuff" — +3/+3 and keyword soup twice (once on cast, once from flashback). Kenji calls it "fantastic." Single card that closes games from nowhere when attached to a large creature.
- **Moonveil Regent + spell-heavy deck**: Regent triggers when you discard (?) and also provides pings to any target with its ability. In a spells-based UR deck, each cast triggers Regent's effects, generating extra damage alongside Festival Crasher.
- **Voldaren Bloodcaster + graveyard enablers**: Bloodcaster flips quickly when creatures die. "Voldaren bloodcaster is good, she flips pretty fast." Once flipped, it generates additional vampires or value each turn.
- **Dreadhound in black**: "Wheeled it, fantastic win con" — provides damage triggers when creatures die or enter, stacks well with black's sacrifice/recursion themes. Not a flashy card but closers games that go long.
- **Double Suspicious Stowaway**: "Two copies is way too good in my opinion, that card wins games by itself." Repeated looting/draw effect that fuels the graveyard while digging for action. Hard to race in a long game.
- **Ill-Tempered Loner combat tricks**: "Pretty hard not to two-for-one" — the Loner flips into a 7/6 or so and becomes a punishing blocker/attacker. Kenji lost to opponent double-Loner: "I couldn't have beaten that."

## Card tips

### Blue (U)
- **Mysterious Tome**: "I take higher than others, love the value." Card advantage looting tool; relevant in any blue deck and especially good in UR Spells.
- **Dissipate**: Gets better in this format because Day/Night incentivizes passing turns without casting spells — Dissipate fits naturally into a plan of passing to trigger Night. "Dissipate is getting better actually — you're obviously not always going to be able to pass" but it flows with the Day/Night rhythm.

### Black (B)
- **Blood Fountain**: "Always want at least one in black." Provides recursion and life gain; especially relevant for fueling graveyard-based vampires and keeping pace with aggressive decks.
- **Voldaren Bloodcaster**: Strong vampire payoff — "she flips pretty fast." Good P2P1 or early-pack pickup in RB Vampires.

### Red (R)
- **Moonveil Regent**: "Pick one pack one easy, splashable." Worth splashing a third color for. One of the best rares in the set.
- **Light Up the Night**: "Kills players, creatures, planeswalkers — does everything, splashable." P1P1 quality. The X cost makes it flexible from turn 2 onward.
- **Ill-Tempered Loner**: "Bomb adjacent — pretty hard not to two-for-one." Drafters often underestimate it; Kenji rates it extremely highly.
- **Cathartic Pyre**: Solid removal that also replaces itself; Kenji frequently runs it as primary cheap removal.
- **Alluring Suitor**: Strong aggressive rare in red-white; "definitely one of the better rares I've picked first" (when better picks weren't available).
- **Neonate's Rush (?)**: Good removal if you have enough 1-toughness targets to clear. Synergizes with black's small vampire tokens.

### Green (G)
- **Hound Tamer**: "Fantastic 3-drop, trample above average." The flip side can find creatures, which snowballs in a creature-heavy board.

### Colorless / artifacts
- **Foreboding Statue (?)**: Ramps and fixes for splashes. Good for reaching a splash bomb like Halana and Alena or Moonveil Regent. "Foreboding statue also ramps us up and fixes our mana."

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Abrupt Caretaker**: "One of the best cards you can open — 4/4 hexproof, basically never dies." P1P1 slam in green. Hard to remove without sacrifice effects.

## Pitfalls
- **Day/Night tracking errors**: Kenji attacked with Aberbruck (?) werewolf at the wrong time, missing double damage trigger. "I attacked with aberbruck at the wrong time — got to remember the double damage thing." Day/Night state changes mid-combat can create wrong assumptions.
- **Not curving out against aggro**: "Not curving out loses to aggro fast — not having 2-drops in opener is crippling on the draw." Any deck without early development is punished.
- **Overextending into sweepers**: "Not overextending into Vampires' Vengeance / By Invitation Only-type wraths." Both MID and VOW have board wipes; playing out your whole hand before threatening the opponent's enchantments/wraths is risky.
- **Punting in close games with punted lines**: Kenji's RB Vampires went 2-3 and he attributed the 3rd loss to "punting game 2." "We punted that one... yeah that was a punt, unfortunately." Late-game lines must be planned ahead.
- **Splashing without fixing**: Splash for Halana and Alena was justified with Foreboding Statue. Without the fixer, splashing two off-color cards leads to mana issues. "I'm taking this and I feel okay about the splash because we have the statue."
- **Undervaluing Day/Night flip timing for offense**: Kenji missed a key turn where flipping to Day would have given his werewolves their stronger front face — "you need to think about when to use your mana efficiently to push the day/night state."
- **Under-drafting festival crasher in UR**: Festival Crasher is easily the best common payoff for spells. If you're UR, take it aggressively early; the ceiling is enormous but requires spell density.
- **Passive play with Suspicious Stowaway**: "That card wins games by itself" — do not treat Stowaway as just incidental looting. Build around it, protect it, and use it as the primary engine.

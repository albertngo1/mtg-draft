# VOW — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- VOW (Innistrad: Crimson Vow) is a **tempo-oriented format** with a clear day/night transformation theme. The format is fast enough that two-drops matter greatly, but games can go long if flyers get stalled.
- The dominant late-format strategy is **Blue-Red (Izzet) tempo** with flying creatures and bounce/chill effects. Kenji describes "too much tempo" as both a winning and losing condition.
- **Werewolves (Green-Red)** is a strong tribal archetype; **Vampires (Black-White / Black-Red)** is the other main tribe. Spirit-based blue-white and human-tribal white also appear.
- Day/night flip mechanic: "it doesn't flip back on your turn if you double-spell on their turn" — flipping to night by not casting spells is a choice. Kenji repeatedly forgets this when evaluating attacks.
- **Blood tokens** are a meaningful resource — they give information (opponent is digging) and provide discard effects. "Nice getting blood here so good."
- Format has meaningful late-game bombs (Henrika Dominathi [?] — "a bomb rare"), but games often end on flying damage before they resolve.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Lantern Bearer + Throng**: 1-drop + 2-drop that immediately makes a flying threat. "One-drop flyer, two-drop flyer. Haha, shake my hand." The core curve of Izzet tempo.
- **Child of the Pack (Puppy) + multiple wolves**: Each wolf you play grows the Puppy by +1/+1 permanently. With 14 wolves/werewolves in deck, the Puppy can become enormous. "You cannot defeat my puppy."
- **Arsonist as finisher**: Kenji opens it in pack 2 and it wins the game. "We only drew arsonist once but it won the game." A bomb rare that doesn't need to be drawn often to matter.
- **Rending Flame bypasses flying restriction**: Can kill high-toughness flyers that other removal can't reach.
- **Geistflame Snare + Reckless Impulse (?)**: Kenji explicitly notes "snares are so awkward with reckless in this deck" and calls it out as anti-synergy — Snare needs mana open while Impulse wants you tapped out.
- **Blessed Bolt + Lantern Bearer combo (attack then bolt)**: "We want to Bolt if they block here prior to damage so we get Trample and a blood." Sequence spells to maximize damage + blood token generation.
- **Cradle of Safety**: Gives flying AND protection to a creature. "If I'm running three Lantern Bearers, it'd be nice to have multiple ways to protect a creature that I get flying." Doubles as offensive and defensive tool.
- **Throng of Starlings + Sac outlet**: Throng sacks to Amalgam (?) for bounce effect. "I don't want the throng to sack to the amalgam bing" — intentionally sequencing when to use Throng as an exploit target.
- **Brian Comer (?) / Brinebound Gift (?) on flyer**: "Double pump the spirit here... shine on briny boy." Stacking auras on evasive creatures for a fast clock. Protection makes it resilient.
- **Train mechanic [?]**: "Traveling minister" trains up creatures; a spirit being trained gains +1/+1 counters through combat. Double minister enables the trainee to level quickly.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Fierce Retribution (?)**: Conditional white removal — Kenji hates it but picks it as necessary filler: "I'll take a stupid removal spell." Play 1, not 2.
- **Piercing Light**: "I hate this card" but it serves a role. Pick it late as needed filler only.

### Blue (U)
- **Lantern Bearer**: "Lantern bear guaranteed two wins" (Kenji's heuristic about value of the card). 1-drop flyer that gives flying upside — always play, always play multiple.
- **Cradle of Safety**: Better than it looks if you have Lantern Bearers or other flyers. Run 2 if you have 3 Lantern Bearers.
- **Scattered Thoughts (?)**: 3-mana scry 1 draw 1 (or similar) — solid blue card draw. "Three mana scry one draw one" — good tempo.
- **Syncopate (?)**: "I can pick them up later and I'm not going to play more than one or two so I'd rather take basically any other blue card any other card that is blue." Don't prioritize early; it's a late-pick filler.
- **Impulse (?)**: "I think we're gonna take the card draw this time." Solid cantrip in Izzet tempo.

### Black (B)
- **Bleed Dry (?)**: Blue removal that Kenji respects highly — "bleed dry" at 5th pick or later is a strong signal for open blue.

### Red (R)
- **Rending Flame**: Draft aggressively in red. "I see a flame I take a flame."
- **Abrade**: One of the best removal spells — "kills artifacts. You know what's an artifact? Freakin knife." Kills equipment that would otherwise be very problematic.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Child of the Pack ("Puppy")**: First-pick material. "First picked a puppy over what — not too much." Grows with every wolf/werewolf you play. In a 14-wolf deck it becomes very large, very fast.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Taxidermist (?)**: Strong 2-drop ramper. "Having a two mana ramper is so much more important... Going from instead of three to five in this format — it's gross." In green, Taxidermist is a high pick.
- **Anya (?)**: "I think Anya is better than dreadfeast demon... it just comes down way too early." On the play vs. any uncommon, Anya is better.
- **Manabase**: Izzet blue-red with 10 islands, 7 mountains works. "Seven mountains, by the way" — Kenji tends to go 10/7 split for blue-heavy decks.

## Pitfalls

- **Forcing mono-color as a meme**: Kenji tries mono-blue, fails immediately when passed Henrika Dominathi — "I'm out, I tried." Don't force colors unless signals are overwhelming.
- **Holding bounce spell for wrong timing**: In one game, Kenji casts retrieval on opponent's turn to counter a play — "I knew better. I should have waited till my turn. Let them play their dude, bounce on my turn, they go for the bleed dry, I counter it and then we win. That game was very winnable." Patience with bounce/counter spells matters enormously.
- **Night/day flip timing**: "It doesn't flip back on your turn if you double-spell on their turn." Know the flip timing — don't pay a spell for night when attacking/pressuring would be better.
- **Flooding in the air**: Two games lost when Kenji draws 12+ of 16 lands. "We've drawn 12 of our 16 lands. Yeah I'd say that's not the best ratio." Running 16 lands in fast decks; the flood happens and there's nothing to do about it.
- **Loner (?) flip timing**: "That's why we didn't flip the loner" — flipping a werewolf to its night side at the wrong moment gives opponents free attacks. Evaluate the board carefully before committing to a flip.
- **Overextending into Wrath**: "Do I play around wrath or do I just dump my hand because I already dumped my hand." In this format wraths exist (Path of Peril [?]); playing around them at the cost of tempo is often wrong, but dumping your entire hand into a board wipe is catastrophic.
- **Geistflame Snare anti-synergy with Reckless Impulse**: Explicitly called out. If your build has multiple Snares, don't also run Reckless Impulse — the impulse forces you to tap out when you want mana up for Snares.
- **Throng sequencing**: Don't sack Throng to an exploit target if you want it as a flying threat. "Is it wrong to throng? It feels wrong but I'm thronging." — sequence exploits deliberately.
- **Bleed Dry on opponent's end step**: Casting removal on your own turn or opponent's upkeep rather than end step costs timing value. Know when each interaction is best used.

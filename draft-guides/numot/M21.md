# M21 — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- Best-of-one is the primary lens Kenji uses for M21 on Arena; he acknowledges it is "not a true representation of draft" compared to Bo3 but adapts accordingly.
- Format is scrappy/lower-power relative to sets like Ikoria; you often win with cards considered below the power curve.
- Four-mana creatures are "so big in this format" — hitting four drops consistently matters.
- Furious Rise (the "mill/ramp" enchantment) is a real threat; if you don't have creature removal early you lose to it.
- Splashing feels "really bad" and is consistently discouraged — straight two-color is the preferred build.
- Lose-to-flood risk is real for most decks; card draw and looting effects (Opt, Thrill of Possibility, Sky Scanner) mitigate it.

## Archetypes
- **Blue-Red tempo/control (most-drafted):** Sublime Epiphany as the dream finisher; fill the deck with Opts and Sky Scanners to dig for it; Volcanic Geyser as a flexible win condition; Opt + Rousing Read + Drowsing Tyranidon (?) as a smooth curve. Read the Tides as a haymaker. Tide Skimmer rewards going wide in the air — two attacking Skimmers draws multiple cards.
- **Black-Red removal-pile:** "Mono removal dot deck." Stack Grasp of Darkness, Scorching Dragonfire, Empower Emulate (?) (the 1/1 "Immolator" — auto-taps/pings on sac), Finishing Blow, Kinetic Augur (Kenji: "huge pickup"), Walking Corpse as filler two-drops alongside actual good two-drops. Chandra as the bomb anchor. Gameplan: two-drop, three-drop, removal, removal, GG.
- **Black-White lifegain:** Built around Mangara, the Diplomat (the 2/4 flying lifelink draw-cards mythic), Scoured Barons, Daybreak Charger (easy way to trigger three-life gain with any lifelinker), Kor Celebrant/Acolyte of Life (?), Infernal Scarring on a Patrician (?) to make it a 3-power lifelinker. Radiant Fountain supports the plan but Kenji caps at two copies.
- **Green-Black midrange/self-mill:** Safari-style value deck; Chrome Replicator (the card-copy artifact) plus pairs of Dreadmaw, Pride Malkin, Sabertooth Mauler, Llanowar Visionary. Drowsing Tyranidon is a card you "just pick up" whenever you see it. Hunter's Edge as premium removal. Lurking Deadeye (Skyway Sniper) surprisingly effective.
- **Green-White counters:** Conclave Mentor is the key build-around; stated as Kenji's favorite M21 archetype when it comes together.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Capture Sphere + Enthralling Hold:** Capture Sphere a creature, then Enthralling Hold it to gain control — you can then target your own controlled creature with Sublime Epiphany to copy it.
- **Infernal Scarring on Scoured Barons / Patrician:** Turns the 2/1 or 2/2 lifelinker into a 3-power lifelinker, which triggers lifegain payoffs on attacks without spending extra mana.
- **Immolator Imp (?) + equipment/scythe:** Equip the Scythe, ping the equipped creature; produces a 3/2 token AND pings the opponent simultaneously — "so juicy."
- **Llanowar Visionary + Read the Tides:** Visionary replaces itself (ETB draw), reduces flood risk, and can be bounced to reset the looter or used as extra mana for the six-drop.
- **Sublime Epiphany copying a Tide Skimmer while multiple Skimmers attack:** Draws an extra card for each Skimmer that attacked in the same combat.
- **Truffle Snout (?) as a three-mana 3/3 on curve:** Relevant lifegain in BO1 — easy to trigger three life if you cast it on turn three, tying in with the Sanctum of Stone Fangs lifegain strategy.

## Card tips
- **Mangara, the Diplomat:** "Gets around all the early burn spells" — do not put it in danger; hold up Feat of Resistance to protect it. Once it starts drawing cards it is very hard to lose.
- **Drowsing Tyranidon:** Grab every copy you see in a green deck; Kenji calls it a pick-up-every-time card.
- **Grasp of Darkness:** Best removal spell in the set; sixth-pick Grasps are a real signal to go black.
- **Sky Scanner:** Great with every card that rewards drawing or discarding; goes in almost any blue deck.
- **Rousing Read:** "Turn two Uro Rail (?) into turn three Rousing Read, opponent scoops" — underrated card draw.
- **Killing Blow (Finishing Blow):** Exiles, which matters against recursion; three copies in the removal-pile deck is one too many — cut one.
- **Hunter's Edge:** Pick this over most green cards; Kenji's go-to green removal in G/B or G/W.
- **Radiant Fountains:** Good with lifegain payoffs and Reassembling Skeleton (?) but Kenji rarely runs more than two; they replace a swamp and produce colorless.
- **Rin Filler (?)** / **Megalodon:** Kenji repeatedly cuts the seven-drops to stay on curve, especially in BO1. "Would rather have a lower curve."
- **Lofty Denial:** Good only in blue-white decks with many flyers; passable elsewhere.
- **Opt:** The cantrip that keeps the blue deck smooth; "just give me two more Opts and I'll call it today."
- **Revitalize:** Only worth running when you have enough three-life gain payoffs (Ghouls, Patricians, Sanctum); cycles out flood otherwise.
- **Thrill of Possibility:** Best flood mitigation in the red deck; run two copies.

## Pitfalls
- **Splashing in M21:** Kenji calls it out every time it comes up — "always feels really bad." Don't splash unless you have multiple dual lands and the spell wins the game.
- **Radiant Fountain over actual land:** Running three Fountains will cost you colored mana; it "would have been a swamp in the deck."
- **Playing equipment before attacking:** Remember to attack first if you want to equip post-combat to save mana; missed damage is a recurring mistake Kenji flags on himself.
- **Ignoring Furious Rise:** If you can't remove it cheaply (Grasp, Scorching Dragonfire), you will lose to the card advantage it generates — especially in the blue deck with no creature removal.
- **Auto-tap traps on Arena:** Arena sometimes taps suboptimally, especially when you want to cast a spell and ping with Immolator Imp in the same turn. Manually plan your mana.
- **Overloading on four-drops:** Green-black builds tend to accumulate four-drops; be willing to cut the weaker ones for two-drops and three-drops.
- **Best-of-one algorithm:** Running 16 lands is standard; 17 affects opening hands in a way Kenji is wary of even with high-end cards.
- **Keeping suspect hands in BO1:** Multiple risky keeps punished Kenji across these drafts — "risky hand, punished, opponent had a good draw, the trifecta of having a bad time."

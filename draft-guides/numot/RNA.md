# RNA — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
RNA is a multi-color format where gates enable fixing across 2–3 color combinations more easily than prior formats. Kenji finds this flexibility a feature, not a bug — gates let you "delve into multiple colors a lot easier." The format supports both aggressive decks (Rakdos) and grindy control/midrange builds (Esper, Azorius, Simic-based gate decks). Kenji's most-drafted pairing is "some combination of Esper" but also runs successful Rakdos aggro and Gruul/Simic builds. Gate payoffs (Gate Colossus) are real but require deliberate construction; splashing a gate-payoff without enough gates is a common mistake. Games can go long — clear the mind-style recursion is viable in creature-light removal-heavy decks.

## Archetypes
- **Esper / Azorius control**: Built around removal (Summary Judgment, Final Payment, Grotesque Demise), card advantage via Precognitive Perception (?)/ Shimmer of Possibility, and Senate Guildmage(?). Uses Absolution as a key engine — makes blocking and combat difficult. Slow curve with Sphinx of Foresight(?) as top end. Kenji notes "you don't need many creatures if you just have removal plus Absolution." Lockets (Dimir Locket, Azorius Locket) provide mana fixing and late card draw.
- **Rakdos aggro**: Low curve: Footlight Fiend, Spear Spewer, Blade Juggler, hackrabat(?)/Clamor Shaman, Goblin Gathering, Trumpeter(?) as the core. Blade Brand as combat trick / damage doubler — can "draw the game" by dealing massive damage when combined with Spear Spewer ping effects. Act of Treason as a finisher/combo piece. Cavalcade of Calamity is a viable build-around when you have enough 1-power creatures (Goblin Gathering + burn weenies). Must run 16 lands. Kenji says "it's so important to get good one drops like [Footlight] Fiend."
- **Simic / Gruul midrange**: Sorcerous Spyglass(?) / Sarovar (?) hybrid as 2-drop value engine. Savage Smash, Collision // Colossus, Gift of Strength, Stony Strength as combat tricks. Growth-Chamber Guardian for recurring threats. Gruul can go 5/5 or bigger with riot creatures. Beastmaster Ascension(?) with tricks is viable.
- **Gate / 5-color**: Gate Colossus as payoff; worth running if you have 4+ gates even without specific payoffs (fixing alone justifies them). Steeple Creeper synergizes well if you're already running gates. Open the Gates as enabler.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Blade Brand + Spear Spewer**: Giving Spear Spewer (1-damage pinger) double strike makes it ping for 2 per trigger(?) — Kenji calls this "draw the game" as a game-ender, especially vs. token boards.
- **Act of Treason + Blood Mist Infiltrator (?)**: Taking an opponent's creature at end of combat then sacrificing it with the infiltrator combo. The infiltrator was taken specifically as the "act of treason combo."
- **Shimmer of Possibility + Absolution**: With Absolution in play, you can dig for more removal and fuel the engine. Kenji recommends using Shimmer main phase to maximize how deep you dig.
- **Sky Tether + consume (?)**: Old sky tether consume combo — tethering a creature and then eating it for value.
- **Gate Colossus + Open the Gates / any gate**: Gate Colossus becomes free if you have enough gates; bounce and replay for repeatable value. Kenji notes "Manuel Tapper Colossus is sometimes GG" vs. certain decks.
- **Clear the Mind + creature-light deck**: Running multiple copies of this in decks with lots of removal but few creatures; recycles the removal engine. Kenji wanted to force an entire "creature-less wins with Clear the Mind" deck.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Raffine's Informant (Senate Courier? actually Raffine's Informant is SNC — this set's equivalent)**: Senate Guildmage(?) — Kenji values it over almost everything, saying "Senate Gill mage is so good."
- **Summary Judgment**: One of the best removal spells; "an easy Summary Judgment over the gate."

### Blue (U)
- **Faerie Duelist**: Important defensive 1/2 flash creature; "one three illusion is opie little cute squid thingy" — refers to Sharktocrab(?). Notably: Faerie Duelist stops most early threats cold.
- **Quench**: Opponents running this — play around it when you have important 2-drops.

### Black (B)
- **Grotesque Demise**: Strong removal even without deathtouch; Kenji takes it over Skewer #3 in some spots.
- **Blade Juggler**: Value discard 2-drop; synergizes with spectacle enabling and Cavalcade.

### Red (R)
- **Skewer the Critics**: Spectacle is easy to enable in Rakdos; 3 copies were drafted without hesitation. Cycles of "skewer skewer skewer" in early picks.

### Green (G)
- **Crocodile of the Crossing (?)**: Blocks so much in this format — "the crocodile might be better, it blocks so much in this format." Take it over most filler.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Footlight Fiend**: "So important to get good one drops like the Fiend." Wheels surprisingly often; grab it.
- **Final Payment**: Excellent cheap removal; keep mana open for it but don't overextend.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Absolution**: Late-game engine that makes attacks unprofitable; Kenji first-picks it and builds around it. Against it, can be blanked with enough non-white, non-black creatures.
- **Lockets**: Running double locket is fine in slow decks; they provide fixing and late-game card draw. Don't justify them with Anticipate(?) or Shimmer if you have better threats.

## Pitfalls
- **Playing Gate Colossus without gates**: "I've played Gate Colossus without any gates but that's not recommended." Draft gates deliberately if you want the payoff.
- **Cutting fixing lands too aggressively**: In Simic Gruul, not having a 6th land for adapt or a 4th green for Zahana (?) is game-losing. Kenji regretted cutting a Simic Guild Gate for a creature.
- **Act of Treason timing**: Kenji missed lethal by failing to ping with Spear Spewer the turn before using Act of Treason — "I messed up I need to do a ping… now I'm one short."
- **Over-cutting Shimmer**: "Cut shimmer when I have so many good cards that I want to dig for" — don't cut your card selection in control decks.
- **Not playing around quench on curve**: Playing 2-drops into open UU without considering Quench costs games.
- **High Alert matchups**: Sky Tether is "not very good if they're running a high alert deck" since it doesn't remove the threat. Recognition of this archetype is needed to adjust.
- **Drawing all one color in Rakdos**: "I feel like this happens a lot with my Rakdos decks — I just draw all of one color consistently." Run 8/8 split strictly; mana bases need to be respected.

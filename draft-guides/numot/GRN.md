# GRN — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?). Set is Guilds of Ravnica (2018 Arena); VODs from early season grinding (Kenji goes from Bronze Day 1 to Platinum in 3 days).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Boros is the most common and powerful aggro deck.** Across all 4 VODs, Boros is either what Kenji drafts or the most common matchup. "At least 50% of opponents are Boros aggro" (implied by matchup frequency). Mentor rewards going wide with cheap creatures.
- **Dimir is the premier control deck.** Kenji's best results come from Dimir. "In the last three days I've gone 53 and 11 in Guilds of Ravnica draft." Disinformation Campaign (2-mana enchantment, surveil 2 on every upkeep) is the defining card — "one of the best cards in the format."
- **Surveil is the dominant mechanic.** Nearly every Dimir card surveils. Surveil 2 fills the graveyard, fixes draws, and feeds Disinformation Campaign. Kenji counts 12 surveil triggers in one build and considers it a "deck defining mechanic."
- **Mephitic Vapors is the premier sweeper.** Kenji main decks it every game. "In this format I just main deck it." Deals 1 damage to all creatures, surveil 2. Excellent against Boros go-wide.
- **Bo1 favors aggro but Dimir punishes it.** Boros exploits the play/draw variance of Bo1 but Dimir's defensive suite (Wall of Mist, Barrier of Bones, Ritual of Soot) stabilizes against aggro.
- **Mentor scales with creature count.** Every Boros mentor creature's trigger depends on having a creature with lower power. Kenji counts "five mentor creatures" and values them specifically for how they feed each other.
- **Format is a trophy machine for Dimir.** Multiple 7-1s described: "Three seven ones in a row. This one we lost round one. Seven in a row."

## Archetypes

### Blue-Black Control (Dimir)
- **Gameplan:** Surveil to find answers, stabilize with Barrier of Bones and Wall of Mist, deploy Thief of Sanity and Muse Drake as threats, control the board with Ritual of Soot + Mephitic Vapors, win by card advantage from Disinformation Campaign + surveil.
- **Key cards:** Thief of Sanity (2/2 flying deathtouch — "I'm going to take the thief even though it's two colors"; must-answer threat), Disinformation Campaign (2B enchantment — surveil 2 on upkeep + drain; "card's very good"), Notion Rain (B/B sorcery draw 2 surveil 2 — "both amazing"), Muse Drake (3U 2/3 flash flyer — draw when it deals damage; "super good"), Ritual of Soot (BB destroy all 2-or-less power; "pretty hard to pass"), Dead Weight (B enchantment, -2/-2; best early removal — "easy"), Price of Fame (2B instant kill legendary + surveil 2; "price of fame over nothing"), Deadly Visit (2B sorcery — kill + surveil 2; "whole moly"), Thought Erasure (UB hand disruption + surveil 1), Dark Blade Agent (1UB 2/2 deathtouch — gains the keywords of surveil cards; "the agents are just amazing"), Watcher in the Mist (4U 3/3 flash flyer — draws when it deals damage), Sinister Sabotage (1UU counterspell + surveil 2 — "good pickup"), Enhanced Surveillance (2U enchantment — resets graveyard and doubles surveil; "very good"), Disinformation Campaign.
- **Wall package:** Wall of Mist (0/5 defender) and Barrier of Bones (0/3 skeleton surveil on death) stabilize against aggro. "Might honestly be running all wall of mist." 2-3 copies of each in heavy aggro metagames.
- **Strength:** Self-sustaining. Disinformation Campaign grinds opponents out of cards. Ritual of Soot resets Boros boards. Thief of Sanity steals opponent's threats.
- **Weakness:** Defensive early; needs 3-5 turns to establish. Vulnerable to Inescapable Blaze (6-mana uncounterable burn) — "the only way I lose is from Inescapable Blaze." Flooding on walls when facing midrange.

### Red-White Aggro (Boros)
- **Gameplan:** Curve 1-2-3 mentor creatures, grow them with mentor triggers, use combat tricks (Sure Strike, Integrity, Maniacal Rage) for surprise damage, close with Cosmotronic Wave (all your creatures are unblockable this turn).
- **Key cards:** Skyknight Legionnaire (2/2 haste flyer — always a strong opener), Sunhome Stalwart (1/2 first strike — one of the best two-drops), Blade Instructor (?) / Barging Sergeant (3/2 or similar mentor creature), Banneret(?) / Healer's Hawk (1/1 flying lifelink — "start off uh, uh mentoring on some creatures"), Healer's Hawk (1/1 lifelink — excellent 1-drop), Direct Current (1R instant deal 2 damage or draw; "one of the best cards in the mirror match"), Fire Urchin (1/1 first strike — gains +1/+1 when you target it; "amazing if you have enough mentor"), Cosmotronic Wave (all creatures are unblockable — "give me that wave. Wave goodbye."), Luminous Bonds (2W enchantment tap; "Luminous Bonds over nothing"), Gird for Battle (Integrity // Intervention — 0/1 mode gives +1/+1 counters to two creatures; good mentor trigger for 1 mana).
- **Note on Direct Current:** In the mirror, DC is the most valuable card — both burn face AND kills a key mentor creature. Kenji: "Direct Current — that is one of the best cards in the mirror match."
- **Note on Fire Urchin:** Urchin grows when targeted by instants/sorceries you control. This anti-synergizes with Cosmotronic Wave (Kenji: "Cosmotronic wave pumps my fire urchin so that bodyguard can't mentor onto it"). Sequence carefully.
- **Strength:** Fast, consistent. Turn-3 Mentor into turn-4 combat trick closes games quickly. Multiple 7-1 records with Boros.
- **Weakness:** No card advantage. Floods badly if opponents stabilize. Mephitic Vapors (- 1 to all at sorcery speed, surveil 2) undoes the board.

### Izzet (Grixis) Spells (UR + black splash)
- **Gameplan:** Cast spells to trigger Murmuring Mystic (1/3 flying, makes 1/1 bird token on instants/sorceries) and guild mage effects, use Hypothesis All (?) and Discovery // Dispersal for card selection, burn face with Beacon Bolt / Inescapable Blaze.
- **Key cards:** Murmuring Mystic (key uncommon — makes 1/1 flyers on all instants/sorceries; "murmuring mystic and spells it's good"), Crackling Drake (2UR 0/2 draw+grow on instants in yard; "I guess I'll just take the damn Drake"), Hypothesis All (?) / Radical Idea (UU sorcery draw 2; jump-start draw), Beacon Bolt (URR — deal damage equal to number of sorceries in yard; "have a feeling I'm gonna lose to blaze"), Guild Mage (activatable effect copies spells?), Selective Snare (X + sorcery — bounce X creatures of same type; "I can selectively snare two cats right now"), Unexplained Disappearance (U bounce + surveil 1), Invert // Invent (1R instant — Invert: two target creatures swap power/toughness; Invent: tutor artifact or enchantment).
- **Note:** The Izzet deck needs both burn and a payoff. Without Murmuring Mystic or Crackling Drake, it's just mediocre blue-red with bad cantrips.
- **Weakness:** Bad against fast Boros starts. Kenji explicitly notes "red was a mistake" in one VOD where he ended up in Izzet without enough spells or payoffs.

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Disinformation Campaign + any surveil:** Campaign drains opponent of 1 life and makes them discard each upkeep if you surveil that turn. Any surveil source keeps it ticking every turn — even Barrier of Bones dying (surveil 1), Dead Weight ETB, Price of Fame, Notion Rain. "I have 12 surveil triggers in this deck." Maintaining a surveil trigger every turn wins games in 5-6 turns of passive drain.

2. **Dark Blade Agent + surveil cards:** Dark Blade Agent (1UB 2/2 deathtouch) gains all keyword abilities of instant/sorcery surveil spells you cast. Kenji notes the agents as "amazing" — after one Notion Rain, the Agent gains extra abilities. Key reason to play surveil-heavy Dimir.

3. **Ritual of Soot + surveil 2:** Ritual destroys all creatures with power 2 or less, then surveil 2. In a Boros-heavy meta, this resets their board while digging two cards deep. "That's about as good as a Ritual of Soot is going to get versus the Demir deck." Aim to have it when they're at 3-4 creatures.

4. **Fire Urchin + Mentor triggers (anti-synergy warning):** Cosmotronic Wave makes your creatures unblockable AND pumps Fire Urchin's power. If Urchin's power is now higher than a Bodyguard/Banneret, Bodyguard can no longer mentor onto it. Sequence: use mentor triggers to grow Urchin BEFORE Wave, or just don't Wave when Urchin is your pump target.

5. **Muse Drake + Watcher in the Mist timing:** Both are flash creatures with "draw card when deals damage." Dropping them at end of opponent's turn gives you a full attack step. Important: against opponents with Whisper Agent (1/1 deathtouch flash), your Drake attack can be blocked by a surprise body. "Why didn't I think about Whisper Agents? It's like one of the most common things in Dimir."

6. **Discovery // Dispersal + Notion Rain sequencing:** Discovery is UB instant — surveil 2, then draw 1. Running it before Notion Rain on the same turn gives two surveil triggers for Disinformation Campaign in one turn. "Got a second notion rain... these are gonna be painful."

7. **Bodyguard + targeting enemy creatures:** Kenji misses blocking with Wall of Mist in one game ("I forgot to block with my wall of mist just took three unnecessary damage"). Always check if your 0-power defenders should be blocking.

8. **Hypothesis All(?)/Invert + Dazzling Lights combo:** Kenji references "dazzling lights within Bert and Ben combo" — Dazzling Lights (taps creatures you control, then they get -2/-2 until EOT) + Invert (swap P/T) = make a large creature into a 0-power blocker then Dazzling it to tap. Incomplete reference but the combination was mentioned as an interaction.

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Sunhome Stalwart** — 1/2 first strike for 1W. "One of the best two-drops." Mentor trigger on attack.
- **Healer's Hawk** — 1/1 flying lifelink for W. Strong 1-drop for Boros; gains life while mentoring up other creatures.
- **Tenth District Guard** — "10th district guard did something again for like the second time in recorded history." Very weak — only note it for how rare its trigger fires.

### Blue (U)
- **Enhanced Surveillance** — "Very good" — resets graveyard, doubles all future surveil. In long games, recycling your deck is relevant.
- **Murmuring Mystic** — 1/3 flying that makes 1/1 birds on each instant/sorcery. "Murmuring Mystic and spells it's good." Premium in Izzet.
- **Wall of Mist** — 0/5 defender. "I'm hoping not to" run it. Dead card against control. Only main-deck against heavy aggro.
- **Enhanced Surveillance** — While good in grindy games, "I don't think I need the surveillance in this deck" when already winning on surveil count.

### Black (B)
- **Dead Weight** — Best common black removal. -2/-2 kills most early threats and the 2/2 Boros staples. "Easy."
- **Ritual of Soot** — 4-mana wrath (power 2 or less), surveil 2. "Pretty hard to pass." Main deck it.
- **Mephitic Vapors** — 2B sorcery deal 1 to all + surveil 2. "In this format I just main deck it." Handles tokens and small creatures while keeping your Walls alive.
- **Price of Fame** — 2B kill legendary + surveil 2. Or "price of fame over nothing" — even as a pure 4-mana kill spell with surveil it's fine. Better vs. legendary-heavy opponents.
- **Barrier of Bones** — 0/3 skeleton surveil 1 on death. Playable but Kenji cuts 2 of 3 in most builds. "Highly unlikely I'm running three barrier bones." Only play 1-2 if aggro is dominant.

### Red (R)
- **Cosmotronic Wave** — 3R make your creatures unblockable this turn + all opponent's creatures can't block. Closes games instantly. One of the best Boros closers.
- **Direct Current** — 1R deal 2 to any target. "One of the best cards in the Boros mirror." Double mode (face or creature) makes it extremely flexible.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Disinformation Campaign** — Best blue-black uncommon. Surveil 2 every upkeep + 1 life drain + 1 discard. "Card's very good." First pick.
- **Notion Rain** — "Both amazing." Draw 2 surveil 2 for 2B. Excellent rate. Take over Muse Drake early.
- **Thief of Sanity** — 2/2 flying deathtouch that lets you cast opponent's cards on damage. "I'm going to take the thief even though it's two colors." First-pick worthy.
- **Dark Blade Agent** — 1UB 2/2 deathtouch + gains keywords from surveil spells. "The agents are just amazing." Pick up multiple.
- **Skyknight Legionnaire** — 2/2 haste flyer for RRW. Best Boros 3-drop. Takes over games quickly.
- **Thought Erasure** — Kenji takes it over blanks but notes it doesn't advance Disinformation Campaign directly. Discard without surveil. "Not huge on Thought Erasure."

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Banneret(?)** — "Banneret's very good" — Kenji first-picks a second copy. Strong mentor enabler.
- **Halberdier** — "I'm definitely not running Halberdier." Just a vanilla beater without upside in these synergy decks.

## Pitfalls

- **Forgetting to block with Wall of Mist.** Happens in the transcript mid-game: "I forgot to block with my wall of mist, I just took three unnecessary damage. Oops." Zero-power defenders can easily be forgotten — always check before passing priority.
- **Dumping your surveil outlet while Disinformation Campaign is active.** Campaign needs a surveil trigger each turn. If you Notion Rain on your turn but binned it to the graveyard, you need another surveil trigger on that upkeep. Track your surveil sources carefully.
- **Over-indexing on flash threats without checking Whisper Agents.** Kenji attacks with Muse Drake into a Dimir opponent and gets blocked by a surprise Whisper Agent (deathtouch flash 1/1). "Why didn't I think about Whisper Agents? It's like one of the most common things in Dimir. What have I done." Always account for the most common flash blockers before committing attacks.
- **Mis-sequencing Bodyguard's protection.** Kenji notes "I forgot with the bodyguard you have to individually click your creatures" — Darkblade Agent's bodyguard ability lets it take damage for another creature, but only if you manually activate it. Don't rely on auto-assign in important combat.
- **Running too many 5+ drops in Boros.** Kenji cuts Command the Storm and Pack Beast to lower curve: "I'm a little bit heavy on the five drops." In Boros, curve must peak at 4 (Cosmotronic Wave); anything above that hurts consistency.
- **Committing to red when signals indicate Dimir is open.** In VOD 4, Kenji regrets red cards: "red was a mistake I unfortunately must agree." Take the first signal of an open color pair — don't force a color because you took one good card.
- **Not running Severed Strands when you have defensive walls.** "Strands with double wall of mist and barrier bones is actually what I want to be." Severed Strands (1G sorcery — sac a creature, destroy target creature, gain life equal to its toughness) + a Wall is an excellent value trade. Dimir should pick up 1-2 copies.

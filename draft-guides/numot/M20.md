# M20 — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- M20 is a core set with a moderately fast format. White-based flyer strategies are dominant — Kenji drafts blue-white flyers in multiple VODs and wins consistently with them.
- Heart-Piercer Bow (?) is called out repeatedly as a build-around weapon: "More and more I think people are just drafting every single bow." Running Renowned Weaponsmith (?) to tutor bows is a real archetype.
- The format has many cheap combat tricks (Infuriate, God's Willing, Inspired Charge) that punish aggressive double-blocks. Kenji warns repeatedly: "There are a lot of good cheap combat tricks in this format so blocking is not advisable."
- Bomb rares (Vivien, Chandra, Agent of Treachery, Gishath) exist and can dominate games, but consistency matters more — Kenji says "curve, curve, curve" is the path to wins.
- Black-green reanimator (Blood for Bones + Meteor Golem) is a real archetype, though Kenji rates it as "medium" with a ceiling of 5 wins.

## Archetypes
- **White-Blue Flyers (primary):** Pacifism, Griffin Protector (?), Dawning Angel (?), Fairy Miscreant (?), Master Splicer (makes a 3/3 golem), and equipment or Gauntlets of Might (?). The deck curves 2-3-4 and wins in the air before ground-based decks can stabilize. Aerial Assault (?), Inspired Charge, and God's Willing serve as pump/protection finishers.
- **White-Red Aggro:** Similar plan but more burn, leaning on Flame Sweep (?) for board control. Kenji runs double Aerial Assault with this shell. Lower curve than white-blue.
- **Renowned Weaponsmith Bow-Tribal:** Renowned Weaponsmith tutors Heart-Piercer Bow at UU; with multiple bows and a Vial of Dragonfire (?) you get a repeatable damage engine. Kenji notes opponents with 3-4 bows are extremely difficult to beat. Bag of Holding (?) has synergy here.
- **Black-Green Reanimator:** Blood for Bones + Meteor Golem (?) as a late-game value engine. Pattern Matcher (??) (2/2 for 2 that puts a copy of a creature in hand when it attacks — possibly "Gorging Vulture"?) as a copy-creature effect. Bone Splinters for removal. Season of Growth (?) for card draw. Growth Cycle (?) for pump and scry. Audacious Thief (?) for card draw. Kenji says "I liked this format... for a core set it's felt good."

## Card interactions & combos
- **Renowned Weaponsmith + Heart-Piercer Bow:** Weaponsmith taps for UU to tutor bows or Vial of Dragonfire. With 3-4 bows in the deck you always have one; equipping a creature that attacks and taps kills any blocker. Kenji says opponents with many bows are "near-impossible to beat."
- **Fencing Ace + Gauntlets of Might (?):** Double-striker with Gauntlets gets first-strike damage, then pumps on regular damage — explicit "combo" Kenji mentions.
- **Griffin Sentinel (?) + Gauntlets of Might:** Same principle — vigilance creature stays back for defense while Gauntlets makes attackers huge.
- **Blood for Bones + Meteor Golem:** Sac any creature to return Meteor Golem; Golem destroys any non-land permanent on ETB. Recursion loop makes the deck resilient. Kenji says "turns out Blood for Boning your Meteor Golem twice is pretty good."
- **Master Splicer + Blood for Bones:** Splicer makes a 3/3 golem token and pumps all golems; Blood for Bones sacs it, returns a bigger creature, and you still have the golem token — clean 2-for-1.
- **God's Willing + white flyer:** Gives protection and scry 1. Can save a key creature from removal or push through a blocker mid-combat. Kenji calls it out as one of his favorite tricks in white.
- **Moment of Heroism + any creature in combat:** +2/+2 and lifelink during combat; Kenji saves these for key defensive moments rather than burning them on early trades.
- **Agent of Treachery + any ramp:** Agent needs 7 mana; Rapacious Dragon makes treasures, making it easier to cast your 7-drops. Kenji notes the dragon's treasure mode is specifically for casting your big ones.

## Card tips
- **Pacifism:** Kenji takes it early across all three archtypes. "I guess I don't care for it as much in this particular format but I'm gonna take it anyways" — still a premium removal piece.
- **Fairy Miscreant (?):** 1-mana 1/1 flyer that draws a card when you control another Fairy Miscreant. Kenji gets very excited at turn-1 + turn-2 Miscreant lines.
- **Griffin Protector (?):** Solid white flyer that triggers off creature arrivals; forms the backbone of the aggro plan.
- **Dawning Angel (?):** 4/4 flying lifelink; described as a finisher. Kenji says "I need to draw Dawning Angel right now" in losing positions.
- **Aerial Assault (?):** White instant that kills a creature blocking a flyer; great in the flyer deck where your opponent often blocks awkwardly. Kenji explicitly takes multiples when going flyers.
- **Inspired Charge:** The classic "alpha strike" pump spell. Kenji fears it from opponents and uses it himself to close games. He drafts it early in white.
- **Rapacious Dragon:** "Treasures mean you a lot easier to cast your big ones." Good rate and fixing for multi-color bombs.
- **Agent of Treachery:** The premium blue bomb — steals any permanent. Kenji agonizes over landing it through removal; often game-winning but requires 7 mana.
- **Hanged Executioner (?):** Black removal; Kenji takes early. "Easy second pick."
- **Audacious Thief:** Aggressive black 2-drop that steals a card when it deals combat damage. Enables mid-range black builds; combines with Blood for Bones.
- **Gorging Vulture (?):** Self-mill enabler for the black deck; turns on Blood for Bones value. Kenji values it specifically in black.
- **God's Willing:** High pick in any white deck — protection, evasion, and scry.
- **Flame Sweep (?):** Board wipe for ground creatures that spares flyers; excellent in white-red flyers.
- **Bone Splinters:** Cheap removal requiring sacrifice; synergizes with all black reanimator builds.
- **Meteor Golem:** 7-mana 3/3 that destroys any non-land permanent on ETB; overcosted but devastates opposing bombs. "Meteor Golem MVP" is a literal VOD title.

## Pitfalls
- **Don't keep 4-drop-heavy hands:** Kenji notes repeatedly that hands "with like four 4-drops" are slow and punishable. Keep hands that do something turns 1-3.
- **Don't blindly block against tricks:** "There are a lot of good cheap combat tricks in this format so blocking is not advisable." Always represent the possibility of Infuriate, God's Willing, or Inspired Charge.
- **Don't take Gauntlets of Might too late:** It's a build-around that needs a critical mass of creatures to fire. Kenji takes it early and plans around it.
- **Don't over-invest in the Weaponsmith-Bow plan without multiple smiths:** "You really want multiple Weaponsmiths is the true ideal." One Smith with 3-4 bows is good; without tutors the bows sit in hand.
- **Don't skip curve creatures for splashy picks:** Kenji emphasizes that core set limited rewards curve-outs. Missing a 2-drop or 3-drop means your hand floods with 4-drops and you fall behind tempo.
- **Aerial Assault is poor in non-flyer decks:** "These aerial assaults just aren't very good in a red-white aggro [without flyers] I'd have to be blue." Commit to flyers before picking multiples.
- **Don't play 3 skeletons (filler black common):** Kenji cuts down to 2 at most. The card is described as going "down and down and down in my opinion."
- **Fencing Ace is weak without equipment:** A 1/1 double-striker without a pump tool does very little. Take the Gauntlets or equipment to make it a real threat.

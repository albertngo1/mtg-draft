# BRO — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read
- The Brothers' War is an artifact-centric format. Most decks want artifacts and have synergies around them. Prototype creatures are central; knowing when to cast them at reduced cost vs. full cost is a key skill.
- Format evolved significantly over its run: "blue green artifact ramp came down a lot as format progressed." By end of format, aggressive red-white and red-black decks were the top tier.
- Power Stones (produced by many cards) are critical mana enablers for casting Prototype creatures at full cost or enabling late-game spells.
- Best-of-three metagame adds the Brotherhood's End question: it is "great, main deckable" in the right deck, and Kenji regretted not maindecking it multiple times.

## Archetypes
- **Red-White Aggro (Siege Veteran)** — Siege Veteran as the P1P1 "one of best rares in the format," Chaplain of Arms (life gain + evasion), Scrapwork Cohort (Haste, multiple instances), Scrapwork Mutt × 3 (discard engine), Recommission (3-or-less reanimate), Brotherhood's End (wrath + artifact sweeper), Tyrant of Kher Ridges as additional bomb. Goal: outrace with Haste creatures, recur threats via Recommission and Mutt. One of the most consistent end-of-format archetypes.
- **Red-Black Aggressive Graveyard** — Blast Runner (?), Carrion Locust (?), Blitz Automaton (?), Excavation Explosion (best red removal), Emergency Weld (best black recursion), Overwhelming Remorse (exile removal that scales down), Thraxodemon (sac engine). Goal: trade resources aggressively, refuel from the graveyard.
- **Green-Black Graveyard Value** — Skyfisher Spider (sac to kill non-land permanents), Emergency Weld × 2, Argothian Sprite, Boulder Branch Golem (7-mana 6/6 life gain), Coarser (?) (Colossal Dreadmaw-style creature with graveyard trigger). Goal: outlast and outvalue with recursive threats. Keening Stone as auxiliary mill win-con.
- **Green-Blue Artifact Ramp** — Mightstone's Animation × 3 (animate artifacts + draw), Over the Top (7-mana red; splashed), Centennial Stalwarts, Zephyr Sentinel (?), Boulder Branch Golem (never prototype). Goal: generate Power Stones and ramp into enormous late-game artifacts. Kenji notes this fell off in power level by end of format.
- **Black-X Graveyard (generic)** — Emergency Weld "insane in black" is the defining card for any black graveyard build.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Sculpting Steel + Portal to Phyrexia**: Sculpting Steel copies the Portal ("portal to frixia" in captions, corrected). When Portal resolves, opponent scoops. When you copy it, you have two Portals — "everyone scoop now." This is the highest-ceiling combo in the format.
- **Portal to Phyrexia + Thraxodemon**: Portal reanimates something each upkeep; Thraxodemon forces sacrifice each turn. Opponents are racing against an inevitability clock where they sacrifice to Thraxodemon while you generate value every upkeep.
- **Scrapwork Mutt + Recommission**: Discard a creature to Mutt's draw trigger → Recommission reanimates that creature the same turn. Generates continuous value; Mutt becomes a filtering + tutoring engine.
- **Keening Stone (standalone mill)**: Keening Stone (often misread in captions as "caning stone" / "cleaning stone") mills 4, then 8, then 16 — exponential. "That's the plan, get Keening Stone going." Vulnerable to artifact removal.
- **Mightstone's Animation + 0-cost artifact**: Animate the artifact as a 4/4 flyer, draw a card. With three copies you have three potential 4/4 flyers and draw three cards from small artifacts.
- **Skyfisher Spider + opponent's big non-land permanent**: Sac the Spider as an instant-speed removal spell. "Skyfisher spider is a card that you use to remove a really big non-land permanent when they put it into play, that's what it's best at." Opponents often don't see it coming.
- **Boulder Branch Golem at full cost (7 mana)**: Always cast for 7 in green-blue ramp. "Boulder branch golem never wanted to prototype it." The 6/6 + 6 life stabilizes virtually every board state.
- **Emergency Weld loops**: Weld reanimates from graveyard to the battlefield. With multiple recursion targets, black decks grind past opponents who run out of removal.

## Card tips
- **Portal to Phyrexia**: "Instant scoop" when cast. If you open it, you are in red-black or you splash it. Kenji says "that card should just be banned in this format" (said as hyperbole, but signals how busted it is).
- **Siege Veteran**: P1P1 material. "One of the best rares in the format." Powers up Scrapwork Cohort, Chaplain of Arms, every creature in the deck.
- **Keening Stone**: Strong standalone win-con. Mill 4 → 8 → 16. Draft it if you're green-black and don't have another win condition.
- **Brotherhood's End**: "Should almost always maindeck it" in white-based decks. Kenji forgot to sideboard it in multiple games in the Decathlon and always regretted it. Main deckable in the right shell.
- **Emergency Weld**: "Insane in black" — recurring threats for free is always powerful. Take it very high in any black deck.
- **Scrapwork Cohort**: "One of the best commons in the set." Haste is invaluable; Cohort recurs and fills the Mutt hand efficiently.
- **Recommission**: Only hits MV 3 or less — important constraint. In the right deck it is a must-have, but do not draft it without viable targets.
- **Ichor Wellspring**: Great early pick; draws two cards over its life and counts as an artifact for synergies.
- **Boulder Branch Golem**: Almost never cast as a 3/3 for prototype cost. Cast for 7 as a 6/6 life gain body. "Never wanted to prototype it."
- **Mightstone's Animation**: Premium blue card — any artifact becomes a 4/4 flyer and draws a card. Three copies is not too many.
- **Excavation Explosion**: Best red removal common in the set. Take it over most commons.
- **Argothian Sprite (?)**: Good in GB; the flier provides evasion the archetype otherwise lacks.
- **Tyrant of Kher Ridges**: Second bomb-level rare behind Siege Veteran in red-white. If you wheel it, take it immediately.
- **Overwhelming Remorse**: Exile removal that can cost 1 mana if opponent has creatures in graveyard. Scales extremely well.
- **Centennial Stalwarts**: Useful in GU ramp for the body and Power Stone generation.
- **Thran Power Suit (?)**: Gives a creature Ward 4 — note that with Ward 4 on a creature, opponents burning removal are taking a significant tempo hit. Strong protection piece in go-wide decks.

## Pitfalls
- **Casting Prototype creatures at reduced cost when full cost is better**: "Boulder branch golem never wanted to prototype it." Evaluate every Prototype creature at the start of the turn — can you afford full cost? If yes, usually do it.
- **Never getting to cast the fun cards**: Kenji drafted Over the Top (7-mana red) and Keening Stone as primary win-con in one draft but Over the Top never resolved. "It just never came up." In high-pressure tournament games, finesse cards that require setup often sit uncast.
- **Artifact removal (Shoot Down) killing your win-con**: Keening Stone as primary win-con is fragile. "If they had a destroy target artifact effect we would have been in a lot of trouble." Never rely on a single artifact to win.
- **Playing for fun vs. playing to win**: Kenji explicitly calls out "I am not playing for the win, I am playing for fun" in one UG ramp build — a refreshing honesty, but also a warning. In a competitive environment, fun-max decks underperform.
- **Forgetting to sideboard Brotherhood's End in BO3**: Happened multiple times. Keep it in mind; "I keep forgetting Brotherhood's End."
- **Only 12 creatures**: Kenji identified one loss was partly from running too few creatures. "One glaring problem with this deck — creature count might be a little bit low overall."
- **Casting over-the-top at wrong phase / not casting it at all**: In the GU ramp draft, the turn to cast Over the Top never arrived because the game ended first. Recognize when your "plan B" is too slow for the board state.
- **Misreading Skyfisher Spider timing**: Best used reactively after opponent plays a big threat. Don't sac it proactively unless you have nothing better.

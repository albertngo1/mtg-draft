# STX — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- STX (Strixhaven: School of Mages) is a **slow, grindy format**. Kenji explicitly contrasts it with faster formats: "you don't necessarily need to have 10 two-drops to survive." Games often go very long.
- The dominant late-format meta is **either black-white aggro** OR **four/five-color control soup**. Kenji drafts and discusses both extensively.
- **Learn/Lesson** is the defining mechanic. Kenji calls it "a very very powerful mechanic" — it's effectively drawing a card every time, and if you have no lessons in your sideboard you can still rummage (discard + draw).
- The mystical archive inserts power-up the format significantly. Cards like Eliminate, Expel, Day of Judgment, Expressive Iteration, and others appear in packs and are often first-pick quality.
- Five-color control ("five-color soup") is a real archetype, not a meme — Kenji consistently goes 6–7 wins with it and describes it as winning "again" at the end of each VOD.

## Archetypes

### Silverquill (Black-White aggro/tokens)
- Gameplan: low curve, go wide with tokens, use Dramatic Finale or similar finishers. "Black-white was the more aggressive strategy in this format."
- Key cards: Exquisite Archivist (?) ("Exodus the Auric Overlord" — 2/4 double strike for 4, magecraft returns creature from graveyard), Dramatic Finale (creatures +1/+1; when non-token creature dies, make a 2/1 token), Silverquill Campus.
- Weakness: needs actual creatures. Kenji's draft ran short on bodies and suffered badly — "a lot of the games if we had just drawn like one more removal spell it seems like we would have been okay since we had so much good removal even though the creature quality was a little bit lacking."
- Lash of Malice and other cheap black removal are important; white has Banishing Verse (exile mono-colored permanent) and Beaming Defiance (hexproof trick).
- Closing Statement (5-mana exile + counter) is a strong uncommon in the archetype.

### Prismari (Blue-Red spells / combo-control)
- Gameplan: cast instants and sorceries to trigger magecraft, make big fractals (XX tokens) with Quandrix, use Rootha (?) ("Ruth it" — 3-mana 1/4, return to hand + copy instant/sorcery) as value engine.
- Key cards: Rootha the Inexorable (?), Heated Debate (3-mana 4 damage, can't be countered — pays through ward), Prismari Command, Torrent Sculptor (flip side makes fractals), Wormhole Serpent (makes creatures unblockable), Bio Mathematician (?), Multiple Choice.
- Kenji finds this archetype the most fun. Rootha is "a really fun one to play with."
- Note: if opponent plays Rootha/Teach by Example, the copy triggers even if the original spell is countered (unlike Rootha which needs the spell on stack).

### Quandrix (Blue-Green value/ramp)
- Gameplan: ramp with Field Trip and Environmental Sciences, make large fractal tokens, stabilize with Wormhole Serpent unblockable finisher.
- Key cards: Gnarled Professor (?) (4-drop that pitches a permanent for value), Overgrown Arch (?) (early blocker that gains life and can later learn), Bio Mathematician (?) (grows with XX counters), Eureka Moment (?), Decisive Denial (counterspell/fight hybrid).
- Hall of Oracles (tap to put a +1/+1 counter on a creature, sorcery speed): "that Hall of Oracles I remember was being really really good."

### Witherbloom (Black-Green life gain/pests)
- Gameplan: gain life to trigger Blood Researcher (?) (2/2 menace, gets +1/+1 counter on life gain), use pests as fodder, Witherbloom Apprentice (1/1, each opponent loses 1 life when you cast instant/sorcery).
- Key cards: Overgrown Arch, Blood Researcher (?), Witherbloom Apprentice, Brackish Trudge (?), Hunt for Specimens (learn + make 1/1 pest), Cram Session (gain 4 life + learn = "Time Walk" per Kenji).
- Kenji faced a Witherbloom deck that went "14/14 for one mana" using a pump spell whose X equals life gained that turn — the combo of Witherbloom Apprentice + mass life gain can create one-hit kills.

### Five-Color Control ("soup")
- Gameplan: get a full set of campuses + Letters of Acceptance for fixing; use Environmental Sciences to ramp/find basics; load up on learn cards; grab lessons as needed; win with Mascot Exhibition (bomb lesson), Wormhole Serpent, big fractals, or Mizzix's Mastery (?) off archive.
- "Where's the fun in just building a three-color deck or a two-color deck when instead you could be five colors."
- Cram Session is the lynchpin: "in like the five-color control decks, Cram Session is literally Time Walk plus."

## Card interactions & combos

- **Cram Session + any win condition**: Gain 4 life AND draw a card (via learn) for 4 mana. Kenji calls this "literally Time Walk plus" and a "Time Walk" repeatedly. The life gain is real tempo and the tutor effect is free.
- **Hunt for Specimens + Mascot Exhibition**: Hunt makes a 1/1 pest and learns. If Mascot Exhibition is in your sideboard (it should be), you grab it — a 6-mana sorcery lesson that makes a 2/1, 3/2, and 4/4 — and suddenly have a battlefield full of tokens.
- **Rootha + Environmental Sciences**: Bounce Rootha to hand after casting Sciences; cast Rootha again next turn to copy another spell. Rootha copying Mascot Exhibition or a big draw spell is devastating.
- **Dramatic Finale + Eye Twitch (?) + token makers**: Whenever a non-token creature of yours dies, make a 2/1 token. Eye Twitch is a 1/1 flyer that learns when it dies — triggers Finale for a free 2/1 while also giving you a lesson. Kenji specifically highlights this.
- **Heated Debate bypasses ward**: "you have heated debate that burn spell... you do not have to pay the ward... because it can't be countered means you don't need to pay the ward right." Important ruling — use Heated Debate to kill warded creatures for free.
- **Witherbloom Apprentice + life gain pump** (e.g., Harness Infinity [?]): triggers double drain AND a giant pump. Can create lethal combos out of nowhere. Opponent went "14/14 for one mana."

## Card tips

### White (W)
- **Professor of Symbology** (?): one of the better two-drops in the format — 2-mana 2/1 that learns on ETB.

### Blue (U)
- **Bury in Books** (?): "Bury" — bounce effect, key tempo play in blue decks.

### Black (B)
- **Necrotic Fumes**: Strong lesson removal (exile target creature, they sacrifice a creature). One of the better lessons to have in sideboard.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Cram Session**: Rate it much higher than others — in aggressive/learn-heavy decks it's good, in five-color it's game-winning. "I take cram session a lot higher than the majority of people."
- **Inkling Summoning**: 3-mana lesson, makes a 2/1 flying inklings. Strong for aggressive lesson packages.
- **Vanishing Verse**: Exile target mono-colored permanent — "pretty sweet" mystical archive card, one of the better removal spells.
- **Lorehold / other campus lands**: Worth running even off-color as late-game mana sinks / scry engines, especially in slower decks. "Not for fixing but just as an additional land that can scry later on."
- **Torrent Sculptor** (?): Can be cast as a big creature OR used as a rummage + removal spell. Double-duty makes it consistently playable.

### Colorless / artifacts
- **Environmental Sciences**: Best lesson in the format. "The number one lesson" — 2-mana colorless, gain 2 life and tutor any basic land. Makes five-color possible and provides fixing whenever you learn.
- **Mascot Exhibition**: "The number one lesson, might even be the best card in the format." Lesson that makes a 2/1, 3/2, and 4/4 simultaneously. Goes in every deck that learns.
- **Introduction to Annihilation**: "if you're able to quote unquote tutor for it with a learned card well then it gets a lot better" — maindecking it is bad but as a lesson it's live removal.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Campus lands** (all five schools): "campuses are insane in this format though." They all ETB tapped, tap for two colors, and activate to scry 1 (? — "Silverquill that's the black white and they all enter tap they all add the two colors of that school and they also have the ability to describe one"). Late-game filtering is extremely valuable.
- **Prioritize learn cards over lessons**: "it's more important to get the learn cards than it is to get the lessons right because if you end up with Five Lessons but then only one way to learn well then you're only going to be able to grab one of them... if you have like five learn cards and then one lesson you can always seemingly grab that lesson." Asymmetry is important — lessons are useless without ways to fetch them.
- **Learn as rummage safety valve**: "with learn if you don't have a card to go grab from your sideboard you can instead choose to rummage so discard a card that's already in your hand and then grab a new one so it's never truly a dead card."

## Pitfalls

- **Too many lessons, not enough learn cards**: The most common draft mistake Kenji identifies. Loading up on lessons when you only have 1–2 learn cards means you almost never draw into them. Draft learn cards early; add lessons as sideboard insurance.
- **Passing Cram Session**: Kenji regrets it every time. "I'm so sad. I'm mad... Where are my crams?"
- **Building Silverquill without creatures**: "Controlly black-white is I don't think where you want to be." The archetype needs cheap creatures to fill the curve — removal + tricks without bodies is not a plan.
- **Lorehold (Red-White) aggro**: "Lorehold generally wants to be more controlly in this format... aggro lorehold is not often good." The red-white aggressive build underperforms; go for value.
- **Underestimating life total changes**: In a slow format, "13 versus 12 is a big difference here" — small life differences become critical in the late turns when you're trying to stabilize. Don't let opponents gain small increments for free.
- **Ignoring the auto-tapper**: "Oh god, the auto-tapper... it always wants to leave your campuses." Manually tap mana to preserve campus activations for end-of-turn scrying.
- **Quintorius** (?): "Quintorius is a trick. He lures you. And then you're like, 'Okay, I believe you, Quintorius.' And then guess what Quintorius does? Makes no tokens." — underpowered; don't be seduced by the mythic.

# EOE — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?). Set: Edge of Eternities (sci-fi/space set on MTG Arena).

## Format speed / meta read
- EOE is a midrange-to-battlecruiser format dominated by Red-Black artifact/sacrifice decks and Green-based Kavu/Lander ramp. Kenji spent multiple drafts in Red-Black.
- **Lander tokens** (land-to-creature tokens from cards like Orbital Plunge, Safe (?), Land Seeker) are a core resource — they enable ramp, provide bodies, and trigger various payoffs. Not having enough lander production is a real draft failure mode.
- **Poison** is a side archetype (Virulent Silencer, Virulent Donsire, Parage Beckoner(?)), achievable but fragile — Kenji tried to win via poison multiple times and only succeeded once (barely) across several drafts.
- **Strip Mine** is in the format and described as "so dumb" — always run it when offered; very little downside and can simply win games by denying opponents colored mana.
- Red is again open/open-ish; Kenji repeatedly drifted to Red-Black.
- Format has enough interaction that "cheap curve + good removal" is the baseline winning strategy.

## Archetypes
- **Red-Black Artifacts/Sacrifice** (dominant archetype Kenji drafts): Built around cheap artifacts (Dubious Delicacy, Grab Kill, Depressurize, Virus Beetle, Perigee Beckoner (?), Intimidator), sacrifice outlets (Scrapper, Umbral Collar Zealot, Swarm Color (?), Embrace Oblivion), and payoffs. Tesarette Cruel Captain (only planeswalker in format) grabs 1-mana artifacts and eventually ultimates to put 3+1/+1 counters on each artifact. Turbo Drone as a cheap recursive draw engine. Father's Faithful as a 1-mana sac artifact. Hull Carver for ramp/fixing. Lava Claw Reaches (BoP land?) always runs itself. Strip Mine nearly mandatory.
  - Kenji's ideal makeup: 9+ artifacts, 5+ sacrifice outlets (at least 3 repeatable), cheap interaction, and 1–2 copies of payoff enchantments.
  - **Weapons Manufacturing** (rare): The "fun" build-around: when you sacrifice an artifact, make a Munitions token; you can then sacrifice Munitions tokens for effects. Requires scrapper (repeatable sacrifice of artifacts) + vault (sacrifice artifacts for draw) + Umbral Collar Zealot (free repeatable sacrifice) + enough cheap artifacts. Kenji attempted it across 4 consecutive drafts with mixed results (mostly 2-3 or 3-4), largely because support cards didn't flow.
- **Green-X Kavu/Lander Ramp**: Seed Ship Impact (?) as premium removal/ramp; Tanic (?) better with double lander triggers; Ice Till Explorer (mills lands into graveyard to cast from there?); Biosynthic Burst (instant pump/fight); Tapestry Warden (2-drop); Blooming Stinger (2-drop). Godma (?) as finisher — "like if they have a reverse Godma, we die." Fungal Colossi scale with basic land types; good with 3 shock lands. Sacred Foundry / Stomping Ground / Breeding Pool (shock lands in format) fix mana and turn on payoffs.
- **Blue-X Weft-Walking Control**: Weft Walking (?) — "very powerful but you need to build around it; take cheap interaction and dump your hand quickly so Weft Walking refuels." Kenji took it once and pivoted out of blue.
- **White Aggro / Ardent**: Banishing Light as premium removal; Tapestry Warden + Rayblade Trooper as early bodies. Kenji drafted into it incidentally but didn't commit.
- **Poison (Grixis/Black-X)**: Virulent Silencer (1/1 infect? that pumps on attack), Virulent Donsire (?), Parage Beckoner (? — 2-drop that provides discard pressure), Perigee Beckoner (? — 3-drop with powerful effect), Hull Carver. Needs critical mass of poison enablers; Kenji describes it as a "fun" build that usually underdelivers.

## Card interactions & combos
- **Weapons Manufacturing + Scrapper**: Scrapper sacrifices artifacts (repeatable); Manufacturing creates Munitions tokens when you do. Scrapper also draws a card per sacrifice. The two together "do both things" — generate tokens and draw cards. Best duo for the archetype.
- **Weapons Manufacturing + Memorial Vault**: Vault sacrifices artifacts for draw. With Manufacturing in play, each vault activation = Munitions token + card draw. "Turns any munitions tokens into 2 damage + draw a card."
- **Tesarette + Scrapbot / 1-mana artifacts**: Tesarette's -3 grabs a 1-mana artifact; once you play that artifact out, Tesarette regains a loyalty counter. "In a sense the tutor for a 1-drop is like a minus-2 if you think about it." Use repeatedly to generate bodies and stay at high loyalty toward the ultimate.
- **Perigee Beckoner + Warp effect + Virus Beetle**: Hard-cast or warp Perigee to make opponent discard; Virus Beetle also forces a discard. Stacking both in the same turn leaves opponent hellbent and draws 3 total cards for Kenji's team.
- **Parage Beckoner + Scrapper**: Sac Beckoner to Scrapper after getting the discard trigger; then use Mechan / Hull Carver to get Beckoner back next turn. Repeated "discard + draw" loop described as "absolutely disgusting."
- **Dubious Delicacy + any creature sac**: Delicacy kills a creature and gives the opponent cards. When you have 2+ copies, they often become the game-ending "6 points of damage just sitting on the battlefield" after racing.
- **Strip Mine + cheap aggro curve**: Turn one Strip Mine on the play sets opponent back a land; combined with a 1-drop + 2-drop, this is often game-ending early. "Turn one Strip Mine is so dumb." Kenji doesn't know if it should be in the format.
- **Ice Till Explorer + land-heavy graveyard**: Allows casting lands from graveyard; helps recover from flood and generate virtual card advantage late.

## Card tips

### Blue (U)
- **Weft Walking**: Powerful card-draw sorcery that works best when you can empty your hand first. Draft cheap spells to enable it; don't take it and then draft expensive cards.

### Black (B)
- **Dubious Delicacy**: Described multiple times as "too good" to pass. 2-mana removal that gives opponent 2 cards is still worth it in a format where tempo matters. Run 2+ copies whenever available.
- **Perigee Beckoner**: "Whenever you're playing black, get as many of these as you can." Consistent value engine.
- **Dubious Delicacy + Father's Faithful (1-mana artifact)**: Faithful is a key 1-mana artifact for Tesarette tutoring; the combination of cheap artifacts makes the deck function.
- **Virus Beetle (1/1 infect that forces discard when it deals damage?)**: Makes opponents discard key cards; good in poison builds and as hand disruption in Red-Black. "Beetle was huge" / "beetle might have just saved us."

### Red (R)
- **Orbital Plunge**: First-rank removal; also creates a Lander token (land-based creature). Take early; key payoff enabler for lander synergies.
- **Grab Kill**: Cheap interactive kill spell; run as many as you can.
- **Scrapper**: Best single card for the Manufacturing deck. Repeatable sacrifice outlet + draws a card per activation. "Might be the best because it does both things."
- **Lava Claw Reaches** (BoP-style dual land?): "A pretty looking land and I don't lose much." Always run it.

### Green (G)
- **Biosynthic Burst**: Good combat trick / fight effect; hold up vs. big threats rather than using offensively into removal.

### Multicolor / gold
- **Interceptor Mecha**: "Nothing in this pack is even remotely close to it" — described as format's best uncommon/rare on its power level.

### Unmatched / caption-garbled
- **Strip Mine**: "There is very little downside and usually just upside to running Strip Mine." Rarely cut it.
- **Godma / Reverse Godma** (?): Named repeatedly as the scary finisher; being on the wrong end of it is almost always fatal. Respect it as a high-priority removal target.
- **Tanic** (?): "Great" when you have double Lander triggers (hitting the ability twice). Much weaker without lander production.

## Pitfalls
- **Weapons Manufacturing without enough sacrifice outlets**: The biggest failure mode. You need 4–5 sacrifice outlets, at least 3 of them repeatable (Scrapper, Umbral Collar Zealot, Swarm Color). If you can't assemble that, don't run the card. Kenji ran it anyway multiple times for fun and went 2-3 repeatedly.
- **Not enough cheap artifacts for the sacrifice engine**: "Fine line between getting enough sacrifice effects and enough artifacts." Draft cheap artifacts (1–2 mana) early when committing to the archetype, not just synergy cards.
- **Mana base imbalance**: Several losses attributed to drawing all one color's sources (9 swamps / 1 mountain). Kenji kept saying "I forgot to swap a swamp for a mountain." When in Red-Black, verify the split before submitting (8/8 is a starting point with a splash land).
- **Lander production insufficient**: Without lander tokens, Tanic's double trigger doesn't work, and Godma-level finishers arrive too slowly. Prioritize lander-making cards (Orbital Plunge, Safe, Ice Till Explorer) appropriately.
- **Playing around vs. going for it**: Kenji notes several game-throws from playing too passively when holding 6+ damage lethal on board. "They were actively trying to lose and we still gave them time."
- **Three-bolt over-removal, thin creatures**: In one deck Kenji had 3 Plasma Bolts and underperformed — eventually cut one for a top-end threat (Kill Ship). Don't over-index on removal; 4–5 pieces is usually enough.
- **Running Weapons Manufacturing in a deck that assembled the tokens but never sacrificed them**: "We made 10 tokens and never sacrificed one" across two drafts. If you run Manufacturing, you must have the sacrifice outlets online; otherwise you're just making 2/2s.

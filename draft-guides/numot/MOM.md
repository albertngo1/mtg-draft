# MOM — Numot draft notes
> Source: NumotTheNummy (Kenji Egashira) regular Arena draft VODs — sampled 4 end-of-format VODs (tier-2 set, thinner coverage). Built 2026-06-10 from YouTube auto-caption transcripts; card/mechanic names corrected where confident, garbled readings marked (?).

## Format speed / meta read

- **Medium-to-slow format.** Control and value-heavy decks thrive; Kenji built blue-black mill/control in two VODs and black-white sacrifice/value in the other two. Games routinely went long — Kenji jokes one draft would be "a two-hour draft" with his control deck.
- **Battles (Invasions) warp the format.** The best uncommons and commons are often Invasions; flipping them mid-combat or via removal triggers represents enormous tempo and card-advantage swings. Knowing how to flip them efficiently is a core skill.
- **Mill is a real win condition.** The blue-black archetype can legitimately deck opponents; Oracle of Tragedy and Halo-Charged Scab contribute to a recursive mill plan. Kenji won one game purely by decking the opponent with triple Invasion of Amonkhet.
- **White-blue convoke is a fast, aggressive counterpoint.** The format has a strong convoke token deck; Kenji lost games to it when he lacked interaction. Speed varies significantly by archetype.
- **41-card decks are occasionally correct.** When running a mill/attrition deck, running 41 cards can prevent self-milling out. "Turns out having that 41st card saved my bacon — if I had 40, they would have milled me exactly."

## Archetypes

### Blue-Black — Mill / Control
- **Gameplan:** Stabilize with cheap blockers, mill both players with Invasion of Amonkhet and Halo-Charged Scab, grind card advantage through Timaret (Timaret, She Who Grasps; a black 4/5 that eats graveyards and gains life?), and win by decking the opponent or assembling a huge finisher (Hoarding Broodlord via convoke, or copying a finisher via Invasion of Amonkhet's flip).
- **Key cards:** Invasion of Amonkhet ("if you ever get to play MOM draft, just know that Invasion of Amonkhet is very good" — mills 3, draws a card, discards an opponent card, flips into a 4/4 that copies any graveyard creature); Hoarding Broodlord (7/6 convoke flyer that tutors a card to exile, then lets you cast it — "hard to pass up an 7/6 flyer"); Chrome Host Seedshark (?); Timaret; Halo Forager (3/1 flyer that recasts an instant/sorcery from a graveyard).
- **Fixing/splash:** Jungle Hollow, Thornwood Falls. Green splash for Eye of Gitaxias (?) (incubate 3, draw), Herbology Instructor, and Invasion of Zendikar is viable but risky; don't go green without 2+ dual lands.

### Black-White — Sacrifice / Counter Synergy
- **Gameplan:** Generate value through creatures dying via Lurrus (companion; recasts 2-mana-or-less permanents from graveyard), sacrifice synergies, and backup counters. Dusk Legion Duelist ("whenever one or more 1/1 counters are put on this creature, draw a card — that card was good") rewards the backup mechanic heavily.
- **Key cards:** Lurrus of the Dream-Den (companion; "Lurrus was fantastic"); Scornblade Berserker (sacrifice for a card draw + pumps others — "one of the best cheap creatures you can have for a Lurrus companion"); Furnace Reigns (steals a creature, makes a Treasure — "Furnace Reigns, fully online"); Juri, Master of the Revue (?; grows on sacrifice); Timaret; Final Flourish; Deadly Derision (destroys + treasure = mana fixing for splashes); Invasion of Tolvada ("invasion of Tolvada just great — good curve, good interaction, that's all you need"; flips into engine generating 2/1 flying lifelink tokens each turn).
- **Backup synergy:** Backup puts a +1/+1 counter AND grants the creature's triggered ability "until end of turn." Kenji exploits this constantly: back up Dusk Legion Duelist to draw extra cards, back up onto cheap creatures to enable sac triggers.

### Red-Blue — Lutri Companion Tempo (MOM Aftermath draft)
- **Gameplan:** Singleton-ish deck with Lutri as companion; use cheap two-drop creatures (Captive Weird, Dredg Recycler?, Omen Hawker) and a few Invasions to curve out and apply pressure.
- **Noted:** Requires very heavy commitment to two-mana-or-less permanents. Kenji hard-forced it "just to try something fun" at end of format.

## Card interactions & combos

- **Invasion of Amonkhet + Hoarding Broodlord:** Amonkhet mills and puts a face-down card into exile; Broodlord's convoke clause means that exiled card can later be cast for cheaper. Also, Amonkhet's flip copies any creature in the graveyard — copying a Hoarding Broodlord produces another Broodlord that tutors yet another card.
- **Lurrus + Furnace Reigns:** After Furnace Reigns (costs 2) goes to the graveyard or is used, Lurrus recasts it from the graveyard each turn to steal a new creature. Kenji describes this loop as "Furnace Reigns fully online" and a near-lock on games.
- **Scornblade Berserker + Juri:** Berserker sacrifices itself or another creature to draw a card; each sacrifice trigger also grows Juri. "I'm putting this on the Juri because of Juri's ability" — equipping a Beatstick (equipment that grants unblockable?) plus sacrifice chains built Juri to a 6-power threat.
- **Dusk Legion Duelist + Backup:** Any backup card that targets the Duelist mid-combat gives the Duelist its counter-draw trigger until end of turn. Kenji stacks this with Scornblade Berserker and Juri on the same turn.
- **Invasion of Tolvada flip + land cyclers / Fleeting Gorilla (?):** Once Tolvada is flipped (generates 2/1 flying lifelink tokens each turn), land cyclers become immediate bodies that fuel the engine. "These land cyclers get a lot better with Invasion of Tolvada."
- **Timaret + graveyard decks:** Playing Timaret against blue-black mirror means exiling cards from your graveyard before the opponent can Breach the Multiverse them. "Remember, there's merit in exiling cards from my graveyard since they're blue-black."
- **Unseal the Necropolis + Halo Forager:** Unseal puts a card on top of the library; Halo Forager (3/1 flyer, pay X to cast instant/sorcery with mana value X from a graveyard) recycles key spells. Kenji specifically called out "Halo Forager + Unsealed Necropolis" as a combo he wanted to execute.
- **Chrome Host Seedshark (?) in control decks:** "Even as a three-mana 2/4 flyer it would be solid enough — this card was sick." Generates insect tokens whenever you cast noncreature spells; in a removal-heavy deck, creates a wide board quickly.

## Card tips

- **Invasion of Amonkhet is the best uncommon in the format.** Take every copy. Provides hand disruption, card draw, mills both players for setup, and flips into a 4/4 that copies any creature in a graveyard — including your own Hoarding Broodlord or opponent's best creature. "Triple Invasion of Amonkhet — can't complain about that."
- **Invasion of Tolvada (five mana; returns a non-battle permanent, flips into token generator) is the best black-white Invasion.** Flipping it means every end step you get a 2/1 flying lifelink. "Flipped Invasion of Tolvada is just so gross — the broken sky."
- **Preening Champion (3-mana 2/2 flyer that makes a 1/1 knight) is the best common in the format** (Kenji calls it "the common of the format"). Take early.
- **Render Inert** (pay mana to remove counters from a battle, flipping it immediately) is a critical enabler for the Battle plan. "Would've been a nice way to insta-flip our invasions." Take it earlier than feels right; it wheels less often than you'd think.
- **Phyrexian Gargantua** (six mana 4/4 that draws two cards on ETB; "Phyrexian Gargantua is amazing") — a must-pick finisher in black decks. Wheels occasionally as a 13th pick; always play it.
- **Glissa, Herald of Predation (5/5 at beginning of combat that incubates 2 twice, then gives incubators first strike + death touch) is one of the biggest bombs** — "that card was absurd." Prioritize early.
- **Talarian Contempt (?)** (enchantment that puts rejection counters on opponent's creatures, then tucks one per turn) — "that card was so brutal, so disgusting." Very high pick in blue-black.
- **Eyes of Gitaxias (3 mana, incubate 3, draw a card) is solid** value; Kenji played multiple copies.
- **Deadly Derision** (destroy creature or planeswalker + make a treasure): "Deadly Derision was good, right?" Also provides fixing for color splashes via the treasure.
- **18 lands is correct in slower control builds.** Kenji repeatedly ran 18 or 41 cards. "I think 18 lands might even be right in this deck."
- **Convoke cards let you cast expensive spells earlier.** Count your creatures before building mana base; "hard to pass up an 7/6 flyer, especially when it has convoke."
- **Ethersworn Adjudicator (?) / Inga Rune-Eyes (aftermath card)** are solid late picks; Inga especially wheels in MOM aftermath drafts.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-attacking when stabilization is the priority.** Kenji explicitly self-identified a punt: "I got too aggressive — that one attack with the Halo Forager was five extra damage I took. I needed to stabilize, not push damage. That's on me." When you have a must-protect threat online, stop attacking with your other creatures.
- **Not exiling your own graveyard in the blue-black mirror.** Against Breach the Multiverse decks, Timaret eating your own graveyard is correct. "There's merit in exiling cards from my graveyard since they're blue-black."
- **Casting Invasion of Amonkhet before you can attack with enough power to flip it.** Invasions need to take damage; plan your attacks around flipping them, not around raw damage racing.
- **Running too few removal spells in the mill deck.** "What's my removal actually? I guess I don't have that much removal." When milling is your plan, you still need removal — 3–4 hard answers minimum.
- **Splashing a third color without 2+ dual lands.** The green splash died every time Kenji didn't pull Thornwood Falls and Jungle Hollow. "If I don't get more fixing, I will not play the green."
- **Trimming the Drinker (Ichor Drinker, 1-mana lifelink that exiles for incubate) too aggressively.** It enables Lurrus, enables sac outlets, and applies chip damage. "Maybe I should have that in the deck."
- **Letting the opponent flip Invasions for free.** Always consider whether blocking a creature attacking your battle costs you more than flipping the battle costs them.

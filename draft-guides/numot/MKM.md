## Format speed / meta read

- **Best-of-one is an aggro lottery; Boros is the single highest win-rate pair.** Kenji: "Boros has by far and away the highest win rate out of all the color pairings." At least ~50% of bo1 opponents are Boros aggro. He returns to Boros repeatedly and keeps winning (confirmed end-of-format — repeatedly LOSES to Boros in the final VODs, reinforcing its dominance, 03-11). By his own count the format "devolved into aggro aggro aggro" after the first day or two.
- **The format mantra: be on the play, curve out, don't punt — "it really is that easy in best of one magic."** Play/draw is enormous: "You need to be winning die rolls if you want to win games in this format." In aggro mirrors the die roll is frequently the whole game. (Late confirmation: "very much a get on the board format," games end turns 4–5, 03-14.)
- **White is one of the best — possibly the best — color in the format** (Kenji says this explicitly early, 02-11). Black is the weakest/fussiest; when black cards (even bombs like Vein Ripper) go late it's usually because nobody can splash them, not because they're bad. (⚠ The "white is THE best" line is an early-only prediction; by end-of-format the settled read is that **Boros the pair** is best and that green AND black are consistently under-drafted and flow late — see below.)
- **Green and black are consistently UNDER-drafted and flow late** (settled late signal-read, NEW vs early notes): "green is generally under drafted. Same thing with black" (03-14); "green was really open this draft kind of wish I had been in Green" (03-23). If you read an open lane, green/black reward the move late.
- **Too many rares/mythics — games are routinely decided by a single bomb.** "A lot of the times the games are just determined by one [rare]. This format in particular has access to so many rares and mythics." A tight all-common/uncommon deck can still go 0-3 into rare-heavy opponents. Standing lesson after an 0-3: "Draft more rares and mythics when you can."
- **Ward-2 disguise warps removal evaluation. Combat tricks beat removal more often than usual.** "A lot of removal in this format is actually worse than it first appears because of all the ward two — Shock frequently is a three-mana kill on a three-drop." Kenji: "combat tricks are just better than a lot of removal often times."
- **Shock > Murder in many spots** — kills a face-down disguise 2/2 for 1 mana, where Murder costs 3 against the same face-down creature (and 5 of raw mana value once you count ward). "A lot of the times you would rather have a shock in this format than the murder."
- **Good two-drops are scarce; four-drops are everywhere.** Kenji aggressively prioritizes two-drop creatures throughout every draft, taking them over removal when the curve needs filling. Decks routinely glut on three-drops ("mono threes"). (Confirmed end-of-format: even a bad two-drop is worth maindecking — "even if like Innocent Bystander isn't a good magic card... just having something to do on turn two makes it worthwhile," 03-11.)
- **The deckbuilding wheel is largely dead (Play Booster structure).** Premium cards (Case of the Gateway Express, Gadget Technician) do not wheel — take needed pieces in the first 2–3 picks; don't count on getting them back. Seeing multiples of one uncommon late is a strong open-lane signal precisely because good cards shouldn't wheel.
- **Best-of-three is "the real magic."** Kenji switches to bo3 to escape the bo1 aggro lottery; hand-smoothing and sideboarding meaningfully change the format and pull it back toward true card-power. "Best of one really really skews it in favor of the fast decks."
- **Wraths exist but are all rare/mythic** — Ill-Timed Explosion (UR), Leave No Witnesses (white), Deadly Cover-Up (BB), the 6/6 wrath-dragon. Aggro decks don't need to fear wraths unless the opponent opened one; Clue card-advantage helps everyone recover, so generally just commit your board.
- **Kenji's overall enjoyment grade is low (~3/10, settled end-of-format 03-23/03-24).** Started as optimistic preview hype (01-30 — ⚠ superseded), soured within days, and settled around 3/10 by format end ("below average... maybe even closer to a three," "it's been wearing me down"). Driven by the bo1 aggro skew and bomb-decided games.

## Archetypes

Ranked by the strength evidence in the notes (Kenji's win rates + how emphatically he rates each).

### Boros (RW) — Aggro — STRONGEST / #1
- **Plan:** Curve out hard, two-drop → three-drop → removal/trick, win before the opponent stabilizes. Wins trophies on commons alone — no bomb required. "The rules of On the Job: curve out, cast On the Job, win." (Confirmed best pair end-of-format: "one of the best color pairings, if not the best... which is Boros," "Aggro is obviously insane," 03-11.)
- **Core curve:** Novice Inspector (1), Market Watch Phantom / Sanitation Automaton / Reckless Detective (2), disguise body / Inside Source / Seasoned Consultant (3), Haazda Vigilante / On the Job (4–5).
- **Key cards:** Novice Inspector ("basically the best one-drop in the format"), Shock, Dog Walker (**one of the best commons — confirmed end-of-format 03-23, "absolutely busted" 11th pick**), Perimeter Enforcer, On the Job (go-wide Overrun that also draws a Clue), Haazda Vigilante ("one of the best top-end cards in the Boros beats decks"), Gorgon's Kiss, Case of the Gateway Express, Sanguine Savior (disguise lifelink flyer closer), Auspicious Arrival, Convenient Target, Fugitive Codebreaker.
- **Strength:** Highest win rate by a wide margin in bo1. Hard to beat from behind. **Land count: 16 with an ultra-low curve, 17 default** (17/~15 creatures confirmed end-of-format 03-11). Target ~15 creatures minimum.
- **Note:** Aggressive enough that combat tricks (cheap two-mana ones) are often better than removal here.
- **Green splash (late insight):** worth splashing green payoffs (Tolsimir-type) in Boros when surveil duals show up — "not a free splash, but worthwhile" (03-23). Single-pip splash wants ~4 sources; don't warp the deck.

### Azorius (WU) — Detectives / Tempo — STRONG / #2
- **Plan:** Low-curve detective tempo. Detectives pump each other (Private Eye), cheap evasion + cheap interaction (Out Cold, Lost in the Maze), close with flyers/go-wide. Doesn't need bombs to function. "We don't have a bomb but we have just so much good synergy, good curve — I'm suspecting this deck is going to average four or five wins."
- **Curve:** Novice Inspector (1) → Market Watch Phantom / Exit Specialist (2) → Private Eye (3) → Granite Witness / flip-up value (4).
- **Key cards:** Private Eye (3/3 that anthems your other detectives — **two of them is game-winning**), Perimeter Enforcer (1/1 flying lifelink, grows with detectives — "such a good two-drop"), Granite Witness (3/4 draws on flip; wheels = WU open), Seasoned Consultant (2-drop detective, 3 toughness), Out Cold, Lost in the Maze ("fantastic in this format" — taps + grants hexproof; caveat end-of-format: disguise/cloak creatures resist it — "the disguisers are so good versus Lost in the Maze," 03-14), Thinking Cap / Investigator's Journal (?) (wants ~5–6 detectives, equip for 1), Exit Specialist, Assemble the Players, Killer Among Us, Faith Flight (protection for Private Eye), Furtive Courier.
- **Strength:** Very strong when open; premium rares (Aurelia, Cryptic Coat) push it over the top but aren't required. Usually 17 lands (18 if it tilts toward a slower value/control build — see Supersessions).
- **Weakness:** Often short on interaction — don't be shy about maindecking bounce/tricks. Can brick flooding on expensive Assemble top-decks.

### Gruul (RG) — Disguise Aggro / Combat Tricks — GOOD
- **Plan:** Curve out with cheap disguise creatures + many pump tricks + 1–2 trampling top-ends. "One of the better aggressive decks" thanks to "access to so many tricks," which makes it hard to play against.
- **Key cards:** Tin Street Gossip (Gruul signpost, 4/4 vigilance, adds R/G for face-down/flip costs), Tunnel Tipster ("premium two-drop"; counters up whenever an opponent fails to pay — "so good on turn two"), Incinerator of the Guilty (6/6 flying trample top-end, "flying plague wind" — see combos), Gadget Technician (best common disguise body), Bull-Rackle Clan Basher (3/2 double strike trample disguise — "ends a lot of games"), Get a Leg Up ("one of the best combat tricks — +5/+5 or greater"), Fanatical Strength (+3/+3 trample), Galvanize, World Soul's Rage, Breakout, Torch the Witness (scales 2/4/6 — **sorcery**, easy to forget), Topiary Panther (fixing + disguise body), Nervous Gardener, Offender at Large, Red Herring (only in dedicated aggro with pump).
- **Strength:** Can 7-1 in bo1 on curve-outs. Highly linear. Panther + Public Thoroughfare + Nervous Gardener gives enough fixing for a W or B splash.
- **Weakness:** **Weak in bo3** — limited interaction, opponent adjusts. Games "aren't very interactive."

### Orzhov (WB) — Vampires / Curve — STRONG when curving
- **Plan:** Aggressive curve (2→3→3→4), vampires drain as they enter/flip, Case of the Gateway Express closes by making all your creatures 2+ power. "Curve out two-drop three-drop three-drop four-drop — that is nice."
- **Key cards:** Teysa Karlov (first-pickable; "very very hard to beat once it gets going"), Wisp-Drinker Vampire (drain when a power-2-or-less creature enters; play disguise to drain 1, flip to drain more), Sanguine Savior (WW flipper, very good WB uncommon), Dog Walker (face-down drain 1 + flip drain 2 = 3 drain for 3 mana; **one of the best commons — confirmed end-of-format**), Inside Source (3-mana 2/2 token + pumps detectives; drain 2), Case of the Gateway Express ("so good"), Gore Hound (aggressive 2-drop w/ surveil), Seasoned Consultant, Dual Leech, Coerce to Kill, Murder, Push // Pull, Granite Witness.
- **Weakness:** Glutted on three-drops — desperately needs two-drops (Kenji took Sanitation Automaton over Murder to hit curve). Multiple anti-synergies (below).
- **Note:** **Do NOT run Thrull Parasite** — it turns off your own vampires' power-2-or-less drain triggers. Same caution for Case of the Gateway Express (anti-synergy, see combos). Don't run wraths (Deadly Cover-Up was "better as a land"/"effectively a mulligan" in aggro).

### Dimir (UB) — Control / Value — VIABLE with bombs, below-average without
- **Plan:** Curve out with cheap disguise creatures + removal, let the graveyard fill naturally for Collect Evidence, win the lategame on card advantage and life drain. **The single most powerful deck Kenji drafted all format** when stacked with Vein Ripper + Cryptic Coat + Outrageous Robbery — "the best deck I've ever drafted in this format." (⚠ But the settled end-of-format read is that UB the pair "is often not considered a very good color pairing," playable only with bombs, 03-24 — the ceiling is high, the floor is low.)
- **Key cards:** Vein Ripper (mythic bomb), Cryptic Coat (rare), Extract a Confession ("black's best common, better than Murder"), Long Goodbye ("black's best uncommon"; kills MV≤3, awkward vs flipped MV-4+), Out Cold (want multiples — "fantastic"/"amazing" tempo removal, confirmed end-of-format), Jaded Analyst (1/3 fantastic blocker + looter), Drag the Canal (instant 2-mana 2/2 detective with upside), Crimestopper Sprite (counts as an 8-mana spell in yard for Collect Evidence — ⚠ **downgraded end-of-format 03-18, "come down a bit on Sprite"; take Forensic Researcher over it**), Forensic Researcher (⚠ **UP end-of-format — take over Sprite; doubles as ramp**), Outrageous Robbery, Push // Pull.
- **Strength:** When black is open ("fifth pick Vein Ripper, seventh pick Long Goodbye — this is what happens when nobody wants to play black") the lategame value package is overwhelming. Black is consistently under-drafted and flows late (settled signal-read).
- **Weakness:** Without bombs, the weakest pair — pure control is hard because everyone has Clue card-advantage. Run more blue than black is fine; supply enough double-black if you have Vein Ripper. **Land count: 18 in the slower value/control builds** (late refinement; aggro stays 17).

### Izzet (UR) — Artifacts / Clues / Thopters — HIGH CEILING, FRAGILE
- **Plan:** Flood the board with cheap artifact-makers (Gadget Technician, Drone Smith, Gear/Gleaming Geardrake, Mistway Spy), generate clues, cash artifacts with Crime Novelist, sacrifice-value engines, top with Out Cold tap-downs. Case of the Filched Falcon / Connecting the Dots as rare payoffs.
- **Key cards:** Detective's Satchel (double/triple it — "builds up and gets you a lot of card advantage if you can stay alive"), Gadget Technician (flip for double-red → 1/1 thopter), Drone Smith (thopter at start of combat — great w/ double Satchel), Crime Novelist (sac artifacts for mana/buffs — needs clue/artifact density), Reckless Detective ("one of the better two-drops"; 3 toughness, sac clues for free draw on attack), Lamplight Phoenix (recurring 3/3 flyer), Case of the Filched Falcon (4/4 flyer; needs 8+ artifacts), Connecting the Dots (rare draw engine), Concealed Weapon ("disgusting" on Satchel tokens), Fuss // Bother, Cornered Crook, Out Cold, Cold Case Cracker, Fugitive Codebreaker, Izzet Thopterist ("one of the best uncommons in the format" — late read 03-xx).
- **Strength:** Won a 3-0 ("deck was great") when Satchels + Technicians + Drone Smiths aligned.
- **Weakness:** High-variance, needs specific pieces, vulnerable to board wipes (Ill-Timed Explosion). Went 0-3 in a bo3 run with only one rare (Connecting the Dots) into all-bomb opponents — "our deck is good but our opponents' decks are simply better."

### Golgari / Sultai (BG/x) — Insidious Roots / Graveyard Value — SITUATIONAL
- **Plan:** Fill the yard (surveil/self-mill/cycling), then repeatedly make creatures **leave** the yard (exile / collect-evidence) to trigger Insidious Roots (0/1 Plants + counters) and Chalk Outline (2/2 detective + investigate). Verify 7+ ways to trigger Roots/Outline before committing. (Late note: **Chalk Outline is the stronger, lower-bar payoff — "better than Insidious Roots,"** 03-xx. Green is under-drafted and flows late, so this lane is often open.)
- **Key cards:** Insidious Roots, Chalk Outline, Surveillance Monitor (collect evidence → 1/1 token; engine with Forensic Researcher's thopters), Gravestone Strider (exiles itself = clean Roots trigger + fixing — **"underrated all-star," fixing + GY removal, late read 03-xx**), Rubblebelt Maverick (surveil + self-exile — **premium one-drop for the green graveyard deck, over removal, late read**), Topiary Panther (cycles to yard for CE fuel AND is a plant that grows off Roots), Leering / Loitering Onlooker (2-drop, self-exiles when another creature ETBs), Snarling Gorehound ("surprisingly great" — surveil on attack), Nervous Gardener, Presume Dead (returns + self-exiles = Roots trigger), Glint-Weaver (7-mana 6/6 lifegain stabilizer — "once you resolve it you're generally winning"; **double copies deck-defining, late**), Sample Collector, Vitu-Ghazi Inspector (collect evidence 5), Vannifar, Evolved Enigma ("just way too good," one of the best cards in the format — locks you green, late 03-14).
- **Strength:** Big surprising upside when it pops off (double Roots + Chalk Outline + multiple triggers makes huge boards fast). Kenji 2-1 then 4-3 across builds.
- **Weakness:** Slow to set up; if behind when Roots lands you can't catch up. Needs MORE graveyard-fillers and MORE collect-evidence cards than feels natural. Not reliable enough for competition. **Creature floor ~13** (double Cadaver lets you shave, late). **18 lands** in the slower value builds.

### Rakdos (RB) — Removal Midrange — SERVICEABLE, not a dedicated archetype
- **Plan:** Cheap removal + disguise creatures + suspects. A midrange shell, not a synergy deck.
- **Key cards:** Push // Pull (premium — want **both** halves; Pull alone is weak), Murder, Slice from the Shadows, Person of Interest ("one of the best red commons" — confirmed end-of-format; excellent 4-drop, two bodies), Dog Walker (**one of the best commons — confirmed end-of-format**), Gadget Technician, Bull-Rackle Clan Basher, Rune-Brand Juggler (2/2 menace turn 2 + loot), Detective's Satchel, Case of the Crimson Pulse.
- **Weakness:** "The problem with red black is I don't remember it having too many ways to mass pump." Went 0-3 in one RB draft. Dog Walker on pick 11 once = colors very open (signal read — and end-of-format Kenji confirms 11th-pick Dog Walker is "absolutely busted," so it's both an open-lane signal AND a real pickup).

### Grixis — Greedy but buildable
- Lazav-centered UB control splashing red for Ill-Timed Explosion. Works only with strong fixing (Escape Tunnel, Public Thoroughfare, surveil duals). Not recommended, but functions.

## Card interactions & combos

1. **Incinerator of the Guilty + trample/connect** — 6/6 flying trample; on combat damage, collect evidence X to deal X to all their creatures AND planeswalkers ("flying plague wind"). "If you connect once with this card it is pretty hard to lose."
2. **Breakout + Incinerator** — Breakout (look at top 6; put a power-2-or-less creature in with haste, else draw it) can't *cheat in* Incinerator (power 6) but at worst draws it, making Breakout a live dig/tutor in a deck running the bomb.
3. **Assemble the Players + disguise creatures** — Assemble casts power-2-or-less creatures off the top; **disguise creatures qualify because face-down they have power 2.** Want ~10–12 qualifying creatures to make it worth it.
4. **Insidious Roots + Topiary Panther / Vengeful Creeper** — both are Plants, so they take +1/+1 counters when Roots triggers (instead of Roots just making a 0/1). "Topiary Panther is a plant and so is the Vengeful Creeper. That's really funny."
5. **Insidious Roots + opponent exiling your yard** — Roots triggers on a creature card leaving your graveyard *for any reason*, including the opponent's exile effects (e.g. ward-bypass exile removal). Their disruption helps you.
6. **Insidious Roots + Gravestone Strider / Surveillance Monitor** — Strider self-exiles; Monitor exiles to make tokens. With two copies of Roots in play, one such exile triggers both.
7. **Detective's Satchel + Drone Smith** — Drone Smith makes a thopter at start of combat with a sacrifice-at-end trigger. **Hold priority manually** to activate the Satchel (sac the token for a clue) before the auto-sac/turn-pass resolves, or Arena passes the value away.
8. **Crime Novelist + Drone Smith / Gadget Technician** — every thopter token leaving (or being made then sacrificed) gives a free Crime Novelist pump trigger. Double Drone Smith = two free triggers per combat.
9. **Case of the Filched Falcon + artifact-dense board** — with Technician/Drone Smith/Geardrake making artifacts, the case solves trivially into a 4/4 flyer. Wasted in decks that can't make the artifacts.
10. **Killer Among Us — offense-only** — the 4/4-attacker mode requires designating an *attacking* token, so the card is weak on defense. "If you're already ahead or the board state is even... the card is insane."
11. **Case of the Gateway Express + vampire drain (ANTI-SYNERGY)** — solving the Case makes all your creatures 2+ power, which turns off Wisp-Drinker / Sanguine Savior's "power 2 or less enters" drain. Accept the trade only when ahead.
12. **Cornered Crook + Gadget Technician / Red Herring** — Crook sacrifices artifacts; feed it Technician's thopter, or sac a Red Herring to "effectively turn the Herring into a bolt."
13. **Dog Walker + Tin Street Gossip** — Gossip's R/G (face-down/flip-only) mana helps pay Dog Walker's and the Technician's flip costs.
14. **Cryptic Coat + Bubble Smuggler** — cloak Bubble Smuggler off the Coat and the resulting creature keeps unblockable; the Coat re-cloaks if removed. "You just go off."
15. **Vein Ripper + creatures dying** — on cast, opponent sacs a creature or loses life; when it's blocked the blocker is sacrificed; and it drains on creature deaths. "Two creatures dying makes the Vein Ripper kill them." Casting it often just wins.
16. **Outrageous Robbery + opponent's graveyard** — exiles from *everything* already in their yard, not just what it mills. "If you're not already dead by the time you cast this, it is just so much card advantage." One or two hits is profitable.
17. **Flotsam // Jetsam ("the double Sams") (?) — graveyard theft** — each opponent mills three, then you cast a spell from *anything* in each opponent's graveyard for free (not just the milled cards). "I've been very very happy with playing that card." Good sideboard vs creature-heavy decks.
18. **Push // Pull + Crimestopper Sprite (Collect Evidence)** — Push // Pull counts as an 8-mana spell in the yard. "Push-Pull into Crime Stopper Sprite's fantastic because this counts as an eight-mana spell in the graveyard." (Note: Sprite itself is downgraded end-of-format — the combo is still real, but don't prioritize the Sprite for it.)
19. **Connecting the Dots + empty hand / Crimestopper Sprite** — rewards casting with no cards in hand: exile top N, cast free. Best line: end of opponent's turn tap a creature with Crimestopper Sprite (hold priority), then sacrifice Dots for the full draw. Kenji punts by not holding priority here.
20. **Curious Inquiry + Private Eye** — make a detective unblockable with Inquiry → connect → make a Clue → repeat next turn. Self-sustaining clue engine.
21. **Inside Source + Due Diligence** — Inside Source turn 3 makes two creatures, then Due Diligence gives both +2/+2 vigilance for a big attack.
22. **Fugitive Codebreaker + Galvanize** — Codebreaker has prowess and its disguise costs one less per instant/sorcery in your yard; casting Galvanize triggers prowess AND fills the yard to cheapen future Codebreaker flips.
23. **Thinking Cap + Mistway Spy** — equip turn 1, turn 2 it's a 2/3 flyer attacking. Equip-for-1 is the strong mode.
24. **Perimeter Enforcer + any detective pump** — 1/1 flying lifelink grows whenever a detective enters/flips; Convenient Target / Thinking Cap make it large fast. Swings the lifelink race hard.
25. **Forensic Researcher + Surveillance Monitor** — Researcher taps a creature and makes a 1/1 thopter; the thopters/clues feed Monitor's collect-evidence to make more thopters — a repeatable tap-down + token engine. (Researcher is UP end-of-format and also turns on a mana ability — "get the mana ability online," 03-14.)
26. **Lazav, Weaver of Faces + any graveyard** — on attack, exile a card from a graveyard then investigate (the Clue is unconditional); Lazav becomes a copy of the exiled card.
27. **Foreboding Familiar + surveil dual land** — bounce a surveil land with Familiar's trigger to "essentially draw a card" (re-surveil when you replay it).
28. **Caught Red-Handed (?) — Act of Treason caveat** — the stolen creature gains Menace (suspected), so you can't profitably block-trade it away afterward; still fine as tempo/removal.

## Pitfalls

- **Curve discipline over late-pick interaction.** Take the two-drop creature over the removal spell when the deck needs the curve. Two-drops are the scarcest resource; four-drops are everywhere; missing the curve is worse than a weak spell. (Confirmed end-of-format: even a bad two-drop is worth maindecking for a turn-two play, 03-11.)
- **Be on the play, curve out, don't punt** — and factor play/draw into how aggressively you keep hands. Don't evaluate aggro hands purely on cards.
- **Watchdog stops YOU flipping face-down creatures.** Kenji self-punts repeatedly: "I need to flip my Technician on my turn cuz the dog prevents me from flipping... I do that every single time even though I know the dog has text." Check Watchdog before attacking.
- **Hold priority for artifact/clue triggers.** Activate Detective's Satchel before a Drone Smith thopter auto-sacs; sac a Clue before its draw resolves to chain Connecting the Dots. Arena auto-pass/auto-tap silently throws away value.
- **Don't trust the auto-tapper.** Manually tap when you have flip effects, triggered abilities, or multi-use permanents — auto-tap has cost Kenji a flip line and a Lost in the Maze (tapped a Plains instead of leaving up the flip).
- **Float mana before enchantments/permanents die or lines require it.** Float in response to the opponent killing your Insidious Roots while you have creatures with relevant mana; "if I had floated second red I would have been able to detonate no matter what land I drew."
- **Sequence removal/tricks deliberately.** "I should have put the Dramatic on the token and then murdered the crowd control." Don't autopilot removal order; maximize tempo.
- **Fire instant removal in response to a face-down play** rather than waiting — Kenji punts by letting a disguise creature live and flip for big damage. "I should probably fire off the murder in response."
- **Don't over-commit into wraths — but they're all rare.** With Clue card advantage everywhere, usually just commit your board unless you have a specific read on a wrath.
- **Don't switch lanes without enough actual playables.** "We just don't have enough playables — one, two, three, four. No." Count cards that would make the deck before pivoting; once committed, "I've made my bed so I'm going to sleep in it" — staying in-lane gets rewarded in packs 2–3.
- **Read signals; commit late but commit.** Pack 1 signals matter most (you pass left again in pack 3). Kenji regrets passing an open red lane. Multiples of an uncommon late = lane open; take the uncommon over the common. (Settled late signal-read: **green and black are consistently under-drafted and flow late** — if you read the lane open, they reward the move, 03-14/03-23.)
- **Take questions over answers** when choosing between similar picks — proactive attackers beat reactive answers on tempo. (Applies when you already have *some* interaction.)
- **"Every piece of the buffalo."** Prefer cards that check multiple synergy boxes (surveil/mill + self-exile + creature + plant). Topiary Panther is the model.
- **Don't sandbag bombs.** "We don't want to bottom the Vein Ripper." Casting a game-winner immediately usually beats holding it for a "perfect" spot.
- **Value toughness for defensive two-drops** in this aggressive format — 1/3s and 0/3s (Reckless Detective, Jaded Analyst) block the format's 2/2s better than 2/2s do.
- **Land count:** **17 default for aggro Boros** (~15 creatures, confirmed end-of-format 03-11); 16 only with an ultra-low curve and no real top-end (and watch double-W/double-R requirements — they make 16 "the most painful"). **18 for slower bomb/value/control decks** (late refinement: "I want 18 lands again," 03-14; "I just like running 18 lands here," 03-18). Don't run 3+ tap-lands in aggro; cap at ~2 (Kenji strongly dislikes tap-lands in Boros, tolerates minimally for a splash). Single-pip splash wants ~4 sources.
- **Disguise is curve-filler, not a primary plan.** Disguising on 3 trades evenly and isn't strongly punished, so it's fine when you'd otherwise do nothing — but don't build *around* it.
- **Mindset check (Kenji's own).** He suspects his losing is partly a mindset mismatch — "stuck in the mindset that is not working with formats like these especially for best of one" (03-24). The format rewards proactive get-on-the-board play over reactive value; if you keep losing, lean harder into tempo.
